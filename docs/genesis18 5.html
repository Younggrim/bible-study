Genesis — Chapter 18

1And the LORD appeared to him by the oaks of Mamre, as he sat at the door of his tent in the heat of the day.

2He lifted up his eyes and looked, and behold, three men were standing in front of him. When he saw them, he ran from the tent door to meet them and bowed himself to the earth

3and said, “O Lord, if I have found favor in your sight, do not pass by your servant.

4Let a little water be brought, and wash your feet, and rest yourselves under the tree,

5while I bring a morsel of bread, that you may refresh yourselves, and after that you may pass on—since you have come to your servant.” So they said, “Do as you have said.”

6And Abraham went quickly into the tent to Sarah and said, “Quick! Three seahs of fine flour! Knead it, and make cakes.”

7And Abraham ran to the herd and took a calf, tender and good, and gave it to a young man, who prepared it quickly.

8Then he took curds and milk and the calf that he had prepared, and set it before them. And he stood by them under the tree while they ate.

9They said to him, “Where is Sarah your wife?” And he said, “She is in the tent.”

10The LORD said, “I will surely return to you about this time next year, and Sarah your wife shall have a son.” And Sarah was listening at the tent door behind him.

11Now Abraham and Sarah were old, advanced in years. The way of women had ceased to be with Sarah.

12So Sarah laughed to herself, saying, “After I am worn out, and my lord is old, shall I have pleasure?”

13The LORD said to Abraham, “Why did Sarah laugh and say, ‘Shall I indeed bear a child, now that I am old?’

14Is anything too hard for the LORD? At the appointed time I will return to you, about this time next year, and Sarah shall have a son.”

15But Sarah denied it, saying, “I did not laugh,” for she was afraid. He said, “No, but you did laugh.”

16Then the men set out from there, and they looked down toward Sodom. And Abraham went with them to set them on their way.

17The LORD said, “Shall I hide from Abraham what I am about to do,

18seeing that Abraham shall surely become a great and mighty nation, and all the nations of the earth shall be blessed in him?

19For I have chosen him, that he may command his children and his household after him to keep the way of the LORD by doing righteousness and justice, so that the LORD may bring to Abraham what he has promised him.”

20Then the LORD said, “Because the outcry against Sodom and Gomorrah is great and their sin is very grave,

21I will go down to see whether they have done altogether according to the outcry that has come to me. And if not, I will know.”

22So the men turned from there and went toward Sodom, but Abraham still stood before the LORD.

23Then Abraham drew near and said, “Will you indeed sweep away the righteous with the wicked?

24Suppose there are fifty righteous within the city. Will you then sweep away the place and not spare it for the fifty righteous who are in it?

25Far be it from you to do such a thing, to put the righteous to death with the wicked, so that the righteous fare as the wicked! Far be that from you! Shall not the Judge of all the earth do what is just?”

26And the LORD said, “If I find at Sodom fifty righteous in the city, I will spare the whole place for their sake.”

27Abraham answered and said, “Behold, I have undertaken to speak to the Lord, I who am but dust and ashes.

28Suppose five of the fifty righteous are lacking. Will you destroy the whole city for lack of five?” And he said, “I will not destroy it if I find forty-five there.”

29Again he spoke to him and said, “Suppose forty are found there.” He answered, “For the sake of forty I will not do it.”

30Then he said, “Oh let not the Lord be angry, and I will speak. Suppose thirty are found there.” He answered, “I will not do it, if I find thirty there.”

31He said, “Behold, I have undertaken to speak to the Lord. Suppose twenty are found there.” He answered, “For the sake of twenty I will not destroy it.”

32Then he said, “Oh let not the Lord be angry, and I will speak again but this once. Suppose ten are found there.” He answered, “For the sake of ten I will not destroy it.”

33And the LORD went his way, when he had finished speaking to Abraham, and Abraham returned to his place.

Summary

Three visitors announce Isaac's birth to Abraham and Sarah; Abraham intercedes for Sodom, bargaining with God for the righteous.

Key Verses

v.14
"Is anything too hard for the LORD?" (ESV) — The rhetorical question that answers all doubt.
v.17
"Shall I hide from Abraham what I am about to do?" (ESV) — God's friendship and transparency with Abraham.
v.19
"For I have chosen him, that he may command his children and his household after him." (ESV) — God's confidence in Abraham's faithfulness.
v.23
"Will you indeed sweep away the righteous with the wicked?" (ESV) — Abraham's bold intercession.
v.25
"Shall not the Judge of all the earth do what is just?" (ESV) — The foundation of trust: God is just.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/genesis-18/ Guzik covers the theophany, Abraham's hospitality, the Isaac promise, and the intercession for Sodom.
  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "Is Anything Too Hard for the LORD?" on v.14 (MTP Vol. 14, No. 835) "Is ANYTHING too hard? Not some things — anything. Too hard for the LORD? Not for man, not for nature, not for circumstances — for the LORD. The God who made the universe from nothing, who raises the dead, who calls things that are not as though they were — is ANYTHING too hard for Him? Sarah laughed at the impossibility. God laughed at her laughter. And Isaac ('laughter') was born. Your impossibility is God's opportunity." Sermon: "Abraham's Intercession" on vv.23-32 (MTP Vol. 11, No. 640) "Abraham drew near — boldly, yet humbly. 'I am but dust and ashes' (v.27) — yet he persists. Six times he asks. Six times God agrees. This is the model of intercession: bold access, humble spirit, persistent pleading, and confidence in God's justice. 'Shall not the Judge of all the earth do right?' — this is the foundation: God IS just. We can trust His decisions."

Translations

  • v.1 — KJV: "the LORD appeared" / ESV: "the LORD appeared" — Both identical. Hebrew "Yahweh" appeared. One of the three visitors IS Yahweh (a theophany — pre-incarnate Christ). The other two are angels (19:1).
  • v.3 — KJV: "My Lord" / ESV: "O Lord" — Both equivalent. Hebrew "Adonai" (capital L) — Abraham addresses the lead visitor as LORD, recognizing the divine presence.
  • v.12 — KJV: "laughed within herself" / ESV: "Sarah laughed to herself" — Both equivalent. Hebrew "titsachaq beqirbah" — laughed inwardly. The same root as "Isaac" (yitschaq). Both parents laugh — and the child is named for it.
  • v.14 — KJV: "Is any thing too hard for the LORD?" / ESV: "Is anything too hard for the LORD?" — Both identical. Hebrew "yippale" — too wonderful, too extraordinary. From "pala" — to be marvelous, beyond human ability. Nothing is too wonderful/extraordinary for God.
  • v.19 — KJV: "I know him" / ESV: "I have chosen him" — Hebrew "yeda'tiv" — I have known him intimately, I have chosen him. ESV renders the relational "know" as "chosen," making the election aspect explicit. God's intimate knowledge of Abraham includes confidence in his faithfulness.
  • v.25 — KJV: "Shall not the Judge of all the earth do right?" / ESV: "Shall not the Judge of all the earth do what is just?" — Both equivalent. Hebrew "hashophet kol ha'arets." ESV uses "just" for Hebrew "mishpat"; KJV uses "right." Abraham appeals to God's character as universal Judge. If God is just, He cannot destroy the righteous with the wicked.
  • v.27 — KJV: "dust and ashes" / ESV: "dust and ashes" — Both identical. Abraham's self-description. Bold in access yet humble in self- assessment. The proper posture of intercession.
  • TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB) - v.1 — NET: "during the hottest time of the day" — renders "the heat of the day" with temporal emphasis, while WEB has "the heat of the day" and ASV "in the heat of the day." All capture the same setting. - v.11 — NET: "Sarah had long since passed menopause" — strikingly clinical modern language where KJV has "it ceased to be with Sarah after the manner of women" and ESV has "the way of women had ceased to be with Sarah." - v.12 — NET: "After I am worn out will I have pleasure" — renders Hebrew "edna" (pleasure/delight) more naturally than KJV's archaic "shall I have pleasure." - v.14 — WEB: "Is anything too hard for Yahweh?" — using the personal name makes this rhetorical question even more intimate and powerful. NET: "Is anything impossible for the Lord?" — replaces "too hard" with "impossible." - v.19 — NET: "I have chosen him so that he may command his children" — interprets Hebrew "yada'tiv" (KJV: "I know him") as "I have chosen him," reflecting the relational/covenantal sense of "know" in Hebrew.

Reflection

  • 1. "Is any thing too hard for the LORD?" (v.14). What "impossible" situation are you facing? Is it too hard for God? Sarah laughed at impossibility — and God delivered. What are you laughing at that God intends to do?
  • 2. "Shall I hide from Abraham?" (v.17). God shares His plans with His friends. Are you close enough to God to hear His heart? Do you seek His purposes, not just His blessings?
  • 3. "Shall not the Judge of all the earth do right?" (v.25). When you don't understand God's actions, can you trust His character? He is just. He does right. Always.
  • 4. Abraham interceded for Sodom — a wicked city. Are you interceding for your city, your nation, the lost? Who needs your prayers today?
  • 5. Abraham's hospitality (vv.1-8) led to divine encounter. Are you generous with your home, your time, your resources? You never know who you might be hosting.
