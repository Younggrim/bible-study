Women of the Bible

Sarah

Her Story

Sarah was Abraham's wife and partner in the journey of faith from Ur to Canaan. She left everything familiar to follow her husband into an unknown future based on God's promise. For decades she bore the weight of barrenness in a culture where a woman's worth was measured by her children. The promise of a son seemed to mock her with each passing year, yet God had not forgotten.

When three visitors came to Abraham's tent and declared that Sarah would bear a son within a year, she laughed — not from joy but from the bitter impossibility of it. She was ninety years old. Yet God's response was not anger but a question that echoes through the ages: "Is anything too hard for the LORD?" Within the year, Isaac was born, and Sarah's laughter transformed from cynicism to pure delight.

Sarah's story is marked by both faith and failure. She gave Hagar to Abraham in an attempt to fulfill God's promise through human engineering, creating generations of conflict. Yet the New Testament honors her as a woman of faith and a model of hope. Her life teaches us that God's promises do not depend on our ability to make them happen — they depend on His faithfulness alone.

Key Scriptures

  • Genesis 18:14 — "Is anything too hard for the LORD? At the appointed time I will return to you."
  • Genesis 21:1-2 — The LORD visited Sarah as He had said, and she bore Abraham a son in his old age.
  • Hebrews 11:11 — "By faith Sarah herself received power to conceive, because she considered him faithful who had promised."
  • 1 Peter 3:6 — Sarah obeyed Abraham, calling him lord — and you are her children if you do good.
  • Romans 4:19-21 — Abraham did not weaken in faith... fully convinced that God was able to do what He had promised.

Lessons from Her Life

Sarah teaches us that doubt and faith can coexist within the same heart. She laughed at God's promise, yet Scripture calls her a woman of faith. This apparent contradiction reveals that God does not demand perfect confidence — He asks only that we stay in the conversation with Him, even when His promises seem absurd. Sarah's journey from cynical laughter to joyful laughter shows that God is patient enough to transform our doubt into delight.

Sarah also warns us about the danger of trying to help God fulfill His promises through human scheming. Her decision regarding Hagar produced consequences that persist to this day. When God makes a promise, our job is to trust and wait — not to engineer solutions that seem more realistic. The hardest act of faith is often simply refusing to act when God has not yet moved.

Reflection Questions

  • What promise of God seems impossible to you right now? Can you trust that nothing is too hard for Him?
  • Have you ever tried to 'help God' fulfill a promise through your own efforts? What happened?
  • How does Sarah's journey from doubt to delight encourage your own waiting seasons?
