Leviticus — Chapter 23

1The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

2“Speak to the people of Israel and say to them, These are the appointed feasts of the LORD that you shall proclaim as holy convocations; they are my appointed feasts.

3“Six days shall work be done, but on the seventh day is a Sabbath of solemn rest, a holy convocation. You shall do no work. It is a Sabbath to the LORD in all your dwelling places.

4“These are the appointed feasts of the LORD, the holy convocations, which you shall proclaim at the time appointed for them.

5In the first month, on the fourteenth day of the month at twilight, is the LORD’s Passover.

6And on the fifteenth day of the same month is the Feast of Unleavened Bread to the LORD; for seven days you shall eat unleavened bread.

7On the first day you shall have a holy convocation; you shall not do any ordinary work.

8But you shall present a food offering to the LORD for seven days. On the seventh day is a holy convocation; you shall not do any ordinary work.”

9And the LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

10“Speak to the people of Israel and say to them, When you come into the land that I give you and reap its harvest, you shall bring the sheaf of the firstfruits of your harvest to the priest,

11and he shall wave the sheaf before the LORD, so that you may be accepted. On the day after the Sabbath the priest shall wave it.

12And on the day when you wave the sheaf, you shall offer a male lamb a year old without blemish as a burnt offering to the LORD.

13And the grain offering with it shall be two tenths of an ephah of fine flour mixed with oil, a food offering to the LORD with a pleasing aroma, and the drink offering with it shall be of wine, a fourth of a hin.

14And you shall eat neither bread nor grain parched or fresh until this same day, until you have brought the offering of your God: it is a statute forever throughout your generations in all your dwellings.

15“You shall count seven full weeks from the day after the Sabbath, from the day that you brought the sheaf of the wave offering.

16You shall count fifty days to the day after the seventh Sabbath. Then you shall present a grain offering of new grain to the LORD.

17You shall bring from your dwelling places two loaves of bread to be waved, made of two tenths of an ephah. They shall be of fine flour, and they shall be baked with leaven, as firstfruits to the LORD.

18And you shall present with the bread seven lambs a year old without blemish, and one bull from the herd and two rams. They shall be a burnt offering to the LORD, with their grain offering and their drink offerings, a food offering with a pleasing aroma to the LORD.

19And you shall offer one male goat for a sin offering, and two male lambs a year old as a sacrifice of peace offerings.

20And the priest shall wave them with the bread of the firstfruits as a wave offering before the LORD, with the two lambs. They shall be holy to the LORD for the priest.

21And you shall make a proclamation on the same day. You shall hold a holy convocation. You shall not do any ordinary work. It is a statute forever in all your dwelling places throughout your generations.

22“And when you reap the harvest of your land, you shall not reap your field right up to its edge, nor shall you gather the gleanings after your harvest. You shall leave them for the poor and for the sojourner: I am the LORD your God.”

23And the LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

24“Speak to the people of Israel, saying, In the seventh month, on the first day of the month, you shall observe a day of solemn rest, a memorial proclaimed with blast of trumpets, a holy convocation.

25You shall not do any ordinary work, and you shall present a food offering to the LORD.”

26And the LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

27“Now on the tenth day of this seventh month is the Day of Atonement. It shall be for you a time of holy convocation, and you shall afflict yourselves and present a food offering to the LORD.

28And you shall not do any work on that very day, for it is a Day of Atonement, to make atonement for you before the LORD your God.

29For whoever is not afflicted on that very day shall be cut off from his people.

30And whoever does any work on that very day, that person I will destroy from among his people.

31You shall not do any work. It is a statute forever throughout your generations in all your dwelling places.

32It shall be to you a Sabbath of solemn rest, and you shall afflict yourselves. On the ninth day of the month beginning at evening, from evening to evening shall you keep your Sabbath.”

33And the LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

34“Speak to the people of Israel, saying, On the fifteenth day of this seventh month and for seven days is the Feast of Booths to the LORD.

35On the first day shall be a holy convocation; you shall not do any ordinary work.

36For seven days you shall present food offerings to the LORD. On the eighth day you shall hold a holy convocation and present a food offering to the LORD. It is a solemn assembly; you shall not do any ordinary work.

37“These are the appointed feasts of the LORD, which you shall proclaim as times of holy convocation, for presenting to the LORD food offerings, burnt offerings and grain offerings, sacrifices and drink offerings, each on its proper day,

38besides the LORD’s Sabbaths and besides your gifts and besides all your vow offerings and besides all your freewill offerings, which you give to the LORD.

39“On the fifteenth day of the seventh month, when you have gathered in the produce of the land, you shall celebrate the feast of the LORD seven days. On the first day shall be a solemn rest, and on the eighth day shall be a solemn rest.

40And you shall take on the first day the fruit of splendid trees, branches of palm trees and boughs of leafy trees and willows of the brook, and you shall rejoice before the LORD your God seven days.

41You shall celebrate it as a feast to the LORD for seven days in the year. It is a statute forever throughout your generations; you shall celebrate it in the seventh month.

42You shall dwell in booths for seven days. All native Israelites shall dwell in booths,

43that your generations may know that I made the people of Israel dwell in booths when I brought them out of the land of Egypt: I am the LORD your God.”

44Thus Moses declared to the people of Israel the appointed feasts of the LORD.

Summary

God establishes Israel's sacred calendar — Sabbath, Passover, Firstfruits, Pentecost, Trumpets, Day of Atonement, and Tabernacles — each pointing to Christ.

Key Verses

v.2
"These are my feasts." — Not Israel's feasts — GOD'S appointed times. He sets the calendar; He determines the fulfillment.
v.3
"The seventh day is the sabbath of rest, an holy convocation; ye shall do no work therein: it is the sabbath of the LORD." — The Sabbath principle underlies all feasts: God's work, God's rest.
v.5
"The LORD's passover." — The foundational feast. Everything begins with the blood of the lamb.
v.11
"On the morrow after the sabbath the priest shall wave it." — Firstfruits on the day after the Sabbath — resurrection day.
v.17
"They shall be baken with leaven; they are the firstfruits unto the LORD." — Two leavened loaves at Pentecost: Jew and Gentile, still with sin, united in one offering.
v.24
"A memorial of blowing of trumpets, an holy convocation." — Trumpets announce the King's coming and the gathering of His people.
v.27
"On the tenth day of this seventh month there shall be a day of atonement... ye shall afflict your souls." — National repentance and atonement.
v.42
"You shall dwell in booths for seven days." (ESV) — Remembering dependence on God; pointing to God dwelling with man.
v.43
"That your generations may know that I made the children of Israel to dwell in booths, when I brought them out of the land of Egypt." — Memory and hope: what God did, what God will do.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/leviticus-23/ Guzik provides an excellent overview of the prophetic significance of each feast, noting that the spring feasts were fulfilled precisely on their calendar dates at Christ's first coming, giving confidence that the fall feasts will be fulfilled with equal precision at His return.
  • Charles Spurgeon: "These feasts are God's great object lessons written on the calendar of Israel. Passover — the blood that saves. Unleavened Bread — the life that is purged of sin. Firstfruits — the resurrection that guarantees ours. Pentecost — the Spirit poured out upon the Church. And then silence — a long summer of waiting. But the trumpets will sound again. The great Day of Atonement for the nation will come. And then — oh, then! — the great feast of Tabernacles, when God shall dwell with His people forever. Every feast is a prophecy. Every prophecy points to Christ."

Translations

  • v.2 — KJV: "the feasts of the LORD" / ESV: "the appointed feasts of the LORD" — Hebrew "mo'adei YHWH" — from "mo'ed" (appointed time, meeting, season). These are not merely celebrations but divinely appointed times — scheduled meetings between God and His people. ESV's "appointed feasts" captures this better than KJV's simple "feasts."
  • v.2 — KJV: "holy convocations" / ESV: "holy convocations" — Hebrew "miqra'ei qodesh" — from "qara" (to call, proclaim, read). A "miqra" is a calling together, a rehearsal, a proclamation. The feasts are sacred assemblies AND prophetic rehearsals.
  • v.3 — KJV: "the sabbath of rest" / ESV: "a Sabbath of solemn rest" — Hebrew "shabbat shabbaton" — an intensive form: a sabbath of complete cessation. ESV's "solemn rest" captures the intensification.
  • v.5 — KJV: "at even" / ESV: "at twilight" — Hebrew "bein ha-arbayim" — literally "between the evenings." Debated: either between noon and sunset, or between sunset and dark. The Passover lamb was slain in the late afternoon. KJV's "at even" and ESV's "at twilight" both approximate.
  • v.7 — KJV: "no servile work" / ESV: "no ordinary work" — Hebrew "melekhet avodah" — work of labor/service. Distinguished from "all work" (kol melakhah) used for Sabbath and Day of Atonement. On feast days, food preparation was permitted; on Sabbath/Atonement, NO work at all. KJV's "servile" is archaic; ESV's "ordinary" is clearer.
  • v.11 — KJV: "on the morrow after the sabbath" / ESV: "On the day after the Sabbath" — Hebrew "mi-macharat ha-shabbat." This phrase generated significant debate: which Sabbath? The Pharisees said the first day of Unleavened Bread (Nisan 16 always); the Sadducees said the weekly Sabbath during the feast (always a Sunday). Christ's resurrection on Sunday confirms the Sunday interpretation.
  • v.13 — KJV: "the meat offering" / ESV: "the grain offering" — Hebrew "minchah." KJV's "meat" is archaic English for "food" (not flesh). ESV correctly renders "grain offering."
  • v.15 — KJV: "seven sabbaths shall be complete" / ESV: "seven full weeks" — Hebrew "sheva shabbatot temimot" — seven complete sabbaths/weeks. "Shabbat" can mean either "sabbath" or "week." The count of 49 days (7x7) leads to the 50th day — Pentecost (Greek: "fiftieth").
  • v.17 — KJV: "baken with leaven" / ESV: "baked with leaven" — Hebrew "chametz te'afenah." Remarkable: leaven is normally forbidden in offerings (2:11). These two loaves are the exception — because they represent the Church (Jew and Gentile), which still contains sin. A sin offering accompanies (v.19) for this reason.
  • v.24 — KJV: "a memorial of blowing of trumpets" / ESV: "a memorial proclaimed with blast of trumpets" — Hebrew "zikron teru'ah" — a memorial of shouting/trumpet blast. "Teru'ah" can mean a shout, an alarm, or a trumpet blast. The specific purpose is not stated — it is a memorial, but of what? The mystery adds to its prophetic significance.
  • v.34 — KJV: "the feast of tabernacles" / ESV: "the Feast of Booths" — Hebrew "chag ha-sukkot" — the festival of booths/shelters. "Sukkah" is a temporary shelter made of branches. KJV's "tabernacles" (from Latin "tabernaculum" — tent) and ESV's "booths" both translate correctly. The temporary nature is the point: life on earth is temporary; God's dwelling with us will be permanent.
  • v.40 — KJV: "boughs of goodly trees, branches of palm trees, and the boughs of thick trees, and willows of the brook" / ESV: "the fruit of splendid trees, branches of palm trees and boughs of leafy trees and willows of the brook" — Hebrew identifies four types of vegetation. Jewish tradition identifies these as citron (etrog), palm (lulav), myrtle (hadas), and willow (aravah) — the "four species" still used in Sukkot celebrations today.

Reflection

  • 1. "These are MY feasts" (v.2). God sets the calendar. He determines the times and seasons. The feasts are not human inventions but divine appointments. Christ fulfilled the spring feasts on their EXACT dates — Passover (crucifixion), Unleavened Bread (burial), Firstfruits (resurrection), Pentecost (Spirit). This gives absolute confidence that the fall feasts will be fulfilled with equal precision. God is never early, never late.
  • 2. The prophetic gap between spring and fall feasts. We live in the gap — between Pentecost and Trumpets. The Church Age was a mystery hidden in the Old Testament (Ephesians 3:4-6). But the trumpets WILL sound. The next feast on God's calendar is coming. Are you ready?
  • 3. Firstfruits and resurrection (vv.10-11). Christ rose on the day of Firstfruits — and His resurrection GUARANTEES ours. "If the firstfruits be holy, the lump is also holy" (Romans 11:16). Because He rose, we will rise. The sheaf waved before God is the pledge of the full harvest to come.
  • 4. Two leavened loaves (v.17). The Church is not yet perfect. We are redeemed but still contain sin (leaven). Yet God accepts us — with a sin offering (v.19). We are accepted not because we are sinless but because Christ's sacrifice covers our remaining sin. Grace does not wait for perfection.
  • 5. "Ye shall dwell in booths" (v.42). Life is temporary. We are pilgrims and strangers (1 Peter 2:11). The booth reminds us: this world is not our home. But Tabernacles is also the feast of JOY — because the temporary leads to the permanent. God will dwell with us forever (Revelation 21:3).
