Exodus — Chapter 9

1Then the LORD said to Moses, “Go in to Pharaoh and say to him, ‘Thus says the LORD, the God of the Hebrews, “Let my people go, that they may serve me.

2For if you refuse to let them go and still hold them,

3behold, the hand of the LORD will fall with a very severe plague upon your livestock that are in the field, the horses, the donkeys, the camels, the herds, and the flocks.

4But the LORD will make a distinction between the livestock of Israel and the livestock of Egypt, so that nothing of all that belongs to the people of Israel shall die.”’”

5And the LORD set a time, saying, “Tomorrow the LORD will do this thing in the land.”

6And the next day the LORD did this thing. All the livestock of the Egyptians died, but not one of the livestock of the people of Israel died.

7And Pharaoh sent, and behold, not one of the livestock of Israel was dead. But the heart of Pharaoh was hardened, and he did not let the people go.

8And the LORD said to Moses and Aaron, “Take handfuls of soot from the kiln, and let Moses throw them in the air in the sight of Pharaoh.

9It shall become fine dust over all the land of Egypt, and become boils breaking out in sores on man and beast throughout all the land of Egypt.”

10So they took soot from the kiln and stood before Pharaoh. And Moses threw it in the air, and it became boils breaking out in sores on man and beast.

11And the magicians could not stand before Moses because of the boils, for the boils came upon the magicians and upon all the Egyptians.

12But the LORD hardened the heart of Pharaoh, and he did not listen to them, as the LORD had spoken to Moses.

13Then the LORD said to Moses, “Rise up early in the morning and present yourself before Pharaoh and say to him, ‘Thus says the LORD, the God of the Hebrews, “Let my people go, that they may serve me.

14For this time I will send all my plagues on you yourself, and on your servants and your people, so that you may know that there is none like me in all the earth.

15For by now I could have put out my hand and struck you and your people with pestilence, and you would have been cut off from the earth.

16But for this purpose I have raised you up, to show you my power, so that my name may be proclaimed in all the earth.

17You are still exalting yourself against my people and will not let them go.

18Behold, about this time tomorrow I will cause very heavy hail to fall, such as never has been in Egypt from the day it was founded until now.

19Now therefore send, get your livestock and all that you have in the field into safe shelter, for every man and beast that is in the field and is not brought home will die when the hail falls on them.”’”

20Then whoever feared the word of the LORD among the servants of Pharaoh hurried his slaves and his livestock into the houses,

21but whoever did not pay attention to the word of the LORD left his slaves and his livestock in the field.

22Then the LORD said to Moses, “Stretch out your hand toward heaven, so that there may be hail in all the land of Egypt, on man and beast and every plant of the field, in the land of Egypt.”

23Then Moses stretched out his staff toward heaven, and the LORD sent thunder and hail, and fire ran down to the earth. And the LORD rained hail upon the land of Egypt.

24There was hail and fire flashing continually in the midst of the hail, very heavy hail, such as had never been in all the land of Egypt since it became a nation.

25The hail struck down everything that was in the field in all the land of Egypt, both man and beast. And the hail struck down every plant of the field and broke every tree of the field.

26Only in the land of Goshen, where the people of Israel were, was there no hail.

27Then Pharaoh sent and called Moses and Aaron and said to them, “This time I have sinned; the LORD is in the right, and I and my people are in the wrong.

28Plead with the LORD, for there has been enough of God’s thunder and hail. I will let you go, and you shall stay no longer.”

29Moses said to him, “As soon as I have gone out of the city, I will stretch out my hands to the LORD. The thunder will cease, and there will be no more hail, so that you may know that the earth is the LORD’s.

30But as for you and your servants, I know that you do not yet fear the LORD God.”

31(The flax and the barley were struck down, for the barley was in the ear and the flax was in bud.

32But the wheat and the emmer were not struck down, for they are late in coming up.)

33So Moses went out of the city from Pharaoh and stretched out his hands to the LORD, and the thunder and the hail ceased, and the rain no longer poured upon the earth.

34But when Pharaoh saw that the rain and the hail and the thunder had ceased, he sinned yet again and hardened his heart, he and his servants.

35So the heart of Pharaoh was hardened, and he did not let the people of Israel go, just as the LORD had spoken through Moses.

Summary

God strikes Egypt with livestock disease, boils, and devastating hail; Pharaoh admits sin but hardens his heart again.

Key Verses

v.6
"All the livestock of Egypt died, but not one of the livestock of the people of Israel died." (ESV) — God's precise discrimination.
v.11
"The magicians could not stand before Moses because of the boils." (ESV) — Counterfeit power completely defeated.
v.14
"That you may know that there is none like me in all the earth." (ESV) — The purpose of the plagues: revelation.
v.16
"But for this purpose I have raised you up, to show you my power, so that my name may be proclaimed in all the earth." (ESV) — God's sovereign purpose in Pharaoh.
v.27
"This time I have sinned; the LORD is in the right, and I and my people are in the wrong." (ESV) — Pharaoh's confession — true words without true repentance.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/exodus-9/ Guzik covers the escalation of plagues, God's stated purpose in raising up Pharaoh, the mercy shown in warning before the hail, and the distinction between true and false repentance.
  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "For This Cause Have I Raised Thee Up" on v.16 (MTP Vol. 11, No. 617) "For this cause have I RAISED THEE UP — God raised Pharaoh to the throne of Egypt for a purpose: to display divine power and declare the divine name throughout all the earth. And it worked. Rahab in Jericho heard about it (Joshua 2:10). The Philistines heard (1 Samuel 4:8). The whole ancient world heard. Pharaoh thought he was building his own empire — he was building God's reputation. Every tyrant who opposes God becomes an unwitting advertisement for God's power. The harder Pharaoh resisted, the more glorious God's victory became."

Translations

  • v.3 — KJV: "murrain" / ESV: "severe plague" — Hebrew "dever" — pestilence, plague. The KJV's "murrain" is archaic English for animal plague; the ESV's "severe plague" is accessible to modern readers.
  • v.9 — KJV: "boil breaking forth with blains" / ESV: "boils breaking out in sores" — Hebrew "shechin pore'ach avabu'ot" — an inflamed sore erupting in blisters. The ESV is clearer; the KJV's "blains" is archaic for blisters.
  • v.14 — KJV: "all my plagues upon thine heart" / ESV: "all my plagues on you yourself" — Hebrew "al libbeka" — upon your heart. The KJV is more literal; the ESV interprets "heart" as "you yourself." The plagues target Pharaoh's will — the seat of decision.
  • v.16 — KJV: "raised thee up" / ESV: "raised you up" — Hebrew "he'emadtikha" — caused you to stand, established you, preserved you. Both translations equivalent. God sustained Pharaoh through the plagues for a purpose — not creating evil but using an evil man for divine glory.
  • v.24 — KJV: "fire mingled with the hail" / ESV: "fire flashing continually in the midst of the hail" — Hebrew "esh mitlaqqachat betokh habarad" — fire flashing/taking hold within the hail. The ESV is more descriptive; the KJV is more concise. Lightning within hailstones — supernatural and terrifying.
  • v.27 — KJV: "I have sinned this time" / ESV: "This time I have sinned" — Both equivalent. "This time" (happa'am) suggests Pharaoh admits fault only for this instance — not genuine, deep repentance.
  • v.31 — KJV: "bolled" / ESV: "in bud" — Hebrew "giv'ol" — in bud/bloom. The KJV's "bolled" is extremely archaic; the ESV's "in bud" is clear. This detail helps date the plague to January-February.
  • ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB) - v.3 — ASV: "a very grievous murrain" — preserves the archaic English "murrain" (livestock disease); NET: "a very terrible plague"; WEB: "a very grievous pestilence." ASV's term directly links to the traditional English plague vocabulary. - v.9 — ASV: "boil breaking forth with blains" — "blains" is an archaic term for blisters/pustules, revealing the Hebrew "avabu'ot" (blisters); NET: "boils to break out and fester" — adds "fester" interpretively; WEB: "boil breaking out with boils." - v.16 — ASV: "for this cause have I made thee to stand" / NET: "for this purpose I have caused you to stand" / WEB: "for this cause I have made you stand" — the verse quoted in Romans 9:17. NET's "caused you to stand" subtly implies divine sovereignty over Pharaoh's existence. - v.23 — WEB: "lightning flashed down to the earth" — WEB interprets Hebrew "esh" (fire) as "lightning"; ASV: "fire ran down unto the earth" — more literal; NET: "fire fell to the earth" — a middle rendering. - v.27 — WEB: "Yahweh is righteous" — Pharaoh acknowledges "Yahweh" by name, making his confession more personal in WEB; in ASV: "Jehovah is righteous" achieves the same effect.

Reflection

  • 1. "For this cause have I raised thee up" (v.16). God has a purpose for every person — even those who oppose Him. Nothing is wasted in God's economy. What is God accomplishing through your circumstances — even the difficult ones?
  • 2. "He that feared the word of the LORD... made his servants and his cattle flee into the houses" (v.20). God offers warning before judgment. Some Egyptians listened; some didn't. When God warns you — through Scripture, through conscience, through others — do you listen?
  • 3. "I have sinned... the LORD is righteous" (v.27). Pharaoh says the right words but doesn't mean them. Is your repentance genuine — producing lasting change? Or is it pressure-driven confession that evaporates when things improve?
  • 4. The magicians "could not stand" (v.11). They started by imitating God's power; now they can't even show up. Every counterfeit eventually fails. Don't be impressed by imitations — they have an expiration date.
  • 5. "That my name may be declared throughout all the earth" (v.16). God's ultimate purpose is His glory — His name known everywhere. Is your life contributing to that purpose? Are people learning who God is by watching your story?
