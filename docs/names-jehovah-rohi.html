Names of God

Jehovah Rohi

Understanding Jehovah Rohi

Jehovah Rohi — "The LORD My Shepherd" — is revealed in the beloved opening of Psalm 23: "The LORD is my shepherd, I lack nothing." The Hebrew word "rohi" (ro'i) means "my shepherd," and it paints a picture of God as the personal, attentive caretaker of His people. In the ancient Near East, a shepherd was not merely a herdsman but a protector, provider, guide, and constant companion to his flock — living with them day and night, knowing each one by name.

David, who wrote Psalm 23, understood shepherding intimately. As a young man tending his father's sheep, he had fought lions and bears to protect his flock. He knew the shepherd's rod (for defense against predators) and staff (for guiding and rescuing wandering sheep). When David declared God as his Shepherd, he was saying: the same fierce protection, tender guidance, and constant provision I gave my sheep, God gives to me — only infinitely greater.

Jesus claimed this name explicitly in John 10:11: "I am the good shepherd. The good shepherd lays down his life for the sheep." Jehovah Rohi is not a hired hand who runs when danger comes. He is the shepherd who personally knows each sheep by name, who leads them to green pastures and still waters, who walks with them through the darkest valleys, and who ultimately gives His own life so they might live. This is shepherding at its most sacrificial and complete.

Key Scriptures

  • Psalm 23:1-3 — "The LORD is my shepherd, I lack nothing. He makes me lie down in green pastures, he leads me beside quiet waters, he refreshes my soul." The complete provision of Jehovah Rohi.
  • John 10:11 — "I am the good shepherd. The good shepherd lays down his life for the sheep." Jesus reveals the ultimate cost of divine shepherding.
  • John 10:14 — "I am the good shepherd; I know my sheep and my sheep know me." Jehovah Rohi's relationship with us is personal and mutual.
  • Isaiah 40:11 — "He tends his flock like a shepherd: He gathers the lambs in his arms and carries them close to his heart." The tender, gentle care of our Shepherd.
  • Ezekiel 34:11-12 — "I myself will search for my sheep and look after them... I will rescue them from all the places where they were scattered." God's personal commitment to seek the lost.

What This Name Means for Us

Jehovah Rohi means we are never without guidance, provision, or protection. The Shepherd goes before us into every situation — He has already scouted the terrain, identified the dangers, and prepared a path. We do not need to figure out life alone or navigate by our own wisdom. Our role is simply to follow — to listen for His voice, to stay close to Him, and to trust that He is leading us to good places even when the path goes through dark valleys.

This name also speaks to rest. In Psalm 23, the Shepherd "makes" the sheep lie down — because sheep, like us, often refuse to rest unless someone they trust tells them it is safe. Jehovah Rohi gives us permission to stop striving, to cease our anxious running, and to receive the rest our souls desperately need. Under His watchful care, we can lie down in peace knowing that He is on guard, He is in control, and nothing will pluck us from His hand.

Reflection Questions

  • In what ways have you experienced God as your Shepherd — guiding, providing, protecting, or restoring you?
  • Are you currently in a "green pasture" season or a "valley of the shadow" season? How is the Shepherd's presence different or the same in each?
  • Do you struggle to rest and trust, or do you tend to run ahead of the Shepherd? What would it look like to follow more closely?
  • How does knowing that the Good Shepherd laid down His life for you affect your trust in His ongoing care?
