Psalms — Chapter 112

1Praise the LORD! Blessed is the man who fears the LORD, who greatly delights in his commandments!

2His offspring will be mighty in the land; the generation of the upright will be blessed.

3Wealth and riches are in his house, and his righteousness endures forever.

4Light dawns in the darkness for the upright; he is gracious, merciful, and righteous.

5It is well with the man who deals generously and lends; who conducts his affairs with justice.

6For the righteous will never be moved; he will be remembered forever.

7He is not afraid of bad news; his heart is firm, trusting in the LORD.

8His heart is steady; he will not be afraid, until he looks in triumph on his adversaries.

9He has distributed freely; he has given to the poor; his righteousness endures forever; his horn is exalted in honor.

10The wicked man sees it and is angry; he gnashes his teeth and melts away; the desire of the wicked will perish!

Summary

An acrostic wisdom psalm (companion to 111) — blessed is the man who fears the LORD and delights in His commands; he is generous, righteous, secure, not afraid of evil tidings; the wicked see it and are vexed.

Key Verses

v.1
"Blessed is the man that feareth the LORD, that delighteth greatly in his commandments." (KJV) — Two marks: (1) fears the LORD, (2) DELIGHTS GREATLY in His commandments. Not mere compliance but delight. Not casual obedience but GREAT delight.
v.7
"He shall not be afraid of evil tidings: his heart is fixed, trusting in the LORD." (KJV) — A fixed heart does not fear bad news. Not because bad things won't happen, but because trust in God transcends circumstances. When the phone rings with bad news, the fixed heart stands.
v.9
"He hath dispersed, he hath given to the poor; his righteousness endureth for ever." (KJV) — Quoted by Paul in 2 Corinthians 9:9 regarding generous giving. The righteous person DISPERSES (scatters abroad) wealth to the poor — and paradoxically, their righteousness endures forever. Generosity doesn't deplete; it establishes.

Cross-References

  • v.1 — Psalm 1:1-2 — "Blessed is the man... his delight is in the law of the LORD." v.4 — Psalm 111:4 — Same attributes applied to GOD: "gracious and full of compassion." v.7 — Proverbs 3:25 — "Be not afraid of sudden fear." v.9 — 2 Corinthians 9:9 — Paul quotes this verse on generosity. v.10 — Proverbs 10:28 — "The expectation of the wicked shall perish."

Translations

  • v.4 — KJV: "Unto the upright there ariseth light in the darkness: he is gracious, and full of compassion, and righteous" — The "he" is ambiguous: is it God who arises as light, or is it the upright man who is gracious? Likely both — God arises as light, AND the godly man reflects God's character.
  • v.9 — "dispersed" = Hebrew "pizar" = scattered abroad, distributed widely. The imagery is of scattering seed — generous giving produces harvest (2 Cor 9:6-9).

Reflection

  • 1. Delight, not just obedience (v.1). Many obey God's commands grudgingly. The blessed person DELIGHTS GREATLY. The difference between duty and delight is the difference between religion and relationship.
  • 2. Not afraid of evil tidings (v.7). In an age of constant bad news, this is freedom. A heart fixed on God doesn't panic at every headline. Trust creates stability when circumstances create chaos.
  • 3. Generosity establishes righteousness (v.9). The world says: hoard to secure yourself. Scripture says: scatter to the poor and your righteousness endures forever. Generosity is the pathway to lasting impact.
  • 4. You become like what you worship (v.4 cf. 111:4). The God- fearing person takes on God's attributes: grace, compassion, righteousness. Worship transforms the worshiper.
