Joshua — Chapter 13

1Now Joshua was old and advanced in years, and the LORD said to him, “You are old and advanced in years, and there remains yet very much land to possess.

2This is the land that yet remains: all the regions of the Philistines, and all those of the Geshurites

3(from the Shihor, which is east of Egypt, northward to the boundary of Ekron, it is counted as Canaanite; there are five rulers of the Philistines, those of Gaza, Ashdod, Ashkelon, Gath, and Ekron), and those of the Avvim,

4in the south, all the land of the Canaanites, and Mearah that belongs to the Sidonians, to Aphek, to the boundary of the Amorites,

5and the land of the Gebalites, and all Lebanon, toward the sunrise, from Baal-gad below Mount Hermon to Lebo-hamath,

6all the inhabitants of the hill country from Lebanon to Misrephoth-maim, even all the Sidonians. I myself will drive them out from before the people of Israel. Only allot the land to Israel for an inheritance, as I have commanded you.

7Now therefore divide this land for an inheritance to the nine tribes and half the tribe of Manasseh.”

8With the other half of the tribe of Manasseh the Reubenites and the Gadites received their inheritance, which Moses gave them, beyond the Jordan eastward, as Moses the servant of the LORD gave them:

9from Aroer, which is on the edge of the Valley of the Arnon, and the city that is in the middle of the valley, and all the tableland of Medeba as far as Dibon;

10and all the cities of Sihon king of the Amorites, who reigned in Heshbon, as far as the boundary of the Ammonites;

11and Gilead, and the region of the Geshurites and Maacathites, and all Mount Hermon, and all Bashan to Salecah;

12all the kingdom of Og in Bashan, who reigned in Ashtaroth and in Edrei (he alone was left of the remnant of the Rephaim); these Moses had struck and driven out.

13Yet the people of Israel did not drive out the Geshurites or the Maacathites, but Geshur and Maacath dwell in the midst of Israel to this day.

14To the tribe of Levi alone Moses gave no inheritance. The offerings by fire to the LORD God of Israel are their inheritance, as he said to him.

15And Moses gave an inheritance to the tribe of the people of Reuben according to their clans.

16So their territory was from Aroer, which is on the edge of the Valley of the Arnon, and the city that is in the middle of the valley, and all the tableland by Medeba;

17with Heshbon, and all its cities that are in the tableland; Dibon, and Bamoth-baal, and Beth-baal-meon,

18and Jahaz, and Kedemoth, and Mephaath,

19and Kiriathaim, and Sibmah, and Zereth-shahar on the hill of the valley,

20and Beth-peor, and the slopes of Pisgah, and Beth-jeshimoth,

21that is, all the cities of the tableland, and all the kingdom of Sihon king of the Amorites, who reigned in Heshbon, whom Moses defeated with the leaders of Midian, Evi and Rekem and Zur and Hur and Reba, the princes of Sihon, who lived in the land.

22Balaam also, the son of Beor, the one who practiced divination, was killed with the sword by the people of Israel among the rest of their slain.

23And the border of the people of Reuben was the Jordan as a boundary. This was the inheritance of the people of Reuben, according to their clans with their cities and villages.

24Moses gave an inheritance also to the tribe of Gad, to the people of Gad, according to their clans.

25Their territory was Jazer, and all the cities of Gilead, and half the land of the Ammonites, to Aroer, which is east of Rabbah,

26and from Heshbon to Ramath-mizpeh and Betonim, and from Mahanaim to the territory of Debir,

27and in the valley Beth-haram, Beth-nimrah, Succoth, and Zaphon, the rest of the kingdom of Sihon king of Heshbon, having the Jordan as a boundary, to the lower end of the Sea of Chinnereth, eastward beyond the Jordan.

28This is the inheritance of the people of Gad according to their clans, with their cities and villages.

29And Moses gave an inheritance to the half-tribe of Manasseh. It was allotted to the half-tribe of the people of Manasseh according to their clans.

30Their region extended from Mahanaim, through all Bashan, the whole kingdom of Og king of Bashan, and all the towns of Jair, which are in Bashan, sixty cities,

31and half Gilead, and Ashtaroth, and Edrei, the cities of the kingdom of Og in Bashan. These were allotted to the people of Machir the son of Manasseh for the half of the people of Machir according to their clans.

32These are the inheritances that Moses distributed in the plains of Moab, beyond the Jordan east of Jericho.

33But to the tribe of Levi Moses gave no inheritance; the LORD God of Israel is their inheritance, just as he said to them.

Summary

God tells the aging Joshua that much land remains to be conquered and instructs him to divide it by faith. The inheritance of the Transjordan tribes and the exclusion of Levi from land allotment are reviewed.

Key Verses

  • v.1 — "Now Joshua was old and stricken in years; and the LORD said to him, You art old and stricken in years, and there remains yet very much land to be possessed." (ESV) — God acknowledges Joshua's age but does not release him from duty. There is still work to do. The remaining land must be allocated even if not yet conquered.
  • v.6 — "Only divide you it by lot to the Israelites for an inheritance, as I have commanded you." (ESV) — God commands Joshua to distribute land not yet taken. Faith allocates what God has promised before it is fully realized. The lot ensures God's sovereignty in the distribution (Proverbs 16:33).
  • v.13 — "Nevertheless the children of Israel expelled not the Geshurites, nor the Maachathites: but the Geshurites and the Maachathites dwell among the Israelites until this day." (ESV) — The first recorded failure to complete the conquest. Partial obedience is disobedience. These remaining peoples would become thorns (cf. Numbers 33:55).
  • v.14 — "Only to the tribe of Levi he gave none inheritance; the sacrifices of the LORD God of Israel made by fire are their inheritance, as he said to them." (ESV) — Levi's portion is God Himself and the offerings made to Him. They would receive cities to dwell in (chapter 21) but no territorial block.
  • v.22 — "Balaam also the son of Beor, the soothsayer, did the children of Israel slay with the sword among them that were slain by them." (ESV) — A parenthetical note: Balaam, who could not curse Israel but led them into sin at Baal-peor (Numbers 31:16), met his end by Israel's sword. Sin's wages are death.
  • v.33 — "But to the tribe of Levi Moses gave not any inheritance: the LORD God of Israel was their inheritance, as he said to them." (ESV) — The chapter's closing statement echoes v.14. The repetition emphasizes: God Himself is the greatest inheritance. Those who serve Him need no earthly portion — He is enough.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): enduringword.com Guzik emphasizes that God told Joshua to divide land not yet conquered — an act of faith. He notes the tension: the conquest was real but incomplete. God's promise was certain, but Israel's obedience was partial. The Geshurites and Maacathites remaining "until this day" shows the cost of incomplete obedience.
  • Charles Spurgeon: "The tribe of Levi had God for their inheritance. What a portion! Let those who serve God full-time take comfort: if you have God, you have all things. The cattle on a thousand hills are His. He who possesses God possesses everything. Levi had no fields — but Levi had the LORD. Who would not make that exchange?"

Translations

  • v.1 → KJV: "old and stricken in years" / ESV: "old and advanced in years" — Hebrew "zaqen ba bayamim" (lit. "old, come into the days"). KJV's "stricken" is archaic for "advanced." Both convey that Joshua has reached extreme old age. He was likely 90-100 at this point (he dies at 110 in 24:29).
  • v.2 → KJV: "all the borders of the Philistines, and all Geshuri" / ESV: "all the regions of the Philistines, and all those of the Geshurites" — Hebrew "g'lilot" = regions, districts, circuits. ESV's "regions" is more precise than KJV's "borders."
  • v.3 → KJV: "five lords of the Philistines" / ESV: "five rulers of the Philistines" — Hebrew "sarnei P'lishtim." "Seren" (pl. "seranim") is a title unique to Philistine rulers — not the usual Hebrew "melek" (king) or "sar" (prince). It may be related to the Greek "tyrannos." KJV's "lords" and ESV's "rulers" both approximate this distinctive title.
  • v.12 → KJV: "remnant of the giants" / ESV: "remnant of the Rephaim" — Hebrew "Repha'im." KJV translates the term generically as "giants"; ESV transliterates the proper name. The Rephaim were an ancient race of large stature (cf. Deuteronomy 2:11).
  • v.14 → KJV: "the sacrifices of the LORD God of Israel made by fire are their inheritance" / ESV: "The offerings by fire to the LORD God of Israel are their inheritance" — Hebrew "ishei YHWH." "Isheh" = fire offering. Levi's sustenance came from the portions of sacrifices allotted to priests and Levites (Numbers 18:8-20).
  • v.21 → KJV: "dukes of Sihon" / ESV: "princes of Sihon" — Hebrew "n'sikhei Sichon." "Nasikh" = one anointed, a prince or chief. KJV's "dukes" is archaic; ESV's "princes" is more standard. These were vassal rulers under Sihon's authority.
  • v.22 → KJV: "the soothsayer" / ESV: "the one who practiced divination" — Hebrew "ha-qosem." "Qosem" = diviner, one who practices divination. Note: Balaam is called a prophet elsewhere (2 Peter 2:16) but here is labeled by his practice — divination, which is forbidden in Israel (Deuteronomy 18:10).
  • v.27 → KJV: "the sea of Chinnereth" / ESV: "the Sea of Chinnereth" — This is the Old Testament name for the Sea of Galilee (also called Gennesaret in the NT). "Kinnereth" may derive from "kinnor" (harp) — the lake's shape resembles a harp.
  • v.33 → KJV: "the LORD God of Israel was their inheritance" / ESV: "the LORD God of Israel is their inheritance" — KJV uses past tense "was"; ESV uses present "is." The Hebrew has no explicit verb (verbless clause) — both renderings are grammatically possible. ESV's present tense emphasizes the ongoing reality: God IS their inheritance, not merely was.

Reflection

  • 1. "There remaineth yet very much land to be possessed" (v.1). Even after great victories, there is more to take. The Christian life is never "arrived." There are always more promises to claim, more territory to possess, more growth to pursue. Complacency after past victories is a danger.
  • 2. God commands distribution of unconquered land (v.6). Faith acts on God's promises before seeing full fulfillment. Joshua allocated land not yet taken — because God said it was theirs. We too must act on what God has promised, not merely on what we can see.
  • 3. Partial obedience is disobedience (v.13). Israel did not drive out the Geshurites and Maacathites. These compromises seem small but compound over time. The enemies we tolerate today become the snares of tomorrow. What "Canaanites" are we allowing to remain?
  • 4. "The LORD God of Israel was their inheritance" (v.33). Levi had no land — but they had God. This is the highest inheritance. Every believer is a "priest" (1 Peter 2:9) whose true portion is the Lord Himself. Do we value God's presence above earthly possessions?
  • 5. Balaam's end (v.22). He who sought to profit from God's people died by their sword. Those who use spiritual gifts for personal gain will face judgment. The way of Balaam is a warning to every generation (2 Peter 2:15; Jude 11; Revelation 2:14).
