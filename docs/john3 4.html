John — Chapter 3

1Now there was a man of the Pharisees named Nicodemus, a ruler of the Jews.

2This man came to Jesus by night and said to him, “Rabbi, we know that you are a teacher come from God, for no one can do these signs that you do unless God is with him.”

3Jesus answered him, “Truly, truly, I say to you, unless one is born again he cannot see the kingdom of God.”

4Nicodemus said to him, “How can a man be born when he is old? Can he enter a second time into his mother’s womb and be born?”

5Jesus answered, “Truly, truly, I say to you, unless one is born of water and the Spirit, he cannot enter the kingdom of God.

6That which is born of the flesh is flesh, and that which is born of the Spirit is spirit.

7Do not marvel that I said to you, ‘You must be born again.’

8The wind blows where it wishes, and you hear its sound, but you do not know where it comes from or where it goes. So it is with everyone who is born of the Spirit.”

9Nicodemus said to him, “How can these things be?”

10Jesus answered him, “Are you the teacher of Israel and yet you do not understand these things?

11Truly, truly, I say to you, we speak of what we know, and bear witness to what we have seen, but you do not receive our testimony.

12If I have told you earthly things and you do not believe, how can you believe if I tell you heavenly things?

13No one has ascended into heaven except he who descended from heaven, the Son of Man.

14And as Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, so must the Son of Man be lifted up,

15that whoever believes in him may have eternal life.

16“For God so loved the world, that he gave his only Son, that whoever believes in him should not perish but have eternal life.

17For God did not send his Son into the world to condemn the world, but in order that the world might be saved through him.

18Whoever believes in him is not condemned, but whoever does not believe is condemned already, because he has not believed in the name of the only Son of God.

19And this is the judgment: the light has come into the world, and people loved the darkness rather than the light because their works were evil.

20For everyone who does wicked things hates the light and does not come to the light, lest his works should be exposed.

21But whoever does what is true comes to the light, so that it may be clearly seen that his works have been carried out in God.”

22After this Jesus and his disciples went into the Judean countryside, and he remained there with them and was baptizing.

23John also was baptizing at Aenon near Salim, because water was plentiful there, and people were coming and being baptized

24(for John had not yet been put in prison).

25Now a discussion arose between some of John’s disciples and a Jew over purification.

26And they came to John and said to him, “Rabbi, he who was with you across the Jordan, to whom you bore witness—look, he is baptizing, and all are going to him.”

27John answered, “A person cannot receive even one thing unless it is given him from heaven.

28You yourselves bear me witness, that I said, ‘I am not the Christ, but I have been sent before him.’

29The one who has the bride is the bridegroom. The friend of the bridegroom, who stands and hears him, rejoices greatly at the bridegroom’s voice. Therefore this joy of mine is now complete.

30He must increase, but I must decrease.”

31He who comes from above is above all. He who is of the earth belongs to the earth and speaks in an earthly way. He who comes from heaven is above all.

32He bears witness to what he has seen and heard, yet no one receives his testimony.

33Whoever receives his testimony sets his seal to this, that God is true.

34For he whom God has sent utters the words of God, for he gives the Spirit without measure.

35The Father loves the Son and has given all things into his hand.

36Whoever believes in the Son has eternal life; whoever does not obey the Son shall not see life, but the wrath of God remains on him.

Summary

Jesus tells Nicodemus that no one can see the kingdom of God unless born again of water and the Spirit, declares "God so loved the world that He gave His only begotten Son," and John the Baptist testifies "He must increase, but I must decrease."

Key Verses

v.3
"Truly, truly, I say to you, unless one is born again he cannot see the kingdom of God." (ESV) — The necessity of spiritual rebirth — Jesus' non-negotiable requirement.
v.16
"For God so loved the world, that he gave his only Son, that whoever believes in him should not perish but have eternal life." (ESV) — The Gospel in one verse.
v.17
"For God did not send his Son into the world to condemn the world, but in order that the world might be saved through him." (ESV) — God's motive: salvation, not condemnation.
v.30
"He must increase, but I must decrease." (ESV) — John the Baptist's defining statement of God-centered humility.
v.36
"Whoever believes in the Son has eternal life; whoever does not obey the Son shall not see life, but the wrath of God remains on him." (ESV) — The two eternal destinies plainly stated.

Cross-References

  • v.3 — Ezekiel 36:26-27 — God promises a new heart and new spirit — the OT foundation for regeneration. v.5 — Titus 3:5 — "Renewing of the Holy Ghost" — regeneration by the Spirit. v.8 — 1 Corinthians 2:11 — The Spirit's work is beyond human comprehension. v.14 — Numbers 21:8-9 — Moses lifting up the bronze serpent in the wilderness, the type that Jesus fulfills. v.16 — Romans 5:8 — "God demonstrates His love for us in this: while we were still sinners, Christ died for us." v.16 — 1 John 4:9-10 — God sent His Son as an atoning sacrifice. v.17 — Luke 19:10 — "The Son of Man came to seek and save the lost." v.19 — John 1:5 — Light came into the world and darkness did not overcome it. v.30 — Philippians 1:21 — "For me to live is Christ." v.36 — John 5:24 — Whoever hears and believes has eternal life.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/john-3/ Guzik provides excellent analysis of "born again" (Greek: anothen — meaning both "again" and "from above"), the bronze serpent typology in v.14, and John the Baptist's response to his disciples' jealousy in vv.26-30.
  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "The Necessity of Regeneration" on v.7 (MTP Vol. 1, No. 53) "You must be born again — not reformed, not educated, not improved — born again. The old nature cannot be patched up. God does not renovate; He regenerates. He does not repair the old man; He creates a new one." Sermon: "God So Loved the World" on v.16 (MTP Vol. 26, No. 1531) "These words are not the words of a man trying to persuade you — they are the words of God Himself declaring the motive behind the cross. So loved. Not so pitied. Not so tolerated. So loved."

Translations

  • v.3 — KJV: "born again" / ESV: "born again" — Greek "anothen" — Both identical in rendering, though "anothen" carries both meanings simultaneously — born again (a second time) AND born from above (from God). Nicodemus understood it as "again" (v.4); Jesus meant "from above." The double meaning is intentional. [ASV/NET/WEB]: NET: "unless a person is born from above, he cannot see the kingdom of God" — "from above" (anōthen) — a word with double meaning: "again" (KJV/WEB) AND "from above." NET chooses "from above"; Nicodemus hears "again" (v.4). The ambiguity is intentional in the Greek.
  • v.5 — KJV: "born of water and of the Spirit" / ESV: "born of water and the Spirit" — Both equivalent. "Water" is debated — interpretations include: (1) physical birth (amniotic fluid), (2) baptism, (3) the Word of God (Ephesians 5:26), (4) John's baptism of repentance. Most commentators favor either physical birth or the cleansing work of the Spirit (Ezekiel 36:25-27).
  • v.8 — KJV: "The wind bloweth where it listeth" / ESV: "The wind blows where it wishes" — Greek "pneuma" — Both equivalent. The same Greek word means both "wind" and "spirit" — Jesus uses the wordplay intentionally. [ASV/NET/WEB]: NET: "The wind blows wherever it will, and you hear the sound it makes, but do not know where it comes from and where it is going" — "wind" and "Spirit" are the same Greek word (pneuma). WEB: "The wind blows where it wants to." The wordplay is untranslatable — the Spirit/wind is sovereign and mysterious.
  • v.14 — KJV: "even so must the Son of man be lifted up" / ESV: "so must the Son of Man be lifted up" — Greek "hupsothenai" — Both equivalent. "Lifted up" carries double meaning: physically on the cross and exalted in glory.
  • v.16 — KJV: "only begotten Son" / ESV: "only Son" — Greek "monogenes" — KJV's "only begotten" emphasizes eternal generation; ESV's "only" emphasizes uniqueness. "Monogenes" means one-of-a-kind — Jesus is the unique Son of God, unlike any other. [ASV/NET/WEB]: NET: "For this is the way God loved the world: He gave his one and only Son" — "one and only" (monogenē) vs. KJV "only begotten." WEB: "his one and only Son." ASV: "his only begotten Son." NET avoids "begotten" since monogenēs means "unique/one of a kind" rather than "generated/begotten."
  • v.18 — KJV: "he that believeth not is condemned already" / ESV: "whoever does not believe is condemned already" — Both equivalent. Greek "kekritai" (perfect tense) — condemnation is a present state, not merely a future event.
  • v.30 — NET: "He must become more important while I become less important" — "more important/less important" vs. KJV's elegant "He must increase, but I must decrease." WEB: "He must increase, but I must decrease." ASV: "He must increase, but I must decrease." KJV's phrasing remains superior here.
  • v.36 — KJV: "he that believeth not the Son" / ESV: "whoever does not obey the Son" — Greek "apeithon" — Means both "disbelieve" and "disobey." KJV chose the belief aspect; ESV chose the obedience aspect. True belief and obedience are inseparable in John's theology. [ASV/NET/WEB]: NET: "the one who does not obey the Son will not see life, but God's wrath remains on him" — "does not obey" (ho apeithōn) vs. KJV "believeth not." WEB: "doesn't obey the Son." The Greek word means both "disbelieve" and "disobey" — unbelief IS disobedience.

Reflection

  • 1. Nicodemus was a religious expert who didn't understand the most basic truth about entering God's kingdom. What does this say about the danger of substituting religious knowledge for genuine relationship with God?
  • 2. Jesus compared the new birth to the wind — you can't see it, but you see its effects (v.8). What evidence of the Spirit's work do you see in your own life? What evidence would others see?
  • 3. The bronze serpent in Numbers 21 — those who were bitten by snakes were healed by looking at the bronze serpent Moses lifted up. Jesus says He must be "lifted up" the same way (v.14). What does it mean to "look to" Christ for salvation? Is that a one-time act or ongoing?
  • 4. Verse 19 says men loved darkness rather than light because their deeds were evil. Honesty check: Is there an area of your life you're keeping in the dark because you don't want it exposed? What would it take to bring it to the light?
  • 5. John the Baptist's disciples were upset that people were leaving them to follow Jesus (v.26). John's response was joy, not jealousy (v.29). How do you respond when someone else gets the credit or the following you feel you deserve?
  • 1. Nicodemus came to Jesus at night — perhaps out of fear of what others would think. Many men live their faith privately, afraid of peer judgment. Is there an area of your faith you're keeping in the dark? What would it look like to bring it into the light?
  • 2. Jesus told a highly educated, religiously accomplished man that he needed to start over — be born again. Religious activity, moral effort, and good reputation cannot substitute for genuine spiritual transformation. Have you experienced the new birth, or are you relying on your own goodness?
  • 3. John the Baptist said "He must increase, but I must decrease" (v.30). This is the posture of a man who knows his purpose. As a man — in your career, your family, your ministry — are you building your own name or pointing to Christ? What would it look like to decrease in a specific area this week?
  • 4. John 3:16 is the most memorized verse in the Bible, but familiarity can dull its impact. Read it slowly: God SO loved — the world — that He GAVE. What does it mean to you personally that God gave His Son for you specifically?
