Psalms — Chapter 145

1I will extol you, my God and King, and bless your name forever and ever.

2Every day I will bless you and praise your name forever and ever.

3Great is the LORD, and greatly to be praised, and his greatness is unsearchable.

4One generation shall commend your works to another, and shall declare your mighty acts.

5On the glorious splendor of your majesty, and on your wondrous works, I will meditate.

6They shall speak of the might of your awesome deeds, and I will declare your greatness.

7They shall pour forth the fame of your abundant goodness and shall sing aloud of your righteousness.

8The LORD is gracious and merciful, slow to anger and abounding in steadfast love.

9The LORD is good to all, and his mercy is over all that he has made.

10All your works shall give thanks to you, O LORD, and all your saints shall bless you!

11They shall speak of the glory of your kingdom and tell of your power,

12to make known to the children of man your mighty deeds, and the glorious splendor of your kingdom.

13Your kingdom is an everlasting kingdom, and your dominion endures throughout all generations. [The LORD is faithful in all his words and kind in all his works.]

14The LORD upholds all who are falling and raises up all who are bowed down.

15The eyes of all look to you, and you give them their food in due season.

16You open your hand; you satisfy the desire of every living thing.

17The LORD is righteous in all his ways and kind in all his works.

18The LORD is near to all who call on him, to all who call on him in truth.

19He fulfills the desire of those who fear him; he also hears their cry and saves them.

20The LORD preserves all who love him, but all the wicked he will destroy.

21My mouth will speak the praise of the LORD, and let all flesh bless his holy name forever and ever.

Summary

An acrostic praise psalm of David — 'I will extol thee, my God, O King'; God is great and greatly to be praised, gracious and full of compassion, faithful in all His words; His kingdom is everlasting; He is near to all who call in truth.

Key Verses

v.3
"Great is the LORD, and greatly to be praised; and his greatness is unsearchable." (KJV) — How great is God? UNSEARCHABLE — beyond investigation, beyond measurement, beyond comprehension. You will never reach the end of God's greatness.
v.8-9
"The LORD is gracious, and full of compassion; slow to anger, and of great mercy. The LORD is good to all: and his tender mercies are over all his works." (KJV) — Again the Exodus 34:6 declaration. And extended: "good to ALL" and "tender mercies over ALL his works." Universal compassion — not just to Israel but to all creation.
v.18
"The LORD is nigh unto all them that call upon him, to all that call upon him in truth." (KJV) — God is NEAR to those who call. Not distant, not hard to reach — NIGH. One condition: call in truth (sincerity, genuine need).
v.15-16
"The eyes of all wait upon thee; and thou givest them their meat in due season. Thou openest thine hand, and satisfiest the desire of every living thing." (KJV) — God is the cosmic provider. He opens His hand and satisfies EVERY living thing. Every creature's need is met by God's open hand.

Cross-References

  • v.3 — Romans 11:33 — "O the depth of the riches both of the wisdom and knowledge of God! how unsearchable..." v.8 — Exodus 34:6 — God's self-proclamation: gracious, compassionate, slow to anger. v.9 — Matthew 5:45 — "He maketh his sun to rise on the evil and on the good." v.13 — Daniel 4:3 — "His kingdom is an everlasting kingdom." v.15-16 — Psalm 104:27-28 — "Thou openest thine hand, they are filled with good." v.18 — Deuteronomy 4:7 — "What nation... hath God so nigh unto them?" v.21 — Psalm 150:6 — "Let every thing that hath breath praise the LORD."

Translations

  • v.3 — "unsearchable" (Hebrew "en cheqer") = there is no searching/investigation that can exhaust it. God's greatness has no bottom, no end, no boundary.
  • v.13 — The Hebrew letter Nun is missing from the acrostic in the Masoretic text. The Dead Sea Scrolls include an additional verse: "The LORD is faithful in all his words and gracious/holy in all his deeds." ESV includes it; KJV does not.
  • v.16 — "satisfiest the desire of every living thing" — Hebrew "ratzon" = desire, will, pleasure. God satisfies what creation wants. Not just needs but desires. Abundant provision.

Reflection

  • 1. God's greatness is unsearchable (v.3). Don't reduce God to what you can comprehend. He is always bigger than your understanding. Every discovery of His nature reveals more beyond.
  • 2. God is good to ALL (v.9). Not just to the faithful. Not just to Israel. To ALL. His tender mercies cover all His works. This is universal grace — available before it's asked for.
  • 3. God opens His hand (v.16). He is not stingy, not reluctant, not withholding. His hand is OPEN — pouring out satisfaction. If you're in need, look to the open hand.
  • 4. He is near when you call (v.18). You don't need a priest, a system, or special access. Call on Him in truth and He is NEAR. Immediacy of access is the privilege of prayer.
  • 5. This is David's last psalm — his final word is PRAISE. After all the laments, fears, doubts, and battles, David ends with wholehearted praise. May your last word also be praise.
