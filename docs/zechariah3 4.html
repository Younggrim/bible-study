Zechariah — Chapter 3

1Then he showed me Joshua the high priest standing before the angel of the LORD, and Satan standing at his right hand to accuse him.

2And the LORD said to Satan, “The LORD rebuke you, O Satan! The LORD who has chosen Jerusalem rebuke you! Is not this a brand plucked from the fire?”

3Now Joshua was standing before the angel, clothed with filthy garments.

4And the angel said to those who were standing before him, “Remove the filthy garments from him.” And to him he said, “Behold, I have taken your iniquity away from you, and I will clothe you with pure vestments.”

5And I said, “Let them put a clean turban on his head.” So they put a clean turban on his head and clothed him with garments. And the angel of the LORD was standing by.

6And the angel of the LORD solemnly assured Joshua,

7“Thus says the LORD of hosts: If you will walk in my ways and keep my charge, then you shall rule my house and have charge of my courts, and I will give you the right of access among those who are standing here.

8Hear now, O Joshua the high priest, you and your friends who sit before you, for they are men who are a sign: behold, I will bring my servant the Branch.

9For behold, on the stone that I have set before Joshua, on a single stone with seven eyes, I will engrave its inscription, declares the LORD of hosts, and I will remove the iniquity of this land in a single day.

10In that day, declares the LORD of hosts, every one of you will invite his neighbor to come under his vine and under his fig tree.”

Summary

Joshua the high priest stands accused by Satan but is cleansed by the Angel of the LORD, given pure garments, and told of the coming "Branch" — God will remove the iniquity of the land in a single day.

Key Verses

v.1
"Joshua the high priest standing before the angel of the LORD, and Satan standing at his right hand to resist him" (KJV) — A courtroom scene. Joshua represents sinful Israel. Satan is the prosecutor. The Angel of the LORD is the judge. And the verdict will shock everyone: the guilty is acquitted — not because he is innocent, but because God intervenes.
v.2
"The LORD rebuke thee, O Satan... is not this a brand plucked out of the fire?" (KJV) — God silences the accuser. His argument is not that Joshua is clean (he isn't) but that He has CHOSEN to rescue him. A burning stick snatched from the fire — that is grace. Rescued not because of merit but because of mercy.
v.4
"I have caused thine iniquity to pass from thee, and I will clothe thee with change of raiment" (KJV) / "I have taken your iniquity away from you, and I will clothe you with pure vestments" (ESV) — The double action of justification: iniquity REMOVED and righteousness BESTOWED. Not just forgiveness but a new wardrobe. This is the gospel: your filth taken away, Christ's righteousness put upon you.
v.8
"I will bring forth my servant the BRANCH" (KJV) — One of the great Messianic titles. The Branch (Hebrew "tsemach") appears in Isaiah 4:2, 11:1; Jeremiah 23:5, 33:15; and Zechariah 3:8, 6:12. He is the shoot from David's stump — the Messiah who will grow up from lowly origins to bear royal glory.
v.9
"I will remove the iniquity of that land in one day" (KJV) — This is the cross of Christ in one sentence. One day. One sacrifice. Complete removal of sin. Not gradual improvement but decisive, once-for-all atonement. "It is finished" (John 19:30).
v.10
"In that day... shall ye call every man his neighbour under the vine and under the fig tree" (KJV) — The picture of Millennial peace. Security, prosperity, fellowship. When sin is removed, shalom follows.

Cross-References

  • v.1 — Job 1:6-12 — Satan appearing before God to accuse — the same pattern as in Job. v.1 — Revelation 12:10 — Satan as "the accuser of our brethren" cast down. v.2 — Jude 9 — "The Lord rebuke thee" — Michael uses the same words contending with the devil. v.4 — Isaiah 61:10 — "He hath clothed me with the garments of salvation... the robe of righteousness." v.4 — 2 Corinthians 5:21 — "He made him to be sin for us, who knew no sin; that we might be made the righteousness of God in him." v.8 — Isaiah 11:1 — "A Branch shall grow out of his roots" — the Messiah from David's line. v.8 — Jeremiah 23:5 — "I will raise unto David a righteous Branch." v.9 — Hebrews 10:12 — "This man, after he had offered ONE sacrifice for sins for ever, sat down."

Translations

  • v.3 — "filthy garments" — Hebrew "tso'im" = excrement-stained. This is not mere dirtiness — it is the most revolting defilement possible. Isaiah uses the same root in 64:6: "all our righteousnesses are as filthy rags." Our best efforts are sewage before God's holiness.
  • v.4 — "change of raiment" (KJV) / "pure vestments" (ESV) — Hebrew "machalatsot" = festival/ceremonial garments. These are not work clothes but CELEBRATION garments — the finest attire for the most honored occasion.
  • v.5 — "fair mitre" (KJV) / "clean turban" (ESV) — Hebrew "tsanif tahor" = a pure/clean turban. The high priest's turban bore the inscription "HOLINESS TO THE LORD" (Exodus 28:36). Joshua receives back his priestly authority — now purified.
  • v.8 — "the BRANCH" — Hebrew "tsemach" = a sprout, a growing shoot. It emphasizes organic growth from seemingly dead stock. The Messiah grows up from David's cut-down tree — humble beginnings, glorious destiny.
  • v.9 — "seven eyes" — Representing omniscience and perfect watchfulness. God sees completely. The stone (likely the cornerstone of the Temple, or Messiah Himself) is under God's perfect, all-seeing care.

Reflection

  • 1. Satan accuses, but God defends (vv.1-2). The enemy's strategy is accusation — reminding you of your guilt, your failures, your unworthiness. But God says: "I rebuke you." Your defender is stronger than your accuser. When condemnation rises, appeal to the Judge who already ruled in your favor.
  • 2. Your filth is not the final word (vv.3-4). Joshua stood in excrement-stained garments — and was NOT rejected. God removed the filth and clothed him in purity. No matter how stained you feel, God's grace is greater than your sin. He does not just forgive — He RE-CLOTHES.
  • 3. Justification is God's work, not yours (v.4). Joshua did not clean himself. He did not earn the new garments. He simply STOOD THERE while God did the work. Salvation is received, not achieved. Stop trying to wash your own filthy garments. Let God dress you in His righteousness.
  • 4. "In one day" (v.9). The complete removal of sin happened in ONE day — the day Christ died. Not a process but an event. Not gradual improvement but decisive atonement. If you are in Christ, your iniquity was removed — completely, finally, irrevocably — in one day on Calvary.
  • 5. The Branch grows from lowly places (v.8). The Messiah comes not as a mighty oak but as a small shoot. God's greatest work often begins in the smallest, humblest circumstances. Don't despise your small beginnings — the Branch is growing.
