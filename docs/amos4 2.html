Amos — Chapter 4

1“Hear this word, you cows of Bashan, who are on the mountain of Samaria, who oppress the poor, who crush the needy, who say to your husbands, ‘Bring, that we may drink!’

2The Lord GOD has sworn by his holiness that, behold, the days are coming upon you, when they shall take you away with hooks, even the last of you with fishhooks.

3And you shall go out through the breaches, each one straight ahead; and you shall be cast out into Harmon,” declares the LORD.

4“Come to Bethel, and transgress; to Gilgal, and multiply transgression; bring your sacrifices every morning, your tithes every three days;

5offer a sacrifice of thanksgiving of that which is leavened, and proclaim freewill offerings, publish them; for so you love to do, O people of Israel!” declares the Lord GOD.

6“I gave you cleanness of teeth in all your cities, and lack of bread in all your places, yet you did not return to me,” declares the LORD.

7“I also withheld the rain from you when there were yet three months to the harvest; I would send rain on one city, and send no rain on another city; one field would have rain, and the field on which it did not rain would wither;

8so two or three cities would wander to another city to drink water, and would not be satisfied; yet you did not return to me,” declares the LORD.

9“I struck you with blight and mildew; your many gardens and your vineyards, your fig trees and your olive trees the locust devoured; yet you did not return to me,” declares the LORD.

10“I sent among you a pestilence after the manner of Egypt; I killed your young men with the sword, and carried away your horses, and I made the stench of your camp go up into your nostrils; yet you did not return to me,” declares the LORD.

11“I overthrew some of you, as when God overthrew Sodom and Gomorrah, and you were as a brand plucked out of the burning; yet you did not return to me,” declares the LORD.

12“Therefore thus I will do to you, O Israel; because I will do this to you, prepare to meet your God, O Israel!”

13For behold, he who forms the mountains and creates the wind, and declares to man what is his thought, who makes the morning darkness, and treads on the heights of the earth— the LORD, the God of hosts, is his name!

Summary

God condemns the wealthy women of Samaria ("cows of Bashan") who oppress the poor, and recounts five warning judgments He sent — famine, drought, blight, plague, overthrow — yet "you did not return to Me."

Key Verses

v.1
"Hear this word, ye kine of Bashan, that are in the mountain of Samaria, which oppress the poor, which crush the needy, which say to their masters, Bring, and let us drink" (KJV) — "Cows of Bashan" = the wealthy women of Samaria who demand luxury from their husbands while the poor are crushed to fund their lifestyle. Consumer culture built on exploitation.
v.6
"I also have given you cleanness of teeth in all your cities, and want of bread in all your places: yet have ye not returned unto me" (KJV) — "Cleanness of teeth" = nothing to eat (teeth are clean because there's no food). God's discipline was meant to produce RETURN. It didn't. The refrain haunts: "yet have ye not returned unto me."
v.11
"I have overthrown some of you, as God overthrew Sodom and Gomorrah, and ye were as a firebrand plucked out of the burning: yet have ye not returned unto me" (KJV) — Even SODOM-level judgment didn't produce repentance. They were plucked from the fire like a burning stick — barely saved — and STILL didn't return. Hardness of heart survives even catastrophe.
v.12
"prepare to meet thy God, O Israel" (KJV) — The most solemn summons in Scripture. Since nothing else worked — no discipline, no warning, no catastrophe produced repentance — now prepare for DIRECT encounter with God Himself. This is both terrifying AND gracious: even now, "meeting God" is offered.
v.13
"he that formeth the mountains, and createth the wind, and declareth unto man what is his thought" (KJV) — The God you must prepare to meet is the Creator of mountains and wind, the One who knows your THOUGHTS. You cannot hide from this God or bluff this meeting.

Cross-References

  • v.1 — Psalm 22:12 — "Strong bulls of Bashan have beset me round." v.6 — Haggai 1:6 — "Ye have sown much, and bring in little." Same pattern: God removes blessing to provoke return. v.11 — Genesis 19:24-25 — The overthrow of Sodom and Gomorrah. v.11 — Zechariah 3:2 — "Is not this a brand plucked out of the fire?" Same image of narrow escape. v.12 — Hebrews 10:31 — "It is a fearful thing to fall into the hands of the living God." v.13 — Hebrews 4:13 — "All things are naked and opened unto the eyes of him with whom we have to do."

Translations

  • v.1 — "kine of Bashan" = "cows of Bashan." Bashan (modern Golan Heights) was famous for lush pastures and fat cattle (Deuteronomy 32:14, Psalm 22:12). The metaphor: pampered, overfed, demanding luxury.
  • v.4 — "Come to Bethel, and transgress" — SARCASM. God mocks their religious enthusiasm. "You love your rituals? Fine — go ahead! Sin MORE at Bethel! Your 'worship' is transgression."
  • v.6-11 — Five escalating disciplines: (1) famine (v.6), (2) drought (vv.7-8), (3) blight and locusts (v.9), (4) pestilence and war (v.10), (5) destruction like Sodom (v.11). Each followed by "yet have ye not returned."
  • v.13 — "declareth unto man what is his thought" — God knows your THOUGHTS. Hebrew "sechoh" = meditation, inner thought. Nothing is hidden.

Reflection

  • 1. "Yet have ye not returned unto me" — FIVE TIMES (vv.6-11). God sent increasing difficulty to bring you back. How many warnings have YOU received? Financial trouble, health scares, broken relationships — could these be God's "yet have ye not returned" in YOUR life? Stop ignoring the pattern.
  • 2. "Prepare to meet thy God" (v.12). You WILL meet God. The only question is whether you meet Him as prepared or unprepared. Every day is preparation day. Are you ready?
  • 3. Consumer luxury built on someone else's suffering (v.1). Whose pain funds your comfort? Exploitation doesn't always look dramatic — it looks like demanding more while caring less about where it comes from.
  • 4. Religious enthusiasm can be pure transgression (vv.4-5). Going to church, giving offerings, being visibly spiritual — if your heart hasn't changed and the poor are still crushed, God sarcastically says: "Enjoy your sin! This is what you LOVE." Don't confuse religious activity with actual obedience.
