Genesis — Chapter 8

1But God remembered Noah and all the beasts and all the livestock that were with him in the ark. And God made a wind blow over the earth, and the waters subsided.

2The fountains of the deep and the windows of the heavens were closed, the rain from the heavens was restrained,

3and the waters receded from the earth continually. At the end of 150 days the waters had abated,

4and in the seventh month, on the seventeenth day of the month, the ark came to rest on the mountains of Ararat.

5And the waters continued to abate until the tenth month; in the tenth month, on the first day of the month, the tops of the mountains were seen.

6At the end of forty days Noah opened the window of the ark that he had made

7and sent forth a raven. It went to and fro until the waters were dried up from the earth.

8Then he sent forth a dove from him, to see if the waters had subsided from the face of the ground.

9But the dove found no place to set her foot, and she returned to him to the ark, for the waters were still on the face of the whole earth. So he put out his hand and took her and brought her into the ark with him.

10He waited another seven days, and again he sent forth the dove out of the ark.

11And the dove came back to him in the evening, and behold, in her mouth was a freshly plucked olive leaf. So Noah knew that the waters had subsided from the earth.

12Then he waited another seven days and sent forth the dove, and she did not return to him anymore.

13In the six hundred and first year, in the first month, the first day of the month, the waters were dried from off the earth. And Noah removed the covering of the ark and looked, and behold, the face of the ground was dry.

14In the second month, on the twenty-seventh day of the month, the earth had dried out.

15Then God said to Noah,

16“Go out from the ark, you and your wife, and your sons and your sons’ wives with you.

17Bring out with you every living thing that is with you of all flesh—birds and animals and every creeping thing that creeps on the earth—that they may swarm on the earth, and be fruitful and multiply on the earth.”

18So Noah went out, and his sons and his wife and his sons’ wives with him.

19Every beast, every creeping thing, and every bird, everything that moves on the earth, went out by families from the ark.

20Then Noah built an altar to the LORD and took some of every clean animal and some of every clean bird and offered burnt offerings on the altar.

21And when the LORD smelled the pleasing aroma, the LORD said in his heart, “I will never again curse the ground because of man, for the intention of man’s heart is evil from his youth. Neither will I ever again strike down every living creature as I have done.

22While the earth remains, seedtime and harvest, cold and heat, summer and winter, day and night, shall not cease.”

Summary

The floodwaters recede, Noah sends out birds to test the land, and finally exits the ark to offer sacrifice to God.

Key Verses

v.1
"But God remembered Noah." (ESV) — God acts on behalf of His own.
v.11
"In her mouth was a freshly plucked olive leaf. So Noah knew that the waters had subsided from the earth." (ESV) — The sign of hope and new life.
v.20
"Then Noah built an altar to the LORD and... offered burnt offerings." (ESV) — Worship as the first response to deliverance.
v.21
"I will never again curse the ground because of man." (ESV) — God's gracious promise despite human sinfulness.
v.22
"While the earth remains, seedtime and harvest, cold and heat, summer and winter, day and night, shall not cease." (ESV) — The stability of creation guaranteed.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/genesis-8/ Guzik covers God's remembrance, the re-creation parallels, the birds, Noah's sacrifice, and God's promise.
  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "God Remembered Noah" on v.1 (MTP Vol. 22, No. 1311) "God REMEMBERED Noah — what comfort in these words! In the midst of judgment, God remembers His own. The waters raged, the world perished, but God remembered the man in the ark. He remembers you too — in your trial, in your waiting, in your darkness. He has not forgotten. He remembers — and when He remembers, He acts."

Translations

  • v.1 — KJV: "God remembered Noah" / ESV: "God remembered Noah" — Both identical. Hebrew "zakar" — to remember, to act on behalf of. Not recollection (God doesn't forget) but action. When God "remembers," He intervenes.
  • v.1 — KJV: "God made a wind to pass over the earth, and the waters asswaged" / ESV: "God made a wind blow over the earth, and the waters subsided" — Hebrew "ruach" — wind/spirit/breath. Same word as Genesis 1:2 ("Spirit of God"). The re-creation parallel is intentional. KJV's archaic "asswaged" = ESV's "subsided" — both mean the waters receded.
  • v.4 — KJV: "the ark rested... upon the mountains of Ararat" / ESV: "the ark came to rest on the mountains of Ararat" — Both equivalent. Hebrew "nuach" — same root as "Noah." Noah rests on the mountain. Ararat is a mountain range in modern eastern Turkey (not a single peak).
  • v.9 — KJV: "the dove found no rest for the sole of her foot" / ESV: "the dove found no place to set her foot" — Hebrew "manoach" — resting place. KJV is more literal ("rest for the sole of her foot"); ESV paraphrases for clarity ("no place to set her foot"). Same meaning.
  • v.11 — KJV: "an olive leaf pluckt off" / ESV: "a freshly plucked olive leaf" — Both equivalent. ESV adds "freshly" to clarify the leaf was newly plucked (indicating active growth). The olive tree can survive flooding and sprout quickly.
  • v.17 — KJV: "that they may breed abundantly in the earth, and be fruitful, and multiply" / ESV: "that they may swarm on the earth, and be fruitful and multiply" — Hebrew "sharats" — to swarm, teem. ESV's "swarm" is more literal; KJV's "breed abundantly" interprets the result.
  • v.20 — KJV: "burnt offerings" / ESV: "burnt offerings" — Both identical. Hebrew "oloth" — whole burnt offerings. Completely consumed by fire — total dedication to God. The first altar and first formal sacrifice recorded.
  • v.21 — KJV: "a sweet savour" / ESV: "the pleasing aroma" — Hebrew "reach nichoach" — a soothing/restful aroma. ESV's "pleasing aroma" is more natural modern English; KJV's "sweet savour" is archaic but evocative. God is "satisfied" by the sacrifice — a picture of Christ's sacrifice satisfying God (Eph 5:2).
  • v.21 — KJV: "I will not again curse the ground any more for man's sake" / ESV: "I will never again curse the ground because of man" — Both equivalent. KJV's "for man's sake" could be misread as "for man's benefit"; ESV's "because of man" is clearer.
  • v.21 — KJV: "the imagination of man's heart is evil from his youth" / ESV: "the intention of man's heart is evil from his youth" — Hebrew "yetser" — imagination, inclination, intention. Same word as 6:5. The same diagnosis as before, but now the basis for mercy rather than judgment. God knows our nature and chooses grace.
  • v.22 — KJV: "While the earth remaineth, seedtime and harvest, and cold and heat, and summer and winter, and day and night shall not cease" / ESV: "While the earth remains, seedtime and harvest, cold and heat, summer and winter, day and night, shall not cease" — Both essentially identical. God's guarantee of creation's stability.
  • TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB) - v.4 — NET: "the ark came to rest on one of the mountains of Ararat" — adds "one of" to clarify the Hebrew plural "mountains of Ararat" refers to a range, not a single peak. Other translations leave this ambiguous. - v.21 — NET: "the inclination of their minds is evil from childhood on" — renders Hebrew "yetser" as "inclination of their minds" (vs. "imagination of man's heart" KJV) and "from his youth" as "from childhood on," making the doctrine of innate depravity more psychologically explicit. - v.21 — NET: "said to himself" — renders Hebrew "said in his heart" as "said to himself," a more natural English idiom for internal resolve. ASV/WEB retain the more literal "said in his heart." - v.22 — NET: "While the earth continues to exist" — a more dynamic rendering than "While the earth remaineth" (KJV/ASV), subtly acknowledging the earth has a future end point.

Reflection

  • 1. "God remembered Noah" (v.1). In your waiting — when it feels like God has forgotten — He hasn't. He remembers. And when He remembers, He acts. Can you trust His timing?
  • 2. Noah's first act after deliverance was worship (v.20). What is YOUR first response to God's deliverance — gratitude and worship, or moving on to the next thing?
  • 3. "The imagination of man's heart is evil from his youth" (v.21). God knows our sinful nature — and STILL chooses grace. His mercy is not based on our goodness but on His character. Does this humble you?
  • 4. "While the earth remaineth, seedtime and harvest... shall not cease" (v.22). God guarantees creation's stability. Every sunrise, every season, every harvest is God keeping His promise. Do you notice His faithfulness in the ordinary rhythms of life?
  • 5. The flood is both judgment and salvation — destruction of the wicked and preservation of the righteous. God's actions always have both dimensions. Are you on the salvation side?
