Summary

Women come to the tomb at dawn and find it empty — a young man in white announces "He is risen, He is not here," and the risen Jesus commissions His disciples to preach the gospel to all creation.

Key Verses

  • v.6 — "He has risen; he is not here. See the place where they laid him." (ESV) — The resurrection announcement — the foundation of Christianity.
  • v.7 — "Go, tell his disciples and Peter." (ESV) — Peter specifically named — grace that pursues the one who failed most publicly.
  • v.15 — "Go into all the world and proclaim the gospel to the whole creation." (ESV) — The Great Commission in Mark.
  • v.16 — "Whoever believes and is baptized will be saved, but whoever does not believe will be condemned." (ESV) — The two responses and their eternal consequences.
  • v.20 — "And they went out and preached everywhere, while the Lord worked with them and confirmed the message by accompanying signs." (ESV) — The church's mission: preach, and the Lord works.

Authorship & Background

  • Author: John Mark (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).
  • Historical Context: Chapter 16 records the resurrection of Jesus — the event that validates everything He claimed and everything He accomplished. Mark's resurrection account is the shortest of the four Gospels.
  • The Textual Question (vv.9-20): The most significant textual issue in the New Testament. The two oldest Greek manuscripts (Sinaiticus and Vaticanus) end at verse 8. Most scholars believe Mark's original ending was either lost or that he intentionally ended at v.8 — with the women fleeing in fear and amazement. Verses 9-20 (the "Longer Ending") were likely added later to provide a more complete conclusion. They are consistent with the other Gospels but may not be from Mark's hand.
  • Mark's Ending at Verse 8: If Mark intentionally ended here, it is a masterful literary choice. The women are told to go tell the disciples — but "they said nothing to any man; for they were afraid" (v.8). The reader is left with a question: will YOU tell? Will you overcome your fear and proclaim the risen Christ? The open ending makes every reader a participant.
  • "Tell his disciples AND PETER" (v.7): Only Mark includes "and Peter." Peter — who denied Jesus three times — is specifically named. He is not excluded by his failure; he is specifically included by grace. This detail almost certainly came from Peter himself (Mark's source).

Map & Geography

  • The tomb (vv.1-6): The rock-hewn tomb near Golgotha in Jerusalem where Jesus was buried. The women came at sunrise on the first day of the week.
  • Galilee (v.7): The angel directed the disciples to meet Jesus in Galilee — back where it all began.
  • "All the world" (v.15): The commission extends the geographic scope from Israel to every creature in every place.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): enduringword.com Guzik examines the empty tomb, the textual question of vv.9-20, the resurrection appearances, and the Great Commission.
  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "He Is Risen" on v.6 (MTP Vol. 10, No. 599) "He is risen — he is not here. The tomb is empty. Death could not hold Him. The grave could not keep Him. He is risen — and because He lives, you shall live also. That is the foundation of everything we believe, everything we hope, everything we are." Sermon: "Tell His Disciples and Peter" on v.7 (MTP Vol. 22, No. 1334) "And Peter — two words of pure grace. Peter who denied Him. Peter who cursed and swore. Peter who failed most publicly. And the angel says 'Make sure Peter knows.' That is the Gospel — the worst failure is not excluded from the greatest grace."

Translations

Show translations:

v.1 — NET: "When the Sabbath was over, Mary Magdalene, Mary
the mother of James, and Salome bought aromatic spices so
that they might go and anoint him" — "aromatic spices" is
interpretive. KJV: "sweet spices." WEB: "spices." They
came expecting a dead body, not a resurrection.

v.4 — NET: "But when they looked up, they saw that the stone,
which was very large, had been rolled back" — "very large"
(megas sphodra) — Mark notes the practical problem the women
had discussed (v.3) was already resolved. WEB: "exceedingly
great." The detail adds to the narrative tension.

v.5 — NET: "they saw a young man dressed in a white robe sitting
on the right side" — "a young man" (neaniskon) rather than
"angel" (Matthew's term). Mark's restraint here is
characteristic. WEB: "a young man." KJV: "a young man."

v.6 — KJV: "He is risen; he is not here" / ESV: "He has risen; he is not
here" — Both equivalent. The ESV uses "has risen" (perfect tense)
emphasizing the completed action with ongoing results.
NET: "He has been raised! He is not here" — "has been
raised" (passive: God raised Him) vs. KJV "He is risen"
(intransitive: He rose). The theological nuance: both are
true, but the passive emphasizes the Father's action.

v.8 — KJV: "neither said they any thing to any man; for they were afraid" /
ESV: "and they said nothing to anyone, for they were afraid" — Both
equivalent. If this is Mark's original ending, it is deliberately
abrupt and open-ended. The Greek ends with "gar" (for) — an unusual
way to end a book, creating suspense and inviting the reader to respond.
NET: "They went out and ran from the tomb, for terror and
bewilderment had seized them. And they said nothing to
anyone, because they were afraid" — Mark's original ending
(most scholars agree 16:9-20 was added later). The abrupt
ending on fear is deliberately shocking. WEB includes the
longer ending; NET brackets it with a note.

v.9 — 20 — KJV includes these verses without notation. ESV brackets them with
the note: "Some of the earliest manuscripts do not include 16:9–20."
These verses are absent from the two oldest manuscripts. They summarize
resurrection appearances found in the other Gospels and Acts. Whether
original to Mark or added later, their content is consistent with the
rest of the NT.

v.15 — KJV: "preach the gospel to every creature" / ESV: "proclaim the gospel
to the whole creation" — Both equivalent. Greek "pase te ktisei" — "all
creation" or "every creature." The scope is universal — no person, no
nation, no people group is excluded from the Gospel's reach.

v.19 — KJV: "sat on the right hand of God" / ESV: "sat down at the right hand
of God" — Both equivalent. The position of authority, honor, and
intercession. Jesus' earthly ministry is complete; His heavenly ministry
of intercession continues.

Reflection

  • 1. The women came expecting a dead body (v.1). They found an empty tomb and a living Lord. Where are you expecting death when God may be preparing resurrection? What situation have you given up on?
  • 2. The women "were afraid" (v.8) — even after hearing the resurrection announcement. Fear is a natural response to the supernatural. But fear must not silence testimony. What fear is keeping you from telling others about Christ?
  • 3. Jesus "upbraided them with their unbelief and hardness of heart" (v.14) — even the eleven doubted the resurrection initially. Doubt is not the end of faith — but it must be confronted and overcome. What doubts do you need to bring honestly to Christ?
  • 4. Mark's Gospel may end at v.8 — with the women silent in fear. The open ending asks: will YOU tell? Will you overcome your fear and proclaim the risen Christ? What is your answer?
  • 5. "He sat on the right hand of God" (v.19). Jesus is not absent — He is enthroned, interceding, and working. How does knowing that the risen, ascended Christ is actively ruling and interceding affect your daily confidence and courage?
  • 1. "He is risen; he is not here" (v.6). The tomb is empty. Everything depends on this. If Jesus is risen, then death is defeated, sin is paid for, and every promise He made is guaranteed. Do you live like the resurrection is true — or like it's just a doctrine you affirm?
  • 2. "Tell his disciples AND PETER" (v.7). Peter — the one who failed most publicly — is specifically included by name. No matter how badly you've failed, you are not excluded from grace. Do you believe that for yourself?
  • 3. "Go ye into all the world, and preach the gospel to every creature" (v.15). This is not optional. Every believer is commissioned. What does "going" look like in your specific context?
  • 4. "The Lord working with them" (v.20). You don't go alone. The Lord works WITH you — confirming His word. How does knowing that Christ is actively working alongside you change how you approach the mission?
