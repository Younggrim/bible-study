Isaiah — Chapter 47

1Come down and sit in the dust, O virgin daughter of Babylon; sit on the ground without a throne, O daughter of the Chaldeans! For you shall no more be called tender and delicate.

2Take the millstones and grind flour, put off your veil, strip off your robe, uncover your legs, pass through the rivers.

3Your nakedness shall be uncovered, and your disgrace shall be seen. I will take vengeance, and I will spare no one.

4Our Redeemer—the LORD of hosts is his name— is the Holy One of Israel.

5Sit in silence, and go into darkness, O daughter of the Chaldeans; for you shall no more be called the mistress of kingdoms.

6I was angry with my people; I profaned my heritage; I gave them into your hand; you showed them no mercy; on the aged you made your yoke exceedingly heavy.

7You said, “I shall be mistress forever,” so that you did not lay these things to heart or remember their end.

8Now therefore hear this, you lover of pleasures, who sit securely, who say in your heart, “I am, and there is no one besides me; I shall not sit as a widow or know the loss of children”:

9These two things shall come to you in a moment, in one day; the loss of children and widowhood shall come upon you in full measure, in spite of your many sorceries and the great power of your enchantments.

10You felt secure in your wickedness; you said, “No one sees me”; your wisdom and your knowledge led you astray, and you said in your heart, “I am, and there is no one besides me.”

11But evil shall come upon you, which you will not know how to charm away; disaster shall fall upon you, for which you will not be able to atone; and ruin shall come upon you suddenly, of which you know nothing.

12Stand fast in your enchantments and your many sorceries, with which you have labored from your youth; perhaps you may be able to succeed; perhaps you may inspire terror.

13You are wearied with your many counsels; let them stand forth and save you, those who divide the heavens, who gaze at the stars, who at the new moons make known what shall come upon you.

14Behold, they are like stubble; the fire consumes them; they cannot deliver themselves from the power of the flame. No coal for warming oneself is this, no fire to sit before!

15Such to you are those with whom you have labored, who have done business with you from your youth; they wander about, each in his own direction; there is no one to save you.

Summary

A taunt-song against Babylon: the pampered "virgin daughter" is stripped of her glory, forced to grind grain like a slave, and exposed. Her sorceries and enchantments cannot save her. Disaster will come suddenly — in one day — and no one can prevent it.

Key Verses

v.1
"Come down, and sit in the dust, O virgin daughter of Babylon, sit on the ground: there is no throne" (KJV) — From throne to dust in one command. "Virgin" = never yet conquered (she thought). God says: come down.
v.7
"And thou saidst, I shall be a lady for ever: so that thou didst not lay these things to thy heart, neither didst remember the latter end of it" (KJV) — Babylon's fatal assumption: permanence. "I shall be a lady FOREVER." She didn't consider that empires end.
v.8
"I am, and none else beside me" (KJV) — Babylon claims divine language — the same "I am" God uses. Self-deification: the ultimate pride. She speaks as if she were God.
v.9
"But these two things shall come to thee in a moment in one day, the loss of children, and widowhood: they shall come upon thee in their perfection" (KJV) — Sudden, complete catastrophe. Not gradual decline but ONE DAY of total loss. Fulfilled: Daniel 5 (Belshazzar's feast — that night Babylon fell).
v.13
"Let now the astrologers, the stargazers, the monthly prognosticators, stand up, and save thee from these things that shall come upon thee" (KJV) — Sarcastic challenge: let your stargazers save you! Babylon was famous for astronomy/astrology — yet couldn't foresee her own end.

Cross-References

  • v.1 — Revelation 18:7-8 — "she saith in her heart, I sit a queen... therefore shall her plagues come in one day." v.7 — Revelation 18:7 — "I sit a queen, and am no widow." v.8 — Revelation 18:8 — "in one day" — Revelation echoes Isaiah's language of Babylon's sudden fall. v.9 — Daniel 5:30 — "In that night was Belshazzar the king of the Chaldeans slain." v.12 — Daniel 2:2 — Nebuchadnezzar's magicians, astrologers, sorcerers unable to interpret his dream. v.14 — Nahum 3:15 — "the fire shall devour thee." v.15 — Jeremiah 51:6-9 — "Babylon is suddenly fallen."

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/isaiah-47/
  • Charles Spurgeon: "Babylon said, 'I am, and none beside me.' The creature usurped the Creator's title. This is the essence of all sin: to say 'I am' as if you were God. Humility recognizes that only ONE can truly say 'I AM' — and it is not you."

Translations

  • v.1 — "virgin daughter of Babylon" (KJV/ESV) — "Virgin" = previously unconquered. The title implies invulnerability — about to be shattered.
  • v.2 — "take the millstones, and grind meal" (KJV/ESV) — Slave work. The pampered queen must do the lowest manual labor. Complete social reversal.
  • v.5 — "The Lady of Kingdoms" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "geveret mamlakhot" = mistress/queen of kingdoms. Babylon ruled the known world — now she loses the title permanently.
  • v.11 — "mischief" (KJV) / "ruin" (ESV) — Hebrew "ra'ah" = calamity, evil, disaster. It will fall on Babylon without warning — she can't bribe it away ("atone" = Hebrew "kapparah" used sarcastically: you can't pay a ransom for this).

Reflection

  • 1. "I shall be a lady forever" (v.7). Every empire, institution, and person who assumes permanence is making Babylon's mistake. Nothing human lasts forever. What in your life are you treating as if it will never end?
  • 2. "I am, and none beside me" (v.8). Self-deification isn't just for empires. Every time you live as if the universe revolves around you — your needs, your comfort, your glory — you're echoing Babylon's blasphemy.
  • 3. Sudden destruction (v.9). Babylon didn't see it coming. Neither did Belshazzar (Daniel 5). Life changes in a single day. Are you prepared for the unexpected? Is your foundation God or your circumstances?
  • 4. Sorcery cannot save (vv.12-13). Modern equivalents: horoscopes, self-help gurus, fortune-telling, New Age spirituality — anything that promises control over the future apart from God. It will fail when you need it most.
