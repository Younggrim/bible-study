The Ten Commandments

"And God spoke all these words, saying, 'I am the LORD your God, who brought you out of the land of Egypt, out of the house of slavery.'"

— Exodus 20:1-2 (ESV)

1. No Other Gods

Exodus 20:3
OT Text: "You shall have no other gods before me."

NT Fulfillment: Jesus affirmed this as the greatest commandment — to love God with all your heart, soul, and mind (Matthew 22:37-38).

Application: Anything that takes first place in your heart above God becomes an idol — career, relationships, money, comfort, or self.
Cross-References: Matthew 22:37-38 • 1 Corinthians 8:6 • 1 John 5:21 • Deuteronomy 6:4-5

2. No Idols

Exodus 20:4-6
OT Text: "You shall not make for yourself a carved image, or any likeness of anything that is in heaven above, or that is in the earth beneath."

NT Fulfillment: Paul extends this to covetousness itself (Colossians 3:5) — any created thing we worship in place of the Creator.

Application: We are to worship God in Spirit and truth (John 4:24), not through images or man-made representations of divinity.
Cross-References: Colossians 3:5 • Romans 1:22-23 • Acts 17:29 • John 4:24

3. Do Not Take God's Name in Vain

Exodus 20:7
OT Text: "You shall not take the name of the LORD your God in vain, for the LORD will not hold him guiltless who takes his name in vain."

NT Fulfillment: Jesus taught us to pray "hallowed be your name" (Matthew 6:9) — God's name is to be revered, not emptied of its weight.

Application: This includes careless oaths, flippant use of God's name, and claiming to represent God while living contrary to His character.
Cross-References: Matthew 6:9 • James 5:12 • Philippians 2:9-11 • Leviticus 19:12

4. Remember the Sabbath

Exodus 20:8-11
OT Text: "Remember the Sabbath day, to keep it holy. Six days you shall labor, and do all your work, but the seventh day is a Sabbath to the LORD your God."

NT Fulfillment: Jesus declared Himself Lord of the Sabbath (Mark 2:28). Our true rest is found in Christ (Hebrews 4:9-10), who fulfilled what the Sabbath pointed to.

Application: We honor God by resting in His finished work, setting aside time for worship, and trusting Him rather than our own constant striving.
Cross-References: Mark 2:27-28 • Hebrews 4:9-10 • Colossians 2:16-17 • Matthew 11:28-30

5. Honor Your Father and Mother

Exodus 20:12
OT Text: "Honor your father and your mother, that your days may be long in the land that the LORD your God is giving you."

NT Fulfillment: Paul reaffirms this as "the first commandment with a promise" (Ephesians 6:2-3). Jesus modeled obedience to earthly parents while maintaining ultimate obedience to His Father.

Application: Respect, care for, and value your parents. This extends to honoring authority structures God has established.
Cross-References: Ephesians 6:1-3 • Colossians 3:20 • Proverbs 1:8-9 • Matthew 15:4-6

How to Honor Your Parents: A Guide to Respect

6. You Shall Not Murder

Exodus 20:13
OT Text: "You shall not murder."

NT Fulfillment: Jesus deepened this command — even anger and contempt toward another person violates its spirit (Matthew 5:21-22). John says "everyone who hates his brother is a murderer" (1 John 3:15).

Application: We are called not only to refrain from physical violence but to guard our hearts against hatred, bitterness, and dehumanizing others.
Cross-References: Matthew 5:21-22 • 1 John 3:15 • Romans 12:19-21 • James 4:1-2

7. You Shall Not Commit Adultery

Exodus 20:14
OT Text: "You shall not commit adultery."

NT Fulfillment: Jesus extended this to the heart — "everyone who looks at a woman with lustful intent has already committed adultery in his heart" (Matthew 5:27-28).

Application: Faithfulness in marriage reflects God's covenant faithfulness. This calls us to purity in thought, word, and deed — honoring the marriage covenant.
Cross-References: Matthew 5:27-28 • Hebrews 13:4 • 1 Corinthians 6:18-20 • Proverbs 5:15-19

Why Jesus Connected Anger with Murder and Lust with Adultery

8. You Shall Not Steal

Exodus 20:15
OT Text: "You shall not steal."

NT Fulfillment: Paul transforms this from prohibition to purpose: "Let the thief no longer steal, but rather let him labor, doing honest work with his own hands, so that he may have something to share" (Ephesians 4:28).

Application: Beyond not taking what isn't ours, we are called to generous labor — working honestly to give to others rather than taking from them.
Cross-References: Ephesians 4:28 • Romans 13:7-8 • Proverbs 11:1 • Luke 19:8

9. You Shall Not Bear False Witness

Exodus 20:16
OT Text: "You shall not bear false witness against your neighbor."

NT Fulfillment: Paul urges believers to "put away falsehood" and "speak the truth with his neighbor" (Ephesians 4:25). Jesus identified Himself as "the Truth" (John 14:6).

Application: Integrity in speech — no lying, gossip, slander, or deception. Our words should reflect the character of the God of truth.
Cross-References: Ephesians 4:25 • Colossians 3:9 • Proverbs 12:22 • James 3:1-12

10. You Shall Not Covet

Exodus 20:17
OT Text: "You shall not covet your neighbor's house... wife... or anything that belongs to your neighbor."

NT Fulfillment: Paul identified covetousness as idolatry (Colossians 3:5) and learned the secret of contentment in Christ (Philippians 4:11-13). This commandment convicted Paul of his sin (Romans 7:7-8).

Application: Contentment in God's provision is the antidote to coveting. Gratitude replaces envy when we trust God's goodness.
Cross-References: Philippians 4:11-13 • 1 Timothy 6:6-10 • Hebrews 13:5 • Romans 7:7-8

Context & Background

  • The Ten Commandments were given at Mount Sinai approximately three months after the Exodus from Egypt (Exodus 19:1). God had already delivered Israel — the law was given to a redeemed people, not as a means of redemption.
  • The commandments are divided into two tables: commands 1-4 govern our relationship with God (vertical); commands 5-10 govern our relationships with others (horizontal). Jesus summarized both in the two greatest commandments (Matthew 22:37-40).
  • God spoke audibly to the entire nation (Exodus 20:1, Deuteronomy 5:22) and wrote the commandments with His own finger on stone tablets (Exodus 31:18) — emphasizing their permanence and divine authority.
  • The law's purpose was never to save but to reveal sin (Romans 3:20), act as a guardian leading us to Christ (Galatians 3:24), and reflect God's holy character.
  • Christ fulfilled the law perfectly (Matthew 5:17) — He is the only person who kept all ten commandments flawlessly, and His righteousness is credited to believers by faith (Romans 3:21-22).

Cross-References

  • Matthew 5:17-20 — "Do not think that I have come to abolish the Law... I have not come to abolish them but to fulfill them."
  • Romans 3:19-20 — "Through the law comes knowledge of sin."
  • Galatians 3:23-25 — "The law was our guardian until Christ came, in order that we might be justified by faith."
  • Romans 13:8-10 — "Love is the fulfilling of the law."
  • Matthew 22:37-40 — Jesus summarizes all the law in two commands: love God, love neighbor.
  • 2 Corinthians 3:6-9 — The ministry of the Spirit vs. the ministry of the letter that kills.

Reflection Questions

  • The commandments were given to an already-redeemed people. How does this change your understanding of obedience — is it a means to earn God's love, or a response to it?
  • Jesus deepened the commandments from external behavior to internal heart attitudes. Which commandment convicts you most at the heart level?
  • Paul said the law was a "guardian" leading us to Christ. How has your failure to keep the commandments driven you to depend on grace?
  • "Love is the fulfilling of the law" (Romans 13:10). How would genuine love for God and neighbor naturally fulfill each commandment?
  • Which commandment do you find most countercultural in today's world? How does obeying it set you apart as a follower of Christ?
