The Ten Commandments

"And God spoke all these words, saying, 'I am the LORD your God, who brought you out of the land of Egypt, out of the house of slavery.'"

— Exodus 20:1-2 (ESV)

1. No Other Gods

Exodus 20:3
Old Testament: "You shall have no other gods before me."

New Testament: Jesus affirmed this as the greatest commandment — to love God with all your heart, soul, and mind (Matthew 22:37-38).

Application: Anything that takes first place in your heart above God becomes an idol — career, relationships, money, comfort, or self.
Cross-References:
▸ Matthew 22:37-38
▸ 1 Corinthians 8:6
▸ 1 John 5:21
▸ Deuteronomy 6:4-5

2. No Idols

Exodus 20:4-6
Old Testament: "You shall not make for yourself a carved image, or any likeness of anything that is in heaven above, or that is in the earth beneath."

New Testament: Paul extends this to covetousness itself (Colossians 3:5) — any created thing we worship in place of the Creator.

Application: We are to worship God in Spirit and truth (John 4:24), not through images or man-made representations of divinity.
Cross-References:
▸ Colossians 3:5
▸ Romans 1:22-23
▸ Acts 17:29
▸ John 4:24

3. Do Not Take God's Name in Vain

Exodus 20:7
Old Testament: "You shall not take the name of the LORD your God in vain, for the LORD will not hold him guiltless who takes his name in vain."

New Testament: Jesus taught us to pray "hallowed be your name" (Matthew 6:9) — God's name is to be revered, not emptied of its weight.

Application: This includes careless oaths, flippant use of God's name, and claiming to represent God while living contrary to His character.
Cross-References:
▸ Matthew 6:9
▸ James 5:12
▸ Philippians 2:9-11
▸ Leviticus 19:12

4. Remember the Sabbath

Exodus 20:8-11
Old Testament: "Remember the Sabbath day, to keep it holy. Six days you shall labor, and do all your work, but the seventh day is a Sabbath to the LORD your God."

New Testament: Jesus declared Himself Lord of the Sabbath (Mark 2:28). Our true rest is found in Christ (Hebrews 4:9-10), who fulfilled what the Sabbath pointed to.

Application: We honor God by resting in His finished work, setting aside time for worship, and trusting Him rather than our own constant striving.
Cross-References:
▸ Mark 2:27-28
▸ Hebrews 4:9-10
▸ Colossians 2:16-17
▸ Matthew 11:28-30

5. Honor Your Father and Mother

Exodus 20:12
Old Testament: "Honor your father and your mother, that your days may be long in the land that the LORD your God is giving you."

New Testament: Paul reaffirms this as "the first commandment with a promise" (Ephesians 6:2-3). Jesus modeled obedience to earthly parents while maintaining ultimate obedience to His Father.

Application: Respect, care for, and value your parents. This extends to honoring authority structures God has established.
Cross-References:
▸ Ephesians 6:1-3
▸ Colossians 3:20
▸ Proverbs 1:8-9
▸ Matthew 15:4-6

The 5th Commandment — Honor Your Father and Mother

6. You Shall Not Murder

Exodus 20:13
Old Testament: "You shall not murder."

New Testament: Jesus deepened this command — even anger and contempt toward another person violates its spirit (Matthew 5:21-22). John says "everyone who hates his brother is a murderer" (1 John 3:15).

Application: We are called not only to refrain from physical violence but to guard our hearts against hatred, bitterness, and dehumanizing others.
Cross-References:
▸ Matthew 5:21-22
▸ 1 John 3:15
▸ Romans 12:19-21
▸ James 4:1-2

The 6th Commandment — You Shall Not Murder

7. You Shall Not Commit Adultery

Exodus 20:14
Old Testament: "You shall not commit adultery."

New Testament: Jesus extended this to the heart — "everyone who looks at a woman with lustful intent has already committed adultery in his heart" (Matthew 5:27-28).

Application: Faithfulness in marriage reflects God's covenant faithfulness. This calls us to purity in thought, word, and deed — honoring the marriage covenant.
Cross-References:
▸ Matthew 5:27-28
▸ Hebrews 13:4
▸ 1 Corinthians 6:18-20
▸ Proverbs 5:15-19

The 7th Commandment — You Shall Not Commit Adultery

8. You Shall Not Steal

Exodus 20:15
Old Testament: "You shall not steal."

New Testament: Paul transforms this from prohibition to purpose: "Let the thief no longer steal, but rather let him labor, doing honest work with his own hands, so that he may have something to share" (Ephesians 4:28).

Application: Beyond not taking what isn't ours, we are called to generous labor — working honestly to give to others rather than taking from them.
Cross-References:
▸ Ephesians 4:28
▸ Romans 13:7-8
▸ Proverbs 11:1
▸ Luke 19:8

The 8th Commandment — You Shall Not Steal

9. You Shall Not Bear False Witness

Exodus 20:16
Old Testament: "You shall not bear false witness against your neighbor."

New Testament: Paul urges believers to "put away falsehood" and "speak the truth with his neighbor" (Ephesians 4:25). Jesus identified Himself as "the Truth" (John 14:6).

Application: Integrity in speech — no lying, gossip, slander, or deception. Our words should reflect the character of the God of truth.
Cross-References:
▸ Ephesians 4:25
▸ Colossians 3:9
▸ Proverbs 12:22
▸ James 3:1-12

The 9th Commandment — You Shall Not Bear False Witness

10. You Shall Not Covet

Exodus 20:17
Old Testament: "You shall not covet your neighbor's house... wife... or anything that belongs to your neighbor."

New Testament: Paul identified covetousness as idolatry (Colossians 3:5) and learned the secret of contentment in Christ (Philippians 4:11-13). This commandment convicted Paul of his sin (Romans 7:7-8).

Application: Contentment in God's provision is the antidote to coveting. Gratitude replaces envy when we trust God's goodness.
Cross-References:
▸ Philippians 4:11-13
▸ 1 Timothy 6:6-10
▸ Hebrews 13:5
▸ Romans 7:7-8
