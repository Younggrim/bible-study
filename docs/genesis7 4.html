Genesis — Chapter 7

1Then the LORD said to Noah, “Go into the ark, you and all your household, for I have seen that you are righteous before me in this generation.

2Take with you seven pairs of all clean animals, the male and his mate, and a pair of the animals that are not clean, the male and his mate,

3and seven pairs of the birds of the heavens also, male and female, to keep their offspring alive on the face of all the earth.

4For in seven days I will send rain on the earth forty days and forty nights, and every living thing that I have made I will blot out from the face of the ground.”

5And Noah did all that the LORD had commanded him.

6Noah was six hundred years old when the flood of waters came upon the earth.

7And Noah and his sons and his wife and his sons’ wives with him went into the ark to escape the waters of the flood.

8Of clean animals, and of animals that are not clean, and of birds, and of everything that creeps on the ground,

9two and two, male and female, went into the ark with Noah, as God had commanded Noah.

10And after seven days the waters of the flood came upon the earth.

11In the six hundredth year of Noah’s life, in the second month, on the seventeenth day of the month, on that day all the fountains of the great deep burst forth, and the windows of the heavens were opened.

12And rain fell upon the earth forty days and forty nights.

13On the very same day Noah and his sons, Shem and Ham and Japheth, and Noah’s wife and the three wives of his sons with them entered the ark,

14they and every beast, according to its kind, and all the livestock according to their kinds, and every creeping thing that creeps on the earth, according to its kind, and every bird, according to its kind, every winged creature.

15They went into the ark with Noah, two and two of all flesh in which there was the breath of life.

16And those that entered, male and female of all flesh, went in as God had commanded him. And the LORD shut him in.

17The flood continued forty days on the earth. The waters increased and bore up the ark, and it rose high above the earth.

18The waters prevailed and increased greatly on the earth, and the ark floated on the face of the waters.

19And the waters prevailed so mightily on the earth that all the high mountains under the whole heaven were covered.

20The waters prevailed above the mountains, covering them fifteen cubits deep.

21And all flesh died that moved on the earth, birds, livestock, beasts, all swarming creatures that swarm on the earth, and all mankind.

22Everything on the dry land in whose nostrils was the breath of life died.

23He blotted out every living thing that was on the face of the ground, man and animals and creeping things and birds of the heavens. They were blotted out from the earth. Only Noah was left, and those who were with him in the ark.

24And the waters prevailed on the earth 150 days.

Summary

Noah, his family, and the animals enter the ark; God sends the flood and destroys all life on earth.

"Only Noah was left, and those who were with him in the ark." (ESV) — Salvation through the ark alone.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/genesis-7/ Guzik covers the invitation to the ark, the flood's sources, the devastation, and the typological significance.
  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "The LORD Shut Him In" on v.16 (MTP Vol. 11, No. 634) "The LORD shut him in — not Noah. Noah didn't secure his own safety — God did. The same hand that shut Noah IN shut the world OUT. When God closes the door, no one can open it. When God secures you, no one can unsecure you. Your salvation depends not on your grip on the door handle but on God's hand that shut the door. He shut him in — safe, secure, sealed by God Himself." Sermon: "Come Into the Ark" on v.1 (MTP Vol. 20, No. 1201) "COME — not 'go.' God says 'come' because He is already in the ark. He doesn't send Noah away from Himself but invites him toward Himself. Christ says the same: 'Come unto me.' Not 'go somewhere' but 'come to where I am.' The ark is where God is — and He invites you in."

Translations

  • v.1 — KJV: "Come thou and all thy house into the ark" / ESV: "Go into the ark, you and all your household" — Hebrew "bo" — come/enter. This is a significant translation difference. KJV renders "bo" as "come" (implying God is already in the ark, inviting Noah toward Himself); ESV renders it as "go" (a directional command). The Hebrew "bo" most naturally means "come/enter" — KJV's rendering preserves the theological nuance that God invites Noah toward His presence.
  • v.2 — KJV: "by sevens, the male and his female" / ESV: "seven pairs... the male and his mate" — Hebrew "shib'ah shib'ah" — seven seven. Debated: seven total or seven pairs? ESV interprets as "seven pairs" (14 total); KJV's "by sevens" leaves it ambiguous. The distinction between clean and unclean animals predates the Mosaic Law.
  • v.3 — KJV: "to keep seed alive upon the face of all the earth" / ESV: "to keep their offspring alive on the face of all the earth" — Hebrew "zera" — seed, offspring. ESV's "offspring" is clearer for modern readers.
  • v.4 — KJV: "every living substance that I have made will I destroy" / ESV: "every living thing that I have made I will blot out" — Hebrew "emcheh" — to wipe away, blot out. ESV's "blot out" is more literal; KJV's "destroy" captures the result. "Forty days and forty nights" — both identical. Forty is a number of testing/judgment throughout Scripture.
  • v.11 — KJV: "all the fountains of the great deep broken up, and the windows of heaven were opened" / ESV: "all the fountains of the great deep burst forth, and the windows of the heavens were opened" — Hebrew "nib'qe'u" — burst open, split apart. ESV's "burst forth" is more vivid than KJV's "broken up." Both convey catastrophic eruption of subterranean waters.
  • v.16 — KJV: "the LORD shut him in" / ESV: "the LORD shut him in" — Both identical. Hebrew "Yahweh sagar ba'ado" — Yahweh closed [the door] behind him. God personally sealed the ark. Divine security.
  • v.19 — KJV: "all the high hills, that were under the whole heaven, were covered" / ESV: "all the high mountains under the whole heaven were covered" — Hebrew "harim" — hills or mountains. ESV's "mountains" is stronger; KJV's "hills" may understate the scope. The language indicates a universal, global flood — "under the whole heaven" = comprehensive.
  • v.23 — KJV: "Noah only remained alive, and they that were with him in the ark" / ESV: "Only Noah was left, and those who were with him in the ark" — Both equivalent. Salvation exclusively through the ark.
  • v.24 — KJV: "an hundred and fifty days" / ESV: "150 days" — Both identical in meaning. Five months of prevailing waters. The flood was not a brief rainstorm but a prolonged, catastrophic event.
  • TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB) - v.1 — ASV/WEB both retain "Come" (Hebrew "bo"), preserving the theological nuance that God invites Noah toward Himself. NET also uses "Come into the ark." WEB renders "ark" as "ship" throughout the flood narrative. - v.1 — NET: "I consider you godly" — interprets "righteous" (KJV/ESV) as "godly," shifting from legal/moral standing to character description. - v.11 — NET: "the floodgates of the heavens were opened" — renders Hebrew "arubboth hashamayim" as "floodgates" (vs. "windows" in KJV/ESV/ASV/WEB), a more hydrological image that avoids the literal "windows" metaphor. - v.19 — ASV: "all the high mountains that were under the whole heaven were covered" — uses "mountains" (like ESV) where KJV has weaker "hills," strengthening the global scope of the flood. - v.20 — NET converts "fifteen cubits" to "more than 20 feet," continuing its practice of modernizing measurements for accessibility.

Reflection

  • 1. "Come thou... into the ark" (v.1). God says "come" — toward Himself. Are you responding to His invitation? He's not sending you away; He's inviting you in.
  • 2. "The LORD shut him in" (v.16). God secures your salvation — not you. Your security depends on His hand, not yours. Does this give you confidence?
  • 3. "Noah only remained alive, and they that were with him in the ark" (v.23). There was only one way of salvation — the ark. There is only one way now — Christ. Have you entered?
  • 4. "As the days of Noe were, so shall also the coming of the Son of man be" (Matt 24:37). Jesus compared His return to Noah's flood — sudden judgment on an unprepared world. Are you prepared?
  • 5. Noah obeyed completely (v.5) and was saved. Obedience is the response to grace. God provides the ark (grace); you must enter it (obedience). Have you done both — received grace AND responded with obedience?
