Psalms — Chapter 143

1Hear my prayer, O LORD; give ear to my pleas for mercy! In your faithfulness answer me, in your righteousness!

2Enter not into judgment with your servant, for no one living is righteous before you.

3For the enemy has pursued my soul; he has crushed my life to the ground; he has made me sit in darkness like those long dead.

4Therefore my spirit faints within me; my heart within me is appalled.

5I remember the days of old; I meditate on all that you have done; I ponder the work of your hands.

6I stretch out my hands to you; my soul thirsts for you like a parched land. Selah

7Answer me quickly, O LORD! My spirit fails! Hide not your face from me, lest I be like those who go down to the pit.

8Let me hear in the morning of your steadfast love, for in you I trust. Make me know the way I should go, for to you I lift up my soul.

9Deliver me from my enemies, O LORD! I have fled to you for refuge.

10Teach me to do your will, for you are my God! Let your good Spirit lead me on level ground!

11For your name’s sake, O LORD, preserve my life! In your righteousness bring my soul out of trouble!

12And in your steadfast love you will cut off my enemies, and you will destroy all the adversaries of my soul, for I am your servant.

Summary

A penitential lament — David's spirit is overwhelmed, the enemy has crushed him; he pleads for God's faithfulness (not strict justice, since none living is righteous), thirsts for God like parched land, and asks to be led on level ground.

Key Verses

v.2
"Enter not into judgment with thy servant: for in thy sight shall no man living be justified." (KJV) — Foundational gospel truth. NO ONE can stand justified before God on their own merit. Not David, not any person living. This is why grace is necessary (cf. Rom 3:20, Gal 2:16).
v.6
"I stretch forth my hands unto thee: my soul thirsteth after thee, as a thirsty land." (KJV) — The posture (outstretched hands) and the feeling (desperate thirst) combined. David's soul is parched earth reaching toward rain. Only God can satisfy this drought.
v.10
"Teach me to do thy will; for thou art my God: thy spirit is good; lead me into the land of uprightness." (KJV) — "Thy Spirit is good" — one of the rare OT references to the Holy Spirit as personal guide. David asks for the Spirit's leading into righteous living.

Cross-References

  • v.2 — Romans 3:20 — "By the deeds of the law there shall no flesh be justified in his sight." v.2 — Galatians 2:16 — "A man is not justified by the works of the law, but by the faith of Jesus Christ." v.5 — Psalm 77:5 — "I have considered the days of old, the years of ancient times." v.6 — Psalm 63:1 — "My soul thirsteth for thee, my flesh longeth for thee in a dry and thirsty land." v.10 — Nehemiah 9:20 — "Thou gavest also thy good spirit to instruct them." v.10 — Romans 8:14 — "As many as are led by the Spirit of God."

Reflection

  • 1. No one is justified before God (v.2). This is not false humility — it's reality. Don't approach God on the basis of your performance. Approach on the basis of His faithfulness and mercy.
  • 2. Thirst for God (v.6). Spiritual thirst is not weakness — it's health. A soul that doesn't thirst for God is a soul in danger. Stretch out your hands like parched earth toward rain.
  • 3. Ask for the Spirit's leading (v.10). "Thy Spirit is good — lead me." This is one of the simplest and best prayers: Holy Spirit, lead me where I should go. Your Spirit is good; I trust Your direction.
  • 4. Remember past mercies (v.5). In present crisis, recall past faithfulness. What God has done before, He can do again. Memory fuels faith.
