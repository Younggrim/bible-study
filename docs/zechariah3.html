Zechariah — Chapter 3

1Then he showed me Joshua the high priest standing before the angel of the LORD, and Satan standing at his right hand to accuse him.

2And the LORD said to Satan, “The LORD rebuke you, O Satan! The LORD who has chosen Jerusalem rebuke you! Is not this a brand plucked from the fire?”

3Now Joshua was standing before the angel, clothed with filthy garments.

4And the angel said to those who were standing before him, “Remove the filthy garments from him.” And to him he said, “Behold, I have taken your iniquity away from you, and I will clothe you with pure vestments.”

5And I said, “Let them put a clean turban on his head.” So they put a clean turban on his head and clothed him with garments. And the angel of the LORD was standing by.

6And the angel of the LORD solemnly assured Joshua,

7“Thus says the LORD of hosts: If you will walk in my ways and keep my charge, then you shall rule my house and have charge of my courts, and I will give you the right of access among those who are standing here.

8Hear now, O Joshua the high priest, you and your friends who sit before you, for they are men who are a sign: behold, I will bring my servant the Branch.

9For behold, on the stone that I have set before Joshua, on a single stone with seven eyes, I will engrave its inscription, declares the LORD of hosts, and I will remove the iniquity of this land in a single day.

10In that day, declares the LORD of hosts, every one of you will invite his neighbor to come under his vine and under his fig tree.”

Summary

Joshua the high priest stands accused by Satan but is cleansed by the Angel of the LORD, given pure garments, and told of the coming "Branch" — God will remove the iniquity of the land in a single day.

Key Verses

  • v.1 — "Joshua the high priest standing before the angel of the LORD, and Satan standing at his right hand to resist him" (KJV) — A courtroom scene. Joshua represents sinful Israel. Satan is the prosecutor. The Angel of the LORD is the judge. And the verdict will shock everyone: the guilty is acquitted — not because he is innocent, but because God intervenes.
  • v.2 — "The LORD rebuke thee, O Satan... is not this a brand plucked out of the fire?" (KJV) — God silences the accuser. His argument is not that Joshua is clean (he isn't) but that He has CHOSEN to rescue him. A burning stick snatched from the fire — that is grace. Rescued not because of merit but because of mercy.
  • v.4 — "I have caused thine iniquity to pass from thee, and I will clothe thee with change of raiment" (KJV) / "I have taken your iniquity away from you, and I will clothe you with pure vestments" (ESV) — The double action of justification: iniquity REMOVED and righteousness BESTOWED. Not just forgiveness but a new wardrobe. This is the gospel: your filth taken away, Christ's righteousness put upon you.
  • v.8 — "I will bring forth my servant the BRANCH" (KJV) — One of the great Messianic titles. The Branch (Hebrew "tsemach") appears in Isaiah 4:2, 11:1; Jeremiah 23:5, 33:15; and Zechariah 3:8, 6:12. He is the shoot from David's stump — the Messiah who will grow up from lowly origins to bear royal glory.
  • v.9 — "I will remove the iniquity of that land in one day" (KJV) — This is the cross of Christ in one sentence. One day. One sacrifice. Complete removal of sin. Not gradual improvement but decisive, once-for-all atonement. "It is finished" (John 19:30).
  • v.10 — "In that day... shall ye call every man his neighbour under the vine and under the fig tree" (KJV) — The picture of Millennial peace. Security, prosperity, fellowship. When sin is removed, shalom follows.

Authorship & Background

  • Author: Zechariah son of Berechiah. Prophesied 520-480 BC. Contemporary with Haggai. Key theme: Messianic hope and the coming kingdom. The most Messianic of the Minor Prophets. Famous passages: the Branch (3:8, 6:12), "Not by might... but by my spirit" (4:6), the triumphal entry (9:9), "they shall look upon me whom they have pierced" (12:10), the shepherd struck (13:7), and the LORD's return to the Mount of Olives (14:4).
  • Classification: Prophetic Vision — The Cleansing of Joshua the High Priest and the Promise of the Branch Key Themes: Satan as accuser, divine justification, filthy garments removed, clean garments bestowed, the Branch promised, iniquity removed in one day, the gospel in the Old Testament
  • Historical Context: The fourth night vision is arguably the most theologically profound in Zechariah. Joshua the high priest stands before the Angel of the LORD — representing Israel before God. Satan stands at his right hand to accuse him. And the accusation is valid: Joshua wears FILTHY garments (literally "excrement-covered" in Hebrew). He is guilty.
  • But God does not defend Joshua's innocence — He overrules the accusation by sovereign grace. "Is not this a brand plucked from the fire?" God removes the filthy garments, clothes Joshua in clean vestments, and places a clean turban on his head. This is justification: not making the sinner righteous in himself, but CLOTHING him with a righteousness not his own.
  • The chapter then pivots to the ultimate resolution: "I will bring forth my servant the BRANCH" (v.8). The Branch is the Messiah — the one who will "remove the iniquity of that land in one day" (v.9). This is the cross. One day. One sacrifice. All sin removed.
  • Structure: - Joshua Before the Angel: Satan the Accuser (vv.1-2) - The Filthy Garments Removed (vv.3-4) - The Clean Turban and Pure Vestments (v.5) - The Charge to Walk Faithfully (vv.6-7) - The Promise of the Branch (v.8) - The Stone with Seven Eyes: Iniquity Removed in One Day (v.9) - The Millennial Peace: Vine and Fig Tree (v.10)

Reflection

  • 1. Satan accuses, but God defends (vv.1-2). The enemy's strategy is accusation — reminding you of your guilt, your failures, your unworthiness. But God says: "I rebuke you." Your defender is stronger than your accuser. When condemnation rises, appeal to the Judge who already ruled in your favor.
  • 2. Your filth is not the final word (vv.3-4). Joshua stood in excrement-stained garments — and was NOT rejected. God removed the filth and clothed him in purity. No matter how stained you feel, God's grace is greater than your sin. He does not just forgive — He RE-CLOTHES.
  • 3. Justification is God's work, not yours (v.4). Joshua did not clean himself. He did not earn the new garments. He simply STOOD THERE while God did the work. Salvation is received, not achieved. Stop trying to wash your own filthy garments. Let God dress you in His righteousness.
  • 4. "In one day" (v.9). The complete removal of sin happened in ONE day — the day Christ died. Not a process but an event. Not gradual improvement but decisive atonement. If you are in Christ, your iniquity was removed — completely, finally, irrevocably — in one day on Calvary.
  • 5. The Branch grows from lowly places (v.8). The Messiah comes not as a mighty oak but as a small shoot. God's greatest work often begins in the smallest, humblest circumstances. Don't despise your small beginnings — the Branch is growing.
