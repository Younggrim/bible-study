Malachi — Chapter 2

1“And now, O priests, this command is for you.

2If you will not listen, if you will not take it to heart to give honor to my name, says the LORD of hosts, then I will send the curse upon you and I will curse your blessings. Indeed, I have already cursed them, because you do not lay it to heart.

3Behold, I will rebuke your offspring, and spread dung on your faces, the dung of your offerings, and you shall be taken away with it.

4So shall you know that I have sent this command to you, that my covenant with Levi may stand, says the LORD of hosts.

5My covenant with him was one of life and peace, and I gave them to him. It was a covenant of fear, and he feared me. He stood in awe of my name.

6True instruction was in his mouth, and no wrong was found on his lips. He walked with me in peace and uprightness, and he turned many from iniquity.

7For the lips of a priest should guard knowledge, and people should seek instruction from his mouth, for he is the messenger of the LORD of hosts.

8But you have turned aside from the way. You have caused many to stumble by your instruction. You have corrupted the covenant of Levi, says the LORD of hosts,

9and so I make you despised and abased before all the people, inasmuch as you do not keep my ways but show partiality in your instruction.”

10Have we not all one Father? Has not one God created us? Why then are we faithless to one another, profaning the covenant of our fathers?

11Judah has been faithless, and abomination has been committed in Israel and in Jerusalem. For Judah has profaned the sanctuary of the LORD, which he loves, and has married the daughter of a foreign god.

12May the LORD cut off from the tents of Jacob any descendant of the man who does this, who brings an offering to the LORD of hosts!

13And this second thing you do. You cover the LORD’s altar with tears, with weeping and groaning because he no longer regards the offering or accepts it with favor from your hand.

14But you say, “Why does he not?” Because the LORD was witness between you and the wife of your youth, to whom you have been faithless, though she is your companion and your wife by covenant.

15Did he not make them one, with a portion of the Spirit in their union? And what was the one God seeking? Godly offspring. So guard yourselves in your spirit, and let none of you be faithless to the wife of your youth.

16“For the man who does not love his wife but divorces her, says the LORD, the God of Israel, covers his garment with violence, says the LORD of hosts. So guard yourselves in your spirit, and do not be faithless.”

17You have wearied the LORD with your words. But you say, “How have we wearied him?” By saying, “Everyone who does evil is good in the sight of the LORD, and he delights in them.” Or by asking, “Where is the God of justice?”

Summary

God rebukes faithless marriages and divorce — "I hate divorce" — and warns that the priests have corrupted the covenant of Levi by showing partiality in instruction and breaking faith with the wife of their youth.

Key Verses

v.2
"I will even send a curse upon you, and I will curse your blessings: yea, I have cursed them already" (KJV) — A terrifying reversal: the priests who pronounce blessings will have their own blessings CURSED. God turns the priestly function upside-down. Already done — "I have cursed them already." Past tense. The consequences are already in motion.
v.6
"The law of truth was in his mouth, and iniquity was not found in his lips: he walked with me in peace and equity, and did turn many away from iniquity" (KJV) — The portrait of the faithful priest. Four marks: (1) truth in his teaching, (2) integrity in his speech, (3) peace in his walk with God, (4) turning others FROM sin. This is the standard — and the current priests have failed at every point.
v.7
"the priest's lips should keep knowledge, and they should seek the law at his mouth: for he is the messenger of the LORD of hosts" (KJV) — The priest's primary responsibility: to GUARD knowledge and TEACH truth. People have the right to seek instruction from their spiritual leaders. When leaders fail this duty, people stumble.
v.14
"the LORD hath been witness between thee and the wife of thy youth, against whom thou hast dealt treacherously: yet is she thy companion, and the wife of thy covenant" (KJV) — God is the WITNESS to every marriage covenant. He was present. He remembers. The wife of your youth is your "companion" (Hebrew "chaberah" = partner, friend) and your "wife of covenant." Treachery against her is witnessed by God.
v.16
"the LORD, the God of Israel, saith that he hateth putting away" (KJV) / "the man who does not love his wife but divorces her... covers his garment with violence" (ESV) — God HATES divorce. Not because He condemns the divorced (He extends grace) but because divorce is an act of violence — a breaking of covenant that tears what God joined. It is "covering violence with a garment" — aggression dressed up in legal procedure.
v.17
"Every one that doeth evil is good in the sight of the LORD, and he delighteth in them" (KJV) — The people's moral inversion. They accused God of being unjust by REWARDING evil. This is the cynicism of a spiritually exhausted people who no longer believe in divine justice.

Cross-References

  • v.2 — Numbers 6:24-26 — The priestly blessing — now reversed into a curse because of unfaithful priests. v.6 — Deuteronomy 33:8-11 — The blessing of Levi: "They shall teach Jacob thy judgments, and Israel thy law." v.7 — 2 Timothy 2:15 — "Study to shew thyself approved... rightly dividing the word of truth." v.10 — Ephesians 4:4-6 — "One God and Father of all" — the basis for treating one another faithfully. v.14 — Proverbs 5:18 — "Rejoice with the wife of thy youth." v.14 — Ephesians 5:25 — "Husbands, love your wives, even as Christ also loved the church." v.16 — Matthew 19:6 — "What therefore God hath joined together, let not man put asunder." v.16 — Genesis 2:24 — "They shall be one flesh" — marriage as divine union.

Translations

  • v.3 — "spread dung upon your faces" — Hebrew "peresh" = offal, dung from sacrificed animals. Normally disposed of OUTSIDE the camp (Exod 29:14). God says He will smear it on the priests' faces — the utmost humiliation. Their "holy" festivals are actually DUNG to God.
  • v.7 — "messenger" — Hebrew "mal'ak" = messenger, angel. The same word as "Malachi" and as the messenger of 3:1. The priest IS a messenger of God — that is his fundamental identity and responsibility.
  • v.14 — "companion" — Hebrew "chaberah" = partner, associate, friend. From the root "chaber" = to be joined. Marriage is not ownership but PARTNERSHIP. The wife is friend, equal partner, and covenant companion.
  • v.16 — "putting away" (KJV) — Hebrew "shalach" = to send away, to divorce. The KJV "he hateth putting away" renders the Hebrew "sane shalach" = hates divorce/sending away. The ESV restructures but preserves the meaning: divorce covers violence with a garment.
  • v.16 — "covereth violence with his garment" — The garment may allude to Ruth 3:9, where spreading a garment over someone means taking responsibility for them. Divorce is the OPPOSITE: throwing off the garment of protection and covering yourself in violence instead.

Reflection

  • 1. Leaders bear greater responsibility (vv.1-9). The priests' sin wasn't just personal — it caused others to STUMBLE. If you teach, lead, or influence others spiritually, the standard is higher. Your failure doesn't just affect you — it trips up those who follow you. Handle truth carefully.
  • 2. "The law of truth was in his mouth" (v.6). The faithful priest spoke TRUTH, walked in PEACE, and turned people FROM sin. This is the three-fold calling of every spiritual mentor: accurate teaching, consistent living, and redemptive influence. Which of these areas needs attention in your life?
  • 3. Marriage is a COVENANT, and God is the witness (v.14). Your wedding was not just a ceremony between you and your spouse — God was present as witness. He remembers the vows. Treat your spouse as a covenant partner, not a convenience. Guard your spirit against treachery.
  • 4. God hates divorce because it is violence (v.16). This is not condemnation of those who have experienced divorce — God extends grace to the broken. But it IS a call to fight for your marriage, to refuse treachery, and to "guard your spirit." Divorce often begins with an unguarded spirit that allows bitterness, lust, or indifference to grow.
  • 5. Moral relativism wearies God (v.17). When we call evil good or claim God doesn't care about justice, we weary Him. God IS a God of judgment. He IS coming. Don't mistake His patience for indifference. Justice delayed is not justice denied.
