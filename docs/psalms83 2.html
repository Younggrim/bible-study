Summary

A communal lament — Asaph lists a coalition of nations conspiring to destroy Israel, then prays God would scatter them as He did Midian and Sisera, that they may know the LORD is Most High over all the earth.

Key Verses

  • v.1 — "Keep not thou silence, O God: hold not thy peace, and be not still, O God." (KJV) — Triple plea: don't be silent, don't be peaceful, don't be still. When enemies rage, God's silence feels unbearable. The prayer is for God to SPEAK and ACT.
  • v.4 — "They have said, Come, and let us cut them off from being a nation; that the name of Israel may be no more in remembrance." (KJV) — Genocidal intent. Not merely defeat but annihilation — erasure from history. This has been attempted repeatedly throughout history and has never succeeded.
  • v.16-18 — "Fill their faces with shame; that they may seek thy name, O LORD... That men may know that thou, whose name alone is JEHOVAH, art the most high over all the earth." (KJV) — The remarkable purpose: even the imprecation aims at CONVERSION ("that they may seek thy name") and universal RECOGNITION of God's supremacy. Judgment serves evangelistic purpose.

Cross-References

  • v.4 → Esther 3:6 — Haman's plot to destroy all Jews. Same genocidal intent.
  • v.9 → Judges 4-5 — Sisera and Jabin defeated by Deborah and Barak.
  • v.9 → Judges 7-8 — Midianite defeat (Oreb, Zeeb, Zebah, Zalmunna) through Gideon.
  • v.13 → Isaiah 17:13 — "The nations shall rush like the rushing of many waters: but God shall rebuke them, and they shall flee far off."
  • v.18 → Isaiah 45:6 — "That they may know from the rising of the sun, and from the west, that there is none beside me."
  • v.18 → Philippians 2:10-11 — "Every knee should bow... every tongue confess that Jesus Christ is Lord."

Translations

  • v.13 → KJV: "make them like a wheel" / ESV: "make them like whirling dust" — Hebrew "galgal" = wheel, whirling thing, tumbleweed. ESV's "whirling dust" captures the image better: dry, weightless, driven by wind. They have no substance or stability.
  • v.18 → KJV: "whose name alone is JEHOVAH" / ESV: "whose name is the LORD" — The only use of "JEHOVAH" in the KJV (most places render YHWH as "LORD" in capitals). The divine name is emphasized: God's personal covenant name distinguishes Him from all so-called gods.

Reflection

  • 1. God's silence does not mean God's absence (v.1). When enemies rage and God seems quiet, He is not uninvolved. Sometimes He is silent while He prepares His response. Ask Him to break silence — but trust Him even in the quiet.
  • 2. Enemy coalitions against God's people always fail (vv.4-8). Throughout history, alliances formed to destroy God's people have ultimately failed. No coalition can overpower God's purposes.
  • 3. Even judgment has a redemptive purpose (vv.16-18). "That they may seek thy name." The goal of prayer against enemies is not merely their destruction but their recognition of God. Biblical imprecation aims at glory, not revenge.
  • 4. History provides precedent for faith (vv.9-12). What God did to Midian and Sisera, He can do again. Past victories are not just memories — they are promises of future intervention.
