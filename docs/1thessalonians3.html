1 Thessalonians — Chapter 3

1Therefore when we could bear it no longer, we were willing to be left behind at Athens alone,

2and we sent Timothy, our brother and God’s coworker in the gospel of Christ, to establish and exhort you in your faith,

3that no one be moved by these afflictions. For you yourselves know that we are destined for this.

4For when we were with you, we kept telling you beforehand that we were to suffer affliction, just as it has come to pass, and just as you know.

5For this reason, when I could bear it no longer, I sent to learn about your faith, for fear that somehow the tempter had tempted you and our labor would be in vain.

6But now that Timothy has come to us from you, and has brought us the good news of your faith and love and reported that you always remember us kindly and long to see us, as we long to see you—

7for this reason, brothers, in all our distress and affliction we have been comforted about you through your faith.

8For now we live, if you are standing fast in the Lord.

9For what thanksgiving can we return to God for you, for all the joy that we feel for your sake before our God,

10as we pray most earnestly night and day that we may see you face to face and supply what is lacking in your faith?

11Now may our God and Father himself, and our Lord Jesus, direct our way to you,

12and may the Lord make you increase and abound in love for one another and for all, as we do for you,

13so that he may establish your hearts blameless in holiness before our God and Father, at the coming of our Lord Jesus with all his saints.

Summary

Paul sent Timothy to strengthen the Thessalonians during persecution, rejoices at Timothy's good report of their faith, and prays that the Lord would cause them to increase and abound in love for one another and for all.

Key Verses

  • v.3 — "That no one be moved by these afflictions. For you yourselves know that we are destined for this." (ESV) — Suffering is appointed, not accidental.
  • v.8 — "For now we live, if you are standing fast in the Lord." (ESV) — A pastor's life is bound to his people's faithfulness.
  • v.12 — "May the Lord make you increase and abound in love for one another and for all." (ESV) — Love that grows and overflows.
  • v.13 — "So that he may establish your hearts blameless in holiness before our God and Father, at the coming of our Lord Jesus with all his saints." (ESV) — Holiness in view of Christ's return.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): "https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/1-thessalonians-3/" target="_blank">enduringword.com Guzik covers Paul's anxiety, Timothy's mission, the good report, and Paul's prayer for love and holiness.
  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "Appointed to Affliction" on v.3 (MTP Vol. 20, No. 1185) "We are appointed thereunto. Affliction is not an accident — it is an appointment. God has scheduled your trials as surely as He has scheduled your blessings. The same hand that writes your joys writes your sorrows. Knowing this changes everything: you are not a victim of random misfortune but a child under a Father's wise discipline."

Translations

  • v.1 → KJV: "could no longer forbear" / ESV: "could bear it no longer" — Greek "mēketi stegontes" — no longer able to contain ourselves, unable to bear it any longer. The word "stegō" means to cover, contain, endure. Paul's concern was bursting out.
  • v.2 → KJV: "establish" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "stērixai" — to strengthen, make firm, set solidly. "Comfort" — Greek "parakalesai" — to encourage, come alongside. Timothy's dual mission: strengthen and encourage.
  • v.3 → KJV: "moved" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "sainesthai" — to be shaken, disturbed, swayed. Originally meant "to wag the tail" (of a dog being flattered). Don't be swayed by persecution.
  • v.5 → KJV: "the tempter" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "ho peirazōn" — the one who tests/tempts. Satan's specific role: using suffering to tempt believers to abandon faith.
  • v.10 → KJV: "perfect that which is lacking" / ESV: "supply what is lacking" — Greek "katartisai ta husterēmata" — to mend, complete, supply what is deficient. Their faith was genuine but incomplete — they needed further instruction.
  • v.13 → KJV: "with all his saints" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "hagiōn" — holy ones. Could refer to: (1) believers who have died, (2) angels, or (3) both. Christ returns accompanied by His holy ones.
  • ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
  • v.2 → NET: "we sent Timothy... to strengthen and encourage you about your faith" — "strengthen" (stErixai = establish/ make firm). WEB: "to establish you and to comfort you." KJV: "to establish you, and to comfort you." Two pastoral verbs: stabilize and encourage.
  • v.5 → NET: "I sent to find out about your faith, for fear that the tempter somehow tempted you" — "the tempter" (ho peirazOn = the one who tests/tempts). WEB: "the tempter had tempted you." KJV: "the tempter have tempted you." Satan has a specific role: testing faith through suffering.
  • v.8 → NET: "For now we are alive again, if you stand firm in the Lord" — "alive again" (zOmen = we live). WEB: "we live, if you stand firm in the Lord." Their faithfulness gives Paul life — pastoral joy is tied to converts' perseverance.
  • v.10 → NET: "as we pray earnestly night and day to see you in person and to supply what is lacking in your faith" — "supply what is lacking" (katartisai ta hysterEmata = complete the deficiencies/mend the gaps). WEB: "perfect that which is lacking." Faith has gaps that need filling through ongoing teaching.
  • v.12 → NET: "may the Lord cause you to increase and overflow with love for one another" — "overflow" (perisseuai = abound/ exceed). WEB: "abound in love toward one another." KJV: "abound in love one toward another." Love should exceed normal bounds — not just adequate but overflowing.

Reflection

  • 1. "We are appointed thereunto" (v.3). Suffering is not accidental — it's appointed. How does knowing this change your response to trials? Does it bring comfort or raise questions?
  • 2. "Now we live, if ye stand fast" (v.8). Paul's joy was tied to their faithfulness. Whose spiritual growth brings you joy? Whose faithfulness gives you life? Are you investing in others at that level?
  • 3. "Increase and abound in love" (v.12). Love should be growing — not static. Is your love increasing? Toward fellow believers? Toward "all men" — including difficult people and outsiders?
  • 4. "Unblameable in holiness... at the coming of our Lord" (v.13). If Christ returned today, would your heart be found "unblameable in holiness"? What needs to change to be ready?
