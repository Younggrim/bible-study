Summary

A worship call and warning — 'O come, let us sing unto the LORD; let us kneel before our Maker'; but 'harden not your heart, as in the provocation' at Meribah — quoted extensively in Hebrews 3-4.

Key Verses

v.6
"O come, let us worship and bow down: let us kneel before the LORD our maker." (KJV) — Three physical postures: worship (prostrate), bow down (bend), kneel (knee to ground). The body expresses what the heart believes. True worship involves the whole person — body and soul.
v.7-8
"To day if ye will hear his voice, Harden not your heart, as in the provocation." (KJV) — The psalm's pivot. TODAY — urgency. IF you hear — opportunity. Harden NOT — choice. The warning: Israel heard God's voice at Meribah and HARDENED their hearts. Hearing without responding produces hardness.
v.10-11
"Forty years long was I grieved with this generation... Unto whom I sware in my wrath that they should not enter into my rest." (KJV) — The consequence of hardness: God was "grieved" (Hebrew "qut" = loathed, was disgusted). The entire wilderness generation forfeited God's rest. Unbelief costs EVERYTHING.

Cross-References

  • v.1 — Psalm 100:1 — "Make a joyful noise unto the LORD." Same exuberant worship call. v.7 — Hebrews 3:7-4:11 — The extended commentary on this psalm, warning Christians against the same unbelief. v.7 — 2 Corinthians 6:2 — "Now is the accepted time; behold, now is the day of salvation." Same "today" urgency. v.8 — Exodus 17:1-7 — Meribah/Massah: Israel testing God at the waters of strife. v.11 — Hebrews 4:1-3 — "Let us therefore fear, lest, a promise being left us of entering into his rest, any of you should seem to come short of it." v.11 — Numbers 14:22-23 — "Those men which have seen my glory... and have tempted me now these ten times... they shall not see the land."

Translations

  • v.7 — KJV: "To day if ye will hear his voice" / ESV: "Today, if you hear his voice" — The Hebrew could be conditional ("if you hear") or temporal ("when you hear"). Either way: the moment of hearing demands immediate response. Don't delay.
  • v.8 — KJV: "as in the provocation" / ESV: "as at Meribah" — KJV translates the place name (Meribah = provocation/strife; Massah = temptation/testing). ESV retains the Hebrew names. Both refer to Exodus 17 where Israel quarreled with God at the water.
  • v.11 — KJV: "my rest" / ESV: "my rest" — In the original context, "rest" meant the Promised Land (Canaan). Hebrews reinterprets it as God's eternal Sabbath-rest still available to believers. Multiple layers of meaning.

Reflection

  • 1. Worship should be joyful AND reverent (vv.1-2, 6). Singing AND kneeling. Noise AND prostration. Biblical worship is both exuberant and humble — never merely one without the other.
  • 2. TODAY is the day of decision (v.7). Not tomorrow. Not when conditions improve. Not when you "feel ready." Today. Every delay is a step toward hardness. Respond now.
  • 3. Hardness happens through hearing without obeying (v.8). The Israelites HEARD God — they were not ignorant. They hardened because they heard and REFUSED. Repeated hearing without response produces spiritual calcification.
  • 4. Unbelief forfeits God's rest (v.11). The wilderness generation lost Canaan not because of inability but unbelief. God's rest is still available — but it can be forfeited. Don't assume you'll get another chance tomorrow.
