Jonah — Chapter 2

1Then Jonah prayed to the LORD his God from the belly of the fish,

2saying, “I called out to the LORD, out of my distress, and he answered me; out of the belly of Sheol I cried, and you heard my voice.

3For you cast me into the deep, into the heart of the seas, and the flood surrounded me; all your breakers and your waves passed over me.

4Then I said, ‘I am driven away from your sight; yet I shall again look upon your holy temple.’

5The waters closed in over me to take my life; the deep surrounded me; weeds were wrapped about my head.

6To the roots of the mountains I went down, to the land whose bars closed upon me forever. Yet you brought up my life from the pit, O LORD my God.

7When my life was fainting away, I remembered the LORD, and my prayer came to you, into your holy temple.

8Those who pay regard to vain idols forsake their hope of steadfast love.

9But I with the voice of thanksgiving will sacrifice to you; what I have vowed I will pay. Salvation belongs to the LORD!”

10And the LORD spoke to the fish, and it vomited Jonah out upon the dry land.

Summary

From inside the fish, Jonah prays a psalm of thanksgiving for deliverance — acknowledging that salvation belongs to the LORD — and God commands the fish to vomit him onto dry land.

Key Verses

v.2
"out of the belly of Sheol I cried, and you heard my voice" (ESV) — "Sheol" = the grave/realm of the dead. Jonah was as good as dead — and God HEARD him even THERE. There is no depth from which God cannot hear your cry.
v.4
"I am driven away from your sight; yet I shall again look upon your holy temple" (ESV) — In his lowest moment, Jonah chooses FAITH: "Yet I will look." Though he feels cast out, he turns his face toward God's dwelling. Faith is choosing to look GOD-ward when everything says God is absent.
v.6
"Yet you brought up my life from the pit, O LORD my God" (ESV) — Past tense: God HAS delivered. Even before leaving the fish, Jonah declares deliverance as accomplished fact. Faith speaks of rescue before it is fully manifest.
v.7
"When my life was fainting away, I remembered the LORD, and my prayer came to you, into your holy temple" (ESV) — At the point of fainting/giving up, he REMEMBERED God. Memory of God is the turning point in despair. And his prayer REACHED the holy temple — distance (even inside a fish in the ocean) cannot block prayer.
v.8
"Those who pay regard to vain idols forsake their hope of steadfast love" (ESV) — Those who cling to worthless idols ("vain idols") abandon the mercy that could be theirs. This verse — from JONAH — is ironic: he will later prove unwilling to extend that very mercy to Nineveh.
v.9
"Salvation belongs to the LORD!" (ESV) — The theological center of Jonah. Three words that summarize everything: SALVATION belongs to, originates from, and is controlled by THE LORD. Not by human effort, not by human worthiness, not by human decision about who deserves it. God saves whom He will save.

Translations

  • v.2 — "belly of hell" (KJV) / "belly of Sheol" — Hebrew "beten she'ol." Sheol = the realm of the dead, the grave. Jonah equates the fish's belly with death itself. He was effectively dead and God raised him.
  • v.5 — "waters compassed me about, even to the soul" — Hebrew "nefesh" = soul/life/breath. The waters reached the point of extinguishing life itself.
  • v.6 — "bottoms of the mountains" — Hebrew "qitsbei harim" = the roots of the mountains (underwater mountain bases at the ocean floor). Jonah descended to the deepest possible point.
  • v.8 — "lying vanities" — Hebrew "havlei shav" = empty/worthless delusions. Includes idols and all false sources of hope. "Forsake their own mercy" = they ABANDON the grace (hesed) that could be theirs.
  • v.10 — "the LORD spake unto the fish" — God commands nature. The fish obeys immediately. Creation responds to God's word more readily than His prophet does.

Reflection

  • 1. You can pray from the belly of anything (v.1). Inside a fish, inside addiction, inside consequences, inside rock bottom — PRAYER still works. No location disqualifies your cry. God hears from the belly of hell.
  • 2. "Yet I will look toward thy holy temple" (v.4). In your darkest moment, CHOOSE faith. Feelings say "cast out." Faith says "yet I will look." The "yet" of faith overrides the evidence of circumstances.
  • 3. "Salvation is of the LORD" (v.9). Not of you. Not of your effort, your worthiness, your timing, or your theology. Of the LORD. Period. This humbles the self-righteous and encourages the hopeless simultaneously.
  • 4. The fish obeyed God faster than Jonah (v.10). Animals respond to God's command instantly. The prophet needed a near-death experience. How much does God have to put you through before you simply obey?
  • 5. "They that observe lying vanities forsake their own mercy" (v.8). Every idol you cling to is a mercy you're forfeiting. What "lying vanities" are you holding that are costing you God's mercy? Let them go.
