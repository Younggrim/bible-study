Summary

A Song of Ascents — a lament from one dwelling among hostile liars and warmongers: 'Woe is me, that I sojourn in Mesech'; the psalmist is for peace, but they are for war.

Key Verses

v.1
"In my distress I cried unto the LORD, and he heard me." (KJV) — The journey to God begins with distress and crying out. The pilgrim's first step is not strength but weakness — not arrival but longing.
v.5
"Woe is me, that I sojourn in Mesech, that I dwell in the tents of Kedar!" (KJV) — Meshech (far north) and Kedar (far south/east) represent opposite ends of the known world — both hostile, pagan territories. The point: living far from God's people, surrounded by those who oppose peace.
v.7
"I am for peace: but when I speak, they are for war." (KJV) — The frustration of the peacemaker among warmongers. Every attempt at peace is met with aggression. This is the experience of God's people living in a hostile world.

Cross-References

  • v.1 — Psalm 18:6 — "In my distress I called upon the LORD." v.2 — Psalm 52:2-4 — The deceitful tongue as a weapon. v.4 — Proverbs 25:18 — "A man that beareth false witness... is a sharp arrow." v.5 — Genesis 25:13 — Kedar was Ishmael's son; represents Arab/nomadic hostility. v.7 — Matthew 5:9 — "Blessed are the peacemakers." v.7 — Romans 12:18 — "If it be possible... live peaceably with all men."

Translations

  • v.4 — "Sharp arrows of the mighty, with coals of juniper" — Hebrew "rethem" = broom plant (not juniper). Broom coals burn extremely hot and long. The punishment fits the crime: the false tongue (which burns like fire) receives burning coals; the sharp words receive sharp arrows.
  • v.5 — "Mesech" (Meshech) = a people near the Black Sea region (Gen 10:2). "Kedar" = Arabian desert tribe. Both represent "far from Jerusalem" and "far from peace." The psalmist may not literally dwell in both — they represent the hostility of his environment.

Reflection

  • 1. The journey to God begins in distress (v.1). You don't start the pilgrimage from a place of comfort — you start because where you are is unbearable. Dissatisfaction with the present drives you toward God's presence.
  • 2. Deceitful speech wounds deeply (v.2). Living among liars and slanderers is soul-destroying. The tongue's damage is compared to arrows and burning coals. Guard against both being the target AND being the source.
  • 3. You may be for peace while others are for war (v.7). Being a peacemaker doesn't guarantee peace. Some people are committed to conflict. Your role is to BE peace, not to guarantee others will receive it.
  • 4. Not every environment is where you belong (v.5). If you feel like a stranger — surrounded by values, speech, and actions contrary to God — that discomfort is right. You're on pilgrimage. You haven't arrived yet.
