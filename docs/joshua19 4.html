Joshua — Chapter 19

1The second lot came out for Simeon, for the tribe of the people of Simeon, according to their clans, and their inheritance was in the midst of the inheritance of the people of Judah.

2And they had for their inheritance Beersheba, Sheba, Moladah,

3Hazar-shual, Balah, Ezem,

4Eltolad, Bethul, Hormah,

5Ziklag, Beth-marcaboth, Hazar-susah,

6Beth-lebaoth, and Sharuhen—thirteen cities with their villages;

7Ain, Rimmon, Ether, and Ashan—four cities with their villages,

8together with all the villages around these cities as far as Baalath-beer, Ramah of the Negeb. This was the inheritance of the tribe of the people of Simeon according to their clans.

9The inheritance of the people of Simeon formed part of the territory of the people of Judah. Because the portion of the people of Judah was too large for them, the people of Simeon obtained an inheritance in the midst of their inheritance.

10The third lot came up for the people of Zebulun, according to their clans. And the territory of their inheritance reached as far as Sarid.

11Then their boundary goes up westward and on to Mareal and touches Dabbesheth, then the brook that is east of Jokneam.

12From Sarid it goes in the other direction eastward toward the sunrise to the boundary of Chisloth-tabor. From there it goes to Daberath, then up to Japhia.

13From there it passes along on the east toward the sunrise to Gath-hepher, to Eth-kazin, and going on to Rimmon it bends toward Neah,

14then on the north the boundary turns about to Hannathon, and it ends at the Valley of Iphtahel;

15and Kattath, Nahalal, Shimron, Idalah, and Bethlehem—twelve cities with their villages.

16This is the inheritance of the people of Zebulun, according to their clans—these cities with their villages.

17The fourth lot came out for Issachar, for the people of Issachar, according to their clans.

18Their territory included Jezreel, Chesulloth, Shunem,

19Hapharaim, Shion, Anaharath,

20Rabbith, Kishion, Ebez,

21Remeth, En-gannim, En-haddah, Beth-pazzez.

22The boundary also touches Tabor, Shahazumah, and Beth-shemesh, and its boundary ends at the Jordan—sixteen cities with their villages.

23This is the inheritance of the tribe of the people of Issachar, according to their clans—the cities with their villages.

24The fifth lot came out for the tribe of the people of Asher according to their clans.

25Their territory included Helkath, Hali, Beten, Achshaph,

26Allammelech, Amad, and Mishal. On the west it touches Carmel and Shihor-libnath,

27then it turns eastward, it goes to Beth-dagon, and touches Zebulun and the Valley of Iphtahel northward to Beth-emek and Neiel. Then it continues in the north to Cabul,

28Ebron, Rehob, Hammon, Kanah, as far as Sidon the Great.

29Then the boundary turns to Ramah, reaching to the fortified city of Tyre. Then the boundary turns to Hosah, and it ends at the sea; Mahalab, Achzib,

30Ummah, Aphek and Rehob—twenty-two cities with their villages.

31This is the inheritance of the tribe of the people of Asher according to their clans—these cities with their villages.

32The sixth lot came out for the people of Naphtali, for the people of Naphtali, according to their clans.

33And their boundary ran from Heleph, from the oak in Zaanannim, and Adami-nekeb, and Jabneel, as far as Lakkum, and it ended at the Jordan.

34Then the boundary turns westward to Aznoth-tabor and goes from there to Hukkok, touching Zebulun at the south and Asher on the west and Judah on the east at the Jordan.

35The fortified cities are Ziddim, Zer, Hammath, Rakkath, Chinnereth,

36Adamah, Ramah, Hazor,

37Kedesh, Edrei, En-hazor,

38Yiron, Migdal-el, Horem, Beth-anath, and Beth-shemesh—nineteen cities with their villages.

39This is the inheritance of the tribe of the people of Naphtali according to their clans—the cities with their villages.

40The seventh lot came out for the tribe of the people of Dan, according to their clans.

41And the territory of its inheritance included Zorah, Eshtaol, Ir-shemesh,

42Shaalabbin, Aijalon, Ithlah,

43Elon, Timnah, Ekron,

44Eltekeh, Gibbethon, Baalath,

45Jehud, Bene-berak, Gath-rimmon,

46and Me-jarkon and Rakkon with the territory over against Joppa.

47When the territory of the people of Dan was lost to them, the people of Dan went up and fought against Leshem, and after capturing it and striking it with the sword they took possession of it and settled in it, calling Leshem, Dan, after the name of Dan their ancestor.

48This is the inheritance of the tribe of the people of Dan, according to their clans—these cities with their villages.

49When they had finished distributing the several territories of the land as inheritances, the people of Israel gave an inheritance among them to Joshua the son of Nun.

50By command of the LORD they gave him the city that he asked, Timnath-serah in the hill country of Ephraim. And he rebuilt the city and settled in it.

51These are the inheritances that Eleazar the priest and Joshua the son of Nun and the heads of the fathers’ houses of the tribes of the people of Israel distributed by lot at Shiloh before the LORD, at the entrance of the tent of meeting. So they finished dividing the land.

Summary

The remaining tribes—Simeon, Zebulun, Issachar, Asher, Naphtali, and Dan—receive their allotments by lot. Joshua himself is given Timnath-serah in Ephraim as his personal inheritance.

Key Verses

v.9
"Out of the portion of the children of Judah was the inheritance of the children of Simeon: for the part of the children of Judah was too much for them." (ESV) — God's providence: Judah's excess becomes Simeon's provision. What is too much for one becomes exactly enough for another. God wastes nothing in His economy.
v.47
"And the coast of the children of Dan went out too little for them: therefore the children of Dan went up to fight against Leshem, and took it." (ESV) — Dan's migration. Rather than fighting for their allotted territory (which was difficult due to Philistine pressure), they abandoned it and conquered an easier target far to the north. This is not commended — it represents failure to trust God for the assigned inheritance.
v.49
"When they had made an end of dividing the land for inheritance by their coasts, the children of Israel gave an inheritance to Joshua the son of Nun among them." (ESV) — Joshua takes last. He who led the conquest, who could have claimed the best territory first, waits until every tribe is settled. Servant leadership in action.
v.50
"According to the word of the LORD they gave him the city which he asked, even Timnath-serah in mount Ephraim: and he built the city, and dwelt therein." (ESV) — Joshua asks for a city that needs rebuilding — not a ready-made inheritance but a project. His humility and work ethic are evident to the end.
v.51
"So they made an end of dividing the country." (ESV) — The land distribution is complete. What God promised to Abraham (Genesis 15:18-21), confirmed to Moses (Deuteronomy 1:8), and commanded Joshua to distribute (Joshua 1:6) is now accomplished. Every tribe has its inheritance.

Cross-References

  • v.1 — Genesis 49:7 — "I will divide them in Jacob, and scatter them in Israel" — Jacob's prophecy about Simeon (and Levi). v.1 — Genesis 34:25-30 — Simeon and Levi's violence at Shechem, prompting Jacob's curse. v.9 — Joshua 15:20-63 — Judah's full city list, from which Simeon's cities are drawn. v.13 — 2 Kings 14:25 — Gath-hepher: hometown of the prophet Jonah. v.15 — Bethlehem (of Zebulun) — Not the Bethlehem of Judah where Christ was born, but a different town in Galilee. v.28 — KJV "great Zidon" / ESV "Sidon the Great" — The ancient Phoenician city (Genesis 10:15). v.29 — "The strong city Tyre" — The great Phoenician port city. v.35 — Chinnereth — The Sea of Galilee region. v.47 — Judges 18:1-31 — The full account of Dan's migration north. v.47 — Judges 1:34 — "The Amorites forced the children of Dan into the mountain." v.50 — Joshua 24:30 — Joshua buried at Timnath-serah. v.50 — Judges 2:9 — Called "Timnath-heres" in Judges. v.51 — Genesis 15:18-21 — God's land promise to Abraham fulfilled.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/joshua-19/ Guzik highlights Joshua's humility in taking his inheritance last. He also notes that Dan's migration north was not an act of faith but of failure — they abandoned their God-given territory because it was too hard. The contrast between Dan (who fled difficulty) and Caleb (who embraced it) is stark.
  • Charles Spurgeon: "Joshua took his inheritance last. The general who led the armies, who could have claimed the choicest land, waited until every tribe was served. And what did he ask for? A city in ruins that he must rebuild with his own hands. This is the spirit of Christ — who came not to be served but to serve. The greatest leaders are the last to take and the first to give."

Translations

  • v.1 — KJV: "their inheritance was within the inheritance of the children of Judah" / ESV: "their inheritance was in the midst of the inheritance of the people of Judah" — Hebrew "b'tokh nachalat b'nei Yehudah." Simeon had no independent territory — their cities were embedded within Judah's borders. This is unique among the tribes.
  • v.9 — KJV: "the part of the children of Judah was too much for them" / ESV: "the portion of the people of Judah was too large for them" — Hebrew "rav mehem" = too much/great for them. Judah's territory was disproportionately large for their population, so Simeon was carved out of it. God's redistribution ensures equity.
  • v.15 — KJV: "Bethlehem" / ESV: "Bethlehem" — This is Bethlehem of Zebulun in Galilee (cf. Judges 12:8-10), NOT the Bethlehem of Judah where David was born and Christ was born. There were multiple towns with this name ("house of bread").
  • v.29 — KJV: "the strong city Tyre" / ESV: "the fortified city of Tyre" — Hebrew "ir mivtsar Tsor." Tyre was one of the greatest Phoenician cities, famous for its island fortress. Though within Asher's allotment, it was never conquered by Israel.
  • v.47 — KJV: "the coast of the children of Dan went out too little for them" / ESV: "When the territory of the people of Dan was lost to them" — Hebrew "yatsa me-hem g'vul b'nei Dan." This is translated very differently: KJV suggests Dan's territory was too small; ESV suggests they lost control of it. The Hebrew is ambiguous — literally "the border of Dan went out from them." ESV's reading (territory lost/taken from them) fits the context of Judges 1:34 where Amorites pressed Dan back.
  • v.50 — KJV: "Timnath-serah" / ESV: "Timnath-serah" — Hebrew "Timnat Serach." In Judges 2:9 it is called "Timnath-heres" (letters reversed). "Serah" may mean "portion remaining" or "abundance." "Heres" means "sun." Some suggest the name was reversed to avoid association with sun worship.

Reflection

  • 1. Joshua takes last (vv.49-50). The leader who could have claimed first choice waited until everyone else was served. This is Christ-like leadership: "The last shall be first" (Matthew 20:16). True authority serves rather than seizes.
  • 2. Dan's failure to possess (v.47). Rather than fighting for their God-given territory, Dan abandoned it for an easier conquest. We face the same temptation: when God's assignment is hard, we look for shortcuts. But the easy path is not always God's path. Stay where God planted you.
  • 3. Simeon within Judah (v.9). God's judgment on Simeon (Genesis 49:7) becomes God's provision — they receive cities within the strongest tribe. Even divine discipline is tempered with mercy. God scatters Simeon but places them under Judah's protection.
  • 4. The distribution is complete (v.51). Every tribe has its place. God's promises are fulfilled — not partially but completely. What He promised to Abraham, He delivered to Joshua's generation. God finishes what He starts.
  • 5. Rebuilding what is broken (v.50). Joshua's city needed rebuilding. He did not ask for a finished product but for a project. Sometimes God gives us broken things to rebuild — churches, relationships, communities. The inheritance may require work, but it is still a gift.
