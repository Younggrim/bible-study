Numbers — Chapter 2

1The LORD spoke to Moses and Aaron, saying,

2“The people of Israel shall camp each by his own standard, with the banners of their fathers’ houses. They shall camp facing the tent of meeting on every side.

3Those to camp on the east side toward the sunrise shall be of the standard of the camp of Judah by their companies, the chief of the people of Judah being Nahshon the son of Amminadab,

4his company as listed being 74,600.

5Those to camp next to him shall be the tribe of Issachar, the chief of the people of Issachar being Nethanel the son of Zuar,

6his company as listed being 54,400.

7Then the tribe of Zebulun, the chief of the people of Zebulun being Eliab the son of Helon,

8his company as listed being 57,400.

9All those listed of the camp of Judah, by their companies, were 186,400. They shall set out first on the march.

10“On the south side shall be the standard of the camp of Reuben by their companies, the chief of the people of Reuben being Elizur the son of Shedeur,

11his company as listed being 46,500.

12And those to camp next to him shall be the tribe of Simeon, the chief of the people of Simeon being Shelumiel the son of Zurishaddai,

13his company as listed being 59,300.

14Then the tribe of Gad, the chief of the people of Gad being Eliasaph the son of Reuel,

15his company as listed being 45,650.

16All those listed of the camp of Reuben, by their companies, were 151,450. They shall set out second.

17“Then the tent of meeting shall set out, with the camp of the Levites in the midst of the camps; as they camp, so shall they set out, each in position, standard by standard.

18“On the west side shall be the standard of the camp of Ephraim by their companies, the chief of the people of Ephraim being Elishama the son of Ammihud,

19his company as listed being 40,500.

20And next to him shall be the tribe of Manasseh, the chief of the people of Manasseh being Gamaliel the son of Pedahzur,

21his company as listed being 32,200.

22Then the tribe of Benjamin, the chief of the people of Benjamin being Abidan the son of Gideoni,

23his company as listed being 35,400.

24All those listed of the camp of Ephraim, by their companies, were 108,100. They shall set out third on the march.

25“On the north side shall be the standard of the camp of Dan by their companies, the chief of the people of Dan being Ahiezer the son of Ammishaddai,

26his company as listed being 62,700.

27And those to camp next to him shall be the tribe of Asher, the chief of the people of Asher being Pagiel the son of Ochran,

28his company as listed being 41,500.

29Then the tribe of Naphtali, the chief of the people of Naphtali being Ahira the son of Enan,

30his company as listed being 53,400.

31All those listed of the camp of Dan were 157,600. They shall set out last, standard by standard.”

32These are the people of Israel as listed by their fathers’ houses. All those listed in the camps by their companies were 603,550.

33But the Levites were not listed among the people of Israel, as the LORD commanded Moses.

34Thus did the people of Israel. According to all that the LORD commanded Moses, so they camped by their standards, and so they set out, each one in his clan, according to his fathers’ house.

Summary

God arranges Israel's camp in precise order around the Tabernacle — three tribes on each side, with God at the center of His people.

Key Verses

v.2
"Every man of the children of Israel shall pitch by his own standard, with the ensign of their father's house: far off about the tabernacle of the congregation shall they pitch." — Order around God's presence. Everyone has an assigned place.
v.3
"On the east side toward the rising of the sun shall they of the standard of the camp of Judah pitch." — Judah in the place of honor. The royal tribe leads.
v.9
"These shall first set forth." — Judah leads the march. The Lion of Judah goes first.
v.17
"Then the tabernacle of the congregation shall set forward with the camp of the Levites in the midst of the camp." — God's presence at the center of everything.
v.34
"And the children of Israel did according to all that the LORD commanded Moses: so they pitched by their standards, and so they set forward." — Complete obedience to God's arrangement.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/numbers-2/ Guzik emphasizes the theological significance of the camp arrangement: God at the center, His people around Him in order. He notes that the arrangement teaches that God's presence should be central to all of life, and that God is a God of order, not chaos.
  • Charles Spurgeon: "See how God arranges His people! Not scattered, not confused, but each in his place, each under his banner, each facing the Tabernacle. God is in the center, and all revolves around Him. So it should be in your life — God at the center, everything else arranged around His presence. When God is displaced from the center, all falls into disorder."

Translations

  • v.2 — KJV: "by his own standard, with the ensign of their father's house" / ESV: "by his own standard, with the banners of their fathers' houses" — Hebrew "al diglo b'otot l'veit avotam." Two terms: "degel" (standard/banner for the larger division) and "ot" (sign/ensign for the individual family). KJV uses "standard" and "ensign"; ESV uses "standard" and "banners." Each tribal group had a large banner, and each family had its own identifying sign.
  • v.2 — KJV: "far off about the tabernacle" / ESV: "facing the tent of meeting on every side" — Hebrew "minneged" — at a distance, opposite, facing. The tribes camp at a respectful distance from the Tabernacle (with the Levites between them and it), yet facing toward it. KJV emphasizes the distance; ESV emphasizes the orientation.
  • v.9 — KJV: "These shall first set forth" / ESV: "They shall set out first on the march" — Hebrew "rishonah yissa'u" — first they shall journey. Judah breaks camp first and leads the entire procession.
  • v.14 — KJV: "Reuel" / ESV: "Reuel" — Note: In 1:14, the KJV has "Deuel." The Hebrew letters Resh (R) and Daleth (D) are very similar and easily confused in manuscripts. Most scholars consider "Deuel" the correct reading (as in 1:14; 7:42; 10:20).
  • v.17 — KJV: "the tabernacle of the congregation shall set forward" / ESV: "the tent of meeting shall set out" — The Tabernacle moves in the middle of the formation. When disassembled for travel, the Levites carry it between the second and third divisions.
  • v.31 — KJV: "They shall go hindmost" / ESV: "They shall set out last" — Hebrew "la-acharonah yissa'u" — last they shall journey. Dan's camp forms the rear guard, protecting the back of the procession.
  • v.34 — KJV: "so they pitched by their standards, and so they set forward" / ESV: "so they camped by their standards, and so they set out" — The same order applies to both camping and marching. God's arrangement is consistent whether at rest or in motion.

Reflection

  • 1. God at the center (v.17). The Tabernacle is in the middle of everything. Every Israelite, no matter which direction he looked from his tent, looked toward God's dwelling. Is God at the center of your life, or has He been pushed to the periphery?
  • 2. Everyone has a place (v.2). No one is without assignment. No one is left out. God has a specific place for every person in His community. Your place may not be the "east side" (the place of honor), but it is God-assigned and God-ordained.
  • 3. Judah leads (v.9). The tribe of praise goes first. "Judah" means "praise" (Genesis 29:35). When God's people move forward, praise leads the way. Before the battle, before the journey — praise. (See also 2 Chronicles 20:21-22.)
  • 4. Order, not chaos (v.34). God prescribes exact positions. He is not a God of confusion (1 Corinthians 14:33). This does not mean rigidity without life — it means purposeful arrangement. Freedom without order is chaos; order without freedom is death. God gives both.
  • 5. The camp as a picture of the Church. God dwelling in the midst of His people, each member in their place, each with a function, all oriented toward Him. "Where two or three are gathered together in my name, there am I in the midst of them" (Matthew 18:20).
