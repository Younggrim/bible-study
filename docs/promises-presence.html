Promises of God

God Is With You

The Promise

Of all God's promises, perhaps none is more intimate than His promise of presence. He does not merely act on our behalf from a distance — He walks with us, dwells within us, and promises never to leave or forsake us. This is the promise that transforms every circumstance: whether in joy or sorrow, triumph or trial, we are never alone.

God's presence was the distinguishing mark of Israel among the nations. Moses declared that without God's presence, he would not take another step. The tabernacle and temple were physical reminders that God had chosen to dwell among His people. In the New Covenant, this promise reaches its fullest expression: God's Spirit lives within every believer.

The promise of God's presence means that loneliness is a feeling, not a fact, for the Christian. Even in our darkest valleys, the Shepherd walks beside us. Even at our lowest points, underneath are the everlasting arms. His presence is not dependent on our feelings — it is anchored in His unchanging character and covenant faithfulness.

Key Scriptures

  • Matthew 28:20 — "Surely I am with you always, to the very end of the age."
  • Hebrews 13:5 — "Never will I leave you; never will I forsake you."
  • Psalm 139:7-10 — "Where can I go from your Spirit? Where can I flee from your presence?"
  • Deuteronomy 31:6 — "The LORD your God goes with you; he will never leave you nor forsake you."
  • Joshua 1:9 — "Be strong and courageous. Do not be afraid... for the LORD your God will be with you wherever you go."

Claiming the Promise

Experiencing God's presence requires intentional awareness. He is always with us, but we are not always conscious of Him. Spiritual disciplines like prayer, meditation on Scripture, and worship tune our hearts to recognize what is always true — that He is near. Brother Lawrence called this "practicing the presence of God" in every ordinary moment.

When we feel distant from God, the promise of His presence becomes our anchor. Feelings fluctuate, but His word stands firm. We claim this promise by faith, declaring what Scripture says over what our emotions suggest, and choosing to act as though God is present — because He truly is, regardless of how we feel.

Reflection Questions

  • When have you most powerfully experienced the reality of God's presence?
  • How do you respond when God feels distant — what anchors you to the truth of His nearness?
  • What daily practices help you stay aware of God's presence throughout the day?
  • How does the promise that God will never leave you affect the way you face uncertain situations?
