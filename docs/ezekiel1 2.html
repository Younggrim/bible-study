Ezekiel — Chapter 1

1In the thirtieth year, in the fourth month, on the fifth day of the month, as I was among the exiles by the Chebar canal, the heavens were opened, and I saw visions of God.

2On the fifth day of the month (it was the fifth year of the exile of King Jehoiachin),

3the word of the LORD came to Ezekiel the priest, the son of Buzi, in the land of the Chaldeans by the Chebar canal, and the hand of the LORD was upon him there.

4As I looked, behold, a stormy wind came out of the north, and a great cloud, with brightness around it, and fire flashing forth continually, and in the midst of the fire, as it were gleaming metal.

5And from the midst of it came the likeness of four living creatures. And this was their appearance: they had a human likeness,

6but each had four faces, and each of them had four wings.

7Their legs were straight, and the soles of their feet were like the sole of a calf’s foot. And they sparkled like burnished bronze.

8Under their wings on their four sides they had human hands. And the four had their faces and their wings thus:

9their wings touched one another. Each one of them went straight forward, without turning as they went.

10As for the likeness of their faces, each had a human face. The four had the face of a lion on the right side, the four had the face of an ox on the left side, and the four had the face of an eagle.

11Such were their faces. And their wings were spread out above. Each creature had two wings, each of which touched the wing of another, while two covered their bodies.

12And each went straight forward. Wherever the spirit would go, they went, without turning as they went.

13As for the likeness of the living creatures, their appearance was like burning coals of fire, like the appearance of torches moving to and fro among the living creatures. And the fire was bright, and out of the fire went forth lightning.

14And the living creatures darted to and fro, like the appearance of a flash of lightning.

15Now as I looked at the living creatures, I saw a wheel on the earth beside the living creatures, one for each of the four of them.

16As for the appearance of the wheels and their construction: their appearance was like the gleaming of beryl. And the four had the same likeness, their appearance and construction being as it were a wheel within a wheel.

17When they went, they went in any of their four directions without turning as they went.

18And their rims were tall and awesome, and the rims of all four were full of eyes all around.

19And when the living creatures went, the wheels went beside them; and when the living creatures rose from the earth, the wheels rose.

20Wherever the spirit wanted to go, they went, and the wheels rose along with them, for the spirit of the living creatures was in the wheels.

21When those went, these went; and when those stood, these stood; and when those rose from the earth, the wheels rose along with them, for the spirit of the living creatures was in the wheels.

22Over the heads of the living creatures there was the likeness of an expanse, shining like awe-inspiring crystal, spread out above their heads.

23And under the expanse their wings were stretched out straight, one toward another. And each creature had two wings covering its body.

24And when they went, I heard the sound of their wings like the sound of many waters, like the sound of the Almighty, a sound of tumult like the sound of an army. When they stood still, they let down their wings.

25And there came a voice from above the expanse over their heads. When they stood still, they let down their wings.

26And above the expanse over their heads there was the likeness of a throne, in appearance like sapphire; and seated above the likeness of a throne was a likeness with a human appearance.

27And upward from what had the appearance of his waist I saw as it were gleaming metal, like the appearance of fire enclosed all around. And downward from what had the appearance of his waist I saw as it were the appearance of fire, and there was brightness around him.

28Like the appearance of the bow that is in the cloud on the day of rain, so was the appearance of the brightness all around. Such was the appearance of the likeness of the glory of the LORD. And when I saw it, I fell on my face, and I heard the voice of one speaking.

Summary

The heavens open and Ezekiel receives the most elaborate theophany in Scripture. A storm from the north reveals four living creatures (each with four faces and four wings), whirling wheels within wheels covered with eyes, an expanse of crystal above them, and on the expanse a throne of sapphire with a figure like a man surrounded by fire and rainbow. "This was the appearance of the likeness of the glory of the LORD." Ezekiel falls on his face.

Key Verses

v.1
"the heavens were opened, and I saw visions of God" (KJV) — Heavens OPENED — in Babylon, not Jerusalem. God comes to where His people ARE, not where the temple WAS.
v.4
"And I looked, and, behold, a whirlwind came out of the north, a great cloud, and a fire infolding itself" (KJV) — The theophany arrives as STORM: wind, cloud, fire. God's approach is overwhelming, not gentle.
v.16
"their appearance and their work was as it were a wheel in the middle of a wheel" (KJV) — Wheels within wheels: the divine throne-chariot can move in ANY direction without turning. God is not limited to one direction or one destination. Omnidirectional sovereignty.
v.26
"And above the firmament... the likeness of a throne, as the appearance of a sapphire stone: and upon the likeness of the throne was the likeness as the appearance of a man above upon it" (KJV) — At the HIGHEST point: a throne. On the throne: a HUMAN form (the glory of God in human- like appearance). Layered with "likeness" and "appearance" — language straining to describe the indescribable.
v.28
"This was the appearance of the likeness of the glory of the LORD. And when I saw it, I fell upon my face" (KJV) — Not "the glory" directly but "the appearance of the likeness of the glory." Even this REDUCED representation causes Ezekiel to collapse. The only appropriate response to God's glory: facedown.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/ezekiel-1/
  • Charles Spurgeon: "Ezekiel saw God's throne IN BABYLON. Not in the temple, not in Jerusalem, not in the holy land — in EXILE. If God's glory can appear by a Babylonian canal, it can appear in your dungeon, your hospital room, your cubicle. The throne is MOBILE."

Translations

  • v.1 — "thirtieth year" (KJV/ESV) — Likely Ezekiel's AGE (30 = priestly ordination age). Instead of beginning temple service, he begins prophetic ministry. The priesthood he could never exercise is replaced by prophetic vision.
  • v.4 — "amber" (KJV) / "gleaming metal" (ESV) — Hebrew "chashmal" = a glowing metal alloy (electrum? bronze-gold mixture?). Something bright, molten, shimmering. Modern Hebrew uses "chashmal" for electricity.
  • v.5 — "four living creatures" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "chayyot" = living beings. Later identified as CHERUBIM (10:15,20). Not angels (malakhim) but throne-bearers — creatures whose existence is defined by proximity to God.
  • v.10 — "face of a man... face of a lion... face of an ox... face of an eagle" (KJV/ESV) — Four faces representing: humanity (intelligence), lion (strength/royalty), ox (service/labor), eagle (swiftness/transcendence). Traditionally connected to the four Gospels.
  • v.16 — "wheel in the middle of a wheel" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "ophan betokh ha-ophan." A gyroscopic arrangement allowing movement in any direction without turning. God's throne can go ANYWHERE instantly.
  • v.26 — "sapphire" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "sappir" = lapis lazuli or blue sapphire. The throne is BLUE — the color of heaven/ sky. The throne IS heaven materialized.

Reflection

  • 1. God appeared in BABYLON (v.1). Not in Jerusalem's temple, not on holy ground — by a canal in exile. If you think God can't meet you where you are right now (in your "exile"), think again. His throne is MOBILE.
  • 2. "I fell upon my face" (v.28). The only appropriate response to God's glory is facedown. Not theological analysis, not casual observation — PROSTRATION. When was the last time God's presence (in Scripture, worship, or life) put you on the floor?
  • 3. Wheels within wheels = God moves in any direction instantly (v.16). God is not stuck in one pattern, one approach, one direction. He can pivot instantly. Stop assuming God must work the same way He did last time.
  • 4. The appearance of the LIKENESS of the glory (v.28). Even this was overwhelming. Ezekiel didn't see God directly — he saw a representation of a representation of God's glory — and it was too much. How much MORE is the reality? Let that create appropriate awe.
  • 5. A priest without a temple receives a vision of God (vv.1-3). When God takes away your expected ministry (Ezekiel couldn't serve as priest), He may give you something GREATER. The loss of one calling opened a bigger one.
