Isaiah — Chapter 50

1Thus says the LORD: “Where is your mother’s certificate of divorce, with which I sent her away? Or which of my creditors is it to whom I have sold you? Behold, for your iniquities you were sold, and for your transgressions your mother was sent away.

2Why, when I came, was there no man; why, when I called, was there no one to answer? Is my hand shortened, that it cannot redeem? Or have I no power to deliver? Behold, by my rebuke I dry up the sea, I make the rivers a desert; their fish stink for lack of water and die of thirst.

3I clothe the heavens with blackness and make sackcloth their covering.”

4The Lord GOD has given me the tongue of those who are taught, that I may know how to sustain with a word him who is weary. Morning by morning he awakens; he awakens my ear to hear as those who are taught.

5The Lord GOD has opened my ear, and I was not rebellious; I turned not backward.

6I gave my back to those who strike, and my cheeks to those who pull out the beard; I hid not my face from disgrace and spitting.

7But the Lord GOD helps me; therefore I have not been disgraced; therefore I have set my face like a flint, and I know that I shall not be put to shame.

8He who vindicates me is near. Who will contend with me? Let us stand up together. Who is my adversary? Let him come near to me.

9Behold, the Lord GOD helps me; who will declare me guilty? Behold, all of them will wear out like a garment; the moth will eat them up.

10Who among you fears the LORD and obeys the voice of his servant? Let him who walks in darkness and has no light trust in the name of the LORD and rely on his God.

11Behold, all you who kindle a fire, who equip yourselves with burning torches! Walk by the light of your fire, and by the torches that you have kindled! This you have from my hand: you shall lie down in torment.

Summary

The third Servant Song — the Servant has "the tongue of the learned" to sustain the weary, offers His back to smiters and His cheeks to beard-pluckers, sets His face like flint toward Jerusalem, and trusts God to vindicate Him. Those who walk in darkness should trust; those who kindle their own fire will lie down in sorrow.

Authorship & Background

Author: Isaiah son of Amoz (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).
Classification: Prophetic Oracle — Third Servant Song (vv.4-9) Key Themes: God didn't divorce Israel, the Servant's obedient ear, suffering willingly endured, face set like flint, trust in God amid opposition, the danger of self-made light
Historical Context: The third Servant Song (50:4-9) reveals the Servant's OBEDIENCE amid suffering. Unlike the first song (commission) and second (discouragement overcome), this song shows the Servant voluntarily submitting to physical abuse. He offers His back, His cheeks, His face — actively presenting Himself to suffering. "I gave my back to the smiters" — not "they took it" but "I GAVE it." Luke 9:51: "He steadfastly set his face to go to Jerusalem" directly echoes v.7. The chapter frames the song with God's defense of Israel (vv.1-3) and a choice for the listener: trust God in darkness (v.10) or make your own light and suffer (v.11).
Structure:
  • God Didn't Divorce Israel (vv.1-3)
  • Third Servant Song: Obedient Suffering (vv.4-9)
  • Trust in Darkness vs. Self-Made Fire (vv.10-11)

Reflection

  • 1. "I gave my back" (v.6). Jesus GAVE. Every blow was voluntary. He could have called 10,000 angels (Matt 26:53). He chose to stay. His suffering was not victimhood but LOVE choosing pain for your freedom.
  • 2. "Set my face like a flint" (v.7). Following God requires RESOLVE — flint-hard determination that doesn't flinch at pain or turn aside at opposition. What is God calling you toward that requires this kind of flint-face?
  • 3. Walk in darkness and TRUST (v.10). Sometimes God-fearing people are in darkness — not because of sin but because of season. The instruction: TRUST. Don't demand explanations; trust the God you knew in the light.
  • 4. Don't kindle your own fire (v.11). When God seems silent and the darkness presses in, the temptation is to manufacture your own solutions, guidance, and light. But self-made light leads to sorrow. WAIT for God's dawn rather than lighting your own candle.
  • 5. "A word in season to him that is weary" (v.4). The Servant's gift: knowing what weary people need to hear. Ask God for this gift — the ability to speak the right word at the right time to exhausted people.
