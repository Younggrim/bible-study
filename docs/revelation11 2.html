Revelation — Chapter 11

1Then I was given a measuring rod like a staff, and I was told, “Rise and measure the temple of God and the altar and those who worship there,

2but do not measure the court outside the temple; leave that out, for it is given over to the nations, and they will trample the holy city for forty-two months.

3And I will grant authority to my two witnesses, and they will prophesy for 1,260 days, clothed in sackcloth.”

4These are the two olive trees and the two lampstands that stand before the Lord of the earth.

5And if anyone would harm them, fire pours from their mouth and consumes their foes. If anyone would harm them, this is how he is doomed to be killed.

6They have the power to shut the sky, that no rain may fall during the days of their prophesying, and they have power over the waters to turn them into blood and to strike the earth with every kind of plague, as often as they desire.

7And when they have finished their testimony, the beast that rises from the bottomless pit will make war on them and conquer them and kill them,

8and their dead bodies will lie in the street of the great city that symbolically is called Sodom and Egypt, where their Lord was crucified.

9For three and a half days some from the peoples and tribes and languages and nations will gaze at their dead bodies and refuse to let them be placed in a tomb,

10and those who dwell on the earth will rejoice over them and make merry and exchange presents, because these two prophets had been a torment to those who dwell on the earth.

11But after the three and a half days a breath of life from God entered them, and they stood up on their feet, and great fear fell on those who saw them.

12Then they heard a loud voice from heaven saying to them, “Come up here!” And they went up to heaven in a cloud, and their enemies watched them.

13And at that hour there was a great earthquake, and a tenth of the city fell. Seven thousand people were killed in the earthquake, and the rest were terrified and gave glory to the God of heaven.

14The second woe has passed; behold, the third woe is soon to come.

15Then the seventh angel blew his trumpet, and there were loud voices in heaven, saying, “The kingdom of the world has become the kingdom of our Lord and of his Christ, and he shall reign forever and ever.”

16And the twenty-four elders who sit on their thrones before God fell on their faces and worshiped God,

17saying, “We give thanks to you, Lord God Almighty, who is and who was, for you have taken your great power and begun to reign.

18The nations raged, but your wrath came, and the time for the dead to be judged, and for rewarding your servants, the prophets and saints, and those who fear your name, both small and great, and for destroying the destroyers of the earth.”

19Then God’s temple in heaven was opened, and the ark of his covenant was seen within his temple. There were flashes of lightning, rumblings, peals of thunder, an earthquake, and heavy hail.

Summary

Two witnesses prophesy for 1,260 days, are killed by the beast, lie dead in the street for three days, then are resurrected and ascend to heaven — the seventh trumpet sounds: "The kingdoms of this world are become the kingdoms of our Lord, and of his Christ."

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/revelation-11/ Guzik covers the temple measurement, the two witnesses' identity and ministry, their death and resurrection, and the seventh trumpet.
  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "The Kingdoms of This World" on v.15 (MTP Vol. 20, No. 1199) "The kingdoms of this world ARE BECOME — not 'will become' but 'are become.' From heaven's perspective, it is already accomplished. Christ's reign is certain. Every earthly kingdom will bow. Every human authority will submit. He SHALL reign for ever and ever. This is not a hope — it is a certainty. The question is not IF but WHEN."

Translations

  • v.2 — KJV: "forty and two months" / ESV: "forty-two months" — 3.5 years. The same period as 1,260 days (v.3) and "time, times, and half a time" (12:14). The duration of the tribulation's second half.
  • v.3 — KJV: "two witnesses" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Identity debated: (1) Moses and Elijah (miracles match), (2) Enoch and Elijah (both taken without dying), (3) symbolic of the church's witness, (4) two literal end-times prophets. Their powers echo Moses (plagues) and Elijah (drought).
  • v.4 — KJV: "two olive trees, and the two candlesticks" / ESV: "two olive trees and the two lampstands" — KJV "candlesticks" = ESV "lampstands" (Greek "luchniai" — same word as in 1:20). From Zechariah 4. Olive trees supply oil (the Spirit); lampstands give light (witness). Spirit-empowered testimony.
  • v.8 — KJV: "which spiritually is called Sodom and Egypt" / ESV: "symbolically is called Sodom and Egypt" — Greek "pneumatikōs" — spiritually/symbolically. ESV's "symbolically" clarifies for modern readers. Jerusalem is compared to Sodom (immorality) and Egypt (oppression) — "where also our Lord was crucified" confirms the location.
  • v.7 — KJV: "the beast that ascendeth out of the bottomless pit" / ESV: "the beast that rises from the bottomless pit" — Both equivalent. First mention of "the beast" (to be developed in ch.13). He kills the witnesses only after they FINISH their testimony — God's timing.
  • v.15 — KJV: "The kingdoms of this world are become the kingdoms of our Lord" / ESV: "The kingdom of the world has become the kingdom of our Lord" — ESV follows manuscripts with singular "kingdom" (basileia); KJV uses plural "kingdoms." ESV's singular emphasizes the world as one unified system now transferred to Christ. Either way: all earthly authority transfers to Christ permanently.
  • v.19 — KJV: "the ark of his testament" / ESV: "the ark of his covenant" — Greek "kibōtos tēs diathēkēs" — KJV "testament" = ESV "covenant" (same Greek word "diathēkē"). ESV's "covenant" is more precise in modern English. The ark of the covenant seen in heaven. The earthly ark was a copy; the heavenly original is now revealed. God's faithfulness to His covenant.
  • ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB) v.3 — NET: "I will grant my two witnesses authority to prophesy for 1,260 days" — "1,260 days" = 42 months = 3.5 years (the symbolic half-week of Daniel 9:27). WEB: "my two witnesses will prophesy one thousand two hundred sixty days." Their identity is debated: Moses/Elijah? the church? two individuals?
  • v.7 — NET: "the beast that comes up from the abyss will make war on them and conquer them and kill them" — "the beast" (to thErion) — first appearance of this major figure. WEB: "the beast that comes up out of the abyss." The witnesses are invincible during their ministry (v.5) but killed at its end.
  • v.11 — NET: "after three and a half days a breath of life from God entered them and they stood on their feet" — "breath of life" (pneuma zOEs = spirit of life). WEB: "the breath of life from God entered into them." Echoing Ezekiel 37 (dry bones) — resurrection before their enemies' eyes.
  • v.15 — NET: "The kingdom of the world has become the kingdom of our Lord and of his Christ, and he will reign forever and ever" — "kingdom of the world" (hE basileia tou kosmou — singular: one kingdom transferred). WEB: "The kingdom of the world has become the Kingdom of our Lord." KJV: "The kingdoms of this world." The seventh trumpet's climactic announcement.
  • v.18 — NET: "the time has come to destroy those who destroy the earth" — "destroy those who destroy" (diaphtheirai tous diaphtheirontas). WEB: "to destroy those who destroy the earth." Judgment matches crime — those who corrupt creation are themselves destroyed.

Reflection

  • 1. "The kingdoms of this world are become the kingdoms of our Lord" (v.15). This is history's destination. Every earthly power will bow to Christ. Does this certainty give you confidence when the world seems out of control?
  • 2. The two witnesses are killed only AFTER finishing their testimony (v.7). God's servants are invincible until their work is done. Does this give you courage in your calling?
  • 3. The world celebrates the witnesses' death (v.10) — but God has the last word (v.11). When the world celebrates evil, remember: God always has the final say. Resurrection follows death.
  • 4. "Thou hast taken to thee thy great power, and hast reigned" (v.17). God will assert His power fully. Are you living in anticipation of His reign? Are you on the right side of history?
