1 John — Chapter 1

1That which was from the beginning, which we have heard, which we have seen with our eyes, which we looked upon and have touched with our hands, concerning the word of life—

2the life was made manifest, and we have seen it, and testify to it and proclaim to you the eternal life, which was with the Father and was made manifest to us—

3that which we have seen and heard we proclaim also to you, so that you too may have fellowship with us; and indeed our fellowship is with the Father and with his Son Jesus Christ.

4And we are writing these things so that our joy may be complete.

5This is the message we have heard from him and proclaim to you, that God is light, and in him is no darkness at all.

6If we say we have fellowship with him while we walk in darkness, we lie and do not practice the truth.

7But if we walk in the light, as he is in the light, we have fellowship with one another, and the blood of Jesus his Son cleanses us from all sin.

8If we say we have no sin, we deceive ourselves, and the truth is not in us.

9If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to forgive us our sins and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness.

10If we say we have not sinned, we make him a liar, and his word is not in us.

Summary

John declares the Word of life which was from the beginning — seen, heard, and touched — so that readers may have fellowship with the Father and Son, walking in the light where Christ's blood cleanses from all sin, and confessing sin to find faithful forgiveness.

Key Verses

v.1
"That which was from the beginning, which we have heard, which we have seen with our eyes, which we have looked upon, and have touched with our hands, concerning the word of life." (ESV) — Eyewitness testimony of Christ's reality.
v.3
"So that you too may have fellowship with us; and indeed our fellowship is with the Father and with his Son Jesus Christ." (ESV) — Fellowship as the goal.
v.5
"God is light, and in him is no darkness at all." (ESV) — God's nature: pure light.
v.7
"If we walk in the light... the blood of Jesus his Son cleanses us from all sin." (ESV) — Walking in light + Christ's blood = ongoing cleansing.
v.9
"If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to forgive us our sins and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness." (ESV) — The promise of confession.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/1-john-1/ Guzik covers the eyewitness testimony, fellowship, God as light, walking in light, and the promise of confession and cleansing.
  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "If We Confess Our Sins" on v.9 (MTP Vol. 24, No. 1439) "If we CONFESS — not hide, not excuse, not minimize — confess. Agree with God about your sin. Call it what He calls it. He is FAITHFUL — He promised to forgive, and He keeps His promises. And JUST — the cross satisfied justice, so forgiveness is not unjust but righteous. To FORGIVE — pardon, release. And to CLEANSE — not just forgive but purify. From ALL unrighteousness — no sin too great, no stain too deep. Confess and be clean."

Translations

  • v.1 — KJV: "handled" / ESV: "touched" — Four sensory verbs: heard (akoō), seen (horaō), looked upon (theaomai — gazed at intently), handled/ touched (psēlaphaō — physically felt). John emphasizes: Christ was REAL, physical, tangible. Against Gnostics who denied His true humanity.
  • v.5 — KJV: "God is light" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Not "God has light" or "God gives light" but God IS light. His essential nature is light — truth, purity, holiness. "No darkness at all" — Greek "ouk estin... oudemia" — absolutely none.
  • v.7 — KJV: "cleanseth" / ESV: "cleanses" — Greek "katharizei" — present tense: keeps on cleansing. Ongoing, continuous purification as we walk in the light.
  • v.9 — KJV: "faithful and just" / ESV: Both equivalent. — God's forgiveness is not arbitrary mercy but faithful promise-keeping and just response to Christ's atonement. Because Christ paid, God is JUST to forgive.
  • v.10 — KJV: "we make him a liar" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Denying our sin contradicts God's word that all have sinned. It calls God a liar.
  • ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB) v.1 — NET: "what existed from the beginning, what we have heard, what we have seen with our eyes" — four verbs of firsthand experience: heard, seen, observed, touched. WEB: "that which was from the beginning, that which we have heard." KJV: "That which was from the beginning." John stresses physical, sensory witness against Gnostic denial.
  • v.3 — NET: "so that you may have fellowship with us (and indeed our fellowship is with the Father and with his Son)" — "fellowship" (koinOnia = shared participation/partnership). WEB: "our fellowship is with the Father, and with his Son." Horizontal fellowship is grounded in vertical fellowship.
  • v.5 — NET: "God is light, and in him there is no darkness at all" — "no darkness at all" (skotia en autO ouk estin oudemia = double negative for emphasis). WEB: "in him is no darkness at all." Absolute moral purity — zero shadow.
  • v.7 — NET: "if we walk in the light as he himself is in the light, we have fellowship with one another" — "walk in the light" (en tO phOti peripatOmen). WEB: "walk in the light." Walking = ongoing lifestyle. Light = transparency/holiness. Result = genuine community AND cleansing (v.7b).
  • v.9 — NET: "he is faithful and righteous, forgiving us our sins and cleansing us from all unrighteousness" — "faithful and righteous" (pistos... dikaios) — forgiveness is not mercy contradicting justice but justice fulfilled through Christ. WEB: "faithful and righteous to forgive." God is JUST in forgiving because Christ paid.

Reflection

  • 1. "God is light, and in him is no darkness at all" (v.5). Is there darkness in your life that you're hiding from God? He is pure light — nothing is hidden from Him. Are you walking in the light or hiding in shadows?
  • 2. "If we walk in the light... the blood of Jesus Christ his Son cleanseth us from all sin" (v.7). Walking in the light doesn't mean sinless perfection — it means living openly before God. When you do, Christ's blood keeps cleansing you. Are you walking openly?
  • 3. "If we confess our sins" (v.9). Is there unconfessed sin in your life? Confession is agreeing with God — calling sin what He calls it. He promises to forgive AND cleanse. What do you need to confess today?
  • 4. "If we say that we have no sin, we deceive ourselves" (v.8). Self- deception about sin is dangerous. Are you honest about your sinfulness? Or do you minimize, excuse, or deny?
  • 5. "That your joy may be full" (v.4). John writes so that joy would be complete. Is your joy full? Fellowship with God and honest living produce full joy. What's blocking yours?
