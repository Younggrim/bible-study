Genesis — Chapter 9

1And God blessed Noah and his sons and said to them, “Be fruitful and multiply and fill the earth.

2The fear of you and the dread of you shall be upon every beast of the earth and upon every bird of the heavens, upon everything that creeps on the ground and all the fish of the sea. Into your hand they are delivered.

3Every moving thing that lives shall be food for you. And as I gave you the green plants, I give you everything.

4But you shall not eat flesh with its life, that is, its blood.

5And for your lifeblood I will require a reckoning: from every beast I will require it and from man. From his fellow man I will require a reckoning for the life of man.

6“Whoever sheds the blood of man, by man shall his blood be shed, for God made man in his own image.

7And you, be fruitful and multiply, increase greatly on the earth and multiply in it.”

8Then God said to Noah and to his sons with him,

9“Behold, I establish my covenant with you and your offspring after you,

10and with every living creature that is with you, the birds, the livestock, and every beast of the earth with you, as many as came out of the ark; it is for every beast of the earth.

11I establish my covenant with you, that never again shall all flesh be cut off by the waters of the flood, and never again shall there be a flood to destroy the earth.”

12And God said, “This is the sign of the covenant that I make between me and you and every living creature that is with you, for all future generations:

13I have set my bow in the cloud, and it shall be a sign of the covenant between me and the earth.

14When I bring clouds over the earth and the bow is seen in the clouds,

15I will remember my covenant that is between me and you and every living creature of all flesh. And the waters shall never again become a flood to destroy all flesh.

16When the bow is in the clouds, I will see it and remember the everlasting covenant between God and every living creature of all flesh that is on the earth.”

17God said to Noah, “This is the sign of the covenant that I have established between me and all flesh that is on the earth.”

18The sons of Noah who went forth from the ark were Shem, Ham, and Japheth. (Ham was the father of Canaan.)

19These three were the sons of Noah, and from these the people of the whole earth were dispersed.

20Noah began to be a man of the soil, and he planted a vineyard.

21He drank of the wine and became drunk and lay uncovered in his tent.

22And Ham, the father of Canaan, saw the nakedness of his father and told his two brothers outside.

23Then Shem and Japheth took a garment, laid it on both their shoulders, and walked backward and covered the nakedness of their father. Their faces were turned backward, and they did not see their father’s nakedness.

24When Noah awoke from his wine and knew what his youngest son had done to him,

25he said, “Cursed be Canaan; a servant of servants shall he be to his brothers.”

26He also said, “Blessed be the LORD, the God of Shem; and let Canaan be his servant.

27May God enlarge Japheth, and let him dwell in the tents of Shem, and let Canaan be his servant.”

28After the flood Noah lived 350 years.

29All the days of Noah were 950 years, and he died.

Summary

God establishes a covenant with Noah, sets the rainbow as its sign, and gives new commands for post-flood humanity.

Key Verses

v.1
"Be fruitful and multiply and fill the earth." (ESV) — Humanity re-commissioned after the flood.
v.6
"Whoever sheds the blood of man, by man shall his blood be shed, for God made man in his own image." (ESV) — Capital punishment grounded in the image of God.
v.11
"Never again shall all flesh be cut off by the waters of the flood." (ESV) — God's covenant promise.
v.13
"I have set my bow in the cloud, and it shall be a sign of the covenant between me and the earth." (ESV) — The rainbow as covenant sign.
v.26
"Blessed be the LORD, the God of Shem." (ESV) — The Messianic line identified.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/genesis-9/ Guzik covers the Noahic covenant, the rainbow, Noah's failure, and the prophecy over the three sons.
  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "The Rainbow" on v.13 (MTP Vol. 9, No. 527) "I do set MY bow in the cloud — God's bow, not man's. A weapon of war (the bow) now pointed away from earth, toward heaven — as if God has laid down His weapon. The rainbow is God's promise made visible: I will not destroy again. Every rainbow is a sermon — preaching God's faithfulness, God's mercy, God's covenant-keeping character."

Translations

  • v.1 — KJV: "replenish the earth" / ESV: "fill the earth" — Hebrew "mil'u" — to fill. Same word and same issue as 1:28. ESV's "fill" is more accurate; KJV's "replenish" in 1611 meant "fill" but modern readers may misread it as "refill."
  • v.3 — KJV: "every moving thing that liveth shall be meat for you" / ESV: "Every moving thing that lives shall be food for you" — Hebrew "oklah" — food. KJV's "meat" meant "food" in 1611 (not specifically animal flesh). ESV's "food" avoids confusion. Before the flood, humanity was apparently vegetarian (1:29). Now meat is permitted — but not blood.
  • v.4 — KJV: "flesh with the life thereof, which is the blood thereof, shall ye not eat" / ESV: "But you shall not eat flesh with its life, that is, its blood" — Both equivalent. Blood represents life. Consuming blood is prohibited because life belongs to God. This prohibition predates and transcends the Mosaic Law.
  • v.5 — KJV: "at the hand of every man's brother will I require the life of man" / ESV: "From his fellow man I will require a reckoning for the life of man" — Hebrew "darash" — to seek, require, demand an accounting. ESV's "require a reckoning" makes the judicial sense clearer.
  • v.6 — KJV: "Whoso sheddeth man's blood, by man shall his blood be shed" / ESV: "Whoever sheds the blood of man, by man shall his blood be shed" — Both equivalent. The institution of capital punishment. The basis: the image of God. To murder a human is to assault God's image.
  • v.9 — KJV: "with your seed after you" / ESV: "your offspring after you" — Hebrew "zera" — seed, offspring. ESV's "offspring" is clearer for modern readers.
  • v.12 — KJV: "the token of the covenant" / ESV: "the sign of the covenant" — Hebrew "oth" — sign, token, mark. ESV's "sign" is more natural modern English; KJV's "token" is slightly archaic.
  • v.13 — KJV: "I do set my bow in the cloud" / ESV: "I have set my bow in the cloud" — Both equivalent. Hebrew "qesheth" — a war bow. God sets His weapon in the sky as a sign of peace. The bow points upward (toward God), not downward (toward earth).
  • v.20 — KJV: "Noah began to be an husbandman" / ESV: "Noah began to be a man of the soil" — Hebrew "ish ha'adamah" — a man of the ground/soil. ESV is more literal; KJV's "husbandman" (farmer) is archaic English.
  • v.22 — KJV: "saw the nakedness of his father" / ESV: "saw the nakedness of his father" — Both identical. Hebrew "ra'ah eth ervath" — the exact nature of Ham's sin is debated. At minimum: disrespect and exploitation of his father's vulnerability. He publicized what should have been covered.
  • v.25 — KJV: "Cursed be Canaan; a servant of servants" / ESV: "Cursed be Canaan; a servant of servants shall he be to his brothers" — Both equivalent. Noah curses Canaan (Ham's son), not Ham directly. This prophecy is fulfilled when Israel conquers Canaan. It is NOT a justification for racial slavery — it refers to specific nations, not races.
  • v.27 — KJV: "God shall enlarge Japheth, and he shall dwell in the tents of Shem" / ESV: "May God enlarge Japheth, and let him dwell in the tents of Shem" — Hebrew "yapht" — a wordplay on Japheth's name (enlarge/ make spacious). ESV renders it as a wish/prayer ("May God..."); KJV as a declaration ("God shall..."). Both are valid readings of the Hebrew jussive form. Japheth's descendants will share in Shem's spiritual blessings — fulfilled as Gentiles are included in the Abrahamic covenant through Christ.
  • TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB) - v.2 — NET: "are under your authority" — interprets "into your hand are they delivered" (ASV) as a statement of authority/dominion, making the governance concept explicit where KJV/ESV use physical "hand" imagery. - v.12 — NET: "This is the guarantee of the covenant" — uniquely renders Hebrew "oth" (sign/token) as "guarantee," emphasizing the legal/binding nature of the covenant sign rather than merely visual. - v.13 — WEB: "I set my rainbow in the cloud" — uses "rainbow" explicitly where KJV/ASV have simply "bow" and ESV "my bow." WEB makes the referent immediately clear for modern readers. - v.26 — NET: "Worthy of praise is the Lord, the God of Shem!" — an interpretive expansion of "Blessed be the LORD God of Shem" (KJV/ESV), rendering the Hebrew blessing formula as a declaration of God's praiseworthiness rather than a wish for God to be blessed. - v.27 — NET: "May God enlarge Japheth's territory and numbers!" — adds "territory and numbers" to make explicit what the enlargement entails, going beyond the Hebrew text which simply says "enlarge Japheth."

Reflection

  • 1. "I do set my bow in the cloud" (v.13). Every rainbow is God preaching His faithfulness. Do you notice? Do you remember His promises when you see it?
  • 2. "For in the image of God made he man" (v.6). Every human being bears God's image — regardless of race, status, or condition. How does this shape how you treat people?
  • 3. Noah — the righteous man who walked with God — got drunk and was shamed (vv.20-21). Even the godly can fall. Are you guarding against complacency? Past faithfulness doesn't guarantee future obedience.
  • 4. Shem and Japheth covered their father's shame (v.23). When someone you respect fails, do you expose them (like Ham) or cover them with grace (like Shem and Japheth)?
  • 5. "God shall enlarge Japheth, and he shall dwell in the tents of Shem" (v.27). Gentiles sharing in Israel's spiritual blessings — fulfilled in Christ. If you're a Gentile believer, you are living in this prophecy.
