1We then, as workers together with him, beseech you also that ye receive not the grace of God in vain.

2(For he saith, I have heard thee in a time accepted, and in the day of salvation have I succoured thee: behold, now is the accepted time; behold, now is the day of salvation.)

3Giving no offence in any thing, that the ministry be not blamed:

4But in all things approving ourselves as the ministers of God, in much patience, in afflictions, in necessities, in distresses,

5In stripes, in imprisonments, in tumults, in labours, in watchings, in fastings;

6By pureness, by knowledge, by longsuffering, by kindness, by the Holy Ghost, by love unfeigned,

7By the word of truth, by the power of God, by the armour of righteousness on the right hand and on the left,

8By honour and dishonour, by evil report and good report: as deceivers, and yet true;

9As unknown, and yet well known; as dying, and, behold, we live; as chastened, and not killed;

10As sorrowful, yet alway rejoicing; as poor, yet making many rich; as having nothing, and yet possessing all things.

11O ye Corinthians, our mouth is open unto you, our heart is enlarged.

12Ye are not straitened in us, but ye are straitened in your own bowels.

13Now for a recompence in the same, (I speak as unto my children,) be ye also enlarged.

14Be ye not unequally yoked together with unbelievers: for what fellowship hath righteousness with unrighteousness? and what communion hath light with darkness?

15And what concord hath Christ with Belial? or what part hath he that believeth with an infidel?

16And what agreement hath the temple of God with idols? for ye are the temple of the living God; as God hath said, I will dwell in them, and walk in them; and I will be their God, and they shall be my people.

17Wherefore come out from among them, and be ye separate, saith the Lord, and touch not the unclean thing; and I will receive you,

18And will be a Father unto you, and ye shall be my sons and daughters, saith the Lord Almighty.

Summary

Paul pleads with the Corinthians not to receive God's grace in vain, catalogs his sufferings as credentials of authentic ministry, and commands: "Do not be unequally yoked with unbelievers" — for what fellowship has light with darkness?

Authorship & Background

Author: Paul (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).
Historical Context: Chapter 6 contains Paul's appeal not to receive God's grace "in vain" (v.1), his catalog of sufferings that validate his ministry (vv.3-10), his emotional appeal for the Corinthians to open their hearts (vv.11-13), and the command to separate from unbelievers in binding partnerships (vv.14-18).
The Marks of Authentic Ministry (vv.3-10): One of the most powerful passages on ministry credentials in the NT. Paul lists what validates his ministry — not success or comfort but: patience, afflictions, stripes, imprisonments, labors, purity, knowledge, kindness, the Holy Spirit, genuine love, truth, God's power, and a series of paradoxes (vv.8-10).
The Paradoxes (vv.8-10): "As deceivers, and yet true; as unknown, and yet well known; as dying, and behold, we live; as chastened, and not killed; as sorrowful, yet alway rejoicing; as poor, yet making many rich; as having nothing, and yet possessing all things." The Christian life is a series of paradoxes — the world sees one thing; reality is another.
Unequal Yoking (vv.14-18): "Be ye not unequally yoked together with unbelievers" (v.14). Paul uses five rhetorical questions to show the incompatibility of believers and unbelievers in binding partnerships. The principle: light and darkness cannot coexist in the same yoke.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): enduringword.com Guzik examines the urgency of salvation, the marks of ministry, the paradoxes, and the unequal yoke command.
  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "Now Is the Day of Salvation" on v.2 (MTP Vol. 10, No. 664) "NOW is the accepted time — not tomorrow, not next week, not when you feel ready — NOW. Today. This moment. God's offer has an expiration date that you cannot see. Respond now while the door is open." Sermon: "The Unequal Yoke" on v.14 (MTP Vol. 22, No. 1395) "Be not unequally yoked — light and darkness cannot pull together. Righteousness and unrighteousness cannot share a harness. This applies to marriage, business partnerships, and any binding alliance where a believer is tied to an unbeliever in a way that compromises their faith."

Reflection

  • 1. "Now is the accepted time; behold, now is the day of salvation" (v.2). Is there something God is calling you to respond to TODAY? Don't delay. Tomorrow is not guaranteed. What decision are you postponing?
  • 2. The paradoxes of vv.8-10 describe the Christian life: sorrowful yet rejoicing, poor yet making others rich, having nothing yet possessing all. Which paradox most describes your current experience? How do you hold both realities together?
  • 3. "Be ye not unequally yoked together with unbelievers" (v.14). This applies to any binding partnership — marriage, business, deep alliances. Are you in any relationship that is pulling you away from Christ? What needs to change?
  • 4. "I will be a Father unto you, and ye shall be my sons and daughters" (v.18). The reward of separation: intimate relationship with God as Father. Is the promise of deeper intimacy with God worth whatever you need to separate from?
  • 5. Paul's ministry credentials (vv.4-10) include suffering, not success. How does this challenge the modern tendency to validate ministry by numbers, buildings, and budgets?
  • 6. "As having nothing, and yet possessing all things" (v.10). Paul was materially poor but spiritually wealthy. How do you measure wealth — by what you have or by what you are in Christ?
  • 7. The unequal yoke (v.14) is often applied to marriage — but it extends to any binding partnership. What partnerships in your life (business, close friendships, alliances) might be pulling you in a direction incompatible with your faith?
  • 8. "Come out from among them, and be ye separate" (v.17). Separation is not isolation — Jesus ate with sinners. It is refusing to be BOUND to what opposes God. How do you maintain relationships with unbelievers without being yoked to their values?
  • 9. God promises "I will receive you... I will be a Father unto you" (vv.17-18). The reward of holy separation is deeper intimacy with God. Is there something you need to "come out from" to experience more of God's fatherly presence?
