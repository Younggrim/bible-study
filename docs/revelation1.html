1The Revelation of Jesus Christ, which God gave unto him, to shew unto his servants things which must shortly come to pass; and he sent and signified it by his angel unto his servant John:

2Who bare record of the word of God, and of the testimony of Jesus Christ, and of all things that he saw.

3Blessed is he that readeth, and they that hear the words of this prophecy, and keep those things which are written therein: for the time is at hand.

4John to the seven churches which are in Asia: Grace be unto you, and peace, from him which is, and which was, and which is to come; and from the seven Spirits which are before his throne;

5And from Jesus Christ, who is the faithful witness, and the first begotten of the dead, and the prince of the kings of the earth. Unto him that loved us, and washed us from our sins in his own blood,

6And hath made us kings and priests unto God and his Father; to him be glory and dominion for ever and ever. Amen.

7Behold, he cometh with clouds; and every eye shall see him, and they also which pierced him: and all kindreds of the earth shall wail because of him. Even so, Amen.

8I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the ending, saith the Lord, which is, and which was, and which is to come, the Almighty.

9I John, who also am your brother, and companion in tribulation, and in the kingdom and patience of Jesus Christ, was in the isle that is called Patmos, for the word of God, and for the testimony of Jesus Christ.

10I was in the Spirit on the Lord’s day, and heard behind me a great voice, as of a trumpet,

11Saying, I am Alpha and Omega, the first and the last: and, What thou seest, write in a book, and send it unto the seven churches which are in Asia; unto Ephesus, and unto Smyrna, and unto Pergamos, and unto Thyatira, and unto Sardis, and unto Philadelphia, and unto Laodicea.

12And I turned to see the voice that spake with me. And being turned, I saw seven golden candlesticks;

13And in the midst of the seven candlesticks one like unto the Son of man, clothed with a garment down to the foot, and girt about the paps with a golden girdle.

14His head and his hairs were white like wool, as white as snow; and his eyes were as a flame of fire;

15And his feet like unto fine brass, as if they burned in a furnace; and his voice as the sound of many waters.

16And he had in his right hand seven stars: and out of his mouth went a sharp twoedged sword: and his countenance was as the sun shineth in his strength.

17And when I saw him, I fell at his feet as dead. And he laid his right hand upon me, saying unto me, Fear not; I am the first and the last:

18I am he that liveth, and was dead; and, behold, I am alive for evermore, Amen; and have the keys of hell and of death.

19Write the things which thou hast seen, and the things which are, and the things which shall be hereafter;

20The mystery of the seven stars which thou sawest in my right hand, and the seven golden candlesticks. The seven stars are the angels of the seven churches: and the seven candlesticks which thou sawest are the seven churches.

Summary

The revelation of Jesus Christ — John sees the glorified Son of Man among seven golden lampstands, hears His voice like many waters, and is commissioned to write what he sees to the seven churches of Asia.

Authorship & Background

Author: John (v.1, 4, 9) — the Apostle John, author of the Gospel of John and 1-3 John. Written approximately AD 95-96 during the reign of Emperor Domitian, who demanded worship as "Lord and God."
Recipient: "The seven churches which are in Asia" (v.4) — seven specific churches in the Roman province of Asia (modern western Turkey): Ephesus, Smyrna, Pergamos, Thyatira, Sardis, Philadelphia, and Laodicea.
Setting: John was exiled on the island of Patmos "for the word of God, and for the testimony of Jesus Christ" (v.9). A small, rocky island in the Aegean Sea used by Rome for political prisoners.
Purpose: "The Revelation of Jesus Christ" (v.1) — to reveal Christ in His glory, to show "things which must shortly come to pass" (v.1), and to encourage persecuted believers that Christ is sovereign and will triumph.
Structure of Revelation (v.19): "Write the things which thou hast seen" (ch.1 — the vision of Christ), "the things which are" (chs.2-3 — the seven churches), "the things which shall be hereafter" (chs.4-22 — future events).

Map & Geography

  • Patmos (v.9): Island in the Aegean Sea where John received the Revelation.
  • Ephesus (v.11): Major city in Asia Minor (modern Turkey); location of a key early church.
  • Laodicea (v.11): City in Asia Minor; one of the seven churches in Revelation.
  • Smyrna (v.11): City in Asia Minor; one of the seven churches in Revelation.
  • Sardis (v.11): City in Asia Minor; one of the seven churches in Revelation.
  • Philadelphia (v.11): City in Asia Minor; one of the seven churches in Revelation.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): enduringword.com Guzik covers the prologue, the greeting, the doxology, the vision of Christ, and the commission to write.
  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "Alpha and Omega" on v.8 (MTP Vol. 6, No. 345) "Alpha and Omega — the first and last letters of the Greek alphabet. Christ is the beginning of all things and the end of all things. He started creation and He will conclude history. He is the first word of the Bible and the last. He is the A and Z of salvation — He begins it and He finishes it. There is nothing before Him and nothing after Him. He encompasses all." Sermon: "Fear Not" on vv.17-18 (MTP Vol. 15, No. 876) "Fear not — the same words He spoke to fishermen on Galilee, He speaks to John on Patmos. I am the first and the last — eternal. I am he that liveth — alive. And was dead — He really died. And behold, I am alive for evermore — death could not hold Him. And have the keys of hell and of death — He controls both. If Christ holds the keys, death is not your master — He is."

Reflection

  • 1. "Blessed is he that readeth" (v.3). Revelation comes with a specific blessing for reading it. Are you engaging with this book or avoiding it? Don't let its difficulty keep you from its blessing.
  • 2. "Unto him that loved us, and washed us from our sins" (v.5). Before any prophecy or judgment, John worships. Christ loved you and cleansed you. Start there.
  • 3. "Every eye shall see him" (v.7). Christ's return will be visible and universal. Are you ready? Are you living in light of His coming?
  • 4. "Fear not; I am the first and the last" (v.17). Whatever you fear, Christ is greater. He is eternal, alive, and holds the keys. What fear do you need to release to Him?
  • 5. The glorified Christ (vv.13-16) is majestic, powerful, and awe-inspiring. Is your view of Jesus too small? Too familiar? Let this vision expand your understanding of who He really is.
