The Beatitudes

Blessed Are Those Who Hunger for Righteousness

The Blessing

In Matthew 5:6, Jesus declares: "Blessed are those who hunger and thirst for righteousness, for they shall be satisfied." The language here is deliberately visceral. Jesus does not speak of those who have a mild preference for goodness or a casual interest in spiritual things. He speaks of hunger and thirst — the most urgent, consuming physical drives a human being can experience. In the ancient world, where famine and drought were never far away, His listeners understood that hunger and thirst could mean the difference between life and death.

This beatitude describes a consuming desire for God's righteousness — not merely moral self-improvement or religious performance, but a deep longing for God's ways to prevail in our hearts and in the world. It is the cry of Psalm 42: "As a deer pants for flowing streams, so pants my soul for you, O God." It is the prayer of Psalm 63: "O God, you are my God; earnestly I seek you; my soul thirsts for you." This hunger goes beyond wanting to be a better person; it is a desperate need for God Himself and for His Kingdom to come in fullness.

The promise attached to this hunger is extraordinary: "they shall be satisfied." The Greek word (chortazo) means to be completely filled, gorged, lacking nothing. God promises that those who hunger for His righteousness will not be left wanting. This satisfaction comes progressively as we walk with Christ, and it will come perfectly when His Kingdom is fully realized. Unlike every earthly hunger that returns after being fed, this satisfaction deepens and grows richer over time.

Key Scriptures

  • Matthew 5:6 — "Blessed are those who hunger and thirst for righteousness, for they shall be satisfied."
  • Psalm 42:1-2 — "As a deer pants for flowing streams, so pants my soul for you, O God. My soul thirsts for God, for the living God."
  • Isaiah 55:1-2 — "Come, everyone who thirsts, come to the waters; and he who has no money, come, buy and eat!"
  • John 7:37-38 — "If anyone thirsts, let him come to me and drink. Whoever believes in me... out of his heart will flow rivers of living water."
  • Psalm 63:1 — "O God, you are my God; earnestly I seek you; my soul thirsts for you; my flesh faints for you, as in a dry and weary land."

Living the Beatitude

Living as those who hunger for righteousness means intentionally cultivating our appetite for God. Just as physical hunger grows when we stop filling ourselves with junk food, spiritual hunger increases when we stop trying to satisfy our souls with lesser things. This means creating space in our lives for Scripture, prayer, worship, and silence — not as religious duties to check off, but as the means by which we feast on the bread of life. It also means honestly examining what we are currently hungry for, because our appetites reveal our true priorities.

This hunger also has a communal and prophetic dimension. Those who hunger for righteousness are not satisfied merely with personal holiness — they long to see God's justice and righteousness prevail in their families, communities, and the world. They grieve when the vulnerable are exploited, when truth is suppressed, and when God's name is dishonored. This holy dissatisfaction with the status quo drives them to pray, to act, and to hope for the day when God will make all things right. Their hunger becomes fuel for faithful action in a broken world.

Reflection Questions

  • What are you most hungry for in this season of life? Does your appetite reflect a genuine longing for God's righteousness?
  • What lesser things are you using to satisfy spiritual hunger that only God can fill?
  • How can you create more space in your daily life to cultivate hunger for God through Scripture, prayer, and worship?
  • Where do you see injustice or unrighteousness that stirs a holy dissatisfaction within you — and what is God calling you to do about it?
