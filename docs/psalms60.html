2You have made the land to quake; you have torn it open; repair its breaches, for it totters.

3You have made your people see hard things; you have given us wine to drink that made us stagger.

4You have set up a banner for those who fear you, that they may flee to it from the bow. Selah

5That your beloved ones may be delivered, give salvation by your right hand and answer us!

11Oh, grant us help against the foe, for vain is the salvation of man!

12With God we shall do valiantly; it is he who will tread down our foes.

Summary

A communal lament after military defeat — God has rejected Israel and broken them, yet David cries for help: 'Give us aid against the enemy, for the help of man is vain.' God declares victory over surrounding nations.

Key Verses

v.4
"Thou hast given a banner to them that fear thee, that it may be displayed because of the truth." (KJV) — Even in defeat, God gives a rallying point. The banner (nes) is a standard around which troops gather. God's truth is the banner that keeps His people from scattering in despair.
v.6-8
"God hath spoken in his holiness; I will rejoice, I will divide Shechem... Moab is my washpot; over Edom will I cast out my shoe." (KJV) — God's oracle declares His sovereignty over ALL lands — both Israelite territories (Shechem, Succoth, Gilead, Manasseh, Ephraim, Judah) and enemy nations (Moab, Edom, Philistia). Moab as a "washpot" = a servant's basin. Casting a shoe over Edom = claiming ownership.
v.11
"Give us help from trouble: for vain is the help of man." (KJV) — One of Scripture's clearest statements about the insufficiency of human resources. Military alliances, human strategies, and earthly power are "vain" (shav = empty, worthless) without God.
v.12
"Through God we shall do valiantly: for he it is that shall tread down our enemies." (KJV) — The psalm's resolution. Not "God will do it without us" or "We will do it without God" but "THROUGH God WE shall do valiantly." Divine empowerment of human action.

Cross-References

  • v.1 — 2 Samuel 8:13 — Joab's victory over Edom in the Valley of Salt, the historical context. v.4 — Isaiah 11:10 — "There shall be a root of Jesse, which shall stand for an ensign of the people." Christ as the ultimate banner. v.6-8 — Psalm 108:7-13 — Nearly identical repetition of these verses in a later psalm. v.8 — Genesis 25:23 — "The elder shall serve the younger." God's sovereignty over Edom (Esau) fulfilled here. v.11 — Psalm 146:3 — "Put not your trust in princes." Same warning against human reliance. v.12 — Philippians 4:13 — "I can do all things through Christ which strengtheneth me." Same principle of divine empowerment.

Translations

  • v.4 — KJV: "that it may be displayed because of the truth" / ESV: "that they may flee to it from the bow" — This is a significant difference. Hebrew "qoshet" could mean "truth" or be related to "qesheth" (bow). ESV follows an emendation suggesting a military banner to rally to for safety. KJV keeps "truth" as the reason for the banner.
  • v.8 — KJV: "over Edom will I cast out my shoe" / ESV: "upon Edom I cast my shoe" — Casting a sandal over property indicated ownership (Ruth 4:7). God claims Edom as His possession to dispose of as He wills.
  • v.8 — KJV: "Philistia, triumph thou because of me" / ESV: "over Philistia I shout in triumph" — The Hebrew is ambiguous. KJV reads it as ironic/sarcastic; ESV reads it as God's shout of victory over Philistia. ESV is more consistent with the context of God's dominion over enemy nations.

Reflection

  • 1. God can use defeat to teach dependence (vv.1-3). Israel's initial loss drove them back to God. Sometimes God allows setbacks to break our self-sufficiency and remind us that victory comes from Him alone.
  • 2. God is sovereign over nations we consider enemies (vv.6-8). No nation, no opposition, no human power is outside God's control. Moab, Edom, Philistia — all are His to command. Your "enemies" are already under His authority.
  • 3. Human help is vain without God (v.11). Alliances, strategies, resources — all empty without divine backing. This doesn't mean don't plan; it means don't trust in planning AS God.
  • 4. Through God, we do valiantly (v.12). The partnership: God empowers, we act. Neither passive waiting nor self-reliant striving — but God-dependent, courageous action.
