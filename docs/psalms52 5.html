Psalms — Chapter 52

1Why do you boast of evil, O mighty man? The steadfast love of God endures all the day.

2Your tongue plots destruction, like a sharp razor, you worker of deceit.

3You love evil more than good, and lying more than speaking what is right. Selah

4You love all words that devour, O deceitful tongue.

5But God will break you down forever; he will snatch and tear you from your tent; he will uproot you from the land of the living. Selah

6The righteous shall see and fear, and shall laugh at him, saying,

7“See the man who would not make God his refuge, but trusted in the abundance of his riches and sought refuge in his own destruction!”

8But I am like a green olive tree in the house of God. I trust in the steadfast love of God forever and ever.

9I will thank you forever, because you have done it. I will wait for your name, for it is good, in the presence of the godly.

Summary

A psalm against Doeg the Edomite — rebukes the mighty man who boasts in evil and loves devouring words; God will uproot him, but the righteous will trust in God's steadfast love forever.

Key Verses

v.1
"Why boastest thou thyself in mischief, O mighty man? the goodness of God endureth continually." (KJV) — The contrast is immediate: the mighty man's mischief is temporary; God's goodness is perpetual. The "mighty man" (gibbor) is used ironically — his only strength is in doing evil.
v.5
"God shall likewise destroy thee for ever, he shall take thee away, and pluck thee out of thy dwelling place, and root thee out of the land of the living." (KJV) — Four verbs of destruction: destroy, take away, pluck out, root out. The thoroughness of God's judgment matches the thoroughness of the man's evil.
v.7
"Lo, this is the man that made not God his strength; but trusted in the abundance of his riches, and strengthened himself in his wickedness." (KJV) — The epitaph of the wicked: he chose riches over God as his fortress. His wickedness was his strategy for success.
v.8
"But I am like a green olive tree in the house of God: I trust in the mercy of God for ever and ever." (KJV) — The olive tree: long-lived, fruitful, evergreen. Contrast with the wicked who is "rooted out" (v.5). David is rooted IN God's house; the wicked is rooted OUT of life.

Cross-References

  • v.1 — 1 Samuel 22:9-19 — Doeg's betrayal and massacre of the priests at Nob — the historical backdrop. v.2 — James 3:5-8 — "The tongue is a fire... an unruly evil, full of deadly poison." Same theme of the destructive tongue. v.5 — Proverbs 2:22 — "The wicked shall be cut off from the earth, and the transgressors shall be rooted out of it." v.7 — Psalm 49:6-7 — "They that trust in their wealth, and boast themselves in the multitude of their riches; none of them can by any means redeem his brother." v.8 — Psalm 92:12-14 — "The righteous shall flourish like the palm tree... planted in the house of the LORD." v.8 — Jeremiah 11:16 — "The LORD called thy name, A green olive tree." Israel as God's olive tree.

Translations

  • v.1 — KJV: "the goodness of God endureth continually" / ESV: "The steadfast love of God endures all the day" — Hebrew "hesed El" = God's covenant love. KJV's "goodness" is broader; ESV's "steadfast love" is more precise for hesed.
  • v.5 — KJV: "pluck thee out of thy dwelling place" / ESV: "snatch and tear you from your tent" — ESV captures the violence of the Hebrew more vividly. The image is of being ripped from one's home like a plant torn from the ground.
  • v.7 — KJV: "strengthened himself in his wickedness" / ESV: "sought refuge in his own destruction" — ESV interprets the Hebrew "ma'uzzoh" (his stronghold/refuge) as connected to destruction. Both capture that wickedness was his chosen fortress.

Reflection

  • 1. The tongue can be a weapon of mass destruction (vv.2-4). Doeg spoke words that resulted in 85 deaths. Words are never "just words" — they have power to destroy lives, reputations, and communities.
  • 2. Wealth and wickedness make a false fortress (v.7). The man who trusts in riches instead of God has built his house on sand. It may look strong today, but God's judgment will expose its emptiness.
  • 3. The righteous are rooted; the wicked are uprooted (vv.5,8). Where you plant yourself determines your destiny. The olive tree in God's house endures for generations. The wicked are torn out completely — no roots, no fruit, no legacy.
  • 4. God's steadfast love outlasts all human evil (v.1). Whatever the "mighty man" does, God's hesed endures. Evil is temporary; God's love is eternal. This is the ground of all hope.
