Nahum — Chapter 3

1Woe to the bloody city, all full of lies and plunder— no end to the prey!

2The crack of the whip, and rumble of the wheel, galloping horse and bounding chariot!

3Horsemen charging, flashing sword and glittering spear, hosts of slain, heaps of corpses, dead bodies without end— they stumble over the bodies!

4And all for the countless whorings of the prostitute, graceful and of deadly charms, who betrays nations with her whorings, and peoples with her charms.

5Behold, I am against you, declares the LORD of hosts, and will lift up your skirts over your face; and I will make nations look at your nakedness and kingdoms at your shame.

6I will throw filth at you and treat you with contempt and make you a spectacle.

7And all who look at you will shrink from you and say, “Wasted is Nineveh; who will grieve for her?” Where shall I seek comforters for you?

8Are you better than Thebes that sat by the Nile, with water around her, her rampart a sea, and water her wall?

9Cush was her strength; Egypt too, and that without limit; Put and the Libyans were her helpers.

10Yet she became an exile; she went into captivity; her infants were dashed in pieces at the head of every street; for her honored men lots were cast, and all her great men were bound in chains.

11You also will be drunken; you will go into hiding; you will seek a refuge from the enemy.

12All your fortresses are like fig trees with first-ripe figs— if shaken they fall into the mouth of the eater.

13Behold, your troops are women in your midst. The gates of your land are wide open to your enemies; fire has devoured your bars.

14Draw water for the siege; strengthen your forts; go into the clay; tread the mortar; take hold of the brick mold!

15There will the fire devour you; the sword will cut you off. It will devour you like the locust. Multiply yourselves like the locust; multiply like the grasshopper!

16You increased your merchants more than the stars of the heavens. The locust spreads its wings and flies away.

17Your princes are like grasshoppers, your scribes like clouds of locusts settling on the fences in a day of cold— when the sun rises, they fly away; no one knows where they are.

18Your shepherds are asleep, O king of Assyria; your nobles slumber. Your people are scattered on the mountains with none to gather them.

19There is no easing your hurt; your wound is grievous. All who hear the news about you clap their hands over you. For upon whom has not come your unceasing evil?

Summary

Woe to Nineveh, the city of blood and endless plunder — her wound is incurable, and all who hear of her fall clap their hands, for who has not felt her unceasing cruelty?

Key Verses

v.1
"Woe to the bloody city! it is all full of lies and robbery; the prey departeth not" (KJV) / "Woe to the bloody city, all full of lies and plunder — no end to the prey!" (ESV) — The formal charge. Three sins: blood (violence), lies (deception), and robbery (greed). And the prey "departeth not" — Assyria NEVER stopped devouring. There was no season of mercy.
v.5
"Behold, I am against thee, saith the LORD of hosts" (KJV) — Repeated from 2:13. God's double declaration of opposition. When God repeats Himself, certainty doubles. The judgment is personal — God Himself is the adversary.
v.7
"Nineveh is laid waste: who will bemoan her? whence shall I seek comforters for thee?" (KJV) / "Wasted is Nineveh; who will grieve for her?" (ESV) — The answer is: no one. Nineveh has no friends, no allies who truly care, no one to mourn. She burned every relationship through cruelty. The isolation of the wicked is complete.
v.8
"Art thou better than populous No?" (KJV) / "Are you better than Thebes?" (ESV) — The argument from history. Thebes had the Nile as a moat, Ethiopia and Egypt as allies, and yet she fell. Nineveh has no special immunity. If God judged Thebes, He will judge Nineveh.
v.19
"There is no healing of thy bruise; thy wound is grievous: all that hear the bruit of thee shall clap the hands over thee: for upon whom hath not thy wickedness passed continually?" (KJV) — The final verdict. The wound is terminal, and the world CELEBRATES. The last question is the most damning: everyone has suffered under Nineveh. Universal rejoicing at her fall reveals universal suffering under her reign.

Cross-References

  • v.1 — Habakkuk 2:12 — "Woe to him that buildeth a town with blood, and stablisheth a city by iniquity" — the same charge against Babylon. v.4 — Revelation 17:1-5 — "Mystery Babylon" as the great prostitute who corrupts nations — Nineveh as a type. v.5 — Revelation 18:8-10 — God's public judgment of Babylon the great, echoing Nineveh's exposure. v.7 — Revelation 18:9-11 — "who shall bewail her" — the same question asked of eschatological Babylon. v.8 — Isaiah 19:1-15 — God's oracle against Egypt and the fall of its cities. v.10 — Psalm 137:9 — The horrific violence of ancient warfare against children — Assyria's own methods turned against others. v.18 — 1 Kings 22:17 — "scattered upon the hills, as sheep that have not a shepherd" — leaderless people. v.19 — Revelation 19:1-3 — Heaven rejoices at Babylon's fall because "he hath judged the great whore."

Translations

  • v.1 — "prey departeth not" (KJV) / "no end to the prey" (ESV) — Hebrew indicates that the predatory behavior is CONTINUOUS and UNCEASING. Assyria never had a generation of peace — only perpetual conquest.
  • v.4 — "witchcrafts" (KJV) / "charms" (ESV) — Hebrew "keshaphim" = sorceries, enchantments. This refers to Nineveh's use of occult practices in diplomacy and conquest — religious manipulation to control vassal nations.
  • v.8 — "populous No" (KJV) / "Thebes" (ESV) — Hebrew "No-Amon" = the city of the god Amon. KJV translates descriptively ("populous No"), ESV uses the modern name (Thebes). This was the capital of Upper Egypt, one of the ancient world's greatest cities.
  • v.17 — "crowned" (KJV) / "princes" (ESV) — Hebrew "minnezarayik" is debated. It may refer to "crowned ones" (officials wearing diadems) or be related to Assyrian "naziru" (nobles/guards). Either way: Nineveh's elite will vanish like grasshoppers when the sun warms.
  • v.19 — "bruit" (KJV) = "news/report" (archaic English). Hebrew "shema" = report, rumor, fame. The news of Nineveh's fall spreads, and universal applause follows.

Reflection

  • 1. Sins have a cumulative weight (v.1). Nineveh's judgment came not for one act but for generations of unceasing cruelty — "the prey departeth not." Habitual sin accumulates a debt that will eventually come due. Repent before the account is full.
  • 2. No one is exempt from God's justice (v.8). "Are you better than Thebes?" — Nineveh thought its power made it immune. No nation, institution, or person is above God's reach. Past success does not guarantee future immunity.
  • 3. A life of cruelty produces complete isolation (v.7). When Nineveh falls, no one mourns. A life lived exploiting others builds no genuine relationships. On the day of your need, will anyone grieve for you — or will they clap?
  • 4. Leaders who fail their people ensure destruction (v.18). "Thy shepherds slumber, O king of Assyria." When leaders sleep — spiritually, morally, strategically — the people scatter. Leadership carries eternal weight. Negligent shepherds will answer for scattered sheep.
  • 5. Some wounds are incurable by human means (v.19). Nineveh's wound has "no healing." When God pronounces judgment final, no human remedy exists. But what is incurable for nations under judgment is curable for individuals who repent — the Cross heals what nothing else can (Isaiah 53:5).
