Miracles of Jesus

Healing the Blind Man

The Miracle

In John 9, Jesus encounters a man blind from birth — someone who had never seen light, color, or the faces of those he loved. The disciples asked whose sin caused this condition, reflecting the common assumption that disability was divine punishment. Jesus rejected this theology entirely: "It was not that this man sinned, or his parents, but that the works of God might be displayed in him" (John 9:3). Then He spat on the ground, made mud, anointed the man's eyes, and sent him to wash in the Pool of Siloam.

The man obeyed and came back seeing — a miracle unprecedented in Israel's history. The Pharisees, rather than celebrating, launched an investigation because the healing occurred on the Sabbath. They interrogated the man repeatedly, pressured his parents, and finally expelled him from the synagogue for defending Jesus. Meanwhile, the man's understanding of Jesus progressed throughout the chapter — from "a man called Jesus" to "a prophet" to "Lord, I believe" as he worshiped.

This miracle operates on two levels simultaneously. Physically, a man born blind receives sight. Spiritually, the narrative exposes who is truly blind: the Pharisees who see physically but cannot perceive the work of God happening before their eyes. Jesus declared, "For judgment I came into this world, that those who do not see may see, and those who see may become blind" (John 9:39). The miracle reveals that spiritual sight is the greater gift.

Key Scriptures

  • John 9:1-7 — Jesus heals the man born blind using mud and the Pool of Siloam.
  • John 9:25 — "One thing I do know, that though I was blind, now I see."
  • John 8:12 — "I am the light of the world. Whoever follows me will not walk in darkness, but will have the light of life."
  • Isaiah 42:7 — "To open the eyes that are blind, to bring out the prisoners from the dungeon."
  • 2 Corinthians 4:6 — "For God, who said, 'Let light shine out of darkness,' has shone in our hearts to give the light of the knowledge of the glory of God in the face of Jesus Christ."

What It Reveals About Jesus

This miracle reveals Jesus as the Light of the World — not merely a teacher of truth but the source of spiritual illumination itself. Just as God said "Let there be light" in creation, Jesus speaks light into the darkness of a man's lifelong blindness. He is the fulfillment of Isaiah's messianic prophecy: the servant who would "open the eyes that are blind." His power extends not just over disease but over the very mechanisms of perception and understanding.

The progressive revelation of Jesus' identity throughout the chapter — from man to prophet to Lord — mirrors the journey every believer takes. Encountering Jesus is not an instant arrival at full understanding but a progressive opening of the eyes. And significantly, the man's growing sight brought increasing opposition from religious authorities. True vision of Jesus often costs us the approval of those invested in spiritual blindness. But the man's final posture — worship — shows that seeing Jesus clearly is worth everything it costs.

Reflection Questions

  • What areas of spiritual blindness might you be unaware of — assumptions, prejudices, or misunderstandings about God?
  • How has your understanding of Jesus deepened progressively over time, and what opened your eyes at each stage?
  • Are there ways that religious tradition or cultural expectation might be preventing you from seeing what God is doing right now?
