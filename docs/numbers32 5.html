Numbers — Chapter 32

1Now the people of Reuben and the people of Gad had a very great number of livestock. And they saw the land of Jazer and the land of Gilead, and behold, the place was a place for livestock.

2So the people of Gad and the people of Reuben came and said to Moses and to Eleazar the priest and to the chiefs of the congregation,

3“Ataroth, Dibon, Jazer, Nimrah, Heshbon, Elealeh, Sebam, Nebo, and Beon,

4the land that the LORD struck down before the congregation of Israel, is a land for livestock, and your servants have livestock.”

5And they said, “If we have found favor in your sight, let this land be given to your servants for a possession. Do not take us across the Jordan.”

6But Moses said to the people of Gad and to the people of Reuben, “Shall your brothers go to the war while you sit here?

7Why will you discourage the heart of the people of Israel from going over into the land that the LORD has given them?

8Your fathers did this, when I sent them from Kadesh-barnea to see the land.

9For when they went up to the Valley of Eshcol and saw the land, they discouraged the heart of the people of Israel from going into the land that the LORD had given them.

10And the LORD’s anger was kindled on that day, and he swore, saying,

11‘Surely none of the men who came up out of Egypt, from twenty years old and upward, shall see the land that I swore to give to Abraham, to Isaac, and to Jacob, because they have not wholly followed me,

12none except Caleb the son of Jephunneh the Kenizzite and Joshua the son of Nun, for they have wholly followed the LORD.’

13And the LORD’s anger was kindled against Israel, and he made them wander in the wilderness forty years, until all the generation that had done evil in the sight of the LORD was gone.

14And behold, you have risen in your fathers’ place, a brood of sinful men, to increase still more the fierce anger of the LORD against Israel!

15For if you turn away from following him, he will again abandon them in the wilderness, and you will destroy all this people.”

16Then they came near to him and said, “We will build sheepfolds here for our livestock, and cities for our little ones,

17but we will take up arms, ready to go before the people of Israel, until we have brought them to their place. And our little ones shall live in the fortified cities because of the inhabitants of the land.

18We will not return to our homes until each of the people of Israel has gained his inheritance.

19For we will not inherit with them on the other side of the Jordan and beyond, because our inheritance has come to us on this side of the Jordan to the east.”

20So Moses said to them, “If you will do this, if you will take up arms to go before the LORD for the war,

21and every armed man of you will pass over the Jordan before the LORD, until he has driven out his enemies from before him

22and the land is subdued before the LORD; then after that you shall return and be free of obligation to the LORD and to Israel, and this land shall be your possession before the LORD.

23But if you will not do so, behold, you have sinned against the LORD, and be sure your sin will find you out.

24Build cities for your little ones and folds for your sheep, and do what you have promised.”

25And the people of Gad and the people of Reuben said to Moses, “Your servants will do as my lord commands.

26Our little ones, our wives, our livestock, and all our cattle shall remain there in the cities of Gilead,

27but your servants will pass over, every man who is armed for war, before the LORD to battle, as my lord orders.”

28So Moses gave command concerning them to Eleazar the priest and to Joshua the son of Nun and to the heads of the fathers’ houses of the tribes of the people of Israel.

29And Moses said to them, “If the people of Gad and the people of Reuben, every man who is armed to battle before the LORD, will pass with you over the Jordan and the land shall be subdued before you, then you shall give them the land of Gilead for a possession.

30However, if they will not pass over with you armed, they shall have possessions among you in the land of Canaan.”

31And the people of Gad and the people of Reuben answered, “What the LORD has said to your servants, we will do.

32We will pass over armed before the LORD into the land of Canaan, and the possession of our inheritance shall remain with us beyond the Jordan.”

33And Moses gave to them, to the people of Gad and to the people of Reuben and to the half-tribe of Manasseh the son of Joseph, the kingdom of Sihon king of the Amorites and the kingdom of Og king of Bashan, the land and its cities with their territories, the cities of the land throughout the country.

34And the people of Gad built Dibon, Ataroth, Aroer,

35Atroth-shophan, Jazer, Jogbehah,

36Beth-nimrah and Beth-haran, fortified cities, and folds for sheep.

37And the people of Reuben built Heshbon, Elealeh, Kiriathaim,

38Nebo, and Baal-meon (their names were changed), and Sibmah. And they gave other names to the cities that they built.

39And the sons of Machir the son of Manasseh went to Gilead and captured it, and dispossessed the Amorites who were in it.

40And Moses gave Gilead to Machir the son of Manasseh, and he settled in it.

41And Jair the son of Manasseh went and captured their villages, and called them Havvoth-jair.

42And Nobah went and captured Kenath and its villages, and called it Nobah, after his own name.

Summary

Reuben, Gad, and half of Manasseh request land east of the Jordan; Moses grants it on the condition they fight alongside their brothers.

Key Verses

v.5
"Do not take us across the Jordan." — The request that alarmed Moses. Settling short of the Promised Land — choosing comfort over calling.
v.6
"Shall your brethren go to war, and shall ye sit here?" — Moses' piercing question. Personal comfort at the expense of corporate responsibility is sin.
v.14
"Ye are risen up in your fathers' stead, an increase of sinful men, to augment yet the fierce anger of the LORD toward Israel." — Moses sees history repeating. The sins of the fathers threaten to recur in the children.
v.23
"Be sure your sin will find you out." — One of Scripture's most quoted warnings. Sin cannot be hidden — it will be exposed, whether in this life or the next.
v.32
"We will pass over armed before the LORD into the land of Canaan, that the possession of our inheritance on this side Jordan may be ours." — The compromise: they will fight for others' inheritance to secure their own.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/numbers-32/ Guzik notes that the eastern tribes' request was not inherently sinful — the land was legitimately conquered and suitable for their needs. The problem was the potential effect on the community: discouragement. He highlights that the compromise worked in the short term (they did fight) but the eastern tribes were the first to fall to foreign invasion and the first to adopt pagan worship (1 Chronicles 5:25-26).
  • Charles Spurgeon: "Be sure your sin will find you out. Not 'may' — 'will.' Sin is a bloodhound that never loses the scent. You may bury it deep, cover it with years of respectability, hide it behind religious activity — but it will find you. It found David after a year of silence. It found Achan under the floor of his tent. It found Ananias and Sapphira in the first church. There is no statute of limitations with God. The only escape is not concealment but confession: 'If we confess our sins, He is faithful and just to forgive us.'"

Translations

  • v.1 — KJV: "a very great multitude of cattle" / ESV: "a very great number of livestock" — Hebrew "miqneh rav." "Miqneh" includes all livestock (cattle, sheep, goats). ESV's "livestock" is more accurate than KJV's "cattle" (which in modern English suggests only bovines).
  • v.5 — KJV: "bring us not over Jordan" / ESV: "Do not take us across the Jordan" — Hebrew "al ta'avirenu et ha-Yarden." The request is direct and blunt. Both translations capture the force of the negative imperative — they are asking to be exempted from crossing.
  • v.14 — KJV: "an increase of sinful men" / ESV: "a brood of sinful men" — Hebrew "tarbut anashim chatta'im." "Tarbut" means increase, offspring, brood. ESV's "brood" is more vivid and carries a pejorative tone (like "brood of vipers" in Matthew 3:7). KJV's "increase" is more neutral but still negative in context.
  • v.17 — KJV: "we ourselves will go ready armed before the children of Israel" / ESV: "we will take up arms, ready to go before the people of Israel" — Hebrew "nachnu nechalets chushim lifnei b'nei Yisrael." "Chalets" means equipped, armed, ready for battle. "Chushim" means quickly, hastily. They will arm quickly and go first — as the vanguard, not the rear guard.
  • v.22 — KJV: "ye shall return, and be guiltless before the LORD, and before Israel" / ESV: "you shall return and be free of obligation to the LORD and to Israel" — Hebrew "v'heyitem n'qiyim me-YHWH u-me-Yisrael." "Naqiy" means clean, free, innocent. KJV's "guiltless" emphasizes moral innocence; ESV's "free of obligation" emphasizes release from duty. Both are valid — fulfilling the promise clears them both morally and contractually.
  • v.23 — KJV: "be sure your sin will find you out" / ESV: "be sure your sin will find you out" — Hebrew "u-d'u chattat'khem asher timtza etkhem." Identical in both translations. The Hebrew is vivid: sin is personified as a hunter that will "find" (matza) you. You cannot outrun your own sin.
  • v.33 — KJV: "half the tribe of Manasseh" / ESV: "the half-tribe of Manasseh" — The half-tribe of Manasseh appears here without prior mention in the negotiation. They may have joined the request later, or their inclusion reflects the military conquest of Gilead by Machir's clan (vv.39-42).

Reflection

  • 1. "Do not take us across the Jordan" (v.5). Are you settling for "good enough" when God has "best" waiting across your Jordan? The Transjordan was adequate — but it was not the Promised Land. What comfort or convenience is keeping you from God's full purpose?
  • 2. "Shall your brothers go to war while you sit here?" (v.6). Personal blessing carries corporate responsibility. You cannot enjoy God's provision while your brothers struggle and call yourself faithful. Where is God calling you to fight for others, not just yourself?
  • 3. "Your fathers did this" (v.8). Moses sees the pattern repeating. Generational sins recur unless consciously broken. What patterns from your family history are you in danger of repeating?
  • 4. "Be sure your sin will find you out" (v.23). This is not a threat but a fact of the moral universe. Hidden sin does not stay hidden. The question is not IF but WHEN. Is there unconfessed sin you are hoping will simply disappear? It won't.
  • 5. The compromise worked — partially (vv.16-19). The eastern tribes did fight (Joshua 4:12-13; 22:1-6). But historically, they were the first tribes to fall to paganism and exile (1 Chronicles 5:25-26). Settling short of God's best has long-term consequences even when short-term obedience is maintained.
  • 6. Moses reorders their priorities (v.24). They said "sheepfolds... and cities for our little ones" (v.16). Moses corrects: "cities for your little ones, and folds for your sheep" (v.24). Children before cattle. People before possessions. What are your true priorities — and does your life reflect them?
