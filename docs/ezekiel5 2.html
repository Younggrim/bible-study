Ezekiel — Chapter 5

1“And you, O son of man, take a sharp sword. Use it as a barber’s razor and pass it over your head and your beard. Then take balances for weighing and divide the hair.

2A third part you shall burn in the fire in the midst of the city, when the days of the siege are completed. And a third part you shall take and strike with the sword all around the city. And a third part you shall scatter to the wind, and I will unsheathe the sword after them.

3And you shall take from these a small number and bind them in the skirts of your robe.

4And of these again you shall take some and cast them into the midst of the fire and burn them in the fire. From there a fire will come out into all the house of Israel.

5“Thus says the Lord GOD: This is Jerusalem. I have set her in the center of the nations, with countries all around her.

6And she has rebelled against my rules by doing wickedness more than the nations, and against my statutes more than the countries all around her; for they have rejected my rules and have not walked in my statutes.

7Therefore thus says the Lord GOD: Because you are more turbulent than the nations that are all around you, and have not walked in my statutes or obeyed my rules, and have not even acted according to the rules of the nations that are all around you,

8therefore thus says the Lord GOD: Behold, I, even I, am against you. And I will execute judgments in your midst in the sight of the nations.

9And because of all your abominations I will do with you what I have never yet done, and the like of which I will never do again.

10Therefore fathers shall eat their sons in your midst, and sons shall eat their fathers. And I will execute judgments on you, and any of you who survive I will scatter to all the winds.

11Therefore, as I live, declares the Lord GOD, surely, because you have defiled my sanctuary with all your detestable things and with all your abominations, therefore I will withdraw. My eye will not spare, and I will have no pity.

12A third part of you shall die of pestilence and be consumed with famine in your midst; a third part shall fall by the sword all around you; and a third part I will scatter to all the winds and will unsheathe the sword after them.

13“Thus shall my anger spend itself, and I will vent my fury upon them and satisfy myself. And they shall know that I am the LORD—that I have spoken in my jealousy—when I spend my fury upon them.

14Moreover, I will make you a desolation and an object of reproach among the nations all around you and in the sight of all who pass by.

15You shall be a reproach and a taunt, a warning and a horror, to the nations all around you, when I execute judgments on you in anger and fury, and with furious rebukes—I am the LORD; I have spoken—

16when I send against you the deadly arrows of famine, arrows for destruction, which I will send to destroy you, and when I bring more and more famine upon you and break your supply of bread.

17I will send famine and wild beasts against you, and they will rob you of your children. Pestilence and blood shall pass through you, and I will bring the sword upon you. I am the LORD; I have spoken.”

Summary

God commands Ezekiel to shave his head and beard with a sword, then divide the hair into thirds: one third burned in the city-model, one third struck with the sword around the city, one third scattered to the wind. A tiny remnant is bound in the prophet's garment — but even some of THOSE are thrown into fire. Jerusalem will be made a horror: "I will do in thee that which I have not done."

Key Verses

  • v.5 — "This is Jerusalem: I have set it in the midst of the nations" (KJV) — Jerusalem's PRIVILEGE: placed at the CENTER of nations, given God's law as a light to others. Greater privilege = greater accountability.
  • v.6 — "And she hath changed my judgments into wickedness more than the nations" (KJV) — Not just as bad as the pagans — WORSE. Having God's law and rejecting it is worse than never having it at all.
  • v.9 — "And I will do in thee that which I have not done, and whereunto I will not do any more the like, because of all thine abominations" (KJV) — UNPRECEDENTED judgment. Never before, never again in the same way. The siege of 586 BC was uniquely terrible because of uniquely great sin.
  • v.11 — "Surely, because thou hast defiled my sanctuary with all thy detestable things... therefore will I also diminish thee; neither shall mine eye spare" (KJV) — The trigger: defiling GOD'S SANCTUARY. They brought abominations into the HOLY PLACE. God's eye will NOT spare — no mercy clause.
  • v.13 — "Thus shall mine anger be accomplished, and I will cause my fury to rest upon them, and I will be comforted" (KJV) — God's fury has an ENDPOINT: it will be "accomplished" (completed), and God Himself will be "comforted" (satisfied that justice is done). Wrath is not infinite.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): enduringword.com
  • Charles Spurgeon: "'I will do in thee that which I have not done.' Unprecedented sin produces unprecedented judgment. If your rebellion is unique in its audacity, the consequences will be unique in their severity. God matches penalty to crime with terrifying precision."

Translations

  • v.1 → "take thee a sharp knife, take thee a barber's razor" (KJV) / "take a sharp sword... use it as a barber's razor" (ESV) — A SWORD used as a razor. The instrument of WAR becomes the instrument of SHAMING. Military destruction and personal humiliation merged in one image.
  • v.3 → "bind... in thy skirts" (KJV) / "bind in the skirts of your robe" (ESV) — Hebrew "kanfey" = the wings/corners of the garment. A few hairs tucked into the robe's hem = the tiny remnant preserved close to the prophet (close to God).
  • v.10 → "fathers shall eat the sons" (KJV/ESV) — Siege cannibalism: parents eating children AND children eating parents. The most extreme horror of prolonged starvation.
  • v.13 → "I will be comforted" (KJV) / "I will be satisfied" (ESV) — Hebrew "ve-hinachamti" = I will be comforted/settled/ satisfied. God's wrath reaches its GOAL and STOPS. It's not eternal or purposeless.

Reflection

  • 1. Greater privilege = greater accountability (vv.5-6). You have the FULL Bible, church history, Christian community — more spiritual privilege than ancient Israel had. Are you using it or ignoring it? The nations are more excusable than you.
  • 2. WORSE than pagans (v.6). A professing Christian who lives worse than non-Christians is in a UNIQUELY dangerous position. Having light and choosing darkness is graver than never having light at all.
  • 3. God's fury has an endpoint (v.13). Wrath is not purposeless rage — it reaches its goal and STOPS. If you're under discipline, it won't last forever. God's anger is measured and completed.
  • 4. A few hairs bound in the garment (v.3). Even in the most severe judgment, God preserves a tiny remnant. YOU might be one of those tucked into God's garment — spared while everything around you burns.
  • 5. They defiled God's sanctuary (v.11). What TRIGGERED this extreme judgment: bringing abominations into the holy place. Is there anything unholy that you've brought into your relationship with God — your worship, your prayer life, your inner sanctuary?
