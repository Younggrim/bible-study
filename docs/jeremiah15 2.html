Jeremiah — Chapter 15

1Then the LORD said to me, “Though Moses and Samuel stood before me, yet my heart would not turn toward this people. Send them out of my sight, and let them go!

2And when they ask you, ‘Where shall we go?’ you shall say to them, ‘Thus says the LORD: “‘Those who are for pestilence, to pestilence, and those who are for the sword, to the sword; those who are for famine, to famine, and those who are for captivity, to captivity.’

3I will appoint over them four kinds of destroyers, declares the LORD: the sword to kill, the dogs to tear, and the birds of the air and the beasts of the earth to devour and destroy.

4And I will make them a horror to all the kingdoms of the earth because of what Manasseh the son of Hezekiah, king of Judah, did in Jerusalem.

5“Who will have pity on you, O Jerusalem, or who will grieve for you? Who will turn aside to ask about your welfare?

6You have rejected me, declares the LORD; you keep going backward, so I have stretched out my hand against you and destroyed you— I am weary of relenting.

7I have winnowed them with a winnowing fork in the gates of the land; I have bereaved them; I have destroyed my people; they did not turn from their ways.

8I have made their widows more in number than the sand of the seas; I have brought against the mothers of young men a destroyer at noonday; I have made anguish and terror fall upon them suddenly.

9She who bore seven has grown feeble; she has fainted away; her sun went down while it was yet day; she has been shamed and disgraced. And the rest of them I will give to the sword before their enemies, declares the LORD.”

10Woe is me, my mother, that you bore me, a man of strife and contention to the whole land! I have not lent, nor have I borrowed, yet all of them curse me.

11The LORD said, “Have I not set you free for their good? Have I not pleaded for you before the enemy in the time of trouble and in the time of distress?

12Can one break iron, iron from the north, and bronze?

13“Your wealth and your treasures I will give as spoil, without price, for all your sins, throughout all your territory.

14I will make you serve your enemies in a land that you do not know, for in my anger a fire is kindled that shall burn forever.”

15O LORD, you know; remember me and visit me, and take vengeance for me on my persecutors. In your forbearance take me not away; know that for your sake I bear reproach.

16Your words were found, and I ate them, and your words became to me a joy and the delight of my heart, for I am called by your name, O LORD, God of hosts.

17I did not sit in the company of revelers, nor did I rejoice; I sat alone, because your hand was upon me, for you had filled me with indignation.

18Why is my pain unceasing, my wound incurable, refusing to be healed? Will you be to me like a deceitful brook, like waters that fail?

19Therefore thus says the LORD: “If you return, I will restore you, and you shall stand before me. If you utter what is precious, and not what is worthless, you shall be as my mouth. They shall turn to you, but you shall not turn to them.

20And I will make you to this people a fortified wall of bronze; they will fight against you, but they shall not prevail over you, for I am with you to save you and deliver you, declares the LORD.

21I will deliver you out of the hand of the wicked, and redeem you from the grasp of the ruthless.”

Summary

Even if Moses and Samuel stood before God, He wouldn't relent. Four destroyers are appointed: sword, dogs, birds, beasts. Then Jeremiah's most raw confession: "Woe is me, my mother, that thou hast borne me!" He accuses God of being an unreliable brook — God responds: repent of self-pity, and I will restore you. "I will make thee unto this people a fenced brasen wall."

Key Verses

v.1
"Then said the LORD unto me, Though Moses and Samuel stood before me, yet my mind could not be toward this people" (KJV) — The two greatest intercessors in Israel's history would FAIL if they tried. The decision is irreversible.
v.10
"Woe is me, my mother, that thou hast borne me a man of strife and a man of contention to the whole earth!" (KJV) — Jeremiah curses his existence. Not just his ministry but his BIRTH. The cost of prophetic calling driven to its emotional limit.
v.16
"Thy words were found, and I did eat them; and thy word was unto me the joy and rejoicing of mine heart: for I am called by thy name, O LORD God of hosts" (KJV) — The prophet's core identity: God's word consumed, God's name worn. Despite everything, God's word is JOY.
v.18
"Why is my pain perpetual, and my wound incurable, which refuseth to be healed? wilt thou be altogether unto me as a liar, and as waters that fail?" (KJV) — The most audacious accusation: Jeremiah calls God a "liar" and an "unreliable stream." Raw honesty bordering on blasphemy.
v.19
"Therefore thus saith the LORD, If thou return, then will I bring thee again, and thou shalt stand before me" (KJV) — God doesn't comfort Jeremiah — He corrects him. "IF you repent of this self-pity, I'll RESTORE your ministry." Even prophets need repentance.
v.20
"And I will make thee unto this people a fenced brasen wall: and they shall fight against thee, but they shall not prevail against thee: for I am with thee to save thee" (KJV) — After correction: recommissioning. Jeremiah will be a BRONZE WALL — unbreakable. Opposition will continue but won't succeed.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/jeremiah-15/
  • Charles Spurgeon: "'Thy words were found, and I did eat them.' Jeremiah CONSUMED Scripture — not read it, not studied it, ATE it. Made it part of himself. And even in his darkest moment, he remembers: God's word was joy. That memory is his lifeline."

Translations

  • v.1 — "my mind could not be toward" (KJV) / "I would not relent" (ESV) — Hebrew "en nafshi el" = my soul is not toward/ inclined toward. God's disposition is TURNED AWAY from this people. Not hate but judicial withdrawal.
  • v.16 — "were found" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "nimtse'u" = were found/ discovered. Possibly referencing the discovery of the Book of the Law under Josiah (2 Kings 22:8). Jeremiah consumed the newly-found Scripture with joy.
  • v.18 — "waters that fail" (KJV) / "a deceitful brook" (ESV) — Hebrew "akhzav" = deceptive (waters that dry up when you need them most). A wadi that flows in winter but disappears in summer drought — precisely when you need it.
  • v.19 — "if thou return" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "im tashuv" = if you repent/return. God applies the "return" message to THE PROPHET HIMSELF. Even the messenger of repentance needs repentance.

Reflection

  • 1. "Thy words were found, and I did eat them" (v.16). In your darkest moment, what sustains you? For Jeremiah, it was the memory of consuming God's word with JOY. If you haven't stored that joy, darkness will have nothing to draw on.
  • 2. Jeremiah accuses God of being unreliable (v.18). And God doesn't strike him dead — He CORRECTS him. God can handle your honest accusations. He'd rather you yell at Him than walk away silently. Bring your rage.
  • 3. God corrects the prophet (v.19). Even the most faithful servants need correction. Jeremiah slid into self-pity and God said "repent." Being in ministry doesn't exempt you from personal repentance. Nobody is above correction.
  • 4. Opposition will come but not overcome (v.20). God promises BRONZE WALL status — not absence of attack but inability to be defeated. Expect fighting. Expect pressure. But expect to STAND.
  • 5. Even Moses and Samuel couldn't help (v.1). There are moments when even the best human intercession is insufficient. Some situations require direct divine intervention that transcends human prayer. This is why we need CHRIST's intercession, not just human prophets'.
