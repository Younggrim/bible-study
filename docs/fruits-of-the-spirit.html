Fruits of the Spirit

"But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, self-control; against such things there is no law."

— Galatians 5:22-23 (ESV)

Love

agape (ἀγάπη)

Unconditional, sacrificial love that mirrors God's love for humanity.

Joy

chara (χαρά)

A deep-rooted gladness anchored in God's character, not in circumstances.

Peace

eirene (εἰρήνη)

Wholeness and tranquility that comes from being reconciled to God.

Patience

makrothumia (μακροθυμία)

Long-suffering endurance that bears with others as God bears with us.

Kindness

chrestotes (χρηστότης)

A generous, gracious disposition that seeks to bless others practically.

Goodness

agathosune (ἀγαθωσύνη)

Moral excellence and integrity expressed through righteous action.

Faithfulness

pistis (πίστις)

Trustworthiness and reliability that reflects God's covenant loyalty.

Gentleness

prautes (πραΰτης)

Strength under control — meekness that is powerful yet yielded to God.

Self-Control

egkrateia (ἐγκράτεια)

Mastery over desires and impulses through the power of the Holy Spirit.
