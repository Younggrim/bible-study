Psalms — Chapter 84

1How lovely is your dwelling place, O LORD of hosts!

2My soul longs, yes, faints for the courts of the LORD; my heart and flesh sing for joy to the living God.

3Even the sparrow finds a home, and the swallow a nest for herself, where she may lay her young, at your altars, O LORD of hosts, my King and my God.

4Blessed are those who dwell in your house, ever singing your praise! Selah

5Blessed are those whose strength is in you, in whose heart are the highways to Zion.

6As they go through the Valley of Baca they make it a place of springs; the early rain also covers it with pools.

7They go from strength to strength; each one appears before God in Zion.

8O LORD God of hosts, hear my prayer; give ear, O God of Jacob! Selah

9Behold our shield, O God; look on the face of your anointed!

10For a day in your courts is better than a thousand elsewhere. I would rather be a doorkeeper in the house of my God than dwell in the tents of wickedness.

11For the LORD God is a sun and shield; the LORD bestows favor and honor. No good thing does he withhold from those who walk uprightly.

12O LORD of hosts, blessed is the one who trusts in you!

Summary

A pilgrimage psalm of the Sons of Korah — 'How amiable are thy tabernacles, O LORD'; the psalmist longs for God's courts, envies the sparrow nesting near the altar; 'a day in thy courts is better than a thousand elsewhere.'

Key Verses

v.2
"My soul longeth, yea, even fainteth for the courts of the LORD: my heart and my flesh crieth out for the living God." (KJV) — Total-person desire: soul, heart, flesh. Not intellectual interest but visceral longing. The God they long for is "living" — not an idol but the God who is actively alive and present.
v.6
"Who passing through the valley of Baca make it a well; the rain also filleth the pools." (KJV) — Pilgrims of faith transform dry places into springs. The journey itself creates blessing. Tears become wells. God turns the path of difficulty into a source of refreshment.
v.10
"For a day in thy courts is better than a thousand. I had rather be a doorkeeper in the house of my God, than to dwell in the tents of wickedness." (KJV) — The ultimate valuation: one day with God > 1000 days elsewhere. The lowest position in God's house > the highest position outside it. Proximity to God outweighs everything else.
v.11
"For the LORD God is a sun and shield: the LORD will give grace and glory: no good thing will he withhold from them that walk uprightly." (KJV) — God is BOTH sun (source of life, warmth, growth) AND shield (protection, defense). He gives grace (for today) AND glory (for eternity). He withholds NO good thing from the upright.

Translations

  • v.1 — KJV: "How amiable are thy tabernacles" / ESV: "How lovely is your dwelling place" — Hebrew "yedidot" = beloved, lovely, desirable. "Amiable" (KJV) is archaic; ESV's "lovely" better conveys the warmth of the Hebrew.
  • v.6 — KJV: "the valley of Baca" / ESV: "the Valley of Baca" — "Baca" may mean weeping (from bakah) or balsam trees (which grow in arid places). Either way, it represents a dry, difficult stretch on the pilgrimage route that faith transforms.
  • v.7 — KJV: "They go from strength to strength" / ESV: same — Unlike worldly journeys that deplete, this pilgrimage INCREASES strength. Each stage of the journey adds vigor rather than subtracting it. Spiritual pilgrimage energizes.

Reflection

  • 1. Long for God's presence with your whole being (v.2). Soul, heart, flesh — let every part of you reach for God. Worship is not just spiritual — your body, your emotions, your will should all strain toward Him.
  • 2. Faith transforms deserts into springs (v.6). Whatever "Valley of Baca" you're passing through, faith can make it a well. Tears can become the water that refreshes you and others. Don't waste your valleys.
  • 3. One day with God outweighs everything (v.10). Recalibrate your values. The lowest place in God's presence is better than the highest place outside it. Choose proximity over promotion.
  • 4. God withholds no good thing from the upright (v.11). If He hasn't given it to you, either it's not good, it's not time, or your walk needs adjustment. Trust His withholding as much as His giving — both are love.
  • 5. The journey goes from strength to strength (v.7). God's path doesn't exhaust you — it empowers you. If your spiritual life feels depleting, check your direction. The right path adds strength at every stage.
