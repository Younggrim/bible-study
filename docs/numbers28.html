Numbers — Chapter 28

1The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

2“Command the people of Israel and say to them, ‘My offering, my food for my food offerings, my pleasing aroma, you shall be careful to offer to me at its appointed time.’

3And you shall say to them, This is the food offering that you shall offer to the LORD: two male lambs a year old without blemish, day by day, as a regular offering.

4The one lamb you shall offer in the morning, and the other lamb you shall offer at twilight;

5also a tenth of an ephah of fine flour for a grain offering, mixed with a quarter of a hin of beaten oil.

6It is a regular burnt offering, which was ordained at Mount Sinai for a pleasing aroma, a food offering to the LORD.

7Its drink offering shall be a quarter of a hin for each lamb. In the Holy Place you shall pour out a drink offering of strong drink to the LORD.

8The other lamb you shall offer at twilight. Like the grain offering of the morning, and like its drink offering, you shall offer it as a food offering, with a pleasing aroma to the LORD.

9“On the Sabbath day, two male lambs a year old without blemish, and two tenths of an ephah of fine flour for a grain offering, mixed with oil, and its drink offering:

10this is the burnt offering of every Sabbath, besides the regular burnt offering and its drink offering.

11“At the beginnings of your months, you shall offer a burnt offering to the LORD: two bulls from the herd, one ram, seven male lambs a year old without blemish;

12also three tenths of an ephah of fine flour for a grain offering, mixed with oil, for each bull, and two tenths of fine flour for a grain offering, mixed with oil, for the one ram;

13and a tenth of fine flour mixed with oil as a grain offering for every lamb; for a burnt offering with a pleasing aroma, a food offering to the LORD.

14Their drink offerings shall be half a hin of wine for a bull, a third of a hin for a ram, and a quarter of a hin for a lamb. This is the burnt offering of each month throughout the months of the year.

15Also one male goat for a sin offering to the LORD; it shall be offered besides the regular burnt offering and its drink offering.

16“On the fourteenth day of the first month is the LORD’s Passover,

17and on the fifteenth day of this month is a feast. Seven days shall unleavened bread be eaten.

18On the first day there shall be a holy convocation. You shall not do any ordinary work,

19but offer a food offering, a burnt offering to the LORD: two bulls from the herd, one ram, and seven male lambs a year old; see that they are without blemish;

20also their grain offering of fine flour mixed with oil; three tenths of an ephah shall you offer for a bull, and two tenths for a ram;

21a tenth shall you offer for each of the seven lambs;

22also one male goat for a sin offering, to make atonement for you.

23You shall offer these besides the burnt offering of the morning, which is for a regular burnt offering.

24In the same way you shall offer daily, for seven days, the food of a food offering, with a pleasing aroma to the LORD. It shall be offered besides the regular burnt offering and its drink offering.

25And on the seventh day you shall have a holy convocation. You shall not do any ordinary work.

26“On the day of the firstfruits, when you offer a grain offering of new grain to the LORD at your Feast of Weeks, you shall have a holy convocation. You shall not do any ordinary work,

27but offer a burnt offering, with a pleasing aroma to the LORD: two bulls from the herd, one ram, seven male lambs a year old;

28also their grain offering of fine flour mixed with oil, three tenths of an ephah for each bull, two tenths for one ram,

29a tenth for each of the seven lambs;

30with one male goat, to make atonement for you.

31Besides the regular burnt offering and its grain offering, you shall offer them and their drink offering. See that they are without blemish.

Summary

God reiterates the daily, weekly, and monthly offerings — a rhythm of continual worship that keeps Israel's relationship with Him at the center.

Key Verses

v.2
"My offering, and my bread for my sacrifices made by fire, for a sweet savour unto me, shall ye observe to offer unto me in their due season." — God claims the offerings as "mine" — worship is not optional but obligatory, and timing matters ("due season").
v.3
"Two lambs of the first year without spot day by day, for a continual burnt offering." — Two lambs daily, 365 days a year, 730 lambs annually — the relentless cost of maintaining fellowship with a holy God. Each one points to the Lamb of God.
v.6
"It is a continual burnt offering, which was ordained in mount Sinai." — Continuity: what God established at Sinai remains in force. God's worship requirements do not change with geography.
v.10
"This is the burnt offering of every sabbath, beside the continual burnt offering." — The Sabbath adds to the daily — rest does not mean less worship but more.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/numbers-28/ Guzik emphasizes the relentless regularity of the sacrificial system — every morning and evening, every week, every month, every festival. The sheer volume of blood and sacrifice taught Israel that sin is serious and access to God is costly. All of it points to Christ's sufficient, once-for-all sacrifice.
  • Charles Spurgeon: "Morning and evening, a lamb. Day after day, year after year, the knife fell and the blood flowed. Was God satisfied? Never — for the next morning required another lamb. The repetition was the confession of insufficiency. But when the Lamb of God hung upon the cross, He cried 'It is finished!' — and no morning lamb was ever needed again. The continual offering has found its end in the one offering that continues forever."

Translations

  • v.2 — KJV: "My offering, and my bread for my sacrifices made by fire, for a sweet savour unto me" / ESV: "My offering, my food for my food offerings, my pleasing aroma" — Hebrew "qorbani lachmi l'ishai re'ach nichochi." "Lechem" (bread/food) is used metaphorically — God does not eat (Psalm 50:12-13). KJV's "bread" is more literal; ESV's "food" is clearer for modern readers. Both "sweet savour" (KJV) and "pleasing aroma" (ESV) translate "re'ach nichoach" — the scent of satisfaction/acceptance.
  • v.4 — KJV: "at even" / ESV: "at twilight" — Hebrew "bein ha-arbayim" (literally "between the two evenings"). This phrase is debated: either between noon and sunset, or between sunset and dark. ESV's "twilight" captures the general sense; KJV's "even" (evening) is traditional.
  • v.5 — KJV: "a meat offering" / ESV: "a grain offering" — Hebrew "minchah." In KJV English, "meat" meant "food" generally (not flesh). The minchah is a grain/flour offering. ESV's "grain offering" prevents confusion for modern readers. This is a significant KJV archaism.
  • v.7 — KJV: "the strong wine" / ESV: "a drink offering of strong drink" — Hebrew "shekhar." Shekhar is fermented drink (beer, strong wine, or other intoxicant). It is poured out to God, not consumed by the worshiper. The drink offering symbolizes life poured out completely.
  • v.14 — KJV: "half an hin of wine unto a bullock" / ESV: "half a hin of wine for a bull" — A hin is approximately 6 quarts/liters. The drink offerings are proportional to the animal's size: 1/2 hin for a bull, 1/3 for a ram, 1/4 for a lamb.
  • v.18 — KJV: "ye shall do no manner of servile work" / ESV: "You shall not do any ordinary work" — Hebrew "kol m'lekhet avodah lo ta'asu." "Servile work" (KJV) means laborious/occupational work. ESV's "ordinary work" is clearer — regular daily labor ceases, but festival preparation (cooking, etc.) is permitted.
  • v.26 — KJV: "in the day of the firstfruits, when ye bring a new meat offering unto the LORD, after your weeks be out" / ESV: "On the day of the firstfruits, when you offer a grain offering of new grain to the LORD at your Feast of Weeks" — ESV explicitly names the festival ("Feast of Weeks"); KJV describes it ("after your weeks be out"). Both refer to Shavuot/Pentecost — 50 days after the wave sheaf of Firstfruits.

Reflection

  • 1. "My offering... in their due season" (v.2). God prescribes both the content and the timing of worship. Worship is not merely spontaneous emotion — it is disciplined, regular, and ordered. Do you have a "due season" for meeting with God, or only when you feel like it?
  • 2. Morning and evening, every day (vv.3-4). The day begins and ends with sacrifice. Before any work is done, a lamb dies. Before sleep comes, blood is shed. The Christian life is similarly framed: begin and end each day at the cross.
  • 3. The Sabbath doubles the offering (vv.9-10). Rest is not less worship but more. The day of ceasing from labor is the day of increased sacrifice. True rest is found not in inactivity but in intensified communion with God.
  • 4. The relentless repetition (vv.3, 9, 11, 16, 26). Daily, weekly, monthly, annually — the sacrifices never stop. This repetition confesses insufficiency: no single offering is enough. Only Christ's once-for-all sacrifice ends the cycle (Hebrews 10:11-14).
  • 5. "Without blemish" — repeated throughout. Every animal must be perfect. God accepts no defective worship. This points to Christ, "a lamb without blemish and without spot" (1 Peter 1:19). Are you offering God your best, or your leftovers?
  • 6. The drink offering poured out (v.7). Wine poured at the altar — not consumed but given entirely. Paul describes his life as a drink offering (Philippians 2:17; 2 Timothy 4:6). Total self-giving, holding nothing back. Is your life being poured out for God's purposes?
