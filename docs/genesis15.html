Genesis — Chapter 15

1After these things the word of the LORD came to Abram in a vision: “Fear not, Abram, I am your shield; your reward shall be very great.”

2But Abram said, “O Lord GOD, what will you give me, for I continue childless, and the heir of my house is Eliezer of Damascus?”

3And Abram said, “Behold, you have given me no offspring, and a member of my household will be my heir.”

4And behold, the word of the LORD came to him: “This man shall not be your heir; your very own son shall be your heir.”

5And he brought him outside and said, “Look toward heaven, and number the stars, if you are able to number them.” Then he said to him, “So shall your offspring be.”

6And he believed the LORD, and he counted it to him as righteousness.

7And he said to him, “I am the LORD who brought you out from Ur of the Chaldeans to give you this land to possess.”

8But he said, “O Lord GOD, how am I to know that I shall possess it?”

9He said to him, “Bring me a heifer three years old, a female goat three years old, a ram three years old, a turtledove, and a young pigeon.”

10And he brought him all these, cut them in half, and laid each half over against the other. But he did not cut the birds in half.

11And when birds of prey came down on the carcasses, Abram drove them away.

12As the sun was going down, a deep sleep fell on Abram. And behold, dreadful and great darkness fell upon him.

13Then the LORD said to Abram, “Know for certain that your offspring will be sojourners in a land that is not theirs and will be servants there, and they will be afflicted for four hundred years.

14But I will bring judgment on the nation that they serve, and afterward they shall come out with great possessions.

15As for you, you shall go to your fathers in peace; you shall be buried in a good old age.

16And they shall come back here in the fourth generation, for the iniquity of the Amorites is not yet complete.”

17When the sun had gone down and it was dark, behold, a smoking fire pot and a flaming torch passed between these pieces.

18On that day the LORD made a covenant with Abram, saying, “To your offspring I give this land, from the river of Egypt to the great river, the river Euphrates,

19the land of the Kenites, the Kenizzites, the Kadmonites,

20the Hittites, the Perizzites, the Rephaim,

21the Amorites, the Canaanites, the Girgashites and the Jebusites.”

Summary

God cuts a covenant with Abram, promising innumerable descendants and the land, and Abram believes — counted to him as righteousness.

Authorship & Background

Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).
Historical Context: Chapter 15 is one of the most theologically significant chapters in the OT. It contains: (1) God's self-revelation as Abram's shield and reward (v.1), (2) the promise of a biological heir (v.4), (3) Abram's faith counted as righteousness (v.6) — the foundational text for justification by faith, and (4) the covenant ceremony where God alone passes between the pieces (vv.9-21) — making the covenant unconditional. This chapter is quoted extensively by Paul in Romans 4 and Galatians 3.
God's Promise and Abram's Faith (vv.1-6): "Fear not, Abram: I am thy shield, and thy exceeding great reward" (v.1). Abram's concern: he's still childless (vv.2-3). God promises a biological heir (v.4). "Look now toward heaven, and tell the stars... so shall thy seed be" (v.5). "And he believed in the LORD; and he counted it to him for righteousness" (v.6) — THE verse on justification by faith.
The Covenant Ceremony (vv.7-21): Abram asks: "Whereby shall I know?" (v.8). God commands a sacrifice: animals cut in half, pieces arranged (vv.9-10). A deep sleep and "horror of great darkness" falls on Abram (v.12). God reveals the future: 400 years of slavery, then deliverance (vv.13-14). A smoking furnace and burning lamp pass between the pieces (v.17) — GOD ALONE walks through, making this a unilateral, unconditional covenant. The land promise defined: from the river of Egypt to the Euphrates (v.18).

Map & Geography

  • "From the river of Egypt to the great river, the river Euphrates" (v.18) — the boundaries of the Promised Land defined. The "river of Egypt" is likely the Wadi el-Arish (not the Nile), marking the southwestern border.
  • The Euphrates marks the northeastern boundary — an enormous territory that Israel only briefly controlled under Solomon (1 Kings 4:21).
  • Ten nations listed (vv.19-21): Kenites, Kenizzites, Kadmonites, Hittites, Perizzites, Rephaims, Amorites, Canaanites, Girgashites, Jebusites — all occupying the land between these boundaries.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): enduringword.com Guzik covers God as shield/reward, the star promise, justification by faith, the covenant ceremony, and the land promise.
  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "Abram's Faith Counted for Righteousness" on v.6 (MTP Vol. 14) "He BELIEVED — not worked, not earned, not achieved — believed. In the LORD — not in himself, not in circumstances, not in probability — in the LORD. And HE (God) counted IT (faith) to HIM (Abram) for RIGHTEOUSNESS. This is the gospel before the gospel: righteousness credited to the account of one who simply believes God's promise. Not righteousness earned but righteousness received. Not works rewarded but faith credited. This is how every sinner is justified — by believing God." Sermon: "The Smoking Furnace and Burning Lamp" on v.17 (MTP Vol. 9, No. 528) "God ALONE passed between the pieces. In ancient covenants, both parties walked through — pledging 'may I be cut in pieces if I break this covenant.' But here, Abram sleeps. Only God walks through. This covenant depends on God alone. If it fails, GOD is cut in pieces — not Abram. And at the cross, God WAS 'cut' — Christ's body broken to keep the covenant Abram could never keep. Unconditional. Unbreakable. God's alone to fulfill."

Reflection

  • 1. "I am thy shield, and thy exceeding great reward" (v.1). Is God Himself enough for you? Not just His gifts but HIM? He is both your protection and your prize.
  • 2. "He believed in the LORD; and he counted it to him for righteousness" (v.6). This is how you are saved — by believing God's promise. Not by works but by faith. Have you believed? Is His righteousness credited to your account?
  • 3. God alone passed between the pieces (v.17). Your salvation depends on God's faithfulness, not yours. The covenant is unconditional — God took full responsibility. Does this give you security?
  • 4. "Thy seed shall be a stranger... four hundred years" (v.13). God told Abram the future would include suffering BEFORE fulfillment. Are you in a "four hundred years" season — waiting, suffering, before the promise is fulfilled? God sees the whole timeline.
  • 5. "The iniquity of the Amorites is not yet full" (v.16). God is patient even with the wicked — giving time for repentance. But there is a limit. How does God's patience with evil encourage and challenge you?
