Ezekiel — Chapter 48

1“These are the names of the tribes: Beginning at the northern extreme, beside the way of Hethlon to Lebo-hamath, as far as Hazar-enan (which is on the northern border of Damascus over against Hamath), and extending from the east side to the west, Dan, one portion.

2Adjoining the territory of Dan, from the east side to the west, Asher, one portion.

3Adjoining the territory of Asher, from the east side to the west, Naphtali, one portion.

4Adjoining the territory of Naphtali, from the east side to the west, Manasseh, one portion.

5Adjoining the territory of Manasseh, from the east side to the west, Ephraim, one portion.

6Adjoining the territory of Ephraim, from the east side to the west, Reuben, one portion.

7Adjoining the territory of Reuben, from the east side to the west, Judah, one portion.

8“Adjoining the territory of Judah, from the east side to the west, shall be the portion which you shall set apart, 25,000 cubits in breadth, and in length equal to one of the tribal portions, from the east side to the west, with the sanctuary in the midst of it.

9The portion that you shall set apart for the LORD shall be 25,000 cubits in length, and 20,000 in breadth.

10These shall be the allotments of the holy portion: the priests shall have an allotment measuring 25,000 cubits on the northern side, 10,000 cubits in breadth on the western side, 10,000 in breadth on the eastern side, and 25,000 in length on the southern side, with the sanctuary of the LORD in the midst of it.

11This shall be for the consecrated priests, the sons of Zadok, who kept my charge, who did not go astray when the people of Israel went astray, as the Levites did.

12And it shall belong to them as a special portion from the holy portion of the land, a most holy place, adjoining the territory of the Levites.

13And alongside the territory of the priests, the Levites shall have an allotment 25,000 cubits in length and 10,000 in breadth. The whole length shall be 25,000 cubits and the breadth 20,000.

14They shall not sell or exchange any of it. They shall not alienate this choice portion of the land, for it is holy to the LORD.

15“The remainder, 5,000 cubits in breadth and 25,000 in length, shall be for common use for the city, for dwellings and for open country. In the midst of it shall be the city,

16and these shall be its measurements: the north side 4,500 cubits, the south side 4,500, the east side 4,500, and the west side 4,500.

17And the city shall have open land: on the north 250 cubits, on the south 250, on the east 250, and on the west 250.

18The remainder of the length alongside the holy portion shall be 10,000 cubits to the east, and 10,000 to the west, and it shall be alongside the holy portion. Its produce shall be food for the workers of the city.

19And the workers of the city, from all the tribes of Israel, shall till it.

20The whole portion that you shall set apart shall be 25,000 cubits square, that is, the holy portion together with the property of the city.

21“What remains on both sides of the holy portion and of the property of the city shall belong to the prince. Extending from the 25,000 cubits of the holy portion to the east border, and westward from the 25,000 cubits to the west border, parallel to the tribal portions, it shall belong to the prince. The holy portion with the sanctuary of the temple shall be in its midst.

22It shall be separate from the property of the Levites and the property of the city, which are in the midst of that which belongs to the prince. The portion of the prince shall lie between the territory of Judah and the territory of Benjamin.

23“As for the rest of the tribes: from the east side to the west, Benjamin, one portion.

24Adjoining the territory of Benjamin, from the east side to the west, Simeon, one portion.

25Adjoining the territory of Simeon, from the east side to the west, Issachar, one portion.

26Adjoining the territory of Issachar, from the east side to the west, Zebulun, one portion.

27Adjoining the territory of Zebulun, from the east side to the west, Gad, one portion.

28And adjoining the territory of Gad to the south, the boundary shall run from Tamar to the waters of Meribah-kadesh, from there along the Brook of Egypt to the Great Sea.

29This is the land that you shall allot as an inheritance among the tribes of Israel, and these are their portions, declares the Lord GOD.

30“These shall be the exits of the city: On the north side, which is to be 4,500 cubits by measure,

31three gates, the gate of Reuben, the gate of Judah, and the gate of Levi, the gates of the city being named after the tribes of Israel.

32On the east side, which is to be 4,500 cubits, three gates, the gate of Joseph, the gate of Benjamin, and the gate of Dan.

33On the south side, which is to be 4,500 cubits by measure, three gates, the gate of Simeon, the gate of Issachar, and the gate of Zebulun.

34On the west side, which is to be 4,500 cubits, three gates, the gate of Gad, the gate of Asher, and the gate of Naphtali.

35The circumference of the city shall be 18,000 cubits. And the name of the city from that time on shall be, The LORD Is There.”

Summary

The final chapter of Ezekiel divides the promised land among the twelve tribes in parallel horizontal strips running east to west, with the holy district (containing the temple, priests' territory, Levites' territory, and the city) at the center. The chapter — and the entire book — concludes with the most glorious name in all of Scripture for the city: "THE LORD IS THERE" (YHWH-Shammah). After 48 chapters of judgment, exile, restoration, and temple vision, Ezekiel's last word is simply: GOD IS PRESENT.

Authorship & Background

Author: Ezekiel son of Buzi (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).
Classification: Prophetic Vision — Land Allotment and the City's Eternal Name Key Themes: The equal distribution of the land, the holy district at the nation's center, all twelve tribes restored, God's permanent presence as the nation's defining identity, YHWH- Shammah — "The LORD Is There"
Historical Context: This chapter concludes both the temple vision (chs. 40-48) and the entire book of Ezekiel. The prophet who began in exile, witnessing God's glory depart from Jerusalem, now ends with God permanently dwelling among His restored people. The theological arc is complete: departure → judgment → exile → restoration → permanent presence.
The land division differs significantly from Joshua's allotment. In Joshua, the tribes received territory by lot, resulting in unequal and irregular portions. Here, each tribe receives an equal east-to-west strip. There are no more "better" or "worse" territories. All tribes share equally in the land's bounty. Additionally, Dan (absent from Revelation 7's sealing) is listed FIRST here — restored, included, given prime position.
The positioning matters: seven tribes north of the holy district, five tribes south. Judah (the royal tribe) is immediately north; Benjamin (the loyal tribe) is immediately south — flanking God's presence. Joseph (Ephraim and Manasseh) are both included, maintaining twelve tribes alongside Levi's special status.
The final verse — "and the name of the city from that day shall be, The LORD is there" (YHWH-Shammah) — is not just a name. It is a THESIS STATEMENT for the entire book. Everything Ezekiel saw, suffered, prophesied, and envisioned was building to this one declaration: God is THERE. Present. Permanent. Home.
Structure:
  • Seven Tribes North of the Holy District (vv.1-7)
  • The Holy District: Temple, Priests, Levites, City (vv.8-22)
  • Five Tribes South of the Holy District (vv.23-29)
  • The City Gates: Named for the Twelve Tribes (vv.30-34)
  • The City's Name: YHWH-Shammah (v.35)

Map & Geography

  • Damascus (v.1): Ancient Syrian city; site of Paul's conversion.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): enduringword.com
  • Charles Spurgeon: "THE LORD IS THERE. What a name for a city! What a name for a life! What a name for eternity! When all questions are answered, all battles won, all tears dried — the final word over everything God has made will be this: THE LORD IS THERE. It is enough. It is everything."

Reflection

  • 1. YHWH-SHAMMAH — The LORD Is There (v.35). This is the last word of Ezekiel. After 48 chapters of judgment, exile, visions, and hope — one final statement: GOD IS PRESENT. This is what everything is building toward. Not achievement, not prosperity, not even holiness as an abstract concept — but GOD HIMSELF, present, permanent, near. What is the "name" of your life? Is it characterized by God's presence?
  • 2. Dan is listed FIRST (v.1). The most wayward tribe. The tribe of idolatry and migration and compromise. And God puts them FIRST in the restoration. If you think you've wandered too far, drifted too long, compromised too deeply — Dan's restoration says otherwise. God's grace restores the worst cases to the front of the line.
  • 3. Equal portions for all tribes (vv.1-29). No one gets more because of past faithfulness; no one gets less because of past failure. In the kingdom, every child receives their full inheritance. "In my Father's house are many mansions" (John 14:2). God is not stingy with His restored people.
  • 4. Twelve gates — every tribe has access (vv.30-34). No tribe is excluded from the city. Every tribe has a gate bearing its name. In God's eternal city, there is a door with YOUR name on it. Access is universal for God's people. "Him that cometh to me I will in no wise cast out" (John 6:37).
  • 5. The book ends with PRESENCE, not performance (v.35). Ezekiel does not end with a command, a warning, or a regulation. It ends with a NAME — a declaration of who God is and where He is. The goal of all theology, all obedience, all worship is not achievement — it is ENCOUNTER. Being where God is. And the promise is: He. Is. THERE.
