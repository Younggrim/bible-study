Summary

A communal prayer for revival — God formerly restored Jacob's fortunes; now the people plead for renewal: 'Wilt thou not revive us again?' Mercy and truth meet, righteousness and peace kiss each other.

Key Verses

v.6
"Wilt thou not revive us again: that thy people may rejoice in thee?" (KJV) — The prayer for revival. "Revive" (chayah) = to make alive again, to restore to life. The purpose of revival: not excitement but REJOICING IN GOD. Revival is God-centered joy restored.
v.10
"Mercy and truth are met together; righteousness and peace have kissed each other." (KJV) — Perhaps the most profound verse in Book III. Four divine attributes that seem to conflict find perfect unity. Mercy forgives; Truth accuses. Righteousness condemns; Peace reconciles. Only at the cross do all four perfectly meet and embrace.
v.11
"Truth shall spring out of the earth; and righteousness shall look down from heaven." (KJV) — When God's restoration comes, truth sprouts from below (human faithfulness) and righteousness rains from above (divine blessing). Heaven and earth cooperate in redemption.
v.13
"Righteousness shall go before him; and shall set us in the way of his steps." (KJV) — Righteousness becomes a pathfinder — going before God and preparing a road for His people to follow. Where God walks, righteousness has already paved the way.

Cross-References

  • v.1 — Ezra 1:1-4 — Cyrus's decree releasing the Jews from Babylon. The restoration referenced. v.6 — Habakkuk 3:2 — "O LORD, revive thy work in the midst of the years." Same revival prayer. v.10 — Romans 3:25-26 — God is both "just and the justifier" — the NT explanation of how mercy and truth meet in Christ. v.10 — Isaiah 32:17 — "The work of righteousness shall be peace." Righteousness producing peace. v.10 — 2 Corinthians 5:19-21 — "God was in Christ, reconciling the world unto himself." The kiss of righteousness and peace in action. v.11 — James 3:18 — "The fruit of righteousness is sown in peace of them that make peace."

Translations

  • v.1 — KJV: "thou hast brought back the captivity of Jacob" / ESV: "you restored the fortunes of Jacob" — Same Hebrew phrase as in Psalm 53:6. "Restored fortunes" (ESV) is broader than "brought back captivity" and may better capture the full scope of restoration.
  • v.10 — KJV: "Mercy and truth" / ESV: "Steadfast love and faithfulness" — Hebrew "hesed" and "emeth." ESV's pairing is more precise: hesed = covenant loyalty/steadfast love; emeth = faithfulness/reliability. Both translations capture aspects of these rich Hebrew terms.

Reflection

  • 1. Revival is needed when past blessing has faded (vv.1-6). The return from exile was real but incomplete. Spiritual experiences in the past don't guarantee vibrancy in the present. If your faith has cooled, pray for revival — God can make you alive again.
  • 2. At the cross, mercy and truth meet (v.10). This is the gospel in one verse. How can God be merciful (forgiving sin) AND truthful (punishing sin)? Only through Christ — who bore truth's demand so mercy could flow. The cross is where the conflict resolves.
  • 3. Righteousness and peace are not contradictions (v.10). They kiss — they are intimate allies, not enemies. True peace is built on righteousness, not on ignoring sin. And true righteousness leads to peace, not to perpetual conflict.
  • 4. Revival means rejoicing IN GOD (v.6). Not just excitement, not just activity — rejoicing in GOD Himself. The measure of revival is how much your joy is centered on God rather than on experiences about God.
