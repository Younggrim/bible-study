Zechariah — Chapter 5

1Again I lifted my eyes and saw, and behold, a flying scroll!

2And he said to me, “What do you see?” I answered, “I see a flying scroll. Its length is twenty cubits, and its width ten cubits.”

3Then he said to me, “This is the curse that goes out over the face of the whole land. For everyone who steals shall be cleaned out according to what is on one side, and everyone who swears falsely shall be cleaned out according to what is on the other side.

4I will send it out, declares the LORD of hosts, and it shall enter the house of the thief, and the house of him who swears falsely by my name. And it shall remain in his house and consume it, both timber and stones.”

5Then the angel who talked with me came forward and said to me, “Lift your eyes and see what this is that is going out.”

6And I said, “What is it?” He said, “This is the basket that is going out.” And he said, “This is their iniquity in all the land.”

7And behold, the leaden cover was lifted, and there was a woman sitting in the basket!

8And he said, “This is Wickedness.” And he thrust her back into the basket, and thrust down the leaden weight on its opening.

9Then I lifted my eyes and saw, and behold, two women coming forward! The wind was in their wings. They had wings like the wings of a stork, and they lifted up the basket between earth and heaven.

10Then I said to the angel who talked with me, “Where are they taking the basket?”

11He said to me, “To the land of Shinar, to build a house for it. And when this is prepared, they will set the basket down there on its base.”

Summary

Two visions — a flying scroll that curses every thief and perjurer, and a woman called Wickedness sealed in a basket and carried to Babylon — depict God's removal of sin from the land.

Key Verses

v.3
"This is the curse that goeth forth over the face of the whole earth: for every one that stealeth shall be cut off" (KJV) — God's curse against sin is universal ("the whole earth") and specific (theft and false swearing). No one is exempt. The curse actively seeks out sinners and enters their homes.
v.4
"it shall enter into the house of the thief... and shall consume it with the timber thereof and the stones thereof" (KJV) — Sin consumes from within. It doesn't just bring external punishment — it destroys the very structure of your life. The house built by theft is consumed BY the curse of theft.
v.8
"This is wickedness" (KJV/ESV) — Wickedness personified as a woman (Hebrew "rishah" is feminine). She is thrust into the basket and sealed shut. God confines and controls wickedness — it does not roam free under His sovereign hand.
v.11
"To build it an house in the land of Shinar" (KJV) — Shinar = Babylon (Gen 11:2). Wickedness is sent BACK to its origin — the land of Babel, human pride, and rebellion. God removes sin from His people and relocates it to its proper domain. In the age to come, wickedness will have no place among God's people.

Cross-References

  • v.1 — Ezekiel 2:9-10 — A scroll written on both sides with "lamentations, and mourning, and woe." v.3 — Deuteronomy 27:15-26 — The covenant curses pronounced at Mount Ebal for disobedience. v.3 — Galatians 3:10 — "Cursed is every one that continueth not in all things which are written in the book of the law." v.4 — Malachi 3:5 — God's curse against false swearers and those who defraud. v.8 — Revelation 17:3-5 — "Mystery, Babylon the Great, the mother of harlots" — wickedness personified and judged. v.11 — Genesis 11:1-9 — Shinar as the location of the Tower of Babel — the origin of organized rebellion against God. v.11 — Revelation 18:2 — "Babylon the great is fallen" — wickedness finally and fully judged.

Translations

  • v.1 — "flying roll" (KJV) / "flying scroll" (ESV) — Hebrew "megillah 'aphah" = a scroll in flight. Scrolls in the ancient world contained legal documents and decrees. This flying scroll is a divine court order — God's verdict actively pursuing the guilty.
  • v.6 — "ephah" — A dry measure of approximately one bushel (22 liters). It was the standard commercial measure — fitting that commercial wickedness (greed, theft) is contained in a commercial container.
  • v.7 — "talent of lead" (KJV) / "leaden cover" (ESV) — Hebrew "kikkar ophereth" = a circle/disk of lead. Lead is the heaviest common metal — used to permanently seal wickedness shut. Once God confines sin, it cannot escape.
  • v.11 — "Shinar" — The ancient name for Babylonia (Gen 10:10, 11:2). By sending wickedness to Shinar, God symbolically returns evil to its source. Babylon represents the world system opposed to God.

Reflection

  • 1. Sin has consequences that enter your "house" (v.4). The curse doesn't just visit — it REMAINS and CONSUMES. Hidden sin eats away at the structure of your life: your relationships, your peace, your integrity. Deal with it before it consumes the timber and stones of what you've built.
  • 2. God takes theft and false speech equally seriously (v.3). One is against man, the other against God. Both merit the curse. Integrity in word and deed — this is what God requires. Guard both your hands AND your tongue.
  • 3. God is actively REMOVING wickedness (vv.5-11). This is encouraging: God doesn't tolerate sin indefinitely. He is working to purge, contain, and exile it. In your life, He is working to remove wickedness — cooperate with the process. Don't cling to what God is trying to carry away.
  • 4. Wickedness belongs in Babylon, not in Zion (v.11). Sin has no rightful place among God's people. When you tolerate sin in your life, you're keeping something in God's house that belongs in Shinar. Let God send it back where it came from.
