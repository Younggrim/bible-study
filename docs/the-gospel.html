The Gospel: Salvation

"For the wages of sin is death, but the free gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord."

— Romans 6:23 (ESV)

1. God's Holiness

The starting point: God is perfectly righteous
The Truth: God is infinitely holy — completely separate from sin, perfectly righteous in all His ways. His holiness is not merely one attribute among many; it is the foundation of His character. Because God is holy, He cannot ignore or tolerate sin.

What It Means For Us: We were created to reflect God's holiness, but we have all fallen short. His standard is perfection — not improvement, not "good enough."
Key Scriptures:
▸ Romans 1:18-20
▸ Isaiah 6:3
▸ Habakkuk 1:13
▸ 1 Peter 1:15-16

2. Man's Sin

The problem: all have sinned
The Truth: Every human being has sinned — not just in actions but in nature. We are born with a sinful disposition inherited from Adam. Sin is not merely breaking rules; it is rebellion against God's authority and falling short of His glory.

What It Means For Us: No amount of good works can erase our guilt. We cannot save ourselves. "There is no one righteous, not even one" (Romans 3:10).
Key Scriptures:
▸ Romans 3:23
▸ Romans 3:10-12
▸ Isaiah 64:6

Sin | BibleProject

3. Sin's Penalty

The consequence: death and separation
The Truth: The just penalty for sin is death — spiritual death (separation from God now), physical death (the body dies), and eternal death (separation from God forever). God is just and cannot simply overlook sin without violating His own righteous character.

What It Means For Us: Without intervention, we are under God's righteous judgment. The sentence has been passed. We need a Savior — someone to pay the penalty we owe.
Key Scriptures:
▸ Romans 6:23
▸ Ezekiel 18:20
▸ Romans 2:5-6

4. God's Love

The motivation: God loved us first
The Truth: Despite our rebellion, God loves us with an unfailing, pursuing love. He did not wait for us to clean ourselves up or earn His favor. While we were still sinners — enemies — God demonstrated His love by acting on our behalf.

What It Means For Us: Salvation originates in God's love, not our worthiness. He chose to rescue us when we could not rescue ourselves. This is grace — unmerited, undeserved favor.
Key Scriptures:
▸ Romans 5:8
▸ John 3:16
▸ 1 John 4:9-10

5. Christ's Death

The solution: substitutionary atonement
The Truth: Jesus Christ — fully God, fully man, perfectly sinless — died in our place. On the cross, He bore our sin, absorbed God's wrath, and satisfied divine justice. The innocent died for the guilty. The righteous for the unrighteous. One life given for many.

What It Means For Us: The debt has been paid in full. Jesus took what we deserved so we could receive what He deserved. "It is finished" (John 19:30).
Key Scriptures:
▸ Romans 5:6-8
▸ 1 Peter 3:18
▸ Isaiah 53:5-6

6. Faith Alone

The means: grace through faith, not works
The Truth: Salvation is received by grace alone through faith alone in Christ alone. It is not earned by religious performance, moral effort, or good deeds. Faith is trusting in Christ's finished work — resting your full weight on what He has done, not what you can do.

What It Means For Us: You cannot add to the cross. Salvation is a free gift — received by empty hands of faith, not earned by busy hands of works.
Key Scriptures:
▸ Ephesians 2:8-9
▸ Romans 4:4-5
▸ Titus 3:5

7. Repentance

The response: turning from sin to God
The Truth: Repentance is a change of mind that produces a change of direction. It is not merely feeling sorry for sin (that is remorse) but turning away from sin and toward God. True repentance involves acknowledging guilt, grieving over sin, and choosing a new path.

What It Means For Us: Faith and repentance are two sides of the same coin — we turn from self and sin (repentance) and turn to Christ (faith). There is no saving faith without genuine repentance.
Key Scriptures:
▸ Acts 3:19
▸ 2 Corinthians 7:10
▸ Luke 13:3

8. New Life

The result: new creation in Christ
The Truth: Those who trust in Christ are made new — born again, adopted as children, indwelt by the Spirit, and freed from condemnation. The old has passed away; the new has come. This is not self-improvement but supernatural re-creation by God's power.

What It Means For Us: You are no longer defined by your past, your sin, or your failures. In Christ, you have a new identity, a new power, a new purpose, and a new destiny. There is now no condemnation.
Key Scriptures:
▸ 2 Corinthians 5:17
▸ Romans 8:1
▸ Galatians 2:20
