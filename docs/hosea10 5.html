Hosea — Chapter 10

1Israel is a luxuriant vine that yields its fruit. The more his fruit increased, the more altars he built; as his country improved, he improved his pillars.

2Their heart is false; now they must bear their guilt. The LORD will break down their altars and destroy their pillars.

3For now they will say: “We have no king, for we do not fear the LORD; and a king—what could he do for us?”

4They utter mere words; with empty oaths they make covenants; so judgment springs up like poisonous weeds in the furrows of the field.

5The inhabitants of Samaria tremble for the calf of Beth-aven. Its people mourn for it, and so do its idolatrous priests— those who rejoiced over it and over its glory— for it has departed from them.

6The thing itself shall be carried to Assyria as tribute to the great king. Ephraim shall be put to shame, and Israel shall be ashamed of his idol.

7Samaria’s king shall perish like a twig on the face of the waters.

8The high places of Aven, the sin of Israel, shall be destroyed. Thorn and thistle shall grow up on their altars, and they shall say to the mountains, “Cover us,” and to the hills, “Fall on us.”

9From the days of Gibeah, you have sinned, O Israel; there they have continued. Shall not the war against the unjust overtake them in Gibeah?

10When I please, I will discipline them, and nations shall be gathered against them when they are bound up for their double iniquity.

11Ephraim was a trained calf that loved to thresh, and I spared her fair neck; but I will put Ephraim to the yoke; Judah must plow; Jacob must harrow for himself.

12Sow for yourselves righteousness; reap steadfast love; break up your fallow ground, for it is the time to seek the LORD, that he may come and rain righteousness upon you.

13You have plowed iniquity; you have reaped injustice; you have eaten the fruit of lies. Because you have trusted in your own way and in the multitude of your warriors,

14therefore the tumult of war shall arise among your people, and all your fortresses shall be destroyed, as Shalman destroyed Beth-arbel on the day of battle; mothers were dashed in pieces with their children.

15Thus it shall be done to you, O Bethel, because of your great evil. At dawn the king of Israel shall be utterly cut off.

Summary

Israel is a luxuriant vine that produced fruit for itself — their prosperity led to more altars to false gods, and now God calls them to break up fallow ground and sow righteousness before judgment falls.

Key Verses

v.1
"Israel is an empty vine, he bringeth forth fruit unto himself" (KJV) / "Israel is a luxuriant vine that yields its fruit" (ESV) — The KJV emphasizes emptiness (fruit for self); ESV emphasizes abundance (misused). Either way: prosperity without godliness produces MORE idolatry, not less. Success without God is dangerous.
v.2
"Their heart is divided; now shall they be found faulty" (KJV) / "Their heart is false" (ESV) — A divided heart tries to love God AND idols. It cannot be done. Jesus: "No man can serve two masters" (Matthew 6:24). Division of loyalty is itself the fault.
v.8
"they shall say to the mountains, Cover us; and to the hills, Fall on us" (KJV) — Quoted by Jesus on the road to Calvary (Luke 23:30) and in Revelation 6:16. When judgment comes, people would rather be buried alive than face God. The terror of accountability.
v.12
"Sow to yourselves in righteousness, reap in mercy; break up your fallow ground: for it is time to seek the LORD, till he come and rain righteousness upon you" (KJV) — The chapter's golden verse. Four commands: (1) Sow righteousness. (2) Reap mercy/steadfast love. (3) Break up your fallow (hard, unplowed) ground. (4) Seek the LORD. The promise: He WILL come and rain righteousness upon you. This is the antidote to chapter 8:7 ("sow the wind").
v.13
"Ye have plowed wickedness, ye have reaped iniquity; ye have eaten the fruit of lies: because thou didst trust in thy way, in the multitude of thy mighty men" (KJV) — The contrast with v.12. They sowed WICKEDNESS instead of righteousness. They trusted in their OWN way and military strength instead of God. Wrong seed produces wrong harvest.

Cross-References

  • v.1 — Isaiah 5:1-7 — The vineyard song: Israel as God's vine that produced wild grapes. v.2 — 1 Kings 18:21 — Elijah: "How long halt ye between two opinions?" The same divided heart. v.8 — Luke 23:30 — Jesus quotes this verse on the way to the cross, applied to Jerusalem's coming judgment. v.8 — Revelation 6:16 — "said to the mountains and rocks, Fall on us, and hide us from the face of him that sitteth on the throne." v.12 — Jeremiah 4:3 — "Break up your fallow ground, and sow not among thorns." Same agricultural metaphor for repentance. v.12 — 2 Chronicles 7:14 — "If my people... shall seek my face... then will I hear from heaven." v.13 — Galatians 6:7-8 — "Whatsoever a man soweth, that shall he also reap." v.13 — Proverbs 14:12 — "There is a way which seemeth right unto a man, but the end thereof are the ways of death."

Translations

  • v.1 — "empty vine" (KJV) vs. "luxuriant vine" (ESV) — Hebrew "baqaq" is debated: can mean "emptying" (pouring out fruit for itself) or "luxuriant/spreading." The ESV emphasizes Israel's fruitfulness that is self-directed; KJV emphasizes the spiritual emptiness despite outward production.
  • v.5 — "Beth-aven" = "house of wickedness/vanity" — Hosea's sarcastic name for Bethel ("house of God"), which housed Jeroboam's golden calf.
  • v.8 — "Aven" = shortened form of Beth-aven. "High places" = Hebrew "bamot" = hilltop shrines for pagan worship.
  • v.11 — "heifer that is taught" (KJV) / "trained calf" (ESV) — Ephraim was once a domesticated, useful animal that loved to thresh (easy work: eating while treading grain). Now God will put her to harder labor — plowing and harrowing.
  • v.12 — "fallow ground" — Hebrew "nir" = land left unplowed, hardened by neglect. Requires breaking (deep plowing) before it can receive seed. A metaphor for hearts hardened by spiritual neglect.

Reflection

  • 1. Prosperity can be your greatest spiritual danger (v.1). The MORE Israel had, the MORE they sinned. Success without God produces idolatry, not gratitude. Check: as your life improves, is your worship increasing or decreasing?
  • 2. A divided heart is a false heart (v.2). You cannot maintain loyalty to God and to the world simultaneously. The attempt itself is faithlessness. Choose. "Purify your hearts, ye double minded" (James 4:8).
  • 3. "Break up your fallow ground" (v.12). Hard-heartedness develops through neglect — seasons where you don't pray, don't read, don't respond to conviction. The ground of your heart becomes hard and unresponsive. BREAK IT UP. Repentance is the plow. Confession is the blade. Let God's Word penetrate again.
  • 4. "It is TIME to seek the LORD" (v.12). Urgency. Not tomorrow. Not when circumstances improve. Not when you feel like it. NOW. The time to seek God is always NOW. Every delay makes the ground harder.
  • 5. You always eat what you plant (v.13). Plowed wickedness? You'll eat lies. Sowed righteousness? You'll reap steadfast love. Life is a farm. What are you planting TODAY?
