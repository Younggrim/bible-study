Numbers — Chapter 26

1After the plague, the LORD said to Moses and to Eleazar the son of Aaron, the priest,

2“Take a census of all the congregation of the people of Israel, from twenty years old and upward, by their fathers’ houses, all in Israel who are able to go to war.”

3And Moses and Eleazar the priest spoke with them in the plains of Moab by the Jordan at Jericho, saying,

4“Take a census of the people, from twenty years old and upward,” as the LORD commanded Moses. The people of Israel who came out of the land of Egypt were:

5Reuben, the firstborn of Israel; the sons of Reuben: of Hanoch, the clan of the Hanochites; of Pallu, the clan of the Palluites;

6of Hezron, the clan of the Hezronites; of Carmi, the clan of the Carmites.

7These are the clans of the Reubenites, and those listed were 43,730.

8And the sons of Pallu: Eliab.

9The sons of Eliab: Nemuel, Dathan, and Abiram. These are the Dathan and Abiram, chosen from the congregation, who contended against Moses and Aaron in the company of Korah, when they contended against the LORD

10and the earth opened its mouth and swallowed them up together with Korah, when that company died, when the fire devoured 250 men, and they became a warning.

11But the sons of Korah did not die.

12The sons of Simeon according to their clans: of Nemuel, the clan of the Nemuelites; of Jamin, the clan of the Jaminites; of Jachin, the clan of the Jachinites;

13of Zerah, the clan of the Zerahites; of Shaul, the clan of the Shaulites.

14These are the clans of the Simeonites, 22,200.

15The sons of Gad according to their clans: of Zephon, the clan of the Zephonites; of Haggi, the clan of the Haggites; of Shuni, the clan of the Shunites;

16of Ozni, the clan of the Oznites; of Eri, the clan of the Erites;

17of Arod, the clan of the Arodites; of Areli, the clan of the Arelites.

18These are the clans of the sons of Gad as they were listed, 40,500.

19The sons of Judah were Er and Onan; and Er and Onan died in the land of Canaan.

20And the sons of Judah according to their clans were: of Shelah, the clan of the Shelanites; of Perez, the clan of the Perezites; of Zerah, the clan of the Zerahites.

21And the sons of Perez were: of Hezron, the clan of the Hezronites; of Hamul, the clan of the Hamulites.

22These are the clans of Judah as they were listed, 76,500.

23The sons of Issachar according to their clans: of Tola, the clan of the Tolaites; of Puvah, the clan of the Punites;

24of Jashub, the clan of the Jashubites; of Shimron, the clan of the Shimronites.

25These are the clans of Issachar as they were listed, 64,300.

26The sons of Zebulun, according to their clans: of Sered, the clan of the Seredites; of Elon, the clan of the Elonites; of Jahleel, the clan of the Jahleelites.

27These are the clans of the Zebulunites as they were listed, 60,500.

28The sons of Joseph according to their clans: Manasseh and Ephraim.

29The sons of Manasseh: of Machir, the clan of the Machirites; and Machir was the father of Gilead; of Gilead, the clan of the Gileadites.

30These are the sons of Gilead: of Iezer, the clan of the Iezerites; of Helek, the clan of the Helekites;

31and of Asriel, the clan of the Asrielites; and of Shechem, the clan of the Shechemites;

32and of Shemida, the clan of the Shemidaites; and of Hepher, the clan of the Hepherites.

33Now Zelophehad the son of Hepher had no sons, but daughters. And the names of the daughters of Zelophehad were Mahlah, Noah, Hoglah, Milcah, and Tirzah.

34These are the clans of Manasseh, and those listed were 52,700.

35These are the sons of Ephraim according to their clans: of Shuthelah, the clan of the Shuthelahites; of Becher, the clan of the Becherites; of Tahan, the clan of the Tahanites.

36And these are the sons of Shuthelah: of Eran, the clan of the Eranites.

37These are the clans of the sons of Ephraim as they were listed, 32,500. These are the sons of Joseph according to their clans.

38The sons of Benjamin according to their clans: of Bela, the clan of the Belaites; of Ashbel, the clan of the Ashbelites; of Ahiram, the clan of the Ahiramites;

39of Shephupham, the clan of the Shuphamites; of Hupham, the clan of the Huphamites.

40And the sons of Bela were Ard and Naaman: of Ard, the clan of the Ardites; of Naaman, the clan of the Naamites.

41These are the sons of Benjamin according to their clans, and those listed were 45,600.

42These are the sons of Dan according to their clans: of Shuham, the clan of the Shuhamites. These are the clans of Dan according to their clans.

43All the clans of the Shuhamites, as they were listed, were 64,400.

44The sons of Asher according to their clans: of Imnah, the clan of the Imnites; of Ishvi, the clan of the Ishvites; of Beriah, the clan of the Beriites.

45Of the sons of Beriah: of Heber, the clan of the Heberites; of Malchiel, the clan of the Malchielites.

46And the name of the daughter of Asher was Serah.

47These are the clans of the sons of Asher as they were listed, 53,400.

48The sons of Naphtali according to their clans: of Jahzeel, the clan of the Jahzeelites; of Guni, the clan of the Gunites;

49of Jezer, the clan of the Jezerites; of Shillem, the clan of the Shillemites.

50These are the clans of Naphtali according to their clans, and those listed were 45,400.

51This was the list of the people of Israel, 601,730.

52The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

53“Among these the land shall be divided for inheritance according to the number of names.

54To a large tribe you shall give a large inheritance, and to a small tribe you shall give a small inheritance; every tribe shall be given its inheritance in proportion to its list.

55But the land shall be divided by lot. According to the names of the tribes of their fathers they shall inherit.

56Their inheritance shall be divided according to lot between the larger and the smaller.”

57This was the list of the Levites according to their clans: of Gershon, the clan of the Gershonites; of Kohath, the clan of the Kohathites; of Merari, the clan of the Merarites.

58These are the clans of Levi: the clan of the Libnites, the clan of the Hebronites, the clan of the Mahlites, the clan of the Mushites, the clan of the Korahites. And Kohath was the father of Amram.

59The name of Amram’s wife was Jochebed the daughter of Levi, who was born to Levi in Egypt. And she bore to Amram Aaron and Moses and Miriam their sister.

60And to Aaron were born Nadab, Abihu, Eleazar, and Ithamar.

61But Nadab and Abihu died when they offered unauthorized fire before the LORD.

62And those listed were 23,000, every male from a month old and upward. For they were not listed among the people of Israel, because there was no inheritance given to them among the people of Israel.

63These were those listed by Moses and Eleazar the priest, who listed the people of Israel in the plains of Moab by the Jordan at Jericho.

64But among these there was not one of those listed by Moses and Aaron the priest, who had listed the people of Israel in the wilderness of Sinai.

65For the LORD had said of them, “They shall die in the wilderness.” Not one of them was left, except Caleb the son of Jephunneh and Joshua the son of Nun.

Summary

A second census numbers the new generation at 601,730 — nearly the same total, confirming God's faithfulness despite forty years of death.

Key Verses

v.11
"Notwithstanding the children of Korah died not." — Grace in the midst of judgment. The rebels perished, but their children were spared — and became psalmists and worship leaders.
v.51
"These were the numbered of the children of Israel, six hundred thousand and a thousand seven hundred and thirty." — God preserved a nation through 38 years of wilderness judgment. His purposes cannot be thwarted.
v.53
"Among these the land shall be divided for inheritance according to the number of names." (ESV) — The inheritance is real, specific, and proportional. God's promises are not vague — they are measured.
v.65
"For the LORD had said of them, They shall surely die in the wilderness. And there was not left a man of them, save Caleb the son of Jephunneh, and Joshua the son of Nun." — God's word of judgment is as certain as His word of promise. Not one exception.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/numbers-26/ Guzik notes the remarkable stability of Israel's total population despite 38 years of judgment — God preserved the nation even while disciplining it. He highlights Simeon's dramatic decline as evidence of corporate consequences for tribal sin.
  • Charles Spurgeon: "Here is a new generation standing where their fathers fell. The bones of six hundred thousand unbelievers bleach in the desert sand, and their children stand on the border of Canaan. Let the young take warning: your fathers' unbelief cost them everything. But let them also take courage: God's promise outlives the generation that rejected it. He will have a people in His land. If one generation refuses, He raises another. The promise is not cancelled — only the participants change."

Translations

  • v.4 — KJV: "as the LORD commanded Moses and the children of Israel, which went forth out of the land of Egypt" / ESV: "as the LORD commanded Moses. The people of Israel who came out of the land of Egypt were:" — The Hebrew is ambiguous in its sentence division. ESV treats "The people of Israel who came out of Egypt" as the beginning of the census list. KJV connects it to the command. ESV's reading flows more naturally into the tribal listing that follows.
  • v.9 — KJV: "which were famous in the congregation" / ESV: "chosen from the congregation" — Hebrew "qeru'ei ha-edah." "Qeru'ei" means "called ones" or "summoned ones" — those called to leadership. KJV's "famous" captures their notoriety; ESV's "chosen" captures their appointed status. Both are legitimate readings.
  • v.10 — KJV: "they became a sign" / ESV: "they became a warning" — Hebrew "vayyihyu l'nes." "Nes" can mean sign, banner, or warning signal. ESV interprets the purpose (warning); KJV translates more literally (sign). The context supports "warning" — their fate serves as a cautionary example.
  • v.11 — KJV: "Notwithstanding the children of Korah died not" / ESV: "But the sons of Korah did not die" — Identical meaning. This parenthetical note preserves an important theological point: judgment on the father does not automatically condemn the children (cf. Ezekiel 18:20).
  • v.54 — KJV: "To many thou shalt give the more inheritance, and to few thou shalt give the less inheritance" / ESV: "To a large tribe you shall give a large inheritance, and to a small tribe you shall give a small inheritance" — ESV clarifies that "many" and "few" refer to tribal population size, not individuals. The principle: proportional allocation based on need.
  • v.61 — KJV: "when they offered strange fire before the LORD" / ESV: "when they offered unauthorized fire before the LORD" — Hebrew "esh zarah." "Zarah" means foreign, strange, unauthorized. ESV's "unauthorized" is more precise — the issue was not that the fire was exotic but that it was not commanded. KJV's "strange" is more literal but less clear to modern readers.

Reflection

  • 1. A new generation on the border (vv.1-4). The old generation died in unbelief; the new generation stands ready to inherit. God's purposes are not defeated by one generation's failure — He raises up another. Are you part of the generation that refuses, or the one that enters?
  • 2. "They became a warning" (v.10). Korah's rebellion is preserved in the census record as a cautionary tale. God memorializes both faith and failure. What "warnings" from the past are you heeding — or ignoring?
  • 3. "The sons of Korah did not die" (v.11). Grace breaks the cycle of generational sin. You are not condemned to repeat your parents' failures. The sons of rebels became psalmists. God can redeem any lineage.
  • 4. Simeon's catastrophic decline (v.14). Sin has corporate consequences. One prince's brazen sin (Zimri, 25:14) may reflect a tribal culture of compromise. The choices of leaders affect entire communities.
  • 5. The land divided by lot AND by proportion (vv.53-56). God's sovereignty (the lot) works alongside practical wisdom (proportional allocation). Divine guidance does not eliminate human responsibility — it works through it.
  • 6. "Not one was left" (v.65). God's word of judgment was fulfilled completely — and so will His word of promise. The same faithfulness that executed judgment now guarantees inheritance. If God keeps His hard words, He certainly keeps His good ones.
