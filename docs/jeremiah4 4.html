Jeremiah — Chapter 4

1“If you return, O Israel, declares the LORD, to me you should return. If you remove your detestable things from my presence, and do not waver,

2and if you swear, ‘As the LORD lives,’ in truth, in justice, and in righteousness, then nations shall bless themselves in him, and in him shall they glory.”

3For thus says the LORD to the men of Judah and Jerusalem: “Break up your fallow ground, and sow not among thorns.

4Circumcise yourselves to the LORD; remove the foreskin of your hearts, O men of Judah and inhabitants of Jerusalem; lest my wrath go forth like fire, and burn with none to quench it, because of the evil of your deeds.”

5Declare in Judah, and proclaim in Jerusalem, and say, “Blow the trumpet through the land; cry aloud and say, ‘Assemble, and let us go into the fortified cities!’

6Raise a standard toward Zion, flee for safety, stay not, for I bring disaster from the north, and great destruction.

7A lion has gone up from his thicket, a destroyer of nations has set out; he has gone out from his place to make your land a waste; your cities will be ruins without inhabitant.

8For this put on sackcloth, lament and wail, for the fierce anger of the LORD has not turned back from us.”

9“In that day, declares the LORD, courage shall fail both king and officials. The priests shall be appalled and the prophets astounded.”

10Then I said, “Ah, Lord GOD, surely you have utterly deceived this people and Jerusalem, saying, ‘It shall be well with you,’ whereas the sword has reached their very life.”

11At that time it will be said to this people and to Jerusalem, “A hot wind from the bare heights in the desert toward the daughter of my people, not to winnow or cleanse,

12a wind too full for this comes for me. Now it is I who speak in judgment upon them.”

13Behold, he comes up like clouds; his chariots like the whirlwind; his horses are swifter than eagles— woe to us, for we are ruined!

14O Jerusalem, wash your heart from evil, that you may be saved. How long shall your wicked thoughts lodge within you?

15For a voice declares from Dan and proclaims trouble from Mount Ephraim.

16Warn the nations that he is coming; announce to Jerusalem, “Besiegers come from a distant land; they shout against the cities of Judah.

17Like keepers of a field are they against her all around, because she has rebelled against me, declares the LORD.

18Your ways and your deeds have brought this upon you. This is your doom, and it is bitter; it has reached your very heart.”

19My anguish, my anguish! I writhe in pain! Oh the walls of my heart! My heart is beating wildly; I cannot keep silent, for I hear the sound of the trumpet, the alarm of war.

20Crash follows hard on crash; the whole land is laid waste. Suddenly my tents are laid waste, my curtains in a moment.

21How long must I see the standard and hear the sound of the trumpet?

22“For my people are foolish; they know me not; they are stupid children; they have no understanding. They are ‘wise’—in doing evil! But how to do good they know not.”

23I looked on the earth, and behold, it was without form and void; and to the heavens, and they had no light.

24I looked on the mountains, and behold, they were quaking, and all the hills moved to and fro.

25I looked, and behold, there was no man, and all the birds of the air had fled.

26I looked, and behold, the fruitful land was a desert, and all its cities were laid in ruins before the LORD, before his fierce anger.

27For thus says the LORD, “The whole land shall be a desolation; yet I will not make a full end.

28“For this the earth shall mourn, and the heavens above be dark; for I have spoken; I have purposed; I have not relented, nor will I turn back.”

29At the noise of horseman and archer every city takes to flight; they enter thickets; they climb among rocks; all the cities are forsaken, and no man dwells in them.

30And you, O desolate one, what do you mean that you dress in scarlet, that you adorn yourself with ornaments of gold, that you enlarge your eyes with paint? In vain you beautify yourself. Your lovers despise you; they seek your life.

31For I heard a cry as of a woman in labor, anguish as of one giving birth to her first child, the cry of the daughter of Zion gasping for breath, stretching out her hands, “Woe is me! I am fainting before murderers.”

Summary

God calls for genuine repentance: "Circumcise your hearts!" Then the alarm sounds — invasion from the north! Jeremiah sees a terrifying vision of cosmic devastation: the earth without form and void (Genesis 1 reversed), cities broken down, no man remaining. The prophet groans: "My bowels, my bowels! I am pained at my very heart."

Key Verses

v.3
"Break up your fallow ground, and sow not among thorns" (KJV) — Agricultural metaphor: hardened ground won't receive seed. Break up the hard heart before trying to plant truth in it. Quoted by Hosea 10:12.
v.4
"Circumcise yourselves to the LORD, and take away the foreskins of your heart" (KJV) — Physical circumcision is meaningless without HEART circumcision. The inward reality must match the outward sign. Paul develops this in Romans 2:28-29.
v.19
"My bowels, my bowels! I am pained at my very heart; my heart maketh a noise in me; I cannot hold my peace, because thou hast heard, O my soul, the sound of the trumpet" (KJV) — Jeremiah's visceral pain at what he foresees. True prophecy costs the prophet physically and emotionally.
v.22
"For my people is foolish, they have not known me; they are sottish children, and they have none understanding: they are wise to do evil, but to do good they have no knowledge" (KJV) — God's diagnosis: His people are expertly evil but incompetently good. They have PhD-level knowledge of sin and kindergarten-level knowledge of righteousness.
v.23
"I beheld the earth, and, lo, it was without form, and void; and the heavens, and they had no light" (KJV) — Genesis 1:2 language: "tohu va-vohu." Judgment as UN- creation. Sin reverses the creative work of God — back to chaos.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/jeremiah-4/
  • Charles Spurgeon: "'Circumcise yourselves to the LORD.' God wants the knife applied to your HEART, not merely your flesh. External religion without inward cutting is worse than useless — it's deceptive. Let God's word cut deep."

Translations

  • v.4 — "circumcise... your heart" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "himmolu la-YHWH" = circumcise to/for the LORD. The covenant sign was physical circumcision; God demands its spiritual reality: removal of the "foreskin" (hardness, resistance) from the heart.
  • v.23 — "without form, and void" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "tohu va-vohu" = formlessness and emptiness. Exact quote from Genesis 1:2. Jeremiah sees creation RETURNING to its pre-creation state. Devastating imagery.
  • v.30 — "rentest thy face with painting" (KJV) / "enlarge your eyes with paint" (ESV) — Hebrew "puk" = antimony eye paint. Jerusalem beautifying herself for "lovers" (allies) who will actually despise and destroy her.
  • v.31 — "daughter of Zion" (KJV/ESV) — Jerusalem personified as a woman in labor — gasping, stretching out her hands, fainting. The city in death-throes.

Reflection

  • 1. "Break up your fallow ground" (v.3). Hard hearts don't receive truth. Before God can plant something new in you, the hardness must be broken. What has made your heart hard? What plow does it need?
  • 2. "Circumcise your hearts" (v.4). External religion without internal transformation is the core problem of every spiritual community throughout history. Is your faith a surgery or a costume?
  • 3. "Wise to do evil" (v.22). We can become EXPERTS at sin while remaining amateurs at goodness. Where have you invested more creativity: in justifying sin or in pursuing righteousness?
  • 4. De-creation (v.23). Sin reverses God's creative work — in your life, in your community, in creation itself. What was once ordered and beautiful, sin makes chaotic and empty. Are you building or un-building?
  • 5. The prophet's pain (v.19). Jeremiah doesn't deliver this message coolly. He's in physical agony. Genuine spiritual insight costs something. If someone's suffering because of what they see spiritually, don't dismiss them.
