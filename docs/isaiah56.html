1Thus saith the LORD, Keep ye judgment, and do justice: for my salvation is near to come, and my righteousness to be revealed.

2Blessed is the man that doeth this, and the son of man that layeth hold on it; that keepeth the sabbath from polluting it, and keepeth his hand from doing any evil.

3Neither let the son of the stranger, that hath joined himself to the LORD, speak, saying, The LORD hath utterly separated me from his people: neither let the eunuch say, Behold, I am a dry tree.

4For thus saith the LORD unto the eunuchs that keep my sabbaths, and choose the things that please me, and take hold of my covenant;

5Even unto them will I give in mine house and within my walls a place and a name better than of sons and of daughters: I will give them an everlasting name, that shall not be cut off.

6Also the sons of the stranger, that join themselves to the LORD, to serve him, and to love the name of the LORD, to be his servants, every one that keepeth the sabbath from polluting it, and taketh hold of my covenant;

7Even them will I bring to my holy mountain, and make them joyful in my house of prayer: their burnt offerings and their sacrifices shall be accepted upon mine altar; for mine house shall be called an house of prayer for all people.

8The Lord GOD which gathereth the outcasts of Israel saith, Yet will I gather others to him, beside those that are gathered unto him.

9All ye beasts of the field, come to devour, yea, all ye beasts in the forest.

10His watchmen are blind: they are all ignorant, they are all dumb dogs, they cannot bark; sleeping, lying down, loving to slumber.

11Yea, they are greedy dogs which can never have enough, and they are shepherds that cannot understand: they all look to their own way, every one for his gain, from his quarter.

12Come ye, say they, I will fetch wine, and we will fill ourselves with strong drink; and to morrow shall be as this day, and much more abundant.

Summary

God welcomes foreigners and eunuchs into His house — those previously excluded from the assembly. "My house shall be called an house of prayer for all people." Then Isaiah rebukes Israel's blind watchmen and greedy shepherds who care only for their own gain.

Authorship & Background

Author: Isaiah son of Amoz (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).
Classification: Prophetic Oracle — Inclusivity and Leadership Failure (opens final section, chs. 56-66) Key Themes: Radical inclusion (foreigners, eunuchs), the Sabbath as covenant sign, a house of prayer for ALL people, the failure of Israel's watchmen/shepherds, leaders as blind dogs
Historical Context: Chapter 56 opens the THIRD section of Isaiah (chs.56-66). While 40-55 focused on comfort and the Servant, 56-66 addresses the restored community — its ethics, failures, and ultimate glory. The chapter begins with radical inclusion: foreigners (Gentiles) and eunuchs (excluded by Deut 23:1) are WELCOMED if they keep the Sabbath and hold God's covenant. Jesus quotes v.7 when cleansing the temple (Mark 11:17). Then the tone shifts: Israel's leaders are denounced as blind, ignorant, silent watchdogs, greedy shepherds — the very people who should welcome others are consumed by self-interest.
Structure:
  • Keep Justice: Salvation Is Near (v.1)
  • The Foreigner and Eunuch Welcomed (vv.2-8)
  • A House of Prayer for All Peoples (v.7)
  • Blind Watchmen and Greedy Shepherds (vv.9-12)

Reflection

  • 1. God welcomes those previously excluded (vv.3-8). If you've felt disqualified from God's presence — by your past, your condition, your outsider status — hear this: God explicitly overturns exclusions. His house is for ALL who hold His covenant.
  • 2. "A house of prayer for ALL people" (v.7). Are you (or your church) gatekeeping God's presence? Making some people feel less welcome than others? Jesus was ANGRY about this. God's house has room.
  • 3. Blind watchmen and dumb dogs (v.10). Leadership has ONE JOB: see clearly and speak truthfully. If your spiritual leaders can't identify danger or won't name it, they've failed their function. Seek leaders who can both see and bark.
  • 4. Greedy shepherds (v.11). "Every one for his gain." When ministry becomes a career ladder rather than a service calling, it's corrupted. If you lead others, check your motives: are you feeding them or feeding yourself?
