Jeremiah — Chapter 39

1In the ninth year of Zedekiah king of Judah, in the tenth month, Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon and all his army came against Jerusalem and besieged it.

2In the eleventh year of Zedekiah, in the fourth month, on the ninth day of the month, a breach was made in the city.

3Then all the officials of the king of Babylon came and sat in the middle gate: Nergal-sar-ezer of Samgar, Nebu-sar-sekim the Rab-saris, Nergal-sar-ezer the Rab-mag, with all the rest of the officers of the king of Babylon.

4When Zedekiah king of Judah and all the soldiers saw them, they fled, going out of the city at night by way of the king’s garden through the gate between the two walls; and they went toward the Arabah.

5But the army of the Chaldeans pursued them and overtook Zedekiah in the plains of Jericho. And when they had taken him, they brought him up to Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon, at Riblah, in the land of Hamath; and he passed sentence on him.

6The king of Babylon slaughtered the sons of Zedekiah at Riblah before his eyes, and the king of Babylon slaughtered all the nobles of Judah.

7He put out the eyes of Zedekiah and bound him in chains to take him to Babylon.

8The Chaldeans burned the king’s house and the house of the people, and broke down the walls of Jerusalem.

9Then Nebuzaradan, the captain of the guard, carried into exile to Babylon the rest of the people who were left in the city, those who had deserted to him, and the people who remained.

10Nebuzaradan, the captain of the guard, left in the land of Judah some of the poor people who owned nothing, and gave them vineyards and fields at the same time.

11Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon gave command concerning Jeremiah through Nebuzaradan, the captain of the guard, saying,

12“Take him, look after him well, and do him no harm, but deal with him as he tells you.”

13So Nebuzaradan the captain of the guard, Nebushazban the Rab-saris, Nergal-sar-ezer the Rab-mag, and all the chief officers of the king of Babylon

14sent and took Jeremiah from the court of the guard. They entrusted him to Gedaliah the son of Ahikam, son of Shaphan, that he should take him home. So he lived among the people.

15The word of the LORD came to Jeremiah while he was shut up in the court of the guard:

16“Go, and say to Ebed-melech the Ethiopian, ‘Thus says the LORD of hosts, the God of Israel: Behold, I will fulfill my words against this city for harm and not for good, and they shall be accomplished before you on that day.

17But I will deliver you on that day, declares the LORD, and you shall not be given into the hand of the men of whom you are afraid.

18For I will surely save you, and you shall not fall by the sword, but you shall have your life as a prize of war, because you have put your trust in me, declares the LORD.’”

Summary

Jerusalem FALLS. After 18 months of siege, Babylon breaches the walls. Zedekiah flees, is captured, watches his sons executed, has his eyes put out, and is taken to Babylon in chains. The city is burned. But Jeremiah is released and protected by Nebuchadnezzar's personal order. Ebed-Melech is promised safety: "thy life shall be for a prey unto thee, because thou hast put thy trust in me."

Key Verses

v.2
"In the eleventh year of Zedekiah, in the fourth month, the ninth day of the month, the city was broken up" (KJV) — The EXACT date recorded. Precision matters: God's word was fulfilled to the day.
v.7
"he put out Zedekiah's eyes, and bound him with chains, to carry him to Babylon" (KJV) — The last thing Zedekiah saw: his sons' execution. Then permanent darkness. The king who refused to SEE truth (spiritually) now cannot see anything (physically). Poetic justice.
v.12
"Take him, and look well to him, and do him no harm; but do unto him even as he shall say unto thee" (KJV) — NEBUCHADNEZZAR protects God's prophet while God's OWN PEOPLE tried to kill him. The pagan conqueror treats Jeremiah better than Judah's kings did.
v.18
"I will surely deliver thee, and thou shalt not fall by the sword, but thy life shall be for a prey unto thee: because thou hast put thy trust in me, saith the LORD" (KJV) — Ebed-Melech's reward for rescuing Jeremiah: SURVIVAL. His life as "prey" (plunder) — he escapes with his life. Faith in action rewarded with preservation.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/jeremiah-39/
  • Charles Spurgeon: "40 years of preaching fulfilled in one day. Everything Jeremiah said came true — every detail, every prediction, every warning. The prophet vindicated. The mockers silenced. The question is never 'was God's word true?' but 'will you believe it in time?'"

Translations

  • v.3 — "Nergal-sharezer... Samgar-nebo... Sarsechim... Rabsaris... Nergal-sharezer, Rab-mag" (KJV/ESV) — Babylonian officials with titles: Rab-saris (chief eunuch), Rab-mag (chief magician). Real historical officials confirmed by Babylonian records.
  • v.4 — "by night" (KJV/ESV) — Zedekiah flees at night through the "king's garden" (a gate between the two walls on the south side). Escape by darkness — but God sees in the dark.
  • v.7 — "put out his eyes" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "ivver et einav" = blinded his eyes. Standard Mesopotamian punishment for rebel kings: ensuring they can never rule or escape again.
  • v.18 — "thy life shall be for a prey" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "nafshekha le-shalal" = your life will be your plunder. Same phrase as 21:9; 38:2. You'll escape with ONLY your life — but you'll HAVE your life.

Reflection

  • 1. 40 years of prophecy fulfilled in one day. Every warning Jeremiah gave came true — PRECISELY. God's patience is long but not infinite. If He has warned you, take it seriously. The fulfillment WILL come.
  • 2. Zedekiah's blinding (v.7). He refused to SEE truth for 11 years; now he literally CAN'T see. Spiritual blindness sometimes becomes physical consequence. What are you refusing to see?
  • 3. The enemy protects the prophet (v.12). God's people tried to kill Jeremiah; God's "enemy" protected him. Don't assume safety comes from "your people." God uses whoever He wants.
  • 4. Ebed-Melech's faith rewarded (vv.15-18). One act of courage (rescuing Jeremiah from the cistern) = life preserved in the catastrophe. Small acts of faith in dark times pay ENORMOUS dividends.
  • 5. The precision of fulfilled prophecy. Dates, details, exact outcomes — all as predicted. God's word is reliable down to the smallest detail. What He has promised He WILL perform.
