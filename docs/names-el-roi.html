Names of God

El Roi

Understanding El Roi

El Roi — "The God Who Sees Me" — is the only name for God given by a woman in Scripture. Hagar, an Egyptian slave woman who had been mistreated by Sarah and fled into the wilderness, was met by the Angel of the LORD beside a spring of water (Genesis 16:13). Alone, pregnant, rejected, and running into the desert with no plan and no hope, Hagar encountered a God who saw her — truly saw her — in her desperation and desolation.

The Hebrew word "roi" comes from the verb "ra'ah," meaning to see, to perceive, or to look upon with care. It does not describe a passive observation but an active, compassionate gaze — the kind of seeing that leads to action. El Roi does not merely observe our suffering from a distance; He sees with the intent to intervene, to comfort, and to provide. Hagar's amazed response was: "You are the God who sees me... I have now seen the One who sees me."

This name reveals that no one is invisible to God. Hagar was a slave — the lowest social status in the ancient world. She had no advocate, no protector, no one to plead her case. Yet the God of the universe stooped down to see her in the wilderness. El Roi sees the forgotten, the marginalized, the overlooked, and the abandoned. No person is too insignificant, no situation too hidden, and no pain too private for His attentive, loving gaze.

Key Scriptures

  • Genesis 16:13 — "She gave this name to the LORD who spoke to her: 'You are the God who sees me.'" Hagar's revolutionary encounter with El Roi in the wilderness.
  • Psalm 139:1-4 — "You have searched me, LORD, and you know me... you perceive my thoughts from afar." God's intimate, comprehensive knowledge of every person.
  • Proverbs 15:3 — "The eyes of the LORD are everywhere, keeping watch on the wicked and the good." Nothing escapes El Roi's all-seeing gaze.
  • 2 Chronicles 16:9 — "For the eyes of the LORD range throughout the earth to strengthen those whose hearts are fully committed to him." God actively searches to support His people.
  • Hebrews 4:13 — "Nothing in all creation is hidden from God's sight. Everything is uncovered and laid bare." Complete transparency before El Roi.

What This Name Means for Us

In moments when we feel unseen — overlooked at work, forgotten by friends, invisible in our suffering — El Roi reminds us that God's eyes are fixed on us. He sees the tears we cry alone at night. He sees the sacrifices no one else notices. He sees the silent battles we fight without applause or recognition. We are never truly alone because the God who sees is always watching, always caring, always present.

El Roi also brings both comfort and accountability. His seeing is not only compassionate but holy. He sees our hidden struggles, yes, but He also sees our hidden sins, our secret thoughts, and our true motivations. Living before El Roi means living transparently — knowing that nothing is hidden from Him, and finding freedom in that vulnerability rather than fear. The God who sees everything still loves us completely, and that is the foundation of true intimacy with Him.

Reflection Questions

  • When have you felt most invisible or overlooked? How does knowing El Roi sees you bring comfort in those moments?
  • Like Hagar, have you ever been running from a painful situation? How might God be meeting you in your wilderness?
  • How does the truth that God sees everything — both your struggles and your secrets — affect how you live daily?
  • Who in your community might feel "unseen"? How can you reflect El Roi's character by truly seeing and caring for them?
