Prophecy & Fulfillment

Born of a Virgin

The Prophecy

Seven hundred years before Christ's birth, the prophet Isaiah delivered a sign to King Ahaz: a virgin would conceive and bear a son called Immanuel — "God with us." This prophecy pointed beyond its immediate context to the ultimate fulfillment in Mary, a young virgin from Nazareth, who would bear the Son of God through the power of the Holy Spirit.

The virgin birth is not merely a biological miracle — it is theologically essential. By being born of a woman, Jesus was fully human; by being conceived by the Holy Spirit, He was fully divine. This unique entrance into the world qualified Him to be the sinless mediator between God and humanity — truly God and truly man.

The prophecy reaches even further back than Isaiah to Genesis 3:15, where God promised that the "seed of the woman" would crush the serpent's head. This unusual phrase — seed of the woman rather than seed of a man — hinted at the virgin birth millennia before Isaiah made it explicit. God's redemptive plan was woven into the very fabric of creation.

Key Scriptures

  • Isaiah 7:14 — "The virgin will conceive and give birth to a son, and will call him Immanuel."
  • Matthew 1:22-23 — "All this took place to fulfill what the Lord had said through the prophet."
  • Luke 1:34-35 — "The Holy Spirit will come on you, and the power of the Most High will overshadow you."
  • Galatians 4:4 — "When the set time had fully come, God sent his Son, born of a woman."
  • Genesis 3:15 — "He will crush your head, and you will strike his heel" — the first promise of the Messiah.

The Fulfillment

Matthew explicitly connects Isaiah's prophecy to Jesus' birth, noting that "all this took place to fulfill what the Lord had said through the prophet." The fulfillment came precisely as predicted — a virgin conceived, a son was born, and His identity was Immanuel: God dwelling among us in human flesh.

The virgin birth assures us that Jesus' coming was no accident of history but the deliberate fulfillment of ancient prophecy. God planned salvation from before the foundation of the world and executed that plan with precision across millennia. Every detail of Christ's coming — including His miraculous conception — was foretold and fulfilled, confirming that Scripture is divinely inspired and completely trustworthy.

  • How does seeing prophecy fulfilled strengthen your confidence in Scripture's reliability?
