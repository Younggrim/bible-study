Names of God

Adonai

Understanding Adonai

Adonai is the Hebrew word meaning "Lord" or "Master," and it speaks of God's absolute sovereignty and authority over all things. It is the plural form of "Adon" (lord), intensified to express supreme lordship. In ancient Israel, this title acknowledged God as the ultimate ruler — the One who has the right to command, direct, and govern every aspect of creation and human life.

This name became especially significant because devout Jews would say "Adonai" whenever they encountered the tetragrammaton (YHWH) in Scripture, considering God's covenant name too holy to pronounce. Over time, Adonai became inseparably linked with the concept of divine authority. When Isaiah beheld the Lord "high and lifted up" in his temple vision (Isaiah 6:1), the word used is Adonai — the sovereign Master seated upon His throne in unapproachable majesty.

To call God "Adonai" is to acknowledge that He is not merely a helper or advisor — He is Lord. It implies total submission: a servant does not question the master's right to command. Yet unlike earthly masters, Adonai rules with perfect wisdom, boundless love, and infinite justice. His lordship is not oppressive but liberating, for to be under His authority is to be under His protection and care.

Key Scriptures

  • Psalm 8:1 — "LORD, our Lord, how majestic is your name in all the earth!" David marvels that the sovereign Master of the universe notices mankind.
  • Isaiah 6:1 — "I saw the Lord, high and exalted, seated on a throne." Isaiah encounters Adonai in overwhelming holiness and sovereign glory.
  • Malachi 1:6 — "A son honors his father, and a slave his master. If I am a master, where is the respect due me?" Adonai demands the honor due His lordship.
  • Psalm 110:1 — "The LORD says to my Lord: 'Sit at my right hand.'" A messianic psalm revealing the divine lordship of Christ.
  • Joshua 3:11 — "The ark of the covenant of the Lord of all the earth will go before you." Adonai goes before His people as sovereign over every nation.

What This Name Means for Us

Calling God "Adonai" is an act of surrender. It means acknowledging that our lives are not our own — that we have been bought with a price and belong to a Master who has every right to direct our steps. In a culture that celebrates autonomy and self-determination, this name challenges us to release the illusion of control and place our trust in the One whose plans are always higher and better than our own.

Yet surrendering to Adonai is not a loss — it is the truest freedom. When we submit to His lordship, we are freed from the tyranny of our own limited wisdom, the anxiety of carrying burdens too heavy for us, and the fear of an uncertain future. Adonai is the kind of Master who lays down His life for His servants, who calls us friends, and who works all things together for our good. His authority is perfect love in action.

Reflection Questions

  • Are there areas of your life where you resist God's lordship — where you want to remain in control rather than surrendering to Adonai?
  • How does Isaiah's response to encountering Adonai ("Here am I, send me!") challenge your own posture of availability before God?
  • What is the difference between serving God out of duty and serving Him out of love? How does understanding His character change your motivation?
