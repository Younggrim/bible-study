Joshua — Chapter 23

1A long time afterward, when the LORD had given rest to Israel from all their surrounding enemies, and Joshua was old and well advanced in years,

2Joshua summoned all Israel, its elders and heads, its judges and officers, and said to them, “I am now old and well advanced in years.

3And you have seen all that the LORD your God has done to all these nations for your sake, for it is the LORD your God who has fought for you.

4Behold, I have allotted to you as an inheritance for your tribes those nations that remain, along with all the nations that I have already cut off, from the Jordan to the Great Sea in the west.

5The LORD your God will push them back before you and drive them out of your sight. And you shall possess their land, just as the LORD your God promised you.

6Therefore, be very strong to keep and to do all that is written in the Book of the Law of Moses, turning aside from it neither to the right hand nor to the left,

7that you may not mix with these nations remaining among you or make mention of the names of their gods or swear by them or serve them or bow down to them,

8but you shall cling to the LORD your God just as you have done to this day.

9For the LORD has driven out before you great and strong nations. And as for you, no man has been able to stand before you to this day.

10One man of you puts to flight a thousand, since it is the LORD your God who fights for you, just as he promised you.

11Be very careful, therefore, to love the LORD your God.

12For if you turn back and cling to the remnant of these nations remaining among you and make marriages with them, so that you associate with them and they with you,

13know for certain that the LORD your God will no longer drive out these nations before you, but they shall be a snare and a trap for you, a whip on your sides and thorns in your eyes, until you perish from off this good ground that the LORD your God has given you.

14“And now I am about to go the way of all the earth, and you know in your hearts and souls, all of you, that not one word has failed of all the good things that the LORD your God promised concerning you. All have come to pass for you; not one of them has failed.

15But just as all the good things that the LORD your God promised concerning you have been fulfilled for you, so the LORD will bring upon you all the evil things, until he has destroyed you from off this good land that the LORD your God has given you,

16if you transgress the covenant of the LORD your God, which he commanded you, and go and serve other gods and bow down to them. Then the anger of the LORD will be kindled against you, and you shall perish quickly from off the good land that he has given to you.”

Summary

An aging Joshua gives a farewell address, warning Israel that God will drive them out if they intermarry with Canaanites and serve their gods. He reminds them that God has faithfully kept every promise.

Key Verses

v.3
"You have seen all that the LORD your God has done to all these nations because of you; for the LORD your God is he that has fought for you." (ESV) — The foundation of Joshua's appeal: personal experience. They SAW God fight. They witnessed His power. Faith is built on what God has already done.
v.6
"Be you therefore very courageous to keep and to do all that is written in the book of the law of Moses, that you turn not aside therefrom to the right hand or to the left." (ESV) — Echoes God's charge to Joshua in 1:7. Courage is needed not just for battle but for obedience. It takes strength to remain faithful when surrounded by pagan culture.
v.8
"But hold fast to the LORD your God, as you have done to this day." (ESV) — The positive command: cling to God. The same word ("davaq") used of marriage (Genesis 2:24). Intimate, inseparable devotion. Joshua commends their faithfulness thus far — now continue it.
v.10
"One man of you shall chase a thousand: for the LORD your God, he it is that fighteth for you, as he has promised you." (ESV) — The supernatural ratio of God's power. One Israelite with God is more powerful than a thousand enemies without Him. Cf. Leviticus 26:8; Deuteronomy 32:30.
v.11
"Take good heed therefore to yourselves, that you love the LORD your God." (ESV) — The heart of the matter. All obedience flows from love. All apostasy begins with love growing cold. Guard your love for God above all else.
v.13
"They shall be snares and traps to you, and scourges in your sides, and thorns in your eyes, until you perish from off this good land." (ESV) — Four images of torment: snares (entrapment), traps (deception), scourges/whips (pain), thorns (constant irritation). The nations Israel fails to drive out will become instruments of their suffering. Cf. Numbers 33:55.
v.14
"Not one thing has failed of all the good things which the LORD your God spoke concerning you; all are come to pass to you, and not one thing has failed thereof." (ESV) — Joshua's personal testimony at the end of his life. He has seen God's faithfulness firsthand for over 60 years. Not one promise failed. This is the witness of a dying man — and dying men do not lie.
v.15
"As all good things are come upon you, which the LORD your God promised you; so shall the LORD bring upon you all evil things." (ESV) — The terrifying corollary: God is as faithful to judge as He is to bless. His warnings are as certain as His promises. Do not presume on grace.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/joshua-23/ Guzik notes the balance in Joshua's farewell: past faithfulness (God kept every promise) and future warning (He will also keep His threats). Joshua does not leave Israel with false comfort. He loves them enough to warn them. Guzik also highlights v.11 — "love the LORD your God" — as the single most important command. Everything else flows from this.
  • Charles Spurgeon: "Joshua says, 'Not one thing hath failed.' This is the testimony of a man who walked with God for sixty years through wilderness and war. He tested every promise and found them all true. Can you say the same? Look back over your life — has God ever failed you? Has one word of His fallen to the ground? Then trust Him for the future. What He has been, He will be. What He has done, He will do."

Translations

  • v.1 — KJV: "Joshua waxed old and stricken in age" / ESV: "Joshua was old and well advanced in years" — Hebrew "zaqen ba bayamim." Same phrase as 13:1. KJV's "waxed old" uses archaic "waxed" (grew/became). "Stricken in age" = advanced in years.
  • v.6 — KJV: "Be ye therefore very courageous to keep and to do" / ESV: "be very strong to keep and to do" — Hebrew "chazaqtem m'od lishmor v'la'asot." "Chazaq" = be strong, courageous, firm. Joshua echoes God's words to him (1:7). The courage needed is for obedience, not just battle.
  • v.7 — KJV: "neither make mention of the names of their gods, nor cause to swear by them" / ESV: "or make mention of the names of their gods or swear by them" — Hebrew "lo tazkiru" (not mention) and "lo tashbi'u" (not swear). Even speaking pagan gods' names in oaths was forbidden — it acknowledged their existence and power.
  • v.8 — KJV: "cleave unto the LORD" / ESV: "cling to the LORD" — Hebrew "davaq" again (as in 22:5). The marriage word. Intimate, permanent attachment. The opposite of "cleaving to" the remaining nations (v.12, same word).
  • v.10 — KJV: "One man of you shall chase a thousand" / ESV: "One man of you puts to flight a thousand" — Hebrew "ish echad mikkhem yirdof elef." "Radaph" = to pursue, chase, put to flight. This supernatural ratio demonstrates that Israel's power is not numerical but divine.
  • v.13 — KJV: "scourges in your sides" / ESV: "a whip on your sides" — Hebrew "shotet b'tsiddeikhem." "Shotet" = whip, scourge. The remaining nations will not merely coexist — they will actively torment Israel. What was tolerated becomes what punishes.
  • v.14 — KJV: "I am going the way of all the earth" / ESV: "I am about to go the way of all the earth" — Hebrew "holekh ha-yom b'derekh kol ha-aretz." A euphemism for death — "the way all the earth goes." Every human walks this path. Joshua faces death with the same courage he brought to battle.

Reflection

  • 1. "Not one thing hath failed" (v.14). Joshua's deathbed testimony is God's perfect faithfulness. After a lifetime of testing God's promises, his verdict is: not one failed. Can we say the same? Look back — has God ever broken His word to you? Then trust Him for what lies ahead.
  • 2. God's faithfulness cuts both ways (v.15). He is as faithful to judge as to bless. His warnings are as certain as His promises. We cannot claim His blessings while ignoring His conditions. Grace is not license. Mercy does not cancel holiness.
  • 3. "Be very courageous to keep and to do" (v.6). Obedience requires courage — especially when surrounded by a culture that mocks God's standards. It takes more courage to obey than to fight. The hardest battles are internal, not external.
  • 4. "Love the LORD your God" (v.11). This is the root command. All obedience flows from love. All apostasy begins with love growing cold. Guard your heart's affection for God above all else. When love is right, obedience follows naturally.
  • 5. The danger of intermarriage/mixture (v.12). Joshua warns against intimate association with those who worship other gods. The application is not ethnic but spiritual: "Be ye not unequally yoked together with unbelievers" (2 Corinthians 6:14). Intimate partnership with the world leads to spiritual compromise.
  • 6. Snares, traps, scourges, thorns (v.13). The sins we tolerate become the sins that torment us. What we refuse to drive out becomes what drives us out. The Canaanites Israel kept became the instruments of Israel's suffering. Deal with sin completely — or it will deal with you.
