Jeremiah — Chapter 28

1In that same year, at the beginning of the reign of Zedekiah king of Judah, in the fifth month of the fourth year, Hananiah the son of Azzur, the prophet from Gibeon, spoke to me in the house of the LORD, in the presence of the priests and all the people, saying,

2“Thus says the LORD of hosts, the God of Israel: I have broken the yoke of the king of Babylon.

3Within two years I will bring back to this place all the vessels of the LORD’s house, which Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon took away from this place and carried to Babylon.

4I will also bring back to this place Jeconiah the son of Jehoiakim, king of Judah, and all the exiles from Judah who went to Babylon, declares the LORD, for I will break the yoke of the king of Babylon.”

5Then the prophet Jeremiah spoke to Hananiah the prophet in the presence of the priests and all the people who were standing in the house of the LORD,

6and the prophet Jeremiah said, “Amen! May the LORD do so; may the LORD make the words that you have prophesied come true, and bring back to this place from Babylon the vessels of the house of the LORD, and all the exiles.

7Yet hear now this word that I speak in your hearing and in the hearing of all the people.

8The prophets who preceded you and me from ancient times prophesied war, famine, and pestilence against many countries and great kingdoms.

9As for the prophet who prophesies peace, when the word of that prophet comes to pass, then it will be known that the LORD has truly sent the prophet.”

10Then the prophet Hananiah took the yoke-bars from the neck of Jeremiah the prophet and broke them.

11And Hananiah spoke in the presence of all the people, saying, “Thus says the LORD: Even so will I break the yoke of Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon from the neck of all the nations within two years.” But Jeremiah the prophet went his way.

12Sometime after the prophet Hananiah had broken the yoke-bars from off the neck of Jeremiah the prophet, the word of the LORD came to Jeremiah:

13“Go, tell Hananiah, ‘Thus says the LORD: You have broken wooden bars, but you have made in their place bars of iron.

14For thus says the LORD of hosts, the God of Israel: I have put upon the neck of all these nations an iron yoke to serve Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon, and they shall serve him, for I have given to him even the beasts of the field.’”

15And Jeremiah the prophet said to the prophet Hananiah, “Listen, Hananiah, the LORD has not sent you, and you have made this people trust in a lie.

16Therefore thus says the LORD: ‘Behold, I will remove you from the face of the earth. This year you shall die, because you have uttered rebellion against the LORD.’”

17In that same year, in the seventh month, the prophet Hananiah died.

Summary

The showdown with Hananiah — a false prophet who breaks Jeremiah's yoke off his neck and declares: "Within two years God will bring back the temple vessels and Jehoiachin." Jeremiah initially walks away, but God sends him back with a harder message: the wooden yoke becomes an IRON yoke, and Hananiah will die within the year — he does, within two months.

Key Verses

v.6
"Amen: the LORD do so: the LORD perform thy words" (KJV) — Jeremiah's initial generosity: "I HOPE you're right!" He doesn't attack Hananiah immediately. He genuinely wishes the hopeful message were true.
v.8
"The prophets that have been before me and before thee of old prophesied both against many countries... of war, and of evil, and of pestilence" (KJV) — Jeremiah's test: prophets of DOOM have precedent (the default is judgment). A prophet of PEACE must prove himself by FULFILLMENT.
v.9
"The prophet which prophesieth of peace, when the word of the prophet shall come to pass, then shall the prophet be known, that the LORD hath truly sent him" (KJV) — THE test of a peace-prophet: DID IT COME TRUE? The burden of proof is on the one promising good times.
v.13
"Thou hast broken the yokes of wood; but thou shalt make for them yokes of iron" (KJV) — Breaking the yoke of submission doesn't eliminate discipline — it INTENSIFIES it. Resisting God's discipline makes it heavier, not lighter.
v.16
"This year thou shalt die, because thou hast taught rebellion against the LORD" (KJV) — The sentence: DEATH for teaching rebellion. Not simply being wrong but actively contradicting what God clearly stated.
v.17
"So Hananiah the prophet died the same year in the seventh month" (KJV) — Fulfilled within approximately two months. The swiftness validates Jeremiah and invalidates Hananiah permanently.

Cross-References

  • v.6 — 1 Kings 22:15-28 — Micaiah vs. the 400 false prophets (similar confrontation). v.8 — Deuteronomy 18:21-22 — "if the thing follow not... the LORD hath not spoken it." v.9 — 1 John 4:1 — "try the spirits whether they are of God." v.13 — Deuteronomy 28:48 — "he shall put a yoke of iron upon thy neck." v.15 — Jeremiah 14:14 — "I sent them not." v.16 — Deuteronomy 13:5 — "that prophet... shall be put to death; because he hath spoken to turn you away." v.17 — Acts 5:5,10 — Ananias and Sapphira — sudden death for lying.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/jeremiah-28/
  • Charles Spurgeon: "Hananiah broke the wooden yoke and got an iron one. This is always the result of resisting God's lighter discipline: you don't escape — you get something heavier. Accept the wood before God sends the iron."

Translations

  • v.1 — "Hananiah" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "Chananyah" = "YHWH is gracious." Ironic: his name speaks of grace while his message contradicts God's declared judgment.
  • v.3 — "two full years" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "be'od shnatayim yamim" = within two years of days (precisely measured). A specific, testable timeline — that proves false.
  • v.9 — "prophesieth of peace" (KJV/ESV) — The biblical principle: prophets of judgment need no special validation (judgment is the default for unfaithful covenant people). Prophets of PEACE carry the burden of proof.
  • v.13 — "yokes of iron" (KJV/ESV) — From Deuteronomy 28:48: the intensified covenant curse. Wooden yokes can be broken; iron yokes cannot.

Reflection

  • 1. Breaking the wooden yoke brings iron (v.13). When you resist God's lighter discipline, you don't escape — you get something HEAVIER. Accept the wooden yoke (early correction) before God sends iron (severe consequence).
  • 2. The test of a peace-prophet (v.9). Anyone can promise good things. The TEST: does it come TRUE? Don't follow voices that tell you what you want to hear without checking if their predictions actually materialize.
  • 3. Hananiah's name meant "God is gracious" (v.1). Having a spiritual-sounding name (or title, or platform) doesn't validate your message. Check the CONTENT against God's revealed word, not the messenger's credentials.
  • 4. Jeremiah waited for God's word (v.12). He didn't react immediately to Hananiah — he walked away and waited until God spoke. Not every provocation requires instant response. Sometimes the wisest thing is: wait, pray, then return with God's actual word.
  • 5. Two months vs. two years (vv.3,17). Hananiah predicted two years until restoration; he didn't even LIVE two more months. False confidence about the future is dangerous — you may not have the time you assume.
