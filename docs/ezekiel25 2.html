Ezekiel — Chapter 25

1The word of the LORD came to me:

2“Son of man, set your face toward the Ammonites and prophesy against them.

3Say to the Ammonites, Hear the word of the Lord GOD: Thus says the Lord GOD, Because you said, ‘Aha!’ over my sanctuary when it was profaned, and over the land of Israel when it was made desolate, and over the house of Judah when they went into exile,

4therefore behold, I am handing you over to the people of the East for a possession, and they shall set their encampments among you and make their dwellings in your midst. They shall eat your fruit, and they shall drink your milk.

5I will make Rabbah a pasture for camels and Ammon a fold for flocks. Then you will know that I am the LORD.

6For thus says the Lord GOD: Because you have clapped your hands and stamped your feet and rejoiced with all the malice within your soul against the land of Israel,

7therefore, behold, I have stretched out my hand against you, and will hand you over as plunder to the nations. And I will cut you off from the peoples and will make you perish out of the countries; I will destroy you. Then you will know that I am the LORD.

8“Thus says the Lord GOD: Because Moab and Seir said, ‘Behold, the house of Judah is like all the other nations,’

9therefore I will lay open the flank of Moab from the cities, from its cities on its frontier, the glory of the country, Beth-jeshimoth, Baal-meon, and Kiriathaim.

10I will give it along with the Ammonites to the people of the East as a possession, that the Ammonites may be remembered no more among the nations,

11and I will execute judgments upon Moab. Then they will know that I am the LORD.

12“Thus says the Lord GOD: Because Edom acted revengefully against the house of Judah and has grievously offended in taking vengeance on them,

13therefore thus says the Lord GOD, I will stretch out my hand against Edom and cut off from it man and beast. And I will make it desolate; from Teman even to Dedan they shall fall by the sword.

14And I will lay my vengeance upon Edom by the hand of my people Israel, and they shall do in Edom according to my anger and according to my wrath, and they shall know my vengeance, declares the Lord GOD.

15“Thus says the Lord GOD: Because the Philistines acted revengefully and took vengeance with malice of soul to destroy in never-ending enmity,

16therefore thus says the Lord GOD, Behold, I will stretch out my hand against the Philistines, and I will cut off the Cherethites and destroy the rest of the seacoast.

17I will execute great vengeance on them with wrathful rebukes. Then they will know that I am the LORD, when I lay my vengeance upon them.”

Summary

The oracles against the nations begin. God turns His attention from Jerusalem to four neighboring peoples who rejoiced over Judah's fall: Ammon, Moab, Edom, and Philistia. Each receives a specific charge and a specific sentence. Their sin is not merely paganism — it is the gloating, mocking joy they took in watching God's people suffer. God defends His own, even while disciplining them.

Key Verses

v.3
"And say unto the Ammonites, Hear the word of the Lord GOD; Thus saith the Lord GOD; Because thou saidst, Aha, against my sanctuary, when it was profaned; and against the land of Israel, when it was desolate; and against the house of Judah, when they went into captivity" (KJV) — Ammon's sin: "Aha!" One word of gleeful mockery at the temple's desecration, the land's desolation, and the people's exile. God heard that single syllable.
v.8
"Thus saith the Lord GOD; Because that Moab and Seir do say, Behold, the house of Judah is like unto all the heathen" (KJV) — Moab's sin: theological mockery. "They're no different from any other nation." Denying Israel's special status with God. Reducing the covenant people to just another defeated tribe.
v.12
"Thus saith the Lord GOD; Because that Edom hath dealt against the house of Judah by taking vengeance, and hath greatly offended, and revenged himself upon them" (KJV) — Edom's sin: active vengeance against their OWN BROTHER (Jacob/Esau). Not just gloating but participating in the destruction. Blood betraying blood.
v.17
"And I will execute great vengeance upon them with furious rebukes; and they shall know that I am the LORD, when I shall lay my vengeance upon them" (KJV) — The recognition formula applied to GENTILE nations. God's judgment of the nations has the same purpose as His judgment of Israel: "they shall KNOW that I am the LORD."

Cross-References

  • v.3 — Psalm 83:2-4 — Nations conspire together against Israel: "Come, let us cut them off from being a nation." v.4 — Judges 6:3 — "The children of the east" — desert raiders from Arabia who repeatedly invaded. v.8 — Deuteronomy 7:6 — "Thou art an holy people unto the LORD" — what Moab denied about Israel. v.12 — Obadiah 10-14 — The extended indictment of Edom for violence against brother Jacob. v.12 — Genesis 27:41 — The ancient grudge: Esau hated Jacob. v.13 — Malachi 1:2-4 — "I hated Esau and laid his mountains waste." v.15 — 1 Samuel 13:19-20 — Philistia's long history of oppressing Israel. v.17 — Zephaniah 2:4-7 — Parallel judgment oracle against Philistia's cities.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/ezekiel-25/
  • Charles Spurgeon: "God is never so fierce as when He defends those He is disciplining. The father who spanks his child will still fight any man who harms that child. Ammon said 'Aha!' over Israel's pain and learned that God's rod is one thing, but God's SWORD is quite another. Touch not mine anointed — even when I Myself am correcting them."

Translations

  • v.3 — "Aha!" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "he'ach" = an exclamation of malicious delight, schadenfreude. A mocking cheer at someone's suffering. One syllable of gloating that brings down national judgment.
  • v.4 — "men of the east" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "b'ne qedem" = sons/children of the east. Refers to nomadic Arabian tribes from the Syrian desert who would eventually overrun Ammon's territory.
  • v.7 — "cut thee off from the people" (KJV) — Hebrew "hikrattikha min ha'ammim" = to cut/sever from the peoples. Complete national extinction — Ammon will cease to exist as a distinct people (historically fulfilled).
  • v.14 — "by the hand of my people Israel" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "b'yad ammi yisra'el." God will use Israel itself as His instrument of vengeance against Edom. Partially fulfilled by the Maccabees' conquest of Idumea (the Edomite territory) in the 2nd century BC.

Reflection

  • 1. God hears "Aha!" (v.3). A single syllable of mocking someone else's pain invites divine attention. Schadenfreude is not a small sin — it's a revelation of character that God judges. Do you quietly celebrate when people you dislike suffer?
  • 2. "The house of Judah is like all the heathen" (v.8). Moab's sin was theological reductionism — denying God's special relationship with His people. Don't let the world convince you that the church is "just another institution." God's people are different — not because of their virtue but because of His covenant.
  • 3. Family betrayal cuts deepest (v.12). Edom's sin is not just hostility but FRATERNAL hostility. Betrayal by a brother is worse than attack by a stranger. How do you treat fellow believers when they're suffering? Are you Edom or are you Ruth?
  • 4. God disciplines His children but DEFENDS them from outsiders. Jerusalem is under judgment for real sin — but that doesn't give her neighbors permission to pile on. God's correction of you does not authorize others to condemn you. He alone holds that right.
  • 5. "They shall know that I am the LORD" (v.17). Even in judgment on pagan nations, the goal is KNOWLEDGE of God. God's justice is never purposeless. Every consequence is a lesson. Every judgment is a revelation.
