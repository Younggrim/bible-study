Spiritual Disciplines

Giving & Generosity

The Discipline

Giving is a spiritual discipline that breaks the grip of materialism and trains our hearts to trust God as our provider. When we give generously — of our money, time, and resources — we declare that God is our security, not our bank accounts. Generosity is the practical expression of faith that God owns everything and we are stewards, not owners.

Scripture reveals a generous God who gives extravagantly: He gave creation, redemption, His own Son, and eternal life. When we give, we reflect His character and participate in His mission. Giving is not God's way of funding His work — He owns the cattle on a thousand hills — it is His way of freeing us from the idolatry of money and forming Christ's character within us.

Jesus taught more about money than any other subject except the kingdom of God, because He knew that our treasure and our hearts are inseparably linked. Where we put our money reveals what we truly value. Generosity is the discipline that ensures our treasure — and therefore our hearts — remain fixed on eternal things rather than temporary ones.

Key Scriptures

  • 2 Corinthians 9:6-7 — "Each of you should give what you have decided in your heart to give, not reluctantly or under compulsion, for God loves a cheerful giver."
  • Malachi 3:10 — "Bring the whole tithe into the storehouse... and see if I will not throw open the floodgates of heaven."
  • Acts 20:35 — "It is more blessed to give than to receive."
  • Proverbs 11:25 — "A generous person will prosper; whoever refreshes others will be refreshed."
  • Luke 6:38 — "Give, and it will be given to you. A good measure, pressed down, shaken together and running over."

Practicing It

Practicing generosity begins with the tithe — giving the first ten percent of income to God's work as an act of trust and worship. But biblical generosity goes beyond the tithe to cheerful, sacrificial, Spirit-led giving that responds to needs as God directs. Start where you are and grow from there, letting God stretch your faith incrementally.

Generosity is not just about money. Give your time to serve others, your attention to listen, your skills to help, and your home to welcome. Practice hospitality, tip generously, give secretly, support missions, and look for creative ways to bless others. As you practice giving in all its forms, you will discover that it truly is more blessed to give than to receive.

Reflection Questions

  • What does your giving reveal about what you truly trust — God or money?
  • How might increasing your generosity deepen your trust in God's provision?
  • In what non-financial ways can you practice generosity this week?
