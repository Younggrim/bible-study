Leviticus — Chapter 20

1The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

2“Say to the people of Israel, Any one of the people of Israel or of the strangers who sojourn in Israel who gives any of his children to Molech shall surely be put to death. The people of the land shall stone him with stones.

3I myself will set my face against that man and will cut him off from among his people, because he has given one of his children to Molech, to make my sanctuary unclean and to profane my holy name.

4And if the people of the land do at all close their eyes to that man when he gives one of his children to Molech, and do not put him to death,

5then I will set my face against that man and against his clan and will cut them off from among their people, him and all who follow him in whoring after Molech.

6“If a person turns to mediums and necromancers, whoring after them, I will set my face against that person and will cut him off from among his people.

7Consecrate yourselves, therefore, and be holy, for I am the LORD your God.

8Keep my statutes and do them; I am the LORD who sanctifies you.

9For anyone who curses his father or his mother shall surely be put to death; he has cursed his father or his mother; his blood is upon him.

10“If a man commits adultery with the wife of his neighbor, both the adulterer and the adulteress shall surely be put to death.

11If a man lies with his father’s wife, he has uncovered his father’s nakedness; both of them shall surely be put to death; their blood is upon them.

12If a man lies with his daughter-in-law, both of them shall surely be put to death; they have committed perversion; their blood is upon them.

13If a man lies with a male as with a woman, both of them have committed an abomination; they shall surely be put to death; their blood is upon them.

14If a man takes a woman and her mother also, it is depravity; he and they shall be burned with fire, that there may be no depravity among you.

15If a man lies with an animal, he shall surely be put to death, and you shall kill the animal.

16If a woman approaches any animal and lies with it, you shall kill the woman and the animal; they shall surely be put to death; their blood is upon them.

17“If a man takes his sister, a daughter of his father or a daughter of his mother, and sees her nakedness, and she sees his nakedness, it is a disgrace, and they shall be cut off in the sight of the children of their people. He has uncovered his sister’s nakedness, and he shall bear his iniquity.

18If a man lies with a woman during her menstrual period and uncovers her nakedness, he has made naked her fountain, and she has uncovered the fountain of her blood. Both of them shall be cut off from among their people.

19You shall not uncover the nakedness of your mother’s sister or of your father’s sister, for that is to make naked one’s relative; they shall bear their iniquity.

20If a man lies with his uncle’s wife, he has uncovered his uncle’s nakedness; they shall bear their sin; they shall die childless.

21If a man takes his brother’s wife, it is impurity. He has uncovered his brother’s nakedness; they shall be childless.

22“You shall therefore keep all my statutes and all my rules and do them, that the land where I am bringing you to live may not vomit you out.

23And you shall not walk in the customs of the nation that I am driving out before you, for they did all these things, and therefore I detested them.

24But I have said to you, ‘You shall inherit their land, and I will give it to you to possess, a land flowing with milk and honey.’ I am the LORD your God, who has separated you from the peoples.

25You shall therefore separate the clean beast from the unclean, and the unclean bird from the clean. You shall not make yourselves detestable by beast or by bird or by anything with which the ground crawls, which I have set apart for you to hold unclean.

26You shall be holy to me, for I the LORD am holy and have separated you from the peoples, that you should be mine.

27“A man or a woman who is a medium or a necromancer shall surely be put to death. They shall be stoned with stones; their blood shall be upon them.”

Summary

God prescribes penalties for idolatry, occult practices, and sexual sins — holiness has consequences, and God separates His people from wickedness.

Key Verses

v.3
"I will set my face against that man, and will cut him off from among his people; because he hath given of his seed unto Molech, to defile my sanctuary, and to profane my holy name." — God personally opposes those who sacrifice children.
v.7
"Sanctify yourselves therefore, and be ye holy: for I am the LORD your God." — The call to holiness amid the penalties for sin.
v.8
"I am the LORD which sanctify you." — God both commands holiness and provides it. Sanctification is His work.
v.9
"Every one that curseth his father or his mother shall be surely put to death." — The family is sacred. Dishonoring parents is a capital offense.
v.23
"You shall not walk in the customs of the nation that I am driving out before you, for they did all these things, and therefore I detested them." (ESV) — The Canaanites are judged for these very sins.
v.26
"You shall be holy to me, for I the LORD am holy and have separated you from the peoples, that you should be mine." (ESV) — The purpose of separation: belonging to God.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/leviticus-20/ Guzik notes the relationship between chapters 18 and 20 (prohibition and penalty) and emphasizes that the severity of penalties reflects God's view of these sins, not human cruelty. He highlights v.26 as the key: separation is for the purpose of BELONGING — "that ye should be mine."
  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "A Peculiar People" "God says: I have SEVERED you from other people, that ye should be MINE. There is the whole of it — separation FOR possession. God does not separate His people merely to make them different; He separates them to make them HIS. You are not merely called out of the world; you are called INTO God's own family. The holiness He demands is not cold isolation but warm belonging. 'That ye should be mine' — this is the sweetest word in all of Leviticus. You are His peculiar treasure, His special possession, His beloved people. And because you are His, you must be holy — for He is holy. A king's child bears the king's character."

Translations

  • v.2 — KJV: "giveth any of his seed unto Molech" / ESV: "gives any of his children to Molech" — Hebrew "yitten mi-zar'o la-Molekh" — gives from his seed to Molech. KJV's "seed" is literal; ESV's "children" is interpretive but correct. "Seed" (zera) here means offspring.
  • v.3 — KJV: "to defile my sanctuary" / ESV: "to make my sanctuary unclean" — Hebrew "l'tamme et miqdashi." Both convey the same meaning. Child sacrifice defiles God's sanctuary — even though it occurs outside the sanctuary. Sin committed in the community pollutes the place where God dwells.
  • v.6 — KJV: "familiar spirits... wizards" / ESV: "mediums and necromancers" — Hebrew "ha-ovot v'ha-yid'onim." "Ov" = a spirit of the dead, or one who channels such spirits (a medium). "Yid'oni" = a knowing one, a spiritist (one who claims supernatural knowledge). KJV's "familiar spirits" refers to the spirit that is "familiar" (known/attached) to the medium. ESV uses modern terminology.
  • v.9 — KJV: "curseth his father or his mother" / ESV: "curses his father or his mother" — Hebrew "y'qallel et aviv v'et immo." "Qalal" = to make light of, to curse, to treat with contempt. The opposite of "kavad" (to honor/make heavy). Not merely profanity but fundamental disrespect — treating parents as worthless.
  • v.12 — KJV: "they have wrought confusion" / ESV: "they have committed perversion" — Hebrew "tevel asu" — they have done confusion/ perversion. Same word as 18:23 (bestiality). Implies a violation of created order — mixing what God has distinguished.
  • v.14 — KJV: "it is wickedness" / ESV: "it is depravity" — Hebrew "zimmah hi" — it is a scheme/plan/heinous sin. Implies calculated, deliberate wickedness — not a moment of weakness but a planned perversion.
  • v.17 — KJV: "it is a wicked thing" / ESV: "it is a disgrace" — Hebrew "chesed hu" — here used in its negative sense: a shameful thing, a disgrace. (The same word "chesed" usually means lovingkindness — but in this rare usage it means shame/reproach. Context determines meaning.)
  • v.26 — KJV: "have severed you from other people" / ESV: "have separated you from the peoples" — Hebrew "va-avdil etkhem min ha-ammim" — and I have divided/separated you from the peoples. "Avdil" = to divide, separate (same root as Genesis 1 — God "divided" light from darkness). God's separation of Israel mirrors His creative work of distinction.
  • v.27 — KJV: "a familiar spirit, or that is a wizard" / ESV: "a medium or a necromancer" — Same terms as v.6. The chapter ends where it began — with the death penalty for occult practices. Bookend structure.

Reflection

  • 1. "Sanctify yourselves... I am the LORD which sanctify you" (vv.7-8). Both are true simultaneously. YOU must sanctify yourself (human responsibility). GOD sanctifies you (divine enablement). Holiness is not passive — you must pursue it. But it is not merely human effort — God empowers it. Work out your salvation, for God works in you (Philippians 2:12-13).
  • 2. "If the people of the land do any ways hide their eyes" (v.4). God holds the community responsible for tolerating evil. Looking away is not neutrality — it is complicity. When you see sin and say nothing, do nothing, you share in the guilt. Are you "hiding your eyes" from evil in your family, church, or community?
  • 3. "They committed all these things, and therefore I abhorred them" (v.23). The Canaanites were not expelled for their ethnicity but for their behavior. God's judgment is moral, not racial. And the same standard applies to Israel — and to any nation. No people group is exempt from moral accountability before God.
  • 4. "I the LORD am holy, and have severed you from other people, that ye should be mine" (v.26). The purpose of holiness is BELONGING. You are set apart not for isolation but for intimacy with God. "That ye should be mine" — this is the sweetest motivation for holiness. You are His treasured possession. Live like it.
  • 5. "Such were some of you: but ye are washed" (1 Corinthians 6:11). The penalties in this chapter are severe — but the gospel offers cleansing even from these sins. No one is beyond redemption. The blood of Christ is sufficient for every sin listed here. The question is not "how bad is my sin?" but "how great is His grace?"
