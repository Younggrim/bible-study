Miracles of Jesus

Calming the Storm

The Miracle

As Jesus and His disciples crossed the Sea of Galilee, a furious storm arose — so violent that waves crashed over the boat and it began to fill with water. The disciples, several of whom were experienced fishermen familiar with this lake's sudden squalls, were terrified beyond their expertise. Yet Jesus was asleep in the stern, resting on a cushion with perfect peace. In desperation, they woke Him crying, "Teacher, do you not care that we are perishing?" (Mark 4:38).

Jesus rose, rebuked the wind, and said to the sea, "Peace! Be still!" Immediately the wind ceased and there was a great calm — not the gradual subsiding of a natural storm, but instant, supernatural tranquility. Then He turned to His disciples with a question more challenging than the storm itself: "Why are you so afraid? Have you still no faith?" The disciples were filled with a new kind of fear — not terror of the storm, but awe of the One who commands it.

Their whispered question to one another — "Who then is this, that even the wind and the sea obey him?" — is the central question of Mark's Gospel. In the Old Testament, authority over the sea belongs exclusively to God. The psalmist celebrates: "You rule the raging of the sea; when its waves rise, you still them" (Psalm 89:9). Jesus' command over creation reveals His identity: He is the Creator clothed in human flesh, the God who spoke the waters into existence now speaking them into silence.

Key Scriptures

  • Mark 4:35-41 — The full account of Jesus calming the storm on the Sea of Galilee.
  • Psalm 107:28-30 — "Then they cried to the LORD in their trouble, and he delivered them... He made the storm be still, and the waves of the sea were hushed."
  • Psalm 89:9 — "You rule the raging of the sea; when its waves rise, you still them."
  • Nahum 1:4 — "He rebukes the sea and makes it dry; he dries up all the rivers."
  • Mark 4:41 — "And they were filled with great fear and said to one another, 'Who then is this, that even the wind and the sea obey him?'"

What It Reveals About Jesus

This miracle reveals Jesus' absolute authority over the natural world and His divine identity as the Creator God. The same voice that spoke creation into existence in Genesis 1 now speaks chaos into order on the Sea of Galilee. It also reveals the mystery of the incarnation — the God who never sleeps nor slumbers (Psalm 121:4) was genuinely asleep in human exhaustion, yet His divine authority remained fully intact.

Equally significant is what this miracle reveals about Jesus' relationship with His people. He does not abandon them in the storm. His apparent inactivity (sleeping) is not indifference but trust in the Father's timing. And His question — "Have you still no faith?" — reveals that He expected them to rest in His presence even before He acted. The lesson is not that storms won't come, but that Jesus is in the boat with us, and His presence is sufficient even when He seems silent.

Reflection Questions

  • When life's storms rage, do you tend to assume Jesus is indifferent — "asleep" to your suffering?
  • What would it look like to have faith in the storm rather than only asking for the storm to stop?
  • How does the knowledge that Jesus is "in the boat" with you change the way you face uncertainty and fear?
