Obadiah — Chapter 1

1The vision of Obadiah. Thus says the Lord GOD concerning Edom: We have heard a report from the LORD, and a messenger has been sent among the nations: “Rise up! Let us rise against her for battle!”

Summary

The shortest book in the Old Testament pronounces God's judgment on Edom for violence against brother Jacob — their pride will be brought low, and "the kingdom shall be the LORD's."

Key Verses

v.3
"The pride of your heart has deceived you, you who live in the clefts of the rock, in your lofty dwelling, who say in your heart, 'Who will bring me down to the ground?'" (ESV) — The root sin: PRIDE. They lived in high, seemingly impregnable rock fortresses (Petra). Their geography produced arrogance. "Who can touch me?" God answers: "I will bring you down" (v.4).
v.4
"Though you soar aloft like the eagle, though your nest is set among the stars, from there I will bring you down, declares the LORD." (ESV) — Even if you build among the STARS, God can reach you. No height, no fortress, no security system is beyond God's reach. Pride ALWAYS falls.
v.12
"But do not gloat over the day of your brother in the day of his misfortune." (ESV) — Eight times "do not" (vv.12-14). Edom's sins were specific: gloating, rejoicing, boasting, entering the gate, laying hands on goods, cutting off fugitives, delivering up survivors. Each action is condemned individually.
v.15
"As you have done, it shall be done to you; your deeds shall return on your own head." (ESV) — The principle of reciprocal justice. What you did to others will be done to YOU. God's judgment is not arbitrary — it is proportional and reciprocal. Sow cruelty, reap cruelty.
v.21
"The kingdom shall be the LORD's." (ESV) — The book's final word. After all judgment, after all human pride falls — the KINGDOM belongs to God. This is the conclusion of all history.

Cross-References

  • v.3 — Proverbs 16:18 — "Pride goeth before destruction, and an haughty spirit before a fall." v.3 — Isaiah 14:13-15 — Lucifer: "I will ascend... I will exalt my throne above the stars." Same arrogance, same fall. v.4 — Isaiah 63:1 — "Who is this that cometh from Edom?" The warrior who tramples Edom. v.10 — Genesis 27:41 — "Esau hated Jacob because of the blessing." The ancient grudge. v.10 — Amos 1:11 — "He pursued his brother with the sword, and did cast off all pity." v.12 — Psalm 137:7 — "Remember, O LORD, the children of Edom in the day of Jerusalem; who said, Rase it, rase it." v.15 — Galatians 6:7 — "Whatsoever a man soweth, that shall he also reap." v.17 — Joel 2:32 — "In mount Zion and in Jerusalem shall be deliverance." Same promise. v.21 — Revelation 11:15 — "The kingdoms of this world are become the kingdoms of our Lord, and of his Christ."

Translations

  • v.3 — "clefts of the rock" — Hebrew "sela" = rock, cliff. Likely referring to Petra (Greek for "rock"), Edom's famous rock fortress carved into cliff faces.
  • v.5 — The rhetorical comparison: even THIEVES leave something; even grape-gatherers leave gleanings. But God's judgment on Edom will be TOTAL — worse than robbery.
  • v.7 — "thy confederacy... the men at peace with thee... they that eat thy bread" — Edom's own allies will betray them. The betrayer will be betrayed. Poetic justice.
  • v.15 — "the day of the LORD" — Shifts from Edom to ALL nations. Edom's judgment is a preview of universal divine judgment.
  • v.21 — "saviours" (KJV) — Hebrew "moshiim" = deliverers/judges (like in the book of Judges). Some see Messianic anticipation in this plural leading to the singular ultimate Savior.

Reflection

  • 1. "Who shall bring me down?" (v.3). Whenever you feel untouchable — secure in position, wealth, health, or relationships — check yourself. Pride says "no one can reach me." God says "I will bring you down." Security apart from God is illusion.
  • 2. "Thou shouldest not have" (vv.12-14). Gloating over someone's downfall is JUDGED. Rejoicing in an enemy's pain, profiting from their loss, blocking their escape — these are not neutral actions. God sees your response to other people's suffering. Compassion or cruelty — which will He find?
  • 3. "As thou hast done, it shall be done unto thee" (v.15). The golden rule has a dark side: what you sow toward others returns upon your OWN head. Be careful how you treat people in their weakness — you may find yourself there.
  • 4. "The kingdom shall be the LORD's" (v.21). All empires fall. All pride collapses. All human kingdoms end. Only ONE kingdom endures: God's. Align yourself with the eternal kingdom, not temporal ones.
