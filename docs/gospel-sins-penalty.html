The Gospel

Sin's Penalty

The Truth

Sin carries a penalty — and that penalty is death. Not merely physical death, though sin introduced that into the world, but spiritual death: eternal separation from God, the source of all life, joy, and goodness. This is not arbitrary punishment but the natural consequence of severing the relationship with the One who is life itself.

The concept of wages is instructive: wages are earned. Death is not an unfair imposition but something sin rightfully earns. Just as a worker deserves payment for labor performed, sin deserves death as its just recompense. God would be unjust to ignore sin — His holy nature demands that it be addressed, and the only adequate payment is death.

This truth is sobering but essential. Without understanding the penalty, we cannot appreciate the rescue. A person who does not know they are drowning will not reach for the lifeline. The bad news of sin's penalty creates the urgency that makes the good news of God's grace truly good. We cannot understand the cross without first understanding what the cross saves us from.

Key Scriptures

  • Romans 6:23 — "The wages of sin is death, but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord."
  • Ezekiel 18:20 — "The one who sins is the one who will die."
  • Revelation 20:15 — "Anyone whose name was not found written in the book of life was thrown into the lake of fire."
  • Romans 5:12 — "Sin entered the world through one man, and death through sin, and in this way death came to all people."
  • Hebrews 9:27 — "People are destined to die once, and after that to face judgment."

Why It Matters

The penalty of sin matters because it reveals what is at stake. This is not a minor infraction with a small fine — it is a capital offense with an eternal consequence. Understanding the gravity of the penalty helps us understand the magnitude of God's love: He did not save us from a trivial danger but from the ultimate catastrophe of eternal separation from Himself.

But Romans 6:23 does not end with bad news. "The wages of sin is death, but the gift of God is eternal life." The penalty has been paid — not by us, but by Christ. What justice demanded, grace provided. This is the heart of the gospel: we deserve death, but God offers life. We earned judgment, but God gives a gift. The penalty is real, but so is the provision.

Reflection Questions

  • How does understanding sin's penalty deepen your appreciation for Christ's sacrifice?
  • Why is it important that God takes sin seriously rather than simply overlooking it?
  • How does the contrast between wages (earned) and gift (unearned) illuminate the gospel?
