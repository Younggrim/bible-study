Names of God

El Elyon

Understanding El Elyon

El Elyon, meaning "God Most High," first appears in Genesis 14:18-20 when Melchizedek — the mysterious priest-king of Salem — blessed Abraham "by God Most High, Creator of heaven and earth." This name declares God's supreme position above all other powers, authorities, and spiritual beings. There is no one higher, no one greater, and no one who can challenge His ultimate sovereignty over all creation.

The title "Elyon" comes from the Hebrew root meaning "to go up" or "to ascend." It speaks of absolute supremacy — God is above all heavens, above all thrones, above all principalities, and above every name that is named. When Nebuchadnezzar was humbled by God and restored to his sanity, he declared: "I praised the Most High; I honored and glorified him who lives forever" (Daniel 4:34). Even the most powerful earthly ruler had to acknowledge El Elyon's unchallenged supremacy.

This name is particularly meaningful in the context of the ancient Near East, where nations each claimed their gods were supreme. By revealing Himself as El Elyon, God declared that He is not merely the God of Israel but the supreme God over all nations, all peoples, and all other claimed deities. His sovereignty is not regional or tribal — it is universal and absolute.

Key Scriptures

  • Genesis 14:18-20 — Melchizedek blesses Abraham by "God Most High, Creator of heaven and earth," establishing El Elyon's universal sovereignty.
  • Psalm 91:1-2 — "Whoever dwells in the shelter of the Most High will rest in the shadow of the Almighty." El Elyon provides ultimate protection.
  • Daniel 4:34-35 — "His dominion is an eternal dominion... He does as he pleases with the powers of heaven." Nebuchadnezzar's confession of El Elyon's supreme rule.
  • Psalm 47:2 — "For the LORD Most High is awesome, the great King over all the earth." El Elyon reigns over every nation without exception.
  • Isaiah 14:14 — "I will make myself like the Most High." Satan's ultimate ambition was to rival El Elyon — an ambition that was utterly defeated.

What This Name Means for Us

Knowing God as El Elyon means that no power in heaven or on earth can override His purposes for our lives. When governments seem unstable, when evil appears to triumph, when circumstances spiral beyond our control — El Elyon sits enthroned above it all. He is not wringing His hands in heaven; He is working all things according to the counsel of His will. Nothing catches Him off guard, nothing threatens His throne, and nothing can prevent His good plans from coming to pass.

This name also gives us proper perspective. When we are tempted to elevate anything — career, relationships, money, reputation — to the highest place in our lives, El Elyon reminds us that only God belongs on the throne. He alone is Most High, and when we acknowledge His supremacy, everything else finds its proper order. Our anxieties shrink when we remember that the Most High God is our shelter and our defender.

Reflection Questions

  • What situations in your life feel "out of control"? How does knowing El Elyon reigns above all things change your perspective?
  • Is there anything you have elevated to a place of supremacy in your heart that belongs only to God?
  • How does Nebuchadnezzar's story — a powerful king humbled and then restored — speak to your own need for humility before El Elyon?
  • What does it mean to "dwell in the shelter of the Most High" as a daily practice, not just a Sunday declaration?
