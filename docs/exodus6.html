Exodus — Chapter 6

1But the LORD said to Moses, “Now you shall see what I will do to Pharaoh; for with a strong hand he will send them out, and with a strong hand he will drive them out of his land.”

2God spoke to Moses and said to him, “I am the LORD.

3I appeared to Abraham, to Isaac, and to Jacob, as God Almighty, but by my name the LORD I did not make myself known to them.

4I also established my covenant with them to give them the land of Canaan, the land in which they lived as sojourners.

5Moreover, I have heard the groaning of the people of Israel whom the Egyptians hold as slaves, and I have remembered my covenant.

6Say therefore to the people of Israel, ‘I am the LORD, and I will bring you out from under the burdens of the Egyptians, and I will deliver you from slavery to them, and I will redeem you with an outstretched arm and with great acts of judgment.

7I will take you to be my people, and I will be your God, and you shall know that I am the LORD your God, who has brought you out from under the burdens of the Egyptians.

8I will bring you into the land that I swore to give to Abraham, to Isaac, and to Jacob. I will give it to you for a possession. I am the LORD.’”

9Moses spoke thus to the people of Israel, but they did not listen to Moses, because of their broken spirit and harsh slavery.

10So the LORD said to Moses,

11“Go in, tell Pharaoh king of Egypt to let the people of Israel go out of his land.”

12But Moses said to the LORD, “Behold, the people of Israel have not listened to me. How then shall Pharaoh listen to me, for I am of uncircumcised lips?”

13But the LORD spoke to Moses and Aaron and gave them a charge about the people of Israel and about Pharaoh king of Egypt: to bring the people of Israel out of the land of Egypt.

14These are the heads of their fathers’ houses: the sons of Reuben, the firstborn of Israel: Hanoch, Pallu, Hezron, and Carmi; these are the clans of Reuben.

15The sons of Simeon: Jemuel, Jamin, Ohad, Jachin, Zohar, and Shaul, the son of a Canaanite woman; these are the clans of Simeon.

16These are the names of the sons of Levi according to their generations: Gershon, Kohath, and Merari, the years of the life of Levi being 137 years.

17The sons of Gershon: Libni and Shimei, by their clans.

18The sons of Kohath: Amram, Izhar, Hebron, and Uzziel, the years of the life of Kohath being 133 years.

19The sons of Merari: Mahli and Mushi. These are the clans of the Levites according to their generations.

20Amram took as his wife Jochebed his father’s sister, and she bore him Aaron and Moses, the years of the life of Amram being 137 years.

21The sons of Izhar: Korah, Nepheg, and Zichri.

22The sons of Uzziel: Mishael, Elzaphan, and Sithri.

23Aaron took as his wife Elisheba, the daughter of Amminadab and the sister of Nahshon, and she bore him Nadab, Abihu, Eleazar, and Ithamar.

24The sons of Korah: Assir, Elkanah, and Abiasaph; these are the clans of the Korahites.

25Eleazar, Aaron’s son, took as his wife one of the daughters of Putiel, and she bore him Phinehas. These are the heads of the fathers’ houses of the Levites by their clans.

26These are the Aaron and Moses to whom the LORD said: “Bring out the people of Israel from the land of Egypt by their hosts.”

27It was they who spoke to Pharaoh king of Egypt about bringing out the people of Israel from Egypt, this Moses and this Aaron.

28On the day when the LORD spoke to Moses in the land of Egypt,

29the LORD said to Moses, “I am the LORD; tell Pharaoh king of Egypt all that I say to you.”

Summary

God reaffirms His covenant promises to Moses and commands him to return to Pharaoh; the genealogy of Moses and Aaron is recorded.

Authorship & Background

Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).
Historical Context: Chapter 6 is God's answer to Moses' complaint in 5:22-23. God responds not with rebuke but with revelation — a deeper unveiling of His name, His covenant, and His plan. The "I AM the LORD" declaration (v.2) is followed by seven "I will" promises (vv.6-8) — God's comprehensive plan of redemption. Yet Israel cannot hear it "for anguish of spirit, and for cruel bondage" (v.9) — suffering can deafen us to God's promises. The genealogy (vv.14-25) establishes Moses and Aaron's credentials within the tribe of Levi. The chapter ends where it began: Moses' objection about his "uncircumcised lips" (v.12, 30).
God's Self-Revelation (vv.1-8): God answers Moses' complaint: "Now shalt thou see what I will do" (v.1). He reveals Himself: "I am the LORD (YHWH)" (v.2). He appeared to the patriarchs as "God Almighty" (El Shaddai) but now reveals the fuller meaning of YHWH (v.3). He has heard Israel's groaning and remembered His covenant (v.5). Then the seven "I will" promises: 1. "I will bring you out" (v.6) 2. "I will rid you out of their bondage" (v.6) 3. "I will redeem you" (v.6) 4. "I will take you to me for a people" (v.7) 5. "I will be to you a God" (v.7) 6. "I will bring you in unto the land" (v.8) 7. "I will give it you for an heritage" (v.8)
Israel's Deafness (v.9): Moses delivers God's message, "but they hearkened not unto Moses for anguish of spirit, and for cruel bondage." Suffering had made them unable to receive hope.
Moses' Renewed Objection (vv.10-13): God commands Moses to go to Pharaoh again (v.11). Moses objects: "The children of Israel have not hearkened unto me; how then shall Pharaoh hear me, who am of uncircumcised lips?" (v.12).
The Genealogy (vv.14-27): A selective genealogy tracing Reuben, Simeon, and especially Levi — establishing Moses and Aaron's lineage. Key names: Amram and Jochebed (Moses' parents, v.20), Aaron's wife Elisheba (v.23), Aaron's sons including Nadab, Abihu, Eleazar, and Ithamar (v.23), and Phinehas (v.25). The genealogy authenticates Moses and Aaron as God's chosen instruments.
Renewed Commission (vv.28-30): The narrative resumes: God commands Moses to speak to Pharaoh (v.29). Moses repeats his objection: "I am of uncircumcised lips" (v.30) — setting up God's answer in chapter 7.

Map & Geography

  • Egypt: Moses and Aaron operate in Pharaoh's court and among the Israelites in Goshen.
  • The genealogy (vv.14-25) traces the family back to Israel's entry into Egypt, connecting this generation to the land of promise given to Abraham.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): enduringword.com Guzik emphasizes the seven "I will" promises as a complete picture of redemption, the progressive revelation of God's name, and the tragedy of Israel's inability to hear due to suffering.
  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "The Seven I Wills" on vv.6-8 (MTP Vol. 28, No. 1672) "Seven times God says 'I WILL' — and every 'I will' of God is as certain as if it were already done. I will bring you out — deliverance. I will rid you — freedom. I will redeem you — purchase. I will take you — relationship. I will be your God — identity. I will bring you in — inheritance. I will give it you — possession. From bondage to inheritance in seven divine declarations. And not one 'you must' among them. This is grace: God does it all. Our part? Believe it. Receive it. Rest in it."

Reflection

  • 1. The seven "I wills" (vv.6-8): God's redemption is comprehensive. He doesn't just free you FROM something — He brings you INTO relationship, identity, and inheritance. Which of these seven promises do you need to hear today?
  • 2. "They hearkened not... for anguish of spirit" (v.9). Suffering can make us deaf to God's promises. If you're in deep pain right now, you may not be able to "hear" hope. That's okay. God's promises remain true whether you can feel them or not.
  • 3. "I am the LORD" — repeated three times (vv.2, 6, 8). God's identity is the foundation of His promises. He can do what He says because He IS who He says. Do you know Him — not just His name but His character?
  • 4. Moses objects again (v.12) — even after God's magnificent promises. We can hear God's word and still feel inadequate. God doesn't rebuke Moses — He provides Aaron. God meets us in our weakness.
  • 5. The genealogy (vv.14-25) grounds the story in real history — real families, real people. God works through ordinary lineages. Your family history, however messy, is part of God's story.
