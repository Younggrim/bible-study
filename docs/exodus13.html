Exodus — Chapter 13

1The LORD said to Moses,

2“Consecrate to me all the firstborn. Whatever is the first to open the womb among the people of Israel, both of man and of beast, is mine.”

3Then Moses said to the people, “Remember this day in which you came out from Egypt, out of the house of slavery, for by a strong hand the LORD brought you out from this place. No leavened bread shall be eaten.

4Today, in the month of Abib, you are going out.

5And when the LORD brings you into the land of the Canaanites, the Hittites, the Amorites, the Hivites, and the Jebusites, which he swore to your fathers to give you, a land flowing with milk and honey, you shall keep this service in this month.

6Seven days you shall eat unleavened bread, and on the seventh day there shall be a feast to the LORD.

7Unleavened bread shall be eaten for seven days; no leavened bread shall be seen with you, and no leaven shall be seen with you in all your territory.

8You shall tell your son on that day, ‘It is because of what the LORD did for me when I came out of Egypt.’

9And it shall be to you as a sign on your hand and as a memorial between your eyes, that the law of the LORD may be in your mouth. For with a strong hand the LORD has brought you out of Egypt.

10You shall therefore keep this statute at its appointed time from year to year.

11“When the LORD brings you into the land of the Canaanites, as he swore to you and your fathers, and shall give it to you,

12you shall set apart to the LORD all that first opens the womb. All the firstborn of your animals that are males shall be the LORD’s.

13Every firstborn of a donkey you shall redeem with a lamb, or if you will not redeem it you shall break its neck. Every firstborn of man among your sons you shall redeem.

14And when in time to come your son asks you, ‘What does this mean?’ you shall say to him, ‘By a strong hand the LORD brought us out of Egypt, from the house of slavery.

15For when Pharaoh stubbornly refused to let us go, the LORD killed all the firstborn in the land of Egypt, both the firstborn of man and the firstborn of animals. Therefore I sacrifice to the LORD all the males that first open the womb, but all the firstborn of my sons I redeem.’

16It shall be as a mark on your hand or frontlets between your eyes, for by a strong hand the LORD brought us out of Egypt.”

17When Pharaoh let the people go, God did not lead them by way of the land of the Philistines, although that was near. For God said, “Lest the people change their minds when they see war and return to Egypt.”

18But God led the people around by the way of the wilderness toward the Red Sea. And the people of Israel went up out of the land of Egypt equipped for battle.

19Moses took the bones of Joseph with him, for Joseph had made the sons of Israel solemnly swear, saying, “God will surely visit you, and you shall carry up my bones with you from here.”

20And they moved on from Succoth and encamped at Etham, on the edge of the wilderness.

21And the LORD went before them by day in a pillar of cloud to lead them along the way, and by night in a pillar of fire to give them light, that they might travel by day and by night.

22The pillar of cloud by day and the pillar of fire by night did not depart from before the people.

Summary

God consecrates Israel's firstborn, commands the Feast of Unleavened Bread, and leads His people out by a pillar of cloud and fire.

Key Verses

  • v.2 — "Consecrate to me all the firstborn... it is mine." (ESV) — What God redeems, God owns.
  • v.9 — "It shall be to you as a sign on your hand and as a memorial between your eyes." (ESV) — Constant remembrance of redemption.
  • v.14 — "By a strong hand the LORD brought us out of Egypt." (ESV) — The testimony to pass to children.
  • v.17 — "God did not lead them by way of the land of the Philistines, although that was near." (ESV) — God's longer route is the safer route.
  • v.21 — "The LORD went before them by day in a pillar of cloud... and by night in a pillar of fire." (ESV) — God's visible, constant guidance.

Authorship & Background

  • Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).
  • Historical Context: Chapter 13 covers the immediate aftermath of the exodus: the consecration of the firstborn, instructions for remembrance, and the beginning of the journey. Three major themes: (1) The firstborn belong to God — because He spared Israel's firstborn, all firstborn are His (vv.1-16); (2) God leads by a longer route to protect His people from premature war (vv.17-18); (3) God's visible presence — the pillar of cloud by day and fire by night (vv.21-22). Moses also fulfills Joseph's 400-year-old request by carrying his bones out of Egypt (v.19). The chapter shows that redemption is not just an event but a way of life — remembering, consecrating, following.
  • Consecration of the Firstborn (vv.1-2): "Sanctify unto me all the firstborn" (v.2). Because God spared Israel's firstborn in the tenth plague, every firstborn — human and animal — belongs to Him. This is the principle of redemption: what God saves, God owns.
  • Instructions for Remembrance (vv.3-16): "Remember this day" (v.3). Keep the Feast of Unleavened Bread when you enter the land (vv.5-7). "Thou shalt shew thy son" — teach the next generation (v.8). It shall be "a sign upon thine hand, and for a memorial between thine eyes" (v.9) — later interpreted as phylacteries/tefillin. Firstborn animals are sacrificed; firstborn sons are redeemed (vv.12-13, 15). When your son asks "What is this?" — tell the story (v.14).
  • God's Route (vv.17-18): God does NOT lead them by the shortest route (the coastal road through Philistine territory) — "lest peradventure the people repent when they see war, and they return to Egypt" (v.17). Instead, the wilderness route toward the Red Sea (v.18). God knows their capacity and leads accordingly.
  • Joseph's Bones (v.19): Moses carries Joseph's bones — fulfilling the oath of Genesis 50:25. Joseph's faith, expressed 400 years earlier, is honored.
  • The Pillar of Cloud and Fire (vv.20-22): "The LORD went before them by day in a pillar of a cloud... and by night in a pillar of fire" (v.21). God's visible, constant, guiding presence. "He took not away the pillar" (v.22) — God never leaves.

Map & Geography

  • Succoth to Etham (v.20): Israel's route avoids "the way of the land of the Philistines" (the coastal road) and goes south toward the wilderness.
  • Etham: "In the edge of the wilderness" — the transition from Egyptian territory to the Sinai wilderness.
  • The pillar of cloud and fire (vv.21-22): God visibly guides the route through unfamiliar terrain.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): enduringword.com Guzik covers the consecration of the firstborn as a principle of redemption, God's wisdom in choosing the longer route, and the pillar of cloud/fire as a picture of God's constant guidance.
  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "The Pillar of Cloud and Fire" on v.21 (MTP Vol. 11, No. 620) "The LORD went BEFORE them — not behind, pushing them; not beside, merely accompanying them; but BEFORE them, leading them. And He went in a form they could SEE — cloud by day, fire by night. God adapts His guidance to our need: shade in the heat, light in the darkness. And He never removed it — 'He took not away the pillar.' For forty years, through every rebellion, every failure, every complaint — the pillar remained. God's faithfulness does not depend on our faithfulness. He leads even when we wander."

Reflection

  • 1. "Sanctify unto me all the firstborn... it is mine" (v.2). What God redeems, God owns. You were bought with a price — your life belongs to Him. Are you living as His, or still living as your own?
  • 2. "God led them not through the way... although that was near" (v.17). God sometimes takes you the long way to protect you from battles you're not ready for. Trust His route, even when it seems inefficient.
  • 3. "The LORD went before them" (v.21). God leads — you follow. He doesn't push from behind or merely walk beside. He goes AHEAD. Are you following His lead, or running ahead on your own path?
  • 4. "He took not away the pillar" (v.22). God's presence is constant — even when you fail, even when you complain, even when you doubt. He never leaves. Rest in that faithfulness.
  • 5. "Thou shalt shew thy son" (v.8). Faith must be transmitted to the next generation through intentional storytelling. Are you telling your children what God has done? They need to hear YOUR story of redemption.
