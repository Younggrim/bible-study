What the Bible Says About Loneliness

"Be strong and courageous. Do not fear or be in dread of them, for it is the LORD your God who goes with you. He will not leave you or forsake you."

— Deuteronomy 31:6 (ESV)

Key Scriptures

Psalm 68:6

"God settles the solitary in a home; he leads out the prisoners to prosperity."

Matthew 28:20

"And behold, I am with you always, to the end of the age."

Psalm 139:7-10

"Where shall I go from your Spirit? Or where shall I flee from your presence? If I ascend to heaven, you are there!"

Hebrews 13:5

"For he has said, I will never leave you nor forsake you."

Isaiah 43:2

"When you pass through the waters, I will be with you; and through the rivers, they shall not overwhelm you."

Psalm 25:16

"Turn to me and be gracious to me, for I am lonely and afflicted."

What Scripture Teaches

Loneliness is not the same as being alone. You can be surrounded by people and still feel isolated.

God Himself said it is not good for man to be alone (Genesis 2:18). Your desire for connection is God-given.

The church exists to be the family of God. Step toward community even when it feels uncomfortable.

Jesus experienced ultimate loneliness on the cross so that you would never be truly alone.
