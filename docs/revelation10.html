Revelation — Chapter 10

1Then I saw another mighty angel coming down from heaven, wrapped in a cloud, with a rainbow over his head, and his face was like the sun, and his legs like pillars of fire.

2He had a little scroll open in his hand. And he set his right foot on the sea, and his left foot on the land,

3and called out with a loud voice, like a lion roaring. When he called out, the seven thunders sounded.

4And when the seven thunders had sounded, I was about to write, but I heard a voice from heaven saying, “Seal up what the seven thunders have said, and do not write it down.”

5And the angel whom I saw standing on the sea and on the land raised his right hand to heaven

6and swore by him who lives forever and ever, who created heaven and what is in it, the earth and what is in it, and the sea and what is in it, that there would be no more delay,

7but that in the days of the trumpet call to be sounded by the seventh angel, the mystery of God would be fulfilled, just as he announced to his servants the prophets.

8Then the voice that I had heard from heaven spoke to me again, saying, “Go, take the scroll that is open in the hand of the angel who is standing on the sea and on the land.”

9So I went to the angel and told him to give me the little scroll. And he said to me, “Take and eat it; it will make your stomach bitter, but in your mouth it will be sweet as honey.”

10And I took the little scroll from the hand of the angel and ate it. It was sweet as honey in my mouth, but when I had eaten it my stomach was made bitter.

11And I was told, “You must again prophesy about many peoples and nations and languages and kings.”

Summary

A mighty angel with a little scroll announces that when the seventh trumpet sounds, the mystery of God will be finished — John eats the scroll which is sweet in his mouth but bitter in his stomach, and is told to prophesy again.

Key Verses

v.6
"There would be no more delay." (ESV) — No more delay — God's plan will be completed.
v.7
"The mystery of God would be fulfilled, just as he announced to his servants the prophets." (ESV) — God's plan reaches completion.
v.9
"It will make your stomach bitter, but in your mouth it will be sweet as honey." (ESV) — God's word: sweet truth, bitter reality.
v.11
"You must again prophesy about many peoples and nations and languages and kings." (ESV) — John's continued prophetic mission.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/revelation-10/ Guzik covers the mighty angel's identity, the sealed thunders, the oath of no more delay, and the sweet/bitter scroll.
  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "The Sweet and the Bitter" on vv.9-10 (MTP Vol. 25, No. 1502) "Sweet in the mouth — God's word is delightful to receive. Truth is sweet. Promise is sweet. The gospel is sweet. But bitter in the belly — when you digest it, when you live it out, when you proclaim judgment, when you see its implications for a lost world — it becomes bitter. The prophet who truly digests God's word will know both sweetness and sorrow."

Translations

  • v.1 — KJV: "his feet as pillars of fire" / ESV: "his legs like pillars of fire" — Greek "podes" can mean feet or legs; ESV's "legs" better fits "pillars." The angel's description echoes Christ (1:15-16) but is likely a powerful angel, not Christ Himself (he swears by God in v.6).
  • v.4 — KJV: "seal up" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "sphragison" — seal, close up. Some revelation is withheld. Not everything is revealed — God has secrets.
  • v.6 — KJV: "that there should be time no longer" / ESV: "that there would be no more delay" — Greek "chronos ouketi estai" — ESV's "no more delay" is the better translation. Not that time ceases to exist but that God's delay in completing His plan is over. KJV's "time no longer" can be misread as the end of time itself.
  • v.7 — KJV: "mystery of God" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "mustērion tou theou" — God's hidden plan now brought to completion. The full outworking of His redemptive purpose.
  • v.9 — KJV: "eat it up" / ESV: "take and eat it" — Greek "kataphage" — devour it, consume it completely. Both equivalent in sense. Like Ezekiel (3:1-3), John must internalize God's message before proclaiming it.
  • ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB) v.1 — NET: "I saw another powerful angel descending from heaven, wrapped in a cloud, with a rainbow above his head" — "powerful angel" (angelon ischyron = a strong angel). WEB: "a mighty angel coming down out of the sky." Not Christ (angels are sent; Christ sends) but a glorious messenger.
  • v.6 — NET: "there will be no more delay!" — "no more delay" (chronos ouketi estai = time will be no more/no more time). WEB: "there will no longer be delay." KJV: "there should be time no longer." Debated: no more delay before judgment? or time itself ceasing? Context favors: no further postponement.
  • v.7 — NET: "the mystery of God is completed, just as he announced to his servants the prophets" — "mystery" (mystErion = hidden plan now revealed). WEB: "the mystery of God is finished." KJV: "the mystery of God should be finished." God's secret plan reaches its culmination.
  • v.9 — NET: "Take the scroll and eat it. It will be bitter in your stomach, but sweet as honey in your mouth" — "eat it" (kataphage = devour/consume completely). WEB: "Take it, and eat it up." Echoing Ezekiel 3:1-3 — internalizing God's word. Sweet truth contains bitter content (judgment).
  • v.11 — NET: "You must prophesy again about many peoples, nations, languages, and kings" — "must" (dei = divine necessity). WEB: "You must prophesy again." John's commission is not finished — more revelation follows. The universal scope continues.

Reflection

  • 1. "There should be time no longer" (v.6). God's patience has a limit. Are you living with urgency, knowing that delay will end and God's plan will be completed?
  • 2. "Seal up those things" (v.4). God has secrets — things He chooses not to reveal. Can you accept that some questions won't be answered this side of eternity? Can you trust what He hasn't revealed?
  • 3. "Sweet as honey... belly was bitter" (vv.9-10). God's word is both sweet and bitter. Do you experience both? The sweetness of His promises AND the bitterness of His judgments? A balanced diet of Scripture includes both.
  • 4. "Thou must prophesy again" (v.11). After receiving God's word, you must share it. Are you keeping truth to yourself or proclaiming it to others?
