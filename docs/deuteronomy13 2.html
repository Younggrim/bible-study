Deuteronomy — Chapter 13

1“If a prophet or a dreamer of dreams arises among you and gives you a sign or a wonder,

2and the sign or wonder that he tells you comes to pass, and if he says, ‘Let us go after other gods,’ which you have not known, ‘and let us serve them,’

3you shall not listen to the words of that prophet or that dreamer of dreams. For the LORD your God is testing you, to know whether you love the LORD your God with all your heart and with all your soul.

4You shall walk after the LORD your God and fear him and keep his commandments and obey his voice, and you shall serve him and hold fast to him.

5But that prophet or that dreamer of dreams shall be put to death, because he has taught rebellion against the LORD your God, who brought you out of the land of Egypt and redeemed you out of the house of slavery, to make you leave the way in which the LORD your God commanded you to walk. So you shall purge the evil from your midst.

6“If your brother, the son of your mother, or your son or your daughter or the wife you embrace or your friend who is as your own soul entices you secretly, saying, ‘Let us go and serve other gods,’ which neither you nor your fathers have known,

7some of the gods of the peoples who are around you, whether near you or far off from you, from the one end of the earth to the other,

8you shall not yield to him or listen to him, nor shall your eye pity him, nor shall you spare him, nor shall you conceal him.

9But you shall kill him. Your hand shall be first against him to put him to death, and afterward the hand of all the people.

10You shall stone him to death with stones, because he sought to draw you away from the LORD your God, who brought you out of the land of Egypt, out of the house of slavery.

11And all Israel shall hear and fear and never again do any such wickedness as this among you.

12“If you hear in one of your cities, which the LORD your God is giving you to dwell there,

13that certain worthless fellows have gone out among you and have drawn away the inhabitants of their city, saying, ‘Let us go and serve other gods,’ which you have not known,

14then you shall inquire and make search and ask diligently. And behold, if it be true and certain that such an abomination has been done among you,

15you shall surely put the inhabitants of that city to the sword, devoting it to destruction, all who are in it and its cattle, with the edge of the sword.

16You shall gather all its spoil into the midst of its open square and burn the city and all its spoil with fire, as a whole burnt offering to the LORD your God. It shall be a heap forever. It shall not be built again.

17None of the devoted things shall stick to your hand, that the LORD may turn from the fierceness of his anger and show you mercy and have compassion on you and multiply you, as he swore to your fathers,

18if you obey the voice of the LORD your God, keeping all his commandments that I am commanding you today, and doing what is right in the sight of the LORD your God.

Summary

God warns against false prophets, family members, or cities that entice Israel to worship other gods — such seduction must be dealt with severely.

Key Verses

v.3
"You shall not listen to the words of that prophet, or that dreamer of dreams: for the LORD your God proveth you, to know whether you love the LORD your God with all your heart and with all your soul." (ESV) — Signs do not validate a message. Faithfulness to God is the test. God permits false prophets to test loyalty.
v.4
"You shall walk after the LORD your God, and fear him, and keep his commandments, and obey his voice, and you shall serve him, and hold fast to him." (ESV) — Six verbs of total devotion: walk, fear, keep, obey, serve, cleave. The complete response to God.
v.5
"And that prophet, or that dreamer of dreams, shall be put to death; because he has spoken to turn you away from the LORD your God." (ESV) — The crime is not failed prediction but leading people away from God. Death penalty for spiritual seduction.
v.8
"You shall not consent to him, nor listen to him; neither shall your eye pity him, neither shall you spare, neither shall you conceal him." (ESV) — Five negatives. No compromise with idolatry, even from those closest to you.
v.11
"And all Israel shall hear, and fear, and shall do no more any such wickedness as this is among you." (ESV) — Deterrence through severity. Public justice prevents private sin.
v.14
"Then shall you enquire, and make search, and ask diligently." (ESV) — Due process required even in cases of idolatry. Justice demands investigation before execution.
v.17
"And there shall cleave nought of the cursed thing to your hand: that the LORD may turn from the fierceness of his anger." (ESV) — Tolerated idolatry brings God's wrath on the whole nation. Complete removal brings mercy.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/deuteronomy-13/ Guzik emphasizes that the test of a prophet is not his power but his message. A miracle-worker who leads away from God is a false prophet regardless of his signs. He notes that God actively uses false prophets as a test of His people's loyalty — this is not God tempting to evil (James 1:13) but God testing to reveal what is in the heart.
  • Charles Spurgeon: "The test of a prophet is not 'Does he work wonders?' but 'Does he lead to God?' Satan can produce signs. Demons can work miracles. The question is never the power of the messenger but the direction of the message. Does it lead you closer to the God of Scripture, or away from Him? If away — though the sign be never so great — reject it utterly."

Translations

  • v.3 — KJV: "the LORD your God proveth you" / ESV: "the LORD your God is testing you" — Hebrew "m'naseh." Same root as Genesis 22:1 ("God did tempt/test Abraham"). God tests to reveal and refine faith, not to cause failure.
  • v.4 — KJV: "cleave unto him" / ESV: "hold fast to him" — Hebrew "tidbaqun." Same word as Genesis 2:24 ("shall cleave unto his wife"). Intimate, permanent attachment. The covenant relationship with God is as binding as marriage.
  • v.5 — KJV: "spoken to turn you away" / ESV: "taught rebellion against" — Hebrew "dibber sarah." "Sarah" = turning aside, rebellion, apostasy. ESV interprets the intent; KJV translates the action. Both capture the gravity: leading away from God is rebellion.
  • v.5 — KJV: "put the evil away from the midst of thee" / ESV: "purge the evil from your midst" — Hebrew "u-bi'arta ha-ra mi-qirbekha." This phrase recurs throughout Deuteronomy (17:7,12; 19:19; 21:21; 22:21, 24; 24:7). "Bi'er" = to burn out, purge, remove completely.
  • v.13 — KJV: "children of Belial" / ESV: "worthless fellows" — Hebrew "b'nei v'liya'al." "Belial" = worthlessness, wickedness. KJV retains the Hebrew term; ESV translates the meaning. Later Jewish and Christian literature uses "Belial" as a name for Satan (2 Corinthians 6:15).
  • v.15 — KJV: "destroying it utterly" / ESV: "devoting it to destruction" — Hebrew "hachareim tacharim." The "cherem" — total devotion to God through destruction. Nothing is kept for human use. Same treatment as Canaanite cities (Deuteronomy 7:2).
  • v.16 — KJV: "burn with fire the city, and all the spoil thereof every whit, for the LORD thy God" / ESV: "burn the city and all its spoil with fire, as a whole burnt offering to the LORD your God" — ESV adds the interpretive phrase "as a whole burnt offering" (Hebrew "kalil" = whole/complete). The entire city becomes a sacrifice to God — an offering of judgment.
  • v.17 — KJV: "the cursed thing" / ESV: "the devoted things" — Hebrew "ha-cherem." Same word translated "accursed" in Joshua 7:1 (Achan). KJV emphasizes the curse aspect; ESV emphasizes the devotion/ consecration aspect. Both are present: what is devoted to God for destruction is cursed if taken by man.

Reflection

  • 1. Signs do not validate a message (vv.1-3). A prophet's power proves nothing about his truthfulness. The test is always: does the message align with God's revealed word? In an age of spectacular ministries, this principle is essential. Miracles without sound doctrine are demonic deception, not divine confirmation.
  • 2. God tests loyalty through permitted deception (v.3). God allows false prophets to arise as a test. This is sobering: God is actively interested in whether we love Him supremely. He does not shield us from every deception — He expects us to discern by His word.
  • 3. No relationship supersedes loyalty to God (vv.6-11). Brother, child, spouse, closest friend — if they lead away from God, covenant loyalty demands rejection. Jesus echoes this: "He that loveth father or mother more than me is not worthy of me" (Matthew 10:37). God demands first place — absolutely.
  • 4. Due process even in urgent cases (v.14). Before destroying an idolatrous city: "enquire, and make search, and ask diligently." God demands justice even when the crime is severe. Zeal without investigation is not righteousness but mob violence.
  • 5. Tolerated sin brings corporate judgment (v.17). Achan's sin brought defeat on all Israel (Joshua 7). An idolatrous city left standing brings God's anger on the whole nation. Sin tolerated in the community is sin shared by the community.
  • 6. The six verbs of devotion (v.4): walk, fear, keep, obey, serve, cleave. This is the antidote to deception — active, comprehensive devotion to God. Those who are passively religious are easily led astray. Those who actively pursue God recognize counterfeits.
