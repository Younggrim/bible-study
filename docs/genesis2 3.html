Genesis — Chapter 2

1Thus the heavens and the earth were finished, and all the host of them.

2And on the seventh day God finished his work that he had done, and he rested on the seventh day from all his work that he had done.

3So God blessed the seventh day and made it holy, because on it God rested from all his work that he had done in creation.

4These are the generations of the heavens and the earth when they were created, in the day that the LORD God made the earth and the heavens.

5When no bush of the field was yet in the land and no small plant of the field had yet sprung up—for the LORD God had not caused it to rain on the land, and there was no man to work the ground,

6and a mist was going up from the land and was watering the whole face of the ground—

7then the LORD God formed the man of dust from the ground and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life, and the man became a living creature.

8And the LORD God planted a garden in Eden, in the east, and there he put the man whom he had formed.

9And out of the ground the LORD God made to spring up every tree that is pleasant to the sight and good for food. The tree of life was in the midst of the garden, and the tree of the knowledge of good and evil.

10A river flowed out of Eden to water the garden, and there it divided and became four rivers.

11The name of the first is the Pishon. It is the one that flowed around the whole land of Havilah, where there is gold.

12And the gold of that land is good; bdellium and onyx stone are there.

13The name of the second river is the Gihon. It is the one that flowed around the whole land of Cush.

14And the name of the third river is the Tigris, which flows east of Assyria. And the fourth river is the Euphrates.

15The LORD God took the man and put him in the garden of Eden to work it and keep it.

16And the LORD God commanded the man, saying, “You may surely eat of every tree of the garden,

17but of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil you shall not eat, for in the day that you eat of it you shall surely die.”

18Then the LORD God said, “It is not good that the man should be alone; I will make him a helper fit for him.”

19Now out of the ground the LORD God had formed every beast of the field and every bird of the heavens and brought them to the man to see what he would call them. And whatever the man called every living creature, that was its name.

20The man gave names to all livestock and to the birds of the heavens and to every beast of the field. But for Adam there was not found a helper fit for him.

21So the LORD God caused a deep sleep to fall upon the man, and while he slept took one of his ribs and closed up its place with flesh.

22And the rib that the LORD God had taken from the man he made into a woman and brought her to the man.

23Then the man said, “This at last is bone of my bones and flesh of my flesh; she shall be called Woman, because she was taken out of Man.”

24Therefore a man shall leave his father and his mother and hold fast to his wife, and they shall become one flesh.

25And the man and his wife were both naked and were not ashamed.

Summary

God forms man from dust, breathes life into him, plants Eden, and creates woman from man's side as his perfect complement.

Key Verses

v.2
"And on the seventh day God finished his work that he had done, and he rested." (ESV) — God's rest of completion.
v.7
"Then the LORD God formed the man of dust from the ground and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life, and the man became a living creature." (ESV) — Humanity: dust + divine breath.
v.17
"But of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil you shall not eat, for in the day that you eat of it you shall surely die." (ESV) — The one prohibition.
v.18
"Then the LORD God said, 'It is not good that the man should be alone; I will make him a helper fit for him.'" (ESV) — The need for companionship.
v.24
"Therefore a man shall leave his father and his mother and hold fast to his wife, and they shall become one flesh." (ESV) — The institution of marriage.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/genesis-2/ Guzik covers the Sabbath rest, man's formation, Eden, the prohibition, and the creation of woman and marriage.
  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "The First Man Adam" on v.7 (MTP Vol. 15, No. 888) "Formed of the dust — how humble our origin! Breathed into by God — how exalted our dignity! We are dust animated by divinity. Earthly in substance but heavenly in life. This is man's paradox: made from the lowest material but given the highest gift — the breath of God Himself." Sermon: "The Marriage Bond" on v.24 (MTP Vol. 22, No. 1316) "Leave — separation from the old. Cleave — attachment to the new. One flesh — union that cannot be divided. God designed marriage before sin entered, before the fall corrupted. It is not a human invention but a divine institution — established in paradise by God Himself."

Translations

  • v.2 — KJV: "ended his work" / ESV: "finished his work" — Both equivalent. Hebrew "wayekal" — completed, finished. KJV's "ended" and ESV's "finished" convey the same meaning. KJV: "rested" / ESV: "rested" — Both identical. Hebrew "shabath" — ceased, stopped, desisted. Root of "Sabbath." Not exhaustion but completion. God stopped because the work was finished.
  • v.3 — KJV: "sanctified it" / ESV: "made it holy" — Hebrew "wayeqaddesh" — set apart, made holy. ESV renders the Hebrew verb more transparently for modern readers; KJV uses the Latinate "sanctified." Same meaning.
  • v.4 — KJV: "LORD God" / ESV: "LORD God" — Both identical. Hebrew "Yahweh Elohim." First use of God's covenant name (Yahweh/LORD) combined with His power name (Elohim/God). Personal + powerful. Relational + sovereign.
  • v.5 — KJV: "every plant of the field before it was in the earth, and every herb of the field before it grew" / ESV: "When no bush of the field was yet in the land and no small plant of the field had yet sprung up" — Hebrew "siach" (bush/shrub) and "eseb" (plant/herb). ESV restructures the sentence for clarity and uses "bush" and "small plant" for more precise rendering.
  • v.6 — KJV: "a mist" / ESV: "a mist" — Both identical. Hebrew "ed" — mist, vapor, or spring. The exact meaning is debated; both translations use "mist."
  • v.7 — KJV: "formed man of the dust of the ground" / ESV: "formed the man of dust from the ground" — Both equivalent. Hebrew "yatsar" — to shape, mold, fashion (like a potter with clay). Intimate, hands-on creation. KJV: "a living soul" / ESV: "a living creature" — Hebrew "nephesh chayyah" — a living being, a breathing creature. ESV's "creature" avoids the theological baggage modern readers attach to "soul" (the same Hebrew phrase is used of animals in 1:24). KJV's "soul" reflects older English usage meaning "living being."
  • v.13 — KJV: "Ethiopia" / ESV: "Cush" — Hebrew "Kush." ESV retains the Hebrew name; KJV uses the Greek/English equivalent. Cush refers to the region south of Egypt (modern Sudan/Ethiopia area).
  • v.14 — KJV: "Hiddekel" / ESV: "Tigris" — Hebrew "Chiddeqel." ESV uses the modern name (Tigris) for clarity; KJV transliterates the Hebrew.
  • v.15 — KJV: "to dress it and to keep it" / ESV: "to work it and keep it" — Hebrew "le'abdah uleshomrah" — to serve/work it and to guard/keep it. ESV's "work" is more natural modern English than KJV's "dress" (which meant "tend" in 1611).
  • v.17 — KJV: "thou shalt surely die" / ESV: "you shall surely die" — Both equivalent (difference is only thou/you). Hebrew "moth tamuth" — dying you shall die. An emphatic construction: death is certain. Spiritual death immediately; physical death eventually.
  • v.18 — KJV: "an help meet for him" / ESV: "a helper fit for him" — Hebrew "ezer kenegdo" — a helper corresponding to him, a counterpart suitable for him. KJV's "help meet" is two words (help + meet/ suitable); ESV's "helper fit" captures the same idea more clearly. "Ezer" is used of God Himself (Psalm 33:20, 70:5) — not a subordinate term but a strong one.
  • v.19 — KJV: "every fowl of the air" / ESV: "every bird of the heavens" — Hebrew "oph hashamayim." ESV uses "bird" (modern) vs. KJV's "fowl" (archaic), and "heavens" (literal Hebrew) vs. "air."
  • v.21 — KJV: "rib" / ESV: "rib" — Both identical. Hebrew "tsela" — side, rib, or side-chamber. Woman taken from man's side — not his head (to rule over him) or his feet (to be trampled) but his side (to be equal, near his heart).
  • v.23 — KJV: "This is now bone of my bones" / ESV: "This at last is bone of my bones" — Hebrew "zoth happa'am" — this time, now, at last. ESV's "at last" captures the sense of joyful culmination after naming all the animals and finding no match.
  • v.24 — KJV: "cleave unto his wife" / ESV: "hold fast to his wife" — Hebrew "dabaq" — to cling, stick to, be joined permanently. The strongest word for attachment. ESV's "hold fast" is more accessible to modern readers than KJV's "cleave" (which now commonly means "to split").
  • TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB) - WEB uses "Yahweh" throughout for the divine name (KJV/ESV: "the LORD"). This reflects the actual pronunciation of the Hebrew Tetragrammaton YHWH. ASV uses "Jehovah" — an older English hybrid form of the same name. - v.6 — NET: "Springs would well up from the earth" — renders Hebrew "ed" as "springs" rather than "mist" (KJV/ESV/ASV/WEB), reflecting a scholarly view that the term refers to subterranean water sources, not atmospheric vapor. - v.8 — NET: "planted an orchard" — uniquely renders Hebrew "gan" as "orchard" rather than "garden" (KJV/ESV/ASV/WEB), emphasizing the food-producing trees. - v.18 — NET: "a companion for him who corresponds to him" — interprets "ezer kenegdo" with relational language ("companion") vs. functional ("helper"). WEB: "a helper comparable to him" — emphasizes equality. - v.21 — NET: "took part of the man's side" — avoids committing to "rib," reflecting the broader meaning of Hebrew "tsela" (side/side-chamber). - v.24 — NET: "they become one family" — a distinctive rendering where all others have "one flesh," interpreting the unity as household/kinship.

Reflection

  • 1. "God rested on the seventh day" (v.2). God modeled rest. Do you rest? Or do you work endlessly, as if everything depends on you? Rest is not laziness — it's trust.
  • 2. "Formed man of the dust... breathed into his nostrils the breath of life" (v.7). You are both humble (dust) and dignified (God's breath). Do you live in both realities — humble before God, yet confident in your worth?
  • 3. "Of every tree... thou mayest freely eat: but..." (vv.16-17). God gave enormous freedom with one boundary. How do you respond to God's boundaries — with gratitude for the freedom or resentment at the restriction?
  • 4. "It is not good that the man should be alone" (v.18). God designed us for relationship. Are you investing in deep, meaningful relationships? Or trying to live in isolation?
  • 5. "Leave... cleave... one flesh" (v.24). If you're married, are you fully "leaving" (old attachments) and "cleaving" (new commitment)? Is your marriage reflecting God's design?
