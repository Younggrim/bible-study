Psalms — Chapter 64

1Hear my voice, O God, in my complaint; preserve my life from dread of the enemy.

2Hide me from the secret plots of the wicked, from the throng of evildoers,

3who whet their tongues like swords, who aim bitter words like arrows,

4shooting from ambush at the blameless, shooting at him suddenly and without fear.

5They hold fast to their evil purpose; they talk of laying snares secretly, thinking, “Who can see them?”

6They search out injustice, saying, “We have accomplished a diligent search.” For the inward mind and heart of a man are deep.

7But God shoots his arrow at them; they are wounded suddenly.

8They are brought to ruin, with their own tongues turned against them; all who see them will wag their heads.

9Then all mankind fears; they tell what God has brought about and ponder what he has done.

10Let the righteous one rejoice in the LORD and take refuge in him! Let all the upright in heart exult!

Summary

A lament — David prays for protection from the secret plots of the wicked whose tongues are sharp swords; God will suddenly shoot His arrow at them, and all mankind will fear and declare His work.

Key Verses

v.3
"Who whet their tongue like a sword, and bend their bows to shoot their arrows, even bitter words." (KJV) — Words ARE weapons. The tongue is deliberately sharpened (whet) for maximum damage. Bitter words are arrows aimed from concealment.
v.5
"They encourage themselves in an evil matter: they commune of laying snares privily; they say, Who shall see them?" (KJV) — The psychology of conspiracy: mutual reinforcement in evil ("encourage themselves"), secret planning ("laying snares privily"), and assumed invisibility ("who shall see them?"). They forget God sees everything.
v.7
"But God shall shoot at them with an arrow; suddenly shall they be wounded." (KJV) — The divine reversal. They shot arrows (v.3); now God shoots back. They attacked "suddenly" (v.4); now God strikes "suddenly." Perfect poetic justice — the punishment mirrors the crime.
v.9
"And all men shall fear, and shall declare the work of God; for they shall wisely consider of his doing." (KJV) — The purpose of divine judgment: universal recognition. When God acts, people stop, observe, and acknowledge His hand. His justice is visible and instructive.

Cross-References

  • v.3 — Psalm 57:4 — "Whose teeth are spears and arrows, and their tongue a sharp sword." Same weaponized tongue imagery. v.5 — Psalm 10:11 — "He hath said in his heart, God hath forgotten: he hideth his face." Same assumed divine blindness. v.7 — Psalm 7:12-13 — "He hath bent his bow, and made it ready. He hath also prepared for him the instruments of death." God's weapons against the wicked. v.8 — Psalm 9:15 — "The heathen are sunk down in the pit that they made: in the net which they hid is their own foot taken." Same boomerang justice. v.9 — Psalm 58:11 — "Verily there is a God that judgeth in the earth." Same universal recognition of divine justice. v.10 — Psalm 32:11 — "Be glad in the LORD, and rejoice, ye righteous: and shout for joy, all ye that are upright in heart."

Translations

  • v.1 — KJV: "preserve my life from fear of the enemy" / ESV: "preserve my life from dread of the enemy" — Both translate "pachad" (fear/dread). The prayer is not just for physical preservation but for freedom from the paralyzing fear that enemies create.
  • v.6 — KJV: "They search out iniquities; they accomplish a diligent search" / ESV: "They search out injustice, saying, 'We have accomplished a diligent search'" — The wicked are thorough in their evil planning. They "research" their wickedness with the same diligence a scholar would apply to study. But v.6b adds: "the inward thought of every one of them, and the heart, is deep" — human wickedness is deeper than even their own investigations reveal.
  • v.8 — KJV: "So they shall make their own tongue to fall upon themselves" / ESV: "with their own tongues turned against them" — The tongue that was their weapon becomes their downfall. Their own words condemn them.

Reflection

  • 1. Words aimed in secret are still seen by God (v.5). "Who shall see them?" — God does. Every whispered slander, every secret plot, every hidden cruelty is fully visible to the One who sees all. Privacy from God is an illusion.
  • 2. God's justice often mirrors the crime (v.7). Arrows shot at the righteous become God's arrow at the wicked. What you launch against others often returns to you. This is not karma — it is divine justice with poetic precision.
  • 3. God's timing is sudden (v.7). The wicked assume they have endless time. Then suddenly — God acts. Don't mistake God's patience for God's absence. His intervention comes swiftly when it comes.
  • 4. The purpose of judgment is declaration (v.9). God judges not merely to punish but to teach. His acts of justice declare His character to all who observe. When God acts, wisdom responds by "wisely considering" what He has done.
