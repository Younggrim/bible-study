Psalms — Chapter 102

1Hear my prayer, O LORD; let my cry come to you!

2Do not hide your face from me in the day of my distress! Incline your ear to me; answer me speedily in the day when I call!

3For my days pass away like smoke, and my bones burn like a furnace.

4My heart is struck down like grass and has withered; I forget to eat my bread.

5Because of my loud groaning my bones cling to my flesh.

6I am like a desert owl of the wilderness, like an owl of the waste places;

7I lie awake; I am like a lonely sparrow on the housetop.

8All the day my enemies taunt me; those who deride me use my name for a curse.

9For I eat ashes like bread and mingle tears with my drink,

10because of your indignation and anger; for you have taken me up and thrown me down.

11My days are like an evening shadow; I wither away like grass.

12But you, O LORD, are enthroned forever; you are remembered throughout all generations.

13You will arise and have pity on Zion; it is the time to favor her; the appointed time has come.

14For your servants hold her stones dear and have pity on her dust.

15Nations will fear the name of the LORD, and all the kings of the earth will fear your glory.

16For the LORD builds up Zion; he appears in his glory;

17he regards the prayer of the destitute and does not despise their prayer.

18Let this be recorded for a generation to come, so that a people yet to be created may praise the LORD:

19that he looked down from his holy height; from heaven the LORD looked at the earth,

20to hear the groans of the prisoners, to set free those who were doomed to die,

21that they may declare in Zion the name of the LORD, and in Jerusalem his praise,

22when peoples gather together, and kingdoms, to worship the LORD.

23He has broken my strength in midcourse; he has shortened my days.

24“O my God,” I say, “take me not away in the midst of my days— you whose years endure throughout all generations!”

25Of old you laid the foundation of the earth, and the heavens are the work of your hands.

26They will perish, but you will remain; they will all wear out like a garment. You will change them like a robe, and they will pass away,

27but you are the same, and your years have no end.

28The children of your servants shall dwell secure; their offspring shall be established before you.

Summary

A penitential lament — the afflicted psalmist pours out his complaint: his days vanish like smoke, he is like a lonely bird; yet God will arise and have mercy on Zion, for He is eternal though heaven and earth perish.

Key Verses

v.12
"But thou, O LORD, shalt endure for ever; and thy remembrance unto all generations." (KJV) — The great pivot. After 11 verses of anguish, "BUT THOU." My days decline like a shadow — BUT THOU endure forever. This is the anchor for every sufferer: I am passing away, but God remains.
v.13
"Thou shalt arise, and have mercy upon Zion: for the time to favour her, yea, the set time, is come." (KJV) — God has "appointed times" in His plan. The sufferer's confidence is not that God MIGHT act, but that the SET TIME has come. God works on schedule — His schedule.
v.17
"He will regard the prayer of the destitute, and not despise their prayer." (KJV) — Stunning promise. God does not despise the prayer of the destitute (literally: the stripped bare, the bush of the desert). The more helpless you are, the more attentive God is.
v.25-27
"Of old hast thou laid the foundation of the earth: and the heavens are the work of thy hands. They shall perish, but thou shalt endure... But thou art the same, and thy years shall have no end." (KJV) — Creation itself will wear out like a garment — but God remains unchanged. Quoted in Hebrews 1:10-12 as spoken TO Christ, affirming His deity and eternality.

Cross-References

  • v.3 — James 4:14 — "For what is your life? It is even a vapour, that appeareth for a little time." Human frailty. v.12 — Psalm 90:2 — "From everlasting to everlasting, thou art God." God's eternality. v.13 — Galatians 4:4 — "When the fulness of the time was come, God sent forth his Son." God's set times. v.17 — Psalm 34:18 — "The LORD is nigh unto them that are of a broken heart." v.25-27 — Hebrews 1:10-12 — Direct quotation applied to Christ, affirming His deity and unchangeableness. v.26 — Isaiah 51:6 — "The heavens shall vanish away like smoke, and the earth shall wax old like a garment." v.27 — Malachi 3:6 — "For I am the LORD, I change not."

Translations

  • v.6 — KJV: "pelican of the wilderness" / ESV: "desert owl of the wilderness" — Hebrew "qa'ath" is debated. Could be pelican, owl, or another solitary bird. The point is extreme isolation — a creature of desolate places.
  • v.12 — KJV: "shalt endure for ever" / ESV: "are enthroned forever" — Hebrew "teshev" = sit/dwell/be enthroned. ESV captures the royal connotation; KJV emphasizes permanence. Both are true: God sits enthroned and endures forever.
  • v.26 — KJV: "as a vesture shalt thou change them" / ESV: "You will change them like a robe" — God changes the heavens like a man changes clothes. The universe is God's garment — worn, changed, replaced. He is not subject to it.

Reflection

  • 1. "But thou" (v.12). When everything around you is declining, fading, and withering — insert "BUT THOU, O LORD." Your circumstances change. God does not. This is the hinge-verse of every lament.
  • 2. God has "set times" (v.13). Your suffering has an expiration date on God's calendar. He is not late. He is not unaware. The appointed time WILL come.
  • 3. God hears the destitute (v.17). You don't have to be eloquent. You don't have to be strong. You don't have to have it together. God does not despise the prayer of the completely stripped-down soul.
  • 4. Everything perishes; God remains (vv.25-27). The universe itself will wear out — but God is the same. In a world of constant change, there is One who never changes. Anchor yourself to Him.
  • 5. Written for the generation to come (v.18). This psalm is a gift across time. The sufferer wrote it so that future people — including you — would praise the LORD. Your testimony of suffering and deliverance is also meant for others.
