1Who hath believed our report? and to whom is the arm of the LORD revealed?

2For he shall grow up before him as a tender plant, and as a root out of a dry ground: he hath no form nor comeliness; and when we shall see him, there is no beauty that we should desire him.

3He is despised and rejected of men; a man of sorrows, and acquainted with grief: and we hid as it were our faces from him; he was despised, and we esteemed him not.

4Surely he hath borne our griefs, and carried our sorrows: yet we did esteem him stricken, smitten of God, and afflicted.

5But he was wounded for our transgressions, he was bruised for our iniquities: the chastisement of our peace was upon him; and with his stripes we are healed.

6All we like sheep have gone astray; we have turned every one to his own way; and the LORD hath laid on him the iniquity of us all.

7He was oppressed, and he was afflicted, yet he opened not his mouth: he is brought as a lamb to the slaughter, and as a sheep before her shearers is dumb, so he openeth not his mouth.

8He was taken from prison and from judgment: and who shall declare his generation? for he was cut off out of the land of the living: for the transgression of my people was he stricken.

9And he made his grave with the wicked, and with the rich in his death; because he had done no violence, neither was any deceit in his mouth.

10Yet it pleased the LORD to bruise him; he hath put him to grief: when thou shalt make his soul an offering for sin, he shall see his seed, he shall prolong his days, and the pleasure of the LORD shall prosper in his hand.

11He shall see of the travail of his soul, and shall be satisfied: by his knowledge shall my righteous servant justify many; for he shall bear their iniquities.

12Therefore will I divide him a portion with the great, and he shall divide the spoil with the strong; because he hath poured out his soul unto death: and he was numbered with the transgressors; and he bare the sin of many, and made intercession for the transgressors.

1Who would have believed what we just heard? When was the Lord’s power revealed through him?

2He sprouted up like a twig before God, like a root out of parched soil; he had no stately form or majesty that might catch our attention, no special appearance that we should want to follow him.

3He was despised and rejected by people, one who experienced pain and was acquainted with illness; people hid their faces from him; he was despised, and we considered him insignificant.

4But he lifted up our illnesses, he carried our pain; even though we thought he was being punished, attacked by God, and afflicted for something he had done.

5He was wounded because of our rebellious deeds, crushed because of our sins; he endured punishment that made us well; because of his wounds we have been healed.

6All of us had wandered off like sheep; each of us had strayed off on his own path, but the Lord caused the sin of all of us to attack him.

7He was treated harshly and afflicted, but he did not even open his mouth. Like a lamb led to the slaughtering block, like a sheep silent before her shearers, he did not even open his mouth.

8He was led away after an unjust trial— but who even cared? Indeed, he was cut off from the land of the living; because of the rebellion of his own people he was wounded.

9They intended to bury him with criminals, but he ended up in a rich man’s tomb because he had committed no violent deeds, nor had he spoken deceitfully.

10Though the Lord desired to crush him and make him ill, once restitution is made, he will see descendants and enjoy long life, and the Lord’s purpose will be accomplished through him.

11Having suffered, he will reflect on his work, he will be satisfied when he understands what he has done. “My servant will acquit many, for he carried their sins.

12So I will assign him a portion with the multitudes, he will divide the spoils of victory with the powerful, because he willingly submitted to death and was numbered with the rebels, when he lifted up the sin of many and intervened on behalf of the rebels.”

Summary

The crown jewel of Old Testament prophecy — the Suffering Servant. Despised and rejected, a man of sorrows, acquainted with grief. He was wounded for OUR transgressions, bruised for OUR iniquities. The chastisement of OUR peace was upon Him, and with His stripes WE are healed. The LORD laid on Him the iniquity of us all. Cut off from the living, buried with the rich, yet He sees His offspring and the pleasure of the LORD prospers in His hand.

Authorship & Background

Author: Isaiah son of Amoz (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).
Classification: Fourth Servant Song (52:13-53:12) — Vicarious Suffering and Atonement Key Themes: Substitutionary atonement, the Servant despised yet exalted, bearing others' sins, silent before slaughter, death with transgressors yet burial with the rich, justifying many, interceding for transgressors
Historical Context: Isaiah 52:13-53:12 is the FOURTH and greatest Servant Song. Written approximately 700 BC, it describes the crucifixion, burial, and resurrection of Jesus Christ with astonishing precision — 700 years before the event. The Ethiopian eunuch was reading this passage when Philip explained the gospel (Acts 8:32-35). This chapter is quoted or alluded to more than any other OT passage in the NT. Jewish interpretation historically applied it to the nation of Israel, but the details (innocent suffering for others' sins, death and burial, willing submission) point to an INDIVIDUAL — fulfilled uniquely in Christ.
Structure:
  • [52:13-15] The Servant Exalted Yet Marred
  • Who Has Believed Our Report? (vv.1-3)
  • He Bore OUR Griefs and Carried OUR Sorrows (vv.4-6)
  • Silent Before His Slaughter (vv.7-9)
  • The LORD's Pleasure: Offspring, Satisfaction (vv.10-12)

Reflection

  • 1. "He was wounded for OUR transgressions" (v.5). The pronouns are everything: HE — OUR. HIS suffering — OUR healing. Substitution is personal: He took YOUR specific sins, YOUR specific punishment, YOUR specific guilt. Make it personal.
  • 2. "All we like sheep have gone astray" (v.6). Universal guilt: ALL of us. No one is exempt. "Every one to his OWN way" — sin is self-direction, choosing your path over God's. But the LORD laid it ALL on Him.
  • 3. "He opened not his mouth" (v.7). Innocent yet silent. No defense, no self-justification, no protest. If the innocent Christ didn't demand His rights, what does that say about our insistence on defending ourselves?
  • 4. "He shall see his seed... and be satisfied" (vv.10-11). YOUR salvation satisfies Jesus. He looks at the fruit of His suffering and says "worth it." You are not a disappointing return on His investment — you are His JOY.
  • 5. "He made intercession for the transgressors" (v.12). Jesus is praying for you RIGHT NOW. Not "once prayed" — MAKES intercession, present tense. Whatever you're facing, Christ is actively advocating for you before the Father.
