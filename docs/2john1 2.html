2 John — Chapter 1

1The elder to the elect lady and her children, whom I love in truth, and not only I, but also all who know the truth,

Summary

John writes to the "elect lady" urging her to walk in truth and love, warns against deceivers who deny Christ's coming in the flesh, and instructs believers not to receive false teachers into their homes lest they share in their wicked works.

Key Verses

v.4
"I rejoiced greatly to find some of your children walking in the truth." (ESV) — Joy in others' faithfulness.
v.6
"This is love, that we walk according to his commandments." (ESV) — Love defined as obedience.
v.7
"Many deceivers have gone out into the world, those who do not confess the coming of Jesus Christ in the flesh. Such a one is the deceiver and the antichrist." (ESV) — The incarnation test.
v.8
"Watch yourselves, so that you may not lose what we have worked for, but may win a full reward." (ESV) — Guard your progress.
v.9
"Everyone who goes on ahead and does not abide in the teaching of Christ, does not have God." (ESV) — Staying in Christ's teaching = having God.
v.10
"If anyone comes to you and does not bring this teaching, do not receive him into your house." (ESV) — Don't support false teachers.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/2-john-1/ Guzik covers the balance of truth and love, the incarnation test, and the prohibition against supporting false teachers.
  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "Walking in Truth" on v.4 (MTP Vol. 20, No. 1189) "Walking in truth — not just knowing truth, not just believing truth — walking in it. Truth as a lifestyle, not just a doctrine. And John's joy: nothing greater than hearing his spiritual children walk in truth. This is the pastor's deepest satisfaction — not numbers, not programs — people walking in truth."

Translations

  • v.1 — KJV: "the elder" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "ho presbuteros" — the old man, the elder. John's self-designation in his later years.
  • v.1 — KJV: "elect lady" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "eklektē kuria" — chosen lady. Either a proper name (Lady Electa or Kyria) or a personification of a church.
  • v.6 — KJV: "walk after his commandments" / ESV: "walk according to his commandments" — Both equivalent. Love defined as obedience to God's commands.
  • v.7 — KJV: "confess not that Jesus Christ is come in the flesh" / ESV: "do not confess the coming of Jesus Christ in the flesh" — Greek "erchomenon en sarki" — present participle: coming in flesh. The Gnostic heresy denied Christ's true, ongoing humanity.
  • v.8 — KJV: "look to yourselves, that we lose not those things which we have wrought" / ESV: "watch yourselves, so that you may not lose what we have worked for" — Greek "misthon plērē" — complete reward. Don't lose what you've built through association with false teaching.
  • v.9 — KJV: "transgresseth" / ESV: "goes on ahead" — Greek "proagōn" — goes ahead, goes beyond. Those who "progress" beyond Christ's teaching actually regress away from God.
  • v.10 — KJV: "receive him not into your house" / ESV: Both equivalent. — In the first century, traveling teachers depended on hospitality. Hosting a false teacher = supporting his ministry. Don't provide a platform for error.
  • ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB) v.1 — NET: "To the elect lady and her children, whom I love in truth" — "elect lady" (eklektE kyria) — debated: a specific woman or a church personified? WEB: "the chosen lady." KJV: "the elect lady." If a church, "her children" are its members; "her sister" (v.13) is another church.
  • v.7 — NET: "many deceivers have gone out into the world, people who do not confess Jesus as Christ coming in the flesh" — "coming in the flesh" (erchomenon en sarki = present participle). WEB: "who don't confess that Jesus Christ came in the flesh." The incarnation as the test of orthodoxy — deny it and you have "the deceiver and the antichrist."
  • v.8 — NET: "Watch out, so that you do not lose the things we have worked for" — "worked for" (eirgasametha). WEB: "Watch yourselves, that we don't lose the things which we have accomplished." KJV: "that we lose not those things which we have wrought." Progress can be lost — vigilance required.
  • v.9 — NET: "Everyone who goes on ahead and does not remain in the teaching of Christ does not have God" — "goes on ahead" (proagOn = progresses beyond). WEB: "Whoever transgresses and doesn't remain in the teaching of Christ." "Progress" beyond Christ's teaching is actually regress — departure from God.
  • v.10 — NET: "do not receive him into your house and do not give him any greeting" — "greeting" (chairein = rejoice/welcome). WEB: "don't receive him into your house, and don't welcome him." Hospitality to false teachers = partnership in their deception (v.11). Not rude but protective.

Reflection

  • 1. "Walking in truth" (v.4). Is your life consistent with what you believe? Are you walking in truth or just talking about it?
  • 2. "This is love, that we walk after his commandments" (v.6). Do you define love by feelings or by obedience? Biblical love is expressed through keeping God's commands.
  • 3. "Receive him not into your house" (v.10). Are you discerning about who you support, platform, and endorse? Supporting false teachers makes you a partner in their work.
  • 4. How do you balance truth and love? Some emphasize truth without love (harsh). Others emphasize love without truth (permissive). John holds both together. Where do you lean?
