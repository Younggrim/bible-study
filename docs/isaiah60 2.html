Isaiah — Chapter 60

1Arise, shine, for your light has come, and the glory of the LORD has risen upon you.

2For behold, darkness shall cover the earth, and thick darkness the peoples; but the LORD will arise upon you, and his glory will be seen upon you.

3And nations shall come to your light, and kings to the brightness of your rising.

4Lift up your eyes all around, and see; they all gather together, they come to you; your sons shall come from afar, and your daughters shall be carried on the hip.

5Then you shall see and be radiant; your heart shall thrill and exult, because the abundance of the sea shall be turned to you, the wealth of the nations shall come to you.

6A multitude of camels shall cover you, the young camels of Midian and Ephah; all those from Sheba shall come. They shall bring gold and frankincense, and shall bring good news, the praises of the LORD.

7All the flocks of Kedar shall be gathered to you; the rams of Nebaioth shall minister to you; they shall come up with acceptance on my altar, and I will beautify my beautiful house.

8Who are these that fly like a cloud, and like doves to their windows?

9For the coastlands shall hope for me, the ships of Tarshish first, to bring your children from afar, their silver and gold with them, for the name of the LORD your God, and for the Holy One of Israel, because he has made you beautiful.

10Foreigners shall build up your walls, and their kings shall minister to you; for in my wrath I struck you, but in my favor I have had mercy on you.

11Your gates shall be open continually; day and night they shall not be shut, that people may bring to you the wealth of the nations, with their kings led in procession.

12For the nation and kingdom that will not serve you shall perish; those nations shall be utterly laid waste.

13The glory of Lebanon shall come to you, the cypress, the plane, and the pine, to beautify the place of my sanctuary, and I will make the place of my feet glorious.

14The sons of those who afflicted you shall come bending low to you, and all who despised you shall bow down at your feet; they shall call you the City of the LORD, the Zion of the Holy One of Israel.

15Whereas you have been forsaken and hated, with no one passing through, I will make you majestic forever, a joy from age to age.

16You shall suck the milk of nations; you shall nurse at the breast of kings; and you shall know that I, the LORD, am your Savior and your Redeemer, the Mighty One of Jacob.

17Instead of bronze I will bring gold, and instead of iron I will bring silver; instead of wood, bronze, instead of stones, iron. I will make your overseers peace and your taskmasters righteousness.

18Violence shall no more be heard in your land, devastation or destruction within your borders; you shall call your walls Salvation, and your gates Praise.

19The sun shall be no more your light by day, nor for brightness shall the moon give you light; but the LORD will be your everlasting light, and your God will be your glory.

20Your sun shall no more go down, nor your moon withdraw itself; for the LORD will be your everlasting light, and your days of mourning shall be ended.

21Your people shall all be righteous; they shall possess the land forever, the branch of my planting, the work of my hands, that I might be glorified.

22The least one shall become a clan, and the smallest one a mighty nation; I am the LORD; in its time I will hasten it.

Summary

"Arise, shine; for thy light is come!" — Zion's glorification. Darkness covers the earth but God's glory rises on Jerusalem. Nations stream to her light, bringing wealth, sons, and daughters from afar. The sun and moon are replaced by God Himself as eternal light. Violence ceases, gates stand open perpetually, and the least becomes a thousand.

Key Verses

v.1
"Arise, shine; for thy light is come, and the glory of the LORD is risen upon thee" (KJV) — The imperative: GET UP and SHINE. Not "wait for light" — your light HAS COME. It's here. Rise and reflect it.
v.2
"For, behold, the darkness shall cover the earth, and gross darkness the people: but the LORD shall arise upon thee, and his glory shall be seen upon thee" (KJV) — The contrast: the world in darkness, but ZION illuminated. God's glory creates the contrast — His people shine BECAUSE He shines on them.
v.3
"And the Gentiles shall come to thy light, and kings to the brightness of thy rising" (KJV) — Nations are DRAWN to the light. The Magi following the star to Christ (Matt 2) is a first-fruits fulfillment.
v.19
"The sun shall be no more thy light by day; neither for brightness shall the moon give light unto thee: but the LORD shall be unto thee an everlasting light" (KJV) — No more sun or moon needed — God Himself is the light source. Revelation 21:23 quotes this for the New Jerusalem.
v.22
"A little one shall become a thousand, and a small one a strong nation: I the LORD will hasten it in his time" (KJV) — Explosive growth: the smallest becomes a thousand. God will ACCELERATE it when the time is right.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/isaiah-60/
  • Charles Spurgeon: "'Arise, shine!' Not 'wait and shine eventually.' ARISE — now. SHINE — now. The light has already come; your job is not to create it but to REFLECT it. Get up out of your darkness and let His glory be seen on you."

Translations

  • v.1 — "Arise, shine" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "qumi ori" = rise up, be light/shine. Imperative commands: action required. The light is available; you must RECEIVE and REFLECT it.
  • v.6 — "dromedaries of Midian" (KJV) / "young camels" (ESV) — Hebrew "bikrey" = young camels. Caravans from Arabia bringing wealth — the trade routes redirected toward Zion.
  • v.17 — "gold instead of brass" (KJV) / "gold instead of bronze" (ESV) — Upgrade: every material replaced with something better. Bronze → gold, iron → silver, wood → bronze, stones → iron. Total quality elevation.
  • v.19 — "everlasting light" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "or olam" = light of eternity. Not cyclical (rising/setting) but PERMANENT. No more nightfall, no more darkness, no more shadows.

Reflection

  • 1. "Arise, shine — your light HAS COME" (v.1). Stop waiting for conditions to improve before you shine. The light is already here (Christ has come). YOUR job: rise up and reflect it. Now. Today. In the darkness.
  • 2. The world is dark; you're supposed to contrast (v.2). You're not meant to blend in. God's glory on you creates CONTRAST with surrounding darkness. If you look identical to the dark world, something's wrong.
  • 3. Light attracts (v.3). You don't need to chase people — shine and they'll COME. Nations came to Zion's light. Your life, lit by God's glory, draws people toward Him naturally.
  • 4. God replaces the sun (v.19). Everything you depend on now for "light" — guidance, energy, hope — will one day be replaced by God HIMSELF as direct source. No intermediaries. No more shadows.
  • 5. "A little one shall become a thousand" (v.22). Don't despise small beginnings. In God's economy, the smallest thing becomes the greatest. He HASTENS growth in His time. Be faithful in the "little one" stage.
