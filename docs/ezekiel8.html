Ezekiel — Chapter 8

1In the sixth year, in the sixth month, on the fifth day of the month, as I sat in my house, with the elders of Judah sitting before me, the hand of the Lord GOD fell upon me there.

2Then I looked, and behold, a form that had the appearance of a man. Below what appeared to be his waist was fire, and above his waist was something like the appearance of brightness, like gleaming metal.

3He put out the form of a hand and took me by a lock of my head, and the Spirit lifted me up between earth and heaven and brought me in visions of God to Jerusalem, to the entrance of the gateway of the inner court that faces north, where was the seat of the image of jealousy, which provokes to jealousy.

4And behold, the glory of the God of Israel was there, like the vision that I saw in the valley.

5Then he said to me, “Son of man, lift up your eyes now toward the north.” So I lifted up my eyes toward the north, and behold, north of the altar gate, in the entrance, was this image of jealousy.

6And he said to me, “Son of man, do you see what they are doing, the great abominations that the house of Israel are committing here, to drive me far from my sanctuary? But you will see still greater abominations.”

7And he brought me to the entrance of the court, and when I looked, behold, there was a hole in the wall.

8Then he said to me, “Son of man, dig in the wall.” So I dug in the wall, and behold, there was an entrance.

9And he said to me, “Go in, and see the vile abominations that they are committing here.”

10So I went in and saw. And there, engraved on the wall all around, was every form of creeping things and loathsome beasts, and all the idols of the house of Israel.

11And before them stood seventy men of the elders of the house of Israel, with Jaazaniah the son of Shaphan standing among them. Each had his censer in his hand, and the smoke of the cloud of incense went up.

12Then he said to me, “Son of man, have you seen what the elders of the house of Israel are doing in the dark, each in his room of pictures? For they say, ‘The LORD does not see us, the LORD has forsaken the land.’”

13He said also to me, “You will see still greater abominations that they commit.”

14Then he brought me to the entrance of the north gate of the house of the LORD, and behold, there sat women weeping for Tammuz.

15Then he said to me, “Have you seen this, O son of man? You will see still greater abominations than these.”

16And he brought me into the inner court of the house of the LORD. And behold, at the entrance of the temple of the LORD, between the porch and the altar, were about twenty-five men, with their backs to the temple of the LORD, and their faces toward the east, worshiping the sun toward the east.

17Then he said to me, “Have you seen this, O son of man? Is it too light a thing for the house of Judah to commit the abominations that they commit here, that they should fill the land with violence and provoke me still further to anger? Behold, they put the branch to their nose.

18Therefore I will act in wrath. My eye will not spare, nor will I have pity. And though they cry in my ears with a loud voice, I will not hear them.”

Summary

God transports Ezekiel in vision to Jerusalem's temple and shows him FOUR escalating abominations happening INSIDE: (1) the image of jealousy at the gate, (2) 70 elders burning incense to wall-carvings in the dark, (3) women weeping for Tammuz, and (4) 25 men worshipping the SUN with their backs to the temple. Each worse than the last. "Son of man, hast thou seen this? Turn yet again, and thou shalt see greater abominations."

Authorship & Background

Author: Ezekiel son of Buzi (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).
Classification: Prophetic Vision — The Temple Abominations Key Themes: Idolatry INSIDE God's temple, escalating abominations (each worse than the last), the elders' secret worship, pagan religion in the holy place, the cause of God's departure
Historical Context: Dated to the "sixth year, sixth month, fifth day" (September 592 BC) — 14 months after the initial call vision. Ezekiel is in his house with the elders of Judah when God's hand falls on him and transports him in VISION to Jerusalem's temple. What he sees explains WHY God's glory will DEPART (chs.9-11): the temple is FULL of abominations. The vision reveals what's happening RIGHT NOW in Jerusalem (not past or future). The four scenes escalate: each one God says "turn again and see GREATER abominations" (vv.6,13,15). They culminate with men literally TURNING THEIR BACKS to God's altar to worship the rising sun.
Structure:
  • Transported in Vision to the Temple (vv.1-4)
  • Abomination 1: The Image of Jealousy (vv.5-6)
  • Abomination 2: 70 Elders in the Dark (vv.7-13)
  • Abomination 3: Women Weeping for Tammuz (vv.14-15)
  • Abomination 4: Sun Worship with Backs to the Temple (vv.16-18)

Map & Geography

  • Jerusalem (v.3): Capital of Judah; the holy city where the Temple stood.

Commentary

Reflection

  • 1. Escalating abomination (vv.6,13,15). Sin doesn't stay at one level — it ESCALATES. "Turn again and see greater abominations." What started small in your life may be growing into something worse while you're not looking.
  • 2. "The LORD seeth us not" (v.12). The theology that enables sin: "God doesn't see." But He DOES. He brought Ezekiel in VISION to show him what the elders did in the dark. Nothing is hidden.
  • 3. Backs to the temple (v.16). What are you facing INSTEAD of God? What has your attention, your worship, your orientation — while your back is turned on the One who should be central?
  • 4. Abominations INSIDE the temple (entire chapter). These sins aren't committed in pagan temples — they're in GOD'S HOUSE. The most dangerous idolatry isn't outside the church but INSIDE it. What abominations are tolerated in sacred spaces?
  • 5. "Is it a light thing?" (v.17). We trivialize what God takes seriously. "It's no big deal" is the refrain of those committing the worst offenses. God's question: is it really as trivial as you're pretending?
