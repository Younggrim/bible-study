2 Chronicles — Chapter 31

1Now when all this was finished, all Israel who were present went out to the cities of Judah and broke in pieces the pillars and cut down the Asherim and broke down the high places and the altars throughout all Judah and Benjamin, and in Ephraim and Manasseh, until they had destroyed them all. Then all the people of Israel returned to their cities, every man to his possession.

2And Hezekiah appointed the divisions of the priests and of the Levites, division by division, each according to his service, the priests and the Levites, for burnt offerings and peace offerings, to minister in the gates of the camp of the LORD and to give thanks and praise.

3The contribution of the king from his own possessions was for the burnt offerings: the burnt offerings of morning and evening, and the burnt offerings for the Sabbaths, the new moons, and the appointed feasts, as it is written in the Law of the LORD.

4And he commanded the people who lived in Jerusalem to give the portion due to the priests and the Levites, that they might give themselves to the Law of the LORD.

5As soon as the command was spread abroad, the people of Israel gave in abundance the firstfruits of grain, wine, oil, honey, and of all the produce of the field. And they brought in abundantly the tithe of everything.

6And the people of Israel and Judah who lived in the cities of Judah also brought in the tithe of cattle and sheep, and the tithe of the dedicated things that had been dedicated to the LORD their God, and laid them in heaps.

7In the third month they began to pile up the heaps, and finished them in the seventh month.

8When Hezekiah and the princes came and saw the heaps, they blessed the LORD and his people Israel.

9And Hezekiah questioned the priests and the Levites about the heaps.

10Azariah the chief priest, who was of the house of Zadok, answered him, “Since they began to bring the contributions into the house of the LORD, we have eaten and had enough and have plenty left, for the LORD has blessed his people, so that we have this large amount left.”

11Then Hezekiah commanded them to prepare chambers in the house of the LORD, and they prepared them.

12And they faithfully brought in the contributions, the tithes, and the dedicated things. The chief officer in charge of them was Conaniah the Levite, with Shimei his brother as second,

13while Jehiel, Azaziah, Nahath, Asahel, Jerimoth, Jozabad, Eliel, Ismachiah, Mahath, and Benaiah were overseers assisting Conaniah and Shimei his brother, by the appointment of Hezekiah the king and Azariah the chief officer of the house of God.

14And Kore the son of Imnah the Levite, keeper of the east gate, was over the freewill offerings to God, to apportion the contribution reserved for the LORD and the most holy offerings.

15Eden, Miniamin, Jeshua, Shemaiah, Amariah, and Shecaniah were faithfully assisting him in the cities of the priests, to distribute the portions to their brothers, old and young alike, by divisions,

16except those enrolled by genealogy, males from three years old and upward—all who entered the house of the LORD as the duty of each day required—for their service according to their offices, by their divisions.

17The enrollment of the priests was according to their fathers’ houses; that of the Levites from twenty years old and upward was according to their offices, by their divisions.

18They were enrolled with all their little children, their wives, their sons, and their daughters, the whole assembly, for they were faithful in keeping themselves holy.

19And for the sons of Aaron, the priests, who were in the fields of common land belonging to their cities, there were men in the several cities who were designated by name to distribute portions to every male among the priests and to everyone among the Levites who was enrolled.

20Thus Hezekiah did throughout all Judah, and he did what was good and right and faithful before the LORD his God.

21And every work that he undertook in the service of the house of God and in accordance with the law and the commandments, seeking his God, he did with all his heart, and prospered.

Summary

After the Passover, the people destroy idolatrous sites throughout the land, and Hezekiah organizes the priests and Levites into their divisions for service. The people give so generously in tithes and offerings that heaps of surplus accumulate, and storerooms must be prepared.

Key Verses

v.4
"And he commanded the people who lived in Jerusalem to give the portion due to the priests and the Levites, that they might give themselves to the Law of the LORD." (ESV)— The purpose of supporting ministers financially is theological, not merely practical: freed from material anxiety, they can devote themselves to studying and teaching God's Word. The ESV renders this "that they might give themselves to the Law of the LORD" — financial support enables spiritual focus.
v.5
"As soon as the command was spread abroad, the people of Israel gave in abundance the firstfruits of grain, wine, oil, honey, and of all the produce of the field. And they brought in abundantly the tithe of everything." (ESV)— The people's immediate and generous response demonstrates hearts already changed by revival. Generosity flows naturally from genuine encounter with God.
v.10
"Azariah the chief priest, who was of the house of Zadok, answered him, “Since they began to bring the contributions into the house of the LORD, we have eaten and had enough and have plenty left, for the LORD has blessed his people, so that we have this large amount left.”" (ESV)— Azariah interprets the abundance theologically: "the LORD hath blessed his people." Generous giving is both evidence of and participation in divine blessing.
v.20
"Thus Hezekiah did throughout all Judah, and he did what was good and right and faithful before the LORD his God." (ESV)— A threefold commendation: good (morally excellent), right (just/proper), and truth (faithful/ reliable). This is the Chronicler's highest evaluation of a king's character.
v.21
"And every work that he undertook in the service of the house of God and in accordance with the law and the commandments, seeking his God, he did with all his heart, and prospered." (ESV)— The capstone verse of Hezekiah's reform. Three elements converge: wholehearted devotion, consistent obedience, and divine prosperity. The connection between heart and outcome is the Chronicler's core theology. CROSS-REFERENCES
v.1
2 Kings 18:4 — "He removed the high places, and brake the images, and cut down the groves." The parallel account in Kings summarizes what Chronicles details — the people's zealous destruction of idolatry following revival.
v.4
Numbers 18:21 — "And, behold, I have given the children of Levi all the tenth in Israel for an inheritance, for their service." Hezekiah restores the Mosaic provision that tithes support those who serve at God's house, freeing them for full-time ministry.
v.5
Malachi 3:10 — "Bring ye all the tithes into the storehouse, that there may be meat in mine house, and prove me now herewith... if I will not open you the windows of heaven." Hezekiah's experience validates Malachi's promise — generous tithing overflowed the storehouses.
v.10
Proverbs 3:9-10 — "Honour the LORD with thy substance, and with the firstfruits of all thine increase: So shall thy barns be filled with plenty." Azariah's testimony fulfills the proverbial promise — honoring God with firstfruits produces overflowing abundance.
v.20
Micah 6:8 — "What doth the LORD require of thee, but to do justly, and to love mercy, and to walk humbly with thy God?" Hezekiah's threefold commendation — good, right, truth — parallels Micah's threefold requirement for faithful living.
v.21
Deuteronomy 29:9 — "Keep therefore the words of this covenant, and do them, that ye may prosper in all that ye do." The prosperity principle: obedience to God's covenant brings divine blessing on one's undertakings.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/2-chronicles-31/ Guzik emphasizes that the people's generous giving followed genuine revival — they were not coerced but responded from transformed hearts. He notes the principle that God's workers deserve material support so they can focus on spiritual service, and highlights the organizational wisdom of Hezekiah in creating systems for faithful distribution.
  • Charles Spurgeon: "He did it with all his heart, and prospered — here is the secret of all true success in divine service. Not with half a heart, not with a divided allegiance, not with one eye on God and another on the world, but with ALL his heart. This is what the Lord demands, and this is what the Lord blesses. A half- hearted worker shall have a half-hearted reward. But he that throws himself wholly into God's work, holding nothing back, shall find that the Lord holds nothing back from him. Hezekiah prospered because he was thorough — thorough in destroying idols, thorough in organizing worship, thorough in supporting ministers, thorough in seeking God. May we learn from this king that completeness of devotion is the pathway to completeness of blessing."

Translations

  • v.1 — KJV: "brake the images in pieces, and cut down the groves" / ESV: "broke in pieces the pillars and cut down the Asherim" — Hebrew "matstsevoth" (standing stones/pillars) and "asherim" (sacred poles/trees dedicated to the goddess Asherah). KJV's "images" obscures the specific nature of these cultic objects; "groves" implies natural trees rather than carved poles. ESV preserves the Hebrew terms more precisely — "pillars" and "Asherim" — identifying the specific Canaanite cult objects being destroyed.
  • v.4 — KJV: "that they might be encouraged in the law of the LORD" / ESV: "that they might give themselves to the Law of the LORD" — Hebrew "le-chazaq be-torath YHWH" (to be strong/ hold fast in the Torah of the LORD). KJV reads "encouraged" (strengthened in their work); ESV reads "give themselves to" (devote themselves entirely). The Hebrew "chazaq" means to be firm/strong — financial support strengthens ministers to hold firmly to their calling.
  • v.6 — KJV: "laid them by heaps" / ESV: "laid them in heaps" — Hebrew "arēmot arēmot" (heaps upon heaps). The repetition emphasizes the extraordinary volume — not just heaps but heaps upon heaps. The visual image is of tithed goods literally piling up faster than they could be processed.
  • v.20 — KJV: "wrought that which was good and right and truth" / ESV: "he did what was good and right and faithful" — Hebrew "ha-tov ve-ha-yashar ve-ha-emet" (the good, the straight, and the true/faithful). KJV's "truth" and ESV's "faithful" both render "emet" — a word encompassing reliability, constancy, and faithfulness. The threefold description provides comprehensive moral evaluation.
  • v.21 — KJV: "he did it with all his heart, and prospered" / ESV: "he did with all his heart, and prospered" — Hebrew "be-kol levavo asah ve-hitsliach" (with all his heart he did, and he prospered/succeeded). The verb "hitsliach" indicates divine enabling of success — the prosperity is God's response to wholehearted devotion, not merely human achievement.

Reflection

  • 1. Genuine revival produces practical reformation (v.1). The people's encounter with God at Passover did not end at the Temple door — they went home and destroyed idols. True worship always overflows into life change. If your spiritual experiences produce emotion but no reformation of habits, relationships, or priorities, something essential is missing. Revival that stays in the sanctuary is incomplete.
  • 2. God's ministers deserve material support so they can focus on spiritual work (v.4). Hezekiah's reasoning is clear: support the Levites financially SO THAT they can devote themselves to God's Word. Churches and believers who underresource their pastors and ministers force them into divided attention. Generous support of spiritual leaders is not charity — it is investment in the ministry of the Word.
  • 3. Generous giving is the natural overflow of a revived heart (v.5). The people gave "as soon as the commandment came abroad" — immediately and abundantly. They were not coerced or manipulated; they gave because revival had opened their hearts. If giving feels burdensome, the issue may not be finances but spiritual vitality. Ask God to revive your heart, and generosity will follow naturally.
  • 4. Wholehearted devotion is the secret of spiritual prosperity (v.21). "He did it with all his heart, and prospered." Half- measures produce half-results. Whatever you do in God's service — prayer, giving, serving, studying — do it with your whole heart. The divided heart never experiences the fullness of God's blessing. Completeness of devotion leads to completeness of provision.
  • 5. Organized stewardship honors both givers and receivers (vv.11- 19). Hezekiah did not leave distribution to chance — he appointed named overseers, created systems of accountability, and ensured every minister and family received their portion. Generous giving requires organized, trustworthy stewardship. In your church and personal finances, create systems that ensure resources reach their intended purposes with integrity and accountability.
