Numbers — Chapter 36

1The heads of the fathers’ houses of the clan of the people of Gilead the son of Machir, son of Manasseh, from the clans of the people of Joseph, came near and spoke before Moses and before the chiefs, the heads of the fathers’ houses of the people of Israel.

2They said, “The LORD commanded my lord to give the land for inheritance by lot to the people of Israel, and my lord was commanded by the LORD to give the inheritance of Zelophehad our brother to his daughters.

3But if they are married to any of the sons of the other tribes of the people of Israel, then their inheritance will be taken from the inheritance of our fathers and added to the inheritance of the tribe into which they marry. So it will be taken away from the lot of our inheritance.

4And when the jubilee of the people of Israel comes, then their inheritance will be added to the inheritance of the tribe into which they marry, and their inheritance will be taken from the inheritance of the tribe of our fathers.”

5And Moses commanded the people of Israel according to the word of the LORD, saying, “The tribe of the people of Joseph is right.

6This is what the LORD commands concerning the daughters of Zelophehad: ‘Let them marry whom they think best, only they shall marry within the clan of the tribe of their father.

7The inheritance of the people of Israel shall not be transferred from one tribe to another, for every one of the people of Israel shall hold on to the inheritance of the tribe of his fathers.

8And every daughter who possesses an inheritance in any tribe of the people of Israel shall be wife to one of the clan of the tribe of her father, so that every one of the people of Israel may possess the inheritance of his fathers.

9So no inheritance shall be transferred from one tribe to another, for each of the tribes of the people of Israel shall hold on to its own inheritance.’”

10The daughters of Zelophehad did as the LORD commanded Moses,

11for Mahlah, Tirzah, Hoglah, Milcah, and Noah, the daughters of Zelophehad, were married to sons of their father’s brothers.

12They were married into the clans of the people of Manasseh the son of Joseph, and their inheritance remained in the tribe of their father’s clan.

13These are the commandments and the rules that the LORD commanded through Moses to the people of Israel in the plains of Moab by the Jordan at Jericho.

Summary

God rules that women inheriting land must marry within their tribe to preserve tribal boundaries — completing the book with order and faithfulness.

Key Verses

v.5
"The tribe of the sons of Joseph is right." (ESV) — God validates the concern. Both the daughters (27:7) and the tribal leaders (36:5) are "right" — God's law accommodates legitimate concerns from multiple parties.
v.6
"Let them marry to whom they think best; only to the family of the tribe of their father shall they marry." — Freedom within boundaries. Choice is real but not unlimited. God gives latitude within His structure.
v.7
"So shall not the inheritance of the children of Israel remove from tribe to tribe." — The principle: God's allotments are permanent. What He gives to each remains with each. No tribe gains at another's expense.
v.10
"Even as the LORD commanded Moses, so did the daughters of Zelophehad." — Simple obedience. They asked boldly (ch. 27), received generously, and obeyed willingly. Faith, petition, and obedience in harmony.
v.13
"These are the commandments and the judgments, which the LORD commanded by the hand of Moses unto the children of Israel in the plains of Moab by Jordan near Jericho." — The book's conclusion. Israel stands ready — instructed, organized, and poised to enter.

Cross-References

  • v.2 — Numbers 27:1-11 — The original ruling: daughters may inherit. v.4 — Leviticus 25:8-17 — The Year of Jubilee: land returns to original tribal owners every 50 years. v.6 — 1 Corinthians 7:39 — "She is at liberty to be married to whom she will; only in the Lord" — NT parallel: freedom within divine boundaries. v.6 — 2 Corinthians 6:14 — "Do not be unequally yoked with unbelievers" (ESV) — the principle of marriage within the covenant community. v.7 — 1 Kings 21:3 — Naboth refuses to sell his vineyard: "The LORD forbid it me, that I should give the inheritance of my fathers unto thee." v.8 — Joshua 17:3-6 — The daughters of Zelophehad receive their inheritance in the land, as promised. v.10 — Numbers 27:7 — "The daughters of Zelophehad speak right" — now they obey right. v.12 — Ruth 4:1-12 — Boaz marries Ruth within the kinsman-redeemer system — preserving inheritance within the family/tribe. v.13 — Deuteronomy 1:1-5 — Deuteronomy begins where Numbers ends: Moses addressing Israel on the plains of Moab. v.13 — Numbers 1:1 — Numbers began "in the wilderness of Sinai" — it ends "in the plains of Moab." The journey is complete.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/numbers-36/ Guzik notes that this chapter demonstrates God's concern for both justice (women inherit) and order (tribal boundaries preserved). Neither principle cancels the other — God finds a solution that honors both. He also observes that Numbers ends quietly, with legal detail rather than drama — because faithfulness in the details IS the drama of the spiritual life.
  • Charles Spurgeon: "The book of Numbers ends not with thunder from Sinai or triumph at Jordan, but with a quiet ruling about marriage and inheritance. How like God — who cares as much about the details of daily obedience as about the grand moments of deliverance. The daughters of Zelophehad asked boldly, received graciously, and obeyed simply. That is the whole Christian life in miniature: ask God for what He promises, receive it with gratitude, and walk in obedience within the boundaries He sets. Freedom and obedience are not enemies — they are partners. 'Let them marry whom they think best — only within the tribe.' Choose freely, but choose within God's will. That is not bondage — that is wisdom."

Translations

  • v.1 — KJV: "the chief fathers of the families" / ESV: "The heads of the fathers' houses of the clan" — Hebrew "rashei ha-avot l'mishp'chot." Both translations describe the senior male leaders of the extended family units within the tribe of Manasseh. These are the clan elders responsible for tribal land management.
  • v.3 — KJV: "shall be put to the inheritance of the tribe whereunto they are received" / ESV: "added to the inheritance of the tribe into which they marry" — Hebrew "v'nosaf al nachalat ha-matteh asher tihyenah lahem." Both describe the same concern: land following the woman into her husband's tribe. ESV's "into which they marry" is more direct than KJV's "whereunto they are received."
  • v.4 — KJV: "when the jubile of the children of Israel shall be" / ESV: "when the jubilee of the people of Israel comes" — Hebrew "ba-yovel." The Jubilee (every 50th year) normally returns land to its original tribal owner (Leviticus 25). But if the daughters' land is now "owned" by another tribe through marriage, the Jubilee would confirm it there — not return it to Manasseh. This is the crux of the problem.
  • v.5 — KJV: "hath said well" / ESV: "is right" — Hebrew "ken." The same word God used for the daughters in 27:7 — "right, correct, true." God validates both parties: the daughters were right to ask, and the tribal leaders are right to raise this concern.
  • v.6 — KJV: "Let them marry to whom they think best" / ESV: "Let them marry whom they think best" — Hebrew "la-tov b'eineihem tihyenah l'nashim." Literally "to the good in their eyes let them become wives." The freedom is genuine — they choose their own husbands. The restriction ("only within the tribe") limits the pool but not the choice within that pool.
  • v.11 — KJV: "were married unto their father's brothers' sons" / ESV: "were married to sons of their father's brothers" — Hebrew "li-vnei dodeihen." "Dod" means uncle (father's brother). They married their paternal cousins — the closest eligible men within their clan. This was common and acceptable in ancient Israel.
  • v.13 — KJV: "the commandments and the judgments" / ESV: "the commandments and the rules" — Hebrew "ha-mitzvot v'ha-mishpatim." "Mitzvot" = commandments (direct divine orders). "Mishpatim" = judgments, rules, case law (legal rulings applied to specific situations). Together they encompass all of God's instruction: both absolute commands and situational applications.

Reflection

  • 1. Both parties are "right" (v.5; cf. 27:7). The daughters were right to ask for inheritance; the tribal leaders are right to protect boundaries. God's wisdom accommodates both legitimate concerns without canceling either. In disputes, both sides may have valid points — seek God's solution that honors all legitimate interests.
  • 2. Freedom within boundaries (v.6). "Marry whom they think best — only within the tribe." God gives real freedom — but not unlimited freedom. The boundaries are not punishment but protection. Do you see God's boundaries as cages or as guardrails? They preserve your inheritance.
  • 3. The daughters obey (v.10). They asked boldly in chapter 27; they obey willingly in chapter 36. Bold petition and humble obedience are not contradictory — they are complementary. You can ask God for great things AND submit to His conditions.
  • 4. Inheritance preserved through obedience (v.12). Their inheritance "remained in the tribe of their father's clan." Obedience preserves what God gives. Disobedience forfeits it. What God has given you — are you protecting it through faithful obedience?
  • 5. Numbers ends with order, not drama (v.13). The book closes with a legal ruling and a colophon — not a battle or a miracle. God cares about the mundane details of faithful living as much as the spectacular moments. Faithfulness in the ordinary IS the spiritual life.
  • 6. On the threshold (v.13). Numbers ends with Israel poised at the Jordan — instructed, organized, counted, and ready. The next step is crossing over. Where are you standing on the threshold? What "Jordan" is God calling you to cross? The preparation is complete — it is time to enter.
