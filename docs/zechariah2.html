Zechariah — Chapter 2

1And I lifted my eyes and saw, and behold, a man with a measuring line in his hand!

2Then I said, “Where are you going?” And he said to me, “To measure Jerusalem, to see what is its width and what is its length.”

3And behold, the angel who talked with me came forward, and another angel came forward to meet him

4and said to him, “Run, say to that young man, ‘Jerusalem shall be inhabited as villages without walls, because of the multitude of people and livestock in it.

5And I will be to her a wall of fire all around, declares the LORD, and I will be the glory in her midst.’”

6Up! Up! Flee from the land of the north, declares the LORD. For I have spread you abroad as the four winds of the heavens, declares the LORD.

7Up! Escape to Zion, you who dwell with the daughter of Babylon.

8For thus said the LORD of hosts, after his glory sent me to the nations who plundered you, for he who touches you touches the apple of his eye:

9“Behold, I will shake my hand over them, and they shall become plunder for those who served them. Then you will know that the LORD of hosts has sent me.

10Sing and rejoice, O daughter of Zion, for behold, I come and I will dwell in your midst, declares the LORD.

11And many nations shall join themselves to the LORD in that day, and shall be my people. And I will dwell in your midst, and you shall know that the LORD of hosts has sent me to you.

12And the LORD will inherit Judah as his portion in the holy land, and will again choose Jerusalem.”

13Be silent, all flesh, before the LORD, for he has roused himself from his holy dwelling.

Summary

Zechariah sees a man measuring Jerusalem and is told the city will be inhabited without walls because of its multitude — God Himself will be a wall of fire around her and the glory within.

Key Verses

v.4
"Jerusalem shall be inhabited as towns without walls for the multitude of men and cattle therein" (KJV) — God's future Jerusalem will be so populated that walls cannot contain it. This speaks to a prosperity beyond human planning — a city bursting its boundaries because God's blessing overflows all limitation.
v.5
"For I, saith the LORD, will be unto her a wall of fire round about, and will be the glory in the midst of her" (KJV) — Two extraordinary promises: God as external PROTECTION (wall of fire) and internal PRESENCE (glory in the midst). You don't need walls when the living God encircles you with fire and fills you with glory.
v.8
"he that toucheth you toucheth the apple of his eye" (KJV) — Israel is the pupil of God's eye — the most sensitive, protected, precious part. To harm God's people is to poke God in the eye. A stunning image of divine protectiveness and tenderness.
v.10
"Sing and rejoice, O daughter of Zion: for, lo, I come, and I will dwell in the midst of thee, saith the LORD" (KJV) — A Messianic prophecy. God Himself will come and dwell IN Zion — fulfilled in the Incarnation (John 1:14, "the Word dwelt among us") and ultimately in the New Jerusalem (Rev 21:3).
v.11
"many nations shall be joined to the LORD in that day, and shall be my people" (KJV) — The inclusion of Gentiles. Not just Israel but MANY NATIONS will belong to God. This foreshadows the church — peoples from every nation joined to the LORD through Christ.
v.13
"Be silent, O all flesh, before the LORD: for he is raised up out of his holy habitation" (KJV) — When God rises to act, the only appropriate response is silence. Awe. Reverent stillness. God is moving — be quiet and watch.

Cross-References

  • v.4 → Isaiah 54:2-3 — "Enlarge the place of thy tent... thou shalt break forth on the right hand and on the left." v.5 — Exodus 13:21 — The pillar of fire that led and protected Israel in the wilderness — God as fire-wall. v.5 — Isaiah 60:19 — "The LORD shall be unto thee an everlasting light, and thy God thy glory." v.8 — Deuteronomy 32:10 — "He kept him as the apple of his eye" — God's tender care for Israel. v.10 — John 1:14 — "The Word was made flesh, and dwelt among us" — the ultimate fulfillment of God dwelling in Zion. v.11 — Ephesians 2:13-14 — Gentiles brought near through Christ, making one people from Jew and Gentile. v.13 — Habakkuk 2:20 — "The LORD is in his holy temple: let all the earth keep silence before him." v.13 — Zephaniah 1:7 — "Hold thy peace at the presence of the Lord GOD."

Translations

  • v.4 → "towns without walls" (KJV) / "villages without walls" (ESV) — Hebrew "perazot" = open, unwalled settlements. The picture is of a city so secure it needs no fortification — because God IS the fortification.
  • v.5 → "wall of fire" — Not a static barrier but a CONSUMING fire that destroys any who approach with hostile intent. God's protection is active, not passive.
  • v.8 → "apple of his eye" — Hebrew "babat eino" = the pupil, the gate of the eye. The most delicate, reflexively-protected part of the body. God protects Israel as instinctively as you protect your own eye.
  • v.13 → "raised up" (KJV) / "roused himself" (ESV) — Hebrew "ne'or" = to be awakened, stirred up. As if God has been silent and now rises from His dwelling to act decisively. When God "rouses Himself," history changes.

Reflection

  • 1. God's plan is always bigger than our measurements (vv.1-4). The young man tries to measure Jerusalem — to define its limits. But God's vision for your life exceeds what you can measure or plan. Stop trying to contain what God is building. His blessing will overflow your boundaries.
  • 2. "A wall of fire" (v.5). Your best defense is not human strategy but divine presence. When God surrounds you with fire, no enemy can penetrate. Stop building walls of anxiety and self-protection. Let God be your wall.
  • 3. You are the "apple of His eye" (v.8). God is not indifferent to what touches you. Every attack against you touches HIM in His most sensitive place. You are that precious to Him. Live with the confidence of someone fiercely loved and protected.
  • 4. "I come, and I will dwell in the midst of thee" (v.10). God's ultimate goal is not just to bless FROM a distance but to dwell WITH you. Emmanuel — God with us. The Christian life is not about getting things from God; it is about having GOD Himself.
  • 5. "Be silent, all flesh" (v.13). Sometimes the holiest response is silence. When God is working, stop talking. Stop planning. Stop worrying. Be still and let God be God.
