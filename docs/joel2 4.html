Joel — Chapter 2

1Blow a trumpet in Zion; sound an alarm on my holy mountain! Let all the inhabitants of the land tremble, for the day of the LORD is coming; it is near,

2a day of darkness and gloom, a day of clouds and thick darkness! Like blackness there is spread upon the mountains a great and powerful people; their like has never been before, nor will be again after them through the years of all generations.

3Fire devours before them, and behind them a flame burns. The land is like the garden of Eden before them, but behind them a desolate wilderness, and nothing escapes them.

4Their appearance is like the appearance of horses, and like war horses they run.

5As with the rumbling of chariots, they leap on the tops of the mountains, like the crackling of a flame of fire devouring the stubble, like a powerful army drawn up for battle.

6Before them peoples are in anguish; all faces grow pale.

7Like warriors they charge; like soldiers they scale the wall. They march each on his way; they do not swerve from their paths.

8They do not jostle one another; each marches in his path; they burst through the weapons and are not halted.

9They leap upon the city, they run upon the walls, they climb up into the houses, they enter through the windows like a thief.

10The earth quakes before them; the heavens tremble. The sun and the moon are darkened, and the stars withdraw their shining.

11The LORD utters his voice before his army, for his camp is exceedingly great; he who executes his word is powerful. For the day of the LORD is great and very awesome; who can endure it?

12“Yet even now,” declares the LORD, “return to me with all your heart, with fasting, with weeping, and with mourning;

13and rend your hearts and not your garments.” Return to the LORD your God, for he is gracious and merciful, slow to anger, and abounding in steadfast love; and he relents over disaster.

14Who knows whether he will not turn and relent, and leave a blessing behind him, a grain offering and a drink offering for the LORD your God?

15Blow the trumpet in Zion; consecrate a fast; call a solemn assembly;

16gather the people. Consecrate the congregation; assemble the elders; gather the children, even nursing infants. Let the bridegroom leave his room, and the bride her chamber.

17Between the vestibule and the altar let the priests, the ministers of the LORD, weep and say, “Spare your people, O LORD, and make not your heritage a reproach, a byword among the nations. Why should they say among the peoples, ‘Where is their God?’”

18Then the LORD became jealous for his land and had pity on his people.

19The LORD answered and said to his people, “Behold, I am sending to you grain, wine, and oil, and you will be satisfied; and I will no more make you a reproach among the nations.

20“I will remove the northerner far from you, and drive him into a parched and desolate land, his vanguard into the eastern sea, and his rear guard into the western sea; the stench and foul smell of him will rise, for he has done great things.

21“Fear not, O land; be glad and rejoice, for the LORD has done great things!

22Fear not, you beasts of the field, for the pastures of the wilderness are green; the tree bears its fruit; the fig tree and vine give their full yield.

23“Be glad, O children of Zion, and rejoice in the LORD your God, for he has given the early rain for your vindication; he has poured down for you abundant rain, the early and the latter rain, as before.

24“The threshing floors shall be full of grain; the vats shall overflow with wine and oil.

25I will restore to you the years that the swarming locust has eaten, the hopper, the destroyer, and the cutter, my great army, which I sent among you.

26“You shall eat in plenty and be satisfied, and praise the name of the LORD your God, who has dealt wondrously with you. And my people shall never again be put to shame.

27You shall know that I am in the midst of Israel, and that I am the LORD your God and there is none else. And my people shall never again be put to shame.

28“And it shall come to pass afterward, that I will pour out my Spirit on all flesh; your sons and your daughters shall prophesy, your old men shall dream dreams, and your young men shall see visions.

29Even on the male and female servants in those days I will pour out my Spirit.

30“And I will show wonders in the heavens and on the earth, blood and fire and columns of smoke.

31The sun shall be turned to darkness, and the moon to blood, before the great and awesome day of the LORD comes.

32And it shall come to pass that everyone who calls on the name of the LORD shall be saved. For in Mount Zion and in Jerusalem there shall be those who escape, as the LORD has said, and among the survivors shall be those whom the LORD calls.

Summary

Joel calls for wholehearted repentance ("rend your heart, not your garments") and promises that God will pour out His Spirit on all flesh — a prophecy fulfilled at Pentecost — accompanied by cosmic signs before the great Day of the LORD.

Key Verses

v.11
"the day of the LORD is great and very awesome; who can endure it?" (ESV) — The rhetorical question that hangs over the chapter: who can SURVIVE this? The answer comes in v.32: whoever CALLS on the LORD.
v.12
"'Yet even now,' declares the LORD, 'return to me with all your heart, with fasting, with weeping, and with mourning'" (ESV) — even at this late hour, even with judgment at the gates: RETURN. It's not too late. God extends the invitation even in the eleventh hour.
v.13
"rend your hearts and not your garments." Return to the LORD your God, for he is gracious and merciful, slow to anger, and abounding in steadfast love; and he relents over disaster." (ESV) — The heart of Joel's message: God wants INNER transformation, not OUTWARD show. Tearing garments is easy; tearing your heart is painful. And the motivation: God IS gracious, merciful, slow to anger. The character of God is the foundation of genuine repentance.
v.25
"I will restore to you the years that the swarming locust has eaten" (ESV) — One of the most beloved promises in Scripture. God doesn't just stop the destruction — He RESTORES what was lost. Years. Not just future blessing but compensation for past devastation. The God of restoration.
v.28
"And it shall come to pass afterward, that I will pour out my Spirit on all flesh; your sons and your daughters shall prophesy, your old men shall dream dreams, and your young men shall see visions." (ESV) — THE Pentecost passage. "All flesh" = not just prophets and kings but ALL — sons, daughters, old, young, servants. The Spirit is POURED (not measured, not rationed). This was fulfilled beginning at Pentecost (Acts 2:16-21) and continues in the church age.
v.32
"everyone who calls on the name of the LORD shall be saved" (ESV) — Universal offer. No exceptions. No qualifications beyond calling. Quoted by Paul in Romans 10:13 as the basis for salvation by faith. "Everyone" — the widest possible invitation.

Cross-References

  • v.10 — Matthew 24:29 — "The sun will be darkened, and the moon will not give its light." (ESV) End-times signs. v.11 — Malachi 3:2 — "But who can endure the day of his coming?" (ESV) v.12 — Jeremiah 29:13 — "You will seek me and find me, when you seek me with all your heart." (ESV) v.13 — Exodus 34:6 — The original proclamation of God's character: "merciful and gracious, slow to anger, and abounding in steadfast love and faithfulness." (ESV) v.13 — Jonah 4:2 — Jonah quotes this same formula about God's character. v.25 — Isaiah 61:7 — "Instead of your shame there shall be a double portion." (ESV) Restoration exceeding loss. v.28 — Acts 2:16-21 — Peter quotes Joel 2:28-32 directly at Pentecost, declaring "this is that." v.28 — Numbers 11:29 — Moses' wish: "Would that all the LORD's people were prophets." (ESV) Joel 2:28 fulfills it. v.32 — Romans 10:13 — Paul quotes this as the basis for salvation: "Everyone who calls on the name of the Lord will be saved." (ESV) v.32 — Acts 2:21 — Peter includes this promise in his Pentecost sermon.

Translations

  • v.13 — "repenteth him of the evil" (KJV) / "relents over disaster" (ESV) — Hebrew "nacham" = to relent, change course, feel compassion. God does not change His NATURE but can change His COURSE in response to repentance. Not arbitrary but relational.
  • v.17 — "between the porch and the altar" — The space between the Temple vestibule and the altar of burnt offering. The holiest intercessory position — priests standing in the gap.
  • v.20 — "the northern army" — Hebrew "tsephoni." Locusts typically invaded from the south; this army comes from the north (like Assyria/Babylon). May indicate a shift from literal locusts to prophetic/eschatological army.
  • v.23 — "the former rain moderately" (KJV) / "the early rain for your vindication" (ESV) — Hebrew "moreh litsedaqah" can mean "teacher of righteousness" or "early rain for righteousness." Some see a Messianic hint: the "Teacher of Righteousness."
  • v.28 — "afterward" — Hebrew "acharei-ken" = after these things. Pentecost is the initial fulfillment; the complete fulfillment extends to "the great and terrible day of the LORD" (v.31).

Reflection

  • 1. "Rend your heart, not your garments" (v.13). External religion is easy. Internal transformation is painful. God is not impressed by your church attendance, your tears in worship, or your religious gestures. He wants a TORN HEART — broken, surrendered, exposed before Him. What are you tearing?
  • 2. "I will restore the years the locust has eaten" (v.25). Whatever years sin, addiction, bad decisions, or tragedy have stolen — God can RESTORE them. Not just give you a fresh start, but COMPENSATE for what was lost. Bring your destroyed years to Him. He is the God of restoration.
  • 3. The Spirit is for ALL flesh (v.28). Not just clergy. Not just men. Not just the educated. Sons AND daughters. Young AND old. Servants AND free. The Spirit's outpouring breaks every social barrier. Whatever category you're in, the Spirit is for you.
  • 4. "Whosoever shall call" (v.32). The simplest gospel formula in Scripture. No prerequisites. No performance threshold. No waiting period. CALL on the name of the LORD, and you SHALL be saved. Have you called?
  • 5. "Who can endure it?" (v.11). The answer: no one in their own strength. But v.32 provides the way: CALL on God. The unanswerable terror of judgment is answered by the gracious availability of salvation.
