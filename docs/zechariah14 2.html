Zechariah — Chapter 14

1Behold, a day is coming for the LORD, when the spoil taken from you will be divided in your midst.

2For I will gather all the nations against Jerusalem to battle, and the city shall be taken and the houses plundered and the women raped. Half of the city shall go out into exile, but the rest of the people shall not be cut off from the city.

3Then the LORD will go out and fight against those nations as when he fights on a day of battle.

4On that day his feet shall stand on the Mount of Olives that lies before Jerusalem on the east, and the Mount of Olives shall be split in two from east to west by a very wide valley, so that one half of the Mount shall move northward, and the other half southward.

5And you shall flee to the valley of my mountains, for the valley of the mountains shall reach to Azal. And you shall flee as you fled from the earthquake in the days of Uzziah king of Judah. Then the LORD my God will come, and all the holy ones with him.

6On that day there shall be no light, cold, or frost.

7And there shall be a unique day, which is known to the LORD, neither day nor night, but at evening time there shall be light.

8On that day living waters shall flow out from Jerusalem, half of them to the eastern sea and half of them to the western sea. It shall continue in summer as in winter.

9And the LORD will be king over all the earth. On that day the LORD will be one and his name one.

10The whole land shall be turned into a plain from Geba to Rimmon south of Jerusalem. But Jerusalem shall remain aloft on its site from the Gate of Benjamin to the place of the former gate, to the Corner Gate, and from the Tower of Hananel to the king’s winepresses.

11And it shall be inhabited, for there shall never again be a decree of utter destruction. Jerusalem shall dwell in security.

12And this shall be the plague with which the LORD will strike all the peoples that wage war against Jerusalem: their flesh will rot while they are still standing on their feet, their eyes will rot in their sockets, and their tongues will rot in their mouths.

13And on that day a great panic from the LORD shall fall on them, so that each will seize the hand of another, and the hand of the one will be raised against the hand of the other.

14Even Judah will fight at Jerusalem. And the wealth of all the surrounding nations shall be collected, gold, silver, and garments in great abundance.

15And a plague like this plague shall fall on the horses, the mules, the camels, the donkeys, and whatever beasts may be in those camps.

16Then everyone who survives of all the nations that have come against Jerusalem shall go up year after year to worship the King, the LORD of hosts, and to keep the Feast of Booths.

17And if any of the families of the earth do not go up to Jerusalem to worship the King, the LORD of hosts, there will be no rain on them.

18And if the family of Egypt does not go up and present themselves, then on them there shall be no rain; there shall be the plague with which the LORD afflicts the nations that do not go up to keep the Feast of Booths.

19This shall be the punishment to Egypt and the punishment to all the nations that do not go up to keep the Feast of Booths.

20And on that day there shall be inscribed on the bells of the horses, “Holy to the LORD.” And the pots in the house of the LORD shall be as the bowls before the altar.

21And every pot in Jerusalem and Judah shall be holy to the LORD of hosts, so that all who sacrifice may come and take of them and boil the meat of the sacrifice in them. And there shall no longer be a trader in the house of the LORD of hosts on that day.

Summary

The Day of the LORD culminates with all nations gathered against Jerusalem, the LORD standing on the Mount of Olives, living waters flowing from the city, and every survivor worshipping the King — "The LORD will be king over all the earth."

Key Verses

v.4
"And his feet shall stand in that day upon the mount of Olives, which is before Jerusalem on the east, and the mount of Olives shall cleave in the midst thereof" (KJV) / "On that day his feet shall stand on the Mount of Olives that lies before Jerusalem on the east, and the Mount of Olives shall be split in two" (ESV) — THE climactic verse of Zechariah. The LORD returns PHYSICALLY, BODILY to the exact location from which Jesus ascended (Acts 1:11-12). The Mount splits east-to-west, creating a great valley. This is literal, physical, geographical — the Creator of mountains returns to reshape one.
v.5
"the LORD my God shall come, and all the saints with thee" (KJV) / "Then the LORD my God will come, and all the holy ones with him" (ESV) — Not alone but with "all the holy ones" — the redeemed, the angels, the hosts of heaven. Compare Revelation 19:14: the armies of heaven following the King of kings on white horses.
v.8
"living waters shall go out from Jerusalem; half of them toward the former sea, and half of them toward the hinder sea: in summer and in winter shall it be" (KJV) — Living (flowing) water from Jerusalem to both the Dead Sea (east) and the Mediterranean (west) — year-round, never-ceasing. Compare Ezekiel 47:1-12 and Revelation 22:1-2. The river of life flows from God's throne.
v.9
"And the LORD shall be king over all the earth: in that day shall there be one LORD, and his name one" (KJV) — The ultimate fulfillment of every kingdom prophecy. Universal, uncontested, eternal kingship. One God. One name. No rivals. No competition. No idolatry. The Shema (Deut 6:4) finally fully realized on earth.
v.16
"every one that is left of all the nations... shall even go up from year to year to worship the King, the LORD of hosts, and to keep the feast of tabernacles" (KJV) — The surviving nations worship God and celebrate Tabernacles (Sukkot) — the feast of God dwelling with His people. The nations CHOOSE to worship or face consequences (no rain, v.17).
v.20
"In that day shall there be upon the bells of the horses, HOLINESS UNTO THE LORD; and the pots in the LORD'S house shall be like the bowls before the altar" (KJV) — The sacred/secular divide is erased. Horse bells (the most common, mundane object) bear the same inscription as the high priest's turban (Exod 28:36). Cooking pots are as holy as altar bowls. ALL of life is worship.

Cross-References

  • v.4 — Acts 1:11-12 — "This same Jesus... shall so come in like manner" — from the Mount of Olives. v.4 — Revelation 19:11-16 — The return of Christ on a white horse, King of kings and Lord of lords. v.5 — 1 Thessalonians 3:13 — "The coming of our Lord Jesus Christ with all his saints." v.5 — Jude 14 — "The Lord cometh with ten thousands of his saints." v.8 — Ezekiel 47:1-12 — Living waters flowing from the Temple, bringing life wherever they go. v.8 — Revelation 22:1-2 — The river of the water of life, flowing from the throne of God and of the Lamb. v.9 — Revelation 11:15 — "The kingdoms of this world are become the kingdoms of our Lord, and of his Christ." v.9 — Deuteronomy 6:4 — "The LORD our God is one LORD" — the Shema fully realized. v.20 — Exodus 28:36 — "HOLINESS TO THE LORD" — originally on the high priest's forehead alone. Now on everything.

Translations

  • v.4 — "Mount of Olives" — Hebrew "Har haZetim" = the Mountain of Olives. East of Jerusalem, separated by the Kidron Valley. Jesus taught there, wept there, was arrested at its base (Gethsemane), and ascended from its summit. He will return to the same spot.
  • v.5 — "saints" (KJV) / "holy ones" (ESV) — Hebrew "qedoshim" = holy ones. Could refer to angels, redeemed humans, or both. The NT suggests both: angels (Matt 25:31) and the redeemed (Rev 19:14, 1 Thess 3:13).
  • v.8 — "living waters" — Hebrew "mayim chayyim" = flowing, running water (as opposed to stagnant cistern water). In John 7:38, Jesus applies this image to the Holy Spirit: "rivers of living water shall flow."
  • v.9 — "one LORD, and his name one" — This echoes the Shema (Deut 6:4). In the kingdom, there will be no competing deities, no rival faiths, no polytheism. God alone will be acknowledged universally as the one true God.
  • v.20 — "bells of the horses" — Hebrew "metsillot hassusim" = the metal pieces/bells on horse harnesses. The most ordinary, everyday object now bears the holiest inscription. This is the end of the sacred/secular divide.
  • v.21 — "Canaanite" (KJV) / "trader" (ESV) — Hebrew "kena'ani" can mean either "Canaanite" (pagan inhabitant) or "trader/ merchant" (Canaanites were known as traders). The ESV reading suggests no more commercial exploitation in God's house — compare Jesus cleansing the Temple (Matt 21:12-13).

Reflection

  • 1. "His feet shall stand on the Mount of Olives" (v.4). Jesus is coming back. Not metaphorically, not spiritually — His FEET will touch the same mountain from which He ascended. This is the Christian's ultimate hope: the physical, visible, bodily return of Jesus Christ to reign on earth. Live in light of His return.
  • 2. "The LORD shall be king over all the earth" (v.9). One day, every rival authority will be dethroned. Every false god will be silenced. Every knee will bow. You are on the winning side. In a world of chaos and competing claims, remember: the outcome is settled. God wins. Completely.
  • 3. "Living waters shall go out from Jerusalem" (v.8). From God's presence flows LIFE — continuously, in every season. In your summer and in your winter, the living water flows. Stay close to the source. Let God's river refresh you year-round.
  • 4. "HOLINESS UNTO THE LORD" on the bells of horses (v.20). The end-goal of redemption is not that some things become holy while others remain secular — it is that EVERYTHING becomes holy. Your work, your cooking, your commute — all of it can bear the inscription "Holy to the Lord." Live ALL of life as worship.
  • 5. The nations must CHOOSE to worship (vv.16-17). Even in the Millennial Kingdom, worship is a choice with consequences. Nations that refuse face drought. Today, the choice is the same: worship the King willingly and receive life — or refuse and experience barrenness. Choose today whom you will serve.
