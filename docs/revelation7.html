Summary

An interlude: 144,000 from Israel are sealed for protection, and a great multitude from every nation stands before the throne in white robes — "these are they which came out of great tribulation, and have washed their robes in the blood of the Lamb."

Authorship & Background

Author: The Apostle John (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).
Historical Context: Chapter 7 is an interlude between the sixth and seventh seals — answering the question of 6:17: "Who shall be able to stand?" Answer: those sealed by God. Two groups are presented: 144,000 sealed from Israel (vv.1-8) and a great innumerable multitude from all nations (vv.9-17). This chapter provides assurance: even during judgment, God protects His own.
The 144,000 Sealed (vv.1-8): Four angels hold back destructive winds (v.1). Another angel with God's seal commands: don't harm the earth until God's servants are sealed (vv.2-3). 144,000 sealed — 12,000 from each of 12 tribes of Israel (vv.4-8). Interpretations: (1) literal Jewish believers preserved during the tribulation, (2) symbolic of the complete church (spiritual Israel), (3) Jewish evangelists during the tribulation.
The Great Multitude (vv.9-17): An innumerable multitude from every nation, in white robes with palm branches (v.9). They cry: "Salvation to our God... and unto the Lamb" (v.10). Angels, elders, and creatures worship (vv.11-12). Identity: "These are they which came out of great tribulation, and have washed their robes... in the blood of the Lamb" (v.14). Their reward: God's presence, no more hunger/thirst/heat, the Lamb as shepherd, tears wiped away (vv.15-17).

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): enduringword.com Guzik covers the sealing of the 144,000, the identity debate, the great multitude, and the promises of eternal comfort.
  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "God Shall Wipe Away All Tears" on v.17 (MTP Vol. 15, No. 886) "God shall wipe away ALL tears — not some, all. Every tear of grief, of loss, of pain, of loneliness, of injustice — wiped away. And wiped by GOD HIMSELF — not an angel, not a servant — God's own hand on your face, removing every trace of sorrow. This is the tenderness of the Almighty — the God who made galaxies stoops to wipe tears from His children's eyes."

Reflection

  • 1. "Sealed the servants of our God" (v.3). God protects His own even in judgment. Are you sealed — marked as His? Do you live with the confidence of divine protection?
  • 2. "A great multitude, which no man could number" (v.9). Heaven will be vastly diverse — every nation, language, people. Does your life and witness contribute to this multitude?
  • 3. "Came out of great tribulation" (v.14). These saints endured the worst and came through. Whatever you're facing, you can come through it too — washed in the Lamb's blood.
  • 4. "God shall wipe away all tears" (v.17). Every sorrow has an expiration date. God Himself will personally remove every tear. Does this hope sustain you in current grief?
  • 5. "The Lamb... shall feed them, and shall lead them" (v.17). The Lamb is your shepherd — feeding, leading, satisfying. Are you following Him to living waters?
