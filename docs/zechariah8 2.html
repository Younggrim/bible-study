Zechariah — Chapter 8

1And the word of the LORD of hosts came, saying,

2“Thus says the LORD of hosts: I am jealous for Zion with great jealousy, and I am jealous for her with great wrath.

3Thus says the LORD: I have returned to Zion and will dwell in the midst of Jerusalem, and Jerusalem shall be called the faithful city, and the mountain of the LORD of hosts, the holy mountain.

4Thus says the LORD of hosts: Old men and old women shall again sit in the streets of Jerusalem, each with staff in hand because of great age.

5And the streets of the city shall be full of boys and girls playing in its streets.

6Thus says the LORD of hosts: If it is marvelous in the sight of the remnant of this people in those days, should it also be marvelous in my sight, declares the LORD of hosts?

7Thus says the LORD of hosts: Behold, I will save my people from the east country and from the west country,

8and I will bring them to dwell in the midst of Jerusalem. And they shall be my people, and I will be their God, in faithfulness and in righteousness.”

9Thus says the LORD of hosts: “Let your hands be strong, you who in these days have been hearing these words from the mouth of the prophets who were present on the day that the foundation of the house of the LORD of hosts was laid, that the temple might be built.

10For before those days there was no wage for man or any wage for beast, neither was there any safety from the foe for him who went out or came in, for I set every man against his neighbor.

11But now I will not deal with the remnant of this people as in the former days, declares the LORD of hosts.

12For there shall be a sowing of peace. The vine shall give its fruit, and the ground shall give its produce, and the heavens shall give their dew. And I will cause the remnant of this people to possess all these things.

13And as you have been a byword of cursing among the nations, O house of Judah and house of Israel, so will I save you, and you shall be a blessing. Fear not, but let your hands be strong.”

14For thus says the LORD of hosts: “As I purposed to bring disaster to you when your fathers provoked me to wrath, and I did not relent, says the LORD of hosts,

15so again have I purposed in these days to bring good to Jerusalem and to the house of Judah; fear not.

16These are the things that you shall do: Speak the truth to one another; render in your gates judgments that are true and make for peace;

17do not devise evil in your hearts against one another, and love no false oath, for all these things I hate, declares the LORD.”

18And the word of the LORD of hosts came to me, saying,

19“Thus says the LORD of hosts: The fast of the fourth month and the fast of the fifth and the fast of the seventh and the fast of the tenth shall be to the house of Judah seasons of joy and gladness and cheerful feasts. Therefore love truth and peace.

20“Thus says the LORD of hosts: Peoples shall yet come, even the inhabitants of many cities.

21The inhabitants of one city shall go to another, saying, ‘Let us go at once to entreat the favor of the LORD and to seek the LORD of hosts; I myself am going.’

22Many peoples and strong nations shall come to seek the LORD of hosts in Jerusalem and to entreat the favor of the LORD.

23Thus says the LORD of hosts: In those days ten men from the nations of every tongue shall take hold of the robe of a Jew, saying, ‘Let us go with you, for we have heard that God is with you.’”

Summary

God promises to return to Zion with jealous love — old men and women will sit in Jerusalem's streets while children play, fasts will become feasts, and nations will seek the LORD because "God is with you."

Key Verses

v.3
"Jerusalem shall be called a city of truth; and the mountain of the LORD of hosts the holy mountain" (KJV) — God's future Jerusalem is defined by TRUTH and HOLINESS. Its identity is not political or military but moral and spiritual. What a city is called reflects God's ultimate purpose for it.
v.5
"the streets of the city shall be full of boys and girls playing in the streets thereof" (KJV) — A portrait of perfect peace and security. Children playing freely in the streets means no war, no poverty, no fear. This is God's vision for human flourishing — the simple joy of safe children at play.
v.6
"If it be marvellous in the eyes of the remnant... should it also be marvellous in mine eyes?" (KJV) — What seems impossible to you is ROUTINE for God. Your astonishment at His promises reflects your smallness, not His limitations. "Is anything too hard for the LORD?" (Gen 18:14).
v.13
"as ye were a curse among the heathen... so will I save you, and ye shall be a blessing" (KJV) — A complete reversal. What was a byword of shame becomes a name of blessing. God transforms Israel's reputation as He transforms their reality.
v.19
"The fast of the fourth month, and the fast of the fifth, and the fast of the seventh, and the fast of the tenth, shall be to the house of Judah joy and gladness, and cheerful feasts" (KJV) — The answer to chapter 7's question. Not only will the fasts cease — they will become CELEBRATIONS. Mourning transformed to joy.
v.23
"ten men shall take hold out of all languages of the nations... of the skirt of him that is a Jew, saying, We will go with you: for we have heard that God is with you" (KJV) — The nations clinging to Israel because God's presence is unmistakable. This foreshadows the gospel going to all nations through Jewish apostles.

Cross-References

  • v.3 — Revelation 21:2-3 — The holy city, New Jerusalem, where God dwells with His people. v.5 — Isaiah 11:6-9 — The peaceable kingdom where children are safe even among wild animals. v.6 — Genesis 18:14 — "Is any thing too hard for the LORD?" v.7 — Isaiah 43:5-6 — "I will bring thy seed from the east, and gather thee from the west." v.8 — Jeremiah 31:33 — "I will be their God, and they shall be my people" — the covenant formula. v.13 — Genesis 12:2-3 — "I will bless thee... and thou shalt be a blessing." v.16 — Ephesians 4:25 — "Putting away lying, speak every man truth with his neighbour." v.23 — Isaiah 2:2-3 — "Many people shall go and say, Come ye, and let us go up to the mountain of the LORD."

Translations

  • v.2 — "jealous" — Hebrew "qana" = zealous, jealous with burning passion. God's jealousy for Zion is not petty envy but fierce protective love. He will fight for what is His.
  • v.6 — "marvellous" — Hebrew "yippale" = wonderful, extraordinary, hard to believe. The root "pala" is used of God's miracles. What is "too wonderful" for man is ordinary for God.
  • v.12 — "seed of peace" (KJV) / "sowing of peace" (ESV) — Hebrew "zera hashalom." Some translations read this as the vine being called "peaceful" or that peace will be sown. The agricultural metaphor: God sows peace as a seed that produces a harvest of prosperity.
  • v.23 — "ten men" — A round number representing completeness. From EVERY language — universality. The ratio (ten Gentiles to one Jew) shows the overwhelming Gentile response to God's work through Israel.

Reflection

  • 1. God's vision includes PLAY and REST (v.5). His picture of the blessed future is not all work and worship — it includes children playing. God values joy, leisure, and human flourishing. Don't reduce Christian life to duty alone. God wants His streets filled with laughter.
  • 2. "Should it also be marvellous in MY eyes?" (v.6). Stop limiting God by your own sense of what's possible. Your impossibility is His Tuesday morning. If the promise seems too good to be true, remember: you're measuring by human standards. God is not limited by your measurements.
  • 3. "Fear not, let your hands be strong" (vv.13, 15). Faith produces action. God says: I WILL bless you — therefore work with confidence. Don't wait for blessing to arrive before you act. Work in faith, knowing God has purposed good for you.
  • 4. "Love truth and peace" (v.19). These are the twin marks of God's people. Not truth without peace (harshness). Not peace without truth (compromise). Both together — the mark of mature spiritual community.
  • 5. "God is with you" (v.23). The ultimate testimony that draws the nations is not our programs or our arguments but the manifest PRESENCE of God among His people. Live in such a way that others say: "God is clearly with you — let us come too."
