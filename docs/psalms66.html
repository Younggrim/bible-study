Psalms — Chapter 66

1Shout for joy to God, all the earth;

2sing the glory of his name; give to him glorious praise!

3Say to God, “How awesome are your deeds! So great is your power that your enemies come cringing to you.

4All the earth worships you and sings praises to you; they sing praises to your name.” Selah

5Come and see what God has done: he is awesome in his deeds toward the children of man.

6He turned the sea into dry land; they passed through the river on foot. There did we rejoice in him,

7who rules by his might forever, whose eyes keep watch on the nations— let not the rebellious exalt themselves. Selah

8Bless our God, O peoples; let the sound of his praise be heard,

9who has kept our soul among the living and has not let our feet slip.

10For you, O God, have tested us; you have tried us as silver is tried.

11You brought us into the net; you laid a crushing burden on our backs;

12you let men ride over our heads; we went through fire and through water; yet you have brought us out to a place of abundance.

13I will come into your house with burnt offerings; I will perform my vows to you,

14that which my lips uttered and my mouth promised when I was in trouble.

15I will offer to you burnt offerings of fattened animals, with the smoke of the sacrifice of rams; I will make an offering of bulls and goats. Selah

16Come and hear, all you who fear God, and I will tell what he has done for my soul.

17I cried to him with my mouth, and high praise was on my tongue.

18If I had cherished iniquity in my heart, the Lord would not have listened.

19But truly God has listened; he has attended to the voice of my prayer.

20Blessed be God, because he has not rejected my prayer or removed his steadfast love from me!

Summary

A communal thanksgiving psalm — 'Make a joyful noise unto God, all ye lands'; the psalmist recounts God's mighty works (the Red Sea crossing), acknowledges testing like silver refined, and offers praise for answered prayer.

Key Verses

v.10
"For thou, O God, hast proved us: thou hast tried us, as silver is tried." (KJV) — God's testing is purposeful — like a silversmith refining metal. The fire is not punishment but purification. God puts His people through trials to remove impurities and prove genuineness.
v.12
"We went through fire and through water: but thou broughtest us out into a wealthy place." (KJV) — The pattern of biblical suffering: THROUGH fire, THROUGH water, but OUT into abundance. God doesn't promise to keep us FROM trials but to bring us THROUGH them to something better.
v.18
"If I regard iniquity in my heart, the Lord will not hear me." (KJV) — A sobering condition for prayer. "Regard" (ra'ah) = to look upon with favor, to cherish. The issue is not sinlessness but harboring sin — clinging to known sin while asking God to listen.
v.20
"Blessed be God, which hath not turned away my prayer, nor his mercy from me." (KJV) — The joyful conclusion: God DID hear, because the psalmist DID NOT cherish sin. The link between v.18 and v.20 is the key: clean conscience = heard prayer.

Translations

  • v.1 — KJV: "Make a joyful noise" / ESV: "Shout for joy" — Hebrew "hari'u" = shout, blast (like a trumpet). Both capture the exuberance; ESV's "shout for joy" is more natural in modern English.
  • v.3 — KJV: "How terrible art thou in thy works" / ESV: "How awesome are your deeds" — "Terrible" (nora) in 1611 meant "inspiring awe/terror." Modern "terrible" means "bad." ESV's "awesome" (in its original sense) is more accurate for modern readers.
  • v.12 — KJV: "a wealthy place" / ESV: "a place of abundance" — Hebrew "revayah" = abundance, overflow, saturation. ESV is clearer; the point is overflow and abundance, not merely material wealth.

Reflection

  • 1. God's testing is refining, not punishing (v.10). Silver is tried by fire not to destroy it but to purify it. When God allows trials, He is working toward a purer you, not a destroyed you.
  • 2. The path to abundance goes THROUGH fire and water (v.12). There is no shortcut to the "wealthy place." The through-ness of trial is the pathway to blessing. Don't demand exemption from the process.
  • 3. Cherished sin blocks prayer (v.18). The issue is not perfection but direction. If you are clinging to known sin — defending it, hiding it, refusing to release it — don't expect God to respond to your prayers. Let it go, and the line is clear again.
  • 4. Testimony is powerful (v.16). "Come and hear... what he hath done for my soul." Personal testimony of God's faithfulness is one of the most convincing forms of witness. Tell your story.
