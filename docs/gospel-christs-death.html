The Gospel

Christ's Death

The Truth

The death of Christ is the central event of human history — the moment when God's holiness and God's love converged in a single act of breathtaking grace. On the cross, the sinless Son of God took upon Himself the penalty that our sins deserved. He died in our place, as our substitute, bearing our judgment so that we might go free.

The great exchange of the gospel is summarized in 2 Corinthians 5:21: God made Christ, who had no sin, to be sin for us — so that in Him we might become the righteousness of God. Our sin was transferred to Christ; His righteousness is transferred to us. He got what we deserved (death); we get what He deserved (life and acceptance before God).

Christ's death was not a tragic accident or a noble martyrdom — it was a deliberate, predetermined act of substitutionary love. Jesus said no one took His life from Him; He laid it down voluntarily. The cross was the Father's plan, the Son's mission, and humanity's only hope — the righteous dying for the unrighteous to bring us to God.

Key Scriptures

  • 1 Peter 3:18 — "Christ suffered once for sins, the righteous for the unrighteous, to bring you to God."
  • Isaiah 53:5-6 — "He was pierced for our transgressions, he was crushed for our iniquities; the punishment that brought us peace was on him."
  • 2 Corinthians 5:21 — "God made him who had no sin to be sin for us, so that in him we might become the righteousness of God."
  • Romans 5:6-8 — "At just the right time, when we were still powerless, Christ died for the ungodly."
  • Galatians 3:13 — "Christ redeemed us from the curse of the law by becoming a curse for us."

Why It Matters

Christ's death matters because without it, we have no way to God. Our sin created an unbridgeable gap between us and a holy God. No amount of good works, religious rituals, or moral improvement could span that chasm. Only the death of the perfect God-man — fully divine and fully human — could satisfy divine justice and open the way for sinners to approach a holy God.

The cross also reveals the full measure of both God's wrath against sin and His love for sinners. He did not minimize sin or pretend it does not matter — He poured out its full penalty on Christ. And He did not abandon sinners to their deserved fate — He took their place. Both truths must be held together to understand the gospel rightly.

Reflection Questions

  • How does the substitutionary nature of Christ's death affect your sense of gratitude and worship?
  • What does it mean to you that Christ died voluntarily — choosing the cross for you?
  • How does the cross demonstrate both God's justice and His mercy simultaneously?
