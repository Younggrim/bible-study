Formal equivalent (1901). Very literal, precise. Good for word studies.

Study Bible with extensive translator notes. Balances accuracy and readability.

Exodus — Chapter 4

1Then Moses answered, “But behold, they will not believe me or listen to my voice, for they will say, ‘The LORD did not appear to you.’”

2The LORD said to him, “What is that in your hand?” He said, “A staff.”

3And he said, “Throw it on the ground.” So he threw it on the ground, and it became a serpent, and Moses ran from it.

4But the LORD said to Moses, “Put out your hand and catch it by the tail”—so he put out his hand and caught it, and it became a staff in his hand—

5“that they may believe that the LORD, the God of their fathers, the God of Abraham, the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob, has appeared to you.”

6Again, the LORD said to him, “Put your hand inside your cloak.” And he put his hand inside his cloak, and when he took it out, behold, his hand was leprous like snow.

7Then God said, “Put your hand back inside your cloak.” So he put his hand back inside his cloak, and when he took it out, behold, it was restored like the rest of his flesh.

8“If they will not believe you,” God said, “or listen to the first sign, they may believe the latter sign.

9If they will not believe even these two signs or listen to your voice, you shall take some water from the Nile and pour it on the dry ground, and the water that you shall take from the Nile will become blood on the dry ground.”

10But Moses said to the LORD, “Oh, my Lord, I am not eloquent, either in the past or since you have spoken to your servant, but I am slow of speech and of tongue.”

11Then the LORD said to him, “Who has made man’s mouth? Who makes him mute, or deaf, or seeing, or blind? Is it not I, the LORD?

12Now therefore go, and I will be with your mouth and teach you what you shall speak.”

13But he said, “Oh, my Lord, please send someone else.”

14Then the anger of the LORD was kindled against Moses and he said, “Is there not Aaron, your brother, the Levite? I know that he can speak well. Behold, he is coming out to meet you, and when he sees you, he will be glad in his heart.

15You shall speak to him and put the words in his mouth, and I will be with your mouth and with his mouth and will teach you both what to do.

16He shall speak for you to the people, and he shall be your mouth, and you shall be as God to him.

17And take in your hand this staff, with which you shall do the signs.”

18Moses went back to Jethro his father-in-law and said to him, “Please let me go back to my brothers in Egypt to see whether they are still alive.” And Jethro said to Moses, “Go in peace.”

19And the LORD said to Moses in Midian, “Go back to Egypt, for all the men who were seeking your life are dead.”

20So Moses took his wife and his sons and had them ride on a donkey, and went back to the land of Egypt. And Moses took the staff of God in his hand.

21And the LORD said to Moses, “When you go back to Egypt, see that you do before Pharaoh all the miracles that I have put in your power. But I will harden his heart, so that he will not let the people go.

22Then you shall say to Pharaoh, ‘Thus says the LORD, Israel is my firstborn son,

23and I say to you, “Let my son go that he may serve me.” If you refuse to let him go, behold, I will kill your firstborn son.’”

24At a lodging place on the way the LORD met him and sought to put him to death.

25Then Zipporah took a flint and cut off her son’s foreskin and touched Moses’ feet with it and said, “Surely you are a bridegroom of blood to me!”

26So he let him alone. It was then that she said, “A bridegroom of blood,” because of the circumcision.

27The LORD said to Aaron, “Go into the wilderness to meet Moses.” So he went and met him at the mountain of God and kissed him.

28And Moses told Aaron all the words of the LORD with which he had sent him to speak, and all the signs that he had commanded him to do.

29Then Moses and Aaron went and gathered together all the elders of the people of Israel.

30Aaron spoke all the words that the LORD had spoken to Moses and did the signs in the sight of the people.

31And the people believed; and when they heard that the LORD had visited the people of Israel and that he had seen their affliction, they bowed their heads and worshiped.

Summary

God gives Moses three signs to confirm his calling, appoints Aaron as his spokesman, and Moses returns to Egypt.

Key Verses

  • v.2 — "What is that in your hand?" (ESV) — God uses what you already have.
  • v.11 — "Who has made man's mouth? Who makes him mute, or deaf, or seeing, or blind? Is it not I, the LORD?" (ESV) — God is sovereign over all human ability and disability.
  • v.13 — "Oh, my Lord, please send someone else." (ESV) — Moses' bare refusal — and God's anger.
  • v.22 — "Israel is my firstborn son." (ESV) — God's declaration of Israel's identity and His fatherly love.
  • v.31 — "And the people believed... they bowed their heads and worshiped." (ESV) — Faith leads to worship.

  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "What Is That in Thine Hand?" on v.2 (MTP Vol. 9, No. 495) "What is that in thine hand? A rod — a shepherd's stick. Nothing special. Nothing impressive. But God said 'Cast it on the ground' — and it became the rod of God. The rod of miracles. The rod that split the sea. What is in YOUR hand? A pen? A hammer? A broom? A Bible? Whatever it is — cast it before God. Surrender it to Him. And He will make it the instrument of His power. God does not ask for what you don't have. He asks for what you DO have — and transforms it."

Translations

  • v.2 → KJV: "rod" / ESV: "staff" — Hebrew "matteh" — a staff, a shepherd's rod. The ESV uses "staff"; the KJV uses "rod." Both are valid. It becomes "the rod of God" (v.20) — ordinary tools transformed by divine commission.
  • v.10 → KJV: "slow of speech, and of a slow tongue" / ESV: "slow of speech and of tongue" — Hebrew "kevad peh ukhvad lashon" — heavy of mouth and heavy of tongue. Both translations capture the sense. Not necessarily a stutter — possibly a lack of fluency in Egyptian after 40 years, or genuine difficulty in public speaking.
  • v.13 → KJV: "by the hand of him whom thou wilt send" / ESV: "by the hand of him whom you will send" — Hebrew "beyad tishlach" — literally "by the hand you will send." Both are identical. A polite refusal disguised as deference: "Send whoever you're going to send — just not me."
  • v.21 → KJV: "I will harden his heart" / ESV: "I will harden his heart" — Hebrew "achazzeq et libbo" — I will strengthen/make firm his heart. Both translations identical. The hardening is complex: sometimes God hardens (4:21, 9:12), sometimes Pharaoh hardens himself (8:15, 8:32).
  • v.22 → KJV: "my firstborn" / ESV: "my firstborn son" — Hebrew "beni bekhori" — my son, my firstborn. The ESV adds "son" for clarity. A declaration of special relationship, inheritance rights, and protection.
  • v.24 → KJV: "sought to kill him" / ESV: "sought to put him to death" — Hebrew "vayevaqesh hamito" — sought to put him to death. The ESV is more literal. Terrifying and mysterious — Moses cannot represent the covenant while neglecting its sign (circumcision).
  • v.25 → KJV: "a bloody husband" / ESV: "a bridegroom of blood" — Hebrew "chatan damim" — a bridegroom/husband of blood(s). The ESV's "bridegroom" is more precise than the KJV's "husband" for the Hebrew "chatan." Zipporah's words express horror at the bloody rite.
  • ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB) - v.2 — ASV: "A rod" / NET: "A staff" / WEB: "A rod" — NET consistently uses "staff" where ASV/WEB retain "rod" (Hebrew "matteh"). NET's choice loses the connection to Aaron's "rod" that later buds (Numbers 17). - v.10 — ASV: "Oh, Lord, I am not eloquent... I am slow of speech, and of a slow tongue" — ASV addresses "Jehovah" but uses "Lord" (Adonai) here, showing Moses switches to the submissive title. WEB uniquely addresses "Yahweh" while also saying "O Lord" — preserving both the divine name context and the respectful address. - v.13 — NET: "please send anyone else whom you wish to send!" — NET's expansive paraphrase makes Moses' reluctance more explicit; WEB simplifies: "please send someone else"; ASV retains the literal Hebrew idiom "by the hand of him whom thou wilt send." - v.24 — WEB: "Yahweh met Moses and wanted to kill him" — uniquely interpretive; "wanted to kill" softens the Hebrew "sought to kill" (ASV) into intention rather than action. NET: "sought to kill him" matches ASV's literalness. - v.25 — NET: "touched it to Moses' feet" — NET interprets the ambiguous "cast it at his feet" (ASV/WEB) as deliberate contact, a significant exegetical choice for this notoriously difficult passage.

David Guzik — Enduring Word Teaching Series (Exodus)

Verse-by-verse audio/video teaching through Exodus.

Reflection

  • 1. "What is that in thine hand?" (v.2). God asks for what you already have — not what you don't. What has God placed in your hand? Surrender it to Him and watch Him transform it.
  • 2. "Who hath made man's mouth?" (v.11). Your limitations are not God's limitations. Stop using your weaknesses as excuses. The God who made you can empower you for whatever He calls you to do.
  • 3. "Send... by the hand of him whom thou wilt send" (v.13). Moses' final objection is simply "I don't want to go." Have you ever said no to God? His anger burned — but He still used Moses. God is patient, but resistance has consequences (Aaron's involvement created problems later).
  • 4. "Israel is my son, even my firstborn" (v.22). God calls you His child. You are not an orphan, not a slave — you are a son/daughter of the Most High. Live from that identity.
  • 5. The bridegroom of blood (vv.24-26): Moses couldn't lead God's covenant people while neglecting God's covenant sign. Is there an area of obedience you're neglecting while trying to serve God in other areas? Partial obedience is not obedience.
