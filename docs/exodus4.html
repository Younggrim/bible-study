1And Moses answered and said, But, behold, they will not believe me, nor hearken unto my voice: for they will say, The LORD hath not appeared unto thee.

2And the LORD said unto him, What is that in thine hand? And he said, A rod.

3And he said, Cast it on the ground. And he cast it on the ground, and it became a serpent; and Moses fled from before it.

4And the LORD said unto Moses, Put forth thine hand, and take it by the tail. And he put forth his hand, and caught it, and it became a rod in his hand:

5That they may believe that the LORD God of their fathers, the God of Abraham, the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob, hath appeared unto thee.

6And the LORD said furthermore unto him, Put now thine hand into thy bosom. And he put his hand into his bosom: and when he took it out, behold, his hand was leprous as snow.

7And he said, Put thine hand into thy bosom again. And he put his hand into his bosom again; and plucked it out of his bosom, and, behold, it was turned again as his other flesh.

8And it shall come to pass, if they will not believe thee, neither hearken to the voice of the first sign, that they will believe the voice of the latter sign.

9And it shall come to pass, if they will not believe also these two signs, neither hearken unto thy voice, that thou shalt take of the water of the river, and pour it upon the dry land: and the water which thou takest out of the river shall become blood upon the dry land.

10And Moses said unto the LORD, O my Lord, I am not eloquent, neither heretofore, nor since thou hast spoken unto thy servant: but I am slow of speech, and of a slow tongue.

11And the LORD said unto him, Who hath made man’s mouth? or who maketh the dumb, or deaf, or the seeing, or the blind? have not I the LORD?

12Now therefore go, and I will be with thy mouth, and teach thee what thou shalt say.

13And he said, O my Lord, send, I pray thee, by the hand of him whom thou wilt send.

14And the anger of the LORD was kindled against Moses, and he said, Is not Aaron the Levite thy brother? I know that he can speak well. And also, behold, he cometh forth to meet thee: and when he seeth thee, he will be glad in his heart.

15And thou shalt speak unto him, and put words in his mouth: and I will be with thy mouth, and with his mouth, and will teach you what ye shall do.

16And he shall be thy spokesman unto the people: and he shall be, even he shall be to thee instead of a mouth, and thou shalt be to him instead of God.

17And thou shalt take this rod in thine hand, wherewith thou shalt do signs.

18And Moses went and returned to Jethro his father in law, and said unto him, Let me go, I pray thee, and return unto my brethren which are in Egypt, and see whether they be yet alive. And Jethro said to Moses, Go in peace.

19And the LORD said unto Moses in Midian, Go, return into Egypt: for all the men are dead which sought thy life.

20And Moses took his wife and his sons, and set them upon an ass, and he returned to the land of Egypt: and Moses took the rod of God in his hand.

21And the LORD said unto Moses, When thou goest to return into Egypt, see that thou do all those wonders before Pharaoh, which I have put in thine hand: but I will harden his heart, that he shall not let the people go.

22And thou shalt say unto Pharaoh, Thus saith the LORD, Israel is my son, even my firstborn:

23And I say unto thee, Let my son go, that he may serve me: and if thou refuse to let him go, behold, I will slay thy son, even thy firstborn.

24And it came to pass by the way in the inn, that the LORD met him, and sought to kill him.

25Then Zipporah took a sharp stone, and cut off the foreskin of her son, and cast it at his feet, and said, Surely a bloody husband art thou to me.

26So he let him go: then she said, A bloody husband thou art, because of the circumcision.

27And the LORD said to Aaron, Go into the wilderness to meet Moses. And he went, and met him in the mount of God, and kissed him.

28And Moses told Aaron all the words of the LORD who had sent him, and all the signs which he had commanded him.

29And Moses and Aaron went and gathered together all the elders of the children of Israel:

30And Aaron spake all the words which the LORD had spoken unto Moses, and did the signs in the sight of the people.

31And the people believed: and when they heard that the LORD had visited the children of Israel, and that he had looked upon their affliction, then they bowed their heads and worshipped.

Summary

God gives Moses three signs to confirm his calling, appoints Aaron as his spokesman, and Moses returns to Egypt.

Authorship & Background

Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).
Historical Context: Chapter 4 continues Moses' dialogue with God at the burning bush. Moses raises four more objections (adding to "Who am I?" from 3:11): "They will not believe me" (v.1), "I am not eloquent" (v.10), and finally the bare refusal: "Send someone else" (v.13). God answers each objection with provision — signs, Aaron as spokesman — but His anger burns at Moses' final resistance. The chapter also contains the mysterious "bridegroom of blood" incident (vv.24-26), God's declaration of Israel as His firstborn son (v.22), and the first mention of God hardening Pharaoh's heart (v.21). Moses finally obeys, meets Aaron, and the elders of Israel believe and worship.
Moses' Objections and God's Signs (vv.1-9): Objection: "They will not believe me" (v.1). God gives three signs: (1) The rod becomes a serpent and back (vv.2-5); (2) Moses' hand becomes leprous then healed (vv.6-7); (3) Water from the Nile becomes blood (v.9). Each sign demonstrates God's power over nature, disease, and Egypt's gods.
Moses' Speech Objection (vv.10-12): "I am slow of speech, and of a slow tongue" (v.10). God's answer: "Who hath made man's mouth?... have not I the LORD?" (v.11). "I will be with thy mouth" (v.12). The Creator of the mouth can certainly empower it.
Moses' Final Refusal (vv.13-17): "Send, I pray thee, by the hand of him whom thou wilt send" — send anyone but me (v.13). God's anger kindles (v.14). He appoints Aaron as Moses' spokesman (vv.14-16). Moses will be "instead of God" to Aaron; Aaron will be Moses' "mouth" (v.16). Moses takes "the rod of God" (v.17).
The Journey to Egypt (vv.18-23): Moses departs Midian (v.18). God tells him those who sought his life are dead (v.19). God warns: "I will harden his heart" (v.21). God declares: "Israel is my son, even my firstborn" (v.22) — and warns Pharaoh: refuse, and "I will slay thy son, even thy firstborn" (v.23). The tenth plague is announced before the first.
The Bridegroom of Blood (vv.24-26): A mysterious, terrifying incident. God "sought to kill" Moses (v.24). Zipporah circumcises their son and touches Moses' feet with the foreskin: "A bloody husband art thou to me" (v.25). God relents (v.26). Moses had neglected circumcision — the sign of the covenant he was sent to uphold.
Reunion and Belief (vv.27-31): God sends Aaron to meet Moses (v.27). Moses tells Aaron everything (v.28). They gather the elders, perform the signs (vv.29-30). "The people believed... they bowed their heads and worshipped" (v.31).

Map & Geography

  • Horeb/Sinai: Moses is still at the mountain of God, continuing the burning bush dialogue from chapter 3.
  • Midian (v.18): Moses returns to Jethro before departing for Egypt.
  • Egypt (v.19-20): Moses takes his family and journeys back to Egypt, the land he fled 40 years earlier.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): enduringword.com Guzik covers Moses' escalating objections, God's patient provision of signs, the significance of "Israel is my firstborn," and the difficult "bridegroom of blood" passage.
  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "What Is That in Thine Hand?" on v.2 (MTP Vol. 9, No. 495) "What is that in thine hand? A rod — a shepherd's stick. Nothing special. Nothing impressive. But God said 'Cast it on the ground' — and it became the rod of God. The rod of miracles. The rod that split the sea. What is in YOUR hand? A pen? A hammer? A broom? A Bible? Whatever it is — cast it before God. Surrender it to Him. And He will make it the instrument of His power. God does not ask for what you don't have. He asks for what you DO have — and transforms it."
  • Sermon: "Who Made Man's Mouth?" on v.11 (MTP Vol. 31, No. 1860) "Who hath made man's mouth? The God who made the instrument can certainly play it. Moses says 'I cannot speak' — God says 'I made your mouth.' Your inability is no obstacle to omnipotence. Stop looking at your limitations and look at your God."

Exodus 4 - I AM Your Calling

Reflection

  • 1. "What is that in thine hand?" (v.2). God asks for what you already have — not what you don't. What has God placed in your hand? Surrender it to Him and watch Him transform it.
  • 2. "Who hath made man's mouth?" (v.11). Your limitations are not God's limitations. Stop using your weaknesses as excuses. The God who made you can empower you for whatever He calls you to do.
  • 3. "Send... by the hand of him whom thou wilt send" (v.13). Moses' final objection is simply "I don't want to go." Have you ever said no to God? His anger burned — but He still used Moses. God is patient, but resistance has consequences (Aaron's involvement created problems later).
  • 4. "Israel is my son, even my firstborn" (v.22). God calls you His child. You are not an orphan, not a slave — you are a son/daughter of the Most High. Live from that identity.
  • 5. The bridegroom of blood (vv.24-26): Moses couldn't lead God's covenant people while neglecting God's covenant sign. Is there an area of obedience you're neglecting while trying to serve God in other areas? Partial obedience is not obedience.
