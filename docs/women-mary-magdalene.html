Women of the Bible

Mary Magdalene

Her Story

Mary Magdalene's life before meeting Jesus was one of torment — Luke records that seven demons had been cast out of her. We can only imagine the suffering, isolation, and hopelessness that characterized her existence before deliverance came. When Jesus freed her, the transformation was so complete that she became one of His most devoted followers, traveling with Him and supporting His ministry from her own resources.

Mary Magdalene's devotion to Jesus was tested at the cross. While most of the male disciples fled in fear, Mary remained, watching her Lord suffer and die. Her presence at Calvary was not passive — it was an act of courage and loyalty that defied the danger of being associated with an executed criminal. Love kept her there when fear drove others away.

On resurrection morning, Mary Magdalene was the first person to whom the risen Christ appeared. She had gone to the tomb in grief, found it empty, and wept in confusion. When Jesus spoke her name — "Mary" — she recognized Him instantly. He then commissioned her to carry the most important message in human history to the other disciples: "I have seen the Lord." The woman once tormented by demons became the first evangelist of the resurrection.

Key Scriptures

  • Luke 8:2 — Mary Magdalene, from whom seven demons had gone out.
  • John 20:1-2 — Early on the first day of the week, Mary Magdalene went to the tomb and saw the stone removed.
  • John 20:16-18 — Jesus said to her, "Mary." She turned and said, "Rabboni!" ... "I have seen the Lord."
  • Mark 16:9 — He appeared first to Mary Magdalene, out of whom he had driven seven demons.
  • Luke 7:47 — "Her many sins have been forgiven — as her great love has shown."

Lessons from Her Life

Mary Magdalene teaches us that those who have been delivered from the most become the most devoted. Her seven-demon deliverance was not her shame — it was her testimony. The depth of her gratitude was proportional to the depth of her former bondage. When we truly understand what God has rescued us from, half-hearted devotion becomes impossible. Great deliverance produces great love.

Mary also shows us that faithful presence — simply showing up — matters more than we realize. She did not preach at the cross or attempt dramatic rescue. She simply stayed. And because she stayed, she was first to witness the resurrection. God honors those who remain faithful in the dark hours, even when they cannot do anything except be present. The reward for faithful presence is often the privilege of seeing God move first.

Reflection Questions

  • What has God delivered you from? How does remembering your deliverance fuel your devotion?
  • Are you willing to stay present in dark, painful situations where you cannot fix anything?
  • How does Mary's story encourage you that God often uses the unlikely to carry His most important messages?
