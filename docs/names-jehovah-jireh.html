Names of God

Jehovah Jireh

Understanding Jehovah Jireh

Jehovah Jireh — "The LORD Will Provide" — is the name Abraham gave to the place on Mount Moriah where God provided a ram as a substitute sacrifice for his son Isaac (Genesis 22:14). The Hebrew word "jireh" (yireh) means "to see" or "to provide," carrying the beautiful implication that God sees our needs before we even express them, and He provides according to His perfect timing and wisdom.

The story behind this name is one of the most dramatic in all of Scripture. God tested Abraham by commanding him to sacrifice his only son — the son of promise, the child he had waited 25 years to receive. Abraham obeyed in faith, trusting that God would somehow provide, even telling Isaac, "God himself will provide the lamb." At the very moment of sacrifice, God stayed Abraham's hand and revealed a ram caught in a thicket — His provision perfectly timed, not a moment early and not a moment late.

This name foreshadows God's ultimate provision: His own Son, Jesus Christ, the Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world. Just as God provided a substitute for Isaac on Mount Moriah, so He provided His Son as our substitute on a cross just outside Jerusalem. Jehovah Jireh is not merely the God who provides bread and shelter — He is the God who provided the ultimate sacrifice at the ultimate cost.

Key Scriptures

  • Genesis 22:14 — "Abraham called that place The LORD Will Provide. And to this day it is said, 'On the mountain of the LORD it will be provided.'"
  • Philippians 4:19 — "My God will meet all your needs according to the riches of his glory in Christ Jesus." God's provision flows from limitless resources.
  • Matthew 6:31-33 — "Do not worry... your heavenly Father knows that you need them. But seek first his kingdom." Jesus teaches trust in Jehovah Jireh.
  • Psalm 23:1 — "The LORD is my shepherd, I lack nothing." David's personal testimony of God's unfailing provision throughout his life.
  • Romans 8:32 — "He who did not spare his own Son... how will he not also, along with him, graciously give us all things?" The logic of divine provision.

What This Name Means for Us

Jehovah Jireh assures us that God is never caught off guard by our needs. He sees ahead — He sees the trial before it arrives, He sees the need before it becomes urgent, and He has already prepared His provision. This does not mean we will never experience want or difficulty, but it means that in every circumstance, God has a plan and His timing is perfect. He often provides at the last moment, not because He is slow, but because He is teaching us to trust.

This name also challenges our tendency toward anxiety and self-reliance. If Jehovah Jireh has promised to provide, then our worry is ultimately a question of faith: do we believe He will do what He has promised? The testimony of Scripture and the testimony of countless believers throughout history confirm that He always provides — sometimes miraculously, sometimes through ordinary means, but always faithfully and always sufficiently.

Reflection Questions

  • Can you recall a time when God provided for you at the last moment? How did that experience strengthen your trust in Jehovah Jireh?
  • What need in your life right now requires you to trust that "on the mountain of the LORD it will be provided"?
  • How does knowing that God provided His own Son as the ultimate sacrifice change how you view your smaller daily needs?
  • In what ways might worry and anxiety reveal areas where you are not fully trusting Jehovah Jireh?
