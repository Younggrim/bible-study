Isaiah — Chapter 53

1Who has believed what he has heard from us? And to whom has the arm of the LORD been revealed?

2For he grew up before him like a young plant, and like a root out of dry ground; he had no form or majesty that we should look at him, and no beauty that we should desire him.

3He was despised and rejected by men, a man of sorrows and acquainted with grief; and as one from whom men hide their faces he was despised, and we esteemed him not.

4Surely he has borne our griefs and carried our sorrows; yet we esteemed him stricken, smitten by God, and afflicted.

5But he was pierced for our transgressions; he was crushed for our iniquities; upon him was the chastisement that brought us peace, and with his wounds we are healed.

6All we like sheep have gone astray; we have turned—every one—to his own way; and the LORD has laid on him the iniquity of us all.

7He was oppressed, and he was afflicted, yet he opened not his mouth; like a lamb that is led to the slaughter, and like a sheep that before its shearers is silent, so he opened not his mouth.

8By oppression and judgment he was taken away; and as for his generation, who considered that he was cut off out of the land of the living, stricken for the transgression of my people?

9And they made his grave with the wicked and with a rich man in his death, although he had done no violence, and there was no deceit in his mouth.

10Yet it was the will of the LORD to crush him; he has put him to grief; when his soul makes an offering for guilt, he shall see his offspring; he shall prolong his days; the will of the LORD shall prosper in his hand.

11Out of the anguish of his soul he shall see and be satisfied; by his knowledge shall the righteous one, my servant, make many to be accounted righteous, and he shall bear their iniquities.

12Therefore I will divide him a portion with the many, and he shall divide the spoil with the strong, because he poured out his soul to death and was numbered with the transgressors; yet he bore the sin of many, and makes intercession for the transgressors.

Summary

The crown jewel of Old Testament prophecy — the Suffering Servant. Despised and rejected, a man of sorrows, acquainted with grief. He was wounded for OUR transgressions, bruised for OUR iniquities. The chastisement of OUR peace was upon Him, and with His stripes WE are healed. The LORD laid on Him the iniquity of us all. Cut off from the living, buried with the rich, yet He sees His offspring and the pleasure of the LORD prospers in His hand.

Key Verses

v.3
"He is despised and rejected of men; a man of sorrows, and acquainted with grief: and we hid as it were our faces from him; he was despised, and we esteemed him not" (KJV) — The summary of Christ's earthly reception: despised, rejected, sorrowful, grief-experienced. We turned away.
v.4
"Surely he hath borne our griefs, and carried our sorrows: yet we did esteem him stricken, smitten of God, and afflicted" (KJV) — The substitution: HE bore what was OURS. Yet the observers assumed God was punishing HIM for His own sins. They misread the cross completely.
v.5
"But he was wounded for our transgressions, he was bruised for our iniquities: the chastisement of our peace was upon him; and with his stripes we are healed" (KJV) — The most explicit atonement statement in the OT. Five key words: wounded, bruised, chastisement, stripes, healed. All HIS suffering; all OUR benefit.
v.6
"All we like sheep have gone astray; we have turned every one to his own way; and the LORD hath laid on him the iniquity of us all" (KJV) — Universal guilt ("ALL we... every ONE"), universal remedy ("iniquity of us ALL"). The LORD Himself placed our sin on the Servant.
v.7
"He was oppressed, and he was afflicted, yet he opened not his mouth: he is brought as a lamb to the slaughter, and as a sheep before her shearers is dumb, so he openeth not his mouth" (KJV) — SILENCE. Jesus before Pilate and Herod: not defending Himself, not protesting, not resisting. Willing submission to slaughter.
v.9
"And he made his grave with the wicked, and with the rich in his death" (KJV) — Crucified with criminals (wicked), buried in Joseph of Arimathea's tomb (rich). Two specific details fulfilled precisely (Luke 23:32-33; Matt 27:57-60).
v.10
"Yet it pleased the LORD to bruise him... he shall see his seed, he shall prolong his days, and the pleasure of the LORD shall prosper in his hand" (KJV) — AFTER death: He sees offspring and has prolonged days. RESURRECTION. The dead Servant lives again and bears fruit.
v.11
"He shall see of the travail of his soul, and shall be satisfied" (KJV) — Christ will LOOK at the fruit of His suffering and be SATISFIED. Your salvation satisfies Jesus. He considers you worth the price.
v.12
"he was numbered with the transgressors; and he bare the sin of many, and made intercession for the transgressors" (KJV) — Three final descriptions: numbered among sinners (Luke 22:37), bore sin for many, and INTERCEDES — present tense (Hebrews 7:25). He's still praying for you.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/isaiah-53/
  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "The Death of Christ" (MTP Vol. 7, No. 386): "'With his stripes we are healed.' It is DONE. Not 'shall be healed' — ARE healed. The atonement is accomplished, the price is paid, the healing is complete. Take it, believe it, rest in it."

Translations

  • v.2 — "tender plant... root out of a dry ground" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "yoneq" (sucker/sprout) and "shoresh" (root). The Messiah's humble origins: from a seemingly dead line (David's stump) in an unpromising environment (Nazareth).
  • v.5 — "wounded" (KJV) / "pierced" (ESV) — Hebrew "mecholal" = pierced through, fatally wounded. The same word used for being run through with a weapon. Fulfilled on the cross.
  • v.5 — "bruised" (KJV) / "crushed" (ESV) — Hebrew "medukka" = crushed to powder, pulverized. Not surface injury but deep, comprehensive damage. The weight of sin crushed Him.
  • v.7 — "lamb to the slaughter" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "seh la-tevach" = a sheep to the slaughter. Echoes the Passover lamb (Exodus 12), the ram of Isaac (Genesis 22), and John's "Lamb of God" (John 1:29).
  • v.10 — "offering for sin" (KJV) / "guilt offering" (ESV) — Hebrew "asham" = the guilt/trespass offering (Leviticus 5). The Servant IS the sacrifice — not animal but human, not involuntary but willing.
  • v.11 — "justify many" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "yatsdiq... la-rabbim" = declare righteous the many. Legal language: the Servant's suffering creates a legal basis for declaring sinners RIGHTEOUS.

Reflection

  • 1. "He was wounded for OUR transgressions" (v.5). The pronouns are everything: HE — OUR. HIS suffering — OUR healing. Substitution is personal: He took YOUR specific sins, YOUR specific punishment, YOUR specific guilt. Make it personal.
  • 2. "All we like sheep have gone astray" (v.6). Universal guilt: ALL of us. No one is exempt. "Every one to his OWN way" — sin is self-direction, choosing your path over God's. But the LORD laid it ALL on Him.
  • 3. "He opened not his mouth" (v.7). Innocent yet silent. No defense, no self-justification, no protest. If the innocent Christ didn't demand His rights, what does that say about our insistence on defending ourselves?
  • 4. "He shall see his seed... and be satisfied" (vv.10-11). YOUR salvation satisfies Jesus. He looks at the fruit of His suffering and says "worth it." You are not a disappointing return on His investment — you are His JOY.
  • 5. "He made intercession for the transgressors" (v.12). Jesus is praying for you RIGHT NOW. Not "once prayed" — MAKES intercession, present tense. Whatever you're facing, Christ is actively advocating for you before the Father.
