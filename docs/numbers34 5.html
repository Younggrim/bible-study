Numbers — Chapter 34

1The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

2“Command the people of Israel, and say to them, When you enter the land of Canaan (this is the land that shall fall to you for an inheritance, the land of Canaan as defined by its borders),

3your south side shall be from the wilderness of Zin alongside Edom, and your southern border shall run from the end of the Salt Sea on the east.

4And your border shall turn south of the ascent of Akrabbim, and cross to Zin, and its limit shall be south of Kadesh-barnea. Then it shall go on to Hazar-addar, and pass along to Azmon.

5And the border shall turn from Azmon to the Brook of Egypt, and its limit shall be at the sea.

6“For the western border, you shall have the Great Sea and its coast. This shall be your western border.

7“This shall be your northern border: from the Great Sea you shall draw a line to Mount Hor.

8From Mount Hor you shall draw a line to Lebo-hamath, and the limit of the border shall be at Zedad.

9Then the border shall extend to Ziphron, and its limit shall be at Hazar-enan. This shall be your northern border.

10“You shall draw a line for your eastern border from Hazar-enan to Shepham.

11And the border shall go down from Shepham to Riblah on the east side of Ain. And the border shall go down and reach to the shoulder of the Sea of Chinnereth on the east.

12And the border shall go down to the Jordan, and its limit shall be at the Salt Sea. This shall be your land as defined by its borders all around.”

13Moses commanded the people of Israel, saying, “This is the land that you shall inherit by lot, which the LORD has commanded to give to the nine tribes and to the half-tribe.

14For the tribe of the people of Reuben by fathers’ houses and the tribe of the people of Gad by their fathers’ houses have received their inheritance, and also the half-tribe of Manasseh.

15The two tribes and the half-tribe have received their inheritance beyond the Jordan east of Jericho, toward the sunrise.”

16The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

17“These are the names of the men who shall divide the land to you for inheritance: Eleazar the priest and Joshua the son of Nun.

18You shall take one chief from every tribe to divide the land for inheritance.

19These are the names of the men: Of the tribe of Judah, Caleb the son of Jephunneh.

20Of the tribe of the people of Simeon, Shemuel the son of Ammihud.

21Of the tribe of Benjamin, Elidad the son of Chislon.

22Of the tribe of the people of Dan a chief, Bukki the son of Jogli.

23Of the people of Joseph: of the tribe of the people of Manasseh a chief, Hanniel the son of Ephod.

24And of the tribe of the people of Ephraim a chief, Kemuel the son of Shiphtan.

25Of the tribe of the people of Zebulun a chief, Elizaphan the son of Parnach.

26Of the tribe of the people of Issachar a chief, Paltiel the son of Azzan.

27And of the tribe of the people of Asher a chief, Ahihud the son of Shelomi.

28Of the tribe of the people of Naphtali a chief, Pedahel the son of Ammihud.”

29These are the men whom the LORD commanded to divide the inheritance for the people of Israel in the land of Canaan.

Summary

God defines the exact boundaries of the Promised Land and appoints leaders to divide it among the tribes.

Key Verses

v.2
"This is the land that shall fall to you for an inheritance, the land of Canaan as defined by its borders." (ESV) — "Shall fall to you" — the land is God's gift, not Israel's achievement. It "falls" to them by divine allotment.
v.6
"Ye shall even have the great sea for a border." — The Mediterranean as western boundary. God's grant is generous — from desert to sea.
v.12
"This shall be your land with the coasts thereof round about." — Defined, bounded, specific. God's promises are not vague — they have precise dimensions.
v.13
"This is the land that you shall inherit by lot." (ESV) — Lot = divine determination. The location of each tribe's inheritance is God's decision, not human negotiation.
v.19
"Of the tribe of Judah, Caleb the son of Jephunneh." — Forty years after his faithful report, Caleb oversees the land distribution. Faithfulness is eventually vindicated.

Cross-References

  • v.2 — Genesis 15:18-21 — God's original land promise to Abraham (even larger boundaries: "from the river of Egypt to the Euphrates"). v.3 — Joshua 15:1-4 — Judah's southern boundary follows this description. v.5 — Genesis 15:18 — "The river of Egypt" — Wadi el-Arish, the traditional southern boundary. v.6 — Joshua 15:12 — "The Great Sea" as western boundary. v.8 — 1 Kings 8:65 — Solomon's kingdom extends to "the entrance of Hamath" — the northern boundary fulfilled. v.8 — 2 Kings 14:25 — Jeroboam II restores borders to "the entrance of Hamath." v.11 — Luke 5:1 — The Sea of Chinnereth = Sea of Galilee = Lake Gennesaret. Jesus' primary ministry region. v.13 — Joshua 14-19 — The actual land distribution under Joshua. v.17 — Numbers 27:18-23 — Joshua's commissioning — now he will execute the distribution. v.19 — Numbers 13:6, 30; 14:24 — Caleb the faithful spy — his reward is finally realized. v.19 — Joshua 14:6-15 — Caleb claims Hebron at age 85: "Give me this mountain." v.29 — Joshua 19:51 — "They made an end of dividing the country."

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/numbers-34/ Guzik emphasizes that God's promises are specific, not vague. The borders are defined — God knows exactly what He is giving. He notes that Israel never fully occupied these boundaries until Solomon, and even then only briefly. The full realization awaits the Messianic Kingdom.
  • Charles Spurgeon: "God draws the borders of your inheritance. Not one acre more, not one acre less than He intends. Your portion is measured by divine wisdom, bounded by divine love, and secured by divine power. You need not covet your neighbor's field — your lot is fallen in pleasant places. And mark this: Caleb, who believed God forty years ago when all others doubted, now stands as overseer of the distribution. Faith is never wasted. What you trusted God for in your youth, you will see fulfilled in your age."

Translations

  • v.2 — KJV: "the land that shall fall unto you for an inheritance" / ESV: "the land that shall fall to you for an inheritance" — Hebrew "ha-aretz asher tippol lakhem b'nachalah." "Tippol" (from "naphal" — to fall) is used for the casting of lots — the land "falls" to them by divine allotment. Both translations preserve this idiom.
  • v.3 — KJV: "your south quarter" / ESV: "your south side" — Hebrew "pe'at negev." "Pe'ah" means side, corner, edge. KJV's "quarter" and ESV's "side" both work — it refers to the entire southern boundary region.
  • v.5 — KJV: "the border shall fetch a compass from Azmon unto the river of Egypt" / ESV: "the border shall turn from Azmon to the Brook of Egypt" — Hebrew "v'nasav ha-g'vul." "Nasav" means to turn, go around. KJV's archaic "fetch a compass" means "make a turn" — ESV's "turn" is clearer. Also: KJV "river of Egypt" vs. ESV "Brook of Egypt" — Hebrew "nachal Mitzrayim." "Nachal" is a wadi (seasonal stream), not a river (nahar). ESV's "Brook" is more accurate — this is Wadi el-Arish, not the Nile.
  • v.7 — KJV: "ye shall point out for you mount Hor" / ESV: "you shall draw a line to Mount Hor" — Hebrew "teta'u lakhem." "Ta'ah" here means to mark out, designate. ESV's "draw a line" is clearer. This Mount Hor is in the north (Lebanon region), not the southern Mount Hor where Aaron died.
  • v.11 — KJV: "the side of the sea of Chinnereth" / ESV: "the shoulder of the Sea of Chinnereth" — Hebrew "katef yam Kinneret." "Katef" means shoulder — used geographically for a ridge or slope beside a body of water. The Sea of Chinnereth is the Sea of Galilee (from "kinnor" — harp, describing its shape).
  • v.18 — KJV: "ye shall take one prince of every tribe" / ESV: "You shall take one chief from every tribe" — Hebrew "nasi echad." "Nasi" means prince, chief, leader. ESV's "chief" is more accessible; KJV's "prince" carries more formal authority. These are tribal representatives with authority to make binding decisions about land allocation.

Reflection

  • 1. God draws the borders (vv.1-12). Your inheritance is defined by God — not too much, not too little. He knows exactly what He has allotted to you. Rest in His wisdom rather than coveting another's portion. "The lines are fallen unto me in pleasant places" (Psalm 16:6).
  • 2. Specific promises, not vague hopes (v.12). God does not say "somewhere nice" — He gives coordinates. His promises to you are equally specific: eternal life, the Holy Spirit, daily provision, ultimate glorification. These are not wishes but guarantees with defined boundaries.
  • 3. Caleb at the table (v.19). Forty years of waiting, and now Caleb oversees the distribution. Faithfulness is vindicated — not always quickly, but always certainly. If you trusted God when others doubted, your vindication is coming.
  • 4. The land "falls" to them (v.2). The Hebrew verb for lot-casting — it "falls." You do not seize your inheritance; it falls to you by God's sovereign choice. Your place in God's plan is not earned but given.
  • 5. Israel never fully occupied these borders. God's grant was generous — Israel's obedience was partial. How much of what God has promised you remains unclaimed because of incomplete obedience or insufficient faith?
  • 6. Leaders appointed for distribution (vv.17-28). God provides structure for receiving His gifts. Inheritance requires administration — leaders who ensure fairness and order. God's generosity works through human organization.
