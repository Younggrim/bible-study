Ten Commandments

You Shall Not Commit Adultery

The Commandment

The seventh commandment, "You shall not commit adultery" (Exodus 20:14), protects the sacred covenant of marriage and the trust that forms its foundation. In God's design, marriage is a one-flesh union — a reflection of His own faithful, covenant love for His people. Adultery does not merely break a social contract; it shatters a sacred bond, wounds the deepest trust two people can share, and ripples destruction through families and communities.

Throughout the Old Testament, marriage fidelity serves as a metaphor for Israel's relationship with God. When the prophets accuse Israel of spiritual adultery, they draw on the seventh commandment to illustrate the devastating pain of covenant betrayal. Hosea's marriage to unfaithful Gomer becomes a living parable of God's heartbreak over His people's idolatry. The commandment thus speaks on two levels — protecting human marriages while pointing to the deeper fidelity God desires.

Jesus deepened this commandment beyond the physical act to the condition of the heart: "Everyone who looks at a woman with lustful intent has already committed adultery with her in his heart" (Matthew 5:27-28). The New Testament calls believers to sexual purity not as mere moral achievement but as an expression of their new identity — temples of the Holy Spirit whose bodies belong to the Lord and to their covenant partner.

Key Scriptures

  • Exodus 20:14 — "You shall not commit adultery."
  • Matthew 5:27-28 — "You have heard that it was said, 'You shall not commit adultery.' But I say to you that everyone who looks at a woman with lustful intent has already committed adultery with her in his heart."
  • Hebrews 13:4 — "Let marriage be held in honor among all, and let the marriage bed be undefiled."
  • 1 Corinthians 6:18-20 — "Flee from sexual immorality... You are not your own, for you were bought with a price. So glorify God in your body."
  • Proverbs 5:15-19 — "Drink water from your own cistern, flowing water from your own well... Let her breasts fill you at all times with delight."

Heart of the Law

The heart of this commandment is faithfulness — the choice to honor one's covenant commitments even when desire, opportunity, or dissatisfaction tempt us otherwise. It calls us to view marriage not as a consumer relationship (evaluated by what it gives us) but as a covenant relationship (defined by what we have promised). Faithfulness in marriage is training ground for faithfulness to God, and vice versa.

In a culture saturated with sexual imagery and casual attitudes toward intimacy, this commandment calls believers to a radically different vision of sexuality — one rooted in commitment, trust, and self-giving love rather than self-gratification. It challenges us to guard not only our bodies but our eyes, minds, and hearts. The goal is not repression but rightly-ordered desire — sexuality experienced as God designed it: within the safety and permanence of covenant love.

Reflection Questions

  • What guardrails do you have in place to protect the purity of your heart and mind in a culture that normalizes sexual compromise?
  • How does understanding marriage as a reflection of God's covenant love change the way you view faithfulness?
  • In what ways might emotional intimacy with someone outside your marriage (or future marriage) be a form of unfaithfulness?
