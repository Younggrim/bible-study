Names of God

Yahweh

Understanding Yahweh

Yahweh is the most sacred and personal name of God in the Hebrew Bible, revealed to Moses at the burning bush in Exodus 3:14 when God declared, "I AM WHO I AM." The Hebrew tetragrammaton YHWH (יהוה) was considered so holy that ancient Israelites would not pronounce it aloud, substituting "Adonai" (Lord) in its place. This name appears over 6,800 times in the Old Testament, making it the most frequently used proper name for God.

The name Yahweh is derived from the Hebrew verb "hayah," meaning "to be" or "to exist." It communicates God's self-existence — He is not contingent on anything or anyone else for His being. He simply IS. He is the uncaused cause, the eternal present, the God who was and is and is to come. Unlike every other being in the universe, Yahweh does not derive His existence from another source; He is existence itself.

Yahweh is distinctly the covenant name of God. It is the name by which He bound Himself to Israel in faithful, steadfast love (hesed). When God uses this name, He is speaking as the One who makes promises and keeps them, who enters into relationship and never abandons His people. It is intimate, personal, and relational — not a distant philosophical concept but a God who draws near, speaks, and acts on behalf of those He loves.

Key Scriptures

  • Exodus 3:14 — "God said to Moses, 'I AM WHO I AM.'" The foundational revelation of God's eternal, self-existent nature.
  • Psalm 83:18 — "That they may know that you alone, whose name is the LORD, are the Most High over all the earth." Yahweh's name is supreme above all.
  • Isaiah 42:8 — "I am the LORD; that is my name! I will not yield my glory to another." God's jealous protection of His unique identity.
  • Deuteronomy 6:4 — "Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God, the LORD is one." The Shema — Israel's central confession of Yahweh's singular lordship.
  • Revelation 1:8 — "'I am the Alpha and the Omega,' says the Lord God, 'who is, and who was, and who is to come, the Almighty.'" The eternal I AM revealed in fullness.

What This Name Means for Us

Knowing God as Yahweh means we are in relationship with a God who is eternally faithful. His covenant promises do not expire, and His character does not change. In a world of shifting circumstances, broken promises, and unreliable people, Yahweh stands as the one constant — the "I AM" who is the same yesterday, today, and forever. We can build our lives on His word because He is incapable of failing.

The name Yahweh also invites us into intimacy. This is not a God who remains hidden behind philosophical abstractions. He revealed His personal name because He desires to be known. When we call upon Yahweh, we are calling upon the God who stooped down to speak to a fugitive shepherd at a burning bush, who heard the cries of slaves in Egypt, and who continues to hear and respond to the prayers of His people today.

Reflection Questions

  • What does it mean to you personally that God revealed His name — that He wants to be known by you?
  • How does the unchanging nature of Yahweh ("I AM") provide stability in seasons of uncertainty or change in your life?
  • In what ways have you experienced God's covenant faithfulness even when your own faithfulness has wavered?
  • How does understanding Yahweh as a relational, covenant-keeping God change the way you approach prayer?
