Ten Commandments

No Other Gods

The Commandment

The first commandment, "You shall have no other gods before Me" (Exodus 20:3), stands as the foundation upon which all other commandments rest. Spoken by God to Israel at Mount Sinai after their deliverance from Egypt, this command establishes the exclusive nature of the relationship between Yahweh and His people. In the ancient Near East, where polytheism was the norm and nations collected deities like trophies, God's declaration was radical — there is one God, and He alone deserves the totality of human devotion.

This commandment is not merely a prohibition against worshiping carved statues; it strikes at the heart of human loyalty. Anything that occupies the supreme place in our affections — career, relationships, wealth, pleasure, or even religious performance — can become a "god before Me." The Hebrew phrase suggests not merely having gods "besides" Yahweh, but placing anything "before His face," in His presence, as a rival to His rightful place.

Jesus affirmed this commandment as the greatest of all when He declared, "You shall love the Lord your God with all your heart, with all your soul, and with all your mind" (Matthew 22:37). The New Testament deepens the first commandment from external compliance to internal allegiance — God desires not just the absence of idol worship but the fullness of devoted love.

Key Scriptures

  • Exodus 20:3 — "You shall have no other gods before Me."
  • Matthew 22:37-38 — "You shall love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your mind. This is the great and first commandment."
  • Deuteronomy 6:4-5 — "Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God, the LORD is one. You shall love the LORD your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your might."
  • 1 Corinthians 8:6 — "Yet for us there is one God, the Father, from whom are all things and for whom we exist."
  • 1 John 5:21 — "Little children, keep yourselves from idols."

Heart of the Law

The heart of the first commandment is not restriction but relationship. God is not a jealous tyrant demanding obedience; He is a loving Father who knows that divided loyalties will always lead to disappointment and destruction. Every idol promises what only God can deliver — security, identity, significance, and satisfaction. When we place anything above God, we inevitably discover that false gods demand everything and deliver nothing.

Living under the first commandment means conducting a regular inventory of our hearts. It means asking honest questions about what we fear losing most, what we think about when our minds are idle, what we would be devastated to live without. These answers reveal where our true worship lies. The call is not to white-knuckle obedience but to a reordering of loves — to discover that when God occupies His rightful place, everything else finds its proper order beneath Him.

Reflection Questions

  • What competes most fiercely for first place in your heart — comfort, approval, security, or control?
  • How would your daily decisions change if you truly believed God alone could satisfy your deepest needs?
  • In what ways does modern culture make it easy to have "other gods" without recognizing them as such?
