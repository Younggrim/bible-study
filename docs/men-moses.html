Men of the Bible

Moses

His Story

Moses was born under a death sentence — Pharaoh had ordered the destruction of every Hebrew male infant. Yet God preserved him through the courage of his mother and the compassion of Pharaoh's daughter, placing the future deliverer of Israel in the very household that sought to destroy his people. Raised with the education and privilege of Egyptian royalty, Moses nevertheless identified with his suffering kinsmen, a choice that would cost him everything and gain him a destiny beyond imagining.

After killing an Egyptian taskmaster and fleeing to the wilderness of Midian, Moses spent forty years as a shepherd — a period of preparation that stripped away self-reliance and cultivated the humility God required for the task ahead. At the burning bush, God revealed Himself as "I AM WHO I AM" and commissioned the reluctant shepherd to confront the most powerful ruler on earth. Moses' objections — his lack of eloquence, his fear of rejection — reveal a man who had learned his own inadequacy, which was precisely the qualification God sought.

As deliverer and lawgiver, Moses led Israel through the Red Sea, received the Ten Commandments on Sinai, and interceded for a rebellious people for forty years in the wilderness. Scripture records that God spoke with Moses "face to face, as a man speaks to his friend," a level of intimacy unmatched in the Old Testament. Though he was barred from entering the Promised Land due to his moment of angry disobedience at Meribah, Moses died with his eyes clear and his strength undiminished, having faithfully completed the work God gave him.

Key Scriptures

  • Exodus 3:10-14 — God commissions Moses at the burning bush and reveals His name.
  • Hebrews 11:24-27 — By faith Moses refused the treasures of Egypt, choosing to suffer with God's people.
  • Deuteronomy 34:10 — "There has not arisen a prophet since in Israel like Moses, whom the LORD knew face to face."
  • Numbers 12:3 — "Now the man Moses was very meek, more than all people on the face of the earth."
  • Exodus 33:11 — "The LORD used to speak to Moses face to face, as a man speaks to his friend."

Lessons from His Life

Moses demonstrates that God's preparation often looks nothing like what we expect. Forty years of herding sheep in obscurity seemed like wasted decades, yet they were the crucible that formed a leader humble enough to steward God's power without being corrupted by it. God's delays are not denials — they are formations. The wilderness seasons of our lives may be the most important chapters of our story.

Moses also teaches us the cost and privilege of intercession. When God offered to destroy Israel and make Moses into a great nation, Moses refused the honor and pleaded for mercy on behalf of a people who had repeatedly rejected his leadership. True spiritual authority is not about position or power — it is about standing in the gap between a holy God and a broken people, absorbing the cost of love on behalf of others.

Reflection Questions

  • Are you in a "wilderness season" right now? How might God be using it to prepare you for something ahead?
  • What excuses do you offer God when He calls you to difficult obedience?
  • Who has God placed in your life for you to intercede for, even when they are difficult to love?
