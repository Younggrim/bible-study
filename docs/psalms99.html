Summary

An enthronement psalm — 'The LORD reigneth; let the people tremble'; He is great in Zion, a lover of justice; Moses, Aaron, and Samuel called upon Him and He answered; He is holy (repeated three times).

Key Verses

v.1
"The LORD reigneth; let the people tremble: he sitteth between the cherubims; let the earth be moved." (KJV) — The response to God's reign is TREMBLING, not mere celebration. He is enthroned on the cherubim (Ark of the Covenant imagery) — both accessible (temple) and terrifying (holiness).
v.5
"Exalt ye the LORD our God, and worship at his footstool; for he is holy." (KJV) — His footstool is the Ark of the Covenant — the place where heaven and earth meet. Worship there. And the reason: HE IS HOLY. This refrain defines the psalm.
v.8
"Thou answeredst them, O LORD our God: thou wast a God that forgavest them, though thou tookest vengeance of their inventions." (KJV) — The profound theological balance: God FORGAVE (personally pardoned) AND took vengeance on their wrongdoings (enforced consequences). Forgiveness does not mean absence of all consequences. God is both gracious and just.
v.9
"Exalt the LORD our God, and worship at his holy hill; for the LORD our God is holy." (KJV) — The final, fullest refrain. Not just "he is holy" but "the LORD our God IS holy." His holiness is not a quality He possesses — it is who He IS.

Cross-References

  • v.1 — Psalm 80:1 — "Thou that dwellest between the cherubims, shine forth." Same enthroned-on-cherubim imagery. v.1 — Isaiah 6:1-3 — "Holy, holy, holy, is the LORD of hosts." The triple holiness declaration. v.6 — 1 Samuel 12:18 — Samuel called on the LORD and the LORD answered with thunder. God responding to intercessors. v.6 — Exodus 32:11-14 — Moses interceding and God answering. v.8 — Numbers 20:12 — Moses forgiven but denied entry to Canaan. Grace with consequences. v.8 — Numbers 12:1-15 — Miriam forgiven but leprous seven days. Same principle.

Translations

  • v.3 — KJV: "thy great and terrible name; for it is holy" / ESV: "your great and awesome name! Holy is he!" — ESV restructures to make "Holy is he" an exclamation/refrain. This helps the reader see the triple "holy" structure (vv.3,5,9).
  • v.8 — KJV: "thou tookest vengeance of their inventions" / ESV: "an avenger of their wrongdoings" — Hebrew "alilotheihem" = their deeds/practices/inventions. God forgave Moses and Aaron personally but still enforced consequences for their specific actions (Moses couldn't enter Canaan; Aaron's sons died).

Reflection

  • 1. Holiness should produce trembling (v.1). Not casual familiarity but holy awe. God is accessible through grace — but He is still HOLY. Approach with reverence, not flippancy.
  • 2. God forgives AND allows consequences (v.8). These are not contradictions. A parent can forgive a child's misdeed while still enforcing natural consequences. God pardons sin while allowing its earthly effects to play out. Grace does not erase all consequences.
  • 3. God answers those who call on Him (v.6). Moses called — God answered. Aaron called — God answered. Samuel called — God answered. The pattern: those who call receive answers. Do you call? Do you expect answers?
  • 4. "He is holy" is the foundation of everything (vv.3,5,9). Not just one attribute among many — holiness IS God's essential character. Everything else flows from this. Worship, justice, forgiveness, consequences — all are expressions of His holiness.
