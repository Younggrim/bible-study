Names of God

Jehovah Shalom

Understanding Jehovah Shalom

Jehovah Shalom — "The LORD Is Peace" — is the name Gideon gave to the altar he built after the Angel of the LORD appeared to him and said, "Peace! Do not be afraid. You are not going to die" (Judges 6:23-24). Gideon was terrified because he had seen God face to face, yet God spoke peace into his fear. The Hebrew word "shalom" means far more than the absence of conflict — it means completeness, wholeness, harmony, welfare, and well-being in every dimension of life.

The context of this revelation is significant: Israel was being oppressed by the Midianites, their crops were destroyed, and they lived in fear. Into this chaos and turmoil, God revealed Himself as Shalom — not because the circumstances had changed, but because His presence transforms any situation. Before the battle was fought, before the victory was won, God declared peace over His trembling servant. His peace is not dependent on peaceful circumstances; it is rooted in His unchanging character.

Throughout Scripture, peace is one of God's most distinctive gifts to His people. Jesus — the Prince of Peace (Isaiah 9:6) — said, "Peace I leave with you; my peace I give you. I do not give to you as the world gives" (John 14:27). The world offers temporary truces and fragile cease-fires. Jehovah Shalom offers a peace that guards hearts and minds, that passes understanding, and that remains unshaken even in the midst of life's fiercest storms.

Key Scriptures

  • Judges 6:24 — "So Gideon built an altar to the LORD there and called it The LORD Is Peace." God's peace declared over a fearful, uncertain servant.
  • Isaiah 26:3 — "You will keep in perfect peace those whose minds are steadfast, because they trust in you." Peace is the fruit of fixed trust in God.
  • John 14:27 — "Peace I leave with you; my peace I give you." Jesus bequeaths His own peace — divine shalom — to His disciples.
  • Philippians 4:6-7 — "The peace of God, which transcends all understanding, will guard your hearts and minds." A peace that defies human logic.
  • Romans 15:33 — "The God of peace be with you all." Paul's benediction reminds us that peace is not just a gift — it is God Himself dwelling with us.

What This Name Means for Us

In a world drowning in anxiety, division, and restlessness, Jehovah Shalom offers what nothing else can: a peace that is not contingent on circumstances. This peace does not require that all problems be solved, all relationships be mended, or all questions be answered. It requires only that we fix our minds on the One who IS peace. When we anchor ourselves in His presence, peace flows not from the resolution of our problems but from the reality of His character.

Jehovah Shalom also calls us to be peacemakers. Having received His peace, we are commissioned to carry it into our homes, workplaces, and communities. We extend peace to the anxious, speak peace over the fearful, and pursue reconciliation where there is division. The peace of God in us becomes the peace of God through us — a tangible expression of His kingdom breaking into a restless world.

Reflection Questions

  • What is currently stealing your peace? How might God be inviting you to turn that anxiety over to Jehovah Shalom?
  • Is your peace dependent on your circumstances or on the character of God? What would it look like to have "steadfast" trust?
  • In what relationships or situations is God calling you to be a peacemaker — carrying His shalom into places of conflict?
  • How can you practically "fix your mind" on God throughout a busy, stressful day to access His transcendent peace?
