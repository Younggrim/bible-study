1And the LORD said unto Noah, Come thou and all thy house into the ark; for thee have I seen righteous before me in this generation.

2Of every clean beast thou shalt take to thee by sevens, the male and his female: and of beasts that are not clean by two, the male and his female.

3Of fowls also of the air by sevens, the male and the female; to keep seed alive upon the face of all the earth.

4For yet seven days, and I will cause it to rain upon the earth forty days and forty nights; and every living substance that I have made will I destroy from off the face of the earth.

5And Noah did according unto all that the LORD commanded him.

6And Noah was six hundred years old when the flood of waters was upon the earth.

7And Noah went in, and his sons, and his wife, and his sons’ wives with him, into the ark, because of the waters of the flood.

8Of clean beasts, and of beasts that are not clean, and of fowls, and of every thing that creepeth upon the earth,

9There went in two and two unto Noah into the ark, the male and the female, as God had commanded Noah.

10And it came to pass after seven days, that the waters of the flood were upon the earth.

11In the six hundredth year of Noah’s life, in the second month, the seventeenth day of the month, the same day were all the fountains of the great deep broken up, and the windows of heaven were opened.

12And the rain was upon the earth forty days and forty nights.

13In the selfsame day entered Noah, and Shem, and Ham, and Japheth, the sons of Noah, and Noah’s wife, and the three wives of his sons with them, into the ark;

14They, and every beast after his kind, and all the cattle after their kind, and every creeping thing that creepeth upon the earth after his kind, and every fowl after his kind, every bird of every sort.

15And they went in unto Noah into the ark, two and two of all flesh, wherein is the breath of life.

16And they that went in, went in male and female of all flesh, as God had commanded him: and the LORD shut him in.

17And the flood was forty days upon the earth; and the waters increased, and bare up the ark, and it was lift up above the earth.

18And the waters prevailed, and were increased greatly upon the earth; and the ark went upon the face of the waters.

19And the waters prevailed exceedingly upon the earth; and all the high hills, that were under the whole heaven, were covered.

20Fifteen cubits upward did the waters prevail; and the mountains were covered.

21And all flesh died that moved upon the earth, both of fowl, and of cattle, and of beast, and of every creeping thing that creepeth upon the earth, and every man:

22All in whose nostrils was the breath of life, of all that was in the dry land, died.

23And every living substance was destroyed which was upon the face of the ground, both man, and cattle, and the creeping things, and the fowl of the heaven; and they were destroyed from the earth: and Noah only remained alive, and they that were with him in the ark.

Summary

Noah, his family, and the animals enter the ark; God sends the flood and destroys all life on earth.

Authorship & Background

Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).
Historical Context: Chapter 7 records the flood itself — God's judgment on a corrupt world and His preservation of Noah's family and the animals. The flood is presented as a de-creation: the waters above and below (separated in 1:6-7) come back together, returning the earth to its pre-creation watery state. It is both judgment (destruction of the wicked) and salvation (preservation of the righteous). The NT uses the flood as a type of both baptism (1 Pet 3:20-21) and future judgment (2 Pet 3:5-7).
Entering the Ark (vv.1-10): God invites Noah: "Come thou and all thy house into the ark" (v.1) — "come" not "go" (God is already there). Clean animals by sevens; unclean by twos (v.2). Seven days' warning before the rain (v.4). "Noah did according unto all that the LORD commanded him" (v.5). Noah was 600 years old when the flood came (v.6).
The Flood Begins (vv.11-16): Precise dating: "the six hundredth year of Noah's life, in the second month, the seventeenth day" (v.11). Two sources: "the fountains of the great deep broken up, and the windows of heaven were opened" (v.11). Rain for forty days and nights (v.12). "The LORD shut him in" (v.16) — God Himself closed the door.
The Flood's Devastation (vv.17-24): Waters increased, lifted the ark (v.17). Waters prevailed "exceedingly" — covering all high hills and mountains (vv.19- 20). "All flesh died" — every land creature and every person outside the ark (vv.21-23). "Noah only remained alive, and they that were with him in the ark" (v.23). Waters prevailed 150 days (v.24).

Map & Geography

  • No specific geography — the flood covers the entire earth (vv.19-20).
  • The "fountains of the great deep" and "windows of heaven" (v.11) describe waters from below and above engulfing all known geography.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): enduringword.com Guzik covers the invitation to the ark, the flood's sources, the devastation, and the typological significance.
  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "The LORD Shut Him In" on v.16 (MTP Vol. 11, No. 634) "The LORD shut him in — not Noah. Noah didn't secure his own safety — God did. The same hand that shut Noah IN shut the world OUT. When God closes the door, no one can open it. When God secures you, no one can unsecure you. Your salvation depends not on your grip on the door handle but on God's hand that shut the door. He shut him in — safe, secure, sealed by God Himself." Sermon: "Come Into the Ark" on v.1 (MTP Vol. 20, No. 1201) "COME — not 'go.' God says 'come' because He is already in the ark. He doesn't send Noah away from Himself but invites him toward Himself. Christ says the same: 'Come unto me.' Not 'go somewhere' but 'come to where I am.' The ark is where God is — and He invites you in."

Reflection

  • 1. "Come thou... into the ark" (v.1). God says "come" — toward Himself. Are you responding to His invitation? He's not sending you away; He's inviting you in.
  • 2. "The LORD shut him in" (v.16). God secures your salvation — not you. Your security depends on His hand, not yours. Does this give you confidence?
  • 3. "Noah only remained alive, and they that were with him in the ark" (v.23). There was only one way of salvation — the ark. There is only one way now — Christ. Have you entered?
  • 4. "As the days of Noe were, so shall also the coming of the Son of man be" (Matt 24:37). Jesus compared His return to Noah's flood — sudden judgment on an unprepared world. Are you prepared?
  • 5. Noah obeyed completely (v.5) and was saved. Obedience is the response to grace. God provides the ark (grace); you must enter it (obedience). Have you done both — received grace AND responded with obedience?
