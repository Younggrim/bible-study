Zechariah — Chapter 10

1Ask rain from the LORD in the season of the spring rain, from the LORD who makes the storm clouds, and he will give them showers of rain, to everyone the vegetation in the field.

2For the household gods utter nonsense, and the diviners see lies; they tell false dreams and give empty consolation. Therefore the people wander like sheep; they are afflicted for lack of a shepherd.

3“My anger is hot against the shepherds, and I will punish the leaders; for the LORD of hosts cares for his flock, the house of Judah, and will make them like his majestic steed in battle.

4From him shall come the cornerstone, from him the tent peg, from him the battle bow, from him every ruler—all of them together.

5They shall be like mighty men in battle, trampling the foe in the mud of the streets; they shall fight because the LORD is with them, and they shall put to shame the riders on horses.

6“I will strengthen the house of Judah, and I will save the house of Joseph. I will bring them back because I have compassion on them, and they shall be as though I had not rejected them, for I am the LORD their God and I will answer them.

7Then Ephraim shall become like a mighty warrior, and their hearts shall be glad as with wine. Their children shall see it and be glad; their hearts shall rejoice in the LORD.

8“I will whistle for them and gather them in, for I have redeemed them, and they shall be as many as they were before.

9Though I scattered them among the nations, yet in far countries they shall remember me, and with their children they shall live and return.

10I will bring them home from the land of Egypt, and gather them from Assyria, and I will bring them to the land of Gilead and to Lebanon, till there is no room for them.

11He shall pass through the sea of troubles and strike down the waves of the sea, and all the depths of the Nile shall be dried up. The pride of Assyria shall be laid low, and the scepter of Egypt shall depart.

12I will make them strong in the LORD, and they shall walk in his name,” declares the LORD.

Summary

God promises to strengthen Judah and save Ephraim, gathering His scattered people from among the nations — they will be as mighty as before, and He will whistle for them and redeem them.

Key Verses

v.1
"Ask ye of the LORD rain in the time of the latter rain" (KJV) — A command to seek GOD for provision rather than idols or diviners. The "latter rain" (spring rain) was essential for harvest. God is the source of EVERY good thing — ask HIM, not substitutes.
v.2
"they went their way as a flock, they were troubled, because there was no shepherd" (KJV) / "the people wander like sheep; they are afflicted for lack of a shepherd" (ESV) — The root cause of Israel's wandering: bad leadership. Without a true shepherd, the flock scatters. Jesus would later look at the crowds and feel the same compassion (Matt 9:36).
v.4
"Out of him came forth the corner, out of him the nail, out of him the battle bow" (KJV) / "From him shall come the cornerstone, from him the tent peg, from him the battle bow" (ESV) — Three Messianic images from Judah: (1) Cornerstone = foundation and alignment; (2) Tent peg = security and stability; (3) Battle bow = victory and authority. Christ is all three.
v.6
"they shall be as though I had not cast them off" (KJV) / "they shall be as though I had not rejected them" (ESV) — Perhaps the most gracious restoration promise in Scripture. God's restoration is SO complete that the shame, the exile, the rejection — it's as if it never happened. This is the power of redemption.
v.8
"I will hiss for them, and gather them" (KJV) / "I will whistle for them and gather them in" (ESV) — The shepherd's whistle that calls the flock home. Intimate, personal, unmistakable. God knows His sheep and they know His voice (John 10:27).
v.12
"I will strengthen them in the LORD; and they shall walk up and down in his name" (KJV) — The final promise: divine strengthening and a life lived entirely in God's name. Walking "in His name" = living under His authority, in His character, for His glory.

Cross-References

  • v.2 → Matthew 9:36 — "He was moved with compassion on them, because they fainted, and were scattered abroad, as sheep having no shepherd." v.2 — Ezekiel 34:5 — "They were scattered, because there is no shepherd." v.4 — Isaiah 28:16 — "Behold, I lay in Zion for a foundation a stone, a tried stone, a precious corner stone." v.4 — Isaiah 22:23 — "I will fasten him as a nail in a sure place" — the tent peg of stability. v.6 — Romans 8:1 — "There is therefore now no condemnation" — as though never rejected. v.8 — John 10:27 — "My sheep hear my voice, and I know them, and they follow me." v.10 — Isaiah 11:11-12 — God's regathering from Assyria, Egypt, and the nations. v.11 — Exodus 14:21-22 — The original parting of the sea — God will do it again.

Translations

  • v.1 → "latter rain" — Hebrew "malqosh" = the spring rain (March-April) that brings crops to maturity before harvest. Without it, the harvest fails. God controls the rain — it is His to give or withhold.
  • v.2 → "idols" (KJV) / "household gods" (ESV) — Hebrew "teraphim" = household idols used for divination. Small figurines consulted for guidance instead of God. A persistent temptation in Israel's history.
  • v.4 → "corner" / "cornerstone" — Hebrew "pinnah" = corner, cornerstone, chief. The stone that determines the alignment of the entire building. Everything is oriented by this stone.
  • v.4 → "nail" / "tent peg" — Hebrew "yated" = a peg or stake. In a tent culture, the peg held everything in place. Remove the peg and the tent collapses. Messiah is the stabilizing fixture of God's people.
  • v.8 → "hiss" (KJV) / "whistle" (ESV) — Hebrew "sharaq" = to whistle, to pipe. Used of shepherds calling flocks and beekeepers summoning bees (Isaiah 7:18). God's call is specific and personal.

Reflection

  • 1. "Ask the LORD for rain" (v.1). Whatever you need — provision, guidance, refreshing — go to GOD first, not substitutes. Not horoscopes, not self-help, not worry. Ask the LORD. He makes the storm clouds. He gives the showers. He alone.
  • 2. Bad leadership causes wandering (v.2). If you are in a place of spiritual confusion or wandering, consider: who are you following? False shepherds produce scattered sheep. Seek leaders who point to Christ, not to themselves.
  • 3. Christ is your cornerstone, tent peg, and battle bow (v.4). He gives you alignment (cornerstone — you know which way to build). He gives stability (tent peg — you won't collapse). He gives victory (battle bow — you can overcome). Everything you need comes "from Him."
  • 4. "As though I had not cast them off" (v.6). God's grace doesn't just forgive — it ERASES the shame. You are not a "recovering sinner" forever marked by failure. In God's eyes, you are restored so completely it's as if the rejection never happened. Stop carrying shame God has already removed.
  • 5. God whistles for you (v.8). When you are scattered and far away, God doesn't shout in anger — He whistles. A familiar, tender call that you recognize. Listen for His whistle today. He is calling you home.
