Psalms — Chapter 55

1Give ear to my prayer, O God, and hide not yourself from my plea for mercy!

2Attend to me, and answer me; I am restless in my complaint and I moan,

3because of the noise of the enemy, because of the oppression of the wicked. For they drop trouble upon me, and in anger they bear a grudge against me.

4My heart is in anguish within me; the terrors of death have fallen upon me.

5Fear and trembling come upon me, and horror overwhelms me.

6And I say, “Oh, that I had wings like a dove! I would fly away and be at rest;

7yes, I would wander far away; I would lodge in the wilderness; Selah

8I would hurry to find a shelter from the raging wind and tempest.”

9Destroy, O Lord, divide their tongues; for I see violence and strife in the city.

10Day and night they go around it on its walls, and iniquity and trouble are within it;

11ruin is in its midst; oppression and fraud do not depart from its marketplace.

12For it is not an enemy who taunts me— then I could bear it; it is not an adversary who deals insolently with me— then I could hide from him.

13But it is you, a man, my equal, my companion, my familiar friend.

14We used to take sweet counsel together; within God’s house we walked in the throng.

15Let death steal over them; let them go down to Sheol alive; for evil is in their dwelling place and in their heart.

16But I call to God, and the LORD will save me.

17Evening and morning and at noon I utter my complaint and moan, and he hears my voice.

18He redeems my soul in safety from the battle that I wage, for many are arrayed against me.

19God will give ear and humble them, he who is enthroned from of old, Selah because they do not change and do not fear God.

20My companion stretched out his hand against his friends; he violated his covenant.

21His speech was smooth as butter, yet war was in his heart; his words were softer than oil, yet they were drawn swords.

22Cast your burden on the LORD, and he will sustain you; he will never permit the righteous to be moved.

23But you, O God, will cast them down into the pit of destruction; men of blood and treachery shall not live out half their days. But I will trust in you.

Summary

A lament over betrayal — David wishes for wings like a dove to fly away; the pain is sharpest because the betrayer is his close companion; he casts his burden on the LORD, who will sustain him.

Key Verses

v.6
"And I said, Oh that I had wings like a dove! for then would I fly away, and be at rest." (KJV) — One of the most human moments in all the Psalms. David the warrior- king wishes he could simply escape. The dove represents peaceful flight — not aggressive departure but gentle withdrawal. Even the strongest believers sometimes wish they could just fly away from their problems.
v.12-13
"For it was not an enemy that reproached me; then I could have borne it... But it was thou, a man mine equal, my guide, and mine acquaintance." (KJV) — The deepest wounds come from the closest people. Enemy opposition is expected and manageable. Friend betrayal is devastating because it violates trust, intimacy, and shared history.
v.21
"The words of his mouth were smoother than butter, but war was in his heart: his words were softer than oil, yet were they drawn swords." (KJV) — The anatomy of betrayal: smooth words concealing violent intentions. The contrast between external speech and internal reality is the essence of treachery.
v.22
"Cast thy burden upon the LORD, and he shall sustain thee: he shall never suffer the righteous to be moved." (KJV) — The great command and promise of this psalm. "Cast" (hashlekh) = throw, hurl. Not gently set down but aggressively throw your burden onto God. He can bear what you cannot.

Cross-References

  • v.6 — Jeremiah 9:2 — "Oh that I had in the wilderness a lodging place of wayfaring men; that I might leave my people." Jeremiah echoes the same desire to flee. v.12-14 — 2 Samuel 15:12,31 — Ahithophel's betrayal of David, the likely historical referent. v.13 — Matthew 26:50 — Jesus to Judas: "Friend, wherefore art thou come?" Jesus experienced this same betrayal. v.15 — Numbers 16:31-33 — Korah's company going down alive into the pit — the precedent for David's imprecation. v.17 — Daniel 6:10 — Daniel praying three times daily, the same pattern David describes. v.22 — 1 Peter 5:7 — "Casting all your care upon him; for he careth for you." Peter quotes/echoes this verse directly. v.22 — Matthew 11:28 — "Come unto me, all ye that labour and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest."

Translations

  • v.2 — KJV: "I mourn in my complaint, and make a noise" / ESV: "I am restless in my complaint and I moan" — Hebrew "arid" = restless, tossing about. The image is of someone who cannot be still because of anxiety. ESV's "restless" captures this better than KJV's "mourn."
  • v.15 — KJV: "let them go down quick into hell" / ESV: "let them go down to Sheol alive" — "Hell" (KJV) is misleading; the Hebrew is "Sheol" = the realm of the dead, not the place of final punishment. ESV is more accurate. "Quick" = alive (archaic English).
  • v.19 — KJV: "Because they have no changes, therefore they fear not God" / ESV: "because they do not change and do not fear God" — "No changes" = no reversals, no suffering that would humble them. Uninterrupted prosperity breeds arrogance and godlessness.
  • v.22 — KJV: "Cast thy burden" / ESV: "Cast your burden" — Hebrew "yehab" appears only here in Psalms. It may mean "what is given to you" (your lot, your portion). Cast your God-given portion — whatever He has assigned you — back onto Him for sustaining.

Reflection

  • 1. It is not weakness to wish for escape (v.6). Even David wished for wings. The desire to flee is human and honest. God does not rebuke this desire — He simply redirects it toward Himself rather than toward physical flight.
  • 2. Betrayal by a friend wounds deeper than attack by an enemy (vv.12-14). The closer the relationship, the deeper the wound. If you have experienced this, know that David — and ultimately Jesus — understands this particular agony completely.
  • 3. God sustains the burden-caster (v.22). The command is not "figure it out" but "throw it on God." The burden doesn't disappear — but it transfers to One who can carry it. Your job is to cast; God's job is to sustain.
  • 4. Smooth words can conceal violent hearts (v.21). Not everyone who speaks kindly is kind. Discernment requires looking beyond words to patterns, actions, and fruit. Words are cheap; character is costly.
  • 5. Three-times-daily prayer sustains through crisis (v.17). Evening, morning, and noon — a rhythm of continual dependence. When life is overwhelming, structure your prayer. Don't wait until you "feel like it" — pray on schedule.
