Leviticus — Chapter 6

1The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

2“If anyone sins and commits a breach of faith against the LORD by deceiving his neighbor in a matter of deposit or security, or through robbery, or if he has oppressed his neighbor

3or has found something lost and lied about it, swearing falsely—in any of all the things that people do and sin thereby—

4if he has sinned and has realized his guilt and will restore what he took by robbery or what he got by oppression or the deposit that was committed to him or the lost thing that he found

5or anything about which he has sworn falsely, he shall restore it in full and shall add a fifth to it, and give it to him to whom it belongs on the day he realizes his guilt.

6And he shall bring to the priest as his compensation to the LORD a ram without blemish out of the flock, or its equivalent, for a guilt offering.

7And the priest shall make atonement for him before the LORD, and he shall be forgiven for any of the things that one may do and thereby become guilty.”

8The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

9“Command Aaron and his sons, saying, This is the law of the burnt offering. The burnt offering shall be on the hearth on the altar all night until the morning, and the fire of the altar shall be kept burning on it.

10And the priest shall put on his linen garment and put his linen undergarment on his body, and he shall take up the ashes to which the fire has reduced the burnt offering on the altar and put them beside the altar.

11Then he shall take off his garments and put on other garments and carry the ashes outside the camp to a clean place.

12The fire on the altar shall be kept burning on it; it shall not go out. The priest shall burn wood on it every morning, and he shall arrange the burnt offering on it and shall burn on it the fat of the peace offerings.

13Fire shall be kept burning on the altar continually; it shall not go out.

14“And this is the law of the grain offering. The sons of Aaron shall offer it before the LORD in front of the altar.

15And one shall take from it a handful of the fine flour of the grain offering and its oil and all the frankincense that is on the grain offering and burn this as its memorial portion on the altar, a pleasing aroma to the LORD.

16And the rest of it Aaron and his sons shall eat. It shall be eaten unleavened in a holy place. In the court of the tent of meeting they shall eat it.

17It shall not be baked with leaven. I have given it as their portion of my food offerings. It is a thing most holy, like the sin offering and the guilt offering.

18Every male among the children of Aaron may eat of it, as decreed forever throughout your generations, from the LORD’s food offerings. Whatever touches them shall become holy.”

19The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

20“This is the offering that Aaron and his sons shall offer to the LORD on the day when he is anointed: a tenth of an ephah of fine flour as a regular grain offering, half of it in the morning and half in the evening.

21It shall be made with oil on a griddle. You shall bring it well mixed, in baked pieces like a grain offering, and offer it for a pleasing aroma to the LORD.

22The priest from among Aaron’s sons, who is anointed to succeed him, shall offer it to the LORD as decreed forever. The whole of it shall be burned.

23Every grain offering of a priest shall be wholly burned. It shall not be eaten.”

24The LORD spoke to Moses, saying,

25“Speak to Aaron and his sons, saying, This is the law of the sin offering. In the place where the burnt offering is killed shall the sin offering be killed before the LORD; it is most holy.

26The priest who offers it for sin shall eat it. In a holy place it shall be eaten, in the court of the tent of meeting.

27Whatever touches its flesh shall be holy, and when any of its blood is splashed on a garment, you shall wash that on which it was splashed in a holy place.

28And the earthenware vessel in which it is boiled shall be broken. But if it is boiled in a bronze vessel, that shall be scoured and rinsed in water.

29Every male among the priests may eat of it; it is most holy.

30But no sin offering shall be eaten from which any blood is brought into the tent of meeting to make atonement in the Holy Place; it shall be burned up with fire.

Summary

God gives further instructions for the priests on maintaining the offerings — the fire on the altar must never go out.

Key Verses

v.2
"If a soul sin, and commit a trespass against the LORD, and lie unto his neighbour" — Sin against man IS sin against God.
v.5
"He shall even restore it in the principal, and shall add the fifth part more thereto" — Restitution plus 20%. Grace costs the sinner something.
v.7
"The priest shall make an atonement for him before the LORD: and it shall be forgiven him" — After restitution, full forgiveness.
v.12
"The fire upon the altar shall be burning in it; it shall not be put out" — The perpetual fire, first mention.
v.13
"The fire shall ever be burning upon the altar; it shall never go out." — Emphatic repetition. Unceasing devotion. God never stops being ready to receive worship.
v.17
"It is most holy, as is the sin offering, and as the trespass offering." — The grain offering shares the same sacred status.
v.30
"No sin offering, whereof any of the blood is brought into the tabernacle of the congregation to reconcile withal in the holy place, shall be eaten: it shall be burnt in the fire." — When atonement reaches the Holy Place, the offering is entirely consumed.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/leviticus-6/ Guzik emphasizes the perpetual fire as representing God's constant readiness to receive worship and the believer's call to unceasing devotion. He notes the restitution principle: sacrifice without making things right with your neighbor is insufficient.
  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "The Fire That Never Goes Out" "The fire shall NEVER go out — here is the heart of worship. God does not sleep. His altar is never cold. His readiness to receive the sinner never wanes. And so our devotion must be: not a flash of enthusiasm that dies by Tuesday, but a steady flame that burns through every season. Keep the fire burning, Christian. Add the wood of prayer every morning. Let the fat of praise ascend continually. The altar of your heart must never grow cold."

Translations

  • v.2 — KJV: "If a soul sin, and commit a trespass against the LORD" / ESV: "If anyone sins and commits a breach of faith against the LORD" — Hebrew "ma'al ma'al" — to act unfaithfully/treacherously. ESV's "breach of faith" captures the covenant-breaking nature. KJV's "trespass" is more general. The Hebrew emphasizes betrayal of trust.
  • v.2 — KJV: "in fellowship" / ESV: "or security" — Hebrew "tesumeth yad" — a pledge/deposit placed in hand. KJV's "fellowship" is misleading; ESV's "security" (a financial pledge) is more accurate.
  • v.5 — KJV: "in the principal" / ESV: "in full" — Hebrew "b'rosho" — literally "in its head/chief amount." Both convey full restitution of the original amount.
  • v.6 — KJV: "trespass offering" / ESV: "guilt offering" — Hebrew "asham." ESV's "guilt offering" better captures the meaning: this offering addresses guilt and requires compensation. KJV's "trespass" focuses on the act; ESV's "guilt" focuses on the condition.
  • v.9 — KJV: "the law of the burnt offering" / ESV: "the law of the burnt offering" — Hebrew "torath ha'olah." "Torah" here means instruction/ regulation, not the whole Law. This is the priestly manual.
  • v.10 — KJV: "linen breeches" / ESV: "linen undergarment" — Hebrew "miknesei bad" — linen undergarments. ESV modernizes; KJV preserves the archaic "breeches."
  • v.14 — KJV: "the meat offering" / ESV: "the grain offering" — Hebrew "minchah." KJV's "meat" is archaic English for "food" (not flesh). ESV's "grain offering" is far clearer for modern readers. This is flour, oil, and frankincense — no animal flesh involved.
  • v.15 — KJV: "a sweet savour, even the memorial of it" / ESV: "a pleasing aroma to the LORD" — Hebrew "re'ach nichoach... azkaratah." KJV preserves "memorial" (azkarah) — the portion that brings the worshipper to God's remembrance. ESV omits this nuance.
  • v.21 — KJV: "in a pan" / ESV: "on a griddle" — Hebrew "al machabath" — a flat plate/griddle. ESV is more precise; KJV's "pan" suggests sides/depth.
  • v.25 — KJV: "the law of the sin offering" / ESV: "the law of the sin offering" — Hebrew "torath ha-chatta'th." Both identical. "Sin offering" — Hebrew "chatta'th" — from the root "to miss/fail."
  • v.28 — KJV: "sodden" / ESV: "boiled" — Hebrew "bushlah." KJV's "sodden" is archaic past participle of "seethe" (to boil). ESV's "boiled" is clear. KJV: "brasen" / ESV: "bronze" — same metal, updated term.
  • v.30 — KJV: "to reconcile withal in the holy place" / ESV: "to make atonement in the Holy Place" — Hebrew "l'khapper ba-qodesh." Both convey the same meaning. KJV's "reconcile" and ESV's "atonement" are both valid renderings of "kaphar."

Reflection

  • 1. "Commit a trespass against the LORD, and lie unto his neighbour" (v.2). Sin against people IS sin against God. You cannot wrong your neighbor and be right with God. Is there anyone you need to make restitution to?
  • 2. "He shall restore it in the principal, and shall add the fifth part" (v.5). God requires MORE than what was taken — not just making it even, but making it MORE than even. Repentance costs something. Are you willing to go beyond "fair" in making things right?
  • 3. "The fire shall ever be burning upon the altar; it shall never go out" (v.13). Is the fire of your devotion still burning? Or has it gone cold? What "wood" do you need to add this morning — prayer, Scripture, worship, obedience — to keep the flame alive?
  • 4. The priest's grain offering is wholly burned — he cannot eat his own offering (v.23). Those who serve God cannot serve themselves with what belongs to God. Ministry is not self-serving.
  • 5. "First be reconciled to thy brother" (Matthew 5:23-24). The guilt offering teaches that worship without reconciliation is empty. God refuses the sacrifice of someone who won't make things right with their neighbor.
