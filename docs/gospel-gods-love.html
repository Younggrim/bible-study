The Gospel

God's Love

The Truth

Here is the stunning truth at the center of the gospel: the holy God who cannot tolerate sin is also the loving God who gave everything to save sinners. His holiness and His love are not in conflict — they meet perfectly at the cross, where justice is satisfied and mercy is extended simultaneously. God did not choose between being holy and being loving; He found a way to be both.

The timing of God's love is what makes it so astonishing. Romans 5:8 emphasizes that Christ died for us "while we were still sinners" — not after we cleaned up, not when we deserved it, not because we asked for it. God's love is initiating love, reaching toward rebels and enemies with sacrifice rather than waiting for us to become lovable.

God's love is not a vague sentiment but a concrete action: He gave His Son. Love is measured not by feelings but by sacrifice, and God's sacrifice is infinite — the death of His only Son for those who hated Him. No greater love exists or can exist. The cross is the definitive answer to every question about whether God truly loves us.

Key Scriptures

  • Romans 5:8 — "God demonstrates his own love for us in this: While we were still sinners, Christ died for us."
  • John 3:16 — "For God so loved the world that he gave his one and only Son."
  • 1 John 4:9-10 — "This is love: not that we loved God, but that he loved us and sent his Son as an atoning sacrifice for our sins."
  • Ephesians 2:4-5 — "Because of his great love for us, God, who is rich in mercy, made us alive with Christ even when we were dead in transgressions."
  • Jeremiah 31:3 — "I have loved you with an everlasting love; I have drawn you with unfailing kindness."

Why It Matters

God's love matters because it is the motivation behind the entire gospel. God did not save us out of obligation, pity, or self-interest — He saved us because He loves us. This means our salvation is secure in something unchangeable: God's nature. If His love could fail, our salvation could fail. But His love is everlasting, and therefore so is our security in Christ.

Understanding God's love also transforms our response from duty to delight. We do not obey in order to earn love — we obey because we have already received it. We do not serve to gain acceptance — we serve from the overflow of being accepted. This is the freedom of the gospel: loved first, loved unconditionally, loved without end.

Reflection Questions

  • How does knowing that God loved you while you were still a sinner change your self-perception?
  • In what ways do you sometimes try to earn God's love rather than simply receiving it?
  • How can the security of God's unchanging love free you from performance-based faith?
