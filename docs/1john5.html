Essentially literal. Modern English, highly accurate. Best for deep study.

Traditional (1611). Beautiful, poetic language. The classic English Bible.

Formal equivalent (1901). Very literal, precise. Good for word studies.

Study Bible with extensive translator notes. Balances accuracy and readability.

Modern, public domain. Based on the Majority Text. Free to use and share.

1 John — Chapter 5

1Everyone who believes that Jesus is the Christ has been born of God, and everyone who loves the Father loves whoever has been born of him.

2By this we know that we love the children of God, when we love God and obey his commandments.

3For this is the love of God, that we keep his commandments. And his commandments are not burdensome.

4For everyone who has been born of God overcomes the world. And this is the victory that has overcome the world—our faith.

5Who is it that overcomes the world except the one who believes that Jesus is the Son of God?

6This is he who came by water and blood—Jesus Christ; not by the water only but by the water and the blood. And the Spirit is the one who testifies, because the Spirit is the truth.

7For there are three that testify:

8the Spirit and the water and the blood; and these three agree.

9If we receive the testimony of men, the testimony of God is greater, for this is the testimony of God that he has borne concerning his Son.

10Whoever believes in the Son of God has the testimony in himself. Whoever does not believe God has made him a liar, because he has not believed in the testimony that God has borne concerning his Son.

11And this is the testimony, that God gave us eternal life, and this life is in his Son.

12Whoever has the Son has life; whoever does not have the Son of God does not have life.

13I write these things to you who believe in the name of the Son of God, that you may know that you have eternal life.

14And this is the confidence that we have toward him, that if we ask anything according to his will he hears us.

15And if we know that he hears us in whatever we ask, we know that we have the requests that we have asked of him.

16If anyone sees his brother committing a sin not leading to death, he shall ask, and God will give him life—to those who commit sins that do not lead to death. There is sin that leads to death; I do not say that one should pray for that.

17All wrongdoing is sin, but there is sin that does not lead to death.

18We know that everyone who has been born of God does not keep on sinning, but he who was born of God protects him, and the evil one does not touch him.

19We know that we are from God, and the whole world lies in the power of the evil one.

20And we know that the Son of God has come and has given us understanding, so that we may know him who is true; and we are in him who is true, in his Son Jesus Christ. He is the true God and eternal life.

Summary

Whoever believes Jesus is the Christ is born of God, faith is the victory that overcomes the world, the Spirit and water and blood bear witness, and John writes "that ye may know that ye have eternal life" — closing with a warning against idols.

Key Verses

v.4
"Everyone who has been born of God overcomes the world. And this is the victory that has overcome the world—our faith." (ESV) — Faith as the world-overcoming victory.
v.11-12
"God gave us eternal life, and this life is in his Son. Whoever has the Son has life; whoever does not have the Son of God does not have life." (ESV) — Life is in the Son — have Him, have life.
v.13
"I write these things to you who believe in the name of the Son of God, that you may know that you have eternal life." (ESV) — The purpose of 1 John: assurance.
v.14-15
"If we ask anything according to his will he hears us. And if we know that he hears us... we know that we have the requests." (ESV) — Confidence in prayer aligned with God's will.
v.20
"We know that the Son of God has come and has given us understanding, so that we may know him who is true." (ESV) — Knowing the true God through Christ.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): "https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/1-john-5/" target="_blank">https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/1-john-5/ Guzik covers overcoming faith, the threefold witness, assurance, prayer, and the three certainties.
  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "The Victory of Faith" on v.4 (MTP Vol. 11, No. 616) "This is the victory — not our works, not our feelings, not our efforts — our FAITH. Faith overcomes the world by looking beyond it to the unseen. Faith overcomes the world's temptations by seeing them as temporary. Faith overcomes the world's threats by trusting a sovereign God. The world is strong — but faith is stronger, because faith connects us to the One who has already overcome." Sermon: "Eternal Life" on vv.11-12 (MTP Vol. 34, No. 2048) "He that hath the Son HATH life. Present tense. Not 'will have' — hath. Right now. Eternal life is not just future — it is present possession. And it is IN the Son — not separate from Him. You don't get life apart from Christ. You get Christ, and life comes with Him. Have Him, have life. Don't have Him, don't have life. It's that simple."

Translations

  • v.3 → KJV: "his commandments are not grievous" / ESV: "his commandments are not burdensome" — Greek "bareiai ouk eisin" — not heavy, not burdensome. God's commands are not oppressive — they are the path of freedom, not bondage.
  • v.4 → KJV: "overcometh the world" / ESV: "overcomes the world" — Greek "nikōsa" — conquers, is victorious over. Present tense: keeps on overcoming. "Our faith" — Greek "hē pistis hēmōn" — THE faith, our faith. Faith is the instrument of victory.
  • v.6 → KJV: "came by water and blood" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Christ's baptism (beginning of ministry) and cross (end of ministry). Against Gnostics who said the divine Christ left Jesus before the cross. No — He came by BOTH water AND blood.
  • v.7-8 → KJV includes the "Johannine Comma" (v.7b-8a): "the Father, the Word, and the Holy Ghost: and these three are one. And there are three that bear witness in earth" — ESV omits this, following most early Greek manuscripts. Most scholars consider it a later addition. The doctrine of the Trinity is established elsewhere regardless.
  • v.13 → KJV: "that ye may KNOW that ye have eternal life" / ESV: "that you may know that you have eternal life" — Both equivalent. Greek "eidēte" — that you may know with certainty. John's stated purpose: assurance of salvation.
  • v.16 → KJV: "sin unto death" / ESV: "sin that leads to death" — Debated. Options: (1) physical death as discipline (cf. Acts 5, 1 Cor 11:30), (2) apostasy/blasphemy against the Spirit, (3) any sin that results in premature physical death.
  • v.19 → KJV: "the whole world lieth in wickedness" / ESV: "the whole world lies in the power of the evil one" — Greek "en tō ponērō keitai" — lies in the evil one. ESV makes the personal reference to Satan clearer.
  • v.21 → KJV: "keep yourselves from idols" / ESV: Both equivalent. — An abrupt ending. Anything that takes God's place is an idol. After a letter about knowing the true God, the final word: don't substitute anything for Him.
  • ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB) v.3 — NET: "For this is the love of God: that we keep his commandments. And his commandments do not weigh us down" — "do not weigh us down" (bareiai ouk eisin = are not heavy/ burdensome). WEB: "his commandments are not grievous." KJV: "his commandments are not grievous." Obedience is not oppressive for the born-again — love makes duty light.
  • v.4 → NET: "this is the conquering power that has conquered the world: our faith" — "conquering power" (nikE = victory). WEB: "this is the victory that has overcome the world: our faith." KJV: "this is the victory that overcometh the world, even our faith." Faith IS the victory — not produces it but IS it.
  • v.7 → NET: "For there are three that testify" — the longer reading (Comma Johanneum: "in heaven, the Father, the Word, and the Holy Ghost") is found in KJV but NOT in NET/ESV/WEB/ ASV. It is absent from all Greek manuscripts before the 16th century. NET omits it following all early manuscript evidence.
  • v.13 → NET: "I have written these things to you who believe in the name of the Son of God so that you may know that you have eternal life" — "that you may KNOW" (hina eidEte = that you may have settled knowledge). WEB: "that you may know that you have eternal life." Assurance is possible and intended — John writes for certainty, not doubt.
  • v.21 → NET: "Little children, guard yourselves from idols" — "guard yourselves" (phylaxate heauta = keep/protect yourselves). WEB: "Little children, keep yourselves from idols." KJV: "Little children, keep yourselves from idols." The abrupt ending — the opposite of loving God is idolatry. A final warning.

Reflection

  • 1. "This is the victory that overcometh the world, even our faith" (v.4). Is your faith overcoming the world — its temptations, its values, its pressure? Or is the world overcoming you?
  • 2. "He that hath the Son hath life" (v.12). Do you have the Son? Then you have life — right now, present tense. Are you living in that assurance?
  • 3. "That ye may KNOW that ye have eternal life" (v.13). Do you know? Not hope, not wish — know. John wrote so you could have certainty. What gives you assurance?
  • 4. "If we ask any thing according to his will, he heareth us" (v.14). Are your prayers aligned with God's will? How do you discern His will so you can pray with confidence?
  • 5. "Keep yourselves from idols" (v.21). What idols compete for God's place in your life — money, comfort, approval, control, entertainment? The final word of 1 John: guard against substitutes.
