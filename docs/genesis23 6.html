Genesis — Chapter 23

1Sarah lived 127 years; these were the years of the life of Sarah.

2And Sarah died at Kiriath-arba (that is, Hebron) in the land of Canaan, and Abraham went in to mourn for Sarah and to weep for her.

3And Abraham rose up from before his dead and said to the Hittites,

4“I am a sojourner and foreigner among you; give me property among you for a burying place, that I may bury my dead out of my sight.”

5The Hittites answered Abraham,

6“Hear us, my lord; you are a prince of God among us. Bury your dead in the choicest of our tombs. None of us will withhold from you his tomb to hinder you from burying your dead.”

7Abraham rose and bowed to the Hittites, the people of the land.

8And he said to them, “If you are willing that I should bury my dead out of my sight, hear me and entreat for me Ephron the son of Zohar,

9that he may give me the cave of Machpelah, which he owns; it is at the end of his field. For the full price let him give it to me in your presence as property for a burying place.”

10Now Ephron was sitting among the Hittites, and Ephron the Hittite answered Abraham in the hearing of the Hittites, of all who went in at the gate of his city,

11“No, my lord, hear me: I give you the field, and I give you the cave that is in it. In the sight of the sons of my people I give it to you. Bury your dead.”

12Then Abraham bowed down before the people of the land.

13And he said to Ephron in the hearing of the people of the land, “But if you will, hear me: I give the price of the field. Accept it from me, that I may bury my dead there.”

14Ephron answered Abraham,

15“My lord, listen to me: a piece of land worth four hundred shekels of silver, what is that between you and me? Bury your dead.”

16Abraham listened to Ephron, and Abraham weighed out for Ephron the silver that he had named in the hearing of the Hittites, four hundred shekels of silver, according to the weights current among the merchants.

17So the field of Ephron in Machpelah, which was to the east of Mamre, the field with the cave that was in it and all the trees that were in the field, throughout its whole area, was made over

18to Abraham as a possession in the presence of the Hittites, before all who went in at the gate of his city.

19After this, Abraham buried Sarah his wife in the cave of the field of Machpelah east of Mamre (that is, Hebron) in the land of Canaan.

20The field and the cave that is in it were made over to Abraham as property for a burying place by the Hittites.

Summary

Sarah dies at age 127; Abraham purchases the cave of Machpelah from the Hittites as a burial place — his first land ownership in Canaan.

Key Verses

  • v.4 — "I am a sojourner and foreigner among you; give me property among you for a burying place." (ESV) — Abraham's pilgrim identity and faith claim.
  • v.16 — "Abraham weighed out for Ephron the silver... four hundred shekels." (ESV) — Full price paid; no obligation to anyone.
  • v.19 — "Abraham buried Sarah his wife in the cave of the field of Machpelah." (ESV) — The only land Abraham owned: a grave in the Promised Land.

Commentary

  • Enduring Word (David Guzik): "https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/genesis-23/" target="_blank">enduringword.com Guzik covers Sarah's death, the negotiation, the faith significance of the purchase, and Machpelah's importance.
  • Charles Spurgeon: Sermon: "Strangers and Pilgrims" (MTP Vol. 14, No. 838) "A stranger and a sojourner — Abraham owned nothing in the Promised Land except a grave. Yet he bought that grave as an act of faith: 'My family belongs here. God promised this land. I will plant my dead in it as a claim on the future.' Every burial in Machpelah was a declaration: 'We believe God's promise. This land is ours — not yet in full, but certainly.'"

Translations

  • v.2 → KJV: "Kirjath-arba" / ESV: "Kiriath-arba" — Both equivalent (transliteration difference). "City of Four" (possibly four giants or four quarters). Later called Hebron. One of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world.
  • v.4 → KJV: "stranger and sojourner" / ESV: "sojourner and foreigner" — Hebrew "ger vetoshab" — a resident alien and temporary dweller. ESV reverses the order and uses "foreigner" for "sojourner." Abraham had no legal land rights.
  • v.6 → KJV: "a mighty prince" / ESV: "a prince of God" — Hebrew "nesi Elohim" — a prince of God. ESV is more literal; KJV interprets "Elohim" as an intensifier ("mighty"). The Hittites recognized Abraham's stature.
  • v.15 → KJV: "four hundred shekels" / ESV: "four hundred shekels" — Both identical. A high price. Jeremiah later bought a field for 17 shekels (Jer 32:9). Ephron's "generosity" was actually shrewd pricing.
  • v.17 → KJV: "were made sure" / ESV: "was made over" — Hebrew "qum" — established, confirmed. Both convey legal transfer. KJV emphasizes certainty; ESV emphasizes the transfer of ownership. A legal transfer with witnesses — binding and permanent.
  • TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB) - v.4 — NET: "I am a foreign resident, a temporary settler, among you" — uses two descriptors to translate "ger vetoshab" (KJV: "stranger and sojourner"), emphasizing Abraham's legal status as a non-landowner. - v.6 — NET: "you are a mighty prince among us" — renders Hebrew "nesi Elohim" (KJV: "a mighty prince" / ASV: "a prince of God") similarly. WEB: "a prince of God among us" — retains the literal "of God" connection. - v.9 — NET: "Let him sell it to me publicly for the full price" — adds "publicly" to emphasize the legal/witness requirement of the transaction. - v.16 — NET: "according to the standard measurement at the time" — adds an explanatory note about the weight standard, clarifying ancient commerce.

Reflection

  • 1. Abraham owned nothing in the Promised Land except a grave — yet he lived by faith that God's promise was certain. Can you live with unfulfilled promises, trusting God's timing?
  • 2. Abraham insisted on paying full price (v.16). He wouldn't be indebted to anyone for what God promised. Where do you need to maintain integrity in how you receive God's provision?
  • 3. Sarah's death reminds us that even the faithful die. But Abraham's purchase of a burial site was an act of faith: "We belong here. God's promise is certain." How do you express faith in the face of death?
  • 4. Abraham was a "stranger and sojourner" (v.4). Are you living as a pilgrim — or have you settled too comfortably in this world?
