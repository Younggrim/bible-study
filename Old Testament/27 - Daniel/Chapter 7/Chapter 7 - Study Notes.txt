Daniel - Chapter 7
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Daniel receives his own vision of four world empires — not as metals (ch.2) but as terrifying beasts rising from the sea. The fourth beast is dreadful beyond description with ten horns and a blasphemous "little horn." Then the Ancient of Days takes His throne, the beast is destroyed, and "one like the Son of man" approaches on clouds and receives everlasting dominion over all peoples, nations, and languages.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Daniel son of nobility, exiled to Babylon in 605 BC.
Chronologically, this vision occurs in Belshazzar's first year
(approximately 553 BC) — BEFORE the events of chapters 5-6.
This is the final chapter of the Aramaic section (2:4b-7:28).

Classification: Apocalyptic Vision / Prophetic Revelation
Key Themes: The four world empires as beasts, the Ancient of Days,
the Son of Man receiving the kingdom, the little horn's
blasphemy, God's ultimate victory, saints possessing the kingdom

Historical Context: Chapter 7 parallels chapter 2 but from
HEAVEN's perspective rather than earth's. Nebuchadnezzar saw
impressive metals; Daniel sees ravenous BEASTS. From God's
viewpoint, world empires aren't magnificent — they're predatory.
The four beasts correspond to: Lion (Babylon), Bear (Medo-
Persia), Leopard (Greece), and the Terrible Fourth Beast (Rome).

The "little horn" of the fourth beast is the most debated figure
in Daniel. It speaks "great words against the most High" and
persecutes saints for "a time and times and the dividing of time"
(3.5 years). Many see this as the Antichrist of the end times,
with partial fulfillment in historical figures like Antiochus
Epiphanes or certain Roman emperors.

The climax (vv.13-14) is among the most important Messianic
passages in Scripture. Jesus's favorite self-designation — "Son
of Man" — comes directly from here. When He told Caiaphas "ye
shall see the Son of man sitting on the right hand of power, and
coming in the clouds of heaven" (Matthew 26:64), He was quoting
Daniel 7:13 — and the high priest understood perfectly, tearing
his robes at the "blasphemy."

Structure:
- The Four Beasts from the Sea (vv.1-8)
- The Ancient of Days Takes His Throne (vv.9-10)
- The Beast Destroyed (vv.11-12)
- The Son of Man Receives Everlasting Dominion (vv.13-14)
- Daniel Troubled; An Angel Interprets (vv.15-18)
- The Fourth Beast and the Little Horn Explained (vv.19-25)
- The Saints Possess the Kingdom Forever (vv.26-28)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.9  — "I beheld till the thrones were cast down, and the Ancient
        of days did sit, whose garment was white as snow, and the
        hair of his head like the pure wool: his throne was like
        the fiery flame, and his wheels as burning fire" (KJV) —
        The ANCIENT OF DAYS: God the Father on His throne. White
        garments = purity, white hair = eternal wisdom, fiery
        throne = consuming holiness. Compare Revelation 1:14
        where Christ shares these attributes.

v.10 — "A fiery stream issued and came forth from before him:
        thousand thousands ministered unto him, and ten thousand
        times ten thousand stood before him: the judgment was set,
        and the books were opened" (KJV) — 100 MILLION angels.
        The books opened. Final judgment commences. No appeal, no
        escape, no delay. The court of heaven is in session.

v.13-14 — "I saw in the night visions, and, behold, one like the
        Son of man came with the clouds of heaven, and came to the
        Ancient of days... And there was given him dominion, and
        glory, and a kingdom, that all people, nations, and
        languages, should serve him: his dominion is an everlasting
        dominion, which shall not pass away" (KJV) — THE Son of
        Man: approaching God's throne on clouds (divine
        prerogative), receiving universal dominion, worshiped by
        ALL peoples. This is Jesus Christ — the title He chose for
        Himself 80+ times in the Gospels.

v.27 — "And the kingdom and dominion, and the greatness of the
        kingdom under the whole heaven, shall be given to the
        people of the saints of the most High, whose kingdom is
        an everlasting kingdom, and all dominions shall serve and
        obey him" (KJV) — The saints SHARE the kingdom. Christ
        receives it (v.14) and gives it to His people (v.27).
        Romans 8:17: "joint-heirs with Christ."

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.9  — "Ancient of days" (KJV/ESV) — Aramaic "Attiq Yomin" =
      the One advanced in days, the Eternal One. Not "old" in the
      sense of weakness but "ancient" in the sense of eternal
      existence. He predates all things and outlasts all things.

v.13 — "Son of man" (KJV/ESV) — Aramaic "bar enash" = son of
      man/son of humanity. The phrase emphasizes true humanity,
      yet this "human one" rides clouds (divine prerogative) and
      receives universal worship. The combination of humanity and
      deity in one figure — Jesus claimed this title deliberately.

v.25 — "time and times and the dividing of time" (KJV) / "a time,
      times, and half a time" (ESV) — Aramaic "iddan ve-iddanin
      u-pelag iddan" = one time + two times + half a time = 3.5
      years. This period appears in Revelation as 42 months or
      1,260 days — the duration of the Antichrist's persecution.

v.27 — "saints of the most High" (KJV/ESV) — Aramaic "qaddishe
      Elyonin" = holy ones of the Most High. These are God's
      people who endure persecution (v.25) and inherit the kingdom
      (v.27). The suffering precedes the reigning.

v.9 — ASV: "one that was ancient" for KJV's "the Ancient"
       NET: "Days took his seat. His attire" for KJV's "days did sit, whose garment"
       WEB: "one who was ancient" for KJV's "the Ancient"
v.13 — ASV: "night-visions," for KJV's "night visions,"
       NET: "the sky, one like a son of man was approaching. He went up" for KJV's "heaven, and came"
       WEB: "there" for KJV's "one like the Son of man"
v.25 — ASV: "Most High;" for KJV's "most High,"
       NET: "Most High continually. His intention will be" for KJV's "most High, and think"
       WEB: "Most High. He will plan" for KJV's "most High, and think"
v.27 — ASV: "Most High: his" for KJV's "most High, whose"
       NET: "eternal kingdom;" for KJV's "everlasting kingdom, and"
       WEB: "Most High. His" for KJV's "most High, whose"

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
The Ancient of Days — God the Father in His eternal majesty.
              "Ancient" speaks of eternal pre-existence, not
              aging. He presides over the final court that
              dismantles every human and demonic kingdom.

Son of Man — Daniel's heavenly figure who receives universal
              dominion. Jesus's primary self-designation — chosen
              because it combined humanity (son of man) with
              divine authority (riding clouds, receiving worship).

The Little Horn — A blasphemous ruler arising from the fourth
              beast (Rome), persecuting saints for 3.5 years.
              Identified variously with Antiochus Epiphanes
              (partial), future Antichrist (ultimate), or both.

The Four Beasts — Lion (Babylon), Bear (Medo-Persia), Leopard
              with four wings (Greece/Alexander's four generals),
              Terrible Beast with ten horns (Rome). God's view of
              empires: not splendid metals but savage predators.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. God sees empires as BEASTS (vv.3-7). We see impressive
   civilizations; God sees predatory animals devouring the earth.
   Don't be impressed by what God considers beastly. Political
   power without God is just a better-dressed predator.

2. The Ancient of Days is seated (v.9). While beasts rage below,
   God is SEATED — calm, enthroned, unhurried. He opens the
   books when He's ready. No beast accelerates His timeline.
   If you're anxious about world events, look at the THRONE.

3. The Son of Man receives everything (v.14). Jesus is not still
   fighting for the kingdom — He has RECEIVED it. The battle
   is won. We live in the "already/not yet" — the kingdom is
   His; its full manifestation is coming. Live like someone on
   the winning side.

4. Saints are persecuted THEN reign (vv.25-27). The order matters:
   suffering first, glory after. Don't be surprised by opposition.
   The little horn "wears out" the saints — but wearing out is
   not the same as winning. Endurance is victory.

5. "All peoples, nations, and languages should serve him" (v.14).
   The multi-ethnic, multilingual worship of Revelation 7:9 is
   guaranteed HERE. Every tongue will confess. Every culture will
   bow. Missions is not uncertain work — it's joining an
   inevitable outcome.
