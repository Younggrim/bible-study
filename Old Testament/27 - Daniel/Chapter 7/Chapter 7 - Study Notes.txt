Daniel - Chapter 7
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Daniel receives his own vision of four world empires — not as metals (ch.2) but as terrifying beasts rising from the sea. The fourth beast is dreadful beyond description with ten horns and a blasphemous "little horn." Then the Ancient of Days takes His throne, the beast is destroyed, and "one like the Son of man" approaches on clouds and receives everlasting dominion over all peoples, nations, and languages.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Daniel son of nobility, exiled to Babylon in 605 BC.
Chronologically, this vision occurs in Belshazzar's first year
(approximately 553 BC) — BEFORE the events of chapters 5-6.
This is the final chapter of the Aramaic section (2:4b-7:28).

Classification: Apocalyptic Vision / Prophetic Revelation
Key Themes: The four world empires as beasts, the Ancient of Days,
the Son of Man receiving the kingdom, the little horn's
blasphemy, God's ultimate victory, saints possessing the kingdom

Historical Context: Chapter 7 parallels chapter 2 but from
HEAVEN's perspective rather than earth's. Nebuchadnezzar saw
impressive metals; Daniel sees ravenous BEASTS. From God's
viewpoint, world empires aren't magnificent — they're predatory.
The four beasts correspond to: Lion (Babylon), Bear (Medo-
Persia), Leopard (Greece), and the Terrible Fourth Beast (Rome).

The "little horn" of the fourth beast is the most debated figure
in Daniel. It speaks "great words against the most High" and
persecutes saints for "a time and times and the dividing of time"
(3.5 years). Many see this as the Antichrist of the end times,
with partial fulfillment in historical figures like Antiochus
Epiphanes or certain Roman emperors.

The climax (vv.13-14) is among the most important Messianic
passages in Scripture. Jesus's favorite self-designation — "Son
of Man" — comes directly from here. When He told Caiaphas "ye
shall see the Son of man sitting on the right hand of power, and
coming in the clouds of heaven" (Matthew 26:64), He was quoting
Daniel 7:13 — and the high priest understood perfectly, tearing
his robes at the "blasphemy."

Structure:
- The Four Beasts from the Sea (vv.1-8)
- The Ancient of Days Takes His Throne (vv.9-10)
- The Beast Destroyed (vv.11-12)
- The Son of Man Receives Everlasting Dominion (vv.13-14)
- Daniel Troubled; An Angel Interprets (vv.15-18)
- The Fourth Beast and the Little Horn Explained (vv.19-25)
- The Saints Possess the Kingdom Forever (vv.26-28)

CROSS-REFERENCES
------------------------------------
v.7   — Revelation 13:1 — The beast from the sea with ten horns
         — directly echoing Daniel's fourth beast.
v.8   — Revelation 13:5 — The mouth "speaking great things" —
         the Antichrist's blasphemy.
v.9   — Revelation 1:14 — Christ's hair "white like wool" —
         sharing the Ancient of Days' attributes.
v.10  — Revelation 20:12 — "the books were opened... the dead
         were judged out of those things written in the books."
v.13  — Matthew 26:64 — Jesus quotes this verse to Caiaphas at
         His trial.
v.13  — Acts 1:9 — Jesus ascending on clouds — fulfilling the
         Son of Man imagery.
v.14  — Philippians 2:9-11 — "every tongue should confess that
         Jesus Christ is Lord."
v.25  — Revelation 12:14 — "time, and times, and half a time" —
         the same period of persecution.

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. God sees empires as BEASTS (vv.3-7). We see impressive
   civilizations; God sees predatory animals devouring the earth.
   Don't be impressed by what God considers beastly. Political
   power without God is just a better-dressed predator.

2. The Ancient of Days is seated (v.9). While beasts rage below,
   God is SEATED — calm, enthroned, unhurried. He opens the
   books when He's ready. No beast accelerates His timeline.
   If you're anxious about world events, look at the THRONE.

3. The Son of Man receives everything (v.14). Jesus is not still
   fighting for the kingdom — He has RECEIVED it. The battle
   is won. We live in the "already/not yet" — the kingdom is
   His; its full manifestation is coming. Live like someone on
   the winning side.

4. Saints are persecuted THEN reign (vv.25-27). The order matters:
   suffering first, glory after. Don't be surprised by opposition.
   The little horn "wears out" the saints — but wearing out is
   not the same as winning. Endurance is victory.

5. "All peoples, nations, and languages should serve him" (v.14).
   The multi-ethnic, multilingual worship of Revelation 7:9 is
   guaranteed HERE. Every tongue will confess. Every culture will
   bow. Missions is not uncertain work — it's joining an
   inevitable outcome.
