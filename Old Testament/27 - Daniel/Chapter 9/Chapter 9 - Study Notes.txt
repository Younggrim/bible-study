Daniel - Chapter 9
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Daniel reads Jeremiah's prophecy of 70 years of exile and responds with one of Scripture's greatest prayers of confession and intercession. Gabriel then appears with the stunning "70 weeks" prophecy: 490 years are decreed for Israel, culminating in Messiah being "cut off" after 69 weeks, the destruction of Jerusalem, and an end-times prince who confirms a covenant for the final week.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Daniel son of nobility, exiled to Babylon in 605 BC.
This vision occurs in "the first year of Darius" (539 BC) —
the very year Babylon fell. Daniel was approximately 82 years
old and had been in exile for 66 years. The 70-year captivity
was nearly complete.

Classification: Intercessory Prayer / Messianic Prophecy
Key Themes: Corporate confession and repentance, the power of
Scripture-driven prayer, God's faithfulness to His word, the
precise timeline to Messiah, the cost of sin and the price of
atonement, prophetic timetables

Historical Context: Daniel was reading Jeremiah 25:11-12 and
29:10 — the 70-year prophecy. With 66 years elapsed, restoration
was NEAR. But rather than passively waiting, Daniel PRAYED. He
understood that God's promises don't eliminate the need for prayer
— they FUEL it. He prayed because God promised, not despite God's
promise.

Daniel's prayer (vv.4-19) is entirely about GOD: His greatness,
His faithfulness, His covenant, His righteousness, His mercy. It
contains NO self-justification. Daniel includes HIMSELF in the
confession ("WE have sinned") though he personally was blameless
(ch.6). He identifies with his people's sin rather than
distancing himself from it.

The 70 weeks prophecy (vv.24-27) is among the most precisely
fulfilled and hotly debated passages in Scripture. "Seventy
weeks" (literally "seventy sevens" = 490 years) are decreed for
Israel. From the decree to restore Jerusalem (445 BC, Nehemiah 2)
to Messiah the Prince = 69 weeks (483 years) — landing precisely
at AD 32/33, the triumphal entry. Messiah is then "cut off" (the
crucifixion). The prophetic clock pauses; the 70th week awaits
future fulfillment.

Structure:
- Daniel Reads Jeremiah: The 70 Years Nearly Complete (vv.1-3)
- Daniel's Prayer of Confession (vv.4-14)
- Daniel's Plea for Mercy (vv.15-19)
- Gabriel Arrives: "Greatly Beloved" (vv.20-23)
- The 70 Weeks Prophecy (vv.24-27)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.3  — "And I set my face unto the Lord God, to seek by prayer
        and supplications, with fasting, and sackcloth, and ashes"
        (KJV) — Daniel didn't just read the promise; he PRAYED the
        promise into fulfillment. God's sovereignty and human
        intercession are not contradictory — they work together.
        Promise-based prayer is the most confident prayer.

v.18 — "for we do not present our supplications before thee for
        our righteousnesses, but for thy great mercies" (KJV) —
        The ground of prayer: NOT our righteousness but God's
        mercy. Even 82-year-old, lion-den-surviving Daniel didn't
        approach God on his own merit. Mercy alone.

v.23 — "for thou art greatly beloved" (KJV) — Gabriel's word to
        Daniel: "greatly beloved" (Hebrew "chamudot" = precious,
        treasured). What a thing to hear from heaven! Daniel was
        treasured by God. The man of prayer is the man God
        treasures.

v.25 — "Know therefore and understand, that from the going forth
        of the commandment to restore and to build Jerusalem unto
        the Messiah the Prince shall be seven weeks, and
        threescore and two weeks" (KJV) — 7 + 62 = 69 weeks
        (483 years). From the decree to rebuild (445 BC, Nehemiah
        2:1-8) to Messiah the Prince = precisely to Jesus's
        triumphal entry. Mathematical prophecy fulfilled to the
        year.

v.26 — "And after threescore and two weeks shall Messiah be cut
        off, but not for himself" (KJV) — "Cut off" = executed/
        killed. "Not for himself" = not for His own sins but for
        OURS. This is the crucifixion, prophesied 500+ years in
        advance. The Innocent One dying for the guilty.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.24 — "Seventy weeks" (KJV) / "Seventy weeks" (ESV) — Hebrew
      "shavu'im shiv'im" = literally "sevens seventy" = seventy
      units of seven. Context demands these are weeks of YEARS
      (not days), giving 70 × 7 = 490 years. The same Hebrew
      word "shavua" is used for a seven-year period in Genesis
      29:27-28 (Jacob's "weeks" of service).

v.25 — "the Messiah the Prince" (KJV) / "an anointed one, a
      prince" (ESV) — Hebrew "Mashiach Nagid" = the Anointed
      Ruler. "Mashiach" = Messiah/Christ (the Anointed One).
      "Nagid" = prince/ruler/leader. The compound title
      emphasizes both His priestly anointing AND His royal
      authority.

v.26 — "cut off" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "yikkaret" = to be cut off,
      to be executed. The same verb used for covenant-breaking
      penalties (Genesis 17:14) and for execution by divine
      judgment. It implies violent, premature death — not natural
      death in old age.

v.27 — "abomination" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "shiqquts" = detestable
      thing, an idol or idolatrous practice. "Abomination of
      desolation" (shiqquts meshomem) = a detestable thing that
      causes horror/desolation. Jesus references this in Matthew
      24:15 as a yet-future event.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Seventy Weeks — 490 years (70 × 7) decreed for Israel. Divided
              into 7 weeks (49 years — Jerusalem rebuilt), 62
              weeks (434 years — to Messiah), and 1 final week
              (7 years — the tribulation period).

Messiah Cut Off — The crucifixion of Christ, prophesied as
              occurring AFTER the 69th week. "Not for himself"
              = vicarious, substitutionary atonement. He died
              for others' sins, not His own.

The Prince That Shall Come — A future ruler (v.27) from the
              people who destroyed Jerusalem (Rome, AD 70). He
              confirms a covenant for one "week" (7 years) then
              breaks it midway — the end-times Antichrist.

Abomination of Desolation — An idolatrous desecration in the
              holy place. Historically: Antiochus (168 BC) and
              Titus (AD 70). Prophetically: a future act by the
              Antichrist (Matthew 24:15, 2 Thessalonians 2:4).

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Daniel PRAYED what he READ (vv.2-3). He found God's promise
   in Scripture and immediately turned it into prayer. How often
   do you read promises and never pray them? Scripture fuels
   prayer; prayer activates Scripture. Read, then ask.

2. "We have sinned" (v.5). Daniel identified with his people's
   sin though he was personally faithful. Do you confess only
   YOUR sins, or do you intercede for your church, your nation,
   your generation? Corporate confession is powerful and rare.

3. "Not for our righteousnesses but for thy great mercies" (v.18).
   Even the holiest man in Babylon didn't approach God on merit.
   If Daniel needed mercy, so do you. Stop trying to earn
   audience with God. Come on the basis of His character, not
   yours.

4. "Greatly beloved" (v.23). You are treasured by heaven. The
   same God who called Daniel precious calls YOU His beloved
   (Ephesians 1:6 — "accepted in the beloved"). You pray to a
   God who delights in hearing from you.

5. Messiah was "cut off, but not for himself" (v.26). The Cross
   was planned 500 years before it happened — 490 years
   precisely mapped. Your salvation isn't an afterthought. It's
   the centerpiece of history, prophesied to the year, executed
   on schedule. You are THAT loved.
