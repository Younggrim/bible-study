Daniel - Chapter 9
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Daniel reads Jeremiah's prophecy of 70 years of exile and responds with one of Scripture's greatest prayers of confession and intercession. Gabriel then appears with the stunning "70 weeks" prophecy: 490 years are decreed for Israel, culminating in Messiah being "cut off" after 69 weeks, the destruction of Jerusalem, and an end-times prince who confirms a covenant for the final week.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Daniel son of nobility, exiled to Babylon in 605 BC.
This vision occurs in "the first year of Darius" (539 BC) —
the very year Babylon fell. Daniel was approximately 82 years
old and had been in exile for 66 years. The 70-year captivity
was nearly complete.

Classification: Intercessory Prayer / Messianic Prophecy
Key Themes: Corporate confession and repentance, the power of
Scripture-driven prayer, God's faithfulness to His word, the
precise timeline to Messiah, the cost of sin and the price of
atonement, prophetic timetables

Historical Context: Daniel was reading Jeremiah 25:11-12 and
29:10 — the 70-year prophecy. With 66 years elapsed, restoration
was NEAR. But rather than passively waiting, Daniel PRAYED. He
understood that God's promises don't eliminate the need for prayer
— they FUEL it. He prayed because God promised, not despite God's
promise.

Daniel's prayer (vv.4-19) is entirely about GOD: His greatness,
His faithfulness, His covenant, His righteousness, His mercy. It
contains NO self-justification. Daniel includes HIMSELF in the
confession ("WE have sinned") though he personally was blameless
(ch.6). He identifies with his people's sin rather than
distancing himself from it.

The 70 weeks prophecy (vv.24-27) is among the most precisely
fulfilled and hotly debated passages in Scripture. "Seventy
weeks" (literally "seventy sevens" = 490 years) are decreed for
Israel. From the decree to restore Jerusalem (445 BC, Nehemiah 2)
to Messiah the Prince = 69 weeks (483 years) — landing precisely
at AD 32/33, the triumphal entry. Messiah is then "cut off" (the
crucifixion). The prophetic clock pauses; the 70th week awaits
future fulfillment.

Structure:
- Daniel Reads Jeremiah: The 70 Years Nearly Complete (vv.1-3)
- Daniel's Prayer of Confession (vv.4-14)
- Daniel's Plea for Mercy (vv.15-19)
- Gabriel Arrives: "Greatly Beloved" (vv.20-23)
- The 70 Weeks Prophecy (vv.24-27)

Charles Spurgeon:
  "Messiah cut off — but NOT for Himself. Here is the gospel in
  the Old Testament: the Innocent dying for the guilty, the
  Prince slain for the rebel. Daniel saw it five centuries before
  Calvary. And the mathematics! God gave the very YEAR His Son
  would die. This is no accident, no coincidence — this is the
  eternal plan of a God who numbers hairs and names stars and
  counts years to the Cross."

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. Daniel PRAYED what he READ (vv.2-3). He found God's promise
   in Scripture and immediately turned it into prayer. How often
   do you read promises and never pray them? Scripture fuels
   prayer; prayer activates Scripture. Read, then ask.

2. "We have sinned" (v.5). Daniel identified with his people's
   sin though he was personally faithful. Do you confess only
   YOUR sins, or do you intercede for your church, your nation,
   your generation? Corporate confession is powerful and rare.

3. "Not for our righteousnesses but for thy great mercies" (v.18).
   Even the holiest man in Babylon didn't approach God on merit.
   If Daniel needed mercy, so do you. Stop trying to earn
   audience with God. Come on the basis of His character, not
   yours.

4. "Greatly beloved" (v.23). You are treasured by heaven. The
   same God who called Daniel precious calls YOU His beloved
   (Ephesians 1:6 — "accepted in the beloved"). You pray to a
   God who delights in hearing from you.

5. Messiah was "cut off, but not for himself" (v.26). The Cross
   was planned 500 years before it happened — 490 years
   precisely mapped. Your salvation isn't an afterthought. It's
   the centerpiece of history, prophesied to the year, executed
   on schedule. You are THAT loved.
