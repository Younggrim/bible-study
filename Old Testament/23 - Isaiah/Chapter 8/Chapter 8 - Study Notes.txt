Isaiah - Chapter 8
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Isaiah names his second son Maher-shalal-hash-baz ("speed the spoil, hasten the plunder") as a sign that Assyria will swiftly destroy Syria and Israel — but Assyria's flood will also reach Judah up to the neck, and the people must fear God alone, not conspiracies.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Isaiah son of Amoz (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Sign-Act and Instruction
Key Themes: The named child as prophetic sign, Assyria as God's
flood overflowing Judah, the Immanuel connection ("thy land, O
Immanuel"), the stumbling stone, rejecting spiritists for God's
word, darkness for those who refuse the light

Historical Context: Continuing from chapter 7, Isaiah performs
another sign-act. Before witnesses, he writes "Maher-shalal-hash-
baz" (the longest name in the Bible — meaning "swift is the booty,
speedy is the prey"). His wife (the prophetess) bears a son with
this name. Before the child can say "mama" or "dada," Damascus
and Samaria will fall to Assyria. But because Judah rejected God's
"gently flowing waters of Shiloah" (v.6 — God's quiet provision
through David's line), God sends instead the "mighty and many
waters" of the Euphrates (Assyria) — flooding all the way to
Judah's neck (v.8). Verses 11-22 shift to personal instruction:
don't fear what the people fear; God Himself is the sanctuary OR
the stumbling stone. And when people suggest consulting the dead
instead of God: "To the law and to the testimony" (v.20).

Structure:
- The Sign-Child: Maher-shalal-hash-baz (vv.1-4)
- The Assyrian Flood Overwhelming the Land (vv.5-8)
- God Challenges the Nations (vv.9-10)
- Fear God, Not Conspiracies (vv.11-13)
- The Stone of Stumbling and Sanctuary (vv.14-15)
- Bind Up the Testimony (vv.16-18)
- Against Spiritism: To the Law! (vv.19-22)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.6  — "this people refuseth the waters of Shiloah that go
        softly" (KJV) — Shiloah (Siloam) — the gentle stream
        supplying Jerusalem. A metaphor for God's quiet, humble
        provision through David's line. Judah wanted dramatic
        power (Assyria); God offered gentle faithfulness.

v.8  — "he shall pass through Judah; he shall overflow and go
        over, he shall reach even to the neck" (KJV) — Assyria
        as a flood reaching Judah's neck — nearly drowning but not
        quite. God's mercy: the flood doesn't go over the HEAD
        (Jerusalem survives, barely). Then: "thy land, O Immanuel"
        — the land belongs to God-with-us, not to Assyria.

v.13 — "Sanctify the LORD of hosts himself; and let him be your
        fear, and let him be your dread" (KJV) — Don't fear
        conspiracies or invasions — fear GOD. When God is your
        dread, nothing else can terrify you. Right fear eliminates
        wrong fear.

v.14 — "And he shall be for a sanctuary; but for a stone of
        stumbling and for a rock of offence to both the houses of
        Israel" (KJV) — God is BOTH sanctuary (for those who trust)
        AND stumbling stone (for those who reject). The same God —
        two responses produce two outcomes. Peter (1 Pet 2:8) and
        Paul (Rom 9:33) both quote this verse about Christ.

v.20 — "To the law and to the testimony: if they speak not
        according to this word, it is because there is no light in
        them" (KJV) — The test of all teaching: does it align with
        God's revealed word? If not, there is NO LIGHT (no dawn,
        no truth) in the speaker. Scripture as the final authority.

CROSS-REFERENCES
------------------------------------
v.4   — 2 Kings 16:9 — Damascus falls to Tiglath-pileser in
         732 BC — fulfilling Isaiah's sign within 2 years.
v.6   — John 9:7 — The pool of Siloam where Jesus sends the
         blind man — the "sent" waters now heal.
v.8   — Isaiah 30:28 — "his breath, as an overflowing stream,
         shall reach to the midst of the neck."
v.14  — Romans 9:33 — "I lay in Sion a stumblingstone and rock
         of offence: and whosoever believeth on him shall not be
         ashamed."
v.14  — 1 Peter 2:6-8 — Peter combines Isaiah 8:14 and 28:16 —
         Christ as cornerstone AND stumbling stone.
v.14  — Luke 2:34 — Simeon: "this child is set for the fall and
         rising again of many in Israel."
v.19  — Deuteronomy 18:10-12 — Prohibition of consulting the
         dead (necromancy) — an abomination to God.
v.20  — 2 Timothy 3:16 — "All scripture is given by inspiration
         of God" — the law and testimony as final authority.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "Mahershalalhashbaz" (KJV) / "Maher-shalal-hash-baz" (ESV)
      — Hebrew: four words compressed into a name meaning "swift
      (is the) spoil, speedy (is the) prey." The longest personal
      name in the Bible — a walking prophecy of Assyria's rapid
      conquest.

v.6  — "Shiloah" (KJV) / "Shiloah" (ESV) — the aqueduct/tunnel
      bringing water from the Gihon spring into Jerusalem. Gentle,
      quiet, life-sustaining — contrasted with the Euphrates'
      violent flood.

v.14 — "sanctuary" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "miqdash" = holy place/
      sacred space. God Himself as the place of safety — not a
      building but a Person.

v.19 — "familiar spirits" (KJV) / "mediums" (ESV) — Hebrew "ovot"
      = those who conjure the dead through pits or ventriloquism.
      "Wizards" (KJV) / "necromancers" (ESV) — "yid'onim" = knowing
      ones, those claiming access to supernatural knowledge through
      the dead.

v.20 — "to the law and to the testimony" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew
      "le-torah u-le-te'udah" — God's revealed instruction and His
      attested word. The double standard by which all claims to
      spiritual authority must be tested.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Maher-shalal-hash-baz — "Swift is the spoil, speedy is the prey."
               Isaiah's second son, a prophetic sign that Assyria
               will quickly plunder Syria and Israel.

Waters of Shiloah — The gentle stream flowing from the Gihon
               spring into Jerusalem through Hezekiah's tunnel.
               Symbolizes God's quiet, faithful provision through
               David's dynasty — rejected by Judah in favor of
               foreign alliances.

Stone of stumbling — God/Christ as an obstacle that trips up those
               who refuse to trust Him. The same Person who is a
               sanctuary to believers becomes a trap to the
               faithless. A key NT Christological text.

Familiar spirits — Practitioners of necromancy (communication with
               the dead). Forbidden in Torah (Lev 19:31, Deut 18:11).
               When people turn from God's word, they turn to the
               occult for guidance.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
- v.8 — All translations: "thy land, O Immanuel" / "your land, Immanuel" / NET: "your entire land, O Immanuel" — This crucial address connects the Assyrian flood to the Immanuel prophecy of 7:14. The land being invaded belongs to "God with us" — even in judgment, divine ownership is affirmed. NET adds "entire" for emphasis.

- v.13 — ASV: "Jehovah of hosts, him shall ye sanctify; and let him be your fear" / WEB: "Yahweh of Armies is who you must respect as holy. He is the one you must fear" / NET: "You must recognize the authority of the Lord of Heaven's Armies. He is the one you must respect; he is the one you must fear" — NET changes "sanctify" (Hebrew qadash = set apart as holy) to "recognize the authority" — a significant theological shift from worship language to mere acknowledgment.

- v.14 — WEB omits a key phrase: "but for both houses of Israel, he will be a trap and a snare" — WEB drops "a stone of stumbling and a rock of offence" that KJV/ASV include. This is the verse Paul quotes in Romans 9:33 about Christ. The ASV preserves both "sanctuary" and "stumbling stone" — God as both refuge and obstacle.

- v.19 — NET: "Seek oracles at the pits used to conjure up underworld spirits, from the magicians who chirp and mutter incantations" vs. KJV: "Seek unto them that have familiar spirits and unto the wizards, that chirp and that mutter" — NET adds extensive interpretive explanation ("pits used to conjure up underworld spirits"), while KJV/ASV/WEB preserve the mysterious language of Hebrew "ovot" and "yid'onim."

- v.20 — ASV: "To the law and to the testimony! if they speak not according to this word, surely there is no morning for them" / WEB: same / NET: "Then you must recall the Lord's instructions and the prophetic testimony of what would happen. Certainly they say such things because their minds are spiritually darkened" — NET completely restructures this verse. The Hebrew's terse command ("To Torah! To testimony!") becomes explanatory prose, and "no morning" (no dawn/no light) becomes "minds are spiritually darkened."

- v.3 — NET: "I then approached the prophetess for marital relations" vs. KJV/ASV/WEB: "I went unto the prophetess" — NET adds "for marital relations" — making explicit what the Hebrew states euphemistically with "went to" (a standard biblical idiom for conjugal relations). KJV/ASV/WEB let the context speak.

- v.1 — ASV: "And Jehovah said unto me" / WEB: "Yahweh said to me" / NET: "The Lord told me" — The divine name usage throughout this chapter: ASV consistently uses "Jehovah," WEB uses "Yahweh," NET uses "the Lord." The name carries covenant weight in a passage about trusting God over political conspiracies.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Judah rejected "the waters of Shiloah that go softly" (v.6).
   God's provision often comes quietly — not flashy, not dramatic,
   not impressive to the watching world. Do you despise God's
   gentle provision because it doesn't look powerful enough?

2. "Let HIM be your fear" (v.13). What are you afraid of right
   now? Conspiracy theories? Political upheaval? Economic collapse?
   Health crisis? Isaiah's answer: make GOD your fear, and
   everything else shrinks to proper proportion.

3. God is BOTH sanctuary and stumbling stone (v.14). Same God,
   same Christ — two utterly different experiences depending on
   your response. Which is He to you? Are you running TO Him for
   refuge or tripping OVER Him in offense?

4. "To the law and to the testimony" (v.20). When confusion
   reigns, when voices multiply, when spiritual claims compete —
   the test is simple: does it agree with Scripture? If not, there
   is no light in it. Period. How well do you know God's word —
   well enough to test what you hear?

5. The chapter ends in darkness for those who reject God's word
   (vv.21-22). No other source of light exists. Reject Scripture
   and you get darkness — looking everywhere for answers and
   finding only "trouble and darkness, dimness of anguish." There
   is no plan B to God's revelation.
