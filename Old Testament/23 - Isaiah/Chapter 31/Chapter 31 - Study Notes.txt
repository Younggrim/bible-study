Isaiah - Chapter 31
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Woe to those who go down to Egypt for help — trusting horses and chariots instead of the Holy One of Israel. Egypt is flesh, not spirit; their horses are not God. Yet the LORD will fight FOR Jerusalem like a lion over its prey, and like birds hovering to protect their young.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Isaiah son of Amoz (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Woe Oracle — Fourth Woe (chs. 28-33)
Key Themes: The folly of trusting Egypt's military, horses vs.
the Holy Spirit, God as a lion defending His prey, God as
protective birds over Jerusalem, the call to turn from idols,
Assyria's supernatural defeat

Historical Context: This short, powerful chapter continues the
anti-Egypt polemic. Judah's military strategists wanted Egyptian
cavalry ("horses") and chariot divisions — the ancient equivalent
of tanks. Isaiah's point: Egypt is FLESH (v.3) — mortal, finite,
killable. God is SPIRIT. You're trading the infinite for the
finite. The LORD Himself will descend to fight on Mount Zion
(v.4) — which was fulfilled when the angel of the LORD destroyed
185,000 Assyrians in one night (2 Kings 19:35, Isaiah 37:36).

Structure:
- Woe: Trusting Egypt's Horses (vv.1-3)
- The LORD as Lion and Hovering Birds (vv.4-5)
- The Call to Turn from Idols (vv.6-7)
- Assyria Falls by a Sword Not of Man (vv.8-9)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "Woe to them that go down to Egypt for help; and stay on
        horses, and trust in chariots, because they are many; and
        in horsemen, because they are very strong; but they look
        not unto the Holy One of Israel, neither seek the LORD!"
        (KJV) — The fundamental sin: looking to human power
        instead of God. Not that horses are evil — but they are
        NOT GOD.

v.3  — "Now the Egyptians are men, and not God; and their horses
        flesh, and not spirit" (KJV) — The simplest, most
        devastating contrast in Isaiah. Men vs. God. Flesh vs.
        Spirit. Finite vs. Infinite. When you choose Egypt, you
        choose the weaker option.

v.4  — "Like as the lion and the young lion roaring on his prey...
        so shall the LORD of hosts come down to fight for mount
        Zion" (KJV) — God is not a tame rescuer. He's a LION —
        fierce, undeterred, refusing to be frightened away from
        His claim (Zion). No group of shepherds (nations) can
        scare Him off.

v.5  — "As birds flying, so will the LORD of hosts defend
        Jerusalem; defending also he will deliver it; and passing
        over he will preserve it" (KJV) — The tenderness after
        the fierceness: protective birds hovering over their young.
        "Passing over" echoes the Passover (Exodus 12) — same God,
        same protection.

v.8  — "Then shall the Assyrian fall with the sword, not of a
        mighty man; and the sword, not of man, shall devour him"
        (KJV) — Assyria's defeat will be supernatural. No human
        army will do it — God's own sword. Fulfilled: 185,000
        killed by the angel of the LORD (2 Kings 19:35).

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.3  — "flesh, and not spirit" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "basar v'lo
      ruach" = flesh and not spirit. The fundamental biblical
      dichotomy: flesh (finite, mortal, weak) vs. spirit (divine,
      eternal, powerful). Egypt is on the wrong side of this line.

v.4  — "come down to fight FOR mount Zion" (ESV) / "fight for
      mount Zion" (KJV) — Hebrew "al" can mean "against" or
      "upon/for." Context determines: God fights ON BEHALF of
      Zion, not against it. He comes DOWN (theophany language).

v.5  — "passing over" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "pasoach" = the same
      root as "Pesach" (Passover). Deliberate Exodus echo: God
      passes over to protect, just as He passed over the blood-
      marked doorposts in Egypt.

v.8  — "not of a mighty man... not of man" (KJV) — Hebrew "lo
      ish... lo adam" = not a warrior... not a human. Emphatic
      double denial of human agency. This victory belongs to
      God alone.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Horses/Chariots — Egypt's primary military export: cavalry and
              chariot divisions. The ancient equivalent of heavy
              armor. Judah's leaders wanted this technology instead
              of trusting God.

Flesh vs. Spirit — The fundamental Isaiah contrast (v.3):
              everything human is "flesh" (limited, mortal);
              only God is "spirit" (unlimited, eternal). Trusting
              flesh over spirit is category confusion.

Passing Over — (v.5) Deliberate Passover language: God's
              protective "passing over" His people while
              destroying the enemy. Same verb, same action,
              same God.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
- v.1 — ASV: "neither seek Jehovah" / WEB: "they don't seek Yahweh" / NET: "do not seek help from the Lord" — NET adds "help from" (not in Hebrew), narrowing the seeking to pragmatic aid rather than the broader relational seeking of God Himself that the Hebrew "darash YHWH" implies.

- v.1 — NET: "Those who go down to Egypt for help are as good as dead" — NET renders "Woe" (Hebrew "hoy") as "as good as dead" — making the funeral connotation explicit but losing the prophetic lament form. KJV/ASV/WEB retain the traditional "Woe" oracle formula.

- v.4 — ASV: "so will Jehovah of hosts come down to fight upon mount Zion" / WEB: "so Yahweh of Armies will come down to fight on Mount Zion" / NET: "the Lord of Heaven's Armies will descend to do battle on Mount Zion" — Three renderings of YHWH Sabaoth at the theophany moment. NET's "descend to do battle" is more formal but captures the warrior-God descending.

- v.5 — WEB: "As birds hovering, so Yahweh of Armies will protect Jerusalem" / ASV: "As birds hovering, so will Jehovah of hosts protect Jerusalem" / NET: "Just as birds hover over a nest, so the Lord of Heaven's Armies will protect Jerusalem" — NET adds "over a nest" (not explicit in Hebrew), clarifying the maternal protection imagery. The Hebrew simply says "birds flying/hovering" — the nest is implied, not stated.

- v.3 — NET: "The Lord will strike with his hand" — KJV: "When the LORD shall stretch out his hand." The Hebrew "natah yad" means to stretch out/extend the hand — a gesture of judgment. NET's "strike" collapses the image into immediate action; KJV/ASV/WEB preserve the deliberate, ominous extension.

- v.9 — ASV: "saith Jehovah, whose fire is in Zion, and his furnace in Jerusalem" / WEB: "says Yahweh, whose fire is in Zion, and his furnace in Jerusalem" / NET: "the one whose fire is in Zion, whose firepot is in Jerusalem" — NET's "firepot" (vs. "furnace") is more technically accurate for the Hebrew "tannur" (clay oven), but "furnace" (KJV/ASV/WEB) carries more intensity. God's fire dwells permanently in Zion.

- v.8 — NET: "Assyria will fall by a sword, but not one human made; a sword not made by humankind will destroy them" — This expanded paraphrase makes explicit what KJV states more mysteriously: "the sword, not of a mighty man... not of a mean man." The ambiguity in KJV heightens the supernatural terror; NET explains it away.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "The Egyptians are men, and not God" (v.3). Write this over
   every human institution you trust. Your government is men,
   not God. Your doctor is a man, not God. Your financial advisor
   is a man, not God. Good gifts — but not God. Only God is God.

2. The lion AND the bird (vv.4-5). God protects with BOTH fierce
   aggression (lion) and tender hovering (bird). He's not one-
   dimensional. He fights like a lion against your enemies and
   hovers like a mother bird over your vulnerability.

3. "Not of man" (v.8). The decisive victories in your life will
   be supernatural. Stop calculating whether YOUR resources are
   enough. They're not — and they don't need to be. The sword
   that defeats your Assyria is "not of man."

4. Turn from your idols (vv.6-7). In the same breath as
   promising deliverance, God calls for repentance. His rescue
   is not unconditional permission to keep sinning. If He's
   going to fight FOR you, He expects you to fight AGAINST
   your idols.
