Isaiah - Chapter 50
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The third Servant Song — the Servant has "the tongue of the learned" to sustain the weary, offers His back to smiters and His cheeks to beard-pluckers, sets His face like flint toward Jerusalem, and trusts God to vindicate Him. Those who walk in darkness should trust; those who kindle their own fire will lie down in sorrow.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Isaiah son of Amoz (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Oracle — Third Servant Song (vv.4-9)
Key Themes: God didn't divorce Israel, the Servant's obedient
ear, suffering willingly endured, face set like flint, trust in
God amid opposition, the danger of self-made light

Historical Context: The third Servant Song (50:4-9) reveals the
Servant's OBEDIENCE amid suffering. Unlike the first song
(commission) and second (discouragement overcome), this song
shows the Servant voluntarily submitting to physical abuse. He
offers His back, His cheeks, His face — actively presenting
Himself to suffering. "I gave my back to the smiters" — not "they
took it" but "I GAVE it." Luke 9:51: "He steadfastly set his
face to go to Jerusalem" directly echoes v.7. The chapter frames
the song with God's defense of Israel (vv.1-3) and a choice for
the listener: trust God in darkness (v.10) or make your own light
and suffer (v.11).

Structure:
- God Didn't Divorce Israel (vv.1-3)
- Third Servant Song: Obedient Suffering (vv.4-9)
- Trust in Darkness vs. Self-Made Fire (vv.10-11)

  could have stopped it with a word. He chose not to. That's love."

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. "I gave my back" (v.6). Jesus GAVE. Every blow was voluntary.
   He could have called 10,000 angels (Matt 26:53). He chose to
   stay. His suffering was not victimhood but LOVE choosing pain
   for your freedom.

2. "Set my face like a flint" (v.7). Following God requires
   RESOLVE — flint-hard determination that doesn't flinch at
   pain or turn aside at opposition. What is God calling you
   toward that requires this kind of flint-face?

3. Walk in darkness and TRUST (v.10). Sometimes God-fearing
   people are in darkness — not because of sin but because of
   season. The instruction: TRUST. Don't demand explanations;
   trust the God you knew in the light.

4. Don't kindle your own fire (v.11). When God seems silent and
   the darkness presses in, the temptation is to manufacture
   your own solutions, guidance, and light. But self-made light
   leads to sorrow. WAIT for God's dawn rather than lighting
   your own candle.

5. "A word in season to him that is weary" (v.4). The Servant's
   gift: knowing what weary people need to hear. Ask God for this
   gift — the ability to speak the right word at the right time
   to exhausted people.
