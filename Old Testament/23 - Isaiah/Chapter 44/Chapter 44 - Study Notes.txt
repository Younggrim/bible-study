Isaiah - Chapter 44
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God pours out His Spirit on Israel's offspring, mocks idol-makers with devastating satire (a man uses half a log for firewood and carves the other half into a "god"), declares "I am the first and the last," and names Cyrus as the shepherd who will rebuild Jerusalem.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Isaiah son of Amoz (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Oracle — Spirit Promise, Idol Satire,
and Cyrus Commission
Key Themes: The Spirit poured on descendants, God as first and
last (Alpha and Omega), the absurdity of idol-making, Israel
redeemed and not forgotten, Cyrus named as God's shepherd

Historical Context: This chapter combines tender promise (vv.1-5),
theological declaration (vv.6-8), devastating satire (vv.9-20),
and stunning prophecy (vv.24-28). The idol-satire section is
Isaiah's longest — a detailed exposé of the absurdity of carving
a god from the same wood you use for cooking. The chapter climaxes
with God naming CYRUS by name (v.28) — over 150 years before
Cyrus conquered Babylon (539 BC). This is one of Scripture's most
remarkable predictive prophecies.

Structure:
- The Spirit Poured on Offspring (vv.1-5)
- God Alone: First and Last (vv.6-8)
- The Absurdity of Idol-Making (vv.9-20)
- Remember: You Are My Servant (vv.21-23)
- Cyrus Named: Rebuild Jerusalem (vv.24-28)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.3  — "For I will pour water upon him that is thirsty, and
        floods upon the dry ground: I will pour my spirit upon thy
        seed" (KJV) — Water for the thirsty, Spirit for the
        offspring. The promise extends to FUTURE GENERATIONS.
        Pentecost fulfills this.

v.6  — "I am the first, and I am the last; and beside me there
        is no God" (KJV) — God's eternal self-identification.
        Jesus claims this title in Revelation 1:17; 22:13 —
        asserting deity.

v.8  — "Is there a God beside me? yea, there is no God; I know
        not any" (KJV) — God Himself testifies: He knows of no
        other god. If the omniscient God knows of none, none exist.

v.16-17 — "He burneth part thereof in the fire... and the residue
        thereof he maketh a god... he falleth down unto it, and
        worshippeth it, and prayeth unto it, and saith, Deliver
        me; for thou art my god" (KJV) — The sharpest satire in
        Scripture: same log — half for cooking dinner, half for
        worship. If the first half is firewood, what makes the
        second half divine?

v.22 — "I have blotted out, as a thick cloud, thy transgressions,
        and, as a cloud, thy sins: return unto me; for I have
        redeemed thee" (KJV) — Sins dispersed like clouds blown
        away by wind. They seemed to cover the sky — now they're
        gone. The invitation: come back, because redemption is
        already accomplished.

v.28 — "That saith of Cyrus, He is my shepherd, and shall perform
        all my pleasure: even saying to Jerusalem, Thou shalt be
        built; and to the temple, Thy foundation shall be laid"
        (KJV) — Cyrus NAMED 150+ years in advance. Called "my
        shepherd" — God's instrument for rebuilding Jerusalem
        and the temple (fulfilled: Ezra 1:1-4).

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.2  — "Jeshurun" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "Yeshurun" = "the upright
      one" — a poetic/affectionate name for Israel (cf.
      Deuteronomy 32:15; 33:5,26). God uses a pet name for
      His people.

v.5  — "surname himself" (KJV) / "write on his hand" (ESV) —
      Hebrew "yichtov yado la-YHWH" = write on his hand "belonging
      to the LORD." Like tattooing God's name on yourself —
      permanent identification with Him.

v.13 — "he marketh it out with a line" (KJV) — Detailed
      description of a craftsman's process: measuring, shaping,
      carving — all his skill goes into making... firewood into
      a god. The skill makes it MORE absurd, not less.

v.28 — "Cyrus" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "Koresh" = Persian royal name.
      Isaiah writes this c. 700 BC; Cyrus conquered Babylon in
      539 BC. Named prophetically before birth — cf. Josiah
      named in 1 Kings 13:2.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
First and Last — God's self-designation of eternal, uncreated
              existence. He precedes everything (first) and
              outlasts everything (last). No one before, no one
              after. Christ claims this title = deity claim.

Jeshurun     — "Upright One" — affectionate nickname for Israel.
              Used in poetry (Deuteronomy 32-33) to recall
              Israel's ideal identity as God's faithful people.

Cyrus        — Cyrus II ("the Great"), Persian emperor 559-530 BC.
              Conquered Babylon in 539 BC without a battle (the
              river was diverted). Issued a decree allowing exiled
              peoples to return home and rebuild temples (Ezra 1).

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
- v.5 — ASV: "One shall say, I am Jehovah's; and another shall call himself by the name of Jacob; and another shall subscribe with his hand unto Jehovah" / WEB: "One will say, 'I am Yahweh's'... another will write with his hand 'to Yahweh'" / NET: "One will say, 'I belong to the Lord,'... One will write on his hand, 'The Lord's'" — The divine name is prominent: people voluntarily mark themselves as belonging to Jehovah/Yahweh. NET substitutes "the Lord" throughout, flattening the personal-name devotion into a generic title. The act of writing God's NAME on one's hand is more intimate than writing a title.

- v.6 — ASV: "Thus saith Jehovah, the King of Israel, and his Redeemer, Jehovah of hosts: I am the first, and I am the last; and besides me there is no God" / WEB: "This is what Yahweh, the King of Israel, and his Redeemer, Yahweh of Armies, says: 'I am the first, and I am the last; and besides me there is no God'" / NET: "This is what the Lord, Israel's King, says, their Protector, the Lord of Heaven's Armies: 'I am the first and I am the last, there is no God but me'" — The most theologically weighty verse in the chapter. ASV/WEB double the divine name: "Jehovah/Yahweh... Jehovah/Yahweh of hosts/Armies." NET again renders "Redeemer" (go'el) as "Protector," removing the kinsman-redemption theology from this supreme monotheistic declaration. "LORD of hosts" becomes "Lord of Heaven's Armies" (NET) — adding "Heaven's" not in the Hebrew.

- v.22 — ASV: "I have blotted out, as a thick cloud, thy transgressions, and, as a cloud, thy sins: return unto me; for I have redeemed thee" / WEB: "I have blotted out, as a thick cloud, your transgressions, and, as a cloud, your sins. Return to me, for I have redeemed you" / NET: "I remove the guilt of your rebellious deeds as if they were a cloud, the guilt of your sins as if they were a cloud. Come back to me, for I protect you" — NET makes three significant changes: (1) "blotted out" becomes "remove the guilt" (adding interpretive layers), (2) "transgressions" becomes "rebellious deeds," and most critically, (3) "I have redeemed thee" (ga'alti) becomes "I protect you" — again eliminating the redemption verb in favor of protection language.

- v.23 — ASV: "Sing, O ye heavens, for Jehovah hath done it... for Jehovah hath redeemed Jacob, and will glorify himself in Israel" / WEB: "Sing, you heavens, for Yahweh has done it!... for Yahweh has redeemed Jacob, and will glorify himself in Israel" / NET: "Shout for joy, O sky, for the Lord intervenes... For the Lord protects Jacob; he reveals his splendor through Israel" — Another striking NET divergence: "redeemed Jacob" becomes "protects Jacob," systematically removing redemption language. "Glorify himself" becomes "reveals his splendor" — a paraphrase losing the reflexive force of God actively glorifying Himself in His people.

- v.24 — ASV: "Thus saith Jehovah, thy Redeemer, and he that formed thee from the womb: I am Jehovah, that maketh all things; that stretcheth forth the heavens alone" / WEB: "Yahweh, your Redeemer, and he who formed you from the womb says: 'I am Yahweh, who makes all things; who alone stretches out the heavens'" / NET: "This is what the Lord, your Protector, says, the one who formed you in the womb: 'I am the Lord, who made everything, who alone stretched out the sky'" — The divine name appears THREE times in ASV/WEB (Jehovah/Yahweh... Jehovah/Yahweh... Redeemer). NET reduces all to "the Lord" and again renders go'el as "Protector." Also: "heavens" (shamayim) becomes mere "sky" in NET — a less majestic rendering.

- v.28 — ASV: "That saith of Cyrus, He is my shepherd, and shall perform all my pleasure" / WEB: "Who says of Cyrus, 'He is my shepherd, and shall perform all my pleasure'" / NET: "who commissions Cyrus, the one I appointed as shepherd to carry out all my wishes" — NET adds interpretive language ("commissions," "appointed") where the Hebrew simply says "says of Cyrus: my shepherd." The simplicity of calling a pagan king "my shepherd" is expanded by NET into bureaucratic language. All translations preserve the stunning naming of Cyrus 150+ years before his reign.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. The Spirit poured on your offspring (v.3). God's promise
   extends to your CHILDREN and grandchildren. Pray and believe
   for generational blessing — God's Spirit on the next generation.

2. Same log: half for fire, half for worship (vv.15-17). What in
   your life are you treating as both ordinary and sacred? What
   created thing have you elevated to worship status? The test:
   if it's made of the same stuff as everything else, it's not God.

3. "I have blotted out, as a thick cloud" (v.22). Your sins WERE
   like clouds covering the sky — dark, oppressive, blocking the
   sun. God blew them away. The sky is clear. Stop looking at
   where the clouds WERE and start enjoying the open sky.

4. Cyrus named before birth (v.28). God knows the future in
   detail — including the names of people not yet born who will
   fulfill His purposes. Your life is not random; it's
   anticipated by a God who names His instruments in advance.
