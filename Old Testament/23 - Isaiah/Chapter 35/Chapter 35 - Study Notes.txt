Isaiah - Chapter 35
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The desert rejoices, the blind see, the deaf hear, the lame leap, streams break forth in the wilderness, and the ransomed of the LORD return to Zion with everlasting joy — sorrow and sighing flee away. The mirror image of chapter 34's judgment: pure, unrestrained redemption.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Isaiah son of Amoz (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Salvation Oracle — The Ransomed Return
Key Themes: Creation restored, physical healing, the Holy Way,
the ransomed returning to Zion, everlasting joy, no predator on
the road, the desert blooming

Historical Context: Chapter 35 is the redemptive counterpart to
chapter 34's judgment — together they present the complete picture:
wrath on God's enemies, salvation for God's people. This chapter
is among Isaiah's most beautiful and most quoted. The imagery
combines creation renewal (desert blooming), physical healing
(blind, deaf, lame, mute healed), and spiritual restoration
(the Highway of Holiness). Jesus explicitly pointed to these
signs when John the Baptist asked "Art thou he that should come?"
(Matthew 11:5). Partially fulfilled in Christ's first coming,
fully realized in the new creation.

Structure:
- The Desert Blooms: Creation Rejoices (vv.1-2)
- Strengthen the Weak: God is Coming (vv.3-4)
- The Healed: Blind, Deaf, Lame, Mute (vv.5-6a)
- Waters in the Wilderness (vv.6b-7)
- The Highway of Holiness (vv.8-9)
- The Ransomed Return to Zion with Joy (v.10)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "The wilderness and the solitary place shall be glad for
        them; and the desert shall rejoice, and blossom as the
        rose" (KJV) — Creation itself celebrates redemption. The
        desert — symbol of death, emptiness, curse — bursts into
        bloom. Nature participates in God's salvation.

v.4  — "Say to them that are of a fearful heart, Be strong, fear
        not: behold, your God will come with vengeance, even God
        with a recompence; he will come and save you" (KJV) — The
        message to the afraid: God is COMING. His arrival brings
        both vengeance (on enemies) and salvation (for His people).
        Same arrival, two outcomes.

v.5-6 — "Then the eyes of the blind shall be opened, and the ears
        of the deaf shall be unstopped. Then shall the lame man
        leap as an hart, and the tongue of the dumb sing" (KJV)
        — Complete physical restoration. Every disability reversed.
        Jesus fulfilled this LITERALLY — and pointed to it as proof
        of His messiahship (Matthew 11:5).

v.8  — "And an highway shall be there, and a way, and it shall be
        called The way of holiness; the unclean shall not pass
        over it" (KJV) — A sacred road: the Holy Way. Safe
        passage home for God's redeemed. No fool wanders onto it
        by accident; no predator lurks along it.

v.10 — "And the ransomed of the LORD shall return, and come to
        Zion with songs and everlasting joy upon their heads: they
        shall obtain joy and gladness, and sorrow and sighing shall
        flee away" (KJV) — The final destination: joy so permanent
        that sorrow literally RUNS AWAY. Not just the absence of
        grief but its banishment.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "rose" (KJV) / "crocus" (ESV) — Hebrew "chavatzelet" =
      probably the autumn crocus or narcissus, a bulb flower that
      blooms suddenly and brilliantly in desert conditions.

v.7  — "parched ground" (KJV) / "burning sand" (ESV) — Hebrew
      "sharav" = mirage/shimmering heat. The desert mirage that
      LOOKS like water but isn't — will become an actual pool.
      False hope replaced by reality.

v.8  — "Highway" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "maslul" = a raised, built
      road (from "salal" = to cast up, to build up). Not a dirt
      path but an engineered road — prepared, elevated, intentional.

v.10 — "ransomed" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "peduyey" (from "padah") =
      those redeemed by payment/purchase. A ransom was PAID for
      their freedom. The returning exiles are not self-rescued but
      bought back by God.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Highway of Holiness — The sacred road God builds for His
              redeemed. Safe (no predators), exclusive (no unclean),
              clear (no fool gets lost). Christ as "the Way"
              (John 14:6) fulfills this.

Ransomed     — Those purchased out of slavery/captivity by a
              kinsman-redeemer. God PAID for their freedom. The
              language of atonement: redemption costs something.

Everlasting Joy — Joy that doesn't end, diminish, or get
              interrupted by sorrow. Not cyclical (joy then grief
              then joy) but permanent and increasing.

Desert Blooming — Creation's response to redemption. The curse
              of Genesis 3 (thorns, barrenness) reversed. Nature
              healed alongside humanity.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
- v.1 — NET: "let the arid rift valley rejoice and bloom like a lily!" — NET paraphrases "desert" as "arid rift valley" (a geographic interpretation) and renders Hebrew "chavatzelet" as "lily" rather than "rose" (KJV/ASV) or the generic "blossom" approach of other versions.

- v.2 — ASV: "they shall see the glory of Jehovah" / WEB: "They will see Yahweh's glory" / NET: "the grandeur of the Lord" — The divine name YHWH is rendered "Jehovah" (ASV), "Yahweh" (WEB), and "the Lord" (NET). NET also substitutes "grandeur" for "glory," a more interpretive choice.

- v.4 — NET: "Tell those who panic, 'Be strong! Do not fear! Look, your God comes to avenge; with divine retribution he comes to deliver you.'" — NET uses "panic" instead of "fearful heart" (KJV/ASV) and "deliver" instead of "save," softening the personal directness of the original.

- v.7 — ASV: "the glowing sand shall become a pool" / WEB: "The burning sand will become a pool" / KJV: "the parched ground shall become a pool" — ASV and WEB translate Hebrew "sharav" as "glowing/burning sand" (referencing the desert mirage phenomenon), while KJV uses "parched ground." NET renders it "The dry soil will become a pool of water."

- v.8 — NET: "A thoroughfare will be there— it will be called the Way of Holiness... it is reserved for those authorized to use it— fools will not stray into it." — NET uniquely uses "thoroughfare" instead of "highway" and adds the interpretive phrase "those authorized to use it," which is not in the Hebrew but clarifies the meaning of exclusivity.

- v.10 — ASV: "the ransomed of Jehovah shall return" / WEB: "The Yahweh's ransomed ones will return" / NET: "those whom the Lord has ransomed will return that way" — Again the divine name distinction. NET adds "that way" (not in Hebrew) and paraphrases "everlasting joy upon their heads" as "Unending joy will crown them" — a dynamic equivalent that loses the concrete Hebrew image.

- v.6 — WEB: "the mute tongue will sing" / NET: "the mute tongue will shout for joy" — KJV/ASV have "the tongue of the dumb shall sing." NET intensifies with "shout for joy" and all modern versions replace "dumb" (KJV) with "mute" for clarity in contemporary English.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Be strong, fear not: your God WILL come" (v.4). If you're
   living in fear right now, this verse is addressed to YOU. Not
   "maybe" — WILL. God will come. Your job is to hold on until
   He arrives.

2. Jesus IS the fulfillment (vv.5-6). When He healed the blind,
   deaf, lame, and mute, He was saying "Isaiah 35 is happening
   NOW." The kingdom isn't just future — it's breaking in.

3. "Sorrow and sighing shall flee away" (v.10). This is your
   destination. Whatever grief you carry now, it has an expiration
   date. You will reach Zion. Joy will stay; sorrow will flee.
   Hold on.

4. The Highway of Holiness (v.8). There IS a safe road home. You
   don't have to navigate the wilderness alone. God built a road
   and cleared it of predators. Stay on it.

5. The desert blooms (v.1). The most barren areas of your life
   are candidates for God's most dramatic transformation. Don't
   write off your desert — it's future garden territory.
