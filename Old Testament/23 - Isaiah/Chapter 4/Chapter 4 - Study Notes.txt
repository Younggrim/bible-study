Isaiah - Chapter 4
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
After judgment purges Zion's filth, a glorious remnant emerges — the Branch of the LORD is beautiful, and God shelters His purified people with a cloud by day and fire by night, a new exodus canopy over Mount Zion.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Isaiah son of Amoz (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Oracle — Judgment Leading to Glory
Key Themes: The remnant who survive judgment, the Branch of the
LORD, purification through a "spirit of burning," the return of
God's glory-cloud over Zion, new exodus imagery

Historical Context: Chapter 4 concludes the section begun in
chapter 2. Verse 1 actually completes the thought of 3:25-26 —
after the men are killed in war, seven women grab one man, willing
to provide for themselves just to bear his name and remove their
reproach of childlessness. Then vv.2-6 shift dramatically to the
glorious future: the Branch of the LORD, the beauty of the land,
the purified remnant, and God's protective presence as a cloud
and fire (recalling the Exodus pilgrimage). This short chapter
is one of the most compressed messianic texts in Isaiah.

Structure:
- The Desperation of the Women After Judgment (v.1)
- The Branch of the LORD: Beautiful and Glorious (v.2)
- The Holy Remnant (v.3)
- Purification by the Spirit of Judgment and Burning (v.4)
- The Glory-Cloud Over Zion: A Canopy of Protection (vv.5-6)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.2  — "In that day shall the branch of the LORD be beautiful and
        glorious, and the fruit of the earth shall be excellent
        and comely for them that are escaped of Israel" (KJV) —
        "The Branch" (tsemach) is a messianic title (cf. Jeremiah
        23:5, Zechariah 3:8, 6:12). Christ — growing up from the
        stump of David after judgment fells the tree.

v.3  — "he that remaineth in Jerusalem, shall be called holy,
        even every one that is written among the living in
        Jerusalem" (KJV) — The remnant concept: not everyone
        survives, but those who do are sanctified. "Written among
        the living" — the Book of Life, God's register of His own.

v.4  — "When the Lord shall have washed away the filth of the
        daughters of Zion, and shall have purged the blood of
        Jerusalem from the midst thereof by the spirit of judgment,
        and by the spirit of burning" (KJV) — Two spirits (or one
        Spirit working two ways): judgment that exposes and burning
        that purifies. Refining fire, not destroying fire.

v.5  — "the LORD will create upon every dwelling place of mount
        Zion... a cloud and smoke by day, and the shining of a
        flaming fire by night: for upon all the glory shall be a
        defence" (KJV) — Exodus imagery returned: the pillar of
        cloud and fire that protected Israel now covers ALL of Zion.
        Not just the tabernacle but every dwelling place.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.2  — "branch" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "tsemach" = a sprout/growth.
      A technical messianic term in the prophets — something
      growing up from what appears dead (David's fallen dynasty).

v.3  — "written among the living" (KJV) / "recorded for life"
      (ESV) — Hebrew "katub lachayim" = inscribed for life.
      The concept of a divine register of those destined for
      life — the Book of Life.

v.5  — "create" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "bara" = the same word as
      Genesis 1:1. God's new protective presence over Zion is an
      act of CREATION — not merely provision but divine making.

v.5  — "defence" (KJV) / "canopy" (ESV) — Hebrew "chuppah" =
      a covering/wedding canopy. The marriage canopy — God's
      protective presence as a bridegroom covering His bride.

v.6  — "tabernacle" (KJV) / "booth" (ESV) — Hebrew "sukkah" =
      a temporary shelter (as in the Feast of Tabernacles/Sukkot).
      Shelter from heat, storm, and rain — comprehensive protection.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Branch of the LORD — (Hebrew: tsemach Yahweh) A messianic title
               emphasizing Christ's divine origin. He "branches out"
               from God — growing from the royal line of David after
               it appears cut down and dead. Used in Isaiah 4:2,
               Jeremiah 23:5, 33:15, Zechariah 3:8, 6:12.

Remnant       — Those who survive judgment. A key Isaiah concept:
               not all Israel perishes; God preserves a holy seed
               (6:13). The faithful few who remain after the
               unfaithful majority is judged.

Spirit of burning — Not destructive fire but REFINING fire. The
               Holy Spirit's purifying work, removing impurity and
               leaving holiness — like a silversmith's flame that
               removes dross without destroying the silver.

Chuppah       — A canopy or covering. In Jewish weddings, the
               couple stands under a chuppah symbolizing God's
               covering/protection. Here God Himself creates a
               wedding-canopy over all Zion.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
- v.2 — ASV: "the branch of Jehovah" / WEB: "Yahweh's branch" / NET: "the crops given by the Lord" — A critical messianic difference. ASV/WEB preserve "tsemach" as "branch" (a messianic title), while NET renders it as "crops" — completely losing the messianic connection to Jeremiah 23:5 and Zechariah 6:12. NET treats this as agricultural rather than prophetic.

- v.2 — NET: "the produce of the land will be a source of pride and delight to those who remain in Israel" vs. KJV "the fruit of the earth shall be excellent and comely for them that are escaped of Israel" — NET interprets "escaped of Israel" (peleitah) as merely "those who remain," softening the remnant theology. KJV/ASV preserve the sense of survivors of catastrophe.

- v.4 — ASV/WEB: "by the spirit of justice, and by the spirit of burning" vs. NET: "as he comes to judge and to bring devastation" — ASV/WEB retain "spirit" (ruach) in both phrases, preserving the possible Holy Spirit reference. NET removes "spirit" entirely, reducing it to divine action ("judge" and "devastation"), losing the pneumatological dimension.

- v.4 — NET: "wash the excrement from Zion's women" vs. KJV "washed away the filth of the daughters of Zion" — NET uses shockingly explicit language ("excrement") where KJV/ASV/WEB use the general "filth" (tso'ah). The Hebrew does mean excrement/refuse, but NET's graphic rendering is unusual for translations.

- v.5 — ASV: "Jehovah will create" / WEB: "Yahweh will create" — Both preserve the divine name with "bara" (create), linking back to Genesis 1:1. NET has "the Lord will create" — same verb but generic title instead of the personal name.

- v.5 — ASV: "over all the glory shall be spread a covering" / WEB: "over all the glory will be a canopy" / NET: "indeed a canopy will accompany the Lord's glorious presence" — Hebrew "chuppah" (wedding canopy). NET adds "accompany" and "Lord's glorious presence" interpretively. ASV/WEB stay closer to the spare beauty of the Hebrew.

- v.6 — ASV: "a pavilion for a shade" / WEB: "a pavilion for a shade" / NET: "a shelter to provide shade" — Hebrew "sukkah" = booth/tabernacle. ASV/WEB retain "pavilion" (a grander term), while NET uses "shelter." KJV uniquely uses "tabernacle" — connecting to the Feast of Tabernacles/Sukkot.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Beauty comes AFTER burning (vv.2-4). The Branch is glorious,
   but only after the filth is washed away. God's purifying work
   in your life — painful as it is — is preparation for beauty.
   What is God currently burning away to make room for glory?

2. "Written among the living" (v.3) — God has a book. Your name
   is either in it or not. This isn't about performance but about
   belonging. Do you have the assurance that you are God's, that
   your name is inscribed in His register?

3. The glory-cloud returns to cover EVERY dwelling (v.5). In
   Exodus, it covered only the tabernacle. In the future, it
   covers every home. God's presence isn't meant for the church
   building alone — it belongs in your house, your workplace,
   your daily life.

4. "A tabernacle for a shadow in the daytime from the heat" (v.6)
   — God provides SHELTER. From heat, from storm, from rain.
   Whatever is beating down on you, there is a covering. Are you
   living under it or standing exposed by self-reliance?

5. The chapter begins with desperate grasping (v.1: seven women
   clinging to one man) and ends with God providing everything
   (v.6: shelter, shade, refuge). Human desperation is the prelude
   to divine provision. Sometimes hitting bottom is what positions
   you to receive God's covering.
