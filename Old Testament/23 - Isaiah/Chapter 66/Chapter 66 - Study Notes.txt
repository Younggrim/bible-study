Isaiah - Chapter 66
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The grand finale of Isaiah. God declares heaven His throne and earth His footstool — no temple can contain Him. He looks to the humble and contrite. Zion gives birth to a nation in one day. God comes in fire and judgment, gathers ALL nations to see His glory, and from new moon to new moon ALL flesh worships before Him forever. The book ends with both eternal worship and eternal judgment.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Isaiah son of Amoz (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Oracle — The Grand Conclusion
Key Themes: Heaven as throne / earth as footstool, God looks to
the humble, Zion's miraculous birth, God's final coming in fire,
all nations gathered, all flesh worships, the everlasting
contrast between the redeemed and the lost

Historical Context: The final chapter of Isaiah brings the entire
66-chapter masterwork to its conclusion. It echoes the opening
(ch.1): God rejecting empty worship (1:11-15 / 66:3), calling for
genuine humility (1:16-17 / 66:2), and promising both judgment
and restoration. The chapter addresses the post-exilic community
(or anticipates it): the temple is being rebuilt, but God warns
against thinking a building can contain Him (v.1). The climax
gathers ALL nations and ALL languages (v.18) to witness God's
glory — the universal vision of 2:2-4 fulfilled. The final verse
(v.24) is sobering: the corpses of rebels in unquenchable fire —
quoted by Jesus in Mark 9:48.

Structure:
- Heaven My Throne: No Temple Contains Me (vv.1-2)
- Empty Worship Rejected Again (vv.3-4)
- Comfort for the Trembling; Judgment on the Scoffers (vv.5-6)
- Zion Gives Birth in One Day (vv.7-9)
- Rejoice with Jerusalem (vv.10-14)
- The LORD Comes in Fire: Final Judgment (vv.15-17)
- All Nations Gathered to See God's Glory (vv.18-21)
- The New Heavens Endure; All Flesh Worships (vv.22-24)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "Thus saith the LORD, The heaven is my throne, and the
        earth is my footstool: where is the house that ye build
        unto me? and where is the place of my rest?" (KJV) —
        God cannot be contained in buildings. Heaven = throne,
        earth = footstool. Even Solomon knew this (1 Kings 8:27).
        Stephen quotes this before his martyrdom (Acts 7:49).

v.2  — "but to this man will I look, even to him that is poor
        and of a contrite spirit, and trembleth at my word" (KJV)
        — God's preferred dwelling: not temples but PEOPLE — poor,
        contrite, trembling at His word. Three qualities that
        attract God's attention.

v.8  — "Shall the earth be made to bring forth in one day? or
        shall a nation be born at once? for as soon as Zion
        travailed, she brought forth her children" (KJV) — A
        nation born in a DAY. Historically applied to Israel's
        rebirth (1948), spiritually to the church at Pentecost
        (3,000 born in one day).

v.13 — "As one whom his mother comforteth, so will I comfort you;
        and ye shall be comforted in Jerusalem" (KJV) — God as
        MOTHER comforting a child. The most intimate, physical
        comfort — scooped up, held close, soothed.

v.18 — "I will gather all nations and tongues; and they shall
        come, and see my glory" (KJV) — The universal gathering:
        EVERY nation, EVERY language. No one excluded from
        witnessing God's glory.

v.22-23 — "For as the new heavens and the new earth, which I will
        make, shall remain before me... so shall your seed and your
        name remain... all flesh shall come to worship before me"
        (KJV) — The eternal state: permanent new creation,
        permanent worship, all flesh before God forever.

v.24 — "And they shall go forth, and look upon the carcases of
        the men that have transgressed against me: for their worm
        shall not die, neither shall their fire be quenched"
        (KJV) — The final verse: undying worm, unquenchable fire.
        Jesus quotes this three times in Mark 9:44,46,48. The
        eternal consequence of rebellion against God.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "footstool" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "hadom ragli" = the place
      where my feet rest. If the entire EARTH is merely God's
      footstool, how vast is He? No building can contain what the
      earth barely supports.

v.2  — "trembleth at my word" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "chared al
      devari" = trembling at my word. Not terror but REVERENCE —
      taking God's word so seriously that it causes physical
      response. The opposite of casual religion.

v.7  — "before she travailed, she brought forth" (KJV/ESV) —
      Miraculous birth: delivery BEFORE labor. Normally pain
      precedes birth; here the nation appears before the agony
      fully begins. Supernatural speed.

v.24 — "worm" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "tola'at" = maggot/worm that
      feeds on corpses. "Shall not die" = perpetual decomposition
      without end. Combined with unquenchable fire = eternal
      judgment imagery.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Heaven as Throne / Earth as Footstool — The cosmic scale of
              God: the entire universe is merely His furniture.
              No human structure can "house" Him.

Poor and Contrite — The three qualities God seeks: poverty of
              spirit (no self-sufficiency), contrition (genuine
              brokenness over sin), trembling at God's word
              (deep reverence for Scripture).

Nation Born in a Day — Instantaneous, miraculous multiplication.
              Applied to: Israel's national rebirth (1948), the
              church at Pentecost (Acts 2), or the eschatological
              restoration.

Worm That Dies Not — Eternal judgment imagery: the decay of the
              rebel never completes and the fire never expires.
              Quoted by Jesus as a warning (Mark 9:48).

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
- v.1 — ASV: "Thus saith Jehovah" / WEB: "Yahweh says" / NET: "This is what the Lord says" — The final chapter opens with the divine name rendered characteristically. God declares that no temple can contain Him — heaven itself is His throne.

- v.2 — NET: "I show special favor to the humble and contrite, who respect what I have to say" / KJV: "but to this man will I look, even to him that is poor and of a contrite spirit, and trembleth at my word" / ASV/WEB: "to him that is poor and of a contrite spirit, and that trembleth at my word" — NET paraphrases "ani" (poor/afflicted), "nekeh-ruach" (stricken/contrite of spirit), and "chared al devari" (trembling at my word). The trembling is more than "respect" — it's awe-filled reverence that shakes before divine speech.

- v.12 — NET: "Look, I am ready to extend to her prosperity that will flow like a river" / KJV: "Behold, I will extend peace to her like a river" / ASV/WEB: "I will extend peace to her like a river" — NET renders "shalom" as "prosperity" rather than "peace." In the new creation context, "shalom" encompasses peace, well-being, wholeness, and prosperity — reducing it to just prosperity misses the scope.

- v.13 — NET: "As a mother consoles a child, so I will console you" / KJV: "As one whom his mother comforteth, so will I comfort you" / ASV/WEB: "As one whom his mother comforteth, so will I comfort you" — All translations preserve this remarkable maternal image of God. Hebrew "nacham" (comfort) is Isaiah's keynote word (40:1 "Comfort ye, comfort ye"). God's final self-portrait in Isaiah is motherly comfort.

- v.22 — NET: "For just as the new heavens and the new earth I am about to make will remain standing before me" / KJV: "For as the new heavens and the new earth, which I will make, shall remain before me" / ASV/WEB: "which I will make, shall remain before me" — NET adds "about to" (not in Hebrew), making the new creation future. The Hebrew simply states "which I am making" — the participle suggests ongoing creative work.

- v.24 — NET: "for the maggots that eat them will not die, and the fire that consumes them will not die out. All people will find the sight abhorrent" / KJV: "for their worm shall not die, neither shall their fire be quenched; and they shall be an abhorring unto all flesh" — NET clarifies "tola'at" (worm/maggot) but the image is of unending decomposition paired with unending fire. Jesus quotes this three times in Mark 9:44-48 about Gehenna, making it His primary text on eternal judgment.

- v.23 — NET: "From one month to the next and from one Sabbath to the next, all people will come to worship me" / KJV: "from one new moon to another, and from one sabbath to another, shall all flesh come to worship before me, saith the LORD" — NET simplifies "new moon" to "month." The verse envisions universal worship in the new creation — ALL flesh, not just Israel, worshipping God on His appointed times.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. God cannot be contained (v.1). Stop trying to put God in a
   box — theological, architectural, or experiential. Heaven is
   His throne; earth is His footstool. He is BIGGER than your
   category for Him.

2. God looks to the humble (v.2). Not the impressive, not the
   confident, not the well-resourced — the POOR, CONTRITE, and
   TREMBLING. If you feel small and broken before God's word,
   congratulations: you have His attention.

3. "As one whom his mother comforteth" (v.13). God's comfort is
   MATERNAL: physical, instinctive, unconditional. If you need
   to be held today — God's arms are open. Not distant, not
   formal. MOTHERLY comfort.

4. All nations will see His glory (v.18). The final gathering is
   UNIVERSAL. Every language, every ethnicity, every culture will
   witness God. The mission isn't optional — it's inevitable.
   Join what God is already doing.

5. The book ends with both worship AND judgment (vv.23-24). Isaiah
   doesn't soften the ending. Eternal worship for some; eternal
   destruction for others. The contrast is stark because the
   choice is real. Choose wisely while the choice is still yours.
