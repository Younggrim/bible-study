Isaiah - Chapter 33
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Woe to the treacherous destroyer (Assyria) — when you finish destroying, YOU will be destroyed. God rises up, sinners in Zion tremble, but those who walk righteously will see the King in His beauty and dwell in a land of broad rivers where no enemy galley can sail.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Isaiah son of Amoz (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Woe Oracle — Sixth Woe (chs. 28-33)
Key Themes: The destroyer destroyed, prayer for God to rise up,
sinners trembling in Zion, the character of those who survive,
seeing the King in His beauty, the land of broad rivers, God as
judge-lawgiver-king-savior

Historical Context: The final woe of the series (chs. 28-33)
is directed at the "treacherous dealer" — Assyria, which broke
treaties with smaller nations after extorting tribute from them.
Sennacherib demanded tribute, received it, then attacked anyway
(2 Kings 18:14-17). The chapter moves from lament to prayer to
vision — ending with one of Isaiah's most beautiful depictions
of the future Zion: a city of peace with broad rivers, where God
Himself is judge, lawgiver, king, and savior (v.22).

Structure:
- Woe to the Treacherous Destroyer (vv.1-4)
- Prayer: O LORD, Be Gracious to Us (vv.2-6)
- The Devastation: Roads Empty, Treaties Broken (vv.7-9)
- God Rises Up (vv.10-13)
- Sinners in Zion Tremble (vv.14-16)
- The King in His Beauty: A Land of Broad Rivers (vv.17-24)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.2  — "O LORD, be gracious unto us; we have waited for thee: be
        thou their arm every morning, our salvation also in the
        time of trouble" (KJV) — The faithful remnant's prayer:
        be our DAILY strength ("every morning") and our crisis
        deliverance ("in time of trouble").

v.14 — "The sinners in Zion are afraid; fearfulness hath surprised
        the hypocrites. Who among us shall dwell with the
        devouring fire? who among us shall dwell with everlasting
        burnings?" (KJV) — When God rises up, it's the INSIDERS
        (sinners IN Zion, hypocrites) who tremble most. They know
        they can't survive God's holiness.

v.15 — "He that walketh righteously, and speaketh uprightly; he
        that despiseth the gain of oppressions... that shaketh his
        hands from holding of bribes" (KJV) — The answer to v.14:
        WHO can survive? The morally upright, the just, the
        uncorrupted. Character determines survival.

v.17 — "Thine eyes shall see the king in his beauty: they shall
        behold the land that is very far off" (KJV) — The reward
        of righteousness: SEEING the King. Not just safety but
        VISION — beholding Christ in His glory and the expansive
        kingdom stretching before you.

v.22 — "For the LORD is our judge, the LORD is our lawgiver, the
        LORD is our king; he will save us" (KJV) — Four roles in
        one: Judge, Lawgiver, King, Savior. The separation of
        powers that human government requires is unified in God.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "treacherous dealer" (KJV) / "traitor" (ESV) — Hebrew
      "boged" = one who acts faithlessly, a covenant-breaker.
      Assyria broke its treaties with vassal states after
      receiving tribute — the ultimate betrayal.

v.14 — "everlasting burnings" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "moqedey olam"
      = perpetual/eternal flames. God's holiness as perpetual fire
      that consumes sin. Not hell per se, but God's nature — only
      the righteous survive proximity to it.

v.21 — "broad rivers and streams" (KJV/ESV) — Jerusalem had no
      river (only the Gihon spring). The future Zion will have
      what the earthly city lacked — broad waterways. Yet no enemy
      ship can sail them (v.21). Provision without vulnerability.

v.22 — "judge... lawgiver... king" (KJV/ESV) — Three branches of
      government unified in God. Human governments separate these
      to prevent tyranny; in God, they unite perfectly because He
      is perfectly just.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
King in His Beauty — The messianic vision: seeing Christ not in
              humiliation but in glorified splendor. The ultimate
              reward of faith — beholding God face to face.

Devouring Fire — God's holiness experienced as consuming fire
              by those with unresolved sin. Not cruelty but purity
              — fire that cannot tolerate impurity.

Broad Rivers — The eschatological Zion's provision: abundant
              water (life, prosperity, blessing) without the
              vulnerability that rivers normally bring (enemy
              navies). All benefit, no danger.

Treacherous Dealer — Assyria specifically; any treaty-breaking
              power generally. Those who exploit trust for gain
              will themselves be betrayed.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
- v.2 — ASV: "O Jehovah, be gracious unto us; we have waited for thee" / WEB: "Yahweh, be gracious to us. We have waited for you" / NET: "Lord, be merciful to us! We wait for you" — The remnant's prayer addresses God by name (ASV/WEB) vs. title (NET). NET changes "gracious" to "merciful" — similar but not identical. Grace is unmerited favor; mercy is compassion toward the suffering.

- v.5 — ASV: "Jehovah is exalted; for he dwelleth on high: he hath filled Zion with justice and righteousness" / WEB: "Yahweh is exalted... He has filled Zion with justice and righteousness" / NET: "The Lord is exalted... he fills Zion with justice and fairness" — NET again uses "fairness" for "righteousness" (tsedaqah), reducing a theological term to a social one.

- v.6 — NET: "He is your constant source of stability; he abundantly provides safety and great wisdom; he gives all this to those who fear him" — A complete paraphrase of KJV's "wisdom and knowledge shall be the stability of thy times, and strength of salvation: the fear of the LORD is his treasure." NET restructures the Hebrew significantly and changes "treasure" to what God "gives."

- v.22 — ASV: "Jehovah is our judge, Jehovah is our lawgiver, Jehovah is our king; he will save us" / WEB: "Yahweh is our judge. Yahweh is our lawgiver. Yahweh is our king. He will save us" / NET: "the Lord, our ruler, the Lord, our commander, the Lord, our king—he will deliver us" — This is theologically critical. NET replaces "judge" and "lawgiver" with "ruler" and "commander," collapsing distinct governmental functions into generic authority terms. The Hebrew "shophet" (judge) and "mechoqeq" (lawgiver/legislator) are specific roles.

- v.14 — KJV: "the hypocrites" / ASV/WEB: "the godless ones" / NET: "the godless" — KJV's "hypocrites" (Hebrew "chaneph" = profane/godless) is interpretive but aptly describes those who sin WITHIN Zion while appearing religious. ASV/WEB/NET's "godless" is more literal.

- v.21 — ASV: "there Jehovah will be with us in majesty" / WEB: "there Yahweh will be with us in majesty" / NET: "the Lord will rule there as our mighty king" — KJV has "the glorious LORD will be unto us a place of broad rivers." NET changes God's PRESENCE into God's RULE. The Hebrew emphasizes God being WITH His people (immanuel-type language), not merely governing them.

- v.10 — ASV: "Now will I arise, saith Jehovah" / WEB: "'Now I will arise,' says Yahweh" / NET: "'Now I will rise up,' says the Lord" — God speaks in first person with His name attached. The dramatic self-revelation (God breaking His silence after vv.7-9's devastation) is more powerful with the name than with a title.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Who among us shall dwell with the devouring fire?" (v.14).
   This is THE question of holiness. Not "how can I get into
   heaven?" but "how can I survive proximity to a holy God?"
   The answer (v.15): character. Not perfection, but genuine
   righteousness enabled by grace.

2. "Thine eyes shall see the king in his beauty" (v.17). THIS
   is the Christian hope — not just escape from hell but SEEING
   Christ in His glory. Let that vision motivate your
   obedience more than fear of punishment.

3. The LORD is judge, lawgiver, king, and savior (v.22). You
   don't need four different sources for these needs. One God
   covers all: He makes the rules, enforces them, governs, and
   RESCUES. All authority in one trustworthy Person.

4. "Be thou their arm every morning" (v.2). Faith isn't a
   one-time event but a daily renewal. Every morning you need
   God's strength again. Yesterday's grace doesn't cover today's
   challenges.
