Isaiah - Chapter 61
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
"The Spirit of the Lord GOD is upon me" — the Servant/Messiah's mission statement. He is anointed to preach good tidings to the poor, bind the brokenhearted, proclaim liberty to captives, announce the acceptable year of the LORD. Beauty for ashes, oil of joy for mourning. Jesus read this in Nazareth and said "Today this scripture is fulfilled."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Isaiah son of Amoz (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Messianic Oracle — The Anointed Preacher
Key Themes: The Spirit-anointed Messiah, good news to the poor,
liberty to captives, the Year of Jubilee, beauty for ashes, the
garment of praise, rebuilding ruins, priests of God, everlasting
covenant, salvation and righteousness as garments

Historical Context: This is the chapter Jesus read in the
Nazareth synagogue (Luke 4:16-21), stopping mid-sentence at "the
acceptable year of the LORD" — because "the day of vengeance"
(second half of v.2) belongs to His second coming, not His first.
The speaker is the Servant/Messiah Himself, declaring His Spirit-
empowered mission. The language draws on the Year of Jubilee
(Leviticus 25): debts cancelled, slaves freed, property returned.
The Messiah IS the ultimate Jubilee — setting everything right.

Structure:
- The Spirit's Anointing: The Messiah's Mission (vv.1-3)
- Rebuilding Ruins: Restoration (vv.4-5)
- Priests of God: A Royal Priesthood (vv.6-7)
- The Everlasting Covenant (vv.8-9)
- Clothed in Salvation: The Bridegroom's Joy (vv.10-11)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "The Spirit of the Lord GOD is upon me; because the LORD
        hath anointed me to preach good tidings unto the meek"
        (KJV) — The Messiah's commissioning. Spirit-empowered,
        God-anointed, sent to the broken. This IS the gospel:
        good news to those who need it most.

v.1-2 — "to bind up the brokenhearted, to proclaim liberty to the
        captives, and the opening of the prison to them that are
        bound; to proclaim the acceptable year of the LORD" (KJV)
        — Five assignments: (1) heal the brokenhearted, (2) free
        captives, (3) open prisons, (4) proclaim God's favorable
        year, (5) comfort mourners.

v.3  — "To appoint unto them that mourn in Zion, to give unto
        them beauty for ashes, the oil of joy for mourning, the
        garment of praise for the spirit of heaviness" (KJV) —
        Three EXCHANGES: ashes → beauty, mourning → joy, heaviness
        → praise. God doesn't just remove the negative — He
        REPLACES it with something better.

v.10 — "he hath clothed me with the garments of salvation, he
        hath covered me with the robe of righteousness, as a
        bridegroom decketh himself with ornaments, and as a bride
        adorneth herself with her jewels" (KJV) — Salvation and
        righteousness as CLOTHING — put on you by God Himself.
        Wedding imagery: the joy of being dressed for the
        celebration.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "anointed" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "mashach" = to anoint with
      oil. The verb from which "Mashiach" (Messiah/Christ)
      derives. The speaker IS the anointed one — the Christ.

v.2  — "acceptable year" (KJV) / "year of the LORD's favor"
      (ESV) — Hebrew "shnat ratson" = a year of divine favor/
      acceptance. Jubilee language: the year everything resets.

v.3  — "beauty for ashes" (KJV) / "a beautiful headdress instead
      of ashes" (ESV) — Hebrew wordplay: "pe'er tachat epher" —
      "pe'er" (beauty/turban) for "epher" (ashes). The sounds
      echo each other — one replaces the other.

v.6  — "Priests of the LORD" (KJV/ESV) — All of redeemed Israel
      becomes priestly — not just the Levites. Universal
      priesthood. Fulfilled in 1 Peter 2:9.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Year of Jubilee — Every 50th year: debts cancelled, slaves freed,
              land returned to original owners (Lev 25). The
              Messiah's coming IS the ultimate Jubilee — the great
              reset of all broken things.

Beauty for Ashes — The divine exchange: God takes your mourning
              symbols and replaces them with celebratory ornaments.
              Not just restoration but UPGRADE.

Garment of Praise — Clothing metaphor: praise as something you
              PUT ON deliberately, replacing the "spirit of
              heaviness." Praise is a choice, not just an emotion.

Oaks of Righteousness — (v.3) The redeemed described as strong,
              deep-rooted trees — planted by God for His glory.
              Stability, longevity, fruitfulness.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "The Spirit of the Lord is upon ME" (v.1). Jesus didn't
   minister in His own strength but in the Spirit's power.
   Neither should you. Every assignment from God comes with
   His Spirit's empowerment. You're not doing this alone.

2. Jesus stopped reading mid-verse (Luke 4:19). He proclaimed
   the year of FAVOR (first coming) but left "the day of
   vengeance" (second coming) unread. We live between the two —
   in the year of grace. Take advantage NOW.

3. Beauty for ashes, joy for mourning, praise for heaviness
   (v.3). These are EXCHANGES. You bring your brokenness; God
   gives beauty. You bring grief; He gives joy. The exchange rate
   is infinitely in your favor. What ashes are you still wearing
   that God wants to replace?

4. "Oaks of righteousness" (v.3). You're meant to be an OAK —
   deep roots, strong trunk, enduring through storms. Oaks grow
   slowly but become immovable. Be patient with your growth.

5. "Clothed with salvation" (v.10). Righteousness isn't something
   you generate — it's something you WEAR. God puts it ON you.
   Stop trying to manufacture righteousness; receive it as a gift.
