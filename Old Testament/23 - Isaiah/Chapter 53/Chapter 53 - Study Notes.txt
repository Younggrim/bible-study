Isaiah - Chapter 53
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The crown jewel of Old Testament prophecy — the Suffering Servant. Despised and rejected, a man of sorrows, acquainted with grief. He was wounded for OUR transgressions, bruised for OUR iniquities. The chastisement of OUR peace was upon Him, and with His stripes WE are healed. The LORD laid on Him the iniquity of us all. Cut off from the living, buried with the rich, yet He sees His offspring and the pleasure of the LORD prospers in His hand.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Isaiah son of Amoz (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Fourth Servant Song (52:13-53:12) — Vicarious
Suffering and Atonement
Key Themes: Substitutionary atonement, the Servant despised yet
exalted, bearing others' sins, silent before slaughter, death
with transgressors yet burial with the rich, justifying many,
interceding for transgressors

Historical Context: Isaiah 52:13-53:12 is the FOURTH and greatest
Servant Song. Written approximately 700 BC, it describes the
crucifixion, burial, and resurrection of Jesus Christ with
astonishing precision — 700 years before the event. The Ethiopian
eunuch was reading this passage when Philip explained the gospel
(Acts 8:32-35). This chapter is quoted or alluded to more than
any other OT passage in the NT. Jewish interpretation historically
applied it to the nation of Israel, but the details (innocent
suffering for others' sins, death and burial, willing submission)
point to an INDIVIDUAL — fulfilled uniquely in Christ.

Structure:
- [52:13-15] The Servant Exalted Yet Marred
- Who Has Believed Our Report? (vv.1-3)
- He Bore OUR Griefs and Carried OUR Sorrows (vv.4-6)
- Silent Before His Slaughter (vv.7-9)
- The LORD's Pleasure: Offspring, Satisfaction (vv.10-12)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.3  — "He is despised and rejected of men; a man of sorrows,
        and acquainted with grief: and we hid as it were our faces
        from him; he was despised, and we esteemed him not" (KJV)
        — The summary of Christ's earthly reception: despised,
        rejected, sorrowful, grief-experienced. We turned away.

v.4  — "Surely he hath borne our griefs, and carried our sorrows:
        yet we did esteem him stricken, smitten of God, and
        afflicted" (KJV) — The substitution: HE bore what was
        OURS. Yet the observers assumed God was punishing HIM for
        His own sins. They misread the cross completely.

v.5  — "But he was wounded for our transgressions, he was bruised
        for our iniquities: the chastisement of our peace was upon
        him; and with his stripes we are healed" (KJV) — The most
        explicit atonement statement in the OT. Five key words:
        wounded, bruised, chastisement, stripes, healed. All
        HIS suffering; all OUR benefit.

v.6  — "All we like sheep have gone astray; we have turned every
        one to his own way; and the LORD hath laid on him the
        iniquity of us all" (KJV) — Universal guilt ("ALL we...
        every ONE"), universal remedy ("iniquity of us ALL"). The
        LORD Himself placed our sin on the Servant.

v.7  — "He was oppressed, and he was afflicted, yet he opened
        not his mouth: he is brought as a lamb to the slaughter,
        and as a sheep before her shearers is dumb, so he openeth
        not his mouth" (KJV) — SILENCE. Jesus before Pilate and
        Herod: not defending Himself, not protesting, not resisting.
        Willing submission to slaughter.

v.9  — "And he made his grave with the wicked, and with the rich
        in his death" (KJV) — Crucified with criminals (wicked),
        buried in Joseph of Arimathea's tomb (rich). Two specific
        details fulfilled precisely (Luke 23:32-33; Matt 27:57-60).

v.10 — "Yet it pleased the LORD to bruise him... he shall see his
        seed, he shall prolong his days, and the pleasure of the
        LORD shall prosper in his hand" (KJV) — AFTER death: He
        sees offspring and has prolonged days. RESURRECTION. The
        dead Servant lives again and bears fruit.

v.11 — "He shall see of the travail of his soul, and shall be
        satisfied" (KJV) — Christ will LOOK at the fruit of His
        suffering and be SATISFIED. Your salvation satisfies Jesus.
        He considers you worth the price.

v.12 — "he was numbered with the transgressors; and he bare the
        sin of many, and made intercession for the transgressors"
        (KJV) — Three final descriptions: numbered among sinners
        (Luke 22:37), bore sin for many, and INTERCEDES — present
        tense (Hebrews 7:25). He's still praying for you.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.2  — "tender plant... root out of a dry ground" (KJV/ESV) —
      Hebrew "yoneq" (sucker/sprout) and "shoresh" (root). The
      Messiah's humble origins: from a seemingly dead line (David's
      stump) in an unpromising environment (Nazareth).

v.5  — "wounded" (KJV) / "pierced" (ESV) — Hebrew "mecholal" =
      pierced through, fatally wounded. The same word used for
      being run through with a weapon. Fulfilled on the cross.

v.5  — "bruised" (KJV) / "crushed" (ESV) — Hebrew "medukka" =
      crushed to powder, pulverized. Not surface injury but deep,
      comprehensive damage. The weight of sin crushed Him.

v.7  — "lamb to the slaughter" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "seh la-tevach"
      = a sheep to the slaughter. Echoes the Passover lamb (Exodus
      12), the ram of Isaac (Genesis 22), and John's "Lamb of God"
      (John 1:29).

v.10 — "offering for sin" (KJV) / "guilt offering" (ESV) —
      Hebrew "asham" = the guilt/trespass offering (Leviticus 5).
      The Servant IS the sacrifice — not animal but human, not
      involuntary but willing.

v.11 — "justify many" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "yatsdiq... la-rabbim"
      = declare righteous the many. Legal language: the Servant's
      suffering creates a legal basis for declaring sinners
      RIGHTEOUS.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "He was wounded for OUR transgressions" (v.5). The pronouns
   are everything: HE — OUR. HIS suffering — OUR healing.
   Substitution is personal: He took YOUR specific sins, YOUR
   specific punishment, YOUR specific guilt. Make it personal.

2. "All we like sheep have gone astray" (v.6). Universal guilt:
   ALL of us. No one is exempt. "Every one to his OWN way" — sin
   is self-direction, choosing your path over God's. But the
   LORD laid it ALL on Him.

3. "He opened not his mouth" (v.7). Innocent yet silent. No
   defense, no self-justification, no protest. If the innocent
   Christ didn't demand His rights, what does that say about our
   insistence on defending ourselves?

4. "He shall see his seed... and be satisfied" (vv.10-11). YOUR
   salvation satisfies Jesus. He looks at the fruit of His
   suffering and says "worth it." You are not a disappointing
   return on His investment — you are His JOY.

5. "He made intercession for the transgressors" (v.12). Jesus
   is praying for you RIGHT NOW. Not "once prayed" — MAKES
   intercession, present tense. Whatever you're facing, Christ
   is actively advocating for you before the Father.
