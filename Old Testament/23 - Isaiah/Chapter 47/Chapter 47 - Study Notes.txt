Isaiah - Chapter 47
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
A taunt-song against Babylon: the pampered "virgin daughter" is stripped of her glory, forced to grind grain like a slave, and exposed. Her sorceries and enchantments cannot save her. Disaster will come suddenly — in one day — and no one can prevent it.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Isaiah son of Amoz (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Taunt Song — Against Babylon
Key Themes: Babylon's pride and fall, from throne to millstone,
cruelty without mercy, the failure of sorcery and astrology,
sudden destruction, no one to save

Historical Context: This chapter is the counterpart to chapter 46
(Babylon's gods fall) — now Babylon HERSELF falls. Personified
as a pampered queen who becomes a slave, Babylon's fall from
luxury to grinding grain mirrors the experience she inflicted on
captive nations. The chapter is remarkable for its denunciation
of Babylon's occult practices (vv.12-13): sorceries, enchantments,
astrology. Despite their famous stargazers and magicians, Babylon
cannot predict or prevent her own destruction. Fulfilled: Babylon
fell in a single night (539 BC, Daniel 5) and gradually declined
into irrelevance.

Structure:
- Come Down from Your Throne (vv.1-4)
- Babylon's Cruelty and Self-Deification (vv.5-7)
- "I Am, and None Beside Me" (vv.8-9)
- Sudden Destruction in One Day (v.9)
- Sorcery and Astrology Cannot Save (vv.10-13)
- Burned Like Stubble: No One to Save (vv.14-15)

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Babylon (v.1): Capital of the Babylonian empire; place of Judah's exile (modern Iraq).

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------
Enduring Word (David Guzik):
  https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/isaiah-47/

Charles Spurgeon:
  "Babylon said, 'I am, and none beside me.' The creature usurped
  the Creator's title. This is the essence of all sin: to say
  'I am' as if you were God. Humility recognizes that only ONE
  can truly say 'I AM' — and it is not you."

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. "I shall be a lady forever" (v.7). Every empire, institution,
   and person who assumes permanence is making Babylon's mistake.
   Nothing human lasts forever. What in your life are you treating
   as if it will never end?

2. "I am, and none beside me" (v.8). Self-deification isn't just
   for empires. Every time you live as if the universe revolves
   around you — your needs, your comfort, your glory — you're
   echoing Babylon's blasphemy.

3. Sudden destruction (v.9). Babylon didn't see it coming. Neither
   did Belshazzar (Daniel 5). Life changes in a single day.
   Are you prepared for the unexpected? Is your foundation God
   or your circumstances?

4. Sorcery cannot save (vv.12-13). Modern equivalents: horoscopes,
   self-help gurus, fortune-telling, New Age spirituality —
   anything that promises control over the future apart from God.
   It will fail when you need it most.
