Isaiah - Chapter 59
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God's hand is not short — YOUR sins have separated you from God. A devastating catalog of Israel's sins: lying, violence, injustice, weaving spider webs. No one stands for justice, so God arms HIMSELF as a warrior, puts on righteousness as a breastplate and salvation as a helmet, and comes as Redeemer to Zion with a covenant: His Spirit and words will never depart.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Isaiah son of Amoz (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Oracle — Confession and Divine Warrior
Key Themes: Sin as separation from God, the catalog of human
wickedness, no human intercessor found, God arms Himself as
warrior, the Redeemer comes to Zion, the Spirit-and-Word covenant

Historical Context: Israel wonders why God hasn't delivered them.
God's answer (v.2): it's not My inability — it's YOUR iniquity.
The chapter provides a comprehensive indictment of social and
moral corruption (vv.3-8), followed by communal confession
(vv.9-15a). When no human champion can be found to intervene
(v.16), God arms HIMSELF with the armor of righteousness and
salvation. Paul draws on this imagery for the Christian's armor
(Ephesians 6:14-17). The chapter ends with a New Covenant promise:
God's Spirit and words will remain with His people FOREVER (v.21).

Structure:
- God's Hand Is Not Short: YOUR Sin Separates (vv.1-2)
- Catalog of Sins: Violence, Lies, Injustice (vv.3-8)
- Communal Confession (vv.9-15a)
- No Human Intercessor Found (vv.15b-16a)
- God Arms Himself as Warrior (vv.16b-19)
- The Redeemer Comes to Zion (v.20)
- The Eternal Covenant: Spirit and Words (v.21)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "Behold, the LORD's hand is not shortened, that it cannot
        save; neither his ear heavy, that it cannot hear" (KJV) —
        The problem is NOT God's power or attention. His hand
        reaches and His ear hears. The blockage is on YOUR side.

v.2  — "But your iniquities have separated between you and your
        God, and your sins have hid his face from you" (KJV) —
        The clearest statement of sin's effect: SEPARATION. Sin
        builds a wall between you and God. Not that God moves
        away — but sin hides His face.

v.16 — "And he saw that there was no man, and wondered that there
        was no intercessor: therefore his arm brought salvation
        unto him" (KJV) — God looked for a human champion and
        found NONE. So He did it Himself. When no one else could
        or would intervene, God's own arm saved.

v.17 — "For he put on righteousness as a breastplate, and an
        helmet of salvation upon his head; and he put on the
        garments of vengeance for clothing" (KJV) — God as Divine
        Warrior, armoring Himself. Paul's Ephesians 6 armor is
        borrowed FROM GOD'S OWN ARMOR. Christians wear God's
        equipment.

v.20 — "And the Redeemer shall come to Zion, and unto them that
        turn from transgression in Jacob" (KJV) — Paul quotes
        this in Romans 11:26: "there shall come out of Sion the
        Deliverer." Christ's coming fulfills this.

v.21 — "my spirit that is upon thee, and my words which I have
        put in thy mouth, shall not depart out of thy mouth, nor
        out of the mouth of thy seed, nor out of the mouth of thy
        seed's seed... for ever" (KJV) — The New Covenant promise:
        Spirit AND Word, perpetual, generational, FOREVER.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.5  — "cockatrice' eggs... spider's web" (KJV) / "adders' eggs
      ... spider's web" (ESV) — Hebrew imagery for the PRODUCTS
      of evil: poisonous eggs (deadly results) and spider webs
      (useless covering that can't hide anything).

v.17 — "breastplate... helmet" (KJV/ESV) — God's personal armor.
      Note what God wears: RIGHTEOUSNESS protects His heart,
      SALVATION covers His head, VENGEANCE is His garment, ZEAL
      is His cloak. The Christian's armor in Ephesians 6 is God
      lending us HIS equipment.

v.19 — "the enemy shall come in like a flood" (KJV) / "he will
      come like a rushing stream" (ESV) — Translation debate:
      KJV reads "when the enemy comes as a flood, the Spirit of
      the LORD shall lift up a standard." ESV reads differently.
      Either way: God's Spirit responds powerfully to opposition.

v.20 — "Redeemer" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "go'el" = kinsman-redeemer.
      The one who buys back, avenges, and restores family. Christ
      as the ultimate kinsman-redeemer of Zion.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Separation   — (v.2) Sin's primary effect: distance between God
              and humanity. Not spatial but relational — God's face
              hidden, His response blocked, His presence withdrawn.

Divine Warrior — God armoring Himself for battle because no human
              warrior is available. The imagery becomes the
              template for Christian spiritual armor (Eph 6).

Intercessor  — (v.16) One who stands between God and people,
              mediating, advocating, intervening. God found NONE
              among humans — so He became His own intercessor
              (Christ, Hebrews 7:25).

Go'el/Redeemer — The kinsman-redeemer who has the right, ability,
              and willingness to buy back what was lost. Christ as
              Zion's go'el — purchasing His people from slavery.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
- v.16 — NET: "he is shocked that no one intervenes. So he takes matters into his own hands; his desire for justice drives him on" / KJV: "wondered that there was no intercessor: therefore his arm brought salvation unto him; and his righteousness, it sustained him" — NET paraphrases extensively. Hebrew preserves the powerful image: God's own "arm" (zero'a) brings salvation when no human can. This becomes the basis for the Warrior-God imagery in vv.17-18.

- v.17 — NET: "He wears his desire for justice like body armor, and his desire to deliver is like a helmet" / KJV: "he put on righteousness as a breastplate, and an helmet of salvation upon his head" — NET interprets "tsedaqah" (righteousness) as "desire for justice" and "yeshu'ah" (salvation) as "desire to deliver." Paul adapts this armor in Ephesians 6:14-17 — the connection is clearer with KJV/ASV/WEB's terminology.

- v.19 — KJV: "When the enemy shall come in like a flood, the Spirit of the LORD shall lift up a standard against him" / ASV: "he will come as a rushing stream, which the breath of Jehovah driveth" / NET: "For he comes like a rushing stream driven on by wind sent from the Lord" — This verse is famously read two different ways. KJV sees the enemy as the flood and the Spirit as the responder. ASV/WEB/NET see God Himself as the rushing stream. The Hebrew allows both readings due to ambiguous punctuation.

- v.20 — NET: "A protector comes to Zion" / KJV: "the Redeemer shall come to Zion" / ASV: "a Redeemer will come to Zion" / WEB: "A Redeemer will come to Zion" — NET again renders "go'el" (kinsman-redeemer) as "protector." Paul quotes this in Romans 11:26 of Christ's return — the Redeemer language is theologically essential for the Messianic interpretation.

- v.20 — ASV: "saith Jehovah" / WEB: "says Yahweh" / NET: "says the Lord" — The divine name characteristically rendered. In a verse about the coming Redeemer, the personal name emphasizes that YHWH Himself comes as Zion's kinsman-redeemer.

- v.21 — NET: "My Spirit, who is upon you, and my words, which I have placed in your mouth" / KJV: "My spirit that is upon thee, and my words which I have put in thy mouth" — Both are similar here. Note the Trinitarian structure: "I" (the Father), "my Spirit," and "my words" — three divine agencies in one covenant promise.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Your sins have separated" (v.2). If God seems distant, the
   problem isn't His hearing — it's your sin. The solution isn't
   more volume in prayer but confession and repentance. Remove
   the wall.

2. God found no one (v.16). No human hero existed. Not Moses, not
   Elijah, not any prophet could solve the sin problem. God HAD
   to do it Himself — which is why Christ is necessary. Human
   solutions fail; divine intervention doesn't.

3. You wear GOD'S armor (v.17 → Eph 6). When you put on the
   armor of God, you're wearing what GOD Himself wears. His
   righteousness, His salvation, His truth. It's not your armor
   — it's HIS, lent to you.

4. Spirit and Words — FOREVER (v.21). The New Covenant guarantee:
   God's Spirit and God's words will NEVER depart from His people.
   Not your generation only but your children's children's
   children. Permanence.

5. "His arm brought salvation" (v.16). What was impossible for
   humanity, God accomplished by His own arm. Stop trying to be
   your own savior. Admit the job is too big for you. Let God's
   arm do what yours cannot.
