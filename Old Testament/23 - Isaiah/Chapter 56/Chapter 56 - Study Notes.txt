Isaiah - Chapter 56
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God welcomes foreigners and eunuchs into His house — those previously excluded from the assembly. "My house shall be called an house of prayer for all people." Then Isaiah rebukes Israel's blind watchmen and greedy shepherds who care only for their own gain.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Isaiah son of Amoz (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Oracle — Inclusivity and Leadership
Failure (opens final section, chs. 56-66)
Key Themes: Radical inclusion (foreigners, eunuchs), the Sabbath
as covenant sign, a house of prayer for ALL people, the failure
of Israel's watchmen/shepherds, leaders as blind dogs

Historical Context: Chapter 56 opens the THIRD section of Isaiah
(chs.56-66). While 40-55 focused on comfort and the Servant,
56-66 addresses the restored community — its ethics, failures,
and ultimate glory. The chapter begins with radical inclusion:
foreigners (Gentiles) and eunuchs (excluded by Deut 23:1) are
WELCOMED if they keep the Sabbath and hold God's covenant. Jesus
quotes v.7 when cleansing the temple (Mark 11:17). Then the tone
shifts: Israel's leaders are denounced as blind, ignorant, silent
watchdogs, greedy shepherds — the very people who should welcome
others are consumed by self-interest.

Structure:
- Keep Justice: Salvation Is Near (v.1)
- The Foreigner and Eunuch Welcomed (vv.2-8)
- A House of Prayer for All Peoples (v.7)
- Blind Watchmen and Greedy Shepherds (vv.9-12)

  people. The doors are wider than you think — wider than
  religious gatekeepers want them to be."

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. God welcomes those previously excluded (vv.3-8). If you've
   felt disqualified from God's presence — by your past, your
   condition, your outsider status — hear this: God explicitly
   overturns exclusions. His house is for ALL who hold His
   covenant.

2. "A house of prayer for ALL people" (v.7). Are you (or your
   church) gatekeeping God's presence? Making some people feel
   less welcome than others? Jesus was ANGRY about this. God's
   house has room.

3. Blind watchmen and dumb dogs (v.10). Leadership has ONE JOB:
   see clearly and speak truthfully. If your spiritual leaders
   can't identify danger or won't name it, they've failed their
   function. Seek leaders who can both see and bark.

4. Greedy shepherds (v.11). "Every one for his gain." When
   ministry becomes a career ladder rather than a service calling,
   it's corrupted. If you lead others, check your motives: are
   you feeding them or feeding yourself?
