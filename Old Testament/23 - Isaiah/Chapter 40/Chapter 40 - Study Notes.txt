Isaiah - Chapter 40
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
"Comfort ye, comfort ye my people" — the magnificent opening of Isaiah's second half. God's warfare is accomplished, her iniquity pardoned. A voice cries in the wilderness to prepare the way. All flesh is grass, but God's word stands forever. The LORD comes with His reward, carries lambs in His bosom, and is incomparably great — measuring the waters in His palm, stretching out the heavens, calling the stars by name. Those who wait on the LORD renew their strength.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Isaiah son of Amoz (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Comfort Oracle — Opening of the
"Book of Comfort" (chs. 40-66)
Key Themes: Comfort after judgment, the voice in the wilderness
(John the Baptist), the transience of humanity vs. the permanence
of God's word, the incomparable greatness of God, mounting up
with eagle's wings

Historical Context: Chapter 40 marks a dramatic shift in Isaiah.
The first 39 chapters dealt primarily with judgment (Assyrian
crisis); chapters 40-66 are dominated by comfort, restoration,
and the work of the Servant. The perspective shifts to address
Babylonian exiles — those who need to hear "your warfare is
accomplished." This chapter functions as the overture to the
entire second half of Isaiah, introducing its major themes:
comfort, God's incomparability, the preparation of the way
(fulfilled in John the Baptist, Mark 1:3), and renewed strength
for the weary.

Structure:
- Comfort My People: Warfare Accomplished (vv.1-2)
- The Voice in the Wilderness (vv.3-5)
- All Flesh is Grass; God's Word Stands (vv.6-8)
- Good Tidings to Zion: God Comes (vv.9-11)
- The Incomparable Creator (vv.12-17)
- Idols vs. the Living God (vv.18-20)
- God Sits Above the Circle of the Earth (vv.21-26)
- Why Do You Say God Doesn't See? (vv.27-28)
- Those Who Wait Renew Their Strength (vv.29-31)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "Comfort ye, comfort ye my people, saith your God" (KJV)
        — The tone shifts from judgment to comfort. Doubled for
        intensity: COMFORT, comfort. After 39 chapters of warning,
        God's heart of tenderness breaks through.

v.3  — "The voice of him that crieth in the wilderness, Prepare
        ye the way of the LORD, make straight in the desert a
        highway for our God" (KJV) — All four Gospels apply this
        to John the Baptist (Matt 3:3, Mark 1:3, Luke 3:4,
        John 1:23). The forerunner's commission: clear the road
        for God's arrival.

v.8  — "The grass withereth, the flower fadeth: but the word of
        our God shall stand for ever" (KJV) — Everything human is
        temporary (grass); only God's word is permanent. Quoted
        in 1 Peter 1:24-25. The most concise statement of
        Scripture's eternality.

v.11 — "He shall feed his flock like a shepherd: he shall gather
        the lambs with his arm, and carry them in his bosom, and
        shall gently lead those that are with young" (KJV) — The
        tender shepherd: gathering, carrying, gently leading.
        The same God who measures oceans in His palm carries
        lambs against His chest.

v.28 — "Hast thou not known? hast thou not heard, that the
        everlasting God, the LORD, the Creator of the ends of the
        earth, fainteth not, neither is weary?" (KJV) — God
        doesn't get tired. He doesn't burn out. His resources are
        infinite. If you're exhausted, His energy is available.

v.31 — "But they that wait upon the LORD shall renew their
        strength; they shall mount up with wings as eagles; they
        shall run, and not be weary; and they shall walk, and not
        faint" (KJV) — The chapter's climax and one of Scripture's
        most beloved promises. WAITING produces flying. The
        sequence: mount up (soar), run (active work), walk
        (daily endurance). All three need divine strength.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "Comfort ye" (KJV) / "Comfort, comfort" (ESV) — Hebrew
      "nachamu, nachamu" = comfort/console (imperative plural,
      repeated). God commands His prophets to speak tenderly to
      His people.

v.2  — "warfare is accomplished" (KJV) / "hard service has
      ended" (ESV) — Hebrew "tsava'ah" = military service, forced
      labor, appointed time of hardship. The exile-punishment is
      OVER. Release has come.

v.3  — "in the wilderness" (KJV/ESV) — The phrase can attach to
      "crying" (a voice crying in the wilderness) or "prepare"
      (prepare in the wilderness a way). The LXX and NT attach it
      to "crying" — placing the forerunner IN the wilderness.

v.12 — "measured the waters in the hollow of his hand" (KJV/ESV)
      — Hebrew "sha'al" = cupped hand. All the oceans fit in God's
      palm. Incomprehensible scale difference between Creator and
      creation.

v.22 — "circle of the earth" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "chug ha-aretz"
      = the vault/circle of the earth. The horizon seen as a
      circle from God's elevated perspective.

v.31 — "mount up with wings" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "ya'alu ever" =
      ascend on pinions. Eagle flight: not powered by frantic
      effort but by catching thermals — resting on wind currents.
      A picture of Spirit-empowered life.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Comfort      — Hebrew "nacham" = to comfort, console, show
              compassion. The word implies MORE than feeling better
              — it means to be strengthened and restored.

Voice in the Wilderness — The forerunner who prepares for God's
              arrival. Fulfilled in John the Baptist, who preached
              in the Judean wilderness, calling people to repentance
              before Jesus' public ministry.

Wait Upon the LORD — Hebrew "qavah" = to wait, to hope, to look
              eagerly for. NOT passive sitting but active,
              expectant trust. Like a taut rope stretched toward
              its anchor point.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Comfort ye, comfort ye" (v.1). If you've been living under
   condemnation (from God, from yourself, from others), hear
   this: God's posture toward His repentant people is COMFORT.
   He's not endlessly angry. After discipline comes tenderness.

2. "All flesh is grass" (v.6). You are temporary. Your career,
   your body, your influence — grass. But God's word is eternal.
   Build your life on what lasts, not on what withers.

3. The shepherd carries lambs in His bosom (v.11). You are not
   too small for God's personal attention. He carries the weak
   ones — not at arm's length but against His chest. If you feel
   small and weak today, that's exactly who He carries closest.

4. "They that WAIT shall renew" (v.31). The hardest spiritual
   discipline: waiting. Not working harder, not running faster —
   WAITING. Eagles fly not by flapping but by catching the wind.
   Stop flapping. Spread your wings and wait for God's thermal.

5. God calls stars by name (v.26). He names and numbers every
   star — and He knows YOUR name. The God of galaxies is the
   God of individuals. You are not lost in the cosmic crowd.
