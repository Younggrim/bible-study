Isaiah - Chapter 51
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
"Hearken unto me, ye that follow after righteousness" — three calls to listen (vv.1,4,7). Look to Abraham and Sarah (one became many). The LORD will comfort Zion. His righteousness is near, His salvation everlasting. "Awake, awake, put on strength, O arm of the LORD" — Israel calls God to act as He did at the Red Sea. Then God responds: "Awake, awake, stand up, O Jerusalem" — the cup of wrath is removed from your hand and given to your oppressors.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Isaiah son of Amoz (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Salvation Oracle
Key Themes: Look to your origins (Abraham/Sarah), God's comfort
for Zion, everlasting salvation, "Awake, awake" (calling God
and Zion to action), the cup of wrath transferred from Israel
to her enemies, Rahab/the sea-dragon defeated

Historical Context: Chapter 51 builds toward the climactic
liberation of 52:1-12. Three "listen" sections address those
who pursue righteousness (v.1), God's people (v.4), and those
who know righteousness (v.7). The central appeal is MEMORY: look
to Abraham (one man became a nation — so God can multiply again).
The "Awake" cry (v.9) recalls God's victory over Rahab (Egypt)
at the Red Sea — do it AGAIN! God answers with His own "Awake"
to Jerusalem (v.17; continued in 52:1): the cup of staggering
is taken away.

Structure:
- Look to Abraham: One Became Many (vv.1-3)
- Listen, My People: Everlasting Salvation (vv.4-6)
- Fear Not Man: He's Grass (vv.7-8)
- Awake, O Arm of the LORD! (vv.9-11)
- I Am He Who Comforts: Why Fear Man? (vv.12-16)
- Awake, O Jerusalem! The Cup Removed (vv.17-23)

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. Look to Abraham (v.1). ONE man. ONE barren wife. And from
   them: a nation. If your situation seems impossible, remember:
   God specializes in making much from little. Your "one" is
   enough for Him.

2. The heavens are temporary; salvation is permanent (v.6). You
   are building on something MORE LASTING than the universe
   itself. Let that sink in. Your faith outlasts the stars.

3. "Who art thou, that thou shouldest be afraid?" (v.12). Put
   your fears in proportion. The person intimidating you is
   MORTAL. The God comforting you is ETERNAL. Measure your fear
   by who's speaking it.

4. The cup is removed (v.22). If you've been drinking the bitter
   cup of God's discipline — hear this: it can be TAKEN from your
   hand. There's a "no more" moment coming. Discipline is not
   permanent.

5. "Awake, awake" — God calls YOU to rise (v.17). He's done His
   part; now He calls you to respond. Don't stay lying down when
   God is saying "stand up!" Sometimes deliverance requires you
   to MOVE.
