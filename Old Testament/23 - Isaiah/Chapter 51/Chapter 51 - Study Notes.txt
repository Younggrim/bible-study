Isaiah - Chapter 51
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
"Hearken unto me, ye that follow after righteousness" — three calls to listen (vv.1,4,7). Look to Abraham and Sarah (one became many). The LORD will comfort Zion. His righteousness is near, His salvation everlasting. "Awake, awake, put on strength, O arm of the LORD" — Israel calls God to act as He did at the Red Sea. Then God responds: "Awake, awake, stand up, O Jerusalem" — the cup of wrath is removed from your hand and given to your oppressors.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Isaiah son of Amoz (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Salvation Oracle
Key Themes: Look to your origins (Abraham/Sarah), God's comfort
for Zion, everlasting salvation, "Awake, awake" (calling God
and Zion to action), the cup of wrath transferred from Israel
to her enemies, Rahab/the sea-dragon defeated

Historical Context: Chapter 51 builds toward the climactic
liberation of 52:1-12. Three "listen" sections address those
who pursue righteousness (v.1), God's people (v.4), and those
who know righteousness (v.7). The central appeal is MEMORY: look
to Abraham (one man became a nation — so God can multiply again).
The "Awake" cry (v.9) recalls God's victory over Rahab (Egypt)
at the Red Sea — do it AGAIN! God answers with His own "Awake"
to Jerusalem (v.17; continued in 52:1): the cup of staggering
is taken away.

Structure:
- Look to Abraham: One Became Many (vv.1-3)
- Listen, My People: Everlasting Salvation (vv.4-6)
- Fear Not Man: He's Grass (vv.7-8)
- Awake, O Arm of the LORD! (vv.9-11)
- I Am He Who Comforts: Why Fear Man? (vv.12-16)
- Awake, O Jerusalem! The Cup Removed (vv.17-23)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "Look unto the rock whence ye are hewn, and to the hole
        of the pit whence ye are digged" (KJV) — Remember your
        origins: you came from Abraham and Sarah — ONE couple. If
        God could make a nation from one, He can restore a nation
        from a remnant.

v.6  — "the heavens shall vanish away like smoke, and the earth
        shall wax old like a garment... but my salvation shall be
        for ever, and my righteousness shall not be abolished"
        (KJV) — The UNIVERSE is temporary; God's salvation is
        PERMANENT. The heavens are the temporary thing; His
        righteousness is the lasting thing.

v.11 — "the redeemed of the LORD shall return, and come with
        singing unto Zion; and everlasting joy shall be upon their
        head" (KJV) — Identical to 35:10. The great return: singing,
        joy, permanence. Sorrow and mourning flee away.

v.12 — "I, even I, am he that comforteth you: who art thou, that
        thou shouldest be afraid of a man that shall die" (KJV) —
        The God of the universe comforts you — so why fear HUMANS
        who are mortal? Put human threats in proportion to divine
        comfort.

v.22 — "I have taken out of thine hand the cup of trembling...
        thou shalt no more drink it again" (KJV) — The cup of
        God's wrath REMOVED from Israel's hand. No more. Done.
        It passes to her oppressors (v.23). Transfer complete.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "rock whence ye are hewn" (KJV/ESV) — Abraham pictured
      as the quarry-stone from which Israel was cut. You came
      from somewhere; your origin story is miraculous.

v.6  — "vanish like smoke" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "nimlach" = be
      dispersed/torn apart. The heavens dissolve like dissipating
      smoke — here one moment, gone the next.

v.9  — "Rahab" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "rahav" = arrogance, storm.
      Mythological sea monster used as a name for Egypt (Psalm
      87:4). God's Red Sea victory described as slaying the dragon.

v.17 — "cup of trembling" (KJV) / "cup of staggering" (ESV) —
      Hebrew "kos hat-tar'elah" = the cup that makes one reel/
      stagger. God's wrath personified as intoxicating drink that
      causes helpless collapse.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Rock and Pit — (v.1) Abraham and Sarah pictured as the quarry/
              mine from which Israel was extracted. National
              identity traced to one miraculous couple.

Cup of Wrath — God's judgment pictured as a cup of wine that
              makes the drinker stagger and collapse. Passed from
              nation to nation. Christ drank this cup (Matt 26:39)
              so His people wouldn't have to.

Rahab        — Poetic name for Egypt (meaning "storm/pride")
              and the sea-monster God defeated at creation and
              at the Red Sea. Symbol of chaotic opposition to God.

Awake, Awake — Repeated refrain: Israel calls GOD to wake up
              (v.9), then God calls ISRAEL to wake up (v.17; 52:1).
              Both "sleeping parties" are roused to action.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
- v.1 — NET: "you who pursue godliness, who seek the Lord" / KJV: "ye that follow after righteousness, ye that seek the LORD" / WEB: "you who follow after righteousness, you who seek Yahweh" — NET renders "tsedeq" (righteousness) as "godliness," a significant shift from the concrete Hebrew concept of right-standing/justice to a more general piety. ASV/WEB preserve "righteousness" with the divine name.

- v.5 — NET: "I am ready to vindicate, I am ready to deliver" / KJV: "My righteousness is near; my salvation is gone forth" / WEB: "My righteousness is near. My salvation has gone out" — NET replaces nouns ("righteousness," "salvation") with verbs ("vindicate," "deliver"), making God's attributes into actions. The Hebrew uses nouns, suggesting these are abiding realities, not just future events.

- v.6 — NET: "the deliverance I give is permanent; the vindication I provide will not disappear" / KJV: "my salvation shall be for ever, and my righteousness shall not be abolished" — NET consistently renders "yeshu'ah" as "deliverance" and "tsedaqah" as "vindication" throughout this chapter, creating a pattern that differs from the traditional "salvation" and "righteousness."

- v.9 — NET: "Did you not smash the Proud One? Did you not wound the sea monster?" / KJV: "Art thou not it that hath cut Rahab, and wounded the dragon?" / ASV: "cut Rahab in pieces... pierced the monster" — NET interprets "Rahab" (meaning "proud/raging one") rather than transliterating the name. All refer to the mythological sea chaos imagery applied to Egypt at the Exodus.

- v.11 — NET: "Those whom the Lord has ransomed will return" / KJV: "the redeemed of the LORD shall return" / ASV: "the ransomed of Jehovah shall return" — NET uses "ransomed" (matching ASV), while KJV uses "redeemed." Hebrew "padah" = ransom/redeem by paying a price. The divine name varies characteristically across translations.

- v.16 — NET: "I commission you as my spokesman" / KJV: "I have put my words in thy mouth" / WEB: "I have put my words in your mouth" — NET completely rephrases — moving from the intimate image of God placing His words IN someone's mouth to a formal commissioning. The Hebrew is concrete and relational; NET is abstract and institutional.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Look to Abraham (v.1). ONE man. ONE barren wife. And from
   them: a nation. If your situation seems impossible, remember:
   God specializes in making much from little. Your "one" is
   enough for Him.

2. The heavens are temporary; salvation is permanent (v.6). You
   are building on something MORE LASTING than the universe
   itself. Let that sink in. Your faith outlasts the stars.

3. "Who art thou, that thou shouldest be afraid?" (v.12). Put
   your fears in proportion. The person intimidating you is
   MORTAL. The God comforting you is ETERNAL. Measure your fear
   by who's speaking it.

4. The cup is removed (v.22). If you've been drinking the bitter
   cup of God's discipline — hear this: it can be TAKEN from your
   hand. There's a "no more" moment coming. Discipline is not
   permanent.

5. "Awake, awake" — God calls YOU to rise (v.17). He's done His
   part; now He calls you to respond. Don't stay lying down when
   God is saying "stand up!" Sometimes deliverance requires you
   to MOVE.
