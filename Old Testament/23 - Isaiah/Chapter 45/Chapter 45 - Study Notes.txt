Isaiah - Chapter 45
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God calls Cyrus "my anointed" (messiah!) — anointing a pagan king to subdue nations for Israel's sake. God declares "I am the LORD, and there is none else," forms light and creates darkness, makes peace and creates calamity. Every knee will bow, every tongue confess. Israel shall be saved with an everlasting salvation.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Isaiah son of Amoz (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Oracle — The Cyrus Oracle / Sovereignty
Declaration
Key Themes: Cyrus as God's "anointed," God's absolute sovereignty
over good and evil, the potter and clay, every knee bowing, the
hidden God who saves, Israel's everlasting salvation

Historical Context: This is the most remarkable oracle concerning
Cyrus — a PAGAN king called "my anointed" (mashiach — the same
word used for Israel's kings and ultimately for Christ). God
anoints Cyrus to conquer nations, open doors, and release Israel
— even though Cyrus doesn't know God (v.4). The chapter contains
some of the strongest monotheistic declarations in Scripture
(vv.5-7, 18, 21-22) and the famous "every knee shall bow"
(v.23) — quoted by Paul of Christ in Philippians 2:10-11. The
sovereignty declarations (forming light AND darkness, making
peace AND calamity) address Zoroastrian dualism that would have
been familiar to Cyrus's Persian context.

Structure:
- Cyrus: God's Anointed Conqueror (vv.1-7)
- Let Righteousness Rain Down (v.8)
- The Potter and the Clay (vv.9-13)
- Nations Will Come to You, Israel (vv.14-17)
- God Didn't Speak in Secret (vv.18-19)
- Every Knee Shall Bow (vv.20-25)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "Thus saith the LORD to his anointed, to Cyrus, whose
        right hand I have holden, to subdue nations before him"
        (KJV) — God calls a pagan king "my anointed" (mashiach).
        Unprecedented. God uses anyone He wants for His purposes.

v.5  — "I am the LORD, and there is none else, there is no God
        beside me: I girded thee, though thou hast not known me"
        (KJV) — Cyrus didn't know God, yet God empowered him.
        Divine sovereignty doesn't require human cooperation or
        even awareness.

v.7  — "I form the light, and create darkness: I make peace, and
        create evil: I the LORD do all these things" (KJV) — The
        strongest sovereignty statement in Scripture. God is behind
        BOTH light and darkness. Nothing exists outside His
        sovereign allowance. (Not moral evil but calamity/disaster
        — Hebrew "ra" = adversity.)

v.9  — "Woe unto him that striveth with his Maker! Let the
        potsherd strive with the potsherds of the earth. Shall
        the clay say to him that fashioneth it, What makest thou?"
        (KJV) — Don't argue with the Potter. Paul quotes this in
        Romans 9:20.

v.22 — "Look unto me, and be ye saved, all the ends of the
        earth: for I am God, and there is none else" (KJV) —
        Universal salvation invitation. Spurgeon was converted by
        hearing a sermon on this verse. Not just Israel — ALL ends
        of the earth.

v.23 — "That unto me every knee shall bow, every tongue shall
        swear" (KJV) — Universal acknowledgment of God's lordship.
        Paul applies this directly to Jesus (Phil 2:10-11) —
        meaning Jesus IS the LORD of Isaiah 45.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1  — "anointed" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "mashiach" = anointed one,
      messiah. The same word used for David, Aaron, and ultimately
      THE Messiah. Applied to Cyrus it means "commissioned by God"
      — not that Cyrus is savior but that God consecrated him for
      a specific task.

v.7  — "evil" (KJV) / "calamity" (ESV) — Hebrew "ra" = bad,
      adversity, calamity, disaster. NOT moral evil (which would
      be "avon" or "chata'at"). God creates adversity/calamity as
      judgment; He does not create moral evil.

v.15 — "a God that hidest thyself" (KJV) / "a God who hides
      himself" (ESV) — Hebrew "El mistatter." God's hiddenness is
      not weakness but sovereignty. He works behind the scenes
      through pagan kings and invisible providence.

v.23 — "every knee shall bow" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "tibra kol
      berekh." Universal — voluntary or involuntary, every being
      will acknowledge God's lordship. Applied to Christ = Christ
      is YHWH.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. God uses people who don't know Him (vv.4-5). Cyrus was a
   pagan — yet God called him "my anointed." God is sovereign
   over people who don't acknowledge Him. Your boss, your
   government, your opponents — all under God's direction whether
   they know it or not.

2. "I form the light and create darkness" (v.7). God is sovereign
   over BOTH. The dark seasons of your life are not outside His
   control. He didn't create moral evil, but He rules over
   adversity. Nothing catches Him off guard.

3. "Look unto me and be saved" (v.22). Salvation is as simple as
   LOOKING. Not earning, not performing, not understanding
   everything — just looking to God. Spurgeon was 15 when this
   verse saved him. It can save you today.

4. Every knee WILL bow (v.23). The only question is: voluntarily
   now or involuntarily later? Every person will eventually
   acknowledge God's lordship. The wise do it now, by faith,
   and receive blessing.

5. The potter and clay (v.9). Stop arguing with God's design for
   your life. The clay doesn't get to redesign itself. Trust the
   Potter — His design is purposeful, even when you can't see
   the finished product.
