Isaiah - Chapter 3
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God removes Judah's competent leadership as judgment, leaving the nation in chaos under immature rulers — and pronounces specific woe on the proud women of Zion whose luxury will be replaced with shame.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Isaiah son of Amoz (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Oracle — Judgment Through Removal
Key Themes: God removes competent leaders as judgment, social
collapse when the righteous are taken away, the sin of oppressing
the poor, the pride of wealthy women exposed and judged

Historical Context: Isaiah continues describing Judah's present
corruption after the glorious future vision of chapter 2. God's
judgment takes a surprising form — not invasion but REMOVAL of
support. He removes bread, water, heroes, judges, prophets, elders,
and skilled craftsmen (vv.1-3). What remains is chaos: youths rule,
the people oppress each other, and society inverts (v.5: the child
behaves proudly against the ancient). Verses 16-26 describe the
wealthy women of Jerusalem in extraordinary detail — their fashion,
jewelry, and haughty walk. God will strip it all away, replacing
perfume with stench, beauty with shame, fine robes with sackcloth.

Structure:
- God Removes Every Support (vv.1-3)
- Chaos Under Incompetent Leadership (vv.4-7)
- Jerusalem's Open Sin (vv.8-9)
- The Righteous vs. the Wicked (vv.10-11)
- Oppressors of God's People Charged (vv.12-15)
- The Proud Women of Zion Judged (vv.16-26)

CROSS-REFERENCES
------------------------------------
v.1 — Ezekiel 4:16 — God withdrawing provision as judgment.
v.4 — Ecclesiastes 10:16 — immature leadership as national curse.
v.9 — Genesis 13:13 — Judah compared again to Sodom, now for open/unashamed sin.
v.10 — Psalm 1:3 — The righteous man prospers even amid judgment.
v.12 — Matthew 15:14 — If the blind lead the blind, both shall fall into the ditch
v.15 — Amos 4:1 — wealthy women criticized for complicity in injustice.
v.24 — 1 Peter 3:3-4 — True beauty is internal, not external adornment — the NT counterpoint.

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------
Enduring Word (David Guzik):
  https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/isaiah-3/
  Guzik examines how God's judgment works through removal of
  competent leadership, the social chaos that follows, and the
  detailed description of Jerusalem's proud women.

Charles Spurgeon:
  "The removal of the stay and the staff is the most terrible
  form of divine judgment — not fire from heaven but the quiet
  withdrawal of God's sustaining hand. When God takes away wise
  men and gives a nation fools for rulers, that IS the judgment."

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. God's judgment sometimes looks like REMOVAL rather than
   destruction (vv.1-3). He simply takes away the competent
   people, the wise leaders, the skilled workers — and lets chaos
   do the rest. What blessings in your life might be God's
   sustaining gifts that you take for granted?

2. When youth despises age and the base mocks the honorable (v.5),
   society is under judgment. How do you honor the experienced,
   the elderly, the wise — even when culture tells you they're
   irrelevant?

3. "They declare their sin as Sodom, they hide it not" (v.9) —
   unashamed, public sin. When a culture loses the capacity for
   shame, it has crossed a dangerous line. What sins has your
   culture normalized that Scripture still calls sin?

4. God takes the oppression of the poor PERSONALLY (v.15). "MY
   people" — He claims them. How do you treat people who have no
   power to benefit you? The waitress, the janitor, the homeless
   person? God is watching.

5. Pride expressed through luxury will be stripped (vv.16-24). The
   things you use to project status, attract attention, or feel
   superior — God can remove them overnight. What identity would
   you have if everything external was taken away?
