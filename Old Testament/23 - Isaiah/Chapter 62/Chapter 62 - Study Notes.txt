Isaiah - Chapter 62
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
"For Zion's sake will I not hold my peace" — God (or His prophet) refuses to be silent until Jerusalem's righteousness shines like a blazing torch. Zion receives a NEW NAME: "Hephzibah" (My Delight Is in Her) and "Beulah" (Married). Watchmen set on the walls give God no rest until He establishes Jerusalem as a praise in the earth.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Isaiah son of Amoz (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Salvation Oracle — Zion's New Name
Key Themes: Relentless intercession, Zion's new name, God's
delight in His people, the marriage metaphor, watchmen who never
rest, giving God no rest until He acts, the highway prepared

Historical Context: Continuing the Zion-glory section (chs.60-62),
this chapter emphasizes PERSISTENT intercession. The speaker (God,
the prophet, or the Servant) refuses silence until Jerusalem's
restoration is visible to all nations. The renaming of Zion from
"Forsaken" and "Desolate" to "Hephzibah" (My Delight) and
"Beulah" (Married) reverses the shame of exile. The watchmen on
the walls (vv.6-7) model unceasing prayer — reminding God of His
promises until they're fulfilled.

Structure:
- I Will Not Be Silent Until Zion Shines (vv.1-3)
- Renamed: Hephzibah and Beulah (vv.4-5)
- Watchmen Who Give God No Rest (vv.6-7)
- God's Oath: No More Plunder (vv.8-9)
- Prepare the Way: The Redeemed Are Coming (vv.10-12)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "For Zion's sake will I not hold my peace, and for
        Jerusalem's sake I will not rest, until the righteousness
        thereof go forth as brightness, and the salvation thereof
        as a lamp that burneth" (KJV) — Relentless pursuit of
        Zion's restoration. No silence, no rest until it's done.

v.4  — "Thou shalt no more be termed Forsaken; neither shall thy
        land any more be termed Desolate: but thou shalt be called
        Hephzibah, and thy land Beulah: for the LORD delighteth in
        thee, and thy land shall be married" (KJV) — Identity
        transformation through renaming. Your old names (Forsaken,
        Desolate) are REVOKED. New names speak God's truth about
        you.

v.5  — "as the bridegroom rejoiceth over the bride, so shall thy
        God rejoice over thee" (KJV) — The intensity of God's joy
        over His people: like a groom's delight on his wedding day.
        Passionate, personal, celebratory.

v.6  — "I have set watchmen upon thy walls, O Jerusalem, which
        shall never hold their peace day nor night: ye that make
        mention of the LORD, keep not silence" (KJV) — Intercessors
        who pray WITHOUT STOPPING. Their job: remind God of His
        promises 24/7.

v.7  — "And give him no rest, till he establish, and till he make
        Jerusalem a praise in the earth" (KJV) — Audacious prayer:
        give GOD no rest! Persistently, repeatedly, boldly remind
        Him until He acts. Not because He forgot — but because
        He invites this kind of boldness.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.4  — "Hephzibah" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "Cheftsi-bah" = "My
      delight is in her." God's pleasure in His people expressed
      as a personal name. Also the name of Hezekiah's wife
      (2 Kings 21:1).

v.4  — "Beulah" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "be'ulah" = married, owned
      by a husband. The land is no longer abandoned like a
      desolate widow but CLAIMED, loved, inhabited.

v.6  — "watchmen" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "shomrim" = guards/
      watchers. Here: intercessors stationed on Jerusalem's walls
      whose job is perpetual prayer. Not military sentinels but
      prayer warriors.

v.10 — "cast up the highway" (KJV) / "build up the highway"
      (ESV) — Hebrew "solu solu ha-mesillah" = pile up, build up
      the road. Same preparation language as 40:3. Clear the road
      for the returning redeemed.

v.4 — ASV: uses "Jehovah" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).
       NET: "called, “Abandoned,” and your" for KJV's "termed Forsaken; neither shall thy"
       WEB: uses "Yahweh" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).
v.6 — ASV: uses "Jehovah" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).
       NET: "Jerusalem; they should keep praying all" for KJV's "Jerusalem, which shall never hold their peace"
       WEB: uses "Yahweh" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).
v.10 — ASV: "an ensign" for KJV's "a standard"
       NET: "Come through! Come" for KJV's "Go through, go"
       WEB: "highway! Gather" for KJV's "highways; gather"

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Your old names are revoked (v.4). Whatever labels your past
   gave you — Forsaken, Desolate, Rejected, Worthless — God says:
   NO. Your new name is "My Delight Is In Her." Accept the
   rename. Stop answering to the old names.

2. God REJOICES over you like a groom (v.5). Not tolerance, not
   grudging acceptance — REJOICING. Wedding-day delight. God
   is thrilled about you. Let that sink in.

3. "Give Him no rest" (v.7). God invites aggressive, persistent,
   relentless prayer. Not polite, once-a-week requests but daily,
   bold, doesn't-give-up intercession. He's not annoyed by your
   persistence — He COMMANDED it.

4. Watchmen who never hold their peace (v.6). Some people are
   called to be PRAY-ERS above all else. If that's you, don't
   apologize for it. The walls need watchmen. The world needs
   intercessors. Pray until you see the breakthrough.

5. "I will not rest" (v.1). God Himself is not passive about your
   restoration. HE won't rest until your righteousness shines.
   You're not the only one working toward your breakthrough —
   God is actively, relentlessly pursuing it too.
