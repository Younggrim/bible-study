Isaiah - Chapter 58
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
True vs. false fasting. Israel complains: "We fasted, but You didn't notice!" God responds: your fasting is self-serving, not God-honoring. TRUE fasting is loosing the bonds of wickedness, feeding the hungry, clothing the naked, sheltering the homeless. THEN your light will break forth, your healing will spring up, and you'll be called "The Repairer of the Breach."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Isaiah son of Amoz (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Oracle — True and False Worship
Key Themes: False fasting (self-serving religion), true fasting
(justice and mercy), the conditions for answered prayer, light
breaking forth, Sabbath delight, repairing the breach

Historical Context: The returned exiles (or the pre-exilic
community — the principle is timeless) practice religious
disciplines (fasting, prayer) but see no results. They accuse God
of ignoring them (v.3). God's response: your FAST is wrong. You
fast while oppressing workers (v.3), fighting (v.4), and pursuing
self-interest. TRUE fasting (vv.6-7) is justice-oriented: free the
oppressed, feed the hungry, house the homeless. This chapter
inspired many social justice movements throughout church history.

Structure:
- Cry Aloud: Declare Their Sins (v.1)
- "Why Have We Fasted and You Don't See?" (vv.2-3a)
- False Fasting Exposed (vv.3b-5)
- True Fasting Defined (vv.6-7)
- The Promises: Light, Healing, Guidance (vv.8-12)
- Sabbath Delight (vv.13-14)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.3  — "Wherefore have we fasted, say they, and thou seest not?
        wherefore have we afflicted our soul, and thou takest no
        knowledge?" (KJV) — The complaint: we did the religious
        stuff and God didn't respond. The assumption: ritual
        performance obligates God to act.

v.6  — "Is not this the fast that I have chosen? to loose the
        bands of wickedness, to undo the heavy burdens, and to
        let the oppressed go free, and that ye break every yoke?"
        (KJV) — GOD'S definition of fasting: not hunger pangs but
        justice action. Setting the oppressed free is worship.

v.7  — "Is it not to deal thy bread to the hungry, and that thou
        bring the poor that are cast out to thy house? when thou
        seest the naked, that thou cover him?" (KJV) — Four
        concrete acts: feed the hungry, house the homeless, clothe
        the naked, don't hide from your own family. THESE are
        fasting.

v.8  — "Then shall thy light break forth as the morning, and
        thine health shall spring forth speedily" (KJV) — The
        result of TRUE fasting: light, healing, God's glory as
        rearguard, ANSWERED prayer (v.9). Justice precedes
        blessing.

v.12 — "thou shalt be called, The repairer of the breach, The
        restorer of paths to dwell in" (KJV) — The identity for
        those who practice true fasting: REPAIRER. Not destroyer,
        not critic — repairer of what's broken, restorer of what's
        lost.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "like a trumpet" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "ka-shofar" = like a
      ram's horn blast. The prophet's voice must be LOUD and
      ALARMING — not gentle persuasion but urgent warning.

v.6  — "yoke" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "motah" = a wooden bar/yoke on
      the neck. Oppressive burdens placed on the vulnerable.
      True fasting BREAKS these — removes oppressive systems.

v.8  — "rereward" (KJV) / "rear guard" (ESV) — Hebrew
      "me'assefekha" = your rear guard/gatherer. God's glory
      protects from behind — covering your exposed back.

v.12 — "repairer of the breach" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "goder
      perets" = one who walls up/closes the gap. A breach is a
      broken-down section of wall leaving the city vulnerable.
      Repairers restore protection and structure.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
True Fasting — God's definition: not merely abstaining from food
              but abstaining from injustice while actively pursuing
              mercy. Justice IS worship.

Repairer of the Breach — A title for those who restore what's
              broken — relationships, communities, justice systems,
              spiritual health. Building, not just critiquing.

Sabbath Delight — (vv.13-14) The Sabbath as JOY, not burden.
              Calling it "a delight" and "honourable" rather than
              a restriction. Rest as pleasure, not obligation.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Is your religion self-serving? (vv.3-5). Fasting to get GOD
   to do what you want is manipulation, not worship. Check your
   motives: is your spiritual discipline about serving God's
   purposes or getting God to serve yours?

2. True fasting = justice (vv.6-7). God cares less about your
   empty stomach than your full hands. Feed someone. House
   someone. Clothe someone. THAT is worship. What injustice can
   you address this week?

3. "Then shall thy light break forth" (v.8). The breakthrough
   you're praying for may be waiting on the other side of JUSTICE.
   When you start doing what God defines as worship (caring for
   the vulnerable), the answers you've been seeking may arrive.

4. "Repairer of the breach" (v.12). What if your calling isn't to
   build something new but to REPAIR something broken? Families,
   communities, churches — they need repairers, not just critics.
   Where's the breach near you?

5. Sabbath as DELIGHT (vv.13-14). Rest isn't punishment — it's
   pleasure. If you see rest as lazy or unproductive, you've
   missed God's heart. He made rest and called it holy. Delight
   in it.
