Isaiah - Chapter 65
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God's answer to the prayer of chapters 63-64: "I was found by those who did not seek me" (Gentile inclusion!), but rebellious Israel provoked Me. God will preserve a remnant ("new wine is found in the cluster — don't destroy it"). He announces NEW HEAVENS AND A NEW EARTH: no more weeping, long life, building houses and living in them, the wolf and lamb together. The old troubles forgotten.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Isaiah son of Amoz (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Oracle — Divine Response + New Creation
Key Themes: Gentiles finding God, Israel's rebellion, the
faithful remnant preserved, judgment on the unfaithful, new
heavens and new earth, joy replacing sorrow, long life, peace
in nature

Historical Context: This chapter answers the anguished prayer of
63:15-64:12. But God's answer is surprising: He was ALREADY being
found — by Gentiles who weren't even looking (v.1, quoted in
Romans 10:20). Meanwhile, Israel was provoking Him with idolatry
(vv.3-5). Yet God will NOT destroy the whole cluster — a remnant
is preserved (v.8). The chapter climaxes (vv.17-25) with the
creation of NEW HEAVENS AND A NEW EARTH — one of the OT's
grandest eschatological visions, expanded in Revelation 21.

Structure:
- Found by Those Who Didn't Seek Me (v.1)
- Israel's Rebellion and Provocation (vv.2-7)
- The Remnant Preserved (vv.8-12)
- Servants Blessed vs. Rebels Cursed (vv.13-16)
- New Heavens and New Earth (vv.17-25)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "I am sought of them that asked not for me; I am found of
        them that sought me not" (KJV) — God found by GENTILES who
        weren't looking. Paul quotes this in Romans 10:20 for
        Gentile inclusion. Grace goes where it's not expected.

v.8  — "As the new wine is found in the cluster, and one saith,
        Destroy it not; for a blessing is in it" (KJV) — The
        remnant principle: don't destroy the whole vine because
        there's still good wine in some clusters. God preserves
        for the sake of the faithful few.

v.17 — "For, behold, I create new heavens and a new earth: and
        the former shall not be remembered, nor come into mind"
        (KJV) — NEW CREATION. Not merely renovation but CREATION
        — the same verb as Genesis 1:1 ("bara"). The old is so
        thoroughly replaced that it's not even remembered.

v.24 — "And it shall come to pass, that before they call, I will
        answer; and while they are yet speaking, I will hear"
        (KJV) — Prayer answered before it's even spoken! God's
        responsiveness in the new creation is instantaneous — He
        anticipates needs.

v.25 — "The wolf and the lamb shall feed together, and the lion
        shall eat straw like the bullock" (KJV) — Nature restored
        to Edenic peace. The predator-prey relationship undone.
        The curse on creation reversed (echoing 11:6-9).

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "sought... found" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "nidrashti...
      nimtseti." Passive voice: "I let myself be sought/found."
      God allows Himself to be discovered by those who weren't
      even looking.

v.17 — "create" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "bore" (from "bara") = to
      create ex nihilo. The same verb used in Genesis 1:1. This
      is not remodeling — it's Genesis-level creation.

v.20 — "the child shall die an hundred years old" (KJV/ESV) —
      In the new creation, dying at 100 is considered premature
      (like a child's death). Lifespans return to pre-flood
      lengths or beyond.

v.25 — "dust shall be the serpent's meat" (KJV/ESV) — The
      serpent's curse (Genesis 3:14) remains. Even in the new
      creation, the serpent remains humbled — a reminder of
      defeated evil.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
New Heavens and New Earth — God's complete recreation of the
              physical and spiritual order. Not renovation but
              replacement. The old order — with its pain, death,
              and curse — is unmade and remade.

Remnant      — The faithful minority preserved within unfaithful
              Israel. "New wine in the cluster" — the blessing
              that prevents total destruction.

Before They Call, I Answer — (v.24) The intimate responsiveness
              of God in the new creation. Prayer and answer become
              simultaneous — no more waiting, no more silence.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. God is found by those not looking (v.1). Grace is surprising.
   God shows up where He's not expected — and sometimes those
   who think they have Him figured out are the ones who miss Him
   (v.2). Stay humble.

2. "Destroy it not; a blessing is in it" (v.8). God preserves
   groups for the sake of the faithful few. YOUR faithfulness may
   be the reason God doesn't give up on your family, church, or
   community. The blessing is in YOU.

3. NEW creation (v.17). God's final plan isn't patching the old
   world — it's making a new one. Your problems aren't being
   managed; they're being REPLACED. Think bigger than repair;
   think recreation.

4. "Before they call, I will answer" (v.24). In the fullness of
   God's kingdom, there will be zero delay between need and
   provision. Every longing met before it's even articulated.
   If that's the destination, trust God's timing now.

5. The wolf and lamb together (v.25). Peace so complete that
   even NATURE is healed. The world you long for — without
   predation, violence, or fear — IS coming. This isn't wishful
   thinking; it's divine promise.
