Isaiah - Chapter 49
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The second Servant Song — the Servant speaks in first person: called from the womb, named before birth, a polished arrow hidden in God's quiver. Though discouraged ("I have laboured in vain"), God expands his mission: not just to restore Israel but to be "a light to the Gentiles, that thou mayest be my salvation unto the end of the earth." Zion says "God has forgotten me" — God answers: "Can a woman forget her nursing child? I have graven thee upon the palms of my hands."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Isaiah son of Amoz (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Oracle — Second Servant Song (vv.1-6)
+ Zion's Restoration (vv.7-26)
Key Themes: The Servant's calling from the womb, apparent failure
redeemed by larger mission, light to the Gentiles, Zion not
forgotten, engraved on God's palms, children returning

Historical Context: The second Servant Song (49:1-6) shifts
perspective: the Servant now speaks in FIRST PERSON (contrasting
with 42:1-9 where God introduces Him). The Servant experiences
discouragement (v.4) yet God expands rather than abandons the
mission. The Servant is too great for merely restoring Israel —
He must reach the whole world (v.6). Simeon quotes v.6 when he
holds baby Jesus (Luke 2:32). The remainder (vv.7-26) assures
Zion that God has NOT forgotten her, using the most intimate
imagery: nursing mothers and tattooed palms.

Structure:
- Second Servant Song: Called from the Womb (vv.1-6)
- The Servant Despised, Then Honored by Kings (v.7)
- The Restoration: Prisoners Released, Highways Opened (vv.8-13)
- Zion: "The LORD Has Forsaken Me" (v.14)
- "Can a Woman Forget?" — Engraved on My Palms (vv.15-16)
- Zion's Children Return (vv.17-26)

  "'I have graven thee upon the palms of my hands.' Not written —
  GRAVEN. Cut into the flesh. Permanent as scar tissue. The nail
  prints in Christ's resurrected hands are God's tattoo of His
  people — He will NEVER forget you."

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. "I have laboured in vain" (v.4). Even the Servant felt
   discouraged. If Jesus can feel that ministry seems fruitless,
   you're allowed to feel it too — without quitting. The next
   verse ALWAYS expands the mission.

2. Light to the Gentiles (v.6). Your calling may be bigger than
   you think. What feels like failure in one arena may be God
   redirecting you to a larger stage. "Too small a thing" —
   God has MORE for you.

3. "Can a woman forget her nursing child?" (v.15). Some women
   DO forget — abuse, abandonment, neglect are real. But even
   if the strongest human love fails you, God's love CANNOT.
   If you were abandoned by a parent, hear this: God's love
   surpasses the best human love.

4. "Graven upon the palms of my hands" (v.16). You are tattooed
   on God's hands. He cannot look at His own palms without seeing
   YOU. Stop believing you're forgotten. The nail prints prove
   otherwise.

5. Hidden in His quiver (v.2). If you're in a hidden season —
   unrecognized, unplatformed, seemingly unused — you may be in
   God's quiver being polished for deployment. Hiddenness is not
   abandonment; it's preparation.
