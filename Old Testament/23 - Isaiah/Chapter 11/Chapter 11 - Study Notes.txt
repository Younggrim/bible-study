Isaiah - Chapter 11
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
A shoot springs from Jesse's stump — the Spirit-anointed Messiah who judges with righteousness, brings peace to creation (the wolf dwelling with the lamb), and gathers Israel's scattered remnant from the nations in a second exodus.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Isaiah son of Amoz (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Messianic Oracle — The Branch and the Kingdom
Key Themes: The Branch from Jesse's root, the sevenfold Spirit,
righteous judgment, the peaceable kingdom (wolf and lamb), the
second exodus gathering, reunification of Israel and Judah

Historical Context: After chapter 10 depicted Assyria as a forest
God would cut down (10:33-34), chapter 11 shows what grows from
ANOTHER stump — Jesse's. David's dynasty appears dead (a cut
stump), but a shoot (netser) springs from the root. This is the
Messiah: Spirit-empowered, righteous in judgment, bringing cosmic
peace. The animal kingdom reconciled (vv.6-9) symbolizes creation
restored to Edenic harmony. Verses 10-16 describe a future
regathering of Israel from all nations — a "second exodus" that
surpasses the first (v.16: "like as it was to Israel in the day
that he came up out of the land of Egypt").

Structure:
- The Shoot from Jesse's Stump (v.1)
- The Sevenfold Spirit (v.2)
- Righteous Judgment (vv.3-5)
- The Peaceable Kingdom (vv.6-9)
- The Root of Jesse as a Banner (v.10)
- The Second Exodus: Gathering the Remnant (vv.11-16)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "And there shall come forth a rod out of the stem of Jesse,
        and a Branch shall grow out of his roots" (KJV) — Jesse
        (not David) — the dynasty reduced to its pre-royal state,
        a stump. Yet life remains in the roots. The Branch (netser
        — possibly connected to "Nazarene," Matt 2:23) grows from
        apparent death.

v.2  — "And the spirit of the LORD shall rest upon him, the spirit
        of wisdom and understanding, the spirit of counsel and
        might, the spirit of knowledge and of the fear of the LORD"
        (KJV) — The sevenfold Spirit: (1) the Spirit of the LORD,
        (2) wisdom, (3) understanding, (4) counsel, (5) might,
        (6) knowledge, (7) fear of the LORD. Complete, total
        anointing — Jesus at His baptism (Luke 3:22; 4:1, 18).

v.6  — "The wolf also shall dwell with the lamb, and the leopard
        shall lie down with the kid; and the calf and the young lion
        and the fatling together; and a little child shall lead them"
        (KJV) — The peaceable kingdom: natural enemies reconciled.
        Predator and prey coexist because the curse is reversed.
        A child leads them — innocence restored, danger removed.

v.9  — "They shall not hurt nor destroy in all my holy mountain:
        for the earth shall be full of the knowledge of the LORD,
        as the waters cover the sea" (KJV) — The reason for peace:
        universal knowledge of God. When everyone knows God, no one
        hurts. The ocean metaphor — not patches of knowledge but
        COVERING — complete, pervasive, deep.

v.10 — "in that day there shall be a root of Jesse, which shall
        stand for an ensign of the people; to it shall the Gentiles
        seek" (KJV) — GENTILES seek Him. The Messiah is not for
        Israel only — He is a banner that draws ALL nations. Paul
        quotes this in Romans 15:12 to justify Gentile inclusion.

CROSS-REFERENCES
------------------------------------
v.1   — Jeremiah 23:5 — "I will raise unto David a righteous
         Branch" — same messianic imagery.
v.1   — Revelation 5:5 — "the Root of David" — Christ as both
         root (source) and branch (descendant) of David.
v.2   — Luke 4:18 — "The Spirit of the Lord is upon me" — Jesus
         claims Isaiah's Spirit-anointing at Nazareth.
v.2   — Revelation 1:4 — "the seven Spirits before his throne" —
         the sevenfold Spirit of Isaiah 11:2.
v.4   — 2 Thessalonians 2:8 — "the Lord shall consume with the
         spirit of his mouth" — the Messiah slays the wicked by
         His word.
v.6   — Romans 8:19-21 — Creation groaning, waiting for liberation
         from the curse — the peaceable kingdom anticipated.
v.9   — Habakkuk 2:14 — "the earth shall be filled with the
         knowledge of the glory of the LORD, as the waters cover
         the sea."
v.10  — Romans 15:12 — Paul quotes this: "There shall be a root
         of Jesse, and he that shall rise to reign over the
         Gentiles; in him shall the Gentiles trust."

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1  — "rod" (KJV) / "shoot" (ESV) — Hebrew "choter" = a twig/
      shoot/sprout. A sign of life from what looks dead. "Branch"
      (KJV) / "branch" (ESV) — "netser" = a green shoot. Possibly
      the etymological source of "Nazarene" (Matt 2:23).

v.2  — "rest upon him" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "nachah" = to settle
      down permanently (not temporary visitation but permanent
      residence). The Spirit doesn't come and go from the Messiah.

v.4  — "the rod of his mouth" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "shevet" =
      staff/scepter. His WORD functions as a weapon — He rules and
      judges by speaking. "The breath of his lips" kills the wicked
      — no sword needed, just divine utterance.

v.6  — "dwell" (KJV) / "dwell" (ESV) — Hebrew "gur" = to sojourn
      together/reside as a guest. The wolf doesn't merely tolerate
      the lamb — they share living space. Intimacy, not just
      coexistence.

v.11 — "a second time" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "shenit" = a second
      occurrence. The first gathering: from Egypt. The second: from
      Assyria, Egypt, Pathros, Cush, Elam, Shinar, Hamath, and the
      coastlands. A global regathering — partially fulfilled in
      1948, ultimately in the messianic age.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. God brings life from stumps (v.1). Whatever looks dead in your
   life — a dream, a relationship, a calling — God specializes in
   resurrection from apparent death. The stump is not the end;
   the roots still live. Is there something you've given up on
   that God might still bring to life?

2. The Spirit's anointing is COMPLETE on the Messiah (v.2) — and
   available to you through Christ. Wisdom for your decisions,
   understanding for your confusion, counsel for your crossroads,
   might for your weakness, knowledge for your ignorance, fear of
   God for your presumption. Which aspect do you most need today?

3. "The earth shall be full of the knowledge of the LORD as the
   waters cover the sea" (v.9). This is the GOAL — not just
   personal salvation but cosmic transformation. Are you
   contributing to the spread of God's knowledge, or are you
   hoarding it?

4. Natural enemies reconciled (vv.6-8). The gospel reconciles
   people who should be enemies — racial, political, economic
   opposites brought together. Who is your "wolf" that you need
   to see as a potential "lamb" — someone the gospel might
   transform?

5. "A little child shall lead them" (v.6). In God's kingdom,
   leadership doesn't require strength, age, or experience — it
   requires innocence and trust. The complicated world is led by
   the simple. What childlike quality have you lost that God might
   want to restore?
