Malachi - Chapter 3
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God will send His messenger to prepare the way, then suddenly come to His temple — He calls Israel to return through faithful tithing ("test Me in this") and promises to distinguish the righteous from the wicked.


AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Malachi ("my messenger"). The last OT prophet,
approximately 450-430 BC. Key theme: post-exilic spiritual
lethargy — the people go through religious motions without
heart. Structured as disputations (God accuses, people
challenge, God responds). Famous: "I have loved you" (1:2),
"Will a man rob God?" (3:8-10), "the Sun of righteousness
arise with healing in his wings" (4:2), and Elijah's return
(4:5-6). The OT ends with a promise and a warning.

Classification: Prophetic Disputation — The Coming Messenger,
Robbing God, and the Book of Remembrance
Key Themes: The forerunner preparing the way, the Lord suddenly
coming to His Temple, refining fire, God's unchanging character,
robbing God in tithes, the open windows of heaven, the book of
remembrance for the faithful, God's treasured possession

Historical Context: Chapter 3 answers the cynical question of
2:17 — "Where is the God of judgment?" Answer: He IS coming.
But first, a messenger will prepare the way. Then "the Lord,
whom ye seek, shall SUDDENLY come to his temple." The double
fulfillment: John the Baptist as forerunner, and Christ suddenly
entering the Temple (both at His first coming and ultimately at
His second).

The middle section (vv.6-12) contains one of the most quoted
passages in Scripture: "Will a man rob God?" The people had
withheld tithes and offerings, and God calls this ROBBERY. But
He also offers an extraordinary invitation: "prove me now" —
test Me, try Me. This is the ONLY place in Scripture where God
invites testing. Bring the tithe, and watch the windows of heaven
open with blessing "that there shall not be room enough."

The chapter closes with a contrast: the cynics who say "it's
useless to serve God" (vv.13-15) versus "those who feared the
LORD" who spoke to one another and were recorded in God's "book
of remembrance" (vv.16-17). God sees, hears, and records the
faithful. They are His "jewels" (KJV) / "treasured possession."

Structure:
- The Messenger Who Prepares the Way (v.1a)
- The Lord Suddenly Coming to His Temple (v.1b)
- The Refiner's Fire: Who Can Stand? (vv.2-4)
- God's Swift Witness Against Sin (v.5)
- "I Change Not": God's Faithfulness (v.6)
- "Return to Me" and the People's Challenge (v.7)
- "Will a Man Rob God?" — Tithes and Offerings (vv.8-9)
- The Open Windows of Heaven (vv.10-12)
- The Cynics: "It Is Vain to Serve God" (vv.13-15)
- The Faithful: The Book of Remembrance (vv.16-17)
- The Final Distinction: Righteous and Wicked (v.18)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "Behold, I will send my messenger, and he shall prepare
        the way before me: and the Lord, whom ye seek, shall
        suddenly come to his temple" (KJV) — Two figures: (1) the
        messenger/forerunner = John the Baptist (Matt 11:10, Mark
        1:2); (2) "the Lord" = Christ Himself. The one they
        sought would come — but not as they expected. Not as
        political liberator but as REFINER. And He comes
        SUDDENLY — without warning to those not watching.

v.2  — "who shall stand when he appeareth? for he is like a
        refiner's fire, and like fullers' sope" (KJV) — The
        Messiah's coming is not comfortable — it is confronting.
        Refiner's fire burns away dross. Fuller's soap scrubs
        away filth. His purpose is PURIFICATION, not destruction.
        But it is intense.

v.6  — "For I am the LORD, I change not; therefore ye sons of
        Jacob are not consumed" (KJV) — God's immutability is
        Israel's salvation. If God CHANGED, He would have destroyed
        them long ago. But because He does NOT change, His covenant
        love endures. Your survival is not because you deserve it
        but because GOD is faithful to Himself.

v.8  — "Will a man rob God? Yet ye have robbed me. But ye say,
        Wherein have we robbed thee? In tithes and offerings" (KJV)
        — The most famous verse in Malachi. Withholding what
        belongs to God is ROBBERY. Not a minor oversight — a
        crime. The tithe belongs to God; keeping it is theft.
        The characteristic "Wherein?" response shows they didn't
        even recognize their sin.

v.10 — "Bring ye all the tithes into the storehouse, that there
        may be meat in mine house, and prove me now herewith,
        saith the LORD of hosts, if I will not open you the
        windows of heaven, and pour you out a blessing, that
        there shall not be room enough to receive it" (KJV) —
        God's extraordinary invitation to TEST HIM. This is the
        only place in Scripture where God says "put me to the
        test." He GUARANTEES blessing for obedient giving: windows
        of heaven opened, blessings overflowing, the devourer
        rebuked. Try it and see.

v.16 — "a book of remembrance was written before him for them
        that feared the LORD, and that thought upon his name"
        (KJV) — God keeps a record. Not of judgment — of
        REMEMBRANCE. Those who fear Him, who think on His name,
        who speak of Him to one another — they are written in
        His book. He notices. He remembers. He treasures.

v.17 — "they shall be mine, saith the LORD of hosts, in that day
        when I make up my jewels" (KJV) / "They shall be mine...
        my treasured possession" (ESV) — God's ultimate claim on
        the faithful: "MINE." His jewels. His special treasure.
        Not slaves but GEMS — precious, valued, displayed with
        pride. This is how God sees those who fear Him.

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1 — "my messenger" — Hebrew "mal'aki" — the same word as the
      prophet's name! Malachi ("my messenger") announces another
      "my messenger" (John the Baptist) who precedes THE Lord.
      Three messengers: Malachi, John, and Christ Himself ("the
      messenger of the covenant").

v.2 — "fullers' sope" (KJV) / "fullers' soap" (ESV) — Hebrew
      "borit mekabesim" = a lye soap used to cleanse and whiten
      cloth. The fuller beats and scrubs fabric clean. God's
      purification is vigorous — not gentle dabbing but thorough
      scrubbing.

v.6 — "I change not" — Hebrew "lo shaniti" = I have not changed,
      I do not change. The theological term is "immutability."
      God's character, promises, and covenant love are fixed and
      unchangeable. This is both comfort and warning.

v.10 — "prove me" — Hebrew "bechanuni" = test me, try me, assay
       me (as testing metal). This is an imperative — a COMMAND
       to test God. Normally testing God is forbidden (Deut 6:16),
       but HERE God invites it. In giving, you may put God to the
       proof.

v.17 — "jewels" (KJV) / "treasured possession" (ESV) — Hebrew
       "segullah" = special treasure, valued property. The same
       word used in Exodus 19:5 for Israel as God's "peculiar
       treasure." The faithful remnant is God's gem collection.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "The Lord shall SUDDENLY come" (v.1). Christ's coming is
   sudden — both to the Temple in His first advent and to the
   earth in His second. Are you ready for the "suddenly"? Live
   in readiness. He does not send advance notice to those who
   are not watching.

2. "I the LORD do not change" (v.6). God's unchanging nature is
   your anchor. In a world of shifting values and broken promises,
   GOD is fixed. His love doesn't fluctuate. His promises don't
   expire. His character doesn't evolve. Rest in His immutability.

3. "Will a man rob God?" (v.8). Sobering question. If the tithe
   belongs to God, then withholding it is not "saving" — it is
   stealing. Examine your giving honestly. Are you giving God
   what is rightfully His, or are you robbing Him while claiming
   you can't afford it?

4. "Prove me now" (v.10). God INVITES you to test Him in giving.
   This is not prosperity gospel — this is God's own challenge.
   Bring the full tithe. Watch what happens. He promises: open
   windows, overflowing blessing, the devourer rebuked. Faith
   in giving is faith God rewards.

5. "They shall be mine... my jewels" (v.17). In a world full of
   cynics (vv.14-15), be among those who FEAR the Lord and think
   on His name (v.16). God is watching. He is recording. And He
   is preparing a day when He will display His treasured ones.
   You are not forgotten — you are remembered in His book.
