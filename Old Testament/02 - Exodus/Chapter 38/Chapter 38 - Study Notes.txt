Exodus - Chapter 38
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The bronze altar, basin, and courtyard are built; an accounting of all the gold, silver, and bronze used in construction.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Exodus 38 continues the construction narrative, moving from
the interior furnishings (ch. 37) to the exterior elements: the Bronze Altar of
Burnt Offering (vv.1–7), the Bronze Laver (v.8), and the Courtyard with its
hangings, pillars, and gate (vv.9–20). The chapter concludes with a detailed
inventory of materials used (vv.21–31) — a financial accounting of the
tabernacle project that demonstrates both transparency and the magnitude of
Israel's generosity.

The Bronze Altar was the first thing a worshiper encountered upon entering the
courtyard — before approaching God's presence, sacrifice must be made. The
Laver, made from the bronze mirrors of the women who served at the entrance,
provided water for priestly washing — cleansing before service. These outer
court items were bronze (not gold), representing judgment and purification
rather than the divine glory of the inner sanctuary.

The materials inventory (vv.21–31) is remarkable: 29 talents and 730 shekels of
gold (approximately 2,200 pounds), 100 talents and 1,775 shekels of silver
(approximately 7,500 pounds), and 70 talents and 2,400 shekels of bronze
(approximately 5,300 pounds). The silver came from the half-shekel census tax
of 603,550 men — connecting the tabernacle's foundation literally to the
redemption price of every Israelite male. God's dwelling rests on the
foundation of His people's redemption.

Section Summaries:
vv.1–7 — The Bronze Altar of Burnt Offering constructed.
v.8 — The Bronze Laver made from women's mirrors.
vv.9–20 — The Courtyard: hangings, pillars, bases, and gate screen.
vv.21–23 — Record-keeping; Bezalel and Oholiab named.

------------------------------------

 sacrifice; the first thing encountered in worship.

 of the ministering women." (ESV) — Mirrors surrendered for cleansing;
 vanity transformed into holiness.

v.21 — "These are the records of the tabernacle, the tabernacle of the
 testimony, as they were recorded at the commandment of Moses."
 (ESV) — Accountability and transparency in God's work.

 and 730 shekels." (ESV) — The staggering cost of God's dwelling
 among His people.

v.26 — "A beka a head (that is, half a shekel)... for 603,550 men." (ESV)
 — Every man's redemption price built into the tabernacle's foundation.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.3 — KJV: "fleshhooks" / ESV: "forks" — Hebrew "mizlagot" — forks or
 flesh-hooks used to handle sacrificial meat on the altar. ESV
 modernizes the term.

v.4 — KJV: "brasen grate of network" / ESV: "grating, a network of bronze"
 — Hebrew "mikbar maʻaseh reshet" — a grating of net-work. A bronze
 mesh placed inside the altar to hold the burning sacrifice.

v.8 — KJV: "lookingglasses" / ESV: "mirrors" — Hebrew "marʼot" — mirrors
 (polished bronze). KJV "looking-glasses" reflects the older English
 term for mirrors. These were the women's personal possessions.

v.8 — KJV: "women assembling" / ESV: "ministering women who ministered" —
 Hebrew "hatsoveʼot ʼasher tsaveʼu" — the women who served/
 assembled. These women had a recognized role of service at the
 tabernacle entrance.

v.21 — KJV: "the sum of the tabernacle" / ESV: "the records of the
 tabernacle" — Hebrew "pequdey hamishkan" — the accounts/records/
 inventory of the tabernacle. A formal reckoning of materials used.

v.26 — KJV: "bekah" / ESV: "beka" — Hebrew "beqaʻ" — a half-shekel
 (approximately 0.2 ounces of silver). The redemption price per
 person, establishing each Israelite's stake in the tabernacle.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Bronze Altar — The altar of burnt offering in the outer courtyard. Five
 cubits square, three cubits high. The place where animal sacrifices
 were burned. A type of the cross.

Laver — A bronze basin for priestly washing, made from women's mirrors.
 Priests washed hands and feet before serving. A type of
 sanctification through God's Word.

Courtyard — The enclosed area surrounding the tabernacle, 100 x 50 cubits,
 bounded by linen hangings on pillars. The public area of worship.

Bekah/Half-shekel — The census tax paid by every male twenty years and
 older. A redemption price that funded the silver bases of the
 tabernacle — literally building God's house on redemption.

Talent — Hebrew "kikkar" — approximately 75 pounds (34 kg). The largest
 unit of weight measurement in ancient Israel.

Ithamar — Aaron's youngest son, placed in charge of the Levites' service
 and the tabernacle inventory. Responsible for accountability.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. The Bronze Altar was the first thing encountered (v.1). There is no
 approach to God without sacrifice. How does the cross of Christ stand at
 the entrance of your relationship with God? Do you try to bypass it?

2. The women gave their mirrors to make the laver (v.8). They surrendered
 instruments of self-focus to create an instrument of cleansing. What
 "mirrors" (sources of vanity or self-absorption) might God be asking you
 to surrender for His purposes?

3. The materials inventory (vv.21-31) shows careful accounting. How do you
 practice transparency and accountability with the resources God has
 entrusted to you — time, money, influence?

4. Every Israelite male contributed a half-shekel (v.26) — rich and poor
 alike, the same amount. What does this teach about equal standing before
 God? How does the gospel level all distinctions?

5. The silver bases (foundation) came from the redemption money (v.27).
 God's dwelling literally rests on redemption. How is your life built on
 the foundation of Christ's redemptive work?
