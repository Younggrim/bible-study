Exodus - Chapter 6
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God reaffirms His covenant promises to Moses and commands him to return to Pharaoh; the genealogy of Moses and Aaron is recorded.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 6 is God's answer to Moses' complaint in 5:22-23.
God responds not with rebuke but with revelation — a deeper unveiling of His
name, His covenant, and His plan. The "I AM the LORD" declaration (v.2) is
followed by seven "I will" promises (vv.6-8) — God's comprehensive plan of
redemption. Yet Israel cannot hear it "for anguish of spirit, and for cruel
bondage" (v.9) — suffering can deafen us to God's promises. The genealogy
(vv.14-25) establishes Moses and Aaron's credentials within the tribe of
Levi. The chapter ends where it began: Moses' objection about his
"uncircumcised lips" (v.12, 30).

God's Self-Revelation (vv.1-8): God answers Moses' complaint: "Now shalt
thou see what I will do" (v.1). He reveals Himself: "I am the LORD (YHWH)"
(v.2). He appeared to the patriarchs as "God Almighty" (El Shaddai) but now
reveals the fuller meaning of YHWH (v.3). He has heard Israel's groaning and
remembered His covenant (v.5). Then the seven "I will" promises:
  1. "I will bring you out" (v.6)
  2. "I will rid you out of their bondage" (v.6)
  3. "I will redeem you" (v.6)
  4. "I will take you to me for a people" (v.7)
  5. "I will be to you a God" (v.7)
  6. "I will bring you in unto the land" (v.8)
  7. "I will give it you for an heritage" (v.8)

Israel's Deafness (v.9): Moses delivers God's message, "but they hearkened
not unto Moses for anguish of spirit, and for cruel bondage." Suffering had
made them unable to receive hope.

Moses' Renewed Objection (vv.10-13): God commands Moses to go to Pharaoh
again (v.11). Moses objects: "The children of Israel have not hearkened unto
me; how then shall Pharaoh hear me, who am of uncircumcised lips?" (v.12).

The Genealogy (vv.14-27): A selective genealogy tracing Reuben, Simeon, and
especially Levi — establishing Moses and Aaron's lineage. Key names: Amram
and Jochebed (Moses' parents, v.20), Aaron's wife Elisheba (v.23), Aaron's
sons including Nadab, Abihu, Eleazar, and Ithamar (v.23), and Phinehas
(v.25). The genealogy authenticates Moses and Aaron as God's chosen
instruments.

Renewed Commission (vv.28-30): The narrative resumes: God commands Moses to
speak to Pharaoh (v.29). Moses repeats his objection: "I am of uncircumcised
lips" (v.30) — setting up God's answer in chapter 7.

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Egypt: Moses and Aaron operate in Pharaoh's court and among the Israelites in Goshen.
- The genealogy (vv.14-25) traces the family back to Israel's entry into Egypt, connecting this generation to the land of promise given to Abraham.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.2  — "I am the LORD." (ESV) — God's self-declaration — the foundation of
        everything that follows.

v.3  — "I appeared to Abraham, to Isaac, and to Jacob, as God Almighty,
        but by my name the LORD I did not make myself known to them." (ESV)
        — A deeper revelation of God's character to this generation.

v.6  — "I will bring you out... I will deliver you... I will redeem you
        with an outstretched arm." (ESV) — God's comprehensive redemption.

v.7  — "I will take you to be my people, and I will be your God." (ESV) —
        The covenant formula: relationship is the goal of redemption.

v.9  — "They did not listen to Moses, because of their broken spirit and
        harsh slavery." (ESV) — Suffering can deafen us to God's promises.

  as if it were already done. I will bring you out — deliverance. I will rid
  you — freedom. I will redeem you — purchase. I will take you — relationship.
  I will be your God — identity. I will bring you in — inheritance. I will
  give it you — possession. From bondage to inheritance in seven divine
  declarations. And not one 'you must' among them. This is grace: God does
  it all. Our part? Believe it. Receive it. Rest in it."

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.3  — KJV: "by my name JEHOVAH was I not known to them" / ESV: "by my
       name the LORD I did not make myself known to them" — Hebrew "ushmi
       YHWH lo nodati lahem." The ESV's "make myself known" captures the
       reflexive Hebrew verb better. This does not mean the patriarchs never
       heard the name YHWH (Genesis 15:7 uses it), but that they did not
       EXPERIENCE its full meaning — God as covenant-keeper who acts in
       history to redeem.

v.6  — KJV: "redeem" / ESV: "redeem" — Hebrew "ga'al" — to redeem as a
       kinsman-redeemer. Both translations identical. God acts as Israel's
       go'el — their nearest relative who pays the price to set them free.

v.6  — KJV: "stretched out arm" / ESV: "outstretched arm" — Hebrew "zeroa
       netuyah" — an extended, powerful arm. Both translations equivalent.
       Military imagery: God as warrior fighting for His people.

v.9  — KJV: "anguish of spirit" / ESV: "broken spirit" — Hebrew "qotser
       ruach" — shortness of spirit/breath. The KJV's "anguish" and the
       ESV's "broken spirit" both capture the idea, though the Hebrew is
       literally "shortness of breath" — spiritual suffocation from
       oppression.

v.12 — KJV: "uncircumcised lips" / ESV: "uncircumcised lips" — Hebrew
       "aral sefatayim" — lips that are blocked, closed, unfit. Both
       translations identical. A metaphor: as uncircumcised flesh is unfit
       for covenant, so Moses' lips feel unfit for God's message.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
- v.3 — ASV: "by my name Jehovah I was not known to them" / WEB: "by my name Yahweh I was not known to them" — both make God's personal name explicit where ESV/KJV hide it behind "the LORD." This verse's theology is much clearer when the actual name is visible.
- v.9 — NET: "because of their discouragement and hard labor" — interpretive paraphrase of Hebrew "miqotser ruach" (shortness of spirit); ASV/WEB retain "anguish of spirit" — more literal and emotionally vivid.
- v.12 — ASV/WEB: "uncircumcised lips" — preserves the striking Hebrew metaphor "aral sefatayim"; NET paraphrases to "I speak with difficulty," losing the theological resonance of "uncircumcised" as a barrier metaphor.
- v.26 — ASV: "according to their hosts" / WEB: "according to their armies" / NET: "by their regiments" — three different renderings of Hebrew "tsiv'otam." NET's "regiments" is uniquely military; WEB's "armies" is similar; ASV's "hosts" preserves the word used in "LORD of hosts."
- v.8 — NET: "I will give it to you as a possession" — renders Hebrew "morasha" (heritage/inheritance) as "possession," a more legal/concrete term; ASV/WEB retain "heritage," which carries covenantal weight.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
YHWH (Jehovah/Yahweh) — God's covenant name, now being DEMONSTRATED through
               redemptive action. The patriarchs knew the name; this
               generation will experience its power.

El Shaddai      — "God Almighty" — the name by which God related to the
               patriarchs. Emphasizes power and provision. YHWH adds
               covenant faithfulness and redemptive action.

Seven "I wills" — God's comprehensive redemption plan: deliverance, freedom,
               redemption, relationship, identity, inheritance, possession.
               All initiated by God, not earned by Israel.

Go'el (Redeemer) — Kinsman-redeemer. God acts as Israel's nearest relative,
               paying the price for their freedom. Fulfilled ultimately in
               Christ.

"Anguish of spirit" — Literally "shortness of breath." Suffering so intense
               it prevents receiving hope. Spiritual suffocation.

"Uncircumcised lips" — Moses' metaphor for feeling unfit to speak for God.
               Inadequacy language — answered by God's empowerment.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. The seven "I wills" (vv.6-8): God's redemption is comprehensive. He
   doesn't just free you FROM something — He brings you INTO relationship,
   identity, and inheritance. Which of these seven promises do you need to
   hear today?

2. "They hearkened not... for anguish of spirit" (v.9). Suffering can make
   us deaf to God's promises. If you're in deep pain right now, you may not
   be able to "hear" hope. That's okay. God's promises remain true whether
   you can feel them or not.

3. "I am the LORD" — repeated three times (vv.2, 6, 8). God's identity is
   the foundation of His promises. He can do what He says because He IS who
   He says. Do you know Him — not just His name but His character?

4. Moses objects again (v.12) — even after God's magnificent promises. We
   can hear God's word and still feel inadequate. God doesn't rebuke Moses
   — He provides Aaron. God meets us in our weakness.

5. The genealogy (vv.14-25) grounds the story in real history — real
   families, real people. God works through ordinary lineages. Your family
   history, however messy, is part of God's story.
