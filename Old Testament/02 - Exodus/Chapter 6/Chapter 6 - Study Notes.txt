Exodus - Chapter 6
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God reaffirms His covenant promises to Moses and commands him to return to Pharaoh; the genealogy of Moses and Aaron is recorded.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 6 is God's answer to Moses' complaint in 5:22-23.
God responds not with rebuke but with revelation — a deeper unveiling of His
name, His covenant, and His plan. The "I AM the LORD" declaration (v.2) is
followed by seven "I will" promises (vv.6-8) — God's comprehensive plan of
redemption. Yet Israel cannot hear it "for anguish of spirit, and for cruel
bondage" (v.9) — suffering can deafen us to God's promises. The genealogy
(vv.14-25) establishes Moses and Aaron's credentials within the tribe of
Levi. The chapter ends where it began: Moses' objection about his
"uncircumcised lips" (v.12, 30).

God's Self-Revelation (vv.1-8): God answers Moses' complaint: "Now shalt
thou see what I will do" (v.1). He reveals Himself: "I am the LORD (YHWH)"
(v.2). He appeared to the patriarchs as "God Almighty" (El Shaddai) but now
reveals the fuller meaning of YHWH (v.3). He has heard Israel's groaning and
remembered His covenant (v.5). Then the seven "I will" promises:
  1. "I will bring you out" (v.6)
  2. "I will rid you out of their bondage" (v.6)
  3. "I will redeem you" (v.6)
  4. "I will take you to me for a people" (v.7)
  5. "I will be to you a God" (v.7)
  6. "I will bring you in unto the land" (v.8)
  7. "I will give it you for an heritage" (v.8)

Israel's Deafness (v.9): Moses delivers God's message, "but they hearkened
not unto Moses for anguish of spirit, and for cruel bondage." Suffering had
made them unable to receive hope.

Moses' Renewed Objection (vv.10-13): God commands Moses to go to Pharaoh
again (v.11). Moses objects: "The children of Israel have not hearkened unto
me; how then shall Pharaoh hear me, who am of uncircumcised lips?" (v.12).

The Genealogy (vv.14-27): A selective genealogy tracing Reuben, Simeon, and
especially Levi — establishing Moses and Aaron's lineage. Key names: Amram
and Jochebed (Moses' parents, v.20), Aaron's wife Elisheba (v.23), Aaron's
sons including Nadab, Abihu, Eleazar, and Ithamar (v.23), and Phinehas
(v.25). The genealogy authenticates Moses and Aaron as God's chosen
instruments.

Renewed Commission (vv.28-30): The narrative resumes: God commands Moses to
speak to Pharaoh (v.29). Moses repeats his objection: "I am of uncircumcised
lips" (v.30) — setting up God's answer in chapter 7.

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Egypt: Moses and Aaron operate in Pharaoh's court and among the Israelites in Goshen.
- The genealogy (vv.14-25) traces the family back to Israel's entry into Egypt, connecting this generation to the land of promise given to Abraham.

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------
Enduring Word (David Guzik):
  https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/exodus-6/
  Guzik emphasizes the seven "I will" promises as a complete picture of
  redemption, the progressive revelation of God's name, and the tragedy of
  Israel's inability to hear due to suffering.

Charles Spurgeon:
  Sermon: "The Seven I Wills" on vv.6-8 (MTP Vol. 28, No. 1672)
  "Seven times God says 'I WILL' — and every 'I will' of God is as certain
  as if it were already done. I will bring you out — deliverance. I will rid
  you — freedom. I will redeem you — purchase. I will take you — relationship.
  I will be your God — identity. I will bring you in — inheritance. I will
  give it you — possession. From bondage to inheritance in seven divine
  declarations. And not one 'you must' among them. This is grace: God does
  it all. Our part? Believe it. Receive it. Rest in it."

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. The seven "I wills" (vv.6-8): God's redemption is comprehensive. He
   doesn't just free you FROM something — He brings you INTO relationship,
   identity, and inheritance. Which of these seven promises do you need to
   hear today?

2. "They hearkened not... for anguish of spirit" (v.9). Suffering can make
   us deaf to God's promises. If you're in deep pain right now, you may not
   be able to "hear" hope. That's okay. God's promises remain true whether
   you can feel them or not.

3. "I am the LORD" — repeated three times (vv.2, 6, 8). God's identity is
   the foundation of His promises. He can do what He says because He IS who
   He says. Do you know Him — not just His name but His character?

4. Moses objects again (v.12) — even after God's magnificent promises. We
   can hear God's word and still feel inadequate. God doesn't rebuke Moses
   — He provides Aaron. God meets us in our weakness.

5. The genealogy (vv.14-25) grounds the story in real history — real
   families, real people. God works through ordinary lineages. Your family
   history, however messy, is part of God's story.
