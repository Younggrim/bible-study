Exodus - Chapter 25
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God instructs Moses to receive offerings and build the Tabernacle — the ark of the covenant, the table of showbread, and the golden lampstand.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 25 begins the Tabernacle instructions (chapters
25-31) — God's blueprint for His dwelling place among Israel. This is given
during Moses' forty days on Sinai. The Tabernacle is not a human invention
but a divine design: "according to all that I shew thee, after the pattern"
(v.9). God's purpose: "that I may dwell among them" (v.8) — the holy God
living in the midst of sinful people. The chapter covers: (1) The offering
of materials (vv.1-9); (2) The Ark of the Covenant (vv.10-22); (3) The Table
of Showbread (vv.23-30); (4) The Golden Lampstand/Menorah (vv.31-40). Every
item points to Christ: the Ark (God's throne of grace), the Table (bread of
life), the Lampstand (light of the world).

The Offering (vv.1-9): God calls for a freewill offering — "of every man
that giveth it willingly with his heart" (v.2). Materials: gold, silver,
bronze, colored yarns, fine linen, animal skins, acacia wood, oil, spices,
precious stones (vv.3-7). Purpose: "let them make me a sanctuary; that I may
dwell among them" (v.8). Instruction: "according to... the pattern" (v.9) —
the earthly Tabernacle copies a heavenly reality (Hebrews 8:5).

The Ark of the Covenant (vv.10-22): Acacia wood overlaid with pure gold,
inside and out (vv.10-11). Gold rings and poles for carrying (vv.12-15) —
poles never removed. Contains "the testimony" — the stone tablets (v.16).
The mercy seat: pure gold lid with two cherubim, wings spread, facing each
other and looking down at the mercy seat (vv.17-20). God's promise: "There
I will meet with thee, and I will commune with thee from above the mercy
seat" (v.22). The Ark is God's throne on earth.

The Table of Showbread (vv.23-30): Acacia wood overlaid with gold (vv.23-28).
Gold dishes and utensils (v.29). "Thou shalt set upon the table shewbread
before me alway" (v.30) — twelve loaves representing the twelve tribes,
always in God's presence.

The Golden Lampstand (vv.31-40): Pure gold, hammered from a single piece
(v.31). Central shaft with six branches — three on each side (v.32). Almond
blossom decorations (vv.33-34). Seven lamps giving light (v.37). Made from
one talent of pure gold (v.39). Final instruction: "make them after their
pattern, which was shewed thee in the mount" (v.40).

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Israel remains encamped at Mount Sinai. Moses receives the Tabernacle instructions on the mountain.
- The Tabernacle is designed as a portable sanctuary — God's dwelling place to travel with Israel through the wilderness.

------------------------------------

        contribution for me." (ESV) — Worship requires a willing heart.

        (ESV) — God's desire: to live among His people.

v.9  — "Exactly as I show you concerning the pattern." (ESV) — The
        Tabernacle copies a heavenly reality.

v.22 — "There I will meet with you, and... I will speak with you from above
        the mercy seat." (ESV) — God meets us at the place of mercy.

        shown you on the mountain." (ESV) — God's design, not human invention.

Charles Spurgeon:
  Sermon: "The Mercy Seat" on v.22 (MTP Vol. 7, No. 390)
  "There I will MEET with thee — God meets man at the mercy seat. Not at the
  law (which is INSIDE the ark, under the mercy seat). Not at the cherubim
  (which represent judgment). But at the MERCY SEAT — the golden lid that
  covers the law and receives the blood. This is where God and man meet:
  where mercy covers law, where blood satisfies justice, where grace
  triumphs over judgment. Christian, you do not approach God at the place of
  law — you approach at the place of mercy. The blood is between you and the
  commandments you've broken. Come boldly to the throne of GRACE."

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1  — Hebrew "YHWH" — the divine name.
       KJV: "the LORD spake unto Moses"
       ESV: "the LORD spoke to Moses"
       ASV: "Jehovah spake unto Moses"
       NET: "The Lord spoke to Moses"
       WEB: "Yahweh spoke to Moses"
       Analysis: The divine name (Hebrew YHWH) is rendered three different
       ways. ASV uses the traditional English "Jehovah," WEB uses the
       scholarly reconstruction "Yahweh," and NET/KJV/ESV use "the LORD"/
       "the Lord." The ASV and WEB preserve the personal name; NET/KJV/ESV
       follow the Jewish practice of not speaking the name aloud.

v.2  — Hebrew "terumah" — a heave offering, a contribution lifted up.
       KJV: "offering"
       ESV: "contribution"
       ASV: "offering"
       NET: "contribution"
       WEB: "offering"
       Analysis: The KJV/ASV/WEB "offering" carries worship connotations;
       ESV/NET "contribution" emphasizes practical giving. The Hebrew
       encompasses both: a gift lifted up to God from a willing heart.

v.3  — Hebrew "nechoshet" — bronze (a copper-tin alloy).
       KJV: "brass"
       ESV: "bronze"
       ASV: "brass"
       NET: "bronze"
       WEB: "brass"
       Analysis: The KJV/ASV/WEB consistently use "brass" but the metal is
       actually bronze. Brass (copper-zinc) was not common in the ancient
       Near East. ESV/NET correctly render it "bronze."

v.5  — Hebrew "orot techashim" — skins of tachash (uncertain animal).
       KJV: "badgers' skins"
       ESV: "goatskins"
       ASV: "sealskins"
       NET: "fine leather"
       WEB: "sea cow hides"
       Analysis: Each translation takes a different approach to this
       uncertain term. ASV identifies seals, WEB identifies dugong/sea cow,
       KJV suggests badger, ESV offers "goatskins," while NET abandons
       animal identification entirely with "fine leather." The diversity
       highlights genuine uncertainty about the Hebrew "tachash."

v.5  — Hebrew "atzei shittim" — acacia wood.
       KJV: "shittim wood"
       ESV: "acacia wood"
       ASV: "acacia wood"
       NET: "acacia wood"
       WEB: "acacia wood"
       Analysis: The KJV transliterates the Hebrew; all modern translations
       translate it correctly as "acacia" — a hard, durable desert wood
       resistant to insects.

v.17 — Hebrew "kapporet" — from "kaphar" (to cover, to atone).
       KJV: "mercy seat"
       ESV: "mercy seat"
       ASV: "mercy-seat"
       NET: "atonement lid"
       WEB: "mercy seat"
       Analysis: NET uniquely renders this as "atonement lid," emphasizing
       both the physical function (a lid) and theological meaning (atonement).
       ASV/WEB/KJV/ESV retain the traditional "mercy seat" (from Tyndale/
       Luther). NET's rendering makes the theological connection explicit but
       loses the traditional resonance with Hebrews 4:16's "throne of grace."

v.22 — Hebrew "dabar" — to speak, to commune, to converse.
       KJV: "I will commune with thee"
       ESV: "I will speak with you"
       ASV: "I will commune with thee"
       NET: "I will speak with you"
       WEB: "I will tell you from above the mercy seat"
       Analysis: ASV's "commune" captures the relational, conversational
       nature of God speaking from the mercy seat — dialogue, not merely
       issuing orders. NET/ESV use "speak." WEB uses "tell." ASV's rendering
       carries richer covenantal weight.

v.29 — Hebrew utensils for the table of showbread.
       KJV: "dishes... spoons... covers... bowls"
       ESV: "plates... dishes... flagons... bowls"
       ASV: "dishes... spoons... flagons... bowls"
       NET: "its plates, its ladles, its pitchers, and its bowls, to be used in pouring out offerings"
       WEB: "dishes... spoons... ladles... bowls"
       Analysis: NET adds the interpretive phrase "to be used in pouring out
       offerings," clarifying the purpose of these utensils. ASV/WEB simply
       list the items. NET's addition is not in the Hebrew but reflects
       scholarly understanding of the ritual function.

v.30 — Hebrew "lechem panim" — bread of the face/presence.
       KJV: "shewbread"
       ESV: "bread of the Presence"
       ASV: "showbread"
       NET: "Bread of the Presence"
       WEB: "bread of the presence"
       Analysis: KJV/ASV follow tradition with "shewbread/showbread" (bread
       on display). ESV/WEB/NET translate the Hebrew literally — "bread of
       the presence/face." NET capitalizes it as a proper title. The literal
       rendering makes the theological point clearer: bread always before
       God's face.

v.31 — Hebrew "menorah" — a lampstand.
       KJV: "candlestick"
       ESV: "lampstand"
       ASV: "candlestick"
       NET: "lampstand"
       WEB: "lamp stand"
       Analysis: KJV/ASV use the archaic "candlestick" (it held oil lamps,
       not candles). ESV/NET/WEB correctly render it "lampstand"/"lamp stand."
       NET further uses "hammered metal" instead of ASV's "beaten work" — a
       more accessible description of the metalworking technique.

v.33 — Hebrew "kaphtor" — a knob, bulb, or calyx.
       KJV: "knops"
       ESV: "calyxes"
       ASV: "buds"
       NET: "buds"
       WEB: "buds"
       Analysis: The ESV's botanical term "calyx" is precise; KJV's "knop"
       (knob) is archaic. ASV/NET/WEB use "buds" — capturing the almond
       blossom imagery.

v.39 — Hebrew "kikkar zahav tahor" — a talent of pure gold.
       KJV: "a talent of pure gold"
       ESV: "a talent of pure gold"
       ASV: "a talent of pure gold"
       NET: "About 75 pounds of pure gold"
       WEB: "a talent of pure gold"
       Analysis: NET converts the ancient measurement into modern equivalents
       throughout the chapter (also v.10: "45 inches" instead of "two cubits
       and a half"). This makes the text immediately accessible but loses the
       original terminology. ASV/WEB/KJV retain "talent" and "cubits,"
       preserving the ancient measurement system.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Tabernacle     — Hebrew "mishkan" — dwelling place. God's portable sanctuary
               in the wilderness. A type of Christ: "The Word... dwelt
               (tabernacled) among us" (John 1:14).

Ark of the Covenant — The most sacred object: God's throne on earth.
               Contains the tablets, Aaron's rod, and a pot of manna
               (Hebrews 9:4). Represents God's presence and His covenant.

Mercy Seat (Kapporet) — The gold lid of the Ark where blood was sprinkled.
               God meets man here — where mercy covers law. A type of
               Christ as our propitiation (Romans 3:25).

Table of Showbread — Twelve loaves always before God. Represents God's
               provision and Israel's constant presence before Him. Christ
               is the bread of life (John 6:35).

Menorah (Lampstand) — Seven-branched golden lampstand. The only light source
               in the Holy Place. Christ is the light of the world (John
               8:12). The church is also called lampstands (Revelation 1).

"The pattern" — The heavenly original that the earthly Tabernacle copies
               (Hebrews 8:5). God's design is precise — not left to human
               creativity.

Acacia wood    — Hard, durable desert wood. Represents Christ's humanity
               (wood) overlaid with deity (gold). Incorruptible — acacia
               resists decay.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "That I may dwell among them" (v.8). God's deepest desire is to live
   WITH His people. The Tabernacle, the Temple, the Incarnation, the
   indwelling Spirit — all express the same longing. Do you desire His
   presence as much as He desires yours?

2. "Of every man that giveth it willingly with his heart" (v.2). God wants
   willing givers, not reluctant ones. Is your giving — of time, money,
   talent — from a willing heart or from obligation?

3. "There I will meet with thee... from above the mercy seat" (v.22). God
   meets you at the place of MERCY — not the place of performance. You
   don't have to be perfect to approach Him. Come to the mercy seat.

4. "Make them after their pattern" (v.40). God's design is specific. He
   doesn't say "build whatever you think is best." Worship and service must
   follow God's pattern, not human preference.

5. Every item points to Christ: the Ark (God's presence), the mercy seat
   (propitiation), the bread (sustenance), the lampstand (light). Jesus is
   the fulfillment of every shadow. Do you see Him in the details?
