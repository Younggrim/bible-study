Exodus - Chapter 26
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God gives detailed instructions for the Tabernacle's curtains, frames, and veil — the dwelling place where He will meet with His people.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 26 provides the detailed construction plan for
the Tabernacle structure itself — its curtains, coverings, framework, and
the all-important veil that separates the Holy Place from the Most Holy Place.
The Tabernacle was approximately 45 feet long, 15 feet wide, and 15 feet
high — a portable tent-sanctuary. Four layers of covering protected it: (1)
fine linen with cherubim (beauty, seen only from inside); (2) goats' hair
(protection); (3) rams' skins dyed red (sacrifice); (4) outer skins
(weather protection). The veil (v.31) is theologically crucial — it
separates sinful humanity from God's holy presence. It was torn from top to
bottom at Christ's death (Matthew 27:51).

The Inner Curtains (vv.1-6): Ten curtains of fine linen — blue, purple,
scarlet — with cherubim woven in (v.1). Each 28 cubits by 4 cubits (v.2).
Joined in two sets of five, connected by fifty gold clasps (vv.3-6). "It
shall be one tabernacle" (v.6) — unity from many parts.

The Goats' Hair Covering (vv.7-13): Eleven curtains of goats' hair — the
tent over the tabernacle (v.7). Each 30 cubits by 4 cubits (v.8). Joined
by fifty bronze clasps (v.11). Extra length hangs over the back and sides
for protection (vv.12-13).

The Outer Coverings (v.14): Rams' skins dyed red over the goats' hair.
Outer skins (badgers'/goatskins) on top — weather protection.

The Framework (vv.15-30): Acacia wood frames, each 10 cubits tall, 1.5
cubits wide (vv.15-16). Twenty frames on the south, twenty on the north,
eight on the west (vv.18-25). Each frame set in two silver sockets/bases
(v.19). Five acacia bars overlaid with gold hold the frames together
(vv.26-29). The middle bar runs the full length (v.28). "Rear up the
tabernacle according to the fashion... shewed thee in the mount" (v.30).

The Veil (vv.31-35): Blue, purple, scarlet, fine linen — with cherubim
(v.31). Hung on four gold-overlaid pillars with silver bases (v.32). "The
vail shall divide unto you between the holy place and the most holy" (v.33).
The Ark and mercy seat go behind the veil (v.34). The table on the north
side, the lampstand on the south (v.35).

The Entrance Screen (vv.36-37): Blue, purple, scarlet, fine linen —
embroidered (v.36). Five pillars of acacia overlaid with gold, on bronze
bases (v.37).

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Israel remains encamped at Mount Sinai. God gives Moses the Tabernacle curtain instructions on the mountain.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "Moreover, you shall make the tabernacle with ten curtains of fine
        twined linen and blue and purple and scarlet yarns; you shall make
        them with cherubim." (ESV) — Beauty and holiness in God's dwelling.

v.6  — "It shall be a single tabernacle." (ESV) — Unity from many parts.

v.30 — "Then you shall erect the tabernacle according to the plan for it
        that you were shown on the mountain." (ESV) — God's pattern, not
        human design.

v.33 — "The veil shall separate for you the Holy Place from the Most Holy."
        (ESV) — Separation between God and man because of sin.

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------
Enduring Word (David Guzik):
  https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/exodus-26/
  Guzik covers the symbolism of each layer (beauty within, plainness
  without — like Christ), the veil as separation torn at the cross, and
  the silver bases as redemption (silver = redemption price).

Charles Spurgeon:
  Sermon: "The Rent Veil" on v.33 (MTP Vol. 34, No. 2015)
  "The vail shall divide — for centuries, this veil stood between God and
  man. Thick, heavy, embroidered with cherubim — guardians barring the way.
  No one passed through except the high priest, once a year, with blood.
  But when Christ died — 'the veil of the temple was rent in twain from the
  TOP to the bottom.' From the TOP — God tore it, not man. From heaven
  downward. God Himself opened the way. The barrier is gone. The separation
  is ended. 'Having boldness to enter into the holiest by the blood of
  Jesus, by a new and living way... through the veil, that is to say, his
  flesh' (Hebrews 10:19-20). Christ's torn flesh IS the torn veil. Enter
  boldly."

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1  — Hebrew "keruvim ma'aseh choshev" — cherubim, work of a skilled craftsman.
       KJV: "cherubims of cunning work"
       ESV: "cherubim skillfully worked into them"
       ASV: "the work of the skilful workman"
       NET: "the work of an artistic designer"
       WEB: "The work of the skillful workman"
       Analysis: KJV's "cunning work" means skillful/artistic work (archaic
       English). ESV "skillfully worked" is the modern equivalent. NET uses
       "artistic designer" — an interpretive rendering emphasizing creative
       design rather than mere craftsmanship. The cherubim were woven INTO
       the fabric — not appliquéd on top.

v.2  — Hebrew "esrim ushmone ammah... arba ammot" — twenty-eight cubits...
       four cubits.
       KJV: "eight and twenty cubits... four cubits"
       ESV: "twenty-eight cubits... four cubits"
       ASV: "eight and twenty cubits... four cubits"
       NET: "42 feet... 6 feet"
       WEB: "twenty-eight cubits... four cubits"
       Analysis: NET consistently converts ancient measurements to modern
       feet/inches throughout the chapter. While accessible, this removes
       readers from the original measurement system. ASV/WEB/KJV retain
       cubits, preserving the biblical terminology that connects to other
       Tabernacle/Temple descriptions.

v.6  — Hebrew "qerasim... echad" — clasps... one.
       KJV: "taches... it shall be one tabernacle"
       ESV: "clasps... it shall be a single tabernacle"
       ASV: "clasps of gold... one tabernacle"
       NET: "50 gold clasps... so that the tabernacle is a unit"
       WEB: "fifty clasps of gold... one tabernacle"
       Analysis: All agree on "clasps" (vs. KJV's archaic "taches"). NET
       interprets the Hebrew "echad" (one) as "a unit," making the unity
       concept explicit. The gold clasps join the inner curtains into one
       unified structure — many parts forming a single whole.

v.14 — Hebrew "orot techashim" — skins of tachash (uncertain animal).
       KJV: "badgers' skins"
       ESV: "goatskins"
       ASV: "sealskins"
       NET: "fine leather"
       WEB: "sea cow hides"
       Analysis: Same divergence as chapter 25:5. The outer covering of the
       Tabernacle used this uncertain material. ASV identifies seals, WEB
       identifies sea cows (dugongs), NET avoids animal identification
       entirely. The theological point remains: the outer covering was plain
       and utilitarian, hiding the beauty within.

v.15 — Hebrew "qerashim" — frames/boards.
       KJV: "boards"
       ESV: "upright frames"
       ASV: "boards"
       NET: "frames"
       WEB: "boards"
       Analysis: KJV/ASV/WEB "boards" suggests solid planks; ESV/NET "frames"
       suggests open frameworks. Archaeological evidence and the Hebrew
       suggest frames rather than solid boards — allowing the inner curtains
       to be visible from inside. NET also converts measurements: "15 feet
       long" instead of cubits (v.16).

v.19 — Hebrew "adnei kesef" — bases/sockets of silver.
       KJV: "sockets of silver"
       ESV: "bases of silver"
       ASV: "sockets of silver"
       NET: "silver bases"
       WEB: "sockets of silver"
       Analysis: KJV/ASV/WEB "sockets" and ESV/NET "bases" both describe the
       silver foundation blocks into which the frames were set. Each weighed
       about 75 pounds (a talent of silver). Silver represents redemption —
       the entire structure rests on redemption.

v.31 — Hebrew "parokhet" — a dividing curtain, a screen of separation.
       KJV: "vail"
       ESV: "veil"
       ASV: "veil"
       NET: "a special curtain"
       WEB: "veil"
       Analysis: KJV uses the archaic spelling "vail"; ESV/ASV/WEB use
       "veil." NET uses "a special curtain," which is technically accurate
       but obscures the traditional connection to Matthew 27:51 — the veil
       torn at Christ's death. The theologically loaded term "veil" is
       preferred for preserving that connection.

v.33 — Hebrew "qodesh haqqodashim" — the Holy of Holies.
       KJV: "the holy place and the most holy"
       ESV: "the Holy Place from the Most Holy"
       ASV: "the holy place and the most holy"
       NET: "the Holy Place and the Most Holy Place"
       WEB: "the holy place from the most holy"
       Analysis: NET capitalizes both as proper names and adds "Place" for
       clarity. All convey the same meaning, but NET's formal capitalization
       treats these as technical architectural terms rather than descriptive
       phrases. The superlative: the holiest place in the holiest structure.

v.34 — Hebrew "kapporet" — the mercy seat / atonement lid.
       KJV: "mercy seat upon the ark of the testimony in the most holy place"
       ESV: "the mercy seat on the ark of the testimony in the Most Holy Place"
       ASV: "the mercy-seat upon the ark of the testimony in the most holy place"
       NET: "the atonement lid on the ark of the testimony in the Most Holy Place"
       WEB: "the mercy seat on the ark of the testimony in the most holy place"
       Analysis: NET's "atonement lid" (vs. "mercy seat") again emphasizes
       theology over tradition. Combined with "Most Holy Place," NET reads
       more like a theological commentary than a translation. ASV/WEB/KJV
       retain the traditional "mercy seat."

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
The Veil (Parokhet) — The curtain separating the Holy Place from the Most
               Holy Place. Represents the barrier between sinful humanity
               and holy God. Torn at Christ's death — access opened forever.

Four coverings — (1) Fine linen with cherubim (inner beauty/holiness);
               (2) Goats' hair (protection/atonement); (3) Rams' skins dyed
               red (sacrifice/blood); (4) Outer skins (plain exterior).
               Like Christ: glory within, humble appearance without.

Silver bases   — The foundation of the Tabernacle. Silver = the redemption
               price (Exodus 30:11-16). The entire structure rests on
               redemption — everything is built on the foundation of grace.

Acacia wood overlaid with gold — Wood (humanity) covered with gold (deity).
               A picture of Christ: fully human, fully divine.

"One tabernacle" — Unity from many parts. The curtains, clasps, frames, and
               bars form a single structure. A picture of the church: many
               members, one body (1 Corinthians 12:12).

Blue, purple, scarlet — Colors of royalty and priesthood. Blue (heavenly),
               purple (royal), scarlet (sacrifice/blood). Together they
               represent Christ: heavenly King who shed His blood.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "The vail shall divide" (v.33). Before Christ, a thick curtain separated
   humanity from God. At the cross, it was torn. You now have direct access
   to God's presence — through Christ's torn flesh. Do you USE that access?
   Or do you live as if the veil is still there?

2. The outer covering was plain; the beauty was inside (v.14 vs. v.1).
   Christ had "no beauty that we should desire him" (Isaiah 53:2) outwardly
   — but inwardly, He was glorious. Don't judge by appearances. The
   greatest beauty is often hidden from casual observers.

3. "It shall be one tabernacle" (v.6). Many parts, one structure. The church
   is the same: many members, one body. Are you contributing to unity or
   division?

4. The entire structure rests on silver bases — redemption (v.19). Everything
   in your spiritual life rests on the foundation of grace. You don't earn
   your standing; you rest on redemption.

5. "According to the fashion... shewed thee in the mount" (v.30). God's
   design is specific. Worship is not "whatever feels right to me" — it
   follows God's pattern. Are you worshipping God His way or yours?
