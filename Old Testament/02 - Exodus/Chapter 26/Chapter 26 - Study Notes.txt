Exodus - Chapter 26
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God gives detailed instructions for the Tabernacle's curtains, frames, and veil — the dwelling place where He will meet with His people.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 26 provides the detailed construction plan for
the Tabernacle structure itself — its curtains, coverings, framework, and
the all-important veil that separates the Holy Place from the Most Holy Place.
The Tabernacle was approximately 45 feet long, 15 feet wide, and 15 feet
high — a portable tent-sanctuary. Four layers of covering protected it: (1)
fine linen with cherubim (beauty, seen only from inside); (2) goats' hair
(protection); (3) rams' skins dyed red (sacrifice); (4) outer skins
(weather protection). The veil (v.31) is theologically crucial — it
separates sinful humanity from God's holy presence. It was torn from top to
bottom at Christ's death (Matthew 27:51).

The Inner Curtains (vv.1-6): Ten curtains of fine linen — blue, purple,
scarlet — with cherubim woven in (v.1). Each 28 cubits by 4 cubits (v.2).
Joined in two sets of five, connected by fifty gold clasps (vv.3-6). "It
shall be one tabernacle" (v.6) — unity from many parts.

The Goats' Hair Covering (vv.7-13): Eleven curtains of goats' hair — the
tent over the tabernacle (v.7). Each 30 cubits by 4 cubits (v.8). Joined
by fifty bronze clasps (v.11). Extra length hangs over the back and sides
for protection (vv.12-13).

The Outer Coverings (v.14): Rams' skins dyed red over the goats' hair.
Outer skins (badgers'/goatskins) on top — weather protection.

The Framework (vv.15-30): Acacia wood frames, each 10 cubits tall, 1.5
cubits wide (vv.15-16). Twenty frames on the south, twenty on the north,
eight on the west (vv.18-25). Each frame set in two silver sockets/bases
(v.19). Five acacia bars overlaid with gold hold the frames together
(vv.26-29). The middle bar runs the full length (v.28). "Rear up the
tabernacle according to the fashion... shewed thee in the mount" (v.30).

The Veil (vv.31-35): Blue, purple, scarlet, fine linen — with cherubim
(v.31). Hung on four gold-overlaid pillars with silver bases (v.32). "The
vail shall divide unto you between the holy place and the most holy" (v.33).
The Ark and mercy seat go behind the veil (v.34). The table on the north
side, the lampstand on the south (v.35).

The Entrance Screen (vv.36-37): Blue, purple, scarlet, fine linen —
embroidered (v.36). Five pillars of acacia overlaid with gold, on bronze
bases (v.37).

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Israel remains encamped at Mount Sinai. God gives Moses the Tabernacle curtain instructions on the mountain.

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------
Enduring Word (David Guzik):
  https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/exodus-26/
  Guzik covers the symbolism of each layer (beauty within, plainness
  without — like Christ), the veil as separation torn at the cross, and
  the silver bases as redemption (silver = redemption price).

Charles Spurgeon:
  Sermon: "The Rent Veil" on v.33 (MTP Vol. 34, No. 2015)
  "The vail shall divide — for centuries, this veil stood between God and
  man. Thick, heavy, embroidered with cherubim — guardians barring the way.
  No one passed through except the high priest, once a year, with blood.
  But when Christ died — 'the veil of the temple was rent in twain from the
  TOP to the bottom.' From the TOP — God tore it, not man. From heaven
  downward. God Himself opened the way. The barrier is gone. The separation
  is ended. 'Having boldness to enter into the holiest by the blood of
  Jesus, by a new and living way... through the veil, that is to say, his
  flesh' (Hebrews 10:19-20). Christ's torn flesh IS the torn veil. Enter
  boldly."

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. "The vail shall divide" (v.33). Before Christ, a thick curtain separated
   humanity from God. At the cross, it was torn. You now have direct access
   to God's presence — through Christ's torn flesh. Do you USE that access?
   Or do you live as if the veil is still there?

2. The outer covering was plain; the beauty was inside (v.14 vs. v.1).
   Christ had "no beauty that we should desire him" (Isaiah 53:2) outwardly
   — but inwardly, He was glorious. Don't judge by appearances. The
   greatest beauty is often hidden from casual observers.

3. "It shall be one tabernacle" (v.6). Many parts, one structure. The church
   is the same: many members, one body. Are you contributing to unity or
   division?

4. The entire structure rests on silver bases — redemption (v.19). Everything
   in your spiritual life rests on the foundation of grace. You don't earn
   your standing; you rest on redemption.

5. "According to the fashion... shewed thee in the mount" (v.30). God's
   design is specific. Worship is not "whatever feels right to me" — it
   follows God's pattern. Are you worshipping God His way or yours?
