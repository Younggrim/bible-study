Exodus - Chapter 27
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God prescribes the bronze altar for burnt offerings, the courtyard dimensions, and the oil for the lampstand's continual light.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 27 completes the Tabernacle's physical structure
with three elements: (1) The bronze altar of burnt offering — the first thing
encountered upon entering the courtyard (vv.1-8); (2) The courtyard itself —
the enclosed space surrounding the Tabernacle (vv.9-19); (3) The oil for the
lampstand — perpetual light (vv.20-21). The bronze altar is where sacrifice
happens — where sin is dealt with before anyone can approach God's presence.
It is the largest piece of furniture and the most public. The courtyard
(150 feet by 75 feet) creates sacred space — a boundary between the common
world and God's dwelling. The perpetual lamp represents God's unceasing
presence and Israel's unceasing worship.

The Bronze Altar (vv.1-8): Acacia wood overlaid with bronze (v.1). Five
cubits square, three cubits high — approximately 7.5 feet square, 4.5 feet
tall (v.1). Four horns on the corners (v.2). Bronze utensils: pans, shovels,
basins, forks, fire pans (v.3). A bronze grating/network inside (vv.4-5).
Poles for carrying (vv.6-7). Hollow construction (v.8). Made "as it was
shewed thee in the mount" (v.8).

The Courtyard (vv.9-19): Fine linen hangings: 100 cubits on the south and
north sides (vv.9-11), 50 cubits on the west (v.12), 50 cubits on the east
with a 20-cubit gate (vv.13-16). Pillars with bronze bases and silver hooks
(v.17). Total dimensions: 100 cubits by 50 cubits, 5 cubits high (v.18) —
approximately 150 by 75 feet, 7.5 feet tall. The gate: blue, purple, scarlet,
fine linen — embroidered (v.16). All pins/pegs of bronze (v.19).

The Oil for the Lamp (vv.20-21): Pure beaten olive oil (v.20). The lamp
burns continually — "from evening to morning before the LORD" (v.21). Aaron
and his sons tend it. "A statute for ever" (v.21).

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Israel remains encamped at Mount Sinai. The bronze altar and courtyard instructions are given here — designed for the center of Israel's camp.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "You shall make the altar of acacia wood, five cubits long and five
        cubits broad." (ESV) — The altar: where sacrifice is made.

v.2  — "You shall make horns for it on its four corners." (ESV) — The horns:
        place of refuge and atonement.

v.8  — "As it has been shown you on the mountain, so shall it be made."
        (ESV) — God's pattern governs.

v.20 — "You shall command the people of Israel to bring you pure beaten
        olive oil for the light, that a lamp may regularly be set up to
        burn." (ESV) — Perpetual light in God's presence.

v.21 — "A statute forever to be observed throughout their generations."
        (ESV) — Unceasing worship.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1  — Hebrew "atzei shittim" — acacia wood.
       KJV: "shittim wood"
       ESV: "acacia wood"
       ASV: "acacia wood"
       NET: "acacia wood"
       WEB: "acacia wood"
       Analysis: Same as chapter 25. The KJV transliterates; all modern
       translations correctly identify acacia — a hard, durable desert wood.
       NET converts dimensions: "7½ feet long, and 7½ feet wide... 4½ feet"
       instead of cubits. ASV/WEB preserve the cubit measurements and their
       symbolic significance (the altar is a perfect 5×5 square = completeness).

v.2  — Hebrew "nechoshet... qarnot" — bronze... horns.
       KJV: "brass... horns"
       ESV: "bronze... horns"
       ASV: "brass... horns"
       NET: "bronze... horns"
       WEB: "brass... horns"
       Analysis: As noted in chapter 25, the metal is bronze (copper-tin),
       not brass (copper-zinc). ESV/NET correctly render "bronze." Bronze
       represents judgment — the altar where sin is judged is made of the
       judgment metal. All agree on "horns" — projections at the four
       corners for binding sacrifices, applying blood, and providing refuge.

v.3  — Hebrew "mizraqot" — from "zaraq," to sprinkle/toss.
       KJV: "his basons"
       ESV: "its basins"
       ASV: "its basins"
       NET: "its tossing bowls"
       WEB: "its basins"
       Analysis: NET's "tossing bowls" uniquely captures the function —
       these bowls were used to toss/dash blood against the altar. KJV/ESV/
       ASV/WEB use the generic "basins." NET's rendering makes the
       sacrificial ritual more vivid.

v.8  — Hebrew "ka'asher her'ah otkha bahar" — as it was shown you on the mountain.
       KJV: "as it was shewed thee in the mount, so shall they make it"
       ESV: "as it has been shown you on the mountain, so shall it be made"
       ASV: "as it hath been showed thee in the mount, so shall they make it"
       NET: "Just as it was shown you on the mountain, so they must make it"
       WEB: "They shall make it as it has been shown you on the mountain"
       Analysis: NET adds emphasis with "Just as" and "must," strengthening
       the divine imperative. The pattern shown on the mountain is not a
       suggestion — it is a command. NET's rendering makes the mandatory
       nature more forceful.

v.10 — Hebrew "chashukeihem" — bands, connecting rods between pillars.
       KJV: "fillets"
       ESV: "fillets"
       ASV: "hooks of the pillars and their fillets shall be of silver"
       NET: "silver hooks and bands"
       WEB: "the hooks of the pillars and their fillets shall be of silver"
       Analysis: KJV/ESV/ASV/WEB retain the archaic "fillets" (thin
       decorative bands connecting pillars). NET modernizes to "bands." The
       silver connecting rods created a unified, beautiful enclosure — silver
       (redemption) linking the courtyard together.

v.16 — Hebrew "masakh" — a screen, a covering.
       KJV: "an hanging"
       ESV: "a screen"
       ASV: "a screen"
       NET: "a curtain"
       WEB: "a screen"
       Analysis: ESV/ASV/WEB's "screen" is more precise than KJV's
       "hanging." NET uses "curtain." This is the gate — the single entrance
       to the courtyard, embroidered with the same colors as the Tabernacle
       veil.

v.19 — Hebrew "yetedot" — pins, pegs.
       KJV: "all the pins thereof"
       ESV: "all its pegs"
       ASV: "All the instruments of the tabernacle... and all the pins thereof"
       NET: "All the utensils of the tabernacle... all its tent pegs"
       WEB: "All the instruments of the tabernacle... and all its pins"
       Analysis: NET's "utensils" and "tent pegs" are more specific than
       ASV/WEB's "instruments" and "pins." NET also adds "used in all its
       service" — making explicit that these bronze items are functional
       tools for Tabernacle worship.

v.20 — Hebrew "shemen zayit zakh katit" — pure olive oil, beaten/pressed.
       KJV: "pure oil olive beaten"
       ESV: "pure beaten olive oil"
       ASV: "pure olive oil beaten"
       NET: "pure oil of pressed olives"
       WEB: "pure olive oil beaten"
       Analysis: NET reverses the word order to "pressed olives" (emphasizing
       the source), while ASV/WEB use "olive oil beaten" (emphasizing the
       process). "Beaten/pressed" (katit) means the first cold pressing —
       highest quality oil, produced by crushing without grinding. A picture
       of Christ: crushed to produce light.

v.21 — Hebrew "lifnei YHWH... chuqqat olam" — before the LORD... a statute
       forever.
       KJV: "before the LORD... a statute for ever"
       ESV: "before the LORD... a statute forever"
       ASV: "before Jehovah... a statute for ever"
       NET: "before the Lord... a lasting ordinance"
       WEB: "before Yahweh... a statute forever"
       Analysis: The divine name (YHWH) rendered three ways: ASV "Jehovah,"
       WEB "Yahweh," NET/KJV/ESV "the Lord/LORD." In this context — the
       perpetual lamp burning "before" God — the personal name emphasizes
       intimate, ongoing relationship. NET's "lasting ordinance" softens the
       permanence slightly compared to "statute forever" — "olam" carries
       stronger eternal weight.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Bronze Altar   — The altar of burnt offering. The first item encountered in
               the courtyard. Where sacrifice is made — where sin is dealt
               with. A type of the cross.

Horns of the altar — Projections at the four corners. Blood was applied to
               them; fugitives grasped them for refuge. Represent the power
               and reach of atonement — extending to all four directions.

The Courtyard  — The enclosed space around the Tabernacle. Creates sacred
               space — a boundary between the common and the holy. One gate
               on the east side — one way in.

The Gate       — A single 20-cubit entrance on the east side. Blue, purple,
               scarlet, fine linen. Christ: "I am the door" (John 10:9).
               There is one way to God — not many.

Beaten olive oil — Oil produced by crushing, not grinding. The purest,
               clearest oil. Christ was "crushed for our iniquities" (Isaiah
               53:5) — and from His crushing comes the light of the world.

Perpetual lamp — The lampstand burns continually — never extinguished.
               Represents God's unceasing presence and the call to
               unceasing worship.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. The altar is the FIRST thing encountered (v.1). You cannot approach God
   without passing the place of sacrifice. The cross is not optional — it's
   the entrance. Have you come through the cross? There is no other way in.

2. The courtyard has ONE gate (v.16). Not multiple entrances, not "many
   paths to God." One gate. Jesus said: "I am the door" (John 10:9). In a
   world of religious pluralism, this is exclusive — and it is true.

3. "Pure oil olive beaten for the light" (v.20). The oil is beaten/crushed
   to produce light. Christ was crushed to bring light to the world. Your
   own crushing — your suffering — can also produce light for others. Don't
   waste your pain.

4. "To cause the lamp to burn always" (v.20). Perpetual light — never
   extinguished. Is your worship constant? Not just Sunday morning but
   every day, every moment? "Pray without ceasing" (1 Thessalonians 5:17).

5. "A statute for ever" (v.21). Some things never change. God's desire for
   worship, for light, for His presence among His people — these are eternal.
   Cultures change; God's requirements don't.
