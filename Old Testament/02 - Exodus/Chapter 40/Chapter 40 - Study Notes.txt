Exodus - Chapter 40
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Moses assembles the Tabernacle; the glory of the Lord fills it so powerfully that even Moses cannot enter — God now dwells among His people.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Exodus 40 is the grand finale of the book — the erection
of the tabernacle and the descent of God's glory to fill it. Nearly a year has
passed since the Exodus (the tabernacle is erected on the first day of the
first month of the second year, v.17 — cf. 12:2). What began with Israel's
cry for deliverance in chapter 1 culminates here with God taking up residence
among His people. The entire book has been moving toward this moment: God
dwelling with man.

Moses personally erects the tabernacle, placing each item according to God's
instructions. The phrase "as the LORD commanded Moses" appears eight times in
this chapter (vv.16, 19, 21, 23, 25, 27, 29, 32), bringing the total in
chapters 39–40 to approximately fifteen — a drumbeat of obedience that echoes
creation's "and God said... and it was so." Moses is both obedient servant and
priestly figure, consecrating everything with anointing oil.

The climax comes in verses 34–38: the cloud covers the tent and the glory of
the LORD fills the tabernacle — so powerfully that even Moses cannot enter.
This is the fulfillment of God's promise in 25:8: "Let them make me a
sanctuary; that I may dwell among them." The book ends not with arrival in the
Promised Land but with something better — God's presence with His people
wherever they go. The cloud by day and fire by night will guide them through
every journey.

Section Summaries:
vv.1–8 — God instructs Moses on setting up the tabernacle and its furnishings.
vv.9–11 — Anointing and consecrating the tabernacle and altar.
vv.12–15 — Aaron and his sons washed, clothed, and anointed for priesthood.
vv.16–33 — Moses erects the tabernacle and places every item; finishes the work.
vv.34–38 — The cloud covers the tent; God's glory fills the tabernacle.

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Mount Sinai / the camp: The Tabernacle is erected on the first day of the first month of the second year (v.17) — nearly one year after the Exodus.
- The cloud covers the tent; God's glory fills the Tabernacle (vv.34-38). From now on, God's visible presence travels with Israel wherever they go.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.17 — "In the first month in the second year, on the first day of the
 month, the tabernacle was erected." (ESV) — A new beginning; God's
 dwelling erected on the first day of the new year.

v.33 — "So Moses finished the work." (ESV) — Completion; echoing Genesis 2:2,
 "God ended his work." The tabernacle as new creation.

v.34 — "Then the cloud covered the tent of meeting, and the glory of the
 LORD filled the tabernacle." (ESV) — The climax of Exodus: God takes
 up residence among His people.

v.35 — "Moses was not able to enter the tent of meeting because the cloud
 settled on it, and the glory of the LORD filled the tabernacle."
 (ESV) — God's glory so overwhelming that even Moses cannot enter.

v.38 — "For the cloud of the LORD was on the tabernacle by day, and fire
 was in it by night, in the sight of all the house of Israel
 throughout all their journeys." (ESV) — God's visible, continual
 presence guiding His people.

Charles Spurgeon:
  Sermon: "The Glory of God Filling the Tabernacle" on v.34 (MTP Vol. 10,
  No. 577)
  "The glory of the LORD filled the tabernacle! This is what God has been
  working toward since Eden. He made man for fellowship. Sin drove man out.
  Redemption brings God back. The tabernacle is God saying, 'I will dwell
  with you again.' And the glory that filled that tent was but a shadow of
  the glory that filled the body of Christ, and the glory that shall fill
  the new creation when God is all in all. Every act of redemption aims at
  this: God with us, God among us, God in us."
  Sermon: "The Pillar of Cloud and Fire" on v.38 (MTP Vol. 8, No. 452)
  "Cloud by day, fire by night — always visible, always present. God does
  not leave His people to guess. He guides. He protects. He illuminates the
  darkness. And He never departs. Throughout ALL their journeys — not some,
  but all. In the wilderness, in the battles, in the waiting — the cloud
  was there. So is Christ with us, always, even unto the end of the age."

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.2  — Hebrew "mishkan ʼohel moʻed" — dwelling-place of the tent of meeting.
       KJV: "the tabernacle of the tent of the congregation"
       ESV: "the tabernacle of the tent of meeting"
       ASV: "the tabernacle of the tent of meeting"
       NET: "the tabernacle, the tent of meeting"
       WEB: "the tabernacle of the Tent of Meeting"
       Analysis: Two terms combined: mishkan (dwelling) and ʼohel moʻed (tent of meeting/appointed time). KJV's "congregation" is less precise; "meeting" (all modern translations) captures that this is where God meets with His people.

v.3  — Hebrew "paroket" — the inner curtain/veil.
       KJV: "thou shalt... cover the ark with the vail"
       ESV: "you shall screen the ark with the veil"
       ASV: "thou shalt screen the ark with the veil"
       NET: "you are to shield the ark with the special curtain"
       WEB: "you shall screen the ark with the veil"
       Analysis: NET avoids the traditional "veil" in favor of "special curtain," and uses "shield" rather than "screen." The theological resonance of "veil" — torn at Christ's death (Matt 27:51) — is lost in NET's functionally descriptive approach.

v.4  — Hebrew "menorah" — lampstand.
       KJV: "thou shalt bring in the candlestick"
       ESV: "you shall bring in the lampstand"
       ASV: "thou shalt bring in the candlestick"
       NET: "you are to bring in the lampstand"
       WEB: "You shall bring in the lamp stand"
       Analysis: ASV/KJV retain the archaic "candlestick" for the menorah. ESV/WEB/NET correctly use "lampstand" since the object burned oil, not wax candles.

v.7  — Hebrew "kiyyor" — laver/basin.
       KJV: "thou shalt set the laver"
       ESV: "you shall put the basin"
       ASV: "thou shalt set the laver"
       NET: "you are to put the large basin"
       WEB: "You shall set the basin"
       Analysis: NET adds the adjective "large" (not in Hebrew) to distinguish this basin from smaller vessels. ASV/KJV preserve the traditional "laver." ESV/WEB use the simpler "basin." The bronze laver was where priests washed before service.

v.9  — Hebrew "veqiddashta" — to make holy, set apart.
       KJV: "thou shalt... hallow it"
       ESV: "you shall... consecrate it"
       ASV: "thou shalt... hallow it"
       NET: "you are to... sanctify it"
       WEB: "you shall... make it holy"
       Analysis: Four different English words for the same Hebrew concept: KJV/ASV "hallow," ESV "consecrate," NET "sanctify," WEB "make it holy." All convey setting apart for sacred use through anointing.

v.15 — Hebrew "kehunnat ʻolam" — priesthood for the age/perpetuity.
       KJV: "an everlasting priesthood"
       ESV: "a perpetual priesthood"
       ASV: "an everlasting priesthood"
       NET: "a lasting priesthood"
       WEB: "an everlasting priesthood"
       Analysis: KJV/ASV/WEB "everlasting" and ESV "perpetual" convey duration without end; NET's "lasting" is weaker. The Aaronic priesthood endured until fulfilled in Christ (Heb 7).

v.16 — Hebrew obedience formula with the divine name.
       KJV: "according to all that the LORD commanded him, so did he"
       ESV: "according to all that the LORD commanded him, so he did"
       ASV: "according to all that Jehovah commanded him, so did he"
       NET: "according to all the Lord had commanded him—so he did"
       WEB: "According to all that Yahweh commanded him, so he did"
       Analysis: The obedience formula summarizing Moses' life. The divine name is prominent: Moses obeys not an abstract principle but a personal God. NET's dash before "so he did" adds rhetorical emphasis. This formula appears in nearly every verse of the chapter.

v.20 — Hebrew "kapporet" — mercy seat/atonement lid.
       KJV: "put the mercy seat above upon the ark"
       ESV: "put the mercy seat on top of the ark"
       ASV: "put the mercy-seat above upon the ark"
       NET: "put the atonement lid on the ark"
       WEB: "put the mercy seat above on the ark"
       Analysis: NET's consistent "atonement lid" for Hebrew "kapporet" appears in this climactic chapter where the most sacred objects are assembled. The "mercy seat" (KJV/ESV/ASV/WEB) emphasizes grace; the "atonement lid" (NET) emphasizes propitiation.

v.29 — Hebrew "minchah" — gift offering, specifically grain/flour.
       KJV: "the meat offering"
       ESV: "the grain offering"
       ASV: "the meal-offering"
       NET: "the grain offering"
       WEB: "the meal offering"
       Analysis: KJV "meat" meant "food" in general (archaic); ESV/NET specify "grain"; ASV/WEB use "meal" (ground grain). All refer to the same non-animal offering of flour/grain.

v.34 — Hebrew "kevod YHWH maleʼ ʼet-hamishkan" — the glory of YHWH filled the dwelling.
       KJV: "the glory of the LORD filled the tabernacle"
       ESV: "the glory of the LORD filled the tabernacle"
       ASV: "the glory of Jehovah filled the tabernacle"
       NET: "the glory of the Lord filled the tabernacle"
       WEB: "Yahweh's glory filled the tabernacle"
       Analysis: The climactic verse of Exodus. "Kavod" = heaviness, splendor, manifest presence. Each translation renders the divine name characteristically in this theologically supreme moment. WEB's possessive "Yahweh's glory" is structurally different from the others' "the glory of [divine name]." God's visible, weighty presence fills the structure His people built.

v.35 — Hebrew "shakan" — to dwell, settle, tabernacle.
       KJV: "the cloud abode thereon"
       ESV: "the cloud settled on it"
       ASV: "the cloud abode thereon"
       NET: "the cloud settled on it"
       WEB: "the cloud stayed on it"
       Analysis: NET/ESV's "settled," ASV/KJV's "abode," and WEB's "stayed" all render Hebrew "shakan" — to dwell, settle, tabernacle. This is the root of "Shekinah" (the dwelling presence) and "mishkan" (tabernacle). God "tabernacles" on the tabernacle.

v.38 — Hebrew "le'einei" — to the eyes of; the final verse of Exodus.
       KJV: "the cloud of the LORD was upon the tabernacle by day, and fire was in it by night, in the sight of all the house of Israel"
       ESV: "the cloud of the LORD was on the tabernacle by day, and fire was in it by night, in the sight of all the house of Israel"
       ASV: "the cloud of Jehovah was upon the tabernacle by day, and there was fire therein by night, in the sight of all the house of Israel"
       NET: "the cloud of the Lord was on the tabernacle by day, but fire would be on it at night, in plain view of all the house of Israel"
       WEB: "the cloud of Yahweh was on the tabernacle by day, and there was fire in the cloud by night, in the sight of all the house of Israel"
       Analysis: The divine name accompanies the final verse of Exodus. NET's "in plain view" and others' "in the sight" both translate "le'einei" (to the eyes of). God's presence was publicly visible — not hidden, not private, but displayed for all Israel to see throughout all their journeys.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Glory of the LORD — Hebrew "kevod YHWH" — the visible, weighty, radiant
 manifestation of God's presence. The cloud and fire that filled the
 tabernacle, making it God's dwelling on earth.

Shekinah — From Hebrew "shakan" (to dwell). Though not a biblical term
 itself, it describes God's manifest dwelling presence — the glory
 cloud that settled on and filled the tabernacle.

Anointing — The application of sacred oil to consecrate persons and objects
 for God's service. Sets apart the common as holy; empowers for
 sacred function.

Cloud and fire — The visible manifestation of God's presence: cloud by day
 (protection from desert sun, guidance) and fire by night
 (illumination, warmth, guidance). Constant and visible to all.

Finished the work — Moses completed the tabernacle construction. Echoes
 God finishing creation (Gen 2:2) and Christ finishing redemption
 (John 19:30). Divine work brought to completion.

Tabernacle — Hebrew "mishkan" — dwelling place. God's portable sanctuary
 among Israel. A type of Christ (John 1:14 — "tabernacled among
 us") and of the believer's body (1 Cor 6:19).

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Exodus begins with Israel enslaved and ends with God dwelling among them
 (v.34). Redemption's goal is not just freedom FROM something but
 fellowship WITH Someone. Is your Christian life defined more by what
 you've been saved from or who you've been saved to?

2. "So Moses finished the work" (v.33). Faithful completion of God-given
 tasks matters. What has God called you to that requires perseverance to
 finish? Are you tempted to leave things incomplete?

3. The glory was so overwhelming that Moses could not enter (v.35). Have you
 ever been overwhelmed by God's presence? Do you create space in your life
 for encounters with His glory, or do you keep Him at a manageable distance?

4. The cloud guided Israel's movements — when it moved, they moved; when it
 stayed, they stayed (vv.36-37). How sensitive are you to God's leading?
 Do you move when He moves and wait when He waits? Or do you run ahead or
 lag behind?

5. "Throughout all their journeys" (v.38) — God's presence was constant, not
 occasional. He was there in the wilderness, not just at the destination.
 How does God's continual presence comfort you in your current "journey" —
 especially if you haven't yet reached your "promised land"?
