Exodus - Chapter 4
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God gives Moses three signs to confirm his calling, appoints Aaron as his spokesman, and Moses returns to Egypt.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 4 continues Moses' dialogue with God at the
burning bush. Moses raises four more objections (adding to "Who am I?" from
3:11): "They will not believe me" (v.1), "I am not eloquent" (v.10), and
finally the bare refusal: "Send someone else" (v.13). God answers each
objection with provision — signs, Aaron as spokesman — but His anger burns
at Moses' final resistance. The chapter also contains the mysterious
"bridegroom of blood" incident (vv.24-26), God's declaration of Israel as
His firstborn son (v.22), and the first mention of God hardening Pharaoh's
heart (v.21). Moses finally obeys, meets Aaron, and the elders of Israel
believe and worship.

Moses' Objections and God's Signs (vv.1-9): Objection: "They will not
believe me" (v.1). God gives three signs: (1) The rod becomes a serpent and
back (vv.2-5); (2) Moses' hand becomes leprous then healed (vv.6-7); (3)
Water from the Nile becomes blood (v.9). Each sign demonstrates God's power
over nature, disease, and Egypt's gods.

Moses' Speech Objection (vv.10-12): "I am slow of speech, and of a slow
tongue" (v.10). God's answer: "Who hath made man's mouth?... have not I the
LORD?" (v.11). "I will be with thy mouth" (v.12). The Creator of the mouth
can certainly empower it.

Moses' Final Refusal (vv.13-17): "Send, I pray thee, by the hand of him
whom thou wilt send" — send anyone but me (v.13). God's anger kindles (v.14).
He appoints Aaron as Moses' spokesman (vv.14-16). Moses will be "instead of
God" to Aaron; Aaron will be Moses' "mouth" (v.16). Moses takes "the rod of
God" (v.17).

The Journey to Egypt (vv.18-23): Moses departs Midian (v.18). God tells him
those who sought his life are dead (v.19). God warns: "I will harden his
heart" (v.21). God declares: "Israel is my son, even my firstborn" (v.22) —
and warns Pharaoh: refuse, and "I will slay thy son, even thy firstborn"
(v.23). The tenth plague is announced before the first.

The Bridegroom of Blood (vv.24-26): A mysterious, terrifying incident. God
"sought to kill" Moses (v.24). Zipporah circumcises their son and touches
Moses' feet with the foreskin: "A bloody husband art thou to me" (v.25). God
relents (v.26). Moses had neglected circumcision — the sign of the covenant
he was sent to uphold.

Reunion and Belief (vv.27-31): God sends Aaron to meet Moses (v.27). Moses
tells Aaron everything (v.28). They gather the elders, perform the signs
(vv.29-30). "The people believed... they bowed their heads and worshipped"
(v.31).

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Horeb/Sinai: Moses is still at the mountain of God, continuing the burning bush dialogue from chapter 3.
- Midian (v.18): Moses returns to Jethro before departing for Egypt.
- Egypt (v.19-20): Moses takes his family and journeys back to Egypt, the land he fled 40 years earlier.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.2  — "What is that in your hand?" (ESV) — God uses what you already have.

v.11 — "Who has made man's mouth? Who makes him mute, or deaf, or seeing,
        or blind? Is it not I, the LORD?" (ESV) — God is sovereign over
        all human ability and disability.

v.13 — "Oh, my Lord, please send someone else." (ESV) — Moses' bare
        refusal — and God's anger.

v.22 — "Israel is my firstborn son." (ESV) — God's declaration of
        Israel's identity and His fatherly love.

v.31 — "And the people believed... they bowed their heads and
        worshiped." (ESV) — Faith leads to worship.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.2  — KJV: "rod" / ESV: "staff" — Hebrew "matteh" — a staff, a
       shepherd's rod. The ESV uses "staff"; the KJV uses "rod." Both are
       valid. It becomes "the rod of God" (v.20) — ordinary tools
       transformed by divine commission.

v.10 — KJV: "slow of speech, and of a slow tongue" / ESV: "slow of speech
       and of tongue" — Hebrew "kevad peh ukhvad lashon" — heavy of mouth
       and heavy of tongue. Both translations capture the sense. Not
       necessarily a stutter — possibly a lack of fluency in Egyptian after
       40 years, or genuine difficulty in public speaking.

v.13 — KJV: "by the hand of him whom thou wilt send" / ESV: "by the hand
       of him whom you will send" — Hebrew "beyad tishlach" — literally "by
       the hand you will send." Both are identical. A polite refusal
       disguised as deference: "Send whoever you're going to send — just
       not me."

v.21 — KJV: "I will harden his heart" / ESV: "I will harden his heart" —
       Hebrew "achazzeq et libbo" — I will strengthen/make firm his heart.
       Both translations identical. The hardening is complex: sometimes God
       hardens (4:21, 9:12), sometimes Pharaoh hardens himself (8:15, 8:32).

v.22 — KJV: "my firstborn" / ESV: "my firstborn son" — Hebrew "beni
       bekhori" — my son, my firstborn. The ESV adds "son" for clarity.
       A declaration of special relationship, inheritance rights, and
       protection.

v.24 — KJV: "sought to kill him" / ESV: "sought to put him to death" —
       Hebrew "vayevaqesh hamito" — sought to put him to death. The ESV is
       more literal. Terrifying and mysterious — Moses cannot represent the
       covenant while neglecting its sign (circumcision).

v.25 — KJV: "a bloody husband" / ESV: "a bridegroom of blood" — Hebrew
       "chatan damim" — a bridegroom/husband of blood(s). The ESV's
       "bridegroom" is more precise than the KJV's "husband" for the Hebrew
       "chatan." Zipporah's words express horror at the bloody rite.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
- v.2 — ASV: "A rod" / NET: "A staff" / WEB: "A rod" — NET consistently uses "staff" where ASV/WEB retain "rod" (Hebrew "matteh"). NET's choice loses the connection to Aaron's "rod" that later buds (Numbers 17).
- v.10 — ASV: "Oh, Lord, I am not eloquent... I am slow of speech, and of a slow tongue" — ASV addresses "Jehovah" but uses "Lord" (Adonai) here, showing Moses switches to the submissive title. WEB uniquely addresses "Yahweh" while also saying "O Lord" — preserving both the divine name context and the respectful address.
- v.13 — NET: "please send anyone else whom you wish to send!" — NET's expansive paraphrase makes Moses' reluctance more explicit; WEB simplifies: "please send someone else"; ASV retains the literal Hebrew idiom "by the hand of him whom thou wilt send."
- v.24 — WEB: "Yahweh met Moses and wanted to kill him" — uniquely interpretive; "wanted to kill" softens the Hebrew "sought to kill" (ASV) into intention rather than action. NET: "sought to kill him" matches ASV's literalness.
- v.25 — NET: "touched it to Moses' feet" — NET interprets the ambiguous "cast it at his feet" (ASV/WEB) as deliberate contact, a significant exegetical choice for this notoriously difficult passage.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
The Rod of God — Moses' shepherd's staff, transformed into an instrument of
               divine power. Ordinary tools surrendered to God become
               extraordinary.

"Slow of speech" — Moses' self-assessment of his speaking ability. God's
               response: the Creator of the mouth can empower it.

Hardening of heart — God strengthening Pharaoh's existing resistance. Not
               creating evil but confirming a choice already made. Divine
               sovereignty and human responsibility coexist.

"Israel is my firstborn" — God's declaration of Israel's special status.
               Firstborn = heir, beloved, protected. Ultimately fulfilled
               in Christ, God's true Firstborn Son.

Bridegroom of blood — Zipporah's words after circumcising her son to save
               Moses. The covenant sign (circumcision) cannot be neglected
               by the covenant mediator.

Circumcision   — The physical sign of God's covenant with Abraham (Genesis
               17). Moses had apparently neglected this for his son —
               perhaps due to Zipporah's objection.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "What is that in thine hand?" (v.2). God asks for what you already have
   — not what you don't. What has God placed in your hand? Surrender it to
   Him and watch Him transform it.

2. "Who hath made man's mouth?" (v.11). Your limitations are not God's
   limitations. Stop using your weaknesses as excuses. The God who made you
   can empower you for whatever He calls you to do.

3. "Send... by the hand of him whom thou wilt send" (v.13). Moses' final
   objection is simply "I don't want to go." Have you ever said no to God?
   His anger burned — but He still used Moses. God is patient, but
   resistance has consequences (Aaron's involvement created problems later).

4. "Israel is my son, even my firstborn" (v.22). God calls you His child.
   You are not an orphan, not a slave — you are a son/daughter of the Most
   High. Live from that identity.

5. The bridegroom of blood (vv.24-26): Moses couldn't lead God's covenant
   people while neglecting God's covenant sign. Is there an area of
   obedience you're neglecting while trying to serve God in other areas?
   Partial obedience is not obedience.
