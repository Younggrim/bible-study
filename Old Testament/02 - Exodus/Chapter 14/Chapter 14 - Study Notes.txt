Exodus - Chapter 14
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God parts the Red Sea, Israel crosses on dry ground, and Pharaoh's army is destroyed when the waters return.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 14 is the climax of the exodus — the crossing
of the Red Sea. This is Israel's defining moment: trapped between Pharaoh's
army and the sea, God parts the waters, Israel crosses on dry ground, and
Egypt's army is destroyed. This event is referenced more than any other in
the Old Testament as proof of God's power and faithfulness. It is Israel's
"baptism" (1 Corinthians 10:1-2) — the definitive break from Egypt. God
deliberately engineers the situation: He leads Israel to a dead end (v.2),
hardens Pharaoh to pursue (v.4), and then displays His glory by doing what
only He can do. The chapter contains Moses' greatest declaration of faith:
"Stand still, and see the salvation of the LORD" (v.13).

God Sets the Trap (vv.1-9): God directs Israel to camp by the sea — a
seemingly trapped position (v.2). Purpose: Pharaoh will think "they are
entangled" (v.3) and pursue. "I will be honoured upon Pharaoh" (v.4).
Pharaoh takes 600 chosen chariots plus all Egypt's chariots (vv.6-7). He
overtakes Israel at the sea (v.9).

Israel's Fear and Moses' Faith (vv.10-14): Israel sees the army and panics:
"Because there were no graves in Egypt, hast thou taken us away to die in
the wilderness?" (v.11). Moses responds with one of Scripture's greatest
faith declarations: "Fear ye not, stand still, and see the salvation of the
LORD" (v.13). "The LORD shall fight for you, and ye shall hold your peace"
(v.14).

The Crossing (vv.15-22): God tells Moses: stop praying and ACT — "speak
unto the children of Israel, that they go forward" (v.15). "Lift thou up thy
rod... and divide it" (v.16). The Angel of God and the pillar move behind
Israel — darkness to Egypt, light to Israel (vv.19-20). Moses stretches his
hand; God sends a strong east wind all night; the sea divides (v.21). Israel
walks through on dry ground — "the waters were a wall unto them on their
right hand, and on their left" (v.22).

Egypt's Destruction (vv.23-28): The Egyptians pursue into the sea (v.23).
God looks through the pillar and troubles them (v.24). Chariot wheels come
off (v.25). The Egyptians realize: "the LORD fighteth for them" (v.25).
Moses stretches his hand — the sea returns — "there remained not so much as
one of them" (v.28).

The Result (vv.29-31): Israel safe on dry land (v.29). Egypt dead on the
shore (v.30). "The people feared the LORD, and believed the LORD, and his
servant Moses" (v.31).

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Pi-hahiroth (v.2): Israel camps here, between Migdol and the sea, before Baal-zephon. The exact location is debated but is on the western shore of the Yam Suph.
- The Red Sea / Sea of Reeds (Yam Suph) (v.21): The site of the miraculous crossing. God divides the waters with a strong east wind.
- The wilderness on the eastern shore (v.29): Israel emerges on the Sinai side; Egypt is behind them.

Charles Spurgeon:
  Sermon: "Stand Still" on v.13 (MTP Vol. 1, No. 16)
  "Stand still — these two words are among the hardest in the Christian
  language. We can march forward; we can retreat; we can fight. But STAND
  STILL? Do nothing? Wait? This is the hardest command. Yet it is often
  God's command. Stand still — not in despair but in faith. Stand still —
  not in idleness but in expectation. Stand still — and SEE. You cannot see
  God's salvation while you're running in panic. You cannot see it while
  you're scheming your own escape. Stand still. Be quiet. Watch. God is
  about to do something you've never seen before."

  Sermon: "The LORD Shall Fight for You" on v.14 (MTP Vol. 23, No. 1348)
  "The LORD shall fight for you, and ye shall hold your peace — your part is
  silence; God's part is warfare. Your part is rest; God's part is victory.
  You hold your peace — He fights. You are still — He moves. This is the
  gospel in miniature: we contribute nothing to our salvation except the sin
  that made it necessary. God does the fighting. God parts the sea. God
  destroys the enemy. We walk through on dry ground — and then we sing."

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Stand still, and see the salvation of the LORD" (v.13). When you're
   trapped — enemy behind, sea ahead — stop panicking and watch God work.
   Your impossible situation is God's opportunity for glory.

2. "The LORD shall fight for you, and ye shall hold your peace" (v.14). You
   don't have to fight every battle yourself. Some battles are God's to
   fight — your job is to be still and trust. Which battle do you need to
   hand over to Him?

3. "Speak unto the children of Israel, that they go forward" (v.15). There's
   a time to pray and a time to move. Faith isn't always waiting — sometimes
   it's stepping into the sea before it parts. What step of obedience is God
   calling you to take?

4. The pillar moved behind them (v.19). God positions Himself between you
   and your enemy. Whatever is pursuing you — God stands in the gap. You are
   protected.

5. "Israel saw the Egyptians dead upon the sea shore" (v.30). The enemies
   that terrified you yesterday will be dead on the shore tomorrow. God
   finishes what He starts. The enemy that chases you will not survive God's
   intervention.
