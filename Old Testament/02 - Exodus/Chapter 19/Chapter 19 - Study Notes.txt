Exodus - Chapter 19
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Israel arrives at Sinai; God proposes a covenant — if they obey, they will be His treasured possession, a kingdom of priests, and a holy nation.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 19 is the arrival at Sinai — the mountain of God
where Moses first saw the burning bush (3:1, 12). God's promise is fulfilled:
"ye shall serve God upon this mountain" (3:12). This chapter establishes
Israel's identity as God's covenant people: "a kingdom of priests, and an
holy nation" (v.6). It also establishes the terrifying holiness of God —
thunder, lightning, fire, smoke, earthquake, and the trumpet blast. The
people tremble. Boundaries are set: touch the mountain and die. This is the
prelude to the giving of the Ten Commandments (chapter 20). The chapter
teaches that God is both near (He comes down) and unapproachable (boundaries
required) — grace and holiness together.

Arrival at Sinai (vv.1-2): Three months after leaving Egypt, Israel arrives
at Sinai (v.1). They camp before the mountain (v.2). The sign of 3:12 is
being fulfilled.

God's Covenant Proposal (vv.3-8): God calls Moses up and delivers His
proposal: "Ye have seen what I did unto the Egyptians, and how I bare you on
eagles' wings, and brought you unto myself" (v.4). The condition: "if ye will
obey my voice indeed, and keep my covenant" (v.5). The promise: "ye shall be
a peculiar treasure unto me... a kingdom of priests, and an holy nation"
(vv.5-6). The people respond: "All that the LORD hath spoken we will do"
(v.8).

Preparation for God's Descent (vv.9-15): God will come "in a thick cloud"
(v.9). Instructions: sanctify yourselves, wash clothes, be ready on the
third day (vv.10-11). Set boundaries around the mountain — death for anyone
who touches it (vv.12-13). Moses sanctifies the people (v.14).

The Theophany (vv.16-25): On the third day: thunders, lightnings, thick
cloud, trumpet blast — "all the people that was in the camp trembled" (v.16).
Moses brings the people to the mountain's base (v.17). Sinai is covered in
smoke and fire — "the whole mount quaked greatly" (v.18). The trumpet grows
louder; Moses speaks; God answers audibly (v.19). God descends to the summit
and calls Moses up (v.20). God warns again: keep the people back "lest they
break through... and many of them perish" (v.21).

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Mount Sinai (v.2): Israel arrives at "the wilderness of Sinai" and camps "before the mount" (v.2). This becomes the central location for Exodus 19-40.
- The mountain itself: God descends on it with fire, smoke, thunder; boundaries set around its base (vv.12, 18). No one may touch it.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.4  — "I bore you on eagles' wings and brought you to myself." (ESV) —
        God's tender care and His purpose: relationship.

v.5  — "You shall be my treasured possession among all peoples." (ESV) —
        Israel's unique identity: God's treasured possession.

v.6  — "You shall be to me a kingdom of priests and a holy nation." (ESV) —
        Israel's calling: priestly mediation and holiness.

v.16 — "There were thunders and lightnings and a thick cloud on the
        mountain and a very loud trumpet blast, so that all the people...
        trembled." (ESV) — God's terrifying holiness.

v.18 — "Now Mount Sinai was wrapped in smoke because the LORD had descended
        on it in fire." (ESV) — God comes down.

COMMENTARY REFERENCES

Enduring Word (David Guzik):

  Guzik covers God's covenant proposal, Israel's identity as a kingdom of
  priests, the terrifying theophany, and the tension between God's nearness

Charles Spurgeon:
  Sermon: "Eagles' Wings" on v.4 (MTP Vol. 34, No. 2010)
  "I bare you on EAGLES' WINGS — the eagle carries her young on her wings,
  not in her claws. Beneath them, not gripping them. If an arrow comes, it
  strikes the mother, not the young. God carries you the same way — beneath
  you are the everlasting arms (Deuteronomy 33:27). If judgment comes, it
  strikes HIM, not you. You ride on His strength. You rest on His wings.
  And where does He carry you? 'Unto MYSELF.' Not merely to safety, not
  merely to the Promised Land — unto HIMSELF. The destination of redemption
  is God Himself. He saves you FOR Himself."

  Sermon: "A Kingdom of Priests" on v.6 (MTP Vol. 22, No. 1316)
  "A kingdom of PRIESTS — every Israelite a priest. Not just Levi, not just
  Aaron — the whole nation. And Peter takes this and applies it to the
  church: 'a royal priesthood' (1 Peter 2:9). Every believer is a priest —
  with access to God, with a sacrifice to offer, with intercession to make.
  You do not need a human mediator between you and God. Christ is your High
  Priest — and you are a priest under Him."

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.4  — Hebrew "kanfei nesharim... va'avi etkhem elai" — wings of eagles... and I brought you to myself.
       KJV: "I bare you on eagles' wings, and brought you unto myself"
       ESV: "I bore you on eagles' wings and brought you to myself"
       ASV: "how I bare you on eagles' wings, and brought you unto myself"
       NET: "how I lifted you on eagles' wings and brought you to myself"
       WEB: "how I bore you on eagles' wings, and brought you to myself"
       Analysis: NET's "lifted" adds vertical imagery; ASV/WEB/KJV's "bare/bore"
       (carried) is closer to Hebrew "nasa" (to carry, bear). All preserve the
       remarkable destination: "unto myself" — God's goal is intimacy, not just
       geography. The eagle carries young on its wings — a picture of protective care.

v.5  — Hebrew "segullah" — a treasured possession, special property of value.
       KJV: "a peculiar treasure unto me above all people"
       ESV: "my treasured possession among all peoples"
       ASV: "ye shall be mine own possession from among all peoples"
       NET: "you will be my special possession out of all the nations"
       WEB: "you shall be my own possession from among all peoples"
       Analysis: NET's "special possession" and ASV/WEB's "mine own possession" both
       capture "segullah." KJV's "peculiar treasure" is archaic but rich — "peculiar"
       meant "one's own." ESV's "treasured possession" is clearest for modern ears.
       Not "peculiar" (odd) but uniquely valued by God.

v.6  — Hebrew "mamlekhet kohanim vegoy qadosh" — a kingdom of priests and a holy nation.
       KJV: "a kingdom of priests, and an holy nation"
       ESV: "a kingdom of priests and a holy nation"
       ASV: "ye shall be unto me a kingdom of priests, and a holy nation"
       NET: "you will be to me a kingdom of priests and a holy nation"
       WEB: "you shall be to me a kingdom of priests, and a holy nation"
       Analysis: All translate this key covenant phrase identically. The entire
       nation has priestly access and responsibility. This becomes the church's
       identity in 1 Peter 2:9.

v.13 — Hebrew "bimshokh hayovel" — when the ram's horn sounds a long blast.
       KJV: "when the trumpet soundeth long"
       ESV: "when the trumpet sounds a long blast"
       ASV: "when the trumpet soundeth long"
       NET: "When the ram's horn sounds a long blast"
       WEB: "When the trumpet sounds long"
       Analysis: NET specifies "ram's horn" (the shofar/yovel), adding precision
       about the instrument. ASV/WEB/KJV's generic "trumpet" obscures that this is
       a ram's horn, not a metal trumpet. The ESV adds "a long blast" for clarity.

v.16 — Hebrew "qol shofar chazaq me'od" — the sound of the ram's horn exceedingly loud.
       KJV: "the voice of the trumpet exceeding loud"
       ESV: "a very loud trumpet blast"
       ASV: "the voice of a trumpet exceeding loud"
       NET: "the sound of a very loud horn"
       WEB: "the sound of an exceedingly loud trumpet"
       Analysis: NET again specifies "horn" rather than "trumpet" (shofar vs.
       chatzotzrah). The theophany sound is organic (animal horn), not manufactured
       metal. KJV/ASV's "voice" for "qol" is more literal than ESV/WEB/NET's
       "sound" — but less natural for a trumpet.

v.18 — Hebrew "vayecherad kol-hahar me'od" — and the whole mountain trembled exceedingly.
       KJV: "quaked greatly"
       ESV: "trembled greatly"
       ASV: "the whole mount quaked greatly"
       NET: "the whole mountain shook violently"
       WEB: "the whole mountain quaked greatly"
       Analysis: NET's "shook violently" and ASV/WEB/KJV's "quaked greatly" both
       capture the seismic intensity of divine descent. The mountain itself shakes
       at God's presence — terrifying holiness made perceptible.

v.22 — Hebrew "pen-yiphrotz bahem YHWH" — lest the LORD break forth/through against them.
       KJV: "lest the LORD break forth upon them"
       ESV: "lest the LORD break out against them"
       ASV: "lest Jehovah break forth upon them"
       NET: "lest the Lord break through against them"
       WEB: "lest Yahweh break out on them"
       Analysis: The image is of God as overwhelming force breaking through barriers.
       All capture this with different prepositions: "upon/on/against" — God's
       holiness is dangerous to the unprepared. The divine name rendered
       characteristically by each translation.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Sinai/Horeb    — The mountain of God. Where Moses saw the burning bush;
               where the Law is given; where Elijah later flees. The place
               of divine encounter.

"Eagles' wings" — God's tender, protective care. He carries Israel — they
               don't carry themselves. Grace precedes law.

"Peculiar treasure" (segullah) — God's treasured possession. Israel is
               uniquely valued — not because of their merit but because of
               God's choice.

"Kingdom of priests" — Every Israelite has priestly access and
               responsibility. Fulfilled in the church: every believer is
               a priest (1 Peter 2:9).

Theophany      — God's visible, audible manifestation. At Sinai: fire,
               smoke, earthquake, thunder, trumpet. Terrifying holiness
               made perceptible.

Boundaries     — The limits set around Sinai. God is approachable (He comes
               down) but not casual (touch the mountain and die). Grace and
               holiness coexist.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "I bare you on eagles' wings, and brought you unto myself" (v.4). God
   carried you — you didn't carry yourself. And His destination for you is
   HIMSELF — not just heaven, not just blessing, but intimate relationship
   with Him. Is that your goal?

2. "A kingdom of priests, and an holy nation" (v.6). You are a priest — with
   direct access to God and responsibility to represent Him to the world.
   Are you living as a priest? Offering worship, making intercession,
   declaring God's excellencies?

3. "All that the LORD hath spoken we will do" (v.8). Israel said yes before
   hearing the terms. Bold — but also naive. They couldn't keep this promise.
   Neither can we — which is why we need grace. The Law reveals our need for
   a Savior.

4. The mountain trembled, smoked, and burned (v.18). God is not tame. He is
   not a comfortable concept. He is holy fire. Do you approach Him with
   appropriate reverence — or have you domesticated Him?

5. God came DOWN (v.20). Despite the fire and boundaries, God descends. He
   initiates. He approaches. The holy God moves toward sinful people. This
   is grace — and it reaches its fullness in the incarnation.
