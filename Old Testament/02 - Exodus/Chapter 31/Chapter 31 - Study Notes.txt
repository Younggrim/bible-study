Exodus - Chapter 31
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God appoints Bezalel and Oholiab as Spirit-filled craftsmen for the Tabernacle, then gives Moses the stone tablets and commands Sabbath observance.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Exodus 31 concludes the section of divine instructions given
to Moses on Mount Sinai (chapters 25–31). After six chapters of detailed
blueprints for the tabernacle, its furnishings, and the priesthood, God now
reveals who will build it all. The chapter divides into two major sections:
the appointment of Spirit-filled craftsmen (vv.1–11) and the reiteration of
the Sabbath command (vv.12–17), followed by the giving of the stone tablets (v.18).

The appointment of Bezalel and Oholiab demonstrates that God not only designs
the work but also equips the workers. Bezalel is the first person in Scripture
explicitly said to be "filled with the Spirit of God" — and the filling is for
artistic craftsmanship, not prophecy or leadership. This establishes that all
legitimate vocations can be Spirit-empowered callings.

The Sabbath command, placed here between the tabernacle instructions and the
tabernacle construction, serves as a theological guardrail: even the holy work
of building God's dwelling must not override the holy rest God commands. The
chapter closes with the dramatic image of God handing Moses two stone tablets
"written with the finger of God" — a moment of completion that immediately
precedes the catastrophe of chapter 32.

Section Summaries:
vv.1–11 — Bezalel and Oholiab appointed; Spirit-given skill for tabernacle work.
vv.12–17 — The Sabbath command reiterated as a perpetual covenant sign.
v.18 — God gives Moses the two tablets of stone.

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Israel remains encamped at Mount Sinai. God appoints Bezalel and Oholiab and gives the Sabbath sign. Moses receives the two stone tablets at the summit.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.3 — "I have filled him with the Spirit of God, with ability and
 intelligence, with knowledge and all craftsmanship." (ESV)
 — The first explicit mention of the Spirit filling a person; given
 for craftsmanship, not just prophecy.

v.6 — "In the heart of every craftsman I have put skill." (ESV)
 — God is the source of all skill and ability; human talent is
 divinely bestowed.

v.13 — "Above all you shall keep my Sabbaths, for this is a sign between me
 and you throughout your generations." (ESV) — The Sabbath as covenant
 sign, identifying Israel as God's sanctified people.

v.17 — "It is a sign forever between me and the people of Israel that in
 six days the LORD made heaven and earth, and on the seventh day he
 rested and was refreshed." (ESV) — Sabbath rooted in creation order.

v.18 — "Two tablets of the testimony, tablets of stone, written with the
 finger of God." (ESV) — The divine authorship of the law; God's
 direct, unmediated communication.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.2 — KJV: "Bezaleel" / ESV: "Bezalel" — Hebrew "Betsalʼel" (בְּצַלְאֵל) —
 meaning "in the shadow of God." The KJV retains the older English
 transliteration; ESV modernizes the spelling.

v.3 — KJV: "wisdom, and in understanding, and in knowledge" / ESV: "ability
 and intelligence, with knowledge" — Hebrew "chokmah" (wisdom),
 "tevunah" (understanding/intelligence), "da'at" (knowledge). Three
 distinct faculties: skill, discernment, and information.

v.4 — KJV: "cunning works" / ESV: "artistic designs" — Hebrew "machashavot"
 — inventive designs, creative plans. KJV "cunning" meant skillful,
 not deceptive.

v.13 — KJV: "Verily my sabbaths ye shall keep" / ESV: "Above all you shall
 keep my Sabbaths" — Hebrew "ʼak" — an emphatic particle meaning
 "surely" or "only." ESV interprets it as priority ("above all");
 KJV as emphasis ("verily").

v.15 — KJV: "sabbath of rest" / ESV: "Sabbath of solemn rest" — Hebrew
 "shabbat shabbaton" — an intensified form: a sabbath of complete
 cessation. The doubling emphasizes totality of rest.

v.17 — KJV: "was refreshed" / ESV: "was refreshed" — Hebrew "vayyinnafash"
 — from "nephesh" (soul/breath). God "took breath" or "was
 refreshed." An anthropomorphic expression showing rest as renewal.

v.18 — KJV: "communing with him" / ESV: "speaking with him" — Hebrew
 "ledabber" — to speak. KJV's "communing" captures the intimate,
 face-to-face nature of the conversation on Sinai.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Bezalel — "In the shadow of God." Chief craftsman from the tribe of Judah,
 grandson of Hur, filled with the Spirit for tabernacle construction.

Oholiab — "The Father is my tent." Assistant craftsman from the tribe of Dan,
 skilled in engraving, design, and embroidery.

Filled with the Spirit — Divine empowerment for specific service. In the OT,
 the Spirit came upon individuals for particular tasks rather than
 indwelling all believers permanently.

Sabbath — Hebrew "shabbat" — cessation, rest. The seventh day set apart as
 holy, rooted in creation and given as a covenant sign to Israel.

Perpetual covenant — A covenant without expiration within its dispensational
 context; the Sabbath as an enduring sign of Israel's relationship
 with God.

Finger of God — An anthropomorphic expression for God's direct, personal
 action. The tablets were not dictated but directly inscribed by God.

Tables of testimony — The two stone tablets containing the Ten Commandments;
 called "testimony" because they testify to God's covenant terms.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. God filled Bezalel with His Spirit for craftsmanship (v.3). Do you view
 your daily work — whatever it is — as a potential arena for the Spirit's
 empowerment? How might your work change if you invited the Spirit into it?

2. "In the hearts of all that are wise hearted I have put wisdom" (v.6). God
 gives skill and then calls people to use it for His purposes. What skills
 has God given you, and how are you using them for His kingdom?

3. The Sabbath command is placed between the instructions and the construction
 (vv.12-17). What does this teach about the relationship between work and
 rest? Are you tempted to skip rest because the work feels "holy"?

4. The Sabbath is called a "sign" (v.13). Signs point to something beyond
 themselves. What does Sabbath rest point to? (cf. Hebrews 4:9-11)

5. The tablets were "written with the finger of God" (v.18). God personally
 inscribed His words. How does the directness of God's communication affect
 how you receive and respond to Scripture?
