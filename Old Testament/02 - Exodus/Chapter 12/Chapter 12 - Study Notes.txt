Exodus - Chapter 12
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God institutes the Passover — the lamb's blood on the doorposts saves Israel's firstborn while Egypt's are struck dead; Israel finally leaves Egypt.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 12 is one of the most important chapters in the
entire Bible — the institution of the Passover and the execution of the
tenth plague. This is the night that defines Israel's identity forever: the
night God passed over the blood-marked houses and struck Egypt's firstborn.
Every detail of the Passover lamb points to Christ: selected on the 10th,
killed on the 14th (the day Jesus was crucified), without blemish, a male,
its blood applied to the doorposts, no bone broken (John 19:36), eaten in
haste with readiness to depart. Paul declares: "Christ our passover is
sacrificed for us" (1 Corinthians 5:7). This chapter establishes Israel's
calendar, their central feast, and the theology of substitutionary atonement
through blood.

The Passover Instructions (vv.1-14): God resets Israel's calendar: "the
beginning of months" (v.2) — redemption creates a new beginning. On the 10th,
select a lamb — without blemish, male, first year (vv.3-5). Keep it until
the 14th, then kill it "in the evening" (v.6). Apply the blood to the
doorposts and lintel with hyssop (v.7). Eat the flesh roasted with fire,
with unleavened bread and bitter herbs (v.8). Eat in readiness: loins girded,
shoes on, staff in hand (v.11). "It is the LORD's passover" (v.11). God's
promise: "When I see the blood, I will pass over you" (v.13). "Against all
the gods of Egypt I will execute judgment" (v.12).

The Feast of Unleavened Bread (vv.15-20): Seven days of eating unleavened
bread — leaven (yeast) represents sin. Remove all leaven from houses (v.15).
Holy convocations on days one and seven (v.16).

The Execution (vv.21-28): Moses instructs the elders: kill the lamb, apply
blood with hyssop, stay inside until morning (vv.21-22). "The LORD will pass
over the door, and will not suffer the destroyer to come in" (v.23). When
children ask "What mean ye by this service?" — tell them (vv.26-27). The
people worship and obey (vv.27-28).

The Tenth Plague and Exodus (vv.29-42): "At midnight the LORD smote all the
firstborn" (v.29) — from Pharaoh's throne to the dungeon. "There was a great
cry in Egypt; for there was not a house where there was not one dead" (v.30).
Pharaoh summons Moses: "Rise up, and get you forth... and bless me also"
(vv.31-32). Egypt urgently expels Israel (v.33). About 600,000 men on foot,
plus women, children, and a mixed multitude (vv.37-38). The sojourning: 430
years to the day (vv.40-41).

Passover Regulations (vv.43-51): No uncircumcised person may eat it (v.48).
No bone shall be broken (v.46). One law for native and stranger (v.49).

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Egypt / Goshen: The Passover takes place in Israelite homes throughout Goshen. Death strikes every Egyptian household "from the firstborn of Pharaoh... unto the firstborn of the captive" (v.29).
- Raamses (v.37): The departure point — Israel journeys from Raamses to Succoth. Approximately 600,000 men on foot, plus families.
- Succoth (v.37): First stop after leaving Egypt; in the eastern Delta region.

Enduring Word (David Guzik):

  Guzik provides extensive treatment of every Passover detail as a type of
  Christ — the lamb, the blood, the hyssop, the unleavened bread, the
  unbroken bones, and the timing.

Charles Spurgeon:
  Sermon: "When I See the Blood" on v.13 (MTP Vol. 32, No. 1899)
  "WHEN I SEE THE BLOOD, I will pass over you — not 'when I see your tears,'
  not 'when I see your good works,' not 'when I see your sincerity,' not
  'when I see your church attendance.' When I see THE BLOOD. The blood is
  everything. The firstborn in the blood-marked house was no better than the
  firstborn in the unmarked house — the difference was the BLOOD. A trembling
  Israelite behind the blood was safer than a confident Egyptian without it.
  God did not say 'When YOU see the blood' — for you might doubt your own
  application. He said 'When I see it.' God sees the blood of Christ applied
  to you — and that is enough. Rest there."

  Sermon: "The Passover" on v.11 (MTP Vol. 6, No. 291)
  "It is the LORD's passover — not Israel's feast, not Moses' invention, not
  a human tradition. It is GOD's passover. He designed it. He commanded it.
  He fulfilled it. And every detail points to His Son: the lamb without
  blemish (Christ's sinlessness), the blood on the door (Christ's atonement),
  the roasting with fire (Christ bearing God's wrath), the bitter herbs
  (our repentance), the unleavened bread (Christ's purity), the haste
  (urgency of faith). This is not a meal — it is a gospel."

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "When I see the blood, I will pass over you" (v.13). Your safety before
   God depends on the blood of Christ — not your performance, not your
   feelings, not your worthiness. Are you trusting the blood? That is enough.

2. "Your lamb shall be without blemish" (v.5). Only a perfect sacrifice
   suffices. Christ alone is without blemish — without sin. No human effort
   can substitute for His perfect sacrifice.

3. The blood had to be APPLIED (v.7). It wasn't enough for the lamb to die
   — the blood had to be put on the doorposts. Christ's death must be
   personally applied by faith. Have you applied it to YOUR life?

4. "Eat it in haste" (v.11). There is urgency to the gospel. Don't delay
   your response to God. Loins girded, shoes on, staff in hand — be ready
   to move when God says go.

5. "What mean ye by this service?" (v.26). Children will ask. Be ready to
   tell the story. The Passover — and the gospel — is meant to be explained
   to the next generation. Are you telling it?
