Exodus - Chapter 18
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Jethro, Moses' father-in-law, visits and advises him to delegate judgment to capable leaders — a model of wise administration.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 18 is a pause between the wilderness journey and
the giving of the Law at Sinai. Jethro (Moses' father-in-law, priest of
Midian) visits, bringing Zipporah and Moses' two sons. He hears the exodus
story, worships God, and then observes Moses judging the people alone from
morning to evening. Jethro's counsel — delegate to qualified leaders — is
one of the Bible's clearest teachings on leadership, delegation, and
organizational structure. Moses humbly receives wisdom from a non-Israelite
— demonstrating that God can speak through unexpected sources. This chapter
establishes the principle: no one person should carry the entire burden of
leadership.

Jethro's Visit (vv.1-12): Jethro hears what God has done (v.1). He brings
Zipporah and Moses' sons — Gershom ("stranger there") and Eliezer ("God is
my help") (vv.2-4). Moses tells the full story (v.8). Jethro rejoices and
confesses: "Now I know that the LORD is greater than all gods" (v.11). He
offers sacrifices and eats with Aaron and the elders "before God" (v.12).

Moses' Unsustainable Pattern (vv.13-16): Moses judges the people alone —
from morning to evening (v.13). Jethro observes and asks: "What is this
thing that thou doest?" (v.14). Moses explains: the people come to him for
God's judgment and to know God's statutes (vv.15-16).

Jethro's Counsel (vv.17-23): "The thing that thou doest is not good" (v.17).
"Thou wilt surely wear away" — burnout (v.18). Jethro's solution: (1) Moses
teaches God's laws (v.20); (2) Appoint qualified leaders — "able men, such
as fear God, men of truth, hating covetousness" (v.21); (3) Organize in
tiers: rulers of thousands, hundreds, fifties, and tens (v.21); (4) They
handle small matters; Moses handles great matters (v.22). Result: "thou shalt
be able to endure, and all this people shall also go to their place in peace"
(v.23).

Moses Implements (vv.24-27): Moses listens and acts (v.24). He appoints
leaders at every level (v.25). The system works: hard cases go to Moses,
small matters are handled locally (v.26). Jethro departs (v.27).

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- The wilderness near Mount Sinai: Jethro comes from Midian to meet Moses at "the mount of God" (v.5).
- Israel is now encamped at the base of Sinai, where they will remain for approximately 11 months.

  qualifications for leaders, and Moses' humility in receiving counsel from

Charles Spurgeon:
  Sermon: "Jethro's Counsel" on v.17 (MTP Vol. 26, No. 1553)
  "The thing that thou doest is NOT GOOD — sometimes the most spiritual
  people need the most practical advice. Moses was doing a good thing —
  judging the people, teaching God's law. But he was doing it in a bad way
  — alone, exhausted, unsustainable. Good work done badly is still not good.
  And God sent the counsel through Jethro — not a prophet, not an Israelite,
  but a father-in-law with common sense. Christian, don't be so spiritual
  that you reject practical wisdom. Don't be so busy serving God that you
  burn out serving God. Delegation is not laziness — it is wisdom."

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "The thing that thou doest is not good" (v.17). Are you doing good work
   in an unsustainable way? Burnout is not a badge of honor — it's a sign
   that something needs to change. Who is your Jethro — speaking honest
   truth into your life?

2. "Thou wilt surely wear away" (v.18). You are not indispensable. If you
   try to do everything yourself, you'll destroy yourself AND the people you
   serve. Delegation is not weakness — it's wisdom.

3. "Able men, such as fear God, men of truth, hating covetousness" (v.21).
   Character qualifications for leadership: competence, godliness, integrity,
   and freedom from greed. Do these describe you? Do they describe the
   leaders you follow?

4. Moses received counsel from a non-Israelite (v.24). God can speak wisdom
   through unexpected people. Are you humble enough to receive good advice
   from outside your usual circle?

5. "All this people shall also go to their place in peace" (v.23). Good
   leadership structure benefits everyone — not just the leader. When you
   delegate well, the whole community thrives.
