Zechariah - Chapter 3
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Joshua the high priest stands accused by Satan but is cleansed by the Angel of the LORD, given pure garments, and told of the coming "Branch" — God will remove the iniquity of the land in a single day.


AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Zechariah son of Berechiah. Prophesied 520-480 BC.
Contemporary with Haggai. Key theme: Messianic hope and the
coming kingdom. The most Messianic of the Minor Prophets.
Famous passages: the Branch (3:8, 6:12), "Not by might... but
by my spirit" (4:6), the triumphal entry (9:9), "they shall
look upon me whom they have pierced" (12:10), the shepherd
struck (13:7), and the LORD's return to the Mount of Olives
(14:4).

Classification: Prophetic Vision — The Cleansing of Joshua the
High Priest and the Promise of the Branch
Key Themes: Satan as accuser, divine justification, filthy
garments removed, clean garments bestowed, the Branch promised,
iniquity removed in one day, the gospel in the Old Testament

Historical Context: The fourth night vision is arguably the most
theologically profound in Zechariah. Joshua the high priest
stands before the Angel of the LORD — representing Israel before
God. Satan stands at his right hand to accuse him. And the
accusation is valid: Joshua wears FILTHY garments (literally
"excrement-covered" in Hebrew). He is guilty.

But God does not defend Joshua's innocence — He overrules the
accusation by sovereign grace. "Is not this a brand plucked from
the fire?" God removes the filthy garments, clothes Joshua in
clean vestments, and places a clean turban on his head. This is
justification: not making the sinner righteous in himself, but
CLOTHING him with a righteousness not his own.

The chapter then pivots to the ultimate resolution: "I will bring
forth my servant the BRANCH" (v.8). The Branch is the Messiah —
the one who will "remove the iniquity of that land in one day"
(v.9). This is the cross. One day. One sacrifice. All sin removed.

Structure:
- Joshua Before the Angel: Satan the Accuser (vv.1-2)
- The Filthy Garments Removed (vv.3-4)
- The Clean Turban and Pure Vestments (v.5)
- The Charge to Walk Faithfully (vv.6-7)
- The Promise of the Branch (v.8)
- The Stone with Seven Eyes: Iniquity Removed in One Day (v.9)
- The Millennial Peace: Vine and Fig Tree (v.10)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "Joshua the high priest standing before the angel of the
        LORD, and Satan standing at his right hand to resist him"
        (KJV) — A courtroom scene. Joshua represents sinful
        Israel. Satan is the prosecutor. The Angel of the LORD is
        the judge. And the verdict will shock everyone: the guilty
        is acquitted — not because he is innocent, but because
        God intervenes.

v.2  — "The LORD rebuke thee, O Satan... is not this a brand
        plucked out of the fire?" (KJV) — God silences the
        accuser. His argument is not that Joshua is clean (he
        isn't) but that He has CHOSEN to rescue him. A burning
        stick snatched from the fire — that is grace. Rescued not
        because of merit but because of mercy.

v.4  — "I have caused thine iniquity to pass from thee, and I
        will clothe thee with change of raiment" (KJV) / "I have
        taken your iniquity away from you, and I will clothe you
        with pure vestments" (ESV) — The double action of
        justification: iniquity REMOVED and righteousness
        BESTOWED. Not just forgiveness but a new wardrobe. This
        is the gospel: your filth taken away, Christ's
        righteousness put upon you.

v.8  — "I will bring forth my servant the BRANCH" (KJV) — One of
        the great Messianic titles. The Branch (Hebrew "tsemach")
        appears in Isaiah 4:2, 11:1; Jeremiah 23:5, 33:15; and
        Zechariah 3:8, 6:12. He is the shoot from David's stump —
        the Messiah who will grow up from lowly origins to bear
        royal glory.

v.9  — "I will remove the iniquity of that land in one day" (KJV)
        — This is the cross of Christ in one sentence. One day.
        One sacrifice. Complete removal of sin. Not gradual
        improvement but decisive, once-for-all atonement. "It is
        finished" (John 19:30).

v.10 — "In that day... shall ye call every man his neighbour
        under the vine and under the fig tree" (KJV) — The picture
        of Millennial peace. Security, prosperity, fellowship.
        When sin is removed, shalom follows.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.3 — "filthy garments" — Hebrew "tso'im" = excrement-stained.
      This is not mere dirtiness — it is the most revolting
      defilement possible. Isaiah uses the same root in 64:6:
      "all our righteousnesses are as filthy rags." Our best
      efforts are sewage before God's holiness.

v.4 — "change of raiment" (KJV) / "pure vestments" (ESV) —
      Hebrew "machalatsot" = festival/ceremonial garments. These
      are not work clothes but CELEBRATION garments — the finest
      attire for the most honored occasion.

v.5 — "fair mitre" (KJV) / "clean turban" (ESV) — Hebrew
      "tsanif tahor" = a pure/clean turban. The high priest's
      turban bore the inscription "HOLINESS TO THE LORD" (Exodus
      28:36). Joshua receives back his priestly authority — now
      purified.

v.8 — "the BRANCH" — Hebrew "tsemach" = a sprout, a growing
      shoot. It emphasizes organic growth from seemingly dead
      stock. The Messiah grows up from David's cut-down tree —
      humble beginnings, glorious destiny.

v.9 — "seven eyes" — Representing omniscience and perfect
      watchfulness. God sees completely. The stone (likely the
      cornerstone of the Temple, or Messiah Himself) is under
      God's perfect, all-seeing care.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Satan accuses, but God defends (vv.1-2). The enemy's strategy
   is accusation — reminding you of your guilt, your failures,
   your unworthiness. But God says: "I rebuke you." Your defender
   is stronger than your accuser. When condemnation rises, appeal
   to the Judge who already ruled in your favor.

2. Your filth is not the final word (vv.3-4). Joshua stood in
   excrement-stained garments — and was NOT rejected. God
   removed the filth and clothed him in purity. No matter how
   stained you feel, God's grace is greater than your sin. He
   does not just forgive — He RE-CLOTHES.

3. Justification is God's work, not yours (v.4). Joshua did not
   clean himself. He did not earn the new garments. He simply
   STOOD THERE while God did the work. Salvation is received,
   not achieved. Stop trying to wash your own filthy garments.
   Let God dress you in His righteousness.

4. "In one day" (v.9). The complete removal of sin happened in
   ONE day — the day Christ died. Not a process but an event. Not
   gradual improvement but decisive atonement. If you are in
   Christ, your iniquity was removed — completely, finally,
   irrevocably — in one day on Calvary.

5. The Branch grows from lowly places (v.8). The Messiah comes
   not as a mighty oak but as a small shoot. God's greatest work
   often begins in the smallest, humblest circumstances. Don't
   despise your small beginnings — the Branch is growing.
