Zechariah - Chapter 5
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Two visions — a flying scroll that curses every thief and perjurer, and a woman called Wickedness sealed in a basket and carried to Babylon — depict God's removal of sin from the land.


AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Zechariah son of Berechiah. Prophesied 520-480 BC.
Contemporary with Haggai. Key theme: Messianic hope and the
coming kingdom. The most Messianic of the Minor Prophets.
Famous passages: the Branch (3:8, 6:12), "Not by might... but
by my spirit" (4:6), the triumphal entry (9:9), "they shall
look upon me whom they have pierced" (12:10), the shepherd
struck (13:7), and the LORD's return to the Mount of Olives
(14:4).

Classification: Prophetic Vision — The Flying Scroll and the
Woman in the Basket
Key Themes: God's curse against sin, judgment on theft and
false oaths, the removal of wickedness from the land,
wickedness personified and exiled to Shinar (Babylon)

Historical Context: Visions six and seven deal with the
PURGING of sin from the land. If chapters 3-4 showed God's
grace (cleansing Joshua, empowering Zerubbabel), chapter 5
shows God's justice. Grace does not mean tolerance of sin.
The restored community must be holy.

The flying scroll (vv.1-4) is enormous — 20 cubits by 10 cubits
(30 feet by 15 feet) — the same dimensions as the porch of
Solomon's Temple (1 Kings 6:3). The curse written on it targets
two representative sins: stealing (against man) and swearing
falsely (against God). These cover both tables of the Law. The
curse enters the house and CONSUMES it — sin destroys from
within.

The woman in the basket (vv.5-11) personifies wickedness itself.
She is stuffed into an ephah (a measuring basket), sealed with a
lead cover, and carried by two winged women to the land of Shinar
(Babylon). The message: wickedness will be REMOVED from God's
land and sent back to where it belongs — Babylon, the biblical
symbol of human rebellion against God.

Structure:
- The Sixth Vision: The Flying Scroll (vv.1-4)
- The Scroll's Dimensions and Its Curse (vv.2-3)
- The Curse Entering the House of the Sinner (v.4)
- The Seventh Vision: The Woman in the Basket (vv.5-8)
- Wickedness Sealed Under Lead (vv.7-8)
- Wickedness Removed to Shinar (vv.9-11)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.3  — "This is the curse that goeth forth over the face of the
        whole earth: for every one that stealeth shall be cut off"
        (KJV) — God's curse against sin is universal ("the whole
        earth") and specific (theft and false swearing). No one is
        exempt. The curse actively seeks out sinners and enters
        their homes.

v.4  — "it shall enter into the house of the thief... and shall
        consume it with the timber thereof and the stones thereof"
        (KJV) — Sin consumes from within. It doesn't just bring
        external punishment — it destroys the very structure of
        your life. The house built by theft is consumed BY the
        curse of theft.

v.8  — "This is wickedness" (KJV/ESV) — Wickedness personified
        as a woman (Hebrew "rishah" is feminine). She is thrust
        into the basket and sealed shut. God confines and controls
        wickedness — it does not roam free under His sovereign
        hand.

v.11 — "To build it an house in the land of Shinar" (KJV) —
        Shinar = Babylon (Gen 11:2). Wickedness is sent BACK to
        its origin — the land of Babel, human pride, and rebellion.
        God removes sin from His people and relocates it to its
        proper domain. In the age to come, wickedness will have no
        place among God's people.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1 — "flying roll" (KJV) / "flying scroll" (ESV) — Hebrew
      "megillah 'aphah" = a scroll in flight. Scrolls in the
      ancient world contained legal documents and decrees. This
      flying scroll is a divine court order — God's verdict
      actively pursuing the guilty.

v.6 — "ephah" — A dry measure of approximately one bushel (22
      liters). It was the standard commercial measure — fitting
      that commercial wickedness (greed, theft) is contained in
      a commercial container.

v.7 — "talent of lead" (KJV) / "leaden cover" (ESV) — Hebrew
      "kikkar ophereth" = a circle/disk of lead. Lead is the
      heaviest common metal — used to permanently seal wickedness
      shut. Once God confines sin, it cannot escape.

v.11 — "Shinar" — The ancient name for Babylonia (Gen 10:10,
       11:2). By sending wickedness to Shinar, God symbolically
       returns evil to its source. Babylon represents the world
       system opposed to God.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Sin has consequences that enter your "house" (v.4). The curse
   doesn't just visit — it REMAINS and CONSUMES. Hidden sin eats
   away at the structure of your life: your relationships, your
   peace, your integrity. Deal with it before it consumes the
   timber and stones of what you've built.

2. God takes theft and false speech equally seriously (v.3). One
   is against man, the other against God. Both merit the curse.
   Integrity in word and deed — this is what God requires. Guard
   both your hands AND your tongue.

3. God is actively REMOVING wickedness (vv.5-11). This is
   encouraging: God doesn't tolerate sin indefinitely. He is
   working to purge, contain, and exile it. In your life, He is
   working to remove wickedness — cooperate with the process.
   Don't cling to what God is trying to carry away.

4. Wickedness belongs in Babylon, not in Zion (v.11). Sin has
   no rightful place among God's people. When you tolerate sin
   in your life, you're keeping something in God's house that
   belongs in Shinar. Let God send it back where it came from.
