Zechariah - Chapter 6
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Four chariots go out to patrol the earth, and Zechariah is told to crown Joshua the high priest, symbolizing the coming "Branch" who will be both priest and king — building the true temple of the LORD.


AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Zechariah son of Berechiah. Prophesied 520-480 BC.
Contemporary with Haggai. Key theme: Messianic hope and the
coming kingdom. The most Messianic of the Minor Prophets.
Famous passages: the Branch (3:8, 6:12), "Not by might... but
by my spirit" (4:6), the triumphal entry (9:9), "they shall
look upon me whom they have pierced" (12:10), the shepherd
struck (13:7), and the LORD's return to the Mount of Olives
(14:4).

Classification: Prophetic Vision — The Four Chariots and the
Crowning of Joshua as the Branch
Key Themes: Divine sovereignty over the nations, the four
spirits/winds of heaven executing judgment, the Branch as
priest-king, the union of priestly and royal offices in one
Person, those "far off" joining in building God's temple

Historical Context: The eighth and final night vision shows
four chariots emerging from between two bronze mountains. These
are "the four spirits of the heavens" — divine agents of
judgment patrolling and pacifying the earth. The vision
bookends with vision one (the horsemen): in chapter 1, the
earth is at rest while Israel suffers; now God's agents bring
divine rest by executing judgment on the nations.

The crowning of Joshua (vv.9-15) is the theological climax of
the vision section. A PRIEST receives a CROWN — an act
unprecedented in Israel (priests wore turbans, kings wore
crowns). This symbolic act declares that the coming "Branch"
will unite the offices of priest and king in one Person. He
will "build the temple of the LORD" (the true Temple — Christ's
body and the church) and "the counsel of peace shall be between
them both" — between the priestly and kingly offices, reconciled
in Christ alone.

Structure:
- The Eighth Vision: Four Chariots from Bronze Mountains (vv.1-3)
- The Interpretation: Four Spirits of Heaven (vv.4-8)
- The Symbolic Crowning of Joshua (vv.9-11)
- The Branch Prophecy: Priest-King (vv.12-13)
- The Crown as a Memorial (v.14)
- Those Far Off Shall Build (v.15)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.5  — "These are the four spirits of the heavens, which go forth
        from standing before the Lord of all the earth" (KJV) —
        Divine agents dispatched from God's throne to execute His
        will in the nations. God is not passive in history. He
        actively governs through heavenly agents who stand in His
        presence and go forth at His command.

v.8  — "these that go toward the north country have quieted my
        spirit in the north country" (KJV) / "have set my Spirit
        at rest in the north country" (ESV) — God's wrath toward
        Babylon (the north) is satisfied. His Spirit finds rest
        when justice is executed. Divine judgment brings divine
        peace.

v.12 — "Behold the man whose name is The BRANCH; and he shall
        grow up out of his place, and he shall build the temple
        of the LORD" (KJV) — The second Branch prophecy in
        Zechariah (cf. 3:8). "Behold the man" echoes Pilate's
        words in John 19:5 ("Ecce homo"). The Branch builds the
        TRUE temple — not stone but the body of Christ and His
        church (Eph 2:19-22).

v.13 — "he shall bear the glory, and shall sit and rule upon his
        throne; and he shall be a priest upon his throne: and the
        counsel of peace shall be between them both" (KJV) — The
        most extraordinary statement: ONE person unites king and
        priest. In OT Israel these were strictly separate (Uzziah
        was struck with leprosy for attempting priestly duties,
        2 Chr 26:16-21). Only the Messiah can hold both offices.
        Christ is our King AND our Priest — and there is PEACE
        between these roles in Him.

v.15 — "they that are far off shall come and build in the temple
        of the LORD" (KJV) — Gentile inclusion. Those "far off"
        (Ephesians 2:13) — the nations, outsiders — will
        participate in building God's house. The church is built
        by Jew AND Gentile together.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1 — "mountains of brass" (KJV) / "mountains of bronze" (ESV) —
      Hebrew "nekhoshet" = bronze/copper. Bronze represents divine
      judgment throughout Scripture (the bronze altar, the bronze
      serpent). These mountains are the gates of God's court of
      justice, from which judgment proceeds.

v.3 — "grisled and bay" (KJV) / "dappled... strong" (ESV) —
      Hebrew "beruddim amutsim." KJV takes these as two
      descriptions of the fourth team; ESV applies "strong" as a
      quality of all the horses. The colors may represent different
      aspects of divine judgment.

v.12 — "The BRANCH" — Hebrew "tsemach" (same as 3:8). "He shall
       grow up out of his place" — "branch out from his place"
       (ESV). He rises from apparent insignificance to universal
       rule.

v.13 — "the counsel of peace shall be between them both" —
       "Them both" likely refers to the two offices (priest and
       king) unified in one Person without conflict. Alternately:
       between the Branch and the LORD. Either way, perfect
       harmony between authority and intercession in the Messiah.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. God is sovereign over all nations (vv.1-8). The chariots go
   north, south, east — covering the entire earth. No empire is
   beyond God's reach. No political power operates outside His
   governance. Whatever threatens you, God has agents already
   dispatched to deal with it.

2. Christ is both your King AND your Priest (v.13). He rules
   with authority AND intercedes with compassion. He commands
   AND He understands. You are governed by a ruler who also
   represents you before God. No tension — only peace between
   His throne and His altar.

3. "Those far off shall come and build" (v.15). If you feel like
   an outsider — far from God, far from the inner circle, far
   from worthiness — hear this: you are INVITED to build. The
   "far off" ones are included. In Christ, there are no outsiders.

4. God's Spirit finds "rest" when justice is done (v.8). Justice
   is not optional in God's economy — it is what brings Him peace.
   Work for justice. Pursue righteousness. When wrongs are righted,
   God's Spirit rests.
