Deuteronomy - Chapter 7
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God commands Israel to completely destroy the Canaanite nations and their idols, reminding them they are chosen not for size but for love.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses. Deuteronomy is the fifth and final book of the Pentateuch,
written at the end of the 40 years of wilderness wandering (approximately
1406 BC). Hebrew title: "Devarim" — "Words/Things." Greek title:
"Deuteronomion" — "Second Law" (a repetition/expansion of the Law for the
new generation). The book consists of Moses' farewell speeches to Israel on
the plains of Moab, just before they enter the Promised Land. Moses will
not enter with them — these are his final words to the nation he has led
for 40 years.

Historical Context: Chapter 7 addresses the most pressing practical
question facing Israel as they prepare to enter Canaan: how to deal with
the nations already there. God's command is total separation — no
covenants, no intermarriage, complete destruction of their worship sites.
The reason is not ethnic hatred but spiritual survival: "they will turn
away thy son from following me" (v.4). Moses then reveals the theological
basis for Israel's election: God chose them not because they were great
(they were the fewest of all peoples, v.7) but because He loved them and
kept His oath to their fathers (v.8). This is pure grace — unconditional,
unmerited election. The chapter balances severity (destroy the nations)
with tenderness (God loves you, chose you, will bless you). It also
addresses the fear of facing nations "greater and mightier" — remember what
God did to Egypt (vv.17-19). The conquest will be gradual ("little by
little," v.22) but certain.

The Command of Separation (vv.1-5): Seven nations listed — Hittites,
Girgashites, Amorites, Canaanites, Perizzites, Hivites, Jebusites — all
"greater and mightier" than Israel (v.1). When God delivers them: utterly
destroy them, make no covenant, show no mercy (v.2). No intermarriage
(v.3) — the reason: "they will turn away thy son from following me, that
they may serve other gods" (v.4). Destroy their religious infrastructure:
altars, pillars, Asherim/groves, carved images (v.5). The prohibition is
not against the people as such but against the spiritual contamination they
represent.

Israel's Election by Grace (vv.6-11): "Thou art an holy people unto the
LORD thy God" (v.6) — set apart, consecrated. God "hath chosen thee to be
a special people unto himself" (KJV) / "a people for his treasured
possession" (ESV) (v.6). Why? Not because of size — "ye were the fewest of
all people" (v.7). But "because the LORD loved you, and because he would
keep the oath which he had sworn unto your fathers" (v.8). God's love is
the sole reason for election. He is "the faithful God, which keepeth
covenant and mercy... to a thousand generations" (v.9). But He also repays
those who hate Him "to their face" (v.10). Faithfulness and justice
together.

Blessings of Obedience (vv.12-16): If they obey, God will keep His
covenant love (v.12). He will love, bless, and multiply them (v.13). Bless
their children, crops, herds (v.13). "Blessed above all people" — no
barrenness among them or their livestock (v.14). No diseases of Egypt
(v.15). They shall consume the nations God delivers — "thine eye shall have
no pity upon them" (v.16) — because their gods would be "a snare."

Do Not Fear — Remember Egypt (vv.17-24): If they think "These nations are
more than I; how can I dispossess them?" (v.17) — do not fear. Remember
what God did to Pharaoh: the trials, signs, wonders, mighty hand,
outstretched arm (vv.18-19). God will do the same to these nations. He
will send hornets (v.20). "The LORD thy God is among you, a mighty God and
terrible" (KJV) / "a great and awesome God" (ESV) (v.21). The conquest
will be "little by little" (v.22) — not all at once, lest wild beasts
multiply. But it will be complete: "there shall no man be able to stand
before thee" (v.24).

Destroy the Idols Completely (vv.25-26): Burn the images. Do not covet the
gold or silver on them — "lest thou be snared therein: for it is an
abomination" (v.25). Do not bring any abomination into your house — "lest
thou be a cursed thing like it" (KJV) / "become devoted to destruction
like it" (ESV) (v.26). "Utterly detest it, and utterly abhor it" — double
emphasis on complete rejection.

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------
Enduring Word (David Guzik):
  https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/deuteronomy-7/
  Guzik emphasizes that Israel's election was entirely by grace — the
  fewest, not the greatest. He notes that the command to destroy the
  nations was not ethnic cleansing but spiritual quarantine: the issue was
  always idolatry, not ethnicity. He highlights "little by little" as
  God's typical method — gradual sanctification, not instant perfection.

Charles Spurgeon:
  "God loved you — not because you were great, not because you were good,
  not because you were many. He loved you because He loved you. That is
  the only answer grace can give. Why does God love sinners? Because He is
  love. There is no reason in us — the reason is entirely in Him. And
  that is the safest ground for assurance: His love depends not on our
  worthiness but on His character."

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Ye were the fewest of all people" (v.7). God did not choose Israel
   because they were impressive. He does not choose you because you are
   worthy. Election is grace — unmerited, unexplained by anything in the
   recipient. This humbles pride and secures assurance.

2. "Because the LORD loved you" (v.8). The only reason for God's choice is
   God's love. Not your performance, not your potential, not your size.
   His love is the cause, not the reward, of election.

3. "They will turn away thy son" (v.4). The prohibition of intermarriage
   is not about ethnicity but about spiritual influence. Intimate
   relationships with those who worship other gods will inevitably pull
   the heart away. Guard your closest relationships.

4. "Little by little" (v.22). God often works gradually. Sanctification is
   usually progressive, not instantaneous. Do not be discouraged by slow
   progress — God is working "little by little" to drive out what does not
   belong in your life.

5. "Utterly detest it, and utterly abhor it" (v.26). Half-measures with
   sin are fatal. You cannot keep the idol's gold while destroying the
   idol. Complete rejection — not negotiation — is the only safe response
   to what God has declared abominable.

6. "The faithful God, which keepeth covenant... to a thousand generations"
   (v.9). God's faithfulness is not measured in days or years but in
   millennia. His covenant love outlasts every human failure. A thousand
   generations — He will still be faithful.
