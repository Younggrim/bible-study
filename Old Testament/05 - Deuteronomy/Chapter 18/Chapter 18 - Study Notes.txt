Deuteronomy - Chapter 18
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God provides for the Levites, forbids occult practices, and promises a Prophet like Moses whom Israel must hear — ultimately fulfilled in Christ.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses. Deuteronomy is the fifth and final book of the Pentateuch,
written at the end of the 40 years of wilderness wandering (approximately
1406 BC). Hebrew title: "Devarim" — "Words/Things." Greek title:
"Deuteronomion" — "Second Law" (a repetition/expansion of the Law for the
new generation). The book consists of Moses' farewell speeches to Israel on
the plains of Moab, just before they enter the Promised Land. Moses will
not enter with them — these are his final words to the nation he has led
for 40 years.

Historical Context: Chapter 18 is one of the most theologically
significant chapters in Deuteronomy, containing the great Messianic
prophecy of "the Prophet like Moses" (vv.15-19). The chapter addresses
three topics: (1) provision for the Levitical priests (vv.1-8), (2)
prohibition of occult practices (vv.9-14), and (3) the promise of the
Prophet to come (vv.15-22). The logic flows naturally: the Levites serve
as God's ministers (vv.1-8); the nations consult demonic sources for
guidance (vv.9-14); but Israel will receive divine guidance through a
Prophet whom God Himself will raise up (vv.15-19). The occult practices
listed (vv.10-11) represent the full range of pagan attempts to access
supernatural knowledge: child sacrifice, divination, fortune-telling,
interpreting omens, sorcery, charming, consulting mediums, wizardry, and
necromancy (consulting the dead). These are not merely forbidden — they
are the very reason God is driving out the Canaanites (v.12). Israel's
alternative is not ignorance but revelation: God will speak through His
Prophet. The prophecy of vv.15-18 is explicitly identified as fulfilled in
Christ (Acts 3:22-23; 7:37). Jesus is THE Prophet like Moses — a mediator
between God and man, who speaks God's words with God's authority. The
chapter closes with the test of a prophet: if his word does not come to
pass, he has spoken presumptuously (vv.21-22). Combined with chapter 13
(the message must align with God's revealed truth), this gives Israel two
tests: (1) Does the message lead to God? (2) Does the prediction come
true?

Provision for Levitical Priests (vv.1-8): "The priests the Levites, and
all the tribe of Levi, shall have no part nor inheritance with Israel"
(v.1). They have no land allotment. Their provision: "they shall eat the
offerings of the LORD made by fire, and his inheritance" (v.1). "The LORD
is their inheritance" (v.2). The priests' due from sacrifices: "the
shoulder, and the two cheeks, and the maw" (KJV) / "the shoulder and the
two cheeks and the stomach" (ESV) (v.3). Also: firstfruits of grain, wine,
oil, and the first fleece of sheep (v.4). Why? "For the LORD thy God hath
chosen him out of all thy tribes, to stand to minister in the name of the
LORD, him and his sons for ever" (v.5). A Levite from any town may come to
the central sanctuary and minister with equal rights (vv.6-8) — he
receives equal portions "beside that which cometh of the sale of his
patrimony" (v.8).

Forbidden Occult Practices (vv.9-14): "Thou shalt not learn to do after
the abominations of those nations" (v.9). The comprehensive list: (1)
"maketh his son or his daughter to pass through the fire" — child
sacrifice (v.10); (2) "useth divination" — seeking hidden knowledge
through rituals (v.10); (3) "observer of times" (KJV) / "tells fortunes"
(ESV) — reading signs/omens (v.10); (4) "enchanter" (KJV) / "interprets
omens" (ESV) (v.10); (5) "witch" (KJV) / "sorcerer" (ESV) (v.10); (6)
"charmer" — one who binds with spells (v.11); (7) "consulter with familiar
spirits" (KJV) / "medium" (ESV) (v.11); (8) "wizard" (KJV) / "necromancer"
(ESV) (v.11); (9) one who "inquires of the dead" (v.11). "For all that do
these things are an abomination unto the LORD" (v.12). This is WHY God
drives out the Canaanites — "because of these abominations" (v.12). "Thou
shalt be perfect with the LORD thy God" (KJV) / "You shall be blameless
before the LORD your God" (ESV) (v.13). The nations listen to diviners;
"but as for thee, the LORD thy God hath not suffered thee so to do" (v.14).

THE PROPHET LIKE MOSES (vv.15-19): "The LORD thy God will raise up unto
thee a Prophet from the midst of thee, of thy brethren, like unto me; unto
him ye shall hearken" (v.15). This is Moses speaking — the Prophet will be
like Moses: (1) from among Israel ("thy brethren"), (2) a mediator between
God and man, (3) speaking God's words with God's authority. Background:
at Horeb/Sinai, the people feared God's direct voice and asked for a
mediator (v.16). God approved: "They have well spoken" (v.17). God's
promise: "I will raise them up a Prophet from among their brethren, like
unto thee, and will put my words in his mouth; and he shall speak unto
them all that I shall command him" (v.18). Warning: "whosoever will not
hearken unto my words which he shall speak in my name, I will require it
of him" (v.19). Rejecting this Prophet brings God's judgment.

Testing a Prophet (vv.20-22): Two categories of false prophets: (1) one
who "presume to speak a word in my name, which I have not commanded him"
(v.20); (2) one who "shall speak in the name of other gods" (v.20). Both
shall die. The test: "When a prophet speaketh in the name of the LORD, if
the thing follow not, nor come to pass, that is the thing which the LORD
hath not spoken" (v.22). The prophet spoke presumptuously. "Thou shalt not
be afraid of him" (v.22) — do not fear his threats or be intimidated by
his claims.

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Moses speaks from the plains of Moab. The Levites "shall have no part nor inheritance with Israel" (v.1) — they are scattered throughout the land in 48 cities (cf. Numbers 35).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.2  — "The LORD is their inheritance." (ESV) — The Levites' portion is God
        Himself. They have no land because they have something better:
        God as their inheritance. The highest privilege.

v.10-11 — "There shall not be found among you any one that makes his son
        or his daughter to pass through the fire, or that useth divination,
        or an observer of times, or an enchanter, or a witch, Or a charmer,
        or a consulter with familiar spirits, or a wizard, or a
        necromancer." (ESV) — The comprehensive prohibition of all occult
        practices. Nine categories covering every form of pagan
        supernatural consultation.

v.12 — "For all that do these things are an abomination to the LORD:
        and because of these abominations the LORD your God does drive them
        out from before you." (ESV) — Occult practices are not merely forbidden
        but are the cause of divine judgment on nations.

v.15 — "The LORD your God will raise up to you a Prophet from the midst
        of you, of your brethren, like to me; to him you shall
        listen." (ESV) — THE great Messianic prophecy. The Prophet like Moses.
        Fulfilled in Christ (Acts 3:22; 7:37).

v.18 — "I will raise them up a Prophet from among their brethren, like
        to you, and will put my words in his mouth; and he shall speak
        to them all that I shall command him." (ESV) — God's own words
        confirming the promise. The Prophet speaks God's words with God's
        authority.

v.19 — "Whosoever will not listen to my words which he shall speak in
        my name, I will require it of him." (ESV) — Rejecting the Prophet =
        rejecting God. God Himself holds accountable those who refuse to
        listen.

v.22 — "When a prophet speaks in the name of the LORD, if the thing
        follow not, nor come to pass, that is the thing which the LORD
        has not spoken." (ESV) — The test of fulfillment. Failed predictions
        expose false prophets.

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------
Enduring Word (David Guzik):
  https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/deuteronomy-18/
  Guzik identifies the Prophet of v.15 as ultimately fulfilled in Christ,
  noting that while there were intermediate prophets (Joshua, Samuel,
  Elijah, etc.), none was "like Moses" until Jesus — who was a mediator,
  lawgiver, deliverer, and spoke face-to-face with God. He emphasizes that
  the occult prohibition is absolute: Israel's alternative to divination
  is not ignorance but divine revelation through the Prophet.

Charles Spurgeon:
  "'I will raise them up a Prophet like unto thee.' Here is the great
  promise of Christ in the Old Testament. Moses was the mediator of the
  old covenant; Christ is the mediator of the new. Moses spoke God's words
  to the people; Christ IS the Word of God to the people. Moses delivered
  from Egypt; Christ delivers from sin. Moses was faithful in God's house
  as a servant; Christ is faithful as the Son over His own house
  (Hebrews 3:5-6). Every prophet pointed forward to this Prophet — the
  final, complete, perfect revelation of God to man."

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1  — KJV: "the offerings of the LORD made by fire, and his inheritance"
       / ESV: "the LORD's food offerings as their inheritance" — Hebrew
       "ishei YHWH v'nachalato." "Ishei" = fire offerings. The Levites
       eat from what is offered to God on the altar — God shares His
       table with His ministers.

v.3  — KJV: "the maw" / ESV: "the stomach" — Hebrew "ha-qevah." The
       fourth stomach of a ruminant animal. KJV's archaic "maw" = stomach.
       These portions (shoulder, cheeks, stomach) were the priest's share
       from every sacrifice.

v.10 — KJV: "observer of times" / ESV: "tells fortunes" — Hebrew
       "m'onen." Uncertain etymology. Possibly from "anan" (cloud) =
       reading clouds/weather signs, or from "ayin" (eye) = casting the
       evil eye. KJV and ESV interpret differently; both refer to
       forbidden divination practices.

v.10 — KJV: "a witch" / ESV: "a sorcerer" — Hebrew "m'khashef." One who
       uses incantations, potions, or rituals to manipulate supernatural
       forces. KJV's "witch" carries later English connotations; ESV's
       "sorcerer" is more neutral. Same word as Exodus 22:18: "Thou shalt
       not suffer a witch to live."

v.11 — KJV: "a consulter with familiar spirits" / ESV: "a medium" —
       Hebrew "sho'el ov." "Ov" = a spirit, possibly a pit-spirit or
       ancestral ghost. One who consults such spirits for information.
       KJV's "familiar spirits" = spirits supposedly familiar with the
       dead. ESV's "medium" = the modern equivalent term.

v.13 — KJV: "Thou shalt be perfect with the LORD thy God" / ESV: "You
       shall be blameless before the LORD your God" — Hebrew "tamim tihyeh
       im YHWH elohekha." "Tamim" = complete, whole, blameless, without
       defect. Not sinless perfection but wholehearted devotion — undivided
       loyalty. Same word used of Noah (Genesis 6:9) and Abraham
       (Genesis 17:1).

v.15 — KJV: "a Prophet from the midst of thee, of thy brethren, like
       unto me" / ESV: "a prophet like me from among you, from your
       brothers" — Hebrew "navi mi-qirb'kha me-acheicha kamoni." Three
       qualifications: (1) "from your midst" — from Israel; (2) "from
       your brothers" — an Israelite; (3) "like me" — like Moses in
       function and authority. Fulfilled in Christ alone.

v.18 — KJV: "I will put my words in his mouth" / ESV: "I will put my
       words in his mouth" — Hebrew "v'natati d'varai b'fiv." God's words
       in the Prophet's mouth. This is the definition of prophetic
       inspiration: God's words spoken through a human vessel. Perfectly
       fulfilled in Christ (John 12:49).

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
The Prophet Like Moses — The Messianic figure promised in vv.15-18. Like
               Moses in being: (1) an Israelite, (2) a mediator between
               God and man, (3) a speaker of God's words, (4) a deliverer.
               Fulfilled in Jesus Christ (Acts 3:22; 7:37). The ultimate
               and final Prophet.

Divination     — Hebrew "qesem" — the practice of seeking hidden knowledge
               through occult means (casting lots, reading entrails,
               interpreting signs). Forbidden because it bypasses God's
               chosen means of revelation (the Prophet/the Word).

Necromancy     — Hebrew "doresh el ha-metim" — "one who inquires of the
               dead." Attempting to communicate with deceased persons for
               information or guidance. Forbidden absolutely. The dead
               cannot help the living (Isaiah 8:19).

Medium         — Hebrew "ov" — one who claims to channel spirits of the
               dead. The medium at Endor (1 Samuel 28) is the most famous
               biblical example. Saul's consultation of her was a
               covenant-breaking act that sealed his judgment.

Familiar Spirit — KJV term for "ov" — a spirit supposedly familiar with
               or connected to the dead. The term implies an ongoing
               relationship between the medium and the spirit entity.

Blameless/Perfect — Hebrew "tamim" — wholeness, completeness, undivided
               devotion. Not sinless perfection but wholehearted loyalty
               to God without mixture of occult practices. The opposite
               of divided allegiance between God and demonic sources.

Patrimony      — Hebrew "mimkarav al ha-avot" — property inherited from
               fathers. A Levite who sells family property and moves to
               the central sanctuary retains those proceeds in addition
               to his priestly portions.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "The LORD is their inheritance" (v.2). The Levites had no land because
   they had God Himself. This is the highest privilege, not a deprivation.
   Under the new covenant, every believer can say: "The LORD is my
   portion" (Lamentations 3:24). God Himself is better than any earthly
   inheritance.

2. The comprehensive occult prohibition (vv.10-11). Nine categories cover
   every possible form of supernatural consultation outside of God. This
   includes modern equivalents: horoscopes, tarot cards, psychics, séances,
   Ouija boards, and any attempt to access spiritual knowledge apart from
   God's word. The prohibition is absolute — not "be careful" but "there
   shall not be found among you."

3. "Because of these abominations the LORD thy God doth drive them out"
   (v.12). Occult practices are not harmless curiosity — they are the
   reason God judges nations. What destroyed the Canaanites will destroy
   anyone who practices these things. God's patience has limits.

4. The Prophet like Moses (vv.15-18). Israel's alternative to the occult
   is not ignorance but revelation. God does not leave His people without
   guidance — He sends His Prophet. In Christ, we have the final, complete
   word from God (Hebrews 1:1-2). We need no other source of spiritual
   knowledge.

5. "I will require it of him" (v.19). Rejecting Christ is not neutral —
   it brings God's judgment. The Prophet speaks God's words; refusing to
   listen is refusing God Himself. This is the most serious warning in
   Scripture: "How shall we escape, if we neglect so great salvation?"
   (Hebrews 2:3).

6. Two tests of a prophet: (1) Does his message lead to God? (chapter 13);
   (2) Does his prediction come true? (v.22). Both must be satisfied. A
   prophet whose signs come true but whose message leads astray is false
   (13:1-3). A prophet whose message sounds right but whose predictions
   fail is also false (18:22). Truth and fulfillment together.
