Psalms - Chapter 96
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
An enthronement hymn — 'O sing unto the LORD a new song; sing unto the LORD, all the earth'; declare His glory among the nations; let creation rejoice, for the LORD comes to judge the earth with righteousness.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Multiple authors — primarily David (73 psalms attributed),
plus Asaph (12), Sons of Korah (11), Solomon (2), Moses (1),
Heman (1), Ethan (1), and anonymous. The Psalter was compiled over
approximately 1000 years and served as Israel's hymnal and prayer
book. The book is divided into five 'books' (1-41, 42-72, 73-89,
90-106, 107-150), paralleling the five books of Moses. Key themes:
worship, lament, praise, trust, kingship, creation, wisdom,
Messianic prophecy, and the full range of human emotion brought
before God.

Classification: Enthronement / Hymn of Praise
Attributed Author: Anonymous (cf. 1 Chronicles 16:23-33 — Davidic)
Key Themes: A new song, declaring God's glory among the nations,
the LORD above all gods, worship in holiness, creation rejoicing,
God coming to judge the earth

Historical Context: This psalm appears (with variations) in
1 Chronicles 16:23-33 as part of the song David commissioned when
the Ark was brought to Jerusalem. It is one of the "enthronement
psalms" proclaiming God's kingship. The call to sing a "new song"
indicates a fresh experience of God's saving power requiring
fresh worship. The missionary scope is remarkable: "Declare his
glory among the nations" (v.3), "all the earth" (v.1), "all
peoples" (v.3). The psalm ends with the most comprehensive call
to creation worship in Scripture — heavens, earth, sea, fields,
and trees all rejoicing because God comes to judge.

Structure:
- Call to Sing a New Song (vv.1-3)
- The LORD Above All Gods (vv.4-6)
- Ascribe Glory to the LORD (vv.7-9)
- The LORD Reigns (v.10)
- All Creation Rejoices at His Coming (vv.11-13)


KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "O sing unto the LORD a new song: sing unto the LORD, all
        the earth." (KJV) — "New song" — not repetitive ritual but
        fresh expression matching fresh experience. "All the earth"
        — not just Israel. Worship is for EVERYONE.

v.5  — "For all the gods of the nations are idols: but the LORD
        made the heavens." (KJV) — The devastating comparison.
        Other "gods" are idols (Hebrew "elilim" = worthless
        nothings). The LORD made HEAVENS. They made nothing; He
        made everything. Incomparable.

v.10 — "Say among the heathen that the LORD reigneth." (KJV) —
        The missionary commission: DECLARE God's kingship to the
        nations. Don't keep this truth private. The nations need
        to hear: there is a King, He is just, and He is coming.

v.13 — "For he cometh, for he cometh to judge the earth: he shall
        judge the world with righteousness, and the people with his
        truth." (KJV) — God's coming is cause for JOY, not terror
        (for creation). His judgment is righteous and truthful —
        under His rule, all wrongs are set right.

CROSS-REFERENCES
------------------------------------
v.1   — 1 Chronicles 16:23-33 — The parallel passage from David's
         Ark-of-the-Covenant celebration.
v.1   — Revelation 5:9 — "Thou art worthy... for thou wast slain,
         and hast redeemed us... out of every... nation." The new
         song of the Lamb.
v.5   — Isaiah 44:9-10 — "They that make a graven image are all of

v.13  — Revelation 22:20 — "Even so, come, Lord Jesus." The
         anticipation of His coming.

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.5 — KJV: "idols" / ESV: "worthless idols" — Hebrew "elilim" =
      literally "nothings" or "worthless things." A wordplay on
      "elohim" (God/gods). The gods of the nations are not
      "elohim" but "elilim" — not gods but nothings.

v.9 — KJV: "worship the LORD in the beauty of holiness" / ESV:
      "Worship the LORD in the splendor of holiness" — "Beauty of
      holiness" could mean holy attire (priestly garments) or the
      inherent beauty/splendor of holiness itself. Either way:
      worship requires holiness, and holiness is beautiful.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
- v.1 — ASV: "sing unto Jehovah a new song" / WEB: "Sing to Yahweh
  a new song!" / NET: "Sing to the Lord a new song" — Divine name
  variation across all three. WEB adds an exclamation mark capturing
  the psalm's exuberant tone.

- v.3 — KJV: "among the heathen" / ASV: "among the nations" / WEB:
  "among the nations" / NET: "Tell the nations about his splendor"
  — KJV's "heathen" carries negative connotations; ASV/WEB's
  "nations" (Hebrew "goyim") is neutral. NET restructures the
  sentence from "declare" to "Tell... about."

- v.5 — KJV: "all the gods of the nations are idols" / ASV: "all the
  gods of the peoples are idols" / WEB: "all the gods of the peoples
  are idols" / NET: "all the gods of the nations are worthless" —
  NET renders "elilim" as "worthless" rather than "idols," preserving
  the Hebrew wordplay on "elohim/elilim" (gods/worthless-nothings).

- v.5 — KJV: "the LORD made the heavens" / NET: "the Lord made the
  sky" — NET uses "sky" instead of "heavens" (same Hebrew word
  "shamayim"). This loses the theological weight of "heavens" but
  may be more natural in modern English.

- v.9 — KJV: "in the beauty of holiness" / ASV: "in holy array" /
  NET: "in holy attire" / WEB: "in holy array" — ASV/WEB/NET all
  interpret "hadrat-qodesh" as referring to sacred garments (priestly
  vestments) rather than KJV's more abstract "beauty of holiness."

- v.10 — KJV: "he shall judge the people righteously" / ASV: "He
  will judge the peoples with equity" / NET: "He judges the nations
  fairly" — ASV uses "equity" (Hebrew "mesharim"); NET paraphrases
  as "fairly." KJV's "righteously" and ASV's "equity" are different
  but complementary concepts in the Hebrew.

- v.13 — KJV: "the people with his truth" / ASV: "the peoples with
  his truth" / WEB: "the peoples with his truth" / NET: "the nations
  in accordance with his justice" — NET replaces "truth" (emunah =
  faithfulness/truth) with "justice" — a significant interpretive
  shift in the final declaration of the psalm.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. God deserves new songs (v.1). Don't let worship become routine.
   Fresh experiences of God call for fresh expressions of praise.
   Sing something new — write something new — let your worship
   match the freshness of His mercies.

2. The gods of the nations are worthless nothings (v.5). Whatever
   competes with God for your worship — career, comfort, approval,
   money — is "elilim." Literally nothing. The LORD made the
   heavens. Nothing else compares. Stop worshipping nothings.

3. God's coming judgment is good news for creation (v.13). Trees
   sing, fields exult, seas roar with JOY — because when God
   judges, He sets things RIGHT. Judgment is not destruction but
   restoration. The earth longs for it.
