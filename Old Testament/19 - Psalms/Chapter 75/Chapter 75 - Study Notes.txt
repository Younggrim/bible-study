Psalms - Chapter 75
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
A psalm of judgment — God declares 'When I select an appointed time, I will judge uprightly'; promotion comes not from east, west, or south, but God puts down one and lifts up another; the wicked will drink His cup.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Multiple authors — primarily David (73 psalms attributed),
plus Asaph (12), Sons of Korah (11), Solomon (2), Moses (1),
Heman (1), Ethan (1), and anonymous. The Psalter was compiled over
approximately 1000 years and served as Israel's hymnal and prayer
book. The book is divided into five 'books' (1-41, 42-72, 73-89,
90-106, 107-150), paralleling the five books of Moses. Key themes:
worship, lament, praise, trust, kingship, creation, wisdom,
Messianic prophecy, and the full range of human emotion brought
before God.

Classification: Thanksgiving / Prophetic Psalm
Attributed Author: Asaph
Key Themes: God as sovereign judge, divine timing ("the set time"),
the cup of God's wrath, God alone exalts and brings down, warning
against pride

Historical Context: This psalm is set to "Do Not Destroy" (Al
Tashheth) and attributed to Asaph. It appears to celebrate a
specific national deliverance (possibly Sennacherib's defeat in
701 BC — 2 Kings 19:35 — or another military deliverance). The
psalm contains divine speech (vv.2-5) where God Himself declares
His sovereignty over judgment and timing. The "horn" imagery
(vv.4-5,10) represents power and pride — the wicked lift their
horn arrogantly; God will cut it off. The cup of wrath (v.8)
becomes a major biblical image — from Jeremiah to Revelation to
Christ's prayer in Gethsemane.

Structure:
- Thanksgiving for God's Nearness (v.1)
- God Speaks: I Will Judge at the Set Time (vv.2-5)
- Human Commentary: God Alone Exalts (vv.6-8)
- The Psalmist's Declaration (vv.9-10)


KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.2  — "When I shall receive the congregation I will judge
        uprightly." (KJV) / ESV: "At the set time that I appoint
        I will judge with equity." — God's timing is sovereign.
        He has appointed a "set time" for judgment. Delayed
        justice is not denied justice — God has a schedule.

v.6-7 — "For promotion cometh neither from the east, nor from the
        west, nor from the south. But God is the judge: he putteth
        down one, and setteth up another." (KJV) — Human promotion
        has no earthly explanation — it comes from GOD. He elevates
        and He humbles. Political power, career advancement, social
        standing — all ultimately from His hand.

v.8  — "For in the hand of the LORD there is a cup, and the wine
        is red; it is full of mixture; and he poureth out of the
        same: but the dregs thereof, all the wicked of the earth
        shall wring them out, and drink them." (KJV) — The cup of
        God's wrath. Fully mixed (potent), poured for the wicked.
        They must drink it to the dregs — every last drop of
        judgment. This image echoes through Scripture to Christ in
        Gethsemane.

v.10 — "All the horns of the wicked also will I cut off; but the
        horns of the righteous shall be exalted." (KJV) — The
        final word: wicked power ("horns") will be cut; righteous
        power will be raised. God's judgment reverses all wrongful
        power structures.

CROSS-REFERENCES
------------------------------------
v.2   — Acts 17:31 — "He hath appointed a day, in the which he
         will judge the world in righteousness." The NT "set time."
v.6   — Daniel 2:21 — "He removeth kings, and setteth up kings."
         Same sovereign authority over human promotion.
v.8   — Jeremiah 25:15 — "Take the wine cup of this fury at my
         hand, and cause all the nations... to drink it."
v.8   — Matthew 26:39 — "O my Father, if it be possible, let this
         cup pass from me." Christ drank the cup meant for us.
v.8   — Revelation 14:10 — "He shall drink of the wine of the
         wrath of God, which is poured out without mixture." The
         final cup.
v.10  — 1 Samuel 2:7-8 — "The LORD... bringeth low, and lifteth
         up." Hannah's song: same theme of divine reversal.

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "thy name is near thy wondrous works declare" / ESV:
      "your name is near. We recount your wondrous deeds" — The
      Hebrew is compressed. "Your name is near" = God's manifest
      presence is close. His wondrous works are the evidence of
      His proximity.

v.2 — KJV: "When I shall receive the congregation" / ESV: "At the
      set time that I appoint" — Hebrew "mo'ed" = appointed time,
      set festival, designated moment. ESV is clearer: God has a
      calendar for judgment. He will act at exactly the right
      moment.

v.6 — KJV: "promotion" / ESV: "lifting up" — Hebrew "harim" = to
      lift, raise, exalt. "Promotion" (KJV) narrows the meaning;
      "lifting up" (ESV) preserves the broader sense of any form
      of elevation or exaltation.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. God has a set time for judgment (v.2). He is not slow — He is
   precise. If justice seems delayed, remember: God has an
   appointed moment. Your timing is not His timing, and His is
   always right.

2. All promotion comes from God alone (vv.6-7). Not from geography
   ("east, west, south"), not from human connections, not from
   self-promotion. God puts down and lifts up. This liberates you
   from political maneuvering — trust Him to promote you in His
   time.

3. The cup of wrath is real and inescapable (v.8). Every wicked
   person will drink it to the dregs. But Christ drank it on our
   behalf in Gethsemane and at Calvary. The cup we deserved, He
   consumed.

4. Pride ("lifting the horn") invites God's cutting (v.10). Don't
   boast. Don't exalt yourself. God opposes the proud. The horns
   you lift in arrogance are the same horns He cuts off in
   judgment.
