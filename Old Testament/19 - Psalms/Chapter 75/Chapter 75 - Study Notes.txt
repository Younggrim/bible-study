Psalms - Chapter 75
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
A psalm of judgment — God declares 'When I select an appointed time, I will judge uprightly'; promotion comes not from east, west, or south, but God puts down one and lifts up another; the wicked will drink His cup.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Multiple authors — primarily David (73 psalms attributed),
plus Asaph (12), Sons of Korah (11), Solomon (2), Moses (1),
Heman (1), Ethan (1), and anonymous. The Psalter was compiled over
approximately 1000 years and served as Israel's hymnal and prayer
book. The book is divided into five 'books' (1-41, 42-72, 73-89,
90-106, 107-150), paralleling the five books of Moses. Key themes:
worship, lament, praise, trust, kingship, creation, wisdom,
Messianic prophecy, and the full range of human emotion brought
before God.

Classification: Thanksgiving / Prophetic Psalm
Attributed Author: Asaph
Key Themes: God as sovereign judge, divine timing ("the set time"),
the cup of God's wrath, God alone exalts and brings down, warning
against pride

Historical Context: This psalm is set to "Do Not Destroy" (Al
Tashheth) and attributed to Asaph. It appears to celebrate a
specific national deliverance (possibly Sennacherib's defeat in
701 BC — 2 Kings 19:35 — or another military deliverance). The
psalm contains divine speech (vv.2-5) where God Himself declares
His sovereignty over judgment and timing. The "horn" imagery
(vv.4-5,10) represents power and pride — the wicked lift their
horn arrogantly; God will cut it off. The cup of wrath (v.8)
becomes a major biblical image — from Jeremiah to Revelation to
Christ's prayer in Gethsemane.

Structure:
- Thanksgiving for God's Nearness (v.1)
- God Speaks: I Will Judge at the Set Time (vv.2-5)
- Human Commentary: God Alone Exalts (vv.6-8)
- The Psalmist's Declaration (vv.9-10)

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. God has a set time for judgment (v.2). He is not slow — He is
   precise. If justice seems delayed, remember: God has an
   appointed moment. Your timing is not His timing, and His is
   always right.

2. All promotion comes from God alone (vv.6-7). Not from geography
   ("east, west, south"), not from human connections, not from
   self-promotion. God puts down and lifts up. This liberates you
   from political maneuvering — trust Him to promote you in His
   time.

3. The cup of wrath is real and inescapable (v.8). Every wicked
   person will drink it to the dregs. But Christ drank it on our
   behalf in Gethsemane and at Calvary. The cup we deserved, He
   consumed.

4. Pride ("lifting the horn") invites God's cutting (v.10). Don't
   boast. Don't exalt yourself. God opposes the proud. The horns
   you lift in arrogance are the same horns He cuts off in
   judgment.
