Psalms - Chapter 104
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
A creation psalm — a grand poetic celebration of God's wisdom in sustaining all creation: light, waters, mountains, springs, animals, the sea, and Leviathan — all look to God to give them food in due season.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Multiple authors — primarily David (73 psalms attributed),
plus Asaph (12), Sons of Korah (11), Solomon (2), Moses (1),
Heman (1), Ethan (1), and anonymous. The Psalter was compiled over
approximately 1000 years and served as Israel's hymnal and prayer
book. The book is divided into five 'books' (1-41, 42-72, 73-89,
90-106, 107-150), paralleling the five books of Moses. Key themes:
worship, lament, praise, trust, kingship, creation, wisdom,
Messianic prophecy, and the full range of human emotion brought
before God.

Classification: Creation Hymn / Nature Psalm
Attributed Author: Anonymous (no superscription)
Key Themes: God as Creator and Sustainer of all life, the majesty
of God in nature, divine providence over all creatures, the order
and beauty of creation, dependence of all life on God, praise
arising from contemplation of creation

Historical Context: Psalm 104 is the great "Creation Psalm" —
a poetic meditation on God's glory displayed in the natural world.
It follows Genesis 1 in rough order: light (v.2), sky/waters
(vv.2-4), land (vv.5-9), water/vegetation (vv.10-18), time
(vv.19-23), sea (vv.25-26). It is the companion to Psalm 103 —
both begin with "Bless the LORD, O my soul." Psalm 103 celebrates
God's grace toward people; Psalm 104 celebrates God's glory in
creation. Some scholars note parallels with the Egyptian Hymn to
Aten (Akhenaten), though the theology is radically different —
Psalm 104 celebrates the Creator, not the sun. This psalm has
been called "the most magnificent nature poem ever written."

Structure:
- God's Majesty in Light and Sky (vv.1-4)
- Foundation of the Earth and Waters (vv.5-9)
- Provision for All Creatures (vv.10-18)
- The Order of Day and Night (vv.19-23)
- Wonder at the Diversity of Creation (vv.24-26)
- All Life Depends on God (vv.27-30)
- Praise and Prayer (vv.31-35)


KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.2  — "Who coverest thyself with light as with a garment: who
        stretchest out the heavens like a curtain." (KJV) — God
        WEARS light. The heavens are His tent curtain. This is
        creation as divine clothing — what we see in the universe
        is merely what God has dressed Himself in.

v.24 — "O LORD, how manifold are thy works! in wisdom hast thou
        made them all: the earth is full of thy riches." (KJV) —
        The exclamation at the center of the psalm. Not just
        powerful, not just beautiful — made in WISDOM. Every
        ecosystem, every food chain, every cycle of day and night
        reflects divine intelligence.

v.27-28 — "These wait all upon thee; that thou mayest give them
        their meat in due season. That thou givest them they
        gather: thou openest thine hand, they are filled with
        good." (KJV) — ALL creatures — from lions to sea creatures
        — depend on God's open hand. Providence is not just for
        humans. God feeds the entire creation moment by moment.

v.30 — "Thou sendest forth thy spirit, they are created: and thou
        renewest the face of the earth." (KJV) — God's Spirit
        (ruach) is the ongoing creative power. Creation is not
        merely past-tense; God continually renews, recreates,
        sustains. Every spring is a fresh sending of His Spirit.

CROSS-REFERENCES
------------------------------------
v.2   — 1 Timothy 6:16 — "Dwelling in the light which no man can
         approach unto." God clothed in light.
v.4   — Hebrews 1:7 — Direct quotation: "Who maketh his angels
         spirits, and his ministers a flame of fire."
v.5   — Job 38:4-6 — "Where wast thou when I laid the foundations
         of the earth?" Same creation theology.
v.14-15 — Acts 14:17 — "He did good... filling our hearts with
         food and gladness." God provides for all.
v.24  — Proverbs 3:19 — "The LORD by wisdom hath founded the
         earth." Wisdom as instrument of creation.
v.29  — Job 34:14-15 — "If he gather unto himself his spirit...
         all flesh shall perish together."
v.30  — Genesis 1:2 — "The Spirit of God moved upon the face of
         the waters." The Spirit in creation.

COMMENTARY REFERENCES

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.4 — KJV: "Who maketh his angels spirits" / ESV: "he makes his
      messengers winds" — Hebrew "malachim" = messengers/angels;
      "ruchot" = winds/spirits. The ambiguity is intentional —
      God commands both supernatural beings and natural forces.
      Hebrews 1:7 uses this verse to describe angels.

v.24 — KJV: "the earth is full of thy riches" / ESV: "the earth
       is full of your creatures" — Hebrew "qinyanekha" can mean
       possessions/riches or creatures/things acquired. ESV reads
       it as created beings; KJV as treasures. Both capture God's
       abundant creative work.

v.26 — "Leviathan" — Not a metaphor but likely a reference to
       great sea creatures (whale, crocodile, or the chaos
       monster of ancient Near Eastern mythology — here
       demythologized as God's plaything). God MADE Leviathan
       to PLAY — what terrifies humans is God's toy.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
- v.1 — ASV/WEB: "Bless Jehovah/Yahweh, O my soul" — ASV uses
  "Jehovah" and WEB uses "Yahweh" throughout, rendering the divine
  name directly rather than substituting "the LORD" as KJV/ESV do.
  NET uses "Praise the Lord" — shifting from "bless" to "praise,"
  a more interpretive rendering.

- v.4 — ASV: "Who maketh winds his messengers; Flames of fire his
  ministers" / NET: "He makes the winds his messengers and the
  flaming fire his attendant" — Both reverse the KJV word order
  ("his angels spirits"). The Hebrew is genuinely ambiguous — are
  angels made into winds, or winds made into messengers? ASV/NET
  read it as nature serving God; KJV reads it as angels described.

- v.16 — WEB: "Yahweh's trees are well watered" / ASV: "The trees
  of Jehovah are filled with moisture" / KJV: "full of sap" — The
  Hebrew implies saturation; KJV's "sap" is idiomatic, while
  ASV/WEB clarify that the moisture comes from God's watering.

- v.24 — NET: "How many living things you have made, O Lord! You
  have exhibited great skill in making all of them" — Significantly
  more interpretive than KJV's "how manifold are thy works! in
  wisdom hast thou made them all." NET turns "wisdom" into "great
  skill" — accurate but loses the theological depth of hokmah.

- v.26 — NET: "over here swims the whale you made to play in it" —
  NET identifies Leviathan as "the whale," an interpretive choice.
  KJV/ASV/WEB retain "leviathan," preserving the mystery of the
  Hebrew term which may denote whale, sea serpent, or mythological
  chaos creature tamed by God.

- v.30 — ASV: "Thou sendest forth thy Spirit" / WEB: "You send out
  your Spirit" / NET: "When you send your life-giving breath" —
  Hebrew "ruach" = spirit/breath/wind. NET's "life-giving breath"
  is interpretive paraphrase. ASV/WEB capitalize "Spirit,"
  suggesting the Holy Spirit; NET emphasizes the animating function.

- v.35 — WEB: "Praise Yah!" / ASV: "Praise ye Jehovah" / NET:
  "Praise the Lord" — WEB uniquely preserves "Yah" (the shortened
  form of Yahweh in "Hallelujah"), giving readers the original
  Hebrew exclamation.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Look at creation and see the Creator (vv.1-4). Nature is not
   God, but nature reveals God. Light, wind, fire, heavens — all
   of it is His garment, His chariot, His handiwork. Creation is
   meant to provoke worship.

2. God provides for EVERYTHING (vv.10-28). From wild donkeys to
   birds to goats to humans to sea creatures — God opens His hand
   and fills them all with good. If He feeds lions and leviathan,
   He will certainly feed you.

3. Wisdom underlies all creation (v.24). The universe is not
   random. It is designed with manifold wisdom. Every discovery of
   science is a footnote to this verse.

4. All life depends on God's breath (vv.29-30). When He hides
   His face, creatures are troubled. When He withdraws breath,
   they die. We live moment by moment by His sustaining will.
   Independence from God is an illusion.

5. The proper response: sing and meditate (vv.33-34). Contemplation
   of creation should produce two things — singing and meditation.
   Not just facts but worship. Not just knowledge but sweetness.
   "My meditation of him shall be sweet."
