Psalms - Chapter 145
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
An acrostic praise psalm of David — 'I will extol thee, my God, O King'; God is great and greatly to be praised, gracious and full of compassion, faithful in all His words; His kingdom is everlasting; He is near to all who call in truth.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Multiple authors — primarily David (73 psalms attributed),
plus Asaph (12), Sons of Korah (11), Solomon (2), Moses (1),
Heman (1), Ethan (1), and anonymous. The Psalter was compiled over
approximately 1000 years and served as Israel's hymnal and prayer
book. The book is divided into five 'books' (1-41, 42-72, 73-89,
90-106, 107-150), paralleling the five books of Moses. Key themes:
worship, lament, praise, trust, kingship, creation, wisdom,
Messianic prophecy, and the full range of human emotion brought
before God.

Classification: Acrostic Hymn of Praise (The last Davidic Psalm)
— begins the Final Hallelujah sequence (145-150)
Attributed Author: David (superscription: "David's Psalm of
praise")
Key Themes: God's greatness beyond comprehension, His kingdom
that endures, God's compassion to all creation, His faithfulness
and nearness to all who call on Him, declaring God's praise to
all generations

Historical Context: Psalm 145 is the LAST psalm attributed to
David and the only one called "tehillah" (praise) in its
superscription — giving its name to the entire book (Tehillim in
Hebrew). It is an acrostic (each verse begins with successive
Hebrew letters) and was considered so important that the Talmud
states: "Everyone who recites Psalm 145 three times daily is
assured a place in the world to come." It is David's final,
comprehensive declaration of who God is — His greatness (v.3),
His compassion (v.8-9), His kingdom (vv.11-13), His providence
(vv.15-16), His nearness (v.18), and His preservation (v.20).
This psalm launches the final Hallelujah sequence (145-150) that
concludes the entire Psalter.

Structure:
- Personal Praise Forever (vv.1-3)
- Generational Testimony (vv.4-7)
- God's Compassionate Character (vv.8-9)
- The Everlasting Kingdom (vv.10-13)
- God's Providence to All (vv.14-16)
- God's Nearness and Preservation (vv.17-20)
- Universal Praise Forever (v.21)

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. God's greatness is unsearchable (v.3). Don't reduce God to
   what you can comprehend. He is always bigger than your
   understanding. Every discovery of His nature reveals more
   beyond.

2. God is good to ALL (v.9). Not just to the faithful. Not just
   to Israel. To ALL. His tender mercies cover all His works.
   This is universal grace — available before it's asked for.

3. God opens His hand (v.16). He is not stingy, not reluctant,
   not withholding. His hand is OPEN — pouring out satisfaction.
   If you're in need, look to the open hand.

4. He is near when you call (v.18). You don't need a priest, a
   system, or special access. Call on Him in truth and He is
   NEAR. Immediacy of access is the privilege of prayer.

5. This is David's last psalm — his final word is PRAISE. After
   all the laments, fears, doubts, and battles, David ends with
   wholehearted praise. May your last word also be praise.
