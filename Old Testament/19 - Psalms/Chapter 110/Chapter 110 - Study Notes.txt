Psalms - Chapter 110
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
A Messianic/royal psalm — 'The LORD said unto my Lord, Sit thou at my right hand'; the Messiah-King is also an eternal priest after the order of Melchizedek; the most-quoted psalm in the New Testament.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Multiple authors — primarily David (73 psalms attributed),
plus Asaph (12), Sons of Korah (11), Solomon (2), Moses (1),
Heman (1), Ethan (1), and anonymous. The Psalter was compiled over
approximately 1000 years and served as Israel's hymnal and prayer
book. The book is divided into five 'books' (1-41, 42-72, 73-89,
90-106, 107-150), paralleling the five books of Moses. Key themes:
worship, lament, praise, trust, kingship, creation, wisdom,
Messianic prophecy, and the full range of human emotion brought
before God.

Classification: Royal/Messianic Psalm — The Most Quoted Psalm in
the New Testament
Attributed Author: David (superscription: "A Psalm of David")
Key Themes: Christ's deity and lordship, the session of Christ at
God's right hand, the eternal priesthood of Melchizedek, the
conquest of all enemies, the union of king and priest in the
Messiah

Historical Context: Psalm 110 is the single most important
Messianic psalm and the most frequently quoted or alluded to Old
Testament passage in the New Testament (over 25 references). Jesus
Himself used verse 1 to prove the Messiah's deity (Matt 22:44) —
if David calls the Messiah "my Lord," how can the Messiah be
merely David's son? He must be divine. The psalm presents two
divine decrees: (1) The LORD seats David's Lord at His right hand
(v.1), and (2) The LORD swears an eternal priesthood (v.4).
This combines the offices of KING and PRIEST in one person —
impossible under the Levitical system (where priests came from
Levi, kings from Judah). Only the Messiah fulfills both. The
order of Melchizedek (Genesis 14:18) provides the precedent:
a priest-king who preceded and exceeded the Levitical order.
Hebrews 5-7 expounds this at length.

Structure:
- Divine Decree 1: Enthronement at God's Right Hand (v.1)
- The Messiah's Rule from Zion (vv.2-3)
- Divine Decree 2: Eternal Priesthood (v.4)
- The Messiah's Conquest (vv.5-7)


KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "The LORD said unto my Lord, Sit thou at my right hand,
        until I make thine enemies thy footstool." (KJV) — The
        most theologically significant verse in the Psalms. "The
        LORD" (Yahweh) speaks to "my Lord" (Adonai) — David's
        Lord. Two divine persons in conversation. Jesus used this
        to silence His opponents (Matt 22:41-46): if David calls
        Him Lord, He is more than David's descendant — He is God.
        "Sit at my right hand" = position of supreme honor and
        authority. "Until" = His enemies' subjugation is certain.

v.4  — "The LORD hath sworn, and will not repent, Thou art a
        priest for ever after the order of Melchizedek." (KJV) —
        God SWORE with an oath — the most solemn possible
        guarantee. This priesthood is: (1) eternal — "for ever,"
        (2) not Levitical — "after Melchizedek," (3) irrevocable
        — "will not repent." Hebrews 7 explains: Jesus is both
        King of righteousness and Priest of the Most High — better
        than Aaron, permanent, without predecessor or successor.

v.2  — "The LORD shall send the rod of thy strength out of Zion:
        rule thou in the midst of thine enemies." (KJV) — The
        Messiah rules NOW, in the MIDST of enemies — not after
        they are defeated but while they still oppose. His kingdom
        advances through resistance, not in the absence of it.

v.3  — "Thy people shall be willing in the day of thy power."
        (KJV) — The Messiah's people volunteer freely. His kingdom
        is not conscription but willing devotion. "The day of thy
        power" = when Christ's Spirit moves, His people respond
        with joyful surrender.

CROSS-REFERENCES
------------------------------------
v.1   — Matthew 22:44 — Jesus quotes this to prove the Messiah's
         deity. Also: Mark 12:36, Luke 20:42-43.
v.1   — Acts 2:34-35 — Peter quotes at Pentecost: Christ is
         enthroned at God's right hand.
v.1   — Hebrews 1:13 — Quoted to prove Christ's superiority to

         God Most High.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1 — "The LORD said unto my Lord" — Hebrew: "ne'um YHVH
      la-Adoni." YHVH (Yahweh, the covenant name of God) speaks
      to Adoni (my Lord/Master). David, the king of Israel, calls
      someone ELSE "my Lord" — implying someone greater than the
      king. This linguistic evidence alone points to deity.

v.3 — KJV: "Thy people shall be willing in the day of thy power,
      in the beauties of holiness from the womb of the morning:
      thou hast the dew of thy youth" / ESV: "Your people will
      offer themselves freely on the day of your power, in holy
      garments; from the womb of the morning, the dew of your
      youth will be yours" — This is one of the most difficult
      Hebrew texts in the Psalter. The imagery suggests fresh
      troops arising like morning dew — innumerable, youthful,
      radiant in holiness.

v.7 — "He shall drink of the brook in the way" — Enigmatic. Most
      likely: the warrior-king refreshes himself briefly during
      pursuit (like Gideon's 300 at the brook), then lifts his
      head in victory. Brief refreshment amid unstoppable advance.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Jesus is LORD, not merely a good teacher (v.1). This psalm
   proves that the Messiah is divine — David's Lord, not merely
   David's son. The Messiah is God. There is no middle ground
   on this claim.

2. Christ is enthroned NOW (v.1). He is not waiting to become
   King. He sat down at the right hand of God after His
   ascension. His kingship is present tense, even while enemies
   remain active.

3. He is both King AND Priest (v.4). No other figure in Israel's
   history could be both. Jesus alone combines perfect
   intercession (priest) with sovereign authority (king). He
   rules AND mediates for you simultaneously.

4. His people are willing (v.3). The mark of belonging to Christ
   is willing devotion, not forced compliance. If your service
   to God is unwilling, something has gone wrong — His people
   VOLUNTEER freely.

5. Every enemy becomes a footstool (v.1). The outcome is not in
   doubt. Whatever opposes Christ will ultimately be placed
   under His feet. The "until" is a guarantee, not a question.
