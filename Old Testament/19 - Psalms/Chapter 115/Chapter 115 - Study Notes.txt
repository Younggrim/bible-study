Psalms - Chapter 115
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
A Hallel psalm — 'Not unto us, O LORD, not unto us, but unto thy name give glory'; the nations' idols are silver and gold, mouths that cannot speak; but Israel trusts the LORD who is their help and shield.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Multiple authors — primarily David (73 psalms attributed),
plus Asaph (12), Sons of Korah (11), Solomon (2), Moses (1),
Heman (1), Ethan (1), and anonymous. The Psalter was compiled over
approximately 1000 years and served as Israel's hymnal and prayer
book. The book is divided into five 'books' (1-41, 42-72, 73-89,
90-106, 107-150), paralleling the five books of Moses. Key themes:
worship, lament, praise, trust, kingship, creation, wisdom,
Messianic prophecy, and the full range of human emotion brought
before God.

Classification: Hallel Psalm / Hymn of Trust
Attributed Author: Anonymous
Key Themes: God's glory vs. human glory, the futility of idols,
trusting in the living God, the nature of idolatry, God as help
and shield, blessing from the Creator

Historical Context: Psalm 115 is part of the Egyptian Hallel
(113-118) sung after the Passover meal — meaning Jesus sang these
words on the night of His betrayal. The psalm addresses a
situation where surrounding nations mock Israel's God ("Where is
their God?" v.2). In response, the psalmist contrasts the living
God of heaven with the dead idols of the nations. The satirical
description of idols (vv.4-8) is one of Scripture's most pointed
— they have every human feature but cannot use any of them. The
devastating punchline: those who make them BECOME LIKE THEM (v.8).
This psalm was likely sung antiphonally in worship, with different
groups responding (Israel, house of Aaron, those who fear the LORD).

Structure:
- Not to Us but to God's Name (vv.1-2)
- Our God vs. Their Idols (vv.3-8)
- Call to Trust (vv.9-11)
- Assurance of Blessing (vv.12-15)
- The Living Praise the Living God (vv.16-18)


KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "Not unto us, O LORD, not unto us, but unto thy name give
        glory, for thy mercy, and for thy truth's sake." (KJV) —
        The motto of true worship: NOT US. All glory belongs to
        God's name — for His mercy and His truth. This verse was
        the battle cry of the Knights Templar: "Non nobis, Domine."

v.3  — "But our God is in the heavens: he hath done whatsoever he
        hath pleased." (KJV) — Where is your God? IN THE HEAVENS.
        And what does He do? WHATEVER HE PLEASES. Total sovereignty.
        No limitation, no containment, no restriction.

v.8  — "They that make them are like unto them; so is every one
        that trusteth in them." (KJV) — The principle of idolatry:
        you become like what you worship. Trust in deaf, blind,
        mute idols and you become deaf, blind, and mute to
        spiritual reality. Worship shapes the worshiper.

v.16 — "The heaven, even the heavens, are the LORD'S: but the
        earth hath he given to the children of men." (KJV) — A
        statement of delegated authority. Heaven is God's domain;
        earth is humanity's stewardship. God has entrusted the
        earth to human care and responsibility.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1 — "for thy mercy, and for thy truth's sake" — Hebrew "hesed"
      and "emet" — God's covenant love and His faithfulness/truth.
      The two reasons His name deserves glory.

v.8 — "like unto them" = become similar to them. This is not
      metaphor but spiritual reality: trusting in lifeless things
      produces spiritual lifelessness. Conversely, trusting in the
      living God produces spiritual life.

v.17 — "The dead praise not the LORD" — From the Old Testament
       perspective, Sheol was a place of silence, not praise. This
       verse urges praise NOW, while alive, because death brings
       silence. The NT reveals fuller hope in resurrection.

v.1 — ASV: uses "Jehovah" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).
       NET: "the sake of your loyal love" for KJV's "thy mercy,"
       WEB: uses "Yahweh" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).
v.8 — ASV: "Yea," for KJV's "so is"
       NET: "them, as will everyone who trusts" for KJV's "unto them; so is every one that trusteth"
       WEB: "yes, everyone who trusts" for KJV's "so is every one that trusteth"
v.17 — ASV: uses "Jehovah" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).
       NET: "of those who descend" for KJV's "that go down"
       WEB: "Yah," for KJV's "not the LORD,"

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Not unto us" (v.1). Make this your daily prayer. Every
   success, every blessing, every accomplishment — not unto us,
   LORD. Redirect all glory to where it belongs.

2. You become what you worship (v.8). This is terrifyingly true.
   If you worship money, you become calculating and cold. If you
   worship power, you become controlling. If you worship God, you
   become gracious, compassionate, and alive. Choose carefully.

3. God does whatever He pleases (v.3). This is either the most
   terrifying or most comforting verse depending on whether you
   trust His character. Because He is good, His sovereignty is
   our comfort.

4. Trust is the dividing line (vv.9-11). Three groups are called
   to trust: Israel, Aaron's house, those who fear the LORD.
   Trust is what separates the living God from dead idols — and
   those who truly live from those who merely exist.

5. Praise belongs to the living (vv.17-18). Don't wait. The dead
   cannot praise. While you have breath, use it for blessing God.
