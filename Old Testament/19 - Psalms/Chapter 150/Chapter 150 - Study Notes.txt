Psalms - Chapter 150
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The grand doxology closing the Psalter — 'Praise God in his sanctuary'; praise Him with every instrument: trumpet, lute, harp, tambourine, strings, pipe, cymbals; 'Let every thing that hath breath praise the LORD. Praise ye the LORD.'

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Multiple authors — primarily David (73 psalms attributed),
plus Asaph (12), Sons of Korah (11), Solomon (2), Moses (1),
Heman (1), Ethan (1), and anonymous. The Psalter was compiled over
approximately 1000 years and served as Israel's hymnal and prayer
book. The book is divided into five 'books' (1-41, 42-72, 73-89,
90-106, 107-150), paralleling the five books of Moses. Key themes:
worship, lament, praise, trust, kingship, creation, wisdom,
Messianic prophecy, and the full range of human emotion brought
before God.

Classification: Doxology / Hallelujah Psalm — The Grand Finale
Attributed Author: Anonymous
Key Themes: Universal praise with every instrument, praise
everywhere (sanctuary and sky), praise for who God is and what
He's done, everything with breath praising the LORD, the Psalter's
climactic conclusion

Historical Context: Psalm 150 is the grand finale of the entire
Book of Psalms — the final word of Israel's hymnal. After 149
psalms spanning every human emotion — joy, sorrow, anger, fear,
love, doubt, confidence, despair — the Psalter ends with PURE
PRAISE. The word "praise" (hallelujah/hallelu) appears 13 times
in 6 verses. The psalm answers five questions: WHERE to praise
(v.1), WHY to praise (v.2), HOW to praise (vv.3-5), and WHO
should praise (v.6). The answer to the last question is the
climax of the entire book: "Let EVERYTHING that hath breath
praise the LORD." Every instrument, every location, every living
being — all exist for one ultimate purpose: praising God. This
is where all of Scripture points: universal, unending praise.

Structure:
- WHERE: Praise in Sanctuary and Heavens (v.1)
- WHY: Praise for His Acts and Greatness (v.2)
- HOW: Praise with Every Instrument (vv.3-5)
- WHO: Everything with Breath (v.6)


KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "Praise God in his sanctuary: praise him in the firmament
        of his power." (KJV) — TWO locations: in His sanctuary
        (the temple, the church, wherever His people gather) AND
        in the firmament (the heavens, all creation). Praise is
        not limited to sacred space — the sky itself is His praise
        arena.

v.2  — "Praise him for his mighty acts: praise him according to
        his excellent greatness." (KJV) — TWO reasons: what He HAS
        DONE (mighty acts) and what He IS (excellent greatness).
        Praise for His works AND His nature. Both are inexhaustible.

v.6  — "Let every thing that hath breath praise the LORD. Praise
        ye the LORD." (KJV) — The final verse of the Psalms.
        EVERYTHING with breath. Not just Israel, not just humans,
        not just the righteous — every breathing creature. This is
        the ultimate purpose of creation: universal praise. The
        entire Psalter resolves into this single sentence.

vv.3-5 — Seven instruments named: trumpet (shofar), psaltery,
        harp, timbrel (tambourine), stringed instruments, organs
        (pipe/flute), loud cymbals, high-sounding cymbals. Wind,
        string, percussion — all represented. No instrument is
        too secular or too loud for God's praise.

CROSS-REFERENCES
------------------------------------
v.1   — Revelation 4:8-11 — Heavenly worship: "Holy, holy, holy."
v.2   — Revelation 15:3 — "Great and marvellous are thy works,
         Lord God Almighty."
v.3   — Psalm 98:6 — "With trumpets and sound of cornet make a
         joyful noise."
v.6   — Revelation 5:13 — "Every creature which is in heaven, and
         on the earth... heard I saying, Blessing, and honour, and
         glory." The fulfillment of Psalm 150:6.
v.6   — Romans 11:36 — "For of him, and through him, and to him,
         are all things: to whom be glory for ever."

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.3 — "trumpet" = Hebrew "shofar" = the ram's horn, Israel's most
      ancient instrument, used in war, worship, and announcement.

v.4 — "timbrel" = hand-drum/tambourine, associated with dancing
      (cf. Miriam, Exodus 15:20). "Dance" = physical movement in
      worship is commanded here.

v.5 — "loud cymbals... high sounding cymbals" — Two types: one
      clashing, one shimmering. Emphasis on VOLUME. This is not
      quiet meditation — this is loud, exuberant, full-throated
      praise.

v.6 — "everything that hath breath" — Hebrew "kol ha-neshamah" =
      everything with the breath of life (neshamah = the breath
      God gave in Genesis 2:7). Every creature that received
      God's breath should return that breath as praise.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
- v.1 — WEB: "Praise Yah! Praise God in his sanctuary! Praise him in his
  heavens for his acts of power!" — WEB uniquely uses "Yah" (shortened form
  of Yahweh) for "Hallelujah" rather than expanding it. Also "his heavens"
  rather than "the firmament of his power" (KJV/ASV). NET: "praise him in the
  sky, which testifies to his strength" — most interpretive, making the
  heavens a witness to power. ASV: "the firmament of his power" — retains
  KJV's rendering.
- v.1 — NET: "Praise the Lord!" for Hebrew "Hallelujah" — standard
  translation. ASV: "Praise ye Jehovah" — divine name explicit. WEB: "Praise
  Yah!" — the most literal, preserving the short form of the divine name
  embedded in "Hallelu-Yah" (praise Yah).
- v.3 — NET: "the blast of the horn" (vs. "trumpet" KJV/ESV, "trumpet sound"
  ASV). "Blast" captures the sudden, arresting sound of the shofar. WEB:
  "the sounding of the trumpet" — emphasizes the act of blowing.
  KJV: "psaltery and harp" / NET: "lyre and harp" / ASV: "psaltery and harp"
  / WEB: "harp and lyre" — the psaltery/lyre is a smaller stringed
  instrument; the harp (Hebrew "kinnor") is David's personal instrument.
- v.4 — KJV: "organs" / NET: "flute" / ASV: "pipe" / WEB: "flute" — Hebrew
  "ugab" is a wind instrument, likely a reed pipe or primitive flute. KJV's
  "organs" is misleading to modern ears (suggesting a church organ). All
  modern translations correctly identify it as a simpler wind instrument.
- v.5 — NET: "loud cymbals...clanging cymbals" / ASV: "loud cymbals...high
  sounding cymbals" / WEB: "loud cymbals...resounding cymbals" — Hebrew
  distinguishes "tsiltselei-shama" (cymbals of hearing/loud) from "tsiltselei-
  teruah" (cymbals of shouting/crashing). WEB's "resounding" captures the
  sustained reverberating sound. The final instruments are the LOUDEST —
  crescendo to maximum volume.
- v.6 — NET: "Let everything that has breath praise the Lord! Praise the
  Lord!" — standard rendering. ASV: "Let everything that hath breath praise
  Jehovah. Praise ye Jehovah." WEB: "Let everything that has breath praise
  Yah! Praise Yah!" — WEB's "Praise Yah" is the most etymologically faithful
  rendering of "Hallelujah" — the final word of the entire Psalter is God's
  own name on the lips of all creation.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. The Psalms end in praise — and so should you. Whatever you've
   been through — lament, doubt, anger, fear — the destination
   is PRAISE. The entire Psalter moves toward this: everything
   resolves in worship.

2. Use everything (vv.3-5). Every gift, every talent, every
   instrument, every capacity you have — use it for praise.
   Nothing is too common, too loud, too physical for worship.
   Dance. Shout. Play. Sing.

3. EVERYTHING with breath (v.6). If you're breathing, you should
   be praising. It's not optional for the gifted — it's the
   purpose of all life. The breath God gave you was meant to
   return to Him as praise.

4. Praise for acts AND greatness (v.2). Don't just praise for
   what God has done FOR YOU — praise Him for what He IS.
   His greatness exceeds His gifts. He is worthy regardless of
   what you've received.

5. This is where everything is heading. Revelation 5:13 pictures
   the fulfillment: EVERY creature praising. History's final
   scene looks exactly like Psalm 150. You're rehearsing the
   finale of the universe.
