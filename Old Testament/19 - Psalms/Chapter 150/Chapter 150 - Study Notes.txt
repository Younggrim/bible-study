Psalms - Chapter 150
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The grand doxology closing the Psalter — 'Praise God in his sanctuary'; praise Him with every instrument: trumpet, lute, harp, tambourine, strings, pipe, cymbals; 'Let every thing that hath breath praise the LORD. Praise ye the LORD.'

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Multiple authors — primarily David (73 psalms attributed),
plus Asaph (12), Sons of Korah (11), Solomon (2), Moses (1),
Heman (1), Ethan (1), and anonymous. The Psalter was compiled over
approximately 1000 years and served as Israel's hymnal and prayer
book. The book is divided into five 'books' (1-41, 42-72, 73-89,
90-106, 107-150), paralleling the five books of Moses. Key themes:
worship, lament, praise, trust, kingship, creation, wisdom,
Messianic prophecy, and the full range of human emotion brought
before God.

Classification: Doxology / Hallelujah Psalm — The Grand Finale
Attributed Author: Anonymous
Key Themes: Universal praise with every instrument, praise
everywhere (sanctuary and sky), praise for who God is and what
He's done, everything with breath praising the LORD, the Psalter's
climactic conclusion

Historical Context: Psalm 150 is the grand finale of the entire
Book of Psalms — the final word of Israel's hymnal. After 149
psalms spanning every human emotion — joy, sorrow, anger, fear,
love, doubt, confidence, despair — the Psalter ends with PURE
PRAISE. The word "praise" (hallelujah/hallelu) appears 13 times
in 6 verses. The psalm answers five questions: WHERE to praise
(v.1), WHY to praise (v.2), HOW to praise (vv.3-5), and WHO
should praise (v.6). The answer to the last question is the
climax of the entire book: "Let EVERYTHING that hath breath
praise the LORD." Every instrument, every location, every living
being — all exist for one ultimate purpose: praising God. This
is where all of Scripture points: universal, unending praise.

Structure:
- WHERE: Praise in Sanctuary and Heavens (v.1)
- WHY: Praise for His Acts and Greatness (v.2)
- HOW: Praise with Every Instrument (vv.3-5)
- WHO: Everything with Breath (v.6)

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. The Psalms end in praise — and so should you. Whatever you've
   been through — lament, doubt, anger, fear — the destination
   is PRAISE. The entire Psalter moves toward this: everything
   resolves in worship.

2. Use everything (vv.3-5). Every gift, every talent, every
   instrument, every capacity you have — use it for praise.
   Nothing is too common, too loud, too physical for worship.
   Dance. Shout. Play. Sing.

3. EVERYTHING with breath (v.6). If you're breathing, you should
   be praising. It's not optional for the gifted — it's the
   purpose of all life. The breath God gave you was meant to
   return to Him as praise.

4. Praise for acts AND greatness (v.2). Don't just praise for
   what God has done FOR YOU — praise Him for what He IS.
   His greatness exceeds His gifts. He is worthy regardless of
   what you've received.

5. This is where everything is heading. Revelation 5:13 pictures
   the fulfillment: EVERY creature praising. History's final
   scene looks exactly like Psalm 150. You're rehearsing the
   finale of the universe.
