Psalms - Chapter 65
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
A harvest thanksgiving psalm — praise awaits God in Zion; He forgives iniquity, answers prayer with awesome deeds, stills the roaring seas, and crowns the year with goodness as the pastures overflow.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Multiple authors — primarily David (73 psalms attributed),
plus Asaph (12), Sons of Korah (11), Solomon (2), Moses (1),
Heman (1), Ethan (1), and anonymous. The Psalter was compiled over
approximately 1000 years and served as Israel's hymnal and prayer
book. The book is divided into five 'books' (1-41, 42-72, 73-89,
90-106, 107-150), paralleling the five books of Moses. Key themes:
worship, lament, praise, trust, kingship, creation, wisdom,
Messianic prophecy, and the full range of human emotion brought
before God.

Classification: Hymn of Praise / Thanksgiving / Creation Psalm
Attributed Author: David
Key Themes: Praise for answered prayer, atonement for sin, the
blessedness of God's chosen ones, God's sovereignty over nature,
abundant provision, the joy of creation

Historical Context: This is likely a harvest thanksgiving psalm,
celebrating God's provision of rain and agricultural abundance
after a period of need. It may have been composed for a specific
harvest festival or after a drought was broken. The psalm is
remarkable for its scope — it moves from the temple (vv.1-4) to
cosmic creation (vv.5-8) to the specific fields and valleys of
Israel (vv.9-13). It is one of the most joyful psalms in the
Psalter, with creation itself personified as shouting and singing
for joy. The progression from sin forgiven to creation rejoicing
shows the connection between spiritual and physical blessing in
Israel's theology.

Structure:
- Praise in Zion and Answered Prayer (vv.1-2)
- Atonement for Sin (v.3)
- Blessedness of the Chosen (v.4)
- God's Awesome Deeds in Creation (vv.5-8)
- God's Provision for the Earth (vv.9-13)


CROSS-REFERENCES
------------------------------------
v.2   — Isaiah 56:7 — "My house shall be called an house of
         prayer for all people." God's universal accessibility.
v.3   — 1 John 2:2 — "He is the propitiation for our sins: and
         not for ours only, but also for the sins of the whole
         world."
v.4   — Ephesians 1:4 — "He hath chosen us in him before the
         foundation of the world." Divine election.
v.4   — John 6:44 — "No man can come to me, except the Father
         which hath sent me draw him." God causing approach.
v.7   — Psalm 89:9 — "Thou rulest the raging of the sea: when the
         waves thereof arise, thou stillest them."
v.9   — Deuteronomy 11:11-12 — "A land which the LORD thy God
         careth for: the eyes of the LORD thy God are always upon
         it." God's agricultural care for Israel.
v.13  — Romans 8:19-22 — Creation groaning and waiting for
         redemption — the counterpart to creation singing.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. God hears prayer — that's His character (v.2). Prayer is not
   shouting into a void. The God of the universe is defined by
   hearing. And ALL flesh can come — no one is excluded from His
   audience.

2. Access to God is a gift, not an achievement (v.4). "Thou
   choosest and causest to approach." If you are drawn to God, it
   is because He drew you first. Proximity to God is grace, not
   merit.

3. God crowns the year with goodness (v.11). Look back over the
   past year — God's goodness is the crown. Even in hard years,
   His provision is present. His wagon tracks leave abundance in
   their wake.

4. All creation sings (v.13). The hills, valleys, and meadows
   worship. If nature sings to its Creator, how much more should
   those made in His image? Our silence is the anomaly, not our
   praise.
