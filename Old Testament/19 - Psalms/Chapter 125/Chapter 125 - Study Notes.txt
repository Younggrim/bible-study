Psalms - Chapter 125
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
A Song of Ascents (trust) — those who trust in the LORD are like Mount Zion, immovable; the scepter of wickedness shall not rest on the righteous; peace be upon Israel.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Multiple authors — primarily David (73 psalms attributed),
plus Asaph (12), Sons of Korah (11), Solomon (2), Moses (1),
Heman (1), Ethan (1), and anonymous. The Psalter was compiled over
approximately 1000 years and served as Israel's hymnal and prayer
book. The book is divided into five 'books' (1-41, 42-72, 73-89,
90-106, 107-150), paralleling the five books of Moses. Key themes:
worship, lament, praise, trust, kingship, creation, wisdom,
Messianic prophecy, and the full range of human emotion brought
before God.

Classification: Song of Ascents / Psalm of Trust
Attributed Author: Anonymous (superscription: "A Song of degrees")
Key Themes: The immovability of those who trust God, God
surrounding His people like mountains, the scepter of wickedness
will not remain, peace upon Israel

Historical Context: Psalm 125 is the sixth Song of Ascents — a
psalm of quiet confidence. Its central image is geographical:
Jerusalem is surrounded by mountains, and God surrounds His people
just as those mountains surround the city. For a pilgrim who has
walked through the hills to reach Jerusalem, this imagery is
viscerally real — they can SEE the mountains encircling the city
and understand the metaphor with their eyes. The psalm also
addresses a danger: if wickedness rules too long, even the
righteous might be tempted to do wrong (v.3). But God will not
allow it.

Structure:
- Immovability of the Faithful (v.1)
- God as Surrounding Mountains (v.2)
- Wickedness Won't Remain (v.3)
- Prayer for the Good (vv.4-5)


KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "They that trust in the LORD shall be as mount Zion, which
        cannot be removed, but abideth for ever." (KJV) — Those
        who trust become like mountains: immovable, permanent,
        enduring. Trust produces stability.

v.2  — "As the mountains are round about Jerusalem, so the LORD
        is round about his people from henceforth even for ever."
        (KJV) — God surrounds you as mountains surround Jerusalem.
        On every side, in every direction — protection, presence,
        permanence. "From henceforth even for ever" — no end date.

v.3  — "For the rod of the wicked shall not rest upon the lot of
        the righteous; lest the righteous put forth their hands
        unto iniquity." (KJV) — God limits wickedness' duration
        over His people. The reason: so that even righteous people
        won't be tempted to compromise. God protects not just
        bodies but integrity.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
- v.1 — ASV: "trust in Jehovah" / WEB: "trust in Yahweh" / NET: "trust in the Lord" — Standard divine name pattern across translations.
- v.3 — KJV: "the rod of the wicked" / ASV/WEB: "the sceptre of wickedness" / NET: "the scepter of a wicked king" — KJV's "rod" suggests punishment; ASV/WEB's "sceptre" suggests governance. NET adds "king" explicitly, interpreting political oppression.
- v.3 — KJV: "upon the lot of the righteous" / ASV/WEB: "upon the lot/allotment of the righteous" / NET: "upon the allotted land of the godly" — NET adds "land" to clarify that "lot" (Hebrew "goral") refers to inherited territory, not chance.
- v.4 — NET: "to the morally upright" — Adds "morally" to KJV's simple "upright in their hearts," making the ethical dimension explicit.
- v.5 — NET: "As for those who are bent on traveling a sinful path, may the Lord remove them" — Significantly interprets KJV's "such as turn aside unto their crooked ways, the LORD shall lead them forth." NET makes "lead forth" into "remove" (judgment) and "crooked ways" into "sinful path."
- v.5 — KJV: "peace shall be upon Israel" / WEB: "Peace be on Israel" / NET: "May Israel experience peace" — NET turns the declaration/prayer into an expressed wish, adding "experience" where the Hebrew simply states "peace upon Israel."

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Trust makes you a mountain (v.1). The trusting person is not
   shaken by circumstances — like Zion, they abide forever.
   Stability comes from trust, not from circumstances.

2. God is around you on every side (v.2). Mountains don't have
   gaps. God's surrounding presence has no openings for the enemy
   to exploit. You are enclosed by His care.

3. Wickedness has an expiration date (v.3). God won't let evil
   rule over you indefinitely. He limits its duration because He
   cares about your integrity, not just your survival.
