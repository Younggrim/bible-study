Psalms - Chapter 133
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
A Song of Ascents of David — 'Behold, how good and how pleasant it is for brethren to dwell together in unity!'; like precious oil on Aaron's head, like dew on Hermon — there the LORD commands blessing and life.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Multiple authors — primarily David (73 psalms attributed),
plus Asaph (12), Sons of Korah (11), Solomon (2), Moses (1),
Heman (1), Ethan (1), and anonymous. The Psalter was compiled over
approximately 1000 years and served as Israel's hymnal and prayer
book. The book is divided into five 'books' (1-41, 42-72, 73-89,
90-106, 107-150), paralleling the five books of Moses. Key themes:
worship, lament, praise, trust, kingship, creation, wisdom,
Messianic prophecy, and the full range of human emotion brought
before God.

Classification: Song of Ascents / Wisdom Psalm
Attributed Author: David (superscription: "A Song of degrees
of David")
Key Themes: The goodness of unity, anointing that flows from head
to body, refreshing dew from above, commanded blessing, brethren
dwelling together

Historical Context: Psalm 133 is one of the most beloved short
psalms — only 3 verses celebrating the beauty of UNITY among
God's people. When pilgrims from all twelve tribes gathered in
Jerusalem for festivals, this psalm captured the joy of
togetherness. David (who unified the kingdom after years of civil
war) was uniquely qualified to praise unity. The two images —
anointing oil flowing down Aaron's beard and Hermon's dew
descending on Zion — both move DOWNWARD: blessing flows from above
onto the gathered community. Where unity exists, God COMMANDS
blessing. Unity doesn't earn blessing; it's where God designates
it.

Structure:
- Declaration: How Good Unity Is (v.1)
- Image 1: Anointing Oil (v.2)
- Image 2: Hermon's Dew (v.3a)
- God Commands Blessing There (v.3b)


KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "Behold, how good and how pleasant it is for brethren to
        dwell together in unity!" (KJV) — Two qualities: GOOD
        (morally right) and PLEASANT (experientially delightful).
        Unity isn't just nice — it's beautiful. Not just tolerable
        but actively delightful to God and man.

v.2  — "It is like the precious ointment upon the head, that ran
        down upon the beard, even Aaron's beard: that went down to
        the skirts of his garments." (KJV) — Anointing oil poured
        on the high priest flows from head to the hem — covering
        everything. Unity is like an anointing that saturates the
        entire body from top to bottom.

v.3  — "For there the LORD commanded the blessing, even life for
        evermore." (KJV) — "COMMANDED the blessing" — God doesn't
        merely permit blessing where there's unity; He COMMANDS it.
        Unity is the address where God directs life and blessing.

CROSS-REFERENCES
------------------------------------
v.1   — John 17:21 — Jesus prayed "that they all may be one."
v.1   — Ephesians 4:3 — "Endeavouring to keep the unity of the
         Spirit in the bond of peace."
v.2   — Exodus 30:25-30 — The holy anointing oil recipe for
         Aaron's consecration.
v.3   — Hermon — The highest mountain in Israel (9,000+ ft),
         source of dew and water for the region.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1 — "dwell together" — Hebrew "shevet achim gam-yachad" =
      brothers also sitting/dwelling together. Not just proximity
      but intentional togetherness. "Gam" (also/even) adds
      emphasis: even TOGETHER, which is remarkable.

v.2 — The anointing oil (Exodus 30) contained myrrh, cinnamon,
      calamus, cassia, and olive oil — extremely precious and
      aromatic. Unity fills the room like sacred perfume.

v.1 — NET: "A song of ascents; by David. Look! How" for KJV's "Behold, how"
v.2 — ASV: "Even Aaron`s beard; That came" for KJV's "even Aaron’s beard: that went"
       NET: "fine oil poured on" for KJV's "the precious ointment upon"
       WEB: "oil on" for KJV's "ointment upon"

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Unity is both good AND pleasant (v.1). Not just doctrinally
   correct but genuinely enjoyable. If your community has truth
   without pleasantness, something is missing.

2. Unity flows down from the head (v.2). True unity starts with
   the Head (Christ) and flows to the body. It's not manufactured
   from below but received from above.

3. God COMMANDS blessing where there's unity (v.3). Want to know
   where God sends blessing? Where His people dwell together. If
   you want commanded blessing, pursue unity.

4. Only 3 verses — because unity is simple, not complicated. We
   complicate what God made beautiful. Dwell together. That's it.
