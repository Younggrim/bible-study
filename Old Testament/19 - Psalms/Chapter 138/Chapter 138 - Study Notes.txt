Psalms - Chapter 138
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
A thanksgiving psalm of David — 'I will praise thee with my whole heart'; God answered when David called and strengthened his soul; though the LORD is high, He regards the lowly; His steadfast love endures forever.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Multiple authors — primarily David (73 psalms attributed),
plus Asaph (12), Sons of Korah (11), Solomon (2), Moses (1),
Heman (1), Ethan (1), and anonymous. The Psalter was compiled over
approximately 1000 years and served as Israel's hymnal and prayer
book. The book is divided into five 'books' (1-41, 42-72, 73-89,
90-106, 107-150), paralleling the five books of Moses. Key themes:
worship, lament, praise, trust, kingship, creation, wisdom,
Messianic prophecy, and the full range of human emotion brought
before God.

Classification: Individual Thanksgiving / Hymn of Praise
Attributed Author: David (superscription: "A Psalm of David")
Key Themes: Wholehearted praise, God's faithfulness to His word,
answered prayer bringing boldness, God's regard for the lowly,
the LORD perfecting what concerns His people

Historical Context: Psalm 138 opens the final Davidic collection
in the Psalter (138-145). It is a confident psalm of thanksgiving
— David praises with his whole heart, declaring that God has
magnified His word above all His name (v.2). The psalm moves from
personal thanksgiving to universal vision (all kings will praise
God) to confident trust that God will complete what He has begun.
Verse 8 contains a powerful theological declaration about God's
ongoing work in the believer's life.

Structure:
- Wholehearted Thanksgiving (vv.1-3)
- All Kings Shall Praise (vv.4-5)
- God's Regard for the Lowly (v.6)
- Confidence in God's Preservation (vv.7-8)


KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.2  — "Thou hast magnified thy word above all thy name." (KJV) —
        Astonishing statement: God has elevated His WORD even above
        His name. God's reputation is staked on His word being
        true. His promises are the ultimate expression of who He
        is.

v.6  — "Though the LORD be high, yet hath he respect unto the
        lowly: but the proud he knoweth afar off." (KJV) — God is
        near to the humble but distant from the proud. The "high"
        God respects the "lowly" — but knows the proud only from
        a distance.

v.8  — "The LORD will perfect that which concerneth me: thy mercy,
        O LORD, endureth for ever: forsake not the works of thine
        own hands." (KJV) — God will COMPLETE what He started in
        you. This is the OT Philippians 1:6. You are God's
        work-in-progress, and He doesn't abandon His projects.

CROSS-REFERENCES
------------------------------------

v.3   — Psalm 18:32 — "God... strengthened me with strength."

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
- v.1 — KJV: "before the gods will I sing praise unto thee" / NET: "before the heavenly assembly I will sing praises to you" — NET interprets "gods" (Hebrew "elohim") as the heavenly court/divine council rather than pagan deities, a significant theological choice.
- v.2 — KJV: "for thy lovingkindness and for thy truth" / ASV: "for thy lovingkindness and for thy truth" / WEB: "for your loving kindness and for your truth" / NET: "because of your loyal love and faithfulness" — Chesed + emet pairing: KJV/ASV/WEB=lovingkindness+truth; NET=loyal love+faithfulness.
- v.2 — KJV: "thou hast magnified thy word above all thy name" / WEB: "you have exalted your Name and your Word above all" / NET: "you have exalted your promise above the entire sky" — Three very different readings of a difficult Hebrew text. NET reads "shamayim" (sky/heavens) rather than "shem" (name).
- v.3 — NET: "You made me bold and energized me" — Dynamic paraphrase of KJV's "strengthenedst me with strength in my soul." NET splits the concept into boldness (attitude) and energy (capacity).
- v.6 — NET: "from far away humbles the proud" — Interprets KJV's "the proud he knoweth afar off" differently. KJV suggests God perceives the proud from a distance; NET says God humbles them from a distance.
- v.8 — KJV: "The LORD will perfect that which concerneth me" / ASV: "Jehovah will perfect that which concerneth me" / WEB: "Yahweh will fulfill that which concerns me" / NET: "The Lord avenges me" — NET dramatically changes "perfect/fulfill" (Hebrew "gamar" = complete/accomplish) to "avenges," a very different theological emphasis.
- v.8 — KJV: "thy mercy, O LORD, endureth for ever" / ASV: "Thy lovingkindness, O Jehovah" / WEB: "your loving kindness, Yahweh, endures forever" / NET: "your loyal love endures" — Chesed in the psalm's final prayer.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. God magnifies His Word (v.2). If God values His word above
   His name, how much should we? Scripture is not secondary —
   it is God's ultimate self-expression. Trust it absolutely.

2. He is near to the lowly, far from the proud (v.6). Want
   nearness to God? Choose lowliness. Want distance? Choose
   pride. The equation is that simple.

3. God will complete His work in you (v.8). You are not finished.
   God is still working. Whatever concerns you, the LORD is
   perfecting. Don't judge the unfinished product — the Potter
   isn't done.

4. "Forsake not the works of thine own hands" (v.8). A prayer
   God loves to answer: Don't abandon me — I'm YOUR work. The
   appeal is not to our worth but to His investment.
