Psalms - Chapter 94
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
A lament calling for divine justice — 'O LORD God, to whom vengeance belongeth, show thyself'; how long will the wicked triumph and crush God's people? The LORD will not forsake His inheritance or let the unjust prevail.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Multiple authors — primarily David (73 psalms attributed),
plus Asaph (12), Sons of Korah (11), Solomon (2), Moses (1),
Heman (1), Ethan (1), and anonymous. The Psalter was compiled over
approximately 1000 years and served as Israel's hymnal and prayer
book. The book is divided into five 'books' (1-41, 42-72, 73-89,
90-106, 107-150), paralleling the five books of Moses. Key themes:
worship, lament, praise, trust, kingship, creation, wisdom,
Messianic prophecy, and the full range of human emotion brought
before God.

Classification: Community Lament / Wisdom Psalm
Attributed Author: Anonymous
Key Themes: God of vengeance, cry against oppressors, the folly
of thinking God cannot see, blessedness of divine discipline,
God's comfort amid anxious thoughts, God as defense and refuge

Historical Context: This psalm follows the enthronement psalms
(93, 95-99) that declare "The LORD reigns," yet addresses the
painful reality that wicked oppressors still crush God's people.
If God reigns, why do the wicked triumph? The psalm answers: God
DOES see (vv.9-10), He WILL judge (vv.23), and meanwhile He
disciplines and comforts His own (vv.12-13,19). The oppressors
described (killing widows, strangers, fatherless — v.6) and their
legislative injustice ("frameth mischief by a law" — v.20) suggest
a period of institutional corruption — possibly the late monarchy
or exile. Paul quotes v.11 in 1 Corinthians 3:20.

Structure:
- Cry for God to Avenge (vv.1-3)
- Description of Oppressors (vv.4-7)
- Warning to the Foolish (vv.8-11)
- Blessedness of Discipline (vv.12-15)
- Personal Testimony of God's Help (vv.16-19)
- Corrupt Thrones Cannot Partner with God (vv.20-21)
- Confidence in God's Justice (vv.22-23)


KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.9  — "He that planted the ear, shall he not hear? he that formed
        the eye, shall he not see?" (KJV) — Devastating logic. The
        Creator of hearing HEARS. The Creator of sight SEES. To
        think God is unaware is supreme foolishness — the Maker of
        perception perceives everything.

v.12 — "Blessed is the man whom thou chastenest, O LORD, and
        teachest him out of thy law." (KJV) — Discipline reframed
        as BLESSING. God's correction is not rejection but
        education. The disciplined person is the instructed person
        — and instruction produces rest (v.13).

v.19 — "In the multitude of my thoughts within me thy comforts
        delight my soul." (KJV) — When anxious thoughts multiply
        inside, God's comforts bring delight. Not the absence of
        anxiety but God's consolation WITHIN the anxiety. Comfort
        does not require the removal of the problem.

CROSS-REFERENCES
------------------------------------
v.9   — Proverbs 20:12 — "The hearing ear, and the seeing eye,
         the LORD hath made even both of them."
v.11  — 1 Corinthians 3:20 — Paul quotes: "The Lord knoweth the
         thoughts of the wise, that they are vain."
v.12  — Hebrews 12:5-6 — "Whom the Lord loveth he chasteneth."
         Discipline as evidence of love.
v.14  — Romans 11:1-2 — "God hath not cast away his people." Same
         assurance.
v.17  — Psalm 115:17 — "The dead praise not the LORD, neither any
         that go down into silence." The "silence" David nearly
         entered.
v.19  — Philippians 4:6-7 — "The peace of God... shall keep your
         hearts and minds." NT version of God's comforts amid
         anxious thoughts.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "O LORD God, to whom vengeance belongeth" / ESV: "O
      LORD, God of vengeance" — "Vengeance" (naqam) is not
      vindictiveness but righteous retribution — setting wrongs
      right. It belongs EXCLUSIVELY to God (Rom 12:19). He alone
      has the right and the impartiality to avenge.

v.20 — KJV: "which frameth mischief by a law" / ESV: "those who
       frame injustice by statute" — Legalized injustice:
       institutionalized oppression. The most dangerous evil is
       evil given legal standing. The psalmist asks: can such
       legislators have fellowship with God? No.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
- v.1 — ASV: "O Jehovah, thou God to whom vengeance belongeth" /
  WEB: "Yahweh, you God to whom vengeance belongs" / NET: "O Lord,
  the God who avenges!" — Divine name variation: ASV "Jehovah," WEB
  "Yahweh," NET "Lord." NET restructures the phrase from a relative
  clause to a title ("the God who avenges").

- v.1 — KJV: "shew thyself" / ASV: "shine forth" / WEB: "shine out"
  / NET: "reveal your splendor" — Hebrew "hophia" = to shine, appear
  in radiance. NET's "reveal your splendor" is a strong interpretive
  paraphrase; ASV/WEB stay closer to the literal "shine forth."

- v.11 — KJV: "that they are vanity" / ASV: "That they are vanity"
  / WEB: "that they are futile" / NET: "that peoples' thoughts are
  morally bankrupt" — NET dramatically intensifies. "Vanity" (hebel =
  breath/vapor) becomes "morally bankrupt" — an interpretive leap
  from emptiness to moral corruption.

- v.17 — KJV: "my soul had almost dwelt in silence" / NET: "I would
  soon have dwelt in the silence of death" — NET adds "of death"
  (not in the Hebrew). "Silence" (dumah) is a euphemism for Sheol/
  death, and NET makes the implication explicit.

- v.18 — KJV: "thy mercy, O LORD, held me up" / ASV: "Thy
  lovingkindness, O Jehovah, held me up" / WEB: "Your loving
  kindness, Yahweh, held me up" / NET: "your loyal love, O Lord,
  supports me" — The classic chesed variation: KJV "mercy," ASV
  "lovingkindness," WEB "loving kindness," NET "loyal love."

- v.19 — KJV: "thy comforts delight my soul" / NET: "your soothing
  touch makes me happy" — NET's "soothing touch" is a free
  paraphrase of Hebrew "tanhumim" (comforts/consolations). The
  physical metaphor ("touch") is not in the original.

- v.22 — KJV: "the LORD is my defence" / ASV: "Jehovah hath been my
  high tower" / WEB: "Yahweh has been my high tower" / NET: "the
  Lord will protect me" — KJV "defence" and ASV/WEB "high tower"
  both render "misgab" (stronghold/refuge). NET interprets the
  metaphor into a verbal phrase.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. God created perception — of course He perceives (v.9). The
   atheist's assumption that God doesn't see is absurd. He made
   ears; He hears. He made eyes; He sees. Your oppressor is
   observed. Every act is witnessed.

2. Divine discipline is blessing in disguise (v.12). If God is
   correcting you, He values you. Uncontested sin means neglect.
   Correction means investment. Receive discipline as education.

3. God's comfort meets anxious thoughts (v.19). You will have
   many thoughts — worried, spinning, overwhelming. God does not
   promise to eliminate them but to insert His comforts INTO them.
   His consolations delight the soul even amid the storm.

4. Legalized injustice will not stand (v.20). When governments
   "frame mischief by law," they do not thereby gain God's
   endorsement. No statute can make injustice just. God opposes
   systemic evil just as He opposes personal evil.
