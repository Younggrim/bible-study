Psalms - Chapter 91
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
A psalm of trust and divine protection — 'He that dwelleth in the secret place of the most High shall abide under the shadow of the Almighty'; no plague, no evil shall befall; angels guard; God promises rescue and long life.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Multiple authors — primarily David (73 psalms attributed),
plus Asaph (12), Sons of Korah (11), Solomon (2), Moses (1),
Heman (1), Ethan (1), and anonymous. The Psalter was compiled over
approximately 1000 years and served as Israel's hymnal and prayer
book. The book is divided into five 'books' (1-41, 42-72, 73-89,
90-106, 107-150), paralleling the five books of Moses. Key themes:
worship, lament, praise, trust, kingship, creation, wisdom,
Messianic prophecy, and the full range of human emotion brought
before God.

Classification: Trust / Confidence Psalm
Attributed Author: Anonymous (Jewish tradition: Moses)
Key Themes: Divine protection, dwelling in God's presence, freedom
from fear, angelic guardianship, treading on enemies, God's
personal promises to the faithful

Historical Context: Psalm 91 has no superscription. Jewish tradition
attributes it to Moses (pairing it with Psalm 90). It is the great
"protection psalm" — the most comprehensive statement of divine
shelter in all of Scripture. Satan quoted vv.11-12 during Christ's
temptation (Matthew 4:6), attempting to twist the promise into
presumption. The psalm uses four divine names: Most High (Elyon),
Almighty (Shaddai), LORD (Yahweh), and God (Elohim) — indicating
comprehensive, covenant protection from every angle. The psalm
moves between voices: the speaker (vv.1-2,9), addressing the
faithful (vv.3-8,10-13), and God speaking directly (vv.14-16).
It was likely used liturgically as a blessing pronounced over
worshippers or warriors.

Structure:
- The Secret Place of the Most High (vv.1-2)
- Deliverance from Dangers (vv.3-8)
- The Condition: Making God Your Dwelling (v.9)
- Angelic Protection (vv.10-13)
- God Speaks Directly (vv.14-16)


KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "He that dwelleth in the secret place of the most High
        shall abide under the shadow of the Almighty." (KJV) —
        The condition and the promise. "Dwelleth" = not visits but
        LIVES. The "secret place" is intimate, habitual communion
        with God. The "shadow of the Almighty" = constant, close
        protection — like shade from scorching heat.

v.4  — "He shall cover thee with his feathers, and under his wings
        shalt thou trust: his truth shall be thy shield and
        buckler." (KJV) — God as mother bird sheltering young.
        Tenderness AND strength: feathers (intimate) and shield/
        buckler (military defense). His truth/faithfulness is the
        actual material of protection.

v.11-12 — "For he shall give his angels charge over thee, to keep
        thee in all thy ways. They shall bear thee up in their
        hands, lest thou dash thy foot against a stone." (KJV) —
        Angelic guardianship. Satan quoted this to Jesus; Jesus
        responded that we must not TEST God (Matt 4:7). The promise
        is real but not a license for recklessness.

v.14-16 — "Because he hath set his love upon me, therefore will I
        deliver him... He shall call upon me, and I will answer
        him: I will be with him in trouble." (KJV) — GOD SPEAKS
        directly. The condition: "set his love upon me" (chashaq =
        to cling, be attached). The promises: deliverance, exalted
        position, answered prayer, divine presence in trouble,
        rescue, honor, long life, shown salvation. Seven promises
        from God's own mouth.

CROSS-REFERENCES
------------------------------------
v.1   — Psalm 27:5 — "In the secret of his tabernacle shall he
         hide me." Same secret place concept.
v.4   — Ruth 2:12 — "Under whose wings thou art come to trust."
         Same wing-protection imagery.
v.11  — Matthew 4:6 — Satan quotes this verse to tempt Jesus.
v.11  — Hebrews 1:14 — "Are they not all ministering spirits,
         sent forth to minister for them who shall be heirs of
         salvation?" Angels serving believers.
v.13  — Luke 10:19 — Jesus: "I give unto you power to tread on
         serpents and scorpions." Authority over evil.
v.13  — Romans 16:20 — "The God of peace shall bruise Satan under
         your feet shortly."

COMMENTARY REFERENCES

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.3 — KJV: "noisome pestilence" / ESV: "deadly pestilence" —
      "Noisome" (KJV) = harmful, noxious (archaic English). ESV's
      "deadly" is clearer. The Hebrew "deber havvot" = plague/
      pestilence of destruction.

v.5-6 — Four dangers listed: terror by night (sudden attack),
      arrow by day (deliberate attack), pestilence in darkness
      (hidden disease), destruction at noonday (open calamity).
      Together they cover all 24 hours and all types of threat.

v.13 — KJV: "the dragon" / ESV: "the serpent" — Hebrew "tannin"
       = serpent, dragon, sea monster. Applied spiritually: the
       believer has authority to tread on every form of evil power.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
- v.1 — NET: "As for you, the one who lives in the shelter of the Most High,
  and resides in the protective shadow of the Sovereign One" — NET uniquely
  renders "Shaddai" (Almighty) as "the Sovereign One," emphasizing God's
  ruling authority. Also adds "protective" to "shadow," making the metaphor
  explicit. WEB: "rest in the shadow of the Almighty" — uses "rest" rather
  than "abide" (KJV/ASV), conveying peaceful security.
- v.2 — ASV/WEB: "Jehovah"/"Yahweh" — the divine personal name made explicit
  where KJV/ESV use "the LORD." NET uses "the Lord."
- v.3 — NET: "the snare of the hunter and from the destructive plague" —
  "hunter" instead of "fowler" (KJV/ASV/WEB) modernizes the bird-trap
  imagery. "Destructive plague" vs. "noisome pestilence" (KJV) or "deadly
  pestilence" (ESV). WEB retains "fowler" — preserving the specific imagery
  of someone who sets traps for birds (Satan's deceptive schemes).
- v.4 — NET: "He will shelter you with his wings; you will find safety under
  his wings. His faithfulness is like a shield or a protective wall" —
  "shelter" (vs. "cover") emphasizes enclosure. "Protective wall" for Hebrew
  "socherah" where KJV has "buckler" and ASV has "buckler." WEB: "His
  faithfulness is your shield and rampart" — "rampart" is a strong military
  fortification image. KJV's "his truth" → WEB/NET/ESV use "faithfulness"
  (Hebrew "emunah" = steadfastness/reliability).
- v.4 — WEB: "He will cover you with his feathers" — retains KJV's tender
  image of feathers/pinions. ASV uses "pinions" (formal wing term). NET drops
  "feathers" for the simpler "wings" repeated twice.
- v.9 — NET: "For you have taken refuge in the Lord, my shelter, the Most
  High" — restructures the confusing KJV/ASV verse into a clear statement.
  The speaker changes in vv.9-13 (from declaration to personal testimony to
  divine speech in vv.14-16). NET clarifies the shifts.
- v.11 — NET: "he will order his angels to protect you in all you do" —
  "order" is more commanding than "give...charge" (KJV/ESV/ASV). WEB: "put
  his angels in charge of you, to guard you" — "guard" conveys active military
  protection. This is the verse Satan quoted to Jesus in the wilderness
  temptation (Matthew 4:6).
- v.13 — NET: "You will subdue a lion and a snake; you will trample underfoot
  a young lion and a serpent" — uses "subdue" where others have "tread upon,"
  implying conquest not just walking over. WEB: "lion and cobra...young lion
  and the serpent" — "cobra" is more specific than KJV's "adder" or NET's
  "snake," identifying a particularly dangerous venomous species.
- v.14-16 — NET uniquely formats these as direct divine speech with "The Lord
  says" introduction, making the speaker change explicit. Other translations
  leave it implicit. NET: "Because he is devoted to me...because he is loyal
  to me" — renders "set his love" as "devoted" and "known my name" as "loyal,"
  interpreting the relational language as covenant commitment.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Protection is for those who DWELL, not visit (v.1). The secret
   place is not for tourists but for residents. Consistent,
   intimate communion with God is the condition for the promises
   that follow. Do you live there or just visit?

2. God's protection covers all angles (vv.5-6). Night and day,
   secret and open, disease and violence — every type of threat
   is addressed. There is no angle from which danger can approach
   that God has not anticipated.

3. God's promises are not a license for presumption (vv.11-12).
   Satan used these words to tempt Jesus into recklessness. Trust
   is not testing. Faith walks in God's ways (v.11 — "in all thy
   WAYS"); it does not manufacture crises to prove God.

4. "I will be with him IN trouble" (v.15). Not "I will keep him
   FROM trouble" but "I will be with him IN it." The promise is
   presence, not exemption. God doesn't promise a trouble-free
   life but a never-alone life.
