Psalms - Chapter 146
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
A Hallel psalm opening the final five — 'Praise ye the LORD; praise the LORD, O my soul'; trust not in princes who perish; happy is he whose help is the God of Jacob who executes justice, feeds the hungry, and lifts up the fallen.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Multiple authors — primarily David (73 psalms attributed),
plus Asaph (12), Sons of Korah (11), Solomon (2), Moses (1),
Heman (1), Ethan (1), and anonymous. The Psalter was compiled over
approximately 1000 years and served as Israel's hymnal and prayer
book. The book is divided into five 'books' (1-41, 42-72, 73-89,
90-106, 107-150), paralleling the five books of Moses. Key themes:
worship, lament, praise, trust, kingship, creation, wisdom,
Messianic prophecy, and the full range of human emotion brought
before God.

Classification: Hallelujah Psalm (begins the Final Five)
Attributed Author: Anonymous
Key Themes: Lifelong praise, trusting God over princes, God as
helper of the helpless, God who executes justice for the
oppressed, the Creator who cares for the marginalized

Historical Context: Psalm 146 begins the final five "Hallelujah
Psalms" (146-150) that close the Psalter. Each begins and ends
with "Praise the LORD" (Hallelujah). These five psalms form a
crescendo of praise that builds to the universal climax of Psalm
150. Psalm 146 focuses on WHY God should be praised: He is the
reliable helper (unlike human princes who die), the Creator who
keeps faith forever, the champion of the oppressed, hungry,
prisoners, blind, bowed down, strangers, widows, and fatherless.
This psalm heavily influenced Mary's Magnificat (Luke 1:46-55).

Structure:
- Personal Commitment to Lifelong Praise (vv.1-2)
- Warning Against Trusting Humans (vv.3-4)
- The Blessed Trust in God (v.5)
- God's Character: Creator and Keeper (v.6)
- God's Actions: Justice and Mercy (vv.7-9)
- God's Eternal Reign (v.10)

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Zion (v.10): The hill on which Jerusalem/the Temple stood; often used poetically for God's dwelling.

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. Don't put ultimate trust in leaders (vv.3-4). Vote, engage,
   participate — but don't TRUST princes with your ultimate hope.
   They are mortal. Their plans perish. Only God's plans endure.

2. God champions the marginalized (vv.7-9). If you want to be
   like God, care for who He cares for: the oppressed, hungry,
   imprisoned, blind, bowed down, strangers, orphans, widows.
   This is God's heart.

3. Praise for a lifetime (v.2). "While I live will I praise the
   LORD: I will sing praises unto my God while I have any being."
   Praise is not seasonal — it's lifelong. As long as you have
   being, you have reason to praise.

4. God reigns forever (v.10). "The LORD shall reign for ever."
   Every other ruler perishes. This King endures. Anchor yourself
   to the eternal throne.
