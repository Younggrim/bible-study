Psalms - Chapter 53
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
A wisdom psalm (variant of Psalm 14) — 'The fool hath said in his heart, There is no God'; all are corrupt, none does good; God scatters the bones of the ungodly, and salvation comes from Zion.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Multiple authors — primarily David (73 psalms attributed),
plus Asaph (12), Sons of Korah (11), Solomon (2), Moses (1),
Heman (1), Ethan (1), and anonymous. The Psalter was compiled over
approximately 1000 years and served as Israel's hymnal and prayer
book. The book is divided into five 'books' (1-41, 42-72, 73-89,
90-106, 107-150), paralleling the five books of Moses. Key themes:
worship, lament, praise, trust, kingship, creation, wisdom,
Messianic prophecy, and the full range of human emotion brought
before God.

Classification: Wisdom Psalm
Attributed Author: David
Key Themes: Universal human depravity, atheistic folly, God's
sovereign observation, total corruption, hope for salvation from
Zion

Historical Context: Psalm 53 is nearly identical to Psalm 14, with
the primary difference being the use of "Elohim" (God) instead of
"Yahweh" (LORD) and a different v.5. The duplication likely
reflects different liturgical traditions — one Davidic/Yahwistic,
one Elohistic. It is a Maskil (instructive poem) set to "Mahalath"
(possibly a tune name meaning "sickness" or "dancing"). The psalm
surveys humanity from God's perspective and finds universal
corruption. Paul quotes this psalm extensively in Romans 3:10-12 to
establish the universal need for grace. Verse 5 differs
significantly from Psalm 14:5-6, suggesting a specific military
deliverance (God scattering the bones of Israel's enemies).

Structure:
- The Fool's Declaration and Its Fruit (v.1)
- God's Investigation of Humanity (vv.2-3)
- The Ignorance of Evildoers (v.4)
- God's Judgment on the Wicked (v.5)
- Longing for Salvation (v.6)


KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "The fool hath said in his heart, There is no God. Corrupt
        are they, and have done abominable iniquity: there is none
        that doeth good." (KJV) — "Fool" (nabal) is not
        intellectual deficiency but moral bankruptcy. The denial of
        God is not a conclusion of reason but a wish of the heart.
        The result: corruption and abominable behavior.

v.2  — "God looked down from heaven upon the children of men, to
        see if there were any that did understand, that did seek
        God." (KJV) — God's omniscient investigation. He is not
        ignorant of human affairs — He actively observes. The
        criteria: understanding and seeking God. The verdict:
        universal failure.

v.3  — "Every one of them is gone back: they are altogether become
        filthy; there is none that doeth good, no, not one." (KJV)
        — The totality is absolute: every one, altogether, none,
        not one. This is the biblical doctrine of total depravity
        — not that every person is as evil as possible, but that
        no part of humanity is untouched by sin.

v.6  — "Oh that the salvation of Israel were come out of Zion!"
        (KJV) — After the devastating survey of human corruption,
        the only hope is divine intervention. Salvation must come
        FROM God (out of Zion), not from human effort.

CROSS-REFERENCES
------------------------------------
v.1   — Psalm 14:1 — The parallel version using "Yahweh." Compare
         the differences to understand editorial intentions.
v.1   — Romans 1:22 — "Professing themselves to be wise, they
         became fools." The progression of suppressing truth.
v.3   — Romans 3:10-12 — Paul's direct quotation: "There is none
         righteous, no, not one... there is none that doeth good."
v.4   — Micah 3:2-3 — Leaders who "eat the flesh of my people"
         — the same imagery of the powerful consuming the weak.
v.5   — 2 Kings 19:35 — God scattering the army of Sennacherib —
         a possible historical referent for God scattering bones.
v.6   — Romans 11:26 — "There shall come out of Sion the
         Deliverer." The ultimate fulfillment of this longing.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "Corrupt are they, and have done abominable iniquity" /
      ESV: "They are corrupt, doing abominable iniquity" — Minor
      difference; ESV's present participle emphasizes ongoing
      corruption rather than a completed act.

v.5 — KJV: "God hath scattered the bones of him that encampeth
      against thee" / ESV: "God scatters the bones of him who
      encamps against you" — This verse differs significantly from
      Psalm 14:5-6 and likely refers to a specific military
      victory where God defeated Israel's besiegers.

v.6 — KJV: "When God bringeth back the captivity" / ESV: "When
      God restores the fortunes" — Hebrew "shub shebut" can mean
      either "return the captivity" or "restore the fortunes."
      ESV's broader rendering works better as the psalm predates
      the Babylonian exile.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Atheism is a heart condition, not a head condition (v.1). "Said
   in his heart" — this is desire masquerading as philosophy. People
   deny God not because the evidence is insufficient but because
   the implications are unwelcome.

2. God sees everything (v.2). There is no hidden corruption. God
   "looks down from heaven" — His investigation is thorough and His
   verdict is final. We cannot hide our true spiritual state.

3. The diagnosis is total (v.3). "Not even one." Before we can
   appreciate grace, we must accept the diagnosis. No one naturally
   seeks God. Every genuine movement toward God is itself evidence
   of His prior movement toward us.

4. Hope comes only from God (v.6). After total depravity, the only
   possible remedy is total grace. Salvation comes "out of Zion" —
   from God's dwelling place, by God's initiative, through God's
   power. This is the gospel in seed form.
