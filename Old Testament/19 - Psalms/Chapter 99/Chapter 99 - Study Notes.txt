Psalms - Chapter 99
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
An enthronement psalm — 'The LORD reigneth; let the people tremble'; He is great in Zion, a lover of justice; Moses, Aaron, and Samuel called upon Him and He answered; He is holy (repeated three times).

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Multiple authors — primarily David (73 psalms attributed),
plus Asaph (12), Sons of Korah (11), Solomon (2), Moses (1),
Heman (1), Ethan (1), and anonymous. The Psalter was compiled over
approximately 1000 years and served as Israel's hymnal and prayer
book. The book is divided into five 'books' (1-41, 42-72, 73-89,
90-106, 107-150), paralleling the five books of Moses. Key themes:
worship, lament, praise, trust, kingship, creation, wisdom,
Messianic prophecy, and the full range of human emotion brought
before God.

Classification: Enthronement / Holiness Psalm
Attributed Author: Anonymous
Key Themes: The LORD reigns, God's holiness (triple refrain),
trembling before God, justice and equity, God answering His
servants, forgiveness AND consequences

Historical Context: The last of the enthronement psalms (93-99).
Its distinguishing feature is the triple refrain "He is holy"
(vv.3,5,9) — echoing the Seraphim's "Holy, holy, holy" in Isaiah
6:3. The psalm moves from God's cosmic reign (vv.1-3) to His just
rule over Jacob (vv.4-5) to His historical relationship with Moses,
Aaron, and Samuel (vv.6-9). These three intercessors exemplify the
psalm's dual theme: God forgives (answered their prayers) AND
avenges (took vengeance on their inventions/wrongdoings). Grace
and accountability coexist in God's holiness. He pardons sin but
does not ignore consequences.

Structure:
- The LORD Reigns: Tremble! (vv.1-3)
- The King Loves Justice (vv.4-5)
- God Answered Moses, Aaron, Samuel (vv.6-7)
- Forgiveness AND Consequences (vv.8-9)

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Zion (v.2): The hill on which Jerusalem/the Temple stood; often used poetically for God's dwelling.

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. Holiness should produce trembling (v.1). Not casual familiarity
   but holy awe. God is accessible through grace — but He is still
   HOLY. Approach with reverence, not flippancy.

2. God forgives AND allows consequences (v.8). These are not
   contradictions. A parent can forgive a child's misdeed while
   still enforcing natural consequences. God pardons sin while
   allowing its earthly effects to play out. Grace does not erase
   all consequences.

3. God answers those who call on Him (v.6). Moses called — God
   answered. Aaron called — God answered. Samuel called — God
   answered. The pattern: those who call receive answers. Do you
   call? Do you expect answers?

4. "He is holy" is the foundation of everything (vv.3,5,9). Not
   just one attribute among many — holiness IS God's essential
   character. Everything else flows from this. Worship, justice,
   forgiveness, consequences — all are expressions of His holiness.
