Psalms - Chapter 80
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
A communal lament — 'Give ear, O Shepherd of Israel'; the vine God brought from Egypt is now burned and broken; three times the refrain pleads: 'Restore us, O God; let thy face shine, and we shall be saved.'

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Multiple authors — primarily David (73 psalms attributed),
plus Asaph (12), Sons of Korah (11), Solomon (2), Moses (1),
Heman (1), Ethan (1), and anonymous. The Psalter was compiled over
approximately 1000 years and served as Israel's hymnal and prayer
book. The book is divided into five 'books' (1-41, 42-72, 73-89,
90-106, 107-150), paralleling the five books of Moses. Key themes:
worship, lament, praise, trust, kingship, creation, wisdom,
Messianic prophecy, and the full range of human emotion brought
before God.

Classification: Community Lament Psalm
Attributed Author: Asaph
Key Themes: God as Shepherd of Israel, the vine metaphor, national
devastation, "restore us" refrain, the shining face of God, prayer
for the "man of thy right hand"

Historical Context: Set to "Shoshannim Eduth" (Lilies of the
Testimony), this psalm addresses the northern tribes specifically
(Ephraim, Benjamin, Manasseh — Joseph's descendants, v.1-2). It
likely dates to the Assyrian crisis — either the fall of the
northern kingdom in 722 BC or the period immediately preceding it.
The vine metaphor (vv.8-16) tells Israel's story: planted by God
out of Egypt, grown to fill the land, then BROKEN — hedges down,
wild animals devouring it. The repeated refrain "Turn us again...
cause thy face to shine... we shall be saved" (vv.3,7,19) is the
psalm's heartbeat. The mysterious "man of thy right hand" (v.17)
may point to Benjamin (whose name means "son of the right hand"),
to the king, or Messianically to Christ.

Structure:
- Invocation: Shepherd, Shine Forth (vv.1-2)
- First Refrain: Restore Us (v.3)
- Lament: Tears and Mockery (vv.4-6)
- Second Refrain: Restore Us (v.7)
- The Vine Allegory (vv.8-16)
- Prayer for the Man of God's Right Hand (vv.17-18)
- Third Refrain: Restore Us (v.19)


CROSS-REFERENCES
------------------------------------
v.1   — Psalm 23:1 — "The LORD is my shepherd." Personal version
         of the corporate appeal here.
v.1   — Exodus 25:22 — God dwelling between the cherubim on the
         ark of the covenant.
v.8   — Isaiah 5:1-7 — The Song of the Vineyard. Same vine/Israel
         allegory with similar judgment language.
v.8   — John 15:1-5 — Jesus: "I am the true vine." The ultimate
         fulfillment of Israel's vine identity.
v.12  — Isaiah 5:5 — "I will take away the hedge thereof, and it
         shall be eaten up." Same broken-hedge imagery.
v.17  — Daniel 7:13 — "One like the Son of man came with the
         clouds of heaven." The Messianic "son of man."

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. God is both tender Shepherd and enthroned King (v.1). He leads
   gently AND reigns absolutely. Approach Him with intimacy AND
   reverence — He is not one or the other but both.

2. God plants, grows, AND prunes (vv.8-12). The same God who
   transplanted you, rooted you, and grew you can also break down
   your hedges. Pruning is not abandonment — it is husbandry.
   Trust the Vinedresser even when He cuts.

3. "Restore us" is a prayer God loves to answer (vv.3,7,19).
   Restoration is God's specialty. He is the God of comebacks.
   Pray it repeatedly, pray it boldly — each time with increasing
   faith.

4. Salvation is tied to God's face shining (v.19). "Cause thy face
   to shine, and we shall be saved." Salvation comes from God's
   favorable attention. His turned face is judgment; His shining
   face is salvation. Seek His face above all.
