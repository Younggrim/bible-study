Psalms - Chapter 82
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
A prophetic psalm — God stands in the divine assembly judging the 'gods' (unjust rulers/judges): 'How long will ye judge unjustly?' They are told 'ye shall die like men'; God is called to arise and judge the earth.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Multiple authors — primarily David (73 psalms attributed),
plus Asaph (12), Sons of Korah (11), Solomon (2), Moses (1),
Heman (1), Ethan (1), and anonymous. The Psalter was compiled over
approximately 1000 years and served as Israel's hymnal and prayer
book. The book is divided into five 'books' (1-41, 42-72, 73-89,
90-106, 107-150), paralleling the five books of Moses. Key themes:
worship, lament, praise, trust, kingship, creation, wisdom,
Messianic prophecy, and the full range of human emotion brought
before God.

Classification: Prophetic / Judgment Psalm
Attributed Author: Asaph
Key Themes: God judging unjust judges, human rulers as "gods,"
defense of the weak, the foundations of earth shaken by injustice,
mortality of corrupt authorities, God inheriting all nations

Historical Context: This brief but powerful psalm depicts a divine
courtroom scene: God stands in the "congregation of the mighty"
(divine council) and pronounces judgment on the "gods" — human
rulers or judges who have perverted justice. Jesus quotes v.6 in
John 10:34 ("Is it not written in your law, I said, Ye are gods?")
to defend His own divine claims. The "gods" (elohim) here refers
either to human judges who represent God's authority on earth (as
in Exodus 21:6; 22:8-9), or to angelic beings assigned to nations
(as in Deuteronomy 32:8 LXX). Either interpretation yields the
same message: those given divine authority who misuse it will be
judged by the Supreme God.

Structure:
- God Stands in Judgment (v.1)
- Accusation: Unjust Judging (v.2)
- Command: Defend the Vulnerable (vv.3-4)
- Verdict: Willful Ignorance Shakes the Earth (v.5)
- Sentence: You Are Gods but Will Die Like Men (vv.6-7)
- Prayer: Arise, O God (v.8)


KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "God standeth in the congregation of the mighty; he
        judgeth among the gods." (KJV) — God presides over ALL
        authorities. No judge, ruler, or power operates outside
        His courtroom. He evaluates every exercise of authority.

v.3-4 — "Defend the poor and fatherless: do justice to the
        afflicted and needy. Deliver the poor and needy: rid them
        out of the hand of the wicked." (KJV) — God's definition
        of what leaders should DO: defend the weak, do justice for
        the afflicted, deliver the needy. Authority exists to
        protect the powerless.

v.6  — "I have said, Ye are gods; and all of you are children of
        the most High." (KJV) — Human judges share in divine
        authority ("gods") but are NOT divine in nature. Jesus
        uses this in John 10:34 — if Scripture calls mere human
        judges "gods," how much more should the actual Son of God
        bear that title?

v.8  — "Arise, O God, judge the earth: for thou shalt inherit all
        nations." (KJV) — The psalm's climax: since human judges
        have failed, GOD must arise and judge directly. His
        inheritance is all nations — universal jurisdiction.

CROSS-REFERENCES
------------------------------------
v.1   — 1 Kings 22:19 — "I saw the LORD sitting on his throne,
         and all the host of heaven standing by him." Divine
         council imagery.
v.3-4 — Isaiah 1:17 — "Learn to do well; seek judgment, relieve
         the oppressed, judge the fatherless, plead for the widow."
v.5   — Isaiah 24:5-6 — "The earth also is defiled under the
         inhabitants thereof; because they have transgressed the
         laws." Injustice destabilizes creation.
v.6   — John 10:34-36 — Jesus quotes this verse in defense of
         His deity claims.
v.6   — Exodus 21:6; 22:8-9 — Judges called "elohim" (gods)
         in the Mosaic law.
v.8   — Revelation 11:15 — "The kingdoms of this world are become
         the kingdoms of our Lord." God inheriting nations.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "congregation of the mighty" / ESV: "divine council" —
      Hebrew "adat-El" = assembly/congregation of God. ESV's
      "divine council" reflects current scholarly understanding of
      the ancient Near Eastern background: God presiding over a
      heavenly court of subordinate beings.

v.5 — KJV: "all the foundations of the earth are out of course" /
      ESV: "all the foundations of the earth are shaken" — When
      justice fails, the very structures of society and creation
      are destabilized. Injustice is cosmic disruption, not merely
      social inconvenience.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Authority exists to serve the vulnerable (vv.3-4). Any
   authority — governmental, ecclesial, familial — that does not
   defend the weak has betrayed its purpose. Leadership is
   measured by its protection of the powerless.

2. Injustice shakes creation's foundations (v.5). When rulers
   pervert justice, it's not just a political problem — it's a
   cosmic one. The world itself becomes unstable. Justice is
   foundational to reality.

3. Human authority is delegated and accountable (vv.6-7). "You are
   gods" — but you will die like men. No human authority is
   ultimate. Every ruler answers to the Supreme Ruler. Power
   without accountability is an illusion.

4. When human justice fails, cry for divine justice (v.8). "Arise,
   O God, judge the earth." When courts are corrupt and leaders
   are unjust, the final appeal is to God Himself. He will judge
   what humans will not.
