Psalms - Chapter 135
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
A Hallel hymn of praise — 'Praise the LORD, for the LORD is good'; He does whatever pleases Him in heaven and earth; He struck Egypt and Canaan's kings; idols are worthless, but Israel blesses the LORD from Zion.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Multiple authors — primarily David (73 psalms attributed),
plus Asaph (12), Sons of Korah (11), Solomon (2), Moses (1),
Heman (1), Ethan (1), and anonymous. The Psalter was compiled over
approximately 1000 years and served as Israel's hymnal and prayer
book. The book is divided into five 'books' (1-41, 42-72, 73-89,
90-106, 107-150), paralleling the five books of Moses. Key themes:
worship, lament, praise, trust, kingship, creation, wisdom,
Messianic prophecy, and the full range of human emotion brought
before God.

Classification: Hymn of Praise / Liturgical Psalm
Attributed Author: Anonymous
Key Themes: God's sovereignty over nature and nations, the
superiority of the LORD over idols, God's covenant name, praise
as the calling of His servants, the futility of idolatry

Historical Context: Psalm 135 is a compilation psalm — drawing
from many earlier texts (Psalms 113, 115, 134, Exodus, Deuteronomy)
to create a comprehensive liturgical praise. It combines creation
theology, Exodus history, and anti-idol polemic into one hymn for
temple worship. Positioned between the Songs of Ascents (120-134)
and the Great Hallel (136), it serves as a transitional praise
that summarizes major themes: God's election of Israel (v.4), His
power over nature (vv.6-7), His deliverance from Egypt (vv.8-12),
and the emptiness of idols (vv.15-18).

Structure:
- Call to Praise (vv.1-4)
- God's Greatness in Nature (vv.5-7)
- God's Acts in History (vv.8-12)
- God's Eternal Name (vv.13-14)
- Futility of Idols (vv.15-18)
- Call to Bless the LORD (vv.19-21)

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. God does whatever He pleases (v.6). There is no domain beyond
   His sovereignty. Heaven, earth, seas, depths — all under His
   will. Rest in a God who is truly in control.

2. He chose you (v.4). "The LORD hath chosen Jacob unto himself."
   Election is personal — God chose you for Himself. You are His
   peculiar treasure.

3. Idols are still empty (vv.15-18). Modern idols don't look like
   silver statues, but they share the same quality: they cannot
   see, hear, or save. Whatever you trust besides God is equally
   impotent.

4. Praise is pleasant (v.3). Don't approach worship as medicine to
   endure. It is PLEASANT — enjoyable, satisfying, good for you
   and delightful to experience.
