Psalms - Chapter 68
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
A triumphal processional psalm — God arises and His enemies scatter; He is father to the fatherless, leads captives to freedom, marches through the wilderness, ascends on high receiving gifts; 'blessed be the Lord who daily loadeth us with benefits.'

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Multiple authors — primarily David (73 psalms attributed),
plus Asaph (12), Sons of Korah (11), Solomon (2), Moses (1),
Heman (1), Ethan (1), and anonymous. The Psalter was compiled over
approximately 1000 years and served as Israel's hymnal and prayer
book. The book is divided into five 'books' (1-41, 42-72, 73-89,
90-106, 107-150), paralleling the five books of Moses. Key themes:
worship, lament, praise, trust, kingship, creation, wisdom,
Messianic prophecy, and the full range of human emotion brought
before God.

Classification: Hymn of Praise / Processional Psalm / Royal Psalm
Attributed Author: David
Key Themes: God as warrior-king, the march of God through history,
care for the vulnerable, the ascension of God, gifts for men,
God's victorious procession, universal praise

Historical Context: One of the most complex and magnificent psalms
in the Psalter. Likely composed for the procession of the Ark of
the Covenant to Jerusalem (2 Samuel 6) or for use in subsequent
festival processions. The opening words echo Numbers 10:35: "Rise
up, LORD, and let thine enemies be scattered" — the ancient cry
when the Ark moved forward. The psalm traces God's march through
history: from Sinai (vv.7-8) through the wilderness to the
Promised Land, to the establishment of Zion as His dwelling place.
Paul quotes v.18 in Ephesians 4:8 and applies it to Christ's
ascension, making this psalm deeply Messianic.

Structure:
- God Arises: Enemies Scatter, Righteous Rejoice (vv.1-6)
- God's March Through History (vv.7-14)
- Zion Chosen Over Bashan (vv.15-18)
- Praise for Daily Benefits and Salvation (vv.19-23)
- The Processional Described (vv.24-27)
- Prayer for God's Power and Universal Praise (vv.28-35)


CROSS-REFERENCES
------------------------------------
v.1   — Numbers 10:35 — "Rise up, LORD, and let thine enemies be
         scattered." The Ark-procession cry.
v.5   — James 1:27 — "Pure religion... is this, To visit the
         fatherless and widows." God's heart reflected.
v.8   — Exodus 19:18 — Sinai trembling at God's presence. Same
         theophany recalled.
v.18  — Ephesians 4:8-10 — Paul's quotation and Christological
         interpretation of this verse.
v.18  — Acts 2:33 — "Having received of the Father the promise of
         the Holy Ghost, he hath shed forth this." Christ's
         ascension gifts.
v.19  — Lamentations 3:22-23 — "His compassions fail not. They are
         new every morning." Daily benefits/mercies.
v.31  — Acts 8:27 — The Ethiopian eunuch — early fulfillment of
         "Ethiopia shall stretch out her hands unto God."

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. The God who scatters enemies fathers the fatherless (vv.1,5).
   God's power and tenderness are not contradictions — they are
   companions. He uses His might to protect the vulnerable. His
   strength serves His compassion.

2. God daily loads us with benefits (v.19). Not weekly, not
   annually — DAILY. The challenge is awareness. Are you noticing
   what God loads onto you each day? Or are you too focused on
   what you lack to see what you have?

3. God holds the exits from death (v.20). The ultimate power. No
   disease, no enemy, no circumstance has the final word — God
   does. Death's door has a key, and God holds it.

4. The ascended Christ gives gifts to His people (v.18). Christ's
   ascension was not departure — it was enthronement for the
   purpose of gift-giving. The Spirit, spiritual gifts, grace —
   all flow from the ascended King to His people.
