Jeremiah - Chapter 36
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God tells Jeremiah to write ALL his prophecies on a scroll. Baruch reads it publicly in the temple; officials hear it and tremble; they bring it to King Jehoiakim. The king cuts the scroll column by column and burns it in his winter fireplace. God tells Jeremiah: write it again — and ADD MORE. Jehoiakim will have no descendant on David's throne.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Jeremiah son of Hilkiah (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Historical Narrative — The Scroll Burned and Rewritten
Key Themes: God's word preserved in writing, the public reading of Scripture, the king's contempt for God's word, you can't destroy God's word by burning it, Baruch the faithful scribe

Historical Context: Dated to Jehoiakim's 4th year (605 BC) — but the burning happens in his 5th year (604 BC). Jeremiah dictates ALL his prophecies (from 627 BC onward — 23 years of messages) to his scribe Baruch. The scroll is read three times: by Baruch publicly (v.10), by Micaiah to officials (vv.11-19), and by Jehudi to the king (vv.21-23). The officials TREMBLE (v.16) and urge Jeremiah to hide. But Jehoiakim shows supreme contempt: he cuts the scroll with a knife and burns it piece by piece. God's response: REWRITE it. Add more. And Jehoiakim's line is cursed.

Structure:
- God's Command: Write a Scroll (vv.1-4)
- Baruch Reads in the Temple (vv.5-10)
- Officials Hear and Tremble (vv.11-19)
- The King Burns the Scroll (vv.20-26)
- God's Response: Write It Again — Plus More (vv.27-32)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.2 — "Take thee a roll of a book, and write therein all the words that I have spoken unto thee" (KJV) — God commands WRITTEN Scripture. Oral tradition is insufficient — these words must be PRESERVED in permanent form.

v.16 — "they were afraid both one and other, and said unto Baruch, We will surely tell the king of all these words" (KJV) — The officials FEAR. They take God's word seriously — unlike the king. Their trembling shows they understood the gravity.

v.23 — "when Jehudi had read three or four leaves, he cut it with the penknife, and cast it into the fire that was on the hearth, until all the roll was consumed" (KJV) — The king destroys God's word physically. Column by column, knife cut, fire. Systematic contempt for Scripture. But you can't destroy God's word by burning paper.

v.24 — "Yet they were not afraid, nor rent their garments, neither the king, nor any of his servants" (KJV) — NO FEAR. Contrast with Josiah (his father) who TORE his garments when Scripture was read (2 Kings 22:11). Same throne, opposite responses.

v.28 — "Take thee again another roll, and write in it all the former words" (KJV) — God's response to book-burning: REWRITE. God's word cannot be destroyed. Burn one copy; God produces another — EXPANDED.

v.32 — "and there were added besides unto them many like words" (KJV) — The second edition is LONGER. Burning God's word doesn't diminish it — it MULTIPLIES it. Attempted destruction produces expansion.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.2 — "roll of a book" (KJV) / "scroll" (ESV) — Hebrew "megillat sepher" = a scroll/roll of writing. Papyrus or leather sheets sewn together into a long roll.

v.18 — "from his mouth" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "mippiv" = from his mouth. Jeremiah DICTATED; Baruch WROTE. Divine inspiration through human process.

v.23 — "penknife" (KJV) / "knife" (ESV) — Hebrew "ta'ar ha-sopher" = the scribe's knife (used for cutting papyrus and trimming pens). Jehoiakim uses the SCRIBE'S tool to destroy the scribe's work — ironic desecration.

v.23 — "leaves" (KJV) / "columns" (ESV) — Hebrew "delathot" = doors/columns. Scrolls were written in columns; Jehoiakim cut off 3-4 columns at a time and burned them.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
The Scroll — Jeremiah's collected prophecies (23 years' worth) dictated to Baruch. The first "edition" of the book of Jeremiah — burned by the king but rewritten by God's command.

Baruch — Jeremiah's faithful scribe/secretary. Wrote at Jeremiah's dictation, read publicly, and preserved the prophet's words. Without Baruch, we might not have the book.

Penknife — The scribe's blade used for cutting writing material. Jehoiakim weaponizes the writing tool against writing itself — using literacy's instrument to destroy literacy's product.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. You can't destroy God's word (vv.23,28). Every attempt to suppress, ban, or burn Scripture has FAILED — and usually produced wider distribution. God's word survives every fire.

2. Josiah vs. Jehoiakim (v.24 + 2 Kings 22:11). Same throne, same scroll, opposite responses. One tore his garments in repentance; one tore the scroll in contempt. How do YOU respond when God's word confronts you?

3. "There were added many like words" (v.32). Attempted destruction produces EXPANSION. When the enemy attacks God's purposes, God doesn't merely recover — He EXCEEDS the original. Expect multiplication after opposition.

4. Baruch's faithfulness. Without a faithful scribe willing to write, read publicly, and risk death — we might not have Jeremiah's book. Not everyone's calling is to SPEAK truth — some are called to PRESERVE it.

5. Officials who trembled (v.16). Some people take God's word seriously even when their king doesn't. In every corrupt system, there are individuals who fear God. Be one of them.
