Jeremiah - Chapter 17
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
"The heart is deceitful above all things, and desperately wicked: who can know it?" Judah's sin is engraved with an iron pen. Cursed is the man who trusts in man; blessed is the man who trusts in the LORD — like a tree planted by waters. God searches the heart. Jeremiah prays for vindication and delivers a Sabbath sermon at the gates of Jerusalem.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Jeremiah son of Hilkiah (see Chapter 1 notes for full
details).

Classification: Prophetic Wisdom Oracle + Confession + Sabbath
Sermon
Key Themes: The incurably wicked heart, cursed vs. blessed (trust
in man vs. trust in God), the tree by waters, God as heart-
searcher, Jeremiah's prayer for vindication, Sabbath faithfulness
as covenant test

Historical Context: This chapter contains some of Jeremiah's most
quoted and theologically profound statements. Verse 9 — "the heart
is deceitful above all things" — is foundational to the biblical
doctrine of human depravity. The contrast between the cursed man
(trusting flesh) and the blessed man (trusting God) parallels
Psalm 1. The Sabbath sermon (vv.19-27) was likely delivered at
one of Jerusalem's gates as a practical test of covenant
obedience — if they can't even keep the Sabbath, how will they
keep anything?

Structure:
- Judah's Sin: Engraved with Iron (vv.1-4)
- Cursed: Trusting Man / Blessed: Trusting God (vv.5-8)
- The Deceitful Heart (vv.9-10)
- The Partridge and Unjust Riches (v.11)
- The Glorious Throne and Fountain (vv.12-13)
- Jeremiah's Prayer: Heal Me, Vindicate Me (vv.14-18)
- The Sabbath Sermon at the Gate (vv.19-27)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.5  — "Thus saith the LORD; Cursed be the man that trusteth in
        man, and maketh flesh his arm, and whose heart departeth
        from the LORD" (KJV) — The curse: trusting human strength
        instead of divine sufficiency. Making FLESH your arm
        (strength) while your heart leaves God.

v.7  — "Blessed is the man that trusteth in the LORD, and whose
        hope the LORD is" (KJV) — The blessing: trust directed
        at God alone. Hope anchored in God, not circumstances.

v.8  — "For he shall be as a tree planted by the waters, and
        that spreadeth out her roots by the river, and shall not
        see when heat cometh, but her leaf shall be green" (KJV)
        — The tree metaphor (parallel to Psalm 1:3): deep roots
        reaching water, unmoved by drought or heat. Fruitful in
        every season because the SOURCE is constant.

v.9  — "The heart is deceitful above all things, and desperately
        wicked: who can know it?" (KJV) — The most devastating
        anthropological statement in Scripture. The heart doesn't
        just HAVE deceit — it IS deceit. Not partially corrupt but
        ABOVE ALL THINGS deceitful.

v.10 — "I the LORD search the heart, I try the reins, even to
        give every man according to his ways" (KJV) — If we can't
        know our own hearts (v.9), GOD can. He searches what is
        unsearchable to us. And He rewards/judges based on what
        He finds.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "iron pen... point of a diamond" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "et
      barzel... tsipporen shamir" = iron stylus with a diamond/
      flint point. Judah's sin is not written in pencil (erasable)
      but ENGRAVED permanently in stone/metal.

v.6  — "heath in the desert" (KJV) / "shrub in the desert" (ESV)
      — Hebrew "ar'ar ba-aravah" = a stunted bush in the
      wasteland. The person trusting flesh: barely surviving,
      alone, in barren ground. No water, no growth.

v.9  — "deceitful" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "aqov" = crooked, deceitful
      (same root as "Jacob" = supplanter). The heart is a
      "Jacob" — it deceives you about yourself.

v.9  — "desperately wicked" (KJV) / "beyond cure" (NIV) /
      "desperately sick" (ESV) — Hebrew "anush" = incurable,
      mortally sick. The heart is not mildly flawed but TERMINALLY
      ill. Only supernatural healing suffices.

v.11 — "partridge" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "qore" = a bird (partridge)
      that gathers/hatches eggs it didn't lay. Like the person who
      gains wealth unjustly — it will leave him mid-life.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Deceitful Heart — (v.9) The biblical doctrine of human depravity:
              the heart is not a reliable guide. It DECEIVES its
              own owner. Self-knowledge requires God's searchlight.

Tree by Waters — (v.8) The person rooted in God: unmoved by
              drought, evergreen, fruitful in all seasons. Stability
              from a constant SOURCE despite variable circumstances.

Iron Pen      — (v.1) Judah's sin permanently engraved — not a
              passing phase but ETCHED into their identity. Only
              God's new covenant (31:33) can overwrite it.

Sabbath Test  — (vv.19-27) A practical litmus test: if you can't
              even rest one day per week as God commanded, your
              obedience is nonexistent. Sabbath-keeping as evidence
              of covenant faithfulness.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "The heart is deceitful above all things" (v.9). Your heart
   LIES TO YOU. About your motives, your condition, your
   righteousness. Don't trust your feelings as the final authority
   — submit them to God's searchlight.

2. "Who can know it?" (v.9). YOU can't — but God can (v.10). If
   you want to know your own heart truthfully, ask God. He
   searches what is unsearchable to you. Pray: "Search me, O God"
   (Psalm 139:23).

3. Tree by waters vs. shrub in desert (vv.6-8). The ONLY
   difference between the two: where their roots go. Same
   environment, different SOURCE. Where are your roots reaching —
   toward God or toward human solutions?

4. Cursed: trusting man. Blessed: trusting God (vv.5,7). Binary
   choice. You're either in the "cursed" category or the "blessed"
   category. Which direction is your TRUST pointed? Not your
   words — your actual confidence?

5. The Sabbath test (vv.19-27). Can you REST? Can you stop
   producing, earning, striving for ONE DAY? If not, you're
   trusting in your own labor, not God's provision. Sabbath is
   a trust exercise.
