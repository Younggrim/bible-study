Jeremiah - Chapter 17
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
"The heart is deceitful above all things, and desperately wicked: who can know it?" Judah's sin is engraved with an iron pen. Cursed is the man who trusts in man; blessed is the man who trusts in the LORD — like a tree planted by waters. God searches the heart. Jeremiah prays for vindication and delivers a Sabbath sermon at the gates of Jerusalem.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Jeremiah son of Hilkiah (see Chapter 1 notes for full
details).

Classification: Prophetic Wisdom Oracle + Confession + Sabbath
Sermon
Key Themes: The incurably wicked heart, cursed vs. blessed (trust
in man vs. trust in God), the tree by waters, God as heart-
searcher, Jeremiah's prayer for vindication, Sabbath faithfulness
as covenant test

Historical Context: This chapter contains some of Jeremiah's most
quoted and theologically profound statements. Verse 9 — "the heart
is deceitful above all things" — is foundational to the biblical
doctrine of human depravity. The contrast between the cursed man
(trusting flesh) and the blessed man (trusting God) parallels
Psalm 1. The Sabbath sermon (vv.19-27) was likely delivered at
one of Jerusalem's gates as a practical test of covenant
obedience — if they can't even keep the Sabbath, how will they
keep anything?

Structure:
- Judah's Sin: Engraved with Iron (vv.1-4)
- Cursed: Trusting Man / Blessed: Trusting God (vv.5-8)
- The Deceitful Heart (vv.9-10)
- The Partridge and Unjust Riches (v.11)
- The Glorious Throne and Fountain (vv.12-13)
- Jeremiah's Prayer: Heal Me, Vindicate Me (vv.14-18)
- The Sabbath Sermon at the Gate (vv.19-27)

  what you cannot even see."

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. "The heart is deceitful above all things" (v.9). Your heart
   LIES TO YOU. About your motives, your condition, your
   righteousness. Don't trust your feelings as the final authority
   — submit them to God's searchlight.

2. "Who can know it?" (v.9). YOU can't — but God can (v.10). If
   you want to know your own heart truthfully, ask God. He
   searches what is unsearchable to you. Pray: "Search me, O God"
   (Psalm 139:23).

3. Tree by waters vs. shrub in desert (vv.6-8). The ONLY
   difference between the two: where their roots go. Same
   environment, different SOURCE. Where are your roots reaching —
   toward God or toward human solutions?

4. Cursed: trusting man. Blessed: trusting God (vv.5,7). Binary
   choice. You're either in the "cursed" category or the "blessed"
   category. Which direction is your TRUST pointed? Not your
   words — your actual confidence?

5. The Sabbath test (vv.19-27). Can you REST? Can you stop
   producing, earning, striving for ONE DAY? If not, you're
   trusting in your own labor, not God's provision. Sabbath is
   a trust exercise.
