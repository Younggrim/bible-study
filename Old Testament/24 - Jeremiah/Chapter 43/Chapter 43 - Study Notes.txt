Jeremiah - Chapter 43
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The remnant refuses God's word and goes to Egypt anyway — dragging Jeremiah with them. Upon arrival in Tahpanhes, God tells Jeremiah to bury stones at the palace entrance as a sign: Nebuchadnezzar will set his throne on THIS SPOT and spread his royal canopy over Egypt. The sword they fled from will find them in Egypt.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Jeremiah son of Hilkiah (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Historical Narrative + Prophetic Sign-Act
Key Themes: Disobedience despite clear guidance, the accusation against Jeremiah (Baruch manipulated you!), forced migration to Egypt, God's judgment reaching into Egypt, Nebuchadnezzar called "my servant" again

Historical Context: Despite God's explicit command to STAY in Judah (ch.42), the remnant leaders (Johanan, Azariah) accuse Jeremiah of lying — blaming Baruch for manipulating him (v.3). They take the entire community to Egypt, FORCING Jeremiah and Baruch along (v.6). They settle in Tahpanhes (a border fortress city in the eastern Nile Delta). God immediately commissions another sign-act: Jeremiah buries large stones at the entrance to Pharaoh's palace in Tahpanhes. Nebuchadnezzar will place his throne on those stones when he invades Egypt (fulfilled c. 568 BC when Nebuchadnezzar campaigned against Pharaoh Amasis).

Structure:
- The People Refuse: "You're Lying!" (vv.1-3)
- They Go to Egypt, Taking Jeremiah (vv.4-7)
- The Buried Stones: Nebuchadnezzar Is Coming HERE (vv.8-13)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.2 — "Thou speakest falsely: the LORD our God hath not sent thee to say, Go not into Egypt" (KJV) — Flat denial of God's word. Not "we disagree" but "God DIDN'T say that." They call God's prophet a liar because they don't like the message.

v.3 — "But Baruch the son of Neriah setteth thee on against us" (KJV) — Blame-shifting: it's not God speaking — it's Baruch manipulating Jeremiah. When you can't deny the message, attack the messenger's credibility.

v.7 — "So they came into the land of Egypt: for they obeyed not the voice of the LORD" (KJV) — The narrator's simple verdict: they disobeyed. All their reasoning, all their fear, all their blame-shifting — reduced to one sentence: they didn't obey.

v.10 — "I will send and take Nebuchadrezzar... and will set his throne upon these stones... and he shall spread his royal pavilion over them" (KJV) — Nebuchadnezzar will rule from the EXACT SPOT where Jeremiah buried the stones. You can't outrun God's instrument of judgment. Even Egypt isn't far enough.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.7 — "Tahpanhes" (KJV/ESV) — Greek "Daphne" — a fortress city on Egypt's eastern border. First stop for refugees entering from Palestine. Had a Pharaonic palace/administrative building.

v.9 — "brickkiln" (KJV) / "pavement" (ESV) — Hebrew "malben" = a brick terrace or brick-making platform at the palace entrance. The stones were buried IN the pavement — visible to locals as markers.

v.10 — "my servant" (KJV/ESV) — Third time Nebuchadnezzar is called God's "servant" (25:9; 27:6; 43:10). A pagan king serving divine purposes unknowingly.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Flight to Egypt — The disobedient remnant choosing Egypt (the place of original bondage) over staying in the promised land. Going BACKWARD in redemptive history.

Buried Stones — Prophetic markers: physical stones placed where Nebuchadnezzar's throne will stand. When fulfilled, those stones PROVE God spoke in advance.

Blame Baruch — The tactic of discrediting the prophet's associates rather than confronting the message itself. Ad hominem deflection.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Asking for guidance you've already rejected (vv.1-2 + ch.42). If you're going to ignore God's answer, don't bother asking. Or better: ask with genuine openness. The worst prayer is one where the answer is predetermined.

2. "Baruch made you say that" (v.3). When you can't handle God's message, you'll find someone to blame: the pastor, the book, the friend. But the message is still GOD'S regardless of who delivers it.

3. You can't outrun judgment (v.10). Egypt felt safe — far from Babylon. But Nebuchadnezzar follows. Changing geography doesn't change your spiritual condition. God's dealings with you aren't location-dependent.

4. They dragged Jeremiah (v.6). Disobedient people often pull faithful people into their consequences. Jeremiah didn't choose Egypt — he was FORCED. Beware: your disobedience affects others.
