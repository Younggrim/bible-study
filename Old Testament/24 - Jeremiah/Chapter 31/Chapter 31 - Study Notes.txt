Jeremiah - Chapter 31
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The crown jewel of Jeremiah — the NEW COVENANT. "I have loved thee with an everlasting love." Rachel weeps for her children but is told to stop crying — they're coming back. The days are coming when God will make a NEW covenant: "I will put my law in their inward parts, and write it in their hearts." No more teaching each other to know God — ALL will know Him, from least to greatest. Sins remembered no more.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Jeremiah son of Hilkiah (see Chapter 1 notes for full
details).

Classification: Prophetic Salvation Oracle — The New Covenant
(Book of Consolation, chs. 30-33)
Key Themes: Everlasting love, the return of Israel and Judah,
Rachel weeping (quoted in Matthew 2), the new covenant written
on hearts, sins forgotten, individual accountability, the
permanence of Israel (as permanent as sun and moon)

Historical Context: This is the MOST IMPORTANT chapter in
Jeremiah — and one of the most important in the OT. Written
during the darkest period (Babylonian siege or early exile), it
contains God's most radical promise: a NEW covenant replacing the
Sinai covenant that Israel broke. The new covenant's distinctive:
INTERNAL (written on hearts, not stone), UNIVERSAL (all will know
God), EFFECTIVE (sins actually forgiven, not merely covered).
Hebrews 8:8-12 quotes vv.31-34 as the longest OT quotation in
the NT, declaring it fulfilled in Christ. Jesus inaugurated the
new covenant at the Last Supper: "This cup is the new testament
in my blood" (Luke 22:20).

Structure:
- "I Have Loved Thee with an Everlasting Love" (vv.1-6)
- The Return of Israel: Sing, Gather, Ransom (vv.7-14)
- Rachel Weeps — But There Is Hope (vv.15-17)
- Ephraim Repents: God's Beloved Son (vv.18-20)
- Return, O Virgin Israel (vv.21-22)
- Judah Restored (vv.23-26)
- Individual Responsibility: No More "Sour Grapes" (vv.27-30)
- THE NEW COVENANT (vv.31-34)
- Israel as Permanent as Sun and Moon (vv.35-37)
- Jerusalem Rebuilt and Holy (vv.38-40)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.3  — "The LORD hath appeared of old unto me, saying, Yea, I
        have loved thee with an everlasting love: therefore with
        lovingkindness have I drawn thee" (KJV) — God's love is
        not temporary, conditional, or reactive. It's EVERLASTING
        — from eternity past. And it DRAWS (doesn't force) — with
        lovingkindness, not coercion.

v.15 — "A voice was heard in Ramah, lamentation, and bitter
        weeping; Rahel weeping for her children refused to be
        comforted for her children, because they were not" (KJV)
        — Rachel (buried near Bethlehem) weeping for her
        descendants being exiled/killed. Matthew 2:18 applies this
        to the slaughter of the innocents.

v.31 — "Behold, the days come, saith the LORD, that I will make
        a new covenant with the house of Israel and with the house
        of Judah" (KJV) — The announcement: NEW covenant. Not
        repaired, not renewed — NEW. Something that didn't exist
        before under the old system.

v.33 — "I will put my law in their inward parts, and write it in
        their hearts; and will be their God, and they shall be my
        people" (KJV) — THE distinctive: law INTERNAL, not
        external. Written on hearts, not tablets. The transformation
        is inside-out, not outside-in.

v.34 — "And they shall teach no more every man his neighbour...
        Know the LORD: for they shall all know me, from the least
        of them unto the greatest of them... for I will forgive
        their iniquity, and I will remember their sin no more"
        (KJV) — THREE characteristics of the new covenant:
        (1) universal knowledge of God, (2) comprehensive
        forgiveness, (3) divine forgetfulness of sin.

v.35-36 — "Thus saith the LORD, which giveth the sun for a light
        by day... If those ordinances depart from before me...
        then the seed of Israel also shall cease" (KJV) — Israel's
        permanence: as long as the sun, moon, and stars exist,
        Israel exists. God stakes His covenant on the COSMOS.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.3  — "everlasting love" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "ahavat olam" =
      love of eternity/perpetuity. Not recent, not conditional,
      not temporary — FROM ETERNITY and TO eternity.

v.3  — "drawn thee" (KJV) / "continued my faithfulness" (ESV) —
      Hebrew "meshakhtikh" (from "mashakh" = to draw/pull). God
      DRAWS His people — attracts, pulls toward Himself with
      lovingkindness. Not coercion but attraction.

v.31 — "new covenant" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "berit chadashah" =
      a covenant that is new/fresh/unprecedented. The ONLY place
      in the OT where "new covenant" appears. Jesus uses these
      exact words at the Last Supper.

v.33 — "inward parts" (KJV) / "within them" (ESV) — Hebrew
      "be-qirbam" = in their midst/interior. Deep inside — not
      surface compliance but core transformation.

v.34 — "remember no more" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "lo ezkor od" =
      I will not remember again. Divine CHOICE not to recall.
      God's omniscience isn't diminished — His judicial memory
      is deliberately cleared. He CHOOSES to forget.

v.3 — ASV: uses "Jehovah" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).
       NET: "In a faraway land the Lord will manifest himself to them...." for KJV's "The LORD hath appeared of old unto me, saying, Yea, I"
       WEB: uses "Yahweh" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).
v.31 — ASV: uses "Jehovah" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).
       NET: "Lord, “when" for KJV's "days come, saith the LORD, that"
       WEB: uses "Yahweh" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).
v.33 — ASV: uses "Jehovah" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).
       NET: "Israel after I plant them back in" for KJV's "Israel; After those days, saith"
       WEB: uses "Yahweh" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).
v.34 — ASV: uses "Jehovah" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).
       NET: "most important, will know me,” says" for KJV's "greatest of them, saith"
       WEB: uses "Yahweh" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
New Covenant — The unprecedented divine-human covenant replacing
              Sinai: internal (heart-written), universal (all know
              God), effective (sins permanently forgiven). Fulfilled
              in Christ's blood. The foundation of Christianity.

Everlasting Love — God's love that has no beginning point and no
              ending point. Not earned by human behavior, not lost
              by human failure. The motivation behind ALL God's
              redemptive acts.

Law on Hearts — The new covenant's defining feature: God's
              requirements internalized rather than externalized.
              Obedience flows from transformed nature, not external
              compulsion.

Rachel Weeping — Mother Rachel mourning her descendants' exile/
              death. Applied by Matthew to Herod's slaughter of
              Bethlehem's infants — connecting exile-grief with
              messianic-era grief.

Remember No More — God's sovereign decision to treat sins as if
              they never happened. Not inability to recall but
              REFUSAL to hold sins against forgiven people. Judicial
              amnesia by choice.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Everlasting love" (v.3). God's love for you didn't BEGIN when
   you were saved — it's EVERLASTING (from eternity). And it
   DRAWS you — His love is magnetic. If you feel drawn toward God,
   that drawing IS His love in action.

2. The new covenant is INTERNAL (v.33). Stop trying to obey from
   the outside in. External rules without internal transformation
   is the OLD covenant — and it failed. Ask God to write His law
   on your HEART, not just your calendar.

3. "They shall ALL know me" (v.34). The new covenant promise:
   personal, direct knowledge of God available to EVERYONE — from
   least to greatest. No spiritual elitism. No exclusive access.
   The janitor knows God as fully as the theologian.

4. "I will remember their sin no more" (v.34). God CHOOSES to
   forget your sin. If the omniscient God doesn't hold your past
   against you, why do YOU? Stop reopening files God has
   permanently sealed.

5. As permanent as the sun (vv.35-36). God's commitment to His
   people is as certain as the sun rising tomorrow. If you looked
   outside and the sun was there this morning — God's covenant is
   equally intact.
