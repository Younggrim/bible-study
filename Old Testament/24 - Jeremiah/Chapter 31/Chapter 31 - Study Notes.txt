Jeremiah - Chapter 31
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The crown jewel of Jeremiah — the NEW COVENANT. "I have loved thee with an everlasting love." Rachel weeps for her children but is told to stop crying — they're coming back. The days are coming when God will make a NEW covenant: "I will put my law in their inward parts, and write it in their hearts." No more teaching each other to know God — ALL will know Him, from least to greatest. Sins remembered no more.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Jeremiah son of Hilkiah (see Chapter 1 notes for full
details).

Classification: Prophetic Salvation Oracle — The New Covenant
(Book of Consolation, chs. 30-33)
Key Themes: Everlasting love, the return of Israel and Judah,
Rachel weeping (quoted in Matthew 2), the new covenant written
on hearts, sins forgotten, individual accountability, the
permanence of Israel (as permanent as sun and moon)

Historical Context: This is the MOST IMPORTANT chapter in
Jeremiah — and one of the most important in the OT. Written
during the darkest period (Babylonian siege or early exile), it
contains God's most radical promise: a NEW covenant replacing the
Sinai covenant that Israel broke. The new covenant's distinctive:
INTERNAL (written on hearts, not stone), UNIVERSAL (all will know
God), EFFECTIVE (sins actually forgiven, not merely covered).
Hebrews 8:8-12 quotes vv.31-34 as the longest OT quotation in
the NT, declaring it fulfilled in Christ. Jesus inaugurated the
new covenant at the Last Supper: "This cup is the new testament
in my blood" (Luke 22:20).

Structure:
- "I Have Loved Thee with an Everlasting Love" (vv.1-6)
- The Return of Israel: Sing, Gather, Ransom (vv.7-14)
- Rachel Weeps — But There Is Hope (vv.15-17)
- Ephraim Repents: God's Beloved Son (vv.18-20)
- Return, O Virgin Israel (vv.21-22)
- Judah Restored (vv.23-26)
- Individual Responsibility: No More "Sour Grapes" (vv.27-30)
- THE NEW COVENANT (vv.31-34)
- Israel as Permanent as Sun and Moon (vv.35-37)
- Jerusalem Rebuilt and Holy (vv.38-40)

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Samaria (v.5): Central region between Judea and Galilee; mixed Jewish-Gentile population.
- Zion (v.6, v.12): The hill on which Jerusalem/the Temple stood; often used poetically for God's dwelling.
- Egypt (v.32): Land to the southwest; place of Israel's bondage and exodus.

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. "Everlasting love" (v.3). God's love for you didn't BEGIN when
   you were saved — it's EVERLASTING (from eternity). And it
   DRAWS you — His love is magnetic. If you feel drawn toward God,
   that drawing IS His love in action.

2. The new covenant is INTERNAL (v.33). Stop trying to obey from
   the outside in. External rules without internal transformation
   is the OLD covenant — and it failed. Ask God to write His law
   on your HEART, not just your calendar.

3. "They shall ALL know me" (v.34). The new covenant promise:
   personal, direct knowledge of God available to EVERYONE — from
   least to greatest. No spiritual elitism. No exclusive access.
   The janitor knows God as fully as the theologian.

4. "I will remember their sin no more" (v.34). God CHOOSES to
   forget your sin. If the omniscient God doesn't hold your past
   against you, why do YOU? Stop reopening files God has
   permanently sealed.

5. As permanent as the sun (vv.35-36). God's commitment to His
   people is as certain as the sun rising tomorrow. If you looked
   outside and the sun was there this morning — God's covenant is
   equally intact.
