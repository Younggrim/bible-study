Jeremiah - Chapter 15
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Even if Moses and Samuel stood before God, He wouldn't relent. Four destroyers are appointed: sword, dogs, birds, beasts. Then Jeremiah's most raw confession: "Woe is me, my mother, that thou hast borne me!" He accuses God of being an unreliable brook — God responds: repent of self-pity, and I will restore you. "I will make thee unto this people a fenced brasen wall."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Jeremiah son of Hilkiah (see Chapter 1 notes for full
details).

Classification: Prophetic Oracle + Personal Confession
Key Themes: Even Moses/Samuel can't intercede, four forms of
destruction, Jeremiah's deep personal crisis, accusation against
God, God's conditional restoration of the prophet, Jeremiah as
a fortified wall

Historical Context: The chapter opens with God citing the two
greatest intercessors in Israel's history — Moses (Exodus 32:11-14)
and Samuel (1 Samuel 7:9; 12:19-25) — and saying even THEY
couldn't change His mind now. Then comes one of Jeremiah's most
devastating confessions (vv.10-18): he curses the day of his
birth, says everyone is against him, and accuses God of being a
"deceitful brook" (v.18). God's response is unexpected: He doesn't
comfort Jeremiah but CORRECTS him — "if thou return, I will bring
thee again" (v.19). Even the prophet needs repentance.

Structure:
- Even Moses and Samuel Can't Intercede (vv.1-4)
- Who Will Pity Jerusalem? (vv.5-9)
- Jeremiah's Confession: Woe Is Me (vv.10-14)
- The Prophet's Raw Prayer (vv.15-18)
- God's Response: Repent and I'll Restore You (vv.19-21)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "Then said the LORD unto me, Though Moses and Samuel stood
        before me, yet my mind could not be toward this people"
        (KJV) — The two greatest intercessors in Israel's history
        would FAIL if they tried. The decision is irreversible.

v.10 — "Woe is me, my mother, that thou hast borne me a man of
        strife and a man of contention to the whole earth!" (KJV)
        — Jeremiah curses his existence. Not just his ministry
        but his BIRTH. The cost of prophetic calling driven to
        its emotional limit.

v.16 — "Thy words were found, and I did eat them; and thy word
        was unto me the joy and rejoicing of mine heart: for I am
        called by thy name, O LORD God of hosts" (KJV) — The
        prophet's core identity: God's word consumed, God's name
        worn. Despite everything, God's word is JOY.

v.18 — "Why is my pain perpetual, and my wound incurable, which
        refuseth to be healed? wilt thou be altogether unto me as
        a liar, and as waters that fail?" (KJV) — The most
        audacious accusation: Jeremiah calls God a "liar" and an
        "unreliable stream." Raw honesty bordering on blasphemy.

v.19 — "Therefore thus saith the LORD, If thou return, then will
        I bring thee again, and thou shalt stand before me" (KJV)
        — God doesn't comfort Jeremiah — He corrects him. "IF you
        repent of this self-pity, I'll RESTORE your ministry." Even
        prophets need repentance.

v.20 — "And I will make thee unto this people a fenced brasen
        wall: and they shall fight against thee, but they shall
        not prevail against thee: for I am with thee to save thee"
        (KJV) — After correction: recommissioning. Jeremiah will be
        a BRONZE WALL — unbreakable. Opposition will continue but
        won't succeed.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1  — "my mind could not be toward" (KJV) / "I would not relent"
      (ESV) — Hebrew "en nafshi el" = my soul is not toward/
      inclined toward. God's disposition is TURNED AWAY from this
      people. Not hate but judicial withdrawal.

v.16 — "were found" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "nimtse'u" = were found/
      discovered. Possibly referencing the discovery of the Book
      of the Law under Josiah (2 Kings 22:8). Jeremiah consumed
      the newly-found Scripture with joy.

v.18 — "waters that fail" (KJV) / "a deceitful brook" (ESV) —
      Hebrew "akhzav" = deceptive (waters that dry up when you
      need them most). A wadi that flows in winter but disappears
      in summer drought — precisely when you need it.

v.19 — "if thou return" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "im tashuv" = if you
      repent/return. God applies the "return" message to THE
      PROPHET HIMSELF. Even the messenger of repentance needs
      repentance.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Moses and Samuel — Israel's two greatest intercessors. Moses
              turned away God's wrath after the golden calf; Samuel
              interceded at Mizpah. If even THEY would fail now,
              the case is beyond human intercession.

Eating God's Word — Consuming Scripture as food: making it
              internal, digestible, life-sustaining. Not external
              reading but internal incorporation. The prophet's
              sustenance.

Deceitful Brook — A seasonal stream that dries up in summer —
              present when water is abundant but ABSENT when
              drought makes it critical. Jeremiah's accusation:
              God disappeared when most needed.

Brasen Wall  — Bronze-covered wall: the strongest fortification
              material available. Jeremiah's recommissioned identity:
              you will be attacked but NEVER overcome.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Thy words were found, and I did eat them" (v.16). In your
   darkest moment, what sustains you? For Jeremiah, it was the
   memory of consuming God's word with JOY. If you haven't stored
   that joy, darkness will have nothing to draw on.

2. Jeremiah accuses God of being unreliable (v.18). And God
   doesn't strike him dead — He CORRECTS him. God can handle your
   honest accusations. He'd rather you yell at Him than walk away
   silently. Bring your rage.

3. God corrects the prophet (v.19). Even the most faithful
   servants need correction. Jeremiah slid into self-pity and God
   said "repent." Being in ministry doesn't exempt you from
   personal repentance. Nobody is above correction.

4. Opposition will come but not overcome (v.20). God promises
   BRONZE WALL status — not absence of attack but inability to
   be defeated. Expect fighting. Expect pressure. But expect to
   STAND.

5. Even Moses and Samuel couldn't help (v.1). There are moments
   when even the best human intercession is insufficient. Some
   situations require direct divine intervention that transcends
   human prayer. This is why we need CHRIST's intercession, not
   just human prophets'.
