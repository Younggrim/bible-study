Jeremiah - Chapter 6
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Flee from Jerusalem! The beautiful daughter of Zion is about to be besieged. God's word is poured out like fire on a generation that refuses to listen. "Stand in the ways and ask for the old paths" — but they said "We will not walk therein." God rejects their sacrifices and sets a stumbling block before them. The enemy comes from the north — cruel, riding horses, arrayed for battle.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Jeremiah son of Hilkiah (see Chapter 1 notes for full
details).

Classification: Prophetic Oracle — The Siege Announced
Key Themes: Flee Jerusalem, the approaching siege, God's word as
fire, the old paths rejected, empty sacrifices, the refiner who
finds only dross, the enemy from the north described

Historical Context: This chapter concludes the first major
section of Jeremiah (chs.2-6). The invasion is now described in
vivid military detail: siege ramps, shepherds with flocks
(armies pitching tents), attacks at noon and night. The famous
"old paths" verse (v.16) offers one last chance: return to
ancient covenant faithfulness. But the people explicitly refuse:
"We will not walk therein." God's response: even their lavish
worship (frankincense from Sheba, sacrifices) is REJECTED because
their hearts are far away. The chapter ends with the refining
metaphor: Jeremiah is the tester, the people are the ore — but
the refining produces only dross. No silver remains. "Reprobate
silver" (v.30) — rejected by the refiner.

Structure:
- Flee! The Siege is Coming (vv.1-8)
- Glean Thoroughly: God's Wrath Poured Out (vv.9-12)
- "Peace, Peace — When There Is No Peace" (vv.13-15)
- Ask for the Old Paths (v.16)
- Watchmen Set, But They Refuse to Listen (vv.17-21)
- The Cruel Army from the North (vv.22-26)
- Jeremiah the Refiner: Only Dross Found (vv.27-30)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.10 — "To whom shall I speak, and give warning, that they may
        hear? behold, their ear is uncircumcised, and they cannot
        hearken" (KJV) — Jeremiah's frustration: WHO will listen?
        Their ears are "uncircumcised" — blocked, closed, unable
        to receive. The word bounces off.

v.14 — "They have healed also the hurt of the daughter of my
        people slightly, saying, Peace, peace; when there is no
        peace" (KJV) — The false prophets' slogan: "Everything's
        fine!" — applying a bandaid to a mortal wound. "Slightly"
        = superficially. Repeated in 8:11.

v.16 — "Thus saith the LORD, Stand ye in the ways, and see, and
        ask for the old paths, where is the good way, and walk
        therein, and ye shall find rest for your souls. But they
        said, We will not walk therein" (KJV) — The invitation:
        stop, look, ask, walk. The response: flat refusal. "We
        will NOT." The clearest picture of willful disobedience.

v.19 — "I will bring evil upon this people, even the fruit of
        their thoughts, because they have not hearkened unto my
        words" (KJV) — Judgment as "the fruit of their thoughts"
        — the natural harvest of their own internal corruption.
        You reap what your MIND plants.

v.20 — "To what purpose cometh there to me incense from Sheba...
        your burnt offerings are not acceptable, nor your
        sacrifices sweet unto me" (KJV) — Expensive worship
        (Sheba's frankincense was luxury) REJECTED. God doesn't
        want premium worship from disobedient hearts.

v.30 — "Reprobate silver shall men call them, because the LORD
        hath rejected them" (KJV) — The refining metaphor's
        conclusion: all dross, no silver. Rejected ore. When the
        refining process finds nothing worth keeping, the material
        is discarded.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.10 — "uncircumcised ear" (KJV) / "uncircumcised ears" (ESV) —
      Hebrew "arelah oznam" = their ear has a foreskin (is
      blocked/sealed). The circumcision metaphor applied to
      hearing: something COVERS the ear, preventing reception.

v.14 — "slightly" (KJV) / "lightly" (ESV) — Hebrew "al neqalah"
      = superficially, as if it were trivial. The wound is DEEP
      but they treat it as SHALLOW. Misdiagnosis leading to
      malpractice.

v.16 — "old paths" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "netivot olam" = paths of
      antiquity/eternity. The ancient covenant way — the proven
      road of faithfulness that patriarchs and Moses walked.

v.27 — "tower and a fortress" (KJV) / "tester and refiner" (ESV)
      — Hebrew "bachon" = assayer/tester (of metals). Jeremiah's
      role: test the people's quality as a metalworker tests ore.

v.30 — "reprobate silver" (KJV) / "rejected silver" (ESV) —
      Hebrew "kesef nim'as" = refused/rejected silver. Ore that
      yields no silver after smelting — worthless and discarded.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Peace, Peace — The false prophets' slogan: declaring safety when
              disaster is imminent. The most dangerous words in
              Israel: "God won't judge." Superficial reassurance
              that prevents genuine repentance.

Old Paths    — The ancient, proven way of covenant faithfulness.
              Not innovation but return. Not novelty but tested
              truth. The "good way" that produces rest for souls.

Reprobate Silver — Ore that fails the refining test: all dross,
              no precious metal. A people tested by God's word and
              found to contain nothing redeemable (in that
              generation's current state).

Uncircumcised Ear — Hearing that is blocked/sealed against God's
              word. The covenant sign (circumcision = openness to
              God) is absent from their EARS — they're spiritually
              deaf by choice.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Ask for the old paths" (v.16). In a culture obsessed with
   the new, God says: go BACK to what works. The ancient truths
   — Scripture, prayer, community, obedience — are the "good
   way." Don't chase novelty; walk proven paths.

2. "We will not walk therein" (v.16). The most honest form of
   rebellion: not "I can't" but "I WON'T." Is there an old path
   God is calling you to that you're simply refusing to walk?
   Honesty about refusal is better than pretending inability.

3. "Peace, peace; when there is no peace" (v.14). Beware of
   voices telling you everything's fine when your soul knows it
   isn't. Comfortable lies feel good temporarily but leave the
   wound untreated.

4. Expensive worship doesn't compensate for disobedience (v.20).
   You can't buy God off with generous giving or impressive
   worship if your Monday-through-Saturday life contradicts
   your Sunday morning.

5. Reprobate silver (v.30). Sometimes God's refining process
   reveals that a person or community has NOTHING left to purify.
   Don't let that be your story. Let the refining FIND something
   precious in you — faith, humility, responsiveness.
