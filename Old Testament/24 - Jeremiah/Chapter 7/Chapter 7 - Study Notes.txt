Jeremiah - Chapter 7
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The Temple Sermon — Jeremiah stands in the temple gate and declares: "Trust ye not in lying words, saying, The temple of the LORD!" The temple won't save people who steal, murder, commit adultery, and worship Baal. God points to Shiloh (where the tabernacle was destroyed) as proof He'll do the same to Jerusalem. He tells Jeremiah: stop praying for this people.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Jeremiah son of Hilkiah (see Chapter 1 notes for full
details).

Classification: Prophetic Sermon — The Temple Gate Sermon
Key Themes: False trust in the temple, the "temple of the LORD"
as a magical incantation, God demands justice not ritual, Shiloh
as precedent, God refuses to hear prayer for them, the Queen of
Heaven, Topheth/child sacrifice

Historical Context: This sermon was likely preached early in
Jehoiakim's reign (c. 609-608 BC) — after Josiah's death and
the beginning of Judah's rapid spiritual decline. The people
believed the temple guaranteed God's protection regardless of
their behavior (a "magical" view of God's presence). Jeremiah
demolishes this: the temple is no talisman. Shiloh — where the
tabernacle stood for ~300 years — was DESTROYED (by the
Philistines, 1 Samuel 4). God will do the same to Solomon's
temple. Chapter 26 records the reaction: they tried to kill
Jeremiah for this sermon.

Structure:
- The Temple Sermon: Don't Trust Lying Words (vv.1-7)
- Stealing, Then Coming to the Temple (vv.8-11)
- The Shiloh Precedent (vv.12-15)
- Stop Praying for This People (vv.16-20)
- Obedience, Not Sacrifice (vv.21-28)
- The Valley of Slaughter: Topheth (vv.29-34)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.4  — "Trust ye not in lying words, saying, The temple of the
        LORD, The temple of the LORD, The temple of the LORD, are
        these" (KJV) — Three-fold repetition mocking their
        superstitious chant. They repeated "temple of the LORD"
        like a protective spell. The building won't save you.

v.9-10 — "Will ye steal, murder, and commit adultery, and swear
        falsely, and burn incense unto Baal... and come and stand
        before me in this house... and say, We are delivered to do
        all these abominations?" (KJV) — The indictment: sin all
        week, then come to the temple and claim protection. Jesus
        echoes this: "den of robbers" (v.11 / Matt 21:13).

v.11 — "Is this house, which is called by my name, become a den
        of robbers in your eyes?" (KJV) — Jesus quotes this
        directly when cleansing the temple (Matt 21:13). A den is
        where robbers HIDE after committing crimes — the temple
        as a hideout for criminals.

v.12 — "But go ye now unto my place which was in Shiloh... and
        see what I did to it for the wickedness of my people
        Israel" (KJV) — Devastating precedent: Shiloh was God's
        chosen dwelling — and He DESTROYED it. If He did it to
        Shiloh, He'll do it to Jerusalem.

v.16 — "Therefore pray not thou for this people... for I will not
        hear thee" (KJV) — God telling a prophet to STOP praying.
        The point of no return has been crossed. Intercession is
        now futile for this generation.

v.23 — "But this thing commanded I them, saying, Obey my voice,
        and I will be your God, and ye shall be my people" (KJV)
        — What God ACTUALLY wanted: not ritual but obedience. Not
        sacrifice but relationship. The original covenant was about
        HEARING His voice, not performing ceremonies.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.4  — "lying words" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "divrey ha-sheqer" =
      words of falsehood/deception. The superstitious chant
      "temple of the LORD" is itself a LIE when used as a
      guarantee of protection regardless of behavior.

v.11 — "den of robbers" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "me'arat paritsim" =
      cave of violent men/robbers. Not where robbing happens but
      where robbers FLEE for safety after robbing. The temple as
      criminal hideout.

v.18 — "queen of heaven" (KJV/ESV) — Likely Ishtar/Astarte, the
      Mesopotamian goddess of fertility and love. Women baked
      cakes in her image (a form of idol worship that Judah
      practiced alongside Yahweh worship).

v.31 — "Topheth" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "topheth" = place of burning.
      Located in the Valley of Hinnom (ge-Hinnom → Gehenna) south
      of Jerusalem. Where children were burned alive as sacrifices
      to Molech.

v.4 — ASV: uses "Jehovah" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).
       NET: "the false belief that says, “We are safe!" for KJV's "lying words, saying,"
       WEB: uses "Yahweh" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).
v.11 — ASV: uses "Jehovah" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).
       NET: "hideout for robbers? You had better take note!" for KJV's "den of robbers in your eyes? Behold, even"
       WEB: uses "Yahweh" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).
v.18 — ASV: "drink-offerings" for KJV's "drink offerings"

v.31 — NET: "Ben Hinnom so that they can sacrifice" for KJV's "the son of Hinnom, to burn"
       WEB: "didn’t command, nor did" for KJV's "commanded them not, neither came"

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Temple Superstition — The belief that God's presence in the temple
              automatically protected Jerusalem regardless of the
              people's behavior. A magical rather than covenantal
              view of God's presence.

Shiloh       — The location of the tabernacle for ~300 years
              (Joshua-Samuel period). Destroyed by the Philistines
              after the ark was captured (1 Sam 4). Proof that
              God will destroy His own dwelling if His people
              corrupt it.

Den of Robbers — Not a place of robbery but a HIDEOUT for robbers.
              The temple misused as a place where criminals feel
              safe from consequences.

Topheth      — The burning place in the Valley of Hinnom where
              children were sacrificed to Molech/Baal. The ultimate
              abomination — killing your children for a false god.
              Later became Gehenna (hell imagery).

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "The temple of the LORD" — don't trust religious buildings
   (v.4). Attending church doesn't save you. Being inside a
   sacred space doesn't neutralize your sins. Are you using
   religious activity as a protective charm rather than genuinely
   following God?

2. Sin all week, worship on Sunday (vv.9-10). The pattern is
   ancient and persistent. If your Monday contradicts your
   Sunday, your Sunday is a lie. God isn't fooled by weekend
   religion.

3. The Shiloh precedent (v.12). If God destroyed His OWN
   tabernacle when His people corrupted worship, what makes you
   think YOUR church/institution/nation is immune? God is loyal
   to His character, not to buildings.

4. "Pray not for this people" (v.16). Sobering: there IS a point
   of no return for communities. Not for individuals (who can
   always repent) but for collective judgment. Don't presume
   God's patience is infinite without limit.

5. Obedience, not sacrifice (v.23). God's original ask was
   simple: "Obey my voice." The sacrificial system was added
   AFTER disobedience. Obedience is primary; worship is the
   response to relationship, not a substitute for it.
