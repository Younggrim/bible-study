Jeremiah - Chapter 23
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Woe to the shepherds who scatter God's flock! God will raise up the Righteous Branch — a king from David's line whose name is "THE LORD OUR RIGHTEOUSNESS." Then a massive condemnation of false prophets: they commit adultery, walk in lies, strengthen evildoers, prophesy from their own imagination, and say "peace" to those walking in stubbornness. "Am I a God at hand... and not a God afar off?"

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Jeremiah son of Hilkiah (see Chapter 1 notes for full
details).

Classification: Prophetic Oracle — Woe to Shepherds + Messianic
Promise + False Prophet Condemnation
Key Themes: Failed shepherds vs. the coming Shepherd-King, the
Righteous Branch from David, "The LORD Our Righteousness,"
false prophets' comprehensive corruption, distinguishing true
from false prophecy, God sees everything

Historical Context: Judah's kings (shepherds) have scattered the
flock through incompetence and idolatry. God pronounces woe on
them (likely Jehoiakim and Zedekiah era). The messianic promise
(vv.5-6) is one of Jeremiah's greatest: a "Righteous Branch"
from David — a king who actually rules with justice. His name:
"YHWH Tsidkenu" (The LORD Our Righteousness) — a pointed contrast
with "Zedekiah" (whose name means "My Righteousness is YHWH" but
whose life contradicted it). The second half (vv.9-40) is
Jeremiah's longest attack on false prophets — their immorality,
their lies, their dreams vs. God's word (chaff vs. wheat).

Structure:
- Woe to the Shepherds Who Scatter (vv.1-4)
- The Righteous Branch: "The LORD Our Righteousness" (vv.5-8)
- False Prophets: Adultery and Lies (vv.9-15)
- They Prophesy from Their Own Heart (vv.16-22)
- God Sees Everything: Near AND Far (vv.23-24)
- Dreams vs. God's Word: Chaff vs. Wheat (vv.25-32)
- The "Burden" of the LORD (vv.33-40)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "Woe be unto the pastors that destroy and scatter the
        sheep of my pasture!" (KJV) — God's indictment against
        every leader who scattered rather than gathered, destroyed
        rather than nurtured. Leadership malpractice under divine
        judgment.

v.5  — "I will raise unto David a righteous Branch, and a King
        shall reign and prosper, and shall execute judgment and
        justice in the earth" (KJV) — The messianic prophecy: a
        KING from David's line who actually does what kings should
        — justice and righteousness. Christ fulfills this.

v.6  — "and this is his name whereby he shall be called, THE LORD
        OUR RIGHTEOUSNESS" (KJV) — Hebrew "YHWH Tsidkenu." The
        Messiah's name IS God's name. He's not just righteous —
        He IS our righteousness. We wear HIS righteousness.

v.16 — "They speak a vision of their own heart, and not out of
        the mouth of the LORD" (KJV) — The definitive diagnosis
        of false prophecy: self-sourced, not God-sourced. Their
        "word from the Lord" came from their own imagination.

v.23-24 — "Am I a God at hand, saith the LORD, and not a God afar
        off? Can any hide himself in secret places that I shall
        not see him?" (KJV) — God is BOTH near and far. You cannot
        hide from Him. He sees the false prophets' secret sins
        and the people's hidden thoughts.

v.29 — "Is not my word like as a fire? saith the LORD; and like a
        hammer that breaketh the rock in pieces?" (KJV) — God's
        word as FIRE (consuming, purifying) and HAMMER (breaking,
        demolishing). It does WORK. False prophets' words do
        nothing; God's word breaks rocks.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.5  — "Branch" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "tsemach" = a sprout, shoot,
      growth. The royal house of David reduced to a stump (Isaiah
      6:13; 11:1) — but from the stump grows a righteous shoot.

v.6  — "THE LORD OUR RIGHTEOUSNESS" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "YHWH
      Tsidkenu." A divine NAME for the Messiah. Wordplay on King
      Zedekiah's name (Tsidqiyahu = "YHWH is my righteousness").
      The fake king's name mocks what the true King will actually
      BE.

v.28 — "chaff" / "wheat" (KJV/ESV) — The test: false prophecy
      is CHAFF (lightweight, blown away, worthless). God's word is
      WHEAT (substantial, nourishing, enduring). The contrast is
      self-evident to anyone honest.

v.33 — "burden" (KJV) / "oracle" (ESV) — Hebrew "massa" = burden/
      oracle (a weighty prophetic message). Wordplay: the people
      ask "what is the burden/message?" God answers: "YOU are the
      burden — and I'll cast you off."

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Righteous Branch — Messianic title: the new shoot from David's
              stump that becomes a righteous king. Fulfilled in
              Christ — descendant of David, ruling in justice.

THE LORD Our Righteousness — The Messiah's name: God Himself
              is the righteousness His people possess. We don't
              generate righteousness — we receive His as a gift.
              Foundational to justification by faith.

Chaff vs. Wheat — (v.28) The test distinguishing false prophecy
              from God's word: chaff blows away; wheat nourishes.
              Time reveals which is which.

Fire and Hammer — (v.29) God's word as both consuming (fire) and
              demolishing (hammer). It doesn't just inform — it
              DESTROYS falsehood and PURIFIES the hearer.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "THE LORD OUR RIGHTEOUSNESS" (v.6). You cannot generate your
   own righteousness — the Messiah IS your righteousness. Stop
   trying to earn what Christ already provides. His name = your
   identity.

2. "Is not my word like fire? Like a hammer?" (v.29). When
   Scripture feels boring or powerless, the problem isn't the
   WORD — it's your engagement with it. God's word breaks rocks.
   Are you letting it hit hard, or shielding yourself from its
   impact?

3. Woe to shepherds who scatter (v.1). If you're in any
   leadership role, hear the weight of this: leaders who harm
   those under their care face divine WOE. Leadership is
   stewardship — and God audits.

4. They prophesy from their own heart (v.16). The most dangerous
   false prophets don't know they're false. They genuinely believe
   their own imagination is God's voice. Test: does the message
   match Scripture? Does it produce holiness or comfort in sin?

5. "Can any hide?" (v.24). No. Whatever you're doing in secret,
   God sees. Whatever you're thinking privately, God knows. This
   is simultaneously terrifying (for rebellion) and comforting
   (for the faithful who feel unseen).
