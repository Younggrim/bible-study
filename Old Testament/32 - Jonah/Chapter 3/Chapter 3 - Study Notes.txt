Jonah - Chapter 3
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Jonah preaches to Nineveh and the entire city repents — from king to commoner they fast in sackcloth, and God relents from the disaster He had planned against them.


AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Jonah son of Amittai (2 Kings 14:25). Set approximately
780-760 BC. Key theme: God's mercy extends beyond Israel to all
nations — even Israel's enemies (Nineveh/Assyria). Jonah resists;
God persists. "Salvation is of the LORD" (2:9). Jesus references
Jonah as a sign (Matthew 12:39-40).

Classification: Prophetic Narrative — Nineveh Repents
Key Themes: The second chance, obedience after failure, the
simplest sermon that produced the greatest revival, corporate
repentance, God relenting from judgment, mercy for enemies

Historical Context: Chapter 3 is the SHORTEST and most POWERFUL
revival account in Scripture. God speaks "a second time" (v.1) —
grace after failure. Jonah finally obeys. His sermon is only
eight words in Hebrew: "Yet forty days, and Nineveh shall be
overthrown." No persuasion, no altar call, no follow-up plan.
Yet the response is TOTAL: from the greatest to the least,
including the KING himself, the entire city repents — humans AND
animals covered in sackcloth. This pagan city responds more
deeply than Israel ever did to its prophets. The chapter ends
with God "repenting" (relenting) of the threatened judgment.
This will infuriate Jonah (ch.4) — but it vindicates God's
character: He is "gracious and merciful" (4:2).

Structure:
- The Second Call: God Speaks Again (vv.1-2)
- Obedience: Jonah Goes (v.3)
- The Sermon: Eight Words (v.4)
- The Response: Total Repentance (vv.5-9)
- God's Response: Judgment Averted (v.10)

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Joppa (modern Jaffa/Tel Aviv) — Mediterranean port city where Jonah boards a ship fleeing God's call.
- Tarshish (likely Tartessus in modern Spain) — the farthest west one could sail; the exact OPPOSITE direction from Nineveh.
- Nineveh (modern Mosul, Iraq) — capital of the Assyrian Empire, located on the Tigris River, approximately 500 miles northeast of Israel.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "Then the word of the LORD came to Jonah the second time"
        (ESV) — "The second time." God gives second chances. After
        rebellion, flight, near-death, and the fish — God speaks
        AGAIN. Same call. Same mission. No demotion, no disqualifi-
        cation. The God of second chances.

v.4  — "Yet forty days, and Nineveh shall be overthrown!" (ESV) —
        The entire sermon. No theology, no arguments, no emotion.
        Just a deadline and a verdict. Yet this produces the
        greatest mass repentance in biblical history. The POWER
        is in God's word, not in the preacher's skill.

v.5  — "And the people of Nineveh believed God. They called for a
        fast and put on sackcloth, from the greatest of them to
        the least of them" (ESV) — They "believed GOD" — not just
        Jonah. Faith in the MESSAGE, directed to GOD Himself.
        Universal response: greatest to least. No one exempted
        themselves.

v.9  — "Who knows? God may turn and relent and turn from his
        fierce anger, so that we may not perish" (ESV) — They
        repented WITHOUT a guarantee of pardon. "Who knows?" —
        they didn't KNOW God would relent. They hoped. They acted.
        They took the risk of faith without assurance. That is
        genuine repentance.

v.10 — "When God saw what they did, how they turned from their
        evil way, God relented of the disaster that he had said he
        would do to them, and he did not do it" (ESV) — God SAW
        their works (not just heard their words). They TURNED
        (actual change of direction). And God RELENTED. Repentance
        changes God's course. This is not God being fickle — it is
        God being relational.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.3 — "three days' journey" — The circumference or diameter of
      greater Nineveh. Archaeological evidence confirms Nineveh
      as a massive city with outlying suburbs and agricultural
      areas that could require three days to traverse.

v.4 — "overthrown" — Hebrew "nehpaket" = overturned, destroyed.
      The same word used for Sodom's destruction (Genesis 19:25).
      Nineveh would have understood: you will become another Sodom.

v.5 — "believed God" — Hebrew "he'eminu b'Elohim." Active faith
      directed toward God. Not just intellectual assent but
      trust-in-action (fasting, sackcloth, repentance).

v.7 — "neither man nor beast" — Even ANIMALS participated in the
      mourning. This is either cultural practice (animals reflecting
      community state) or an expression of total, all-encompassing
      repentance. Everything that breathes participates.

v.10 — "God repented" (KJV) / "God relented" (ESV) — Hebrew
       "nacham." God is not changing His mind arbitrarily — He is
       responding relationally. His threat was conditional (though
       unstated): repent, and judgment is averted. They repented;
       He relented.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. God gives second chances (v.1). "The second time" — He didn't
   disqualify Jonah after chapter 1. He didn't find a replacement
   prophet. He called AGAIN. If you've failed your first commission,
   listen: God may be calling a second time. Don't assume you're
   disqualified.

2. The simplest message can produce the greatest response (v.4).
   Eight words. No eloquence, no strategy, no emotional manipulation.
   Just God's word faithfully delivered. Stop believing you need
   perfect presentation. Just deliver the message God gives you.
   The power is in HIS word, not your delivery.

3. They repented without a guarantee (v.9). "Who can tell?" They
   didn't know God would relent. They repented ANYWAY. Sometimes
   you must act in faith without knowing the outcome. Repent
   regardless of what you think will happen. That's genuine faith.

4. True repentance is visible in WORKS (v.10). God saw their
   WORKS — not just their sackcloth but their TURNING from evil.
   Repentance that doesn't change behavior isn't repentance.
   What have you actually CHANGED?

5. God is more willing to save than you think (v.10). Nineveh —
   the cruelest empire on earth — repented and was spared. If God
   would spare THEM, He will spare you. His mercy is wider than
   your imagination.
