Amos - Chapter 1
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God introduces Amos the shepherd-prophet and pronounces judgment on six surrounding nations (Damascus, Gaza, Tyre, Edom, Ammon) for their crimes against humanity, using the escalating formula "for three transgressions and for four."


AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Amos, a shepherd from Tekoa in Judah who prophesied to
the northern kingdom (approximately 760-750 BC). Key theme: social
justice — God judges nations (including Israel) for oppressing the
poor. "Let judgment run down as waters, and righteousness as a
mighty stream" (5:24).

Classification: Prophetic Oracle — God Judges the Nations
Key Themes: Universal divine justice, crimes against humanity,
the "for three... and for four" formula, fire as judgment, God
holds ALL nations accountable regardless of covenant relationship

Historical Context: Amos was a shepherd and fig-tree cultivator
from Tekoa (south of Bethlehem in Judah) called to prophesy to
the northern kingdom during the reign of Jeroboam II — a time of
unprecedented prosperity AND unprecedented social injustice. The
earthquake referenced (v.1) was so significant it was remembered
centuries later (Zechariah 14:5). Chapter 1 begins God's judgment
oracles against SIX surrounding nations: Damascus (Syria), Gaza
(Philistia), Tyre (Phoenicia), Edom, Ammon — building a spiral
that will ultimately land on ISRAEL itself (2:6). The audience
would have cheered each judgment on their enemies, not realizing
the trap being set.

Structure:
- Introduction: Amos the Shepherd-Prophet (v.1)
- The LORD Roars from Zion (v.2)
- Judgment on Damascus/Syria (vv.3-5)
- Judgment on Gaza/Philistia (vv.6-8)
- Judgment on Tyre/Phoenicia (vv.9-10)
- Judgment on Edom (vv.11-12)
- Judgment on Ammon (vv.13-15)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.2  — "The LORD will roar from Zion, and utter his voice from
        Jerusalem" (KJV) — God speaks as a LION — not whispering
        but ROARING. His voice is from Zion (the place of His
        throne), and it causes withering and mourning across the
        land. This God does not merely observe injustice; He
        addresses it with power.

v.3  — "For three transgressions of Damascus, and for four, I
        will not turn away the punishment thereof" (KJV) — The
        formula "three... and for four" indicates repeated,
        accumulated sin. Not one offense but OVERFLOW. The cup is
        full. God has been patient, but the fourth transgression
        tips the scale. Judgment is now irrevocable.

v.11 — "because he did pursue his brother with the sword, and did
        cast off all pity, and his anger did tear perpetually"
        (KJV) — Edom's sin: pursuing his BROTHER (Israel descended
        from Jacob; Edom from Esau — they are brothers). Betraying
        family. Casting off pity. Holding anger FOREVER. Permanent
        unforgiveness is itself a judgment-worthy sin.

v.13 — "because they have ripped up the women with child of
        Gilead, that they might enlarge their border" (KJV) —
        Ammon's sin: atrocity against pregnant women for TERRITORIAL
        EXPANSION. Violence against the most vulnerable for profit.
        God notices. God judges.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1 — "herdmen" (KJV) / "shepherds" (ESV) — Hebrew "noqed" = a
      specific type of sheep-breeder (used only here and of King
      Mesha of Moab). Amos was not poor but a prosperous livestock
      owner called from secular work to prophetic ministry.

v.1 — "the earthquake" — So well-known it needed no further
      identification. Archaeological evidence confirms a major
      earthquake in Israel approximately 760 BC.

v.3 — "for three... and for four" — A Hebrew numerical idiom
      (X / X+1) meaning "multiple" or "overflowing." The fourth
      is the climactic, tipping-point transgression.

v.3 — "threshed Gilead with threshing instruments of iron" —
      Literal: dragged iron-studded threshing sledges over human
      bodies. Syria's cruelty to the Transjordan Israelites.

v.9 — "the brotherly covenant" — Likely the alliance between
      Solomon and Hiram of Tyre (1 Kings 5:12). Tyre broke this
      ancient treaty of friendship.

v.1 — NET: "what Amos prophesied. He" for KJV's "Amos, who"
v.3 — ASV: uses "Jehovah" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).
       NET: "revoke my decree of judgment. They ripped through" for KJV's "turn away the punishment thereof; because they have threshed"
       WEB: uses "Yahweh" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).
v.9 — ASV: uses "Jehovah" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).
       NET: "revoke my decree of judgment. They sold a whole community..." for KJV's "turn away the punishment thereof; because"
       WEB: uses "Yahweh" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. God judges ALL nations, not just Israel (vv.3-15). No nation
   is exempt from divine accountability. Whether they know God's
   law or not, basic human decency is expected. Cruelty, slave-
   trading, treaty-breaking, and violence against the vulnerable
   will be judged regardless of the perpetrator's religion.

2. "For three... and for four" — God counts (v.3). He is patient,
   but He is not oblivious. Every transgression is recorded. There
   IS a tipping point. Don't mistake His patience for permission
   or His silence for approval.

3. The spiral of judgment moves inward. Amos starts with DISTANT
   nations (Damascus, Gaza) and spirals closer: Tyre (ally), Edom
   (cousin), Ammon (neighbor)... then Judah, then Israel. God's
   people are not exempt from His standards — they are MOST
   accountable. Judgment begins at the house of God (1 Peter 4:17).

4. Permanent anger is judged (v.11). Edom "kept his wrath forever."
   Unforgiveness held perpetually is not neutral — it is sin that
   God judges. Release it before it condemns you.
