Amos - Chapter 6
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Woe to those at ease in Zion — the complacent rich who lounge on ivory beds, feast lavishly, and are not grieved over the ruin of the nation will be first to go into exile.


AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Amos, a shepherd from Tekoa in Judah who prophesied to
the northern kingdom (approximately 760-750 BC). Key theme: social
justice — God judges nations (including Israel) for oppressing the
poor. "Let judgment run down as waters, and righteousness as a
mighty stream" (5:24).

Classification: Prophetic Oracle — Woe to the Complacent
Key Themes: False security, luxury and indifference, "not grieved
for the affliction of Joseph," self-congratulation, God abhorring
pride, a nation raised up against Israel

Historical Context: Chapter 6 pronounces "Woe" upon the wealthy
elite of both Zion (Judah) and Samaria (Israel) who live in luxury
while ignoring the nation's spiritual collapse. They recline on
ivory beds, eat choice meat, invent music like David (but without
David's heart for God), drink wine by the bowlful — and are "not
grieved for the affliction of Joseph" (v.6). Their indifference
to suffering WHILE enjoying abundance is the specific sin condemned.
They boast of their own strength (v.13), but God will raise up a
nation to afflict them across their entire territory.

Structure:
- Woe to the Complacent: False Security (vv.1-3)
- The Luxurious Lifestyle (vv.4-6)
- First to Exile: The Self-Indulgent (v.7)
- God Abhors Jacob's Pride (vv.8-11)
- The Absurdity of Corrupted Justice (vv.12-14)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "Woe to them that are at ease in Zion, and trust in the
        mountain of Samaria" (KJV) — "At ease" = complacent,
        self-satisfied, feeling secure. The problem is not rest
        but false REST — comfort without conscience, security
        without sensitivity to God or others.

v.6  — "they are not grieved for the affliction of Joseph" (KJV)
        — The chapter's central charge. Their sin is not just
        luxury — it is luxury combined with INDIFFERENCE to
        suffering. They feast while the nation crumbles. Comfort
        without compassion condemns.

v.8  — "I abhor the excellency of Jacob, and hate his palaces"
        (KJV) — God ABHORS their pride and HATES their monuments
        to self. Strong language: divine disgust at human arrogance.

v.12 — "Shall horses run upon the rock? will one plow there with
        oxen? for ye have turned judgment into gall, and the fruit
        of righteousness into hemlock" (KJV) — Just as it's absurd
        to run horses on rock or plow rock with oxen, it's absurd
        to turn justice into poison. They've perverted the natural
        moral order.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.4 — "beds of ivory" — Archaeological finds at Samaria confirm
      ivory-inlaid furniture in wealthy homes of this period.

v.5 — "like David" — They compared their musical innovation to
      David's. But David composed for GOD's glory; they compose
      for their own entertainment.

v.6 — "drink wine in bowls" — Not cups but BOWLS — implying
      excess. "Chief ointments" = the finest perfumed oils.

v.13 — "a thing of nought" (KJV) — Referring to Lo-debar (meaning
       "nothing") — a town they conquered. They boast of capturing
       "Nothing" — unknowing irony.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Not grieved for the affliction of Joseph" (v.6). This is
   the sin of the comfortable. Are you aware of suffering around
   you — or has your comfort insulated you from caring? The test
   of spiritual health is not how much you HAVE but how much you
   GRIEVE for those who have not.

2. Complacency is not peace (v.1). Being "at ease" while injustice
   rages is not the peace of God — it is the numbness of self-
   absorption. True peace coexists with active compassion.

3. Your palaces built on pride will be delivered up (v.8). Whatever
   you build as a monument to your own greatness, God abhors.
   Wealth, achievement, reputation — built for self-glory, they
   become targets of divine judgment.

4. "First to go captive" (v.7). The self-indulgent go FIRST into
   exile. Those who were first in luxury will be first in judgment.
