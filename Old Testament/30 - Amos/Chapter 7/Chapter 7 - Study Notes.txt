Amos - Chapter 7
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God shows Amos three visions — locusts, fire, and a plumb line — and the priest Amaziah confronts Amos at Bethel, telling him to leave, to which Amos defends his divine calling as a simple herdsman sent by God.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Amos, a shepherd from Tekoa in Judah who prophesied to
the northern kingdom (approximately 760-750 BC). Key theme: social
justice — God judges nations (including Israel) for oppressing the
poor. "Let judgment run down as waters, and righteousness as a
mighty stream" (5:24).

Classification: Prophetic Visions and Narrative — Locusts, Fire,
the Plumbline, and Amaziah's Opposition
Key Themes: God's mercy delaying judgment (twice), the plumbline
of absolute standard, confrontation with religious establishment,
the calling of a layman-prophet, the cost of speaking truth

Historical Context: Chapter 7 shifts from oracles to VISIONS and
NARRATIVE. Three visions show God's judgment: locusts (v.1), fire
(v.4), and a plumbline (v.7). Twice Amos intercedes successfully
and God relents (vv.1-6). But the third time — the plumbline — God
declares "I will not again pass by them" (v.8). The standard has
been measured; Israel fails. Then comes the dramatic confrontation
with Amaziah, the priest of Bethel, who tells Amos to go home.
Amos's response is one of the most powerful prophet-defenses in
Scripture: "I was no prophet... but the LORD took me."

Structure:
- Vision 1: Locusts — God Relents (vv.1-3)
- Vision 2: Fire — God Relents (vv.4-6)
- Vision 3: The Plumbline — No More Passing By (vv.7-9)
- Amaziah vs. Amos: The Confrontation (vv.10-13)
- Amos's Defense: "The LORD Took Me" (vv.14-17)

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. Intercession can delay judgment (vv.2-3, 5-6). Twice God
   RELENTED because Amos prayed. Your prayers for others are not
   futile — they can change God's course of action. Pray for your
   family, church, and nation. It MATTERS.

2. The plumbline eventually comes (v.7). After mercy comes the
   STANDARD. God is patient, but He does measure. His plumbline
   is straight. How do you measure against His absolute standard?
   (Hint: only through Christ can anyone pass the plumbline test.)

3. "I was no prophet" (v.14). God doesn't call the qualified — He
   qualifies the called. Your lack of formal training, credentials,
   or pedigree is irrelevant when God SENDS you. Don't let
   Amaziahs disqualify what God has commissioned.

4. Religious leaders can be the greatest opponents of truth (v.12).
   Amaziah was a PRIEST — and he was silencing the prophet. Not
   all opposition comes from outside the church. Sometimes the
   establishment resists God's fresh word.

5. There is a "no more" with God (v.8). Don't presume on endless
   patience. There comes a moment when God says "no more passing
   by." Respond while the invitation is still open.
