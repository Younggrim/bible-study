Amos - Chapter 4
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God condemns the wealthy women of Samaria ("cows of Bashan") who oppress the poor, and recounts five warning judgments He sent — famine, drought, blight, plague, overthrow — yet "you did not return to Me."


AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Amos, a shepherd from Tekoa in Judah who prophesied to
the northern kingdom (approximately 760-750 BC). Key theme: social
justice — God judges nations (including Israel) for oppressing the
poor. "Let judgment run down as waters, and righteousness as a
mighty stream" (5:24).

Classification: Prophetic Oracle — "Yet Have Ye Not Returned"
Key Themes: The "cows of Bashan" — wealthy women who oppress the
poor, sarcastic invitation to sin at Bethel, five failed
disciplines, "prepare to meet thy God"

Historical Context: Chapter 4 opens with one of the most
confrontational addresses in Scripture: the wealthy women of
Samaria are called "cows of Bashan" — pampered, fat cattle who
demand luxury while their husbands crush the poor. Then God
employs devastating SARCASM (vv.4-5): "Come to Bethel and SIN!"
He mocks their religious enthusiasm. The chapter's center is a
five-fold refrain: "yet have ye not returned unto me" (vv.6-11).
God sent famine, drought, blight, pestilence, and overthrow — five
escalating disciplines — and Israel ignored every one. The chapter
climaxes with the terrifying command: "Prepare to meet thy God,
O Israel" (v.12).

Structure:
- The Cows of Bashan: Luxury Through Oppression (vv.1-3)
- Sarcastic Invitation: Come to Bethel and Sin! (vv.4-5)
- Five Disciplines, Five Refusals to Return (vv.6-11)
- The Final Warning: Prepare to Meet Your God (vv.12-13)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "Hear this word, ye kine of Bashan, that are in the
        mountain of Samaria, which oppress the poor, which crush
        the needy, which say to their masters, Bring, and let us
        drink" (KJV) — "Cows of Bashan" = the wealthy women of
        Samaria who demand luxury from their husbands while the
        poor are crushed to fund their lifestyle. Consumer culture
        built on exploitation.

v.6  — "I also have given you cleanness of teeth in all your
        cities, and want of bread in all your places: yet have ye
        not returned unto me" (KJV) — "Cleanness of teeth" =
        nothing to eat (teeth are clean because there's no food).
        God's discipline was meant to produce RETURN. It didn't.
        The refrain haunts: "yet have ye not returned unto me."

v.11 — "I have overthrown some of you, as God overthrew Sodom and
        Gomorrah, and ye were as a firebrand plucked out of the
        burning: yet have ye not returned unto me" (KJV) — Even
        SODOM-level judgment didn't produce repentance. They were
        plucked from the fire like a burning stick — barely saved —
        and STILL didn't return. Hardness of heart survives even
        catastrophe.

v.12 — "prepare to meet thy God, O Israel" (KJV) — The most
        solemn summons in Scripture. Since nothing else worked —
        no discipline, no warning, no catastrophe produced
        repentance — now prepare for DIRECT encounter with God
        Himself. This is both terrifying AND gracious: even now,
        "meeting God" is offered.

v.13 — "he that formeth the mountains, and createth the wind, and
        declareth unto man what is his thought" (KJV) — The God
        you must prepare to meet is the Creator of mountains and
        wind, the One who knows your THOUGHTS. You cannot hide
        from this God or bluff this meeting.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1 — "kine of Bashan" = "cows of Bashan." Bashan (modern Golan
      Heights) was famous for lush pastures and fat cattle
      (Deuteronomy 32:14, Psalm 22:12). The metaphor: pampered,
      overfed, demanding luxury.

v.4 — "Come to Bethel, and transgress" — SARCASM. God mocks their
      religious enthusiasm. "You love your rituals? Fine — go
      ahead! Sin MORE at Bethel! Your 'worship' is transgression."

v.6-11 — Five escalating disciplines: (1) famine (v.6), (2)
       drought (vv.7-8), (3) blight and locusts (v.9), (4)
       pestilence and war (v.10), (5) destruction like Sodom
       (v.11). Each followed by "yet have ye not returned."

v.13 — "declareth unto man what is his thought" — God knows your
       THOUGHTS. Hebrew "sechoh" = meditation, inner thought.
       Nothing is hidden.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Yet have ye not returned unto me" — FIVE TIMES (vv.6-11). God
   sent increasing difficulty to bring you back. How many warnings
   have YOU received? Financial trouble, health scares, broken
   relationships — could these be God's "yet have ye not returned"
   in YOUR life? Stop ignoring the pattern.

2. "Prepare to meet thy God" (v.12). You WILL meet God. The only
   question is whether you meet Him as prepared or unprepared.
   Every day is preparation day. Are you ready?

3. Consumer luxury built on someone else's suffering (v.1). Whose
   pain funds your comfort? Exploitation doesn't always look
   dramatic — it looks like demanding more while caring less about
   where it comes from.

4. Religious enthusiasm can be pure transgression (vv.4-5). Going
   to church, giving offerings, being visibly spiritual — if your
   heart hasn't changed and the poor are still crushed, God
   sarcastically says: "Enjoy your sin! This is what you LOVE."
   Don't confuse religious activity with actual obedience.
