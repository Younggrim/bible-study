Amos - Chapter 3
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God declares that Israel's election brings greater accountability, not immunity — "You only have I known, therefore I will punish you" — and the prophet speaks because the Lord GOD has spoken; who can but prophesy?


AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Amos, a shepherd from Tekoa in Judah who prophesied to
the northern kingdom (approximately 760-750 BC). Key theme: social
justice — God judges nations (including Israel) for oppressing the
poor. "Let judgment run down as waters, and righteousness as a
mighty stream" (5:24).

Classification: Prophetic Oracle — Privilege Means Accountability
Key Themes: Election means responsibility not immunity, cause and
effect in judgment, God reveals His plans to prophets, the wealthy
who store up violence, luxury destroyed

Historical Context: Chapter 3 establishes the theological basis
for God's judgment on Israel: because they are chosen ("You only
have I known"), they are MORE accountable, not less. Amos uses a
series of rhetorical cause-and-effect questions (vv.3-6) to prove
that his prophecy is not arbitrary — it has a CAUSE (Israel's sin)
and an inevitable EFFECT (judgment). He invites even pagan nations
(Ashdod, Egypt) to WITNESS Israel's corruption — it's so bad that
pagans would be shocked.

Structure:
- Election = Greater Accountability (vv.1-2)
- Cause and Effect: Seven Rhetorical Questions (vv.3-6)
- God Reveals His Plans to Prophets (vv.7-8)
- Pagan Witnesses to Israel's Corruption (vv.9-10)
- Judgment: Only Scraps Will Remain (vv.11-15)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.2  — "You only have I known of all the families of the earth:
        therefore I will punish you for all your iniquities" (KJV)
        — The most counter-intuitive "therefore" in the Bible.
        Being CHOSEN does not mean being EXEMPT. It means being
        held to a HIGHER standard. Privilege increases accountability.

v.3  — "Can two walk together, except they be agreed?" (KJV) —
        The famous question. Fellowship requires agreement. If God
        and Israel are no longer "agreed" (Israel has departed from
        the covenant), they can no longer walk together. The
        relationship has been broken by Israel's departure.

v.7  — "Surely the Lord GOD will do nothing, but he revealeth his
        secret unto his servants the prophets" (KJV) — God doesn't
        act in stealth. He WARNS through prophets first. Amos's
        presence IS the warning. If you hear the prophet, judgment
        has not yet fallen — there is still time.

v.8  — "The lion hath roared, who will not fear? the Lord GOD hath
        spoken, who can but prophesy?" (KJV) — Amos's defense of
        his ministry. He didn't CHOOSE to prophesy — God SPOKE, and
        he had no choice but to deliver the message. The prophet is
        compelled by God's word.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.2 — "known" — Hebrew "yada" = intimate, relational knowing (not
      just cognitive awareness). God CHOSE Israel in intimate
      covenant. This makes their betrayal worse, not less serious.

v.12 — "two legs, or a piece of an ear" — A shepherd recovers
       scraps from a lion to prove the animal was lost to predation.
       Only FRAGMENTS of Israel will survive. Not a rescue — just
       evidence of what was lost.

v.15 — "houses of ivory" — Excavations at Samaria have uncovered
       ivory inlays in palatial homes, confirming this luxury.
       Built on oppression, destroyed in judgment.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "You only have I known — THEREFORE I will punish" (v.2). Your
   spiritual privileges (upbringing, Bible access, church, teaching)
   do not protect you — they EXPOSE you. Greater knowledge = greater
   responsibility. Stop assuming your background saves you.

2. "Can two walk together except they be agreed?" (v.3). You cannot
   walk with God while walking in the opposite direction. Agreement
   precedes fellowship. Where have you departed from God's ways?
   That's where the fellowship broke down.

3. God always warns before He acts (v.7). The fact that you're
   reading this, feeling convicted — THAT is God's warning. Don't
   ignore it. The prophet speaks before judgment falls. Respond now.
