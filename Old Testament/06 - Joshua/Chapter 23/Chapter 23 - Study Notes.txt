Joshua - Chapter 23
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
An aging Joshua gives a farewell address, warning Israel that God will drive them out if they intermarry with Canaanites and serve their gods. He reminds them that God has faithfully kept every promise.

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Joshua addresses Israel at an unspecified location (likely Shiloh or Timnath-serah) near the end of his life
- The territory described: "from the Jordan to the Great Sea" — the full breadth of the promised land
- Nations remaining among Israel: scattered throughout the tribal territories, a persistent threat

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------
Enduring Word (David Guzik):
  https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/joshua-23/
  Guzik notes the balance in Joshua's farewell: past faithfulness (God
  kept every promise) and future warning (He will also keep His threats).
  Joshua does not leave Israel with false comfort. He loves them enough
  to warn them. Guzik also highlights v.11 — "love the LORD your God" —
  as the single most important command. Everything else flows from this.

Charles Spurgeon:
  "Joshua says, 'Not one thing hath failed.' This is the testimony of a
  man who walked with God for sixty years through wilderness and war. He
  tested every promise and found them all true. Can you say the same?
  Look back over your life — has God ever failed you? Has one word of
  His fallen to the ground? Then trust Him for the future. What He has
  been, He will be. What He has done, He will do."
REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. "Not one thing hath failed" (v.14). Joshua's deathbed testimony is
   God's perfect faithfulness. After a lifetime of testing God's
   promises, his verdict is: not one failed. Can we say the same? Look
   back — has God ever broken His word to you? Then trust Him for what
   lies ahead.

2. God's faithfulness cuts both ways (v.15). He is as faithful to judge
   as to bless. His warnings are as certain as His promises. We cannot
   claim His blessings while ignoring His conditions. Grace is not
   license. Mercy does not cancel holiness.

3. "Be very courageous to keep and to do" (v.6). Obedience requires
   courage — especially when surrounded by a culture that mocks God's
   standards. It takes more courage to obey than to fight. The hardest
   battles are internal, not external.

4. "Love the LORD your God" (v.11). This is the root command. All
   obedience flows from love. All apostasy begins with love growing
   cold. Guard your heart's affection for God above all else. When love
   is right, obedience follows naturally.

5. The danger of intermarriage/mixture (v.12). Joshua warns against
   intimate association with those who worship other gods. The
   application is not ethnic but spiritual: "Be ye not unequally yoked
   together with unbelievers" (2 Corinthians 6:14). Intimate
   partnership with the world leads to spiritual compromise.

6. Snares, traps, scourges, thorns (v.13). The sins we tolerate become
   the sins that torment us. What we refuse to drive out becomes what
   drives us out. The Canaanites Israel kept became the instruments of
   Israel's suffering. Deal with sin completely — or it will deal with
   you.
