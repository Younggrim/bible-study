Joshua - Chapter 23
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
An aging Joshua gives a farewell address, warning Israel that God will drive them out if they intermarry with Canaanites and serve their gods. He reminds them that God has faithfully kept every promise.

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Joshua addresses Israel at an unspecified location (likely Shiloh or Timnath-serah) near the end of his life
- The territory described: "from the Jordan to the Great Sea" — the full breadth of the promised land
- Nations remaining among Israel: scattered throughout the tribal territories, a persistent threat

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.3  — "You have seen all that the LORD your God has done to all these
        nations because of you; for the LORD your God is he that has
        fought for you." (ESV) — The foundation of Joshua's appeal: personal
        experience. They SAW God fight. They witnessed His power. Faith
        is built on what God has already done.

v.6  — "Be you therefore very courageous to keep and to do all that is
        written in the book of the law of Moses, that you turn not aside
        therefrom to the right hand or to the left." (ESV) — Echoes God's
        charge to Joshua in 1:7. Courage is needed not just for battle
        but for obedience. It takes strength to remain faithful when
        surrounded by pagan culture.

v.8  — "But hold fast to the LORD your God, as you have done to this
        day." (ESV) — The positive command: cling to God. The same word
        ("davaq") used of marriage (Genesis 2:24). Intimate, inseparable
        devotion. Joshua commends their faithfulness thus far — now
        continue it.

v.10 — "One man of you shall chase a thousand: for the LORD your God,
        he it is that fighteth for you, as he has promised you." (ESV) — The
        supernatural ratio of God's power. One Israelite with God is
        more powerful than a thousand enemies without Him. Cf. Leviticus
        26:8; Deuteronomy 32:30.

v.11 — "Take good heed therefore to yourselves, that you love the LORD
        your God." (ESV) — The heart of the matter. All obedience flows from
        love. All apostasy begins with love growing cold. Guard your
        love for God above all else.

v.13 — "They shall be snares and traps to you, and scourges in your
        sides, and thorns in your eyes, until you perish from off this
        good land." (ESV) — Four images of torment: snares (entrapment), traps
        (deception), scourges/whips (pain), thorns (constant irritation).
        The nations Israel fails to drive out will become instruments of
        their suffering. Cf. Numbers 33:55.

v.14 — "Not one thing has failed of all the good things which the LORD
        your God spoke concerning you; all are come to pass to you,
        and not one thing has failed thereof." (ESV) — Joshua's personal
        testimony at the end of his life. He has seen God's faithfulness
        firsthand for over 60 years. Not one promise failed. This is
        the witness of a dying man — and dying men do not lie.

v.15 — "As all good things are come upon you, which the LORD your God
        promised you; so shall the LORD bring upon you all evil things." (ESV)
        — The terrifying corollary: God is as faithful to judge as He is
        to bless. His warnings are as certain as His promises. Do not
        presume on grace.

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------
Enduring Word (David Guzik):
  https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/joshua-23/
  Guzik notes the balance in Joshua's farewell: past faithfulness (God
  kept every promise) and future warning (He will also keep His threats).
  Joshua does not leave Israel with false comfort. He loves them enough
  to warn them. Guzik also highlights v.11 — "love the LORD your God" —
  as the single most important command. Everything else flows from this.

Charles Spurgeon:
  "Joshua says, 'Not one thing hath failed.' This is the testimony of a
  man who walked with God for sixty years through wilderness and war. He
  tested every promise and found them all true. Can you say the same?
  Look back over your life — has God ever failed you? Has one word of
  His fallen to the ground? Then trust Him for the future. What He has
  been, He will be. What He has done, He will do."
TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1  — KJV: "Joshua waxed old and stricken in age" / ESV: "Joshua was
       old and well advanced in years" — Hebrew "zaqen ba bayamim."
       Same phrase as 13:1. KJV's "waxed old" uses archaic "waxed"
       (grew/became). "Stricken in age" = advanced in years.

v.6  — KJV: "Be ye therefore very courageous to keep and to do" / ESV:
       "be very strong to keep and to do" — Hebrew "chazaqtem m'od
       lishmor v'la'asot." "Chazaq" = be strong, courageous, firm.
       Joshua echoes God's words to him (1:7). The courage needed is
       for obedience, not just battle.

v.7  — KJV: "neither make mention of the names of their gods, nor cause
       to swear by them" / ESV: "or make mention of the names of their
       gods or swear by them" — Hebrew "lo tazkiru" (not mention) and
       "lo tashbi'u" (not swear). Even speaking pagan gods' names in
       oaths was forbidden — it acknowledged their existence and power.

v.8  — KJV: "cleave unto the LORD" / ESV: "cling to the LORD" — Hebrew
       "davaq" again (as in 22:5). The marriage word. Intimate,
       permanent attachment. The opposite of "cleaving to" the remaining
       nations (v.12, same word).

v.10 — KJV: "One man of you shall chase a thousand" / ESV: "One man of
       you puts to flight a thousand" — Hebrew "ish echad mikkhem
       yirdof elef." "Radaph" = to pursue, chase, put to flight. This
       supernatural ratio demonstrates that Israel's power is not
       numerical but divine.

v.13 — KJV: "scourges in your sides" / ESV: "a whip on your sides" —
       Hebrew "shotet b'tsiddeikhem." "Shotet" = whip, scourge. The
       remaining nations will not merely coexist — they will actively
       torment Israel. What was tolerated becomes what punishes.

v.14 — KJV: "I am going the way of all the earth" / ESV: "I am about
       to go the way of all the earth" — Hebrew "holekh ha-yom b'derekh
       kol ha-aretz." A euphemism for death — "the way all the earth
       goes." Every human walks this path. Joshua faces death with the
       same courage he brought to battle.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Way of All the Earth — A euphemism for death (cf. 1 Kings 2:2 — David
               uses the same phrase). Every person, no matter how great,
               must walk this path. Joshua faces it with faith and uses
               his final moments to exhort faithfulness.

Cleave/Cling (davaq) — To adhere, hold fast, be joined to. Used of
               marriage (Genesis 2:24) and of devotion to God. Joshua
               uses it both positively (cleave to God, v.8) and as a
               warning (do not cleave to the nations, v.12). We will
               cling to something — the question is what.

Snares and Traps — Hebrew "pach" (snare, bird trap) and "moqesh" (bait,
               lure, trap). The remaining nations will entice Israel
               into sin — not through open warfare but through subtle
               attraction. The danger is seduction, not assault.

Scourges/Whips — Hebrew "shotet." What Israel tolerated would become
               what tormented them. The nations they failed to drive out
               would become instruments of divine discipline.

Book of the Law of Moses — The written Torah, the Pentateuch. Joshua's
               authority rests on this written Word. Obedience to it is
               the condition for continued blessing in the land.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
- v.4 — ASV: "I have allotted unto you these nations that remain, to be an inheritance for your tribes" / NET: "I have parceled out to your tribes these remaining nations" — ASV preserves "inheritance" (nachalah); NET uses "parceled out" (casual). Joshua's final address emphasizes that the remaining land is still Israel's God-given inheritance to be claimed.
- v.6 — ASV: "be ye very courageous to keep and to do all that is written in the book of the law of Moses" / NET: "Be very strong! Carefully obey all that is written in the law scroll of Moses" — ASV/KJV's "courageous to keep and do" echoes God's original commission to Joshua (1:7). The courage required is not military but moral — courage to obey. NET separates "strong" from obedience, weakening the connection.
- v.8 — ASV: "cleave unto Jehovah your God" / WEB: "hold fast to Yahweh your God" / NET: "you must be loyal to the Lord your God" — Again, "davak" (cleave/cling) becomes mere "loyalty" in NET. The Hebrew implies intimate, unbreakable attachment — the marriage verb of Genesis 2:24 applied to the divine-human relationship.
- v.13 — ASV: "they shall be a snare and a trap unto you, and a scourge in your sides, and thorns in your eyes" / NET: "They will trap and ensnare you; they will be a whip that tears your sides and thorns that blind your eyes" — NET adds "that tears" and "that blind" (interpretive expansions). ASV/KJV preserve the stark Hebrew imagery without embellishment.
- v.14 — ASV: "not one thing hath failed of all the good things which Jehovah your God spake concerning you; all are come to pass" / NET: "not even one of all the faithful promises the Lord your God made to you is left unfulfilled; every one was realized" — Joshua's deathbed testimony. ASV's double emphasis ("not one thing hath failed...all are come to pass") is stronger than NET's single statement. The repetition hammers home God's absolute faithfulness.
- v.16 — ASV: "When ye transgress the covenant of Jehovah your God" / NET: "If you violate the covenantal laws of the Lord your God" — ASV's "transgress the covenant" (avar b'rit — to cross over/break the covenant) uses the same verb as "crossing the Jordan." The tragic irony: just as they crossed (avar) the Jordan into blessing, they can cross (avar) God's covenant into judgment.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Not one thing hath failed" (v.14). Joshua's deathbed testimony is
   God's perfect faithfulness. After a lifetime of testing God's
   promises, his verdict is: not one failed. Can we say the same? Look
   back — has God ever broken His word to you? Then trust Him for what
   lies ahead.

2. God's faithfulness cuts both ways (v.15). He is as faithful to judge
   as to bless. His warnings are as certain as His promises. We cannot
   claim His blessings while ignoring His conditions. Grace is not
   license. Mercy does not cancel holiness.

3. "Be very courageous to keep and to do" (v.6). Obedience requires
   courage — especially when surrounded by a culture that mocks God's
   standards. It takes more courage to obey than to fight. The hardest
   battles are internal, not external.

4. "Love the LORD your God" (v.11). This is the root command. All
   obedience flows from love. All apostasy begins with love growing
   cold. Guard your heart's affection for God above all else. When love
   is right, obedience follows naturally.

5. The danger of intermarriage/mixture (v.12). Joshua warns against
   intimate association with those who worship other gods. The
   application is not ethnic but spiritual: "Be ye not unequally yoked
   together with unbelievers" (2 Corinthians 6:14). Intimate
   partnership with the world leads to spiritual compromise.

6. Snares, traps, scourges, thorns (v.13). The sins we tolerate become
   the sins that torment us. What we refuse to drive out becomes what
   drives us out. The Canaanites Israel kept became the instruments of
   Israel's suffering. Deal with sin completely — or it will deal with
   you.
