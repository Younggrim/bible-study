Joshua - Chapter 21
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The Levites receive forty-eight cities scattered throughout all the tribes, since the LORD Himself is their inheritance. The chapter concludes: not one word of God's promises has failed—all came to pass.

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- 48 Levitical cities distributed throughout all tribal territories — Levites had no single tribal allotment
- Kohathites received cities in Judah, Simeon, Benjamin, Ephraim, Dan, and west Manasseh
- Gershonites received cities in Issachar, Asher, Naphtali, and east Manasseh; Merarites in Zebulun, Reuben, and Gad

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.2  — "The LORD commanded by the hand of Moses to give us cities to
        dwell in, with the suburbs thereof for our cattle." (ESV) — The
        Levites claim God's promise. Like Zelophehad's daughters and
        Caleb, they know what God has said and they ask for it. God's
        promises are meant to be claimed.

v.3  — "And the children of Israel gave to the Levites out of their
        inheritance, at the commandment of the LORD, these cities and
        their suburbs." (ESV) — Every tribe contributes. Supporting those who
        serve God full-time is a shared responsibility. No tribe is
        exempt from providing for the Levites.

v.41 — "All the cities of the Levites within the possession of the
        children of Israel were forty and eight cities with their
        suburbs." (ESV) — 48 cities scattered across all tribal territories.
        The Levites are everywhere — God's teachers distributed
        throughout the nation. No region is without spiritual leadership.

v.43 — "And the LORD gave to Israel all the land which he swore to
        give to their fathers; and they possessed it, and dwelt
        therein." (ESV) — The promise to Abraham (Genesis 15:18-21) is
        fulfilled. God swore it — God did it. Centuries of waiting end
        in possession.

v.44 — "And the LORD gave them rest round about, according to all that
        he swore to their fathers: and there stood not a man of all
        their enemies before them; the LORD delivered all their enemies
        into their hand." (ESV) — Rest — the goal of the conquest. Not merely
        land but peace. Not merely possession but security. God's rest
        is complete (cf. Hebrews 4:8-9).

v.45 — "Not one word of all the good promises that the LORD had made to
        the house of Israel had failed; all came to pass." (ESV) — The
        chapter's climax and one of the Bible's greatest statements of
        divine faithfulness. NOT ONE WORD failed. Every promise kept.
        Every word fulfilled. God's track record is perfect.

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------
Enduring Word (David Guzik):
  https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/joshua-21/
  Guzik emphasizes vv.43-45 as the theological heart of the book of
  Joshua. God's faithfulness is absolute — not one promise failed. He
  notes that this does not mean every individual Canaanite was expelled
  (chapters 15-17 show otherwise), but that God fulfilled His covenant
  obligations. Israel's incomplete possession was their failure, not
  God's. The promise was kept; the appropriation was partial.

Charles Spurgeon:
  "'There failed not ought of any good thing which the LORD had spoken.'
  Not one thing! Can you find a single promise of God that has fallen to
  the ground? Search the Scriptures — has He ever failed you? Has He
  ever spoken and not done it? Let this verse be your anchor: ALL came
  to pass. What He has promised for your future, He will perform with
  the same perfection. Not one word shall fail."
TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.2  — KJV: "suburbs thereof for our cattle" / ESV: "pasturelands for
       our livestock" — Hebrew "migrash" = open land, pasture ground
       surrounding a city. As noted in 14:4, KJV's "suburbs" is
       misleading to modern readers. ESV's "pasturelands" correctly
       identifies these as grazing areas, not residential neighborhoods.

v.11 — KJV: "the city of Arba the father of Anak" / ESV: "Kiriath-arba
       (Arba being the father of Anak)" — Note that Hebron is
       simultaneously Caleb's inheritance (14:13), a city of refuge
       (20:7), and a Levitical city (21:13). The fields and villages
       belonged to Caleb (v.12); the city itself served multiple
       sacred functions.

v.43 — KJV: "all the land which he sware to give unto their fathers" /
       ESV: "all the land that he swore to give to their fathers" —
       Hebrew "nishba" = swore (niphal of "shava"). God's promise was
       not merely spoken but sworn — an oath. The strongest possible
       commitment (cf. Hebrews 6:13-18).

v.44 — KJV: "there stood not a man of all their enemies before them" /
       ESV: "Not one of all their enemies had withstood them" — Hebrew
       "lo amad ish... mipneihem." Literally "no man stood before
       them." This echoes God's promise to Joshua in 1:5: "There shall
       not any man be able to stand before thee." Promise made — promise
       kept.

v.45 — KJV: "There failed not ought of any good thing which the LORD
       had spoken" / ESV: "Not one word of all the good promises that
       the LORD had made... had failed" — Hebrew "lo naphal davar mi-kol
       ha-davar ha-tov." "Naphal" = to fall. Not one word "fell" — i.e.,
       fell to the ground unfulfilled. Every word stood firm. ESV adds
       "word" and "promises" for clarity; KJV's "ought" (anything) is
       more literal but archaic.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Levitical Cities — 48 cities distributed among the three Levitical clans
               (Kohathites, Gershonites, Merarites) throughout all
               tribal territories. Included the 6 cities of refuge.
               Provided dwelling places and pasturelands for Levites
               who had no territorial inheritance.

Kohathites     — Descendants of Kohath, Levi's second son. Divided into
               priestly (Aaron's line) and non-priestly branches. The
               priests received cities in Judah, Simeon, and Benjamin
               (near the future temple site). Other Kohathites received
               cities in Ephraim, Dan, and Manasseh.

Gershonites    — Descendants of Gershon (or Gershom), Levi's firstborn.
               Received 13 cities in the northern tribes (Issachar,
               Asher, Naphtali) and eastern half-Manasseh.

Merarites      — Descendants of Merari, Levi's third son. Received 12
               cities in Zebulun, Reuben, and Gad — the peripheral
               territories.

Pasturelands (migrash) — Open land surrounding Levitical cities,
               extending 1,000 cubits from the city wall in each
               direction (Numbers 35:4-5). Used for grazing livestock.
               Could not be sold (Leviticus 25:34).

Rest (menukhah) — Hebrew concept of peace, security, and settled
               dwelling. The goal of the conquest was not merely land
               but rest — freedom from enemies, security in God's
               presence. Ultimately points to the eternal rest in Christ
               (Hebrews 4:9-11).

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
- v.2 — NET: "The Lord told Moses to assign us cities in which to live along with the grazing areas for our cattle" / ASV: "Jehovah commanded by Moses to give us cities to dwell in, with the suburbs thereof for our cattle" / KJV: "The LORD commanded by the hand of Moses to give us cities to dwell in, with the suburbs thereof" — KJV/ASV's "suburbs" (migrash — pasture lands/open spaces around a city) is archaic; NET's "grazing areas" and WEB's "pasture lands" clarify the meaning. These were not urban suburbs but agricultural land surrounding each Levitical city.
- v.43 — NET: "So the Lord gave Israel all the land he had solemnly promised to their ancestors, and they conquered it and lived in it" / ASV: "So Jehovah gave unto Israel all the land which he sware to give unto their fathers; and they possessed it, and dwelt therein" / WEB: "So Yahweh gave to Israel all the land which he swore to give to their fathers. They possessed it, and lived in it" — NET uses "solemnly promised" for "swore" (nishba — to swear an oath). The oath language is stronger — God bound Himself by His own character. ASV/WEB preserve "possessed" (yarash) where NET uses "conquered."
- v.44 — NET: "The Lord made them secure, in fulfillment of all he had solemnly promised their ancestors" / ASV: "And Jehovah gave them rest round about, according to all that he sware unto their fathers" / WEB: "Yahweh gave them rest all around, according to all that he swore to their fathers" — NET's "made them secure" interprets Hebrew "menucha" (rest). KJV/ASV/WEB preserve "rest" — the theological rest promised since Deuteronomy 12:10, fulfilled here and ultimately in Christ (Hebrews 4:8-9).
- v.45 — NET: "Not one of the Lord's faithful promises to the family of Israel was left unfulfilled; every one was realized" / KJV: "There failed not ought of any good thing which the LORD had spoken unto the house of Israel; all came to pass" / ASV: "There failed not aught of any good thing which Jehovah had spoken unto the house of Israel; all came to pass" — The climactic verse of Joshua. NET's "faithful promises" interprets "good thing" (davar tov — good word/thing). KJV/ASV preserve the comprehensive "not a thing...of any good thing...all came to pass" — absolute divine faithfulness.
- v.45 — WEB: "Nothing failed of any good thing which Yahweh had spoken to the house of Israel. All came to pass" — WEB's clarity and retention of the divine name makes this perhaps the most transparent modern rendering of this capstone verse.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "There failed not ought of any good thing which the LORD had spoken;
   all came to pass" (v.45). This is the Bible's own testimony about
   God's faithfulness. Not one promise failed. Not one word fell. What
   God says, He does. What He promises, He performs. This is the
   foundation of all faith: God keeps His word — perfectly, completely,
   without exception.

2. Levites scattered as teachers (v.41). What began as a curse (Genesis
   49:7) became a blessing. The Levites' scattering meant every tribe
   had access to God's teachers. God transforms our curses into
   blessings. What seems like judgment may become our greatest ministry.

3. Shared responsibility (v.3). Every tribe gave cities to the Levites.
   Supporting those in full-time ministry is not optional — it is
   commanded. The body of Christ supports its teachers, pastors, and
   missionaries as Israel supported its Levites.

4. God gives rest (v.44). The conquest's goal was not merely territory
   but rest — peace on every side. God's ultimate gift to His people is
   rest (Matthew 11:28; Hebrews 4:9). Have you entered His rest? Or are
   you still striving in your own strength?

5. Promise and possession (v.43). God gave the land AND they possessed
   it AND they dwelt in it. Three stages: God's gift, human
   appropriation, settled enjoyment. God gives — we must receive and
   inhabit. Salvation is a gift, but we must live in it daily.
