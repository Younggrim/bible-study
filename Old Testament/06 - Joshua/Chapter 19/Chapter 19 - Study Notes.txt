Joshua - Chapter 19
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The remaining tribes—Simeon, Zebulun, Issachar, Asher, Naphtali, and Dan—receive their allotments by lot. Joshua himself is given Timnath-serah in Ephraim as his personal inheritance.

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Six tribes receive allotments: Simeon (within Judah's territory, far south), Zebulun and Issachar (Jezreel Valley area), Asher (northwestern coast), Naphtali (upper Galilee), Dan (western lowlands near Philistia)
- Joshua receives Timnath-serah in the hill country of Ephraim — his personal inheritance
- Dan's allotment proves too small; they will later migrate north (Judges 18)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.9  — "Out of the portion of the children of Judah was the inheritance
        of the children of Simeon: for the part of the children of Judah
        was too much for them." (ESV) — God's providence: Judah's excess
        becomes Simeon's provision. What is too much for one becomes
        exactly enough for another. God wastes nothing in His economy.

v.47 — "And the coast of the children of Dan went out too little for
        them: therefore the children of Dan went up to fight against
        Leshem, and took it." (ESV) — Dan's migration. Rather than fighting
        for their allotted territory (which was difficult due to
        Philistine pressure), they abandoned it and conquered an easier
        target far to the north. This is not commended — it represents
        failure to trust God for the assigned inheritance.

v.49 — "When they had made an end of dividing the land for inheritance
        by their coasts, the children of Israel gave an inheritance to
        Joshua the son of Nun among them." (ESV) — Joshua takes last. He who
        led the conquest, who could have claimed the best territory
        first, waits until every tribe is settled. Servant leadership
        in action.

v.50 — "According to the word of the LORD they gave him the city which
        he asked, even Timnath-serah in mount Ephraim: and he built the
        city, and dwelt therein." (ESV) — Joshua asks for a city that needs
        rebuilding — not a ready-made inheritance but a project. His
        humility and work ethic are evident to the end.

v.51 — "So they made an end of dividing the country." (ESV) — The land
        distribution is complete. What God promised to Abraham (Genesis
        15:18-21), confirmed to Moses (Deuteronomy 1:8), and commanded
        Joshua to distribute (Joshua 1:6) is now accomplished. Every
        tribe has its inheritance.

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------
Enduring Word (David Guzik):
  https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/joshua-19/
  Guzik highlights Joshua's humility in taking his inheritance last. He
  also notes that Dan's migration north was not an act of faith but of
  failure — they abandoned their God-given territory because it was too
  hard. The contrast between Dan (who fled difficulty) and Caleb (who
  embraced it) is stark.

Charles Spurgeon:
  "Joshua took his inheritance last. The general who led the armies, who
  could have claimed the choicest land, waited until every tribe was
  served. And what did he ask for? A city in ruins that he must rebuild
  with his own hands. This is the spirit of Christ — who came not to be
  served but to serve. The greatest leaders are the last to take and the
  first to give."
TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1  — Hebrew "b'tokh nachalat b'nei Yehudah" — within the inheritance of the children of Judah.
       KJV: "their inheritance was within the inheritance of the children of Judah"
       ESV: "their inheritance was in the midst of the inheritance of the people of Judah"
       ASV: "their inheritance was in the midst of the inheritance of the children of Judah"
       NET: "Their assigned land was in the middle of Judah's assigned land"
       WEB: "their inheritance was in the midst of the inheritance of the children of Judah"
       Analysis: NET uses "assigned land" consistently where all others preserve "inheritance"
       (nachalah). The sacred lot determined God's will for each tribe's inheritance. Simeon
       had no independent territory — their cities were embedded within Judah's borders,
       unique among the tribes.

v.9  — Hebrew "rav mehem" — too much/great for them.
       KJV: "the part of the children of Judah was too much for them"
       ESV: "the portion of the people of Judah was too large for them"
       ASV: "the portion of the children of Judah was too much for them"
       NET: "Judah's assigned land was too large for them"
       WEB: "the portion of the children of Judah was too much for them"
       Analysis: Judah's territory was disproportionately large for their population, so
       Simeon was carved out of it. God's redistribution ensures equity. NET again uses
       "assigned land" rather than the inheritance vocabulary that permeates the Hebrew.

v.15 — Hebrew "Beit Lechem" — house of bread.
       KJV: "Bethlehem"
       ESV: "Bethlehem"
       ASV: "Bethlehem"
       NET: "Bethlehem"
       WEB: "Bethlehem"
       Analysis: All translations render the name identically. This is Bethlehem of Zebulun
       in Galilee (cf. Judges 12:8-10), NOT the Bethlehem of Judah where David and Christ
       were born. There were multiple towns with this name ("house of bread").

v.29 — Hebrew "ir mivtsar Tsor" — fortified city of Tyre.
       KJV: "the strong city Tyre"
       ESV: "the fortified city of Tyre"
       ASV: "the fortified city of Tyre"
       NET: "the fortified city of Tyre"
       WEB: "the fortified city of Tyre"
       Analysis: KJV's "strong" is slightly less precise than "fortified" (mivtsar). Tyre
       was one of the greatest Phoenician cities, famous for its island fortress. Though
       within Asher's allotment, it was never conquered by Israel.

v.47 — Hebrew "yatsa me-hem g'vul b'nei Dan" — the border of Dan went out from them.
       KJV: "the coast of the children of Dan went out too little for them"
       ESV: "When the territory of the people of Dan was lost to them"
       ASV: "the border of the children of Dan went out beyond them"
       NET: "The Danites failed to conquer their territory, so they went up and fought with Leshem"
       WEB: "the border of the children of Dan went out beyond them"
       Analysis: Significant translation differences. KJV suggests Dan's territory was too
       small; ESV suggests they lost control of it; NET interprets it as failure to conquer;
       ASV/WEB preserve the ambiguous Hebrew literally ("went out beyond/from them"). ESV's
       reading fits Judges 1:34 where Amorites pressed Dan back. NET's interpretive
       rendering makes the narrative clearest but adds theological judgment not explicit
       in the Hebrew.

v.49 — Hebrew "kilu l'nachel et ha-aretz ligvuloteha" — they finished distributing the land by its borders.
       KJV: "when they had made an end of dividing the land for inheritance by their coasts"
       ESV: "When they had finished distributing the several territories of the land as inheritances"
       ASV: "when they had made an end of distributing the land for inheritance by their borders"
       NET: "When they finished dividing the land into its regions"
       WEB: "when they had finished distributing the land for inheritance by their borders"
       Analysis: NET's "regions" loses the inheritance language. KJV/ASV/WEB preserve
       "inheritance by their borders" — the nachalah vocabulary permeates these chapters,
       emphasizing divine gift rather than mere territorial assignment.

v.50 — Hebrew "Timnat Serach" — portion remaining or abundance.
       KJV: "Timnath-serah"
       ESV: "Timnath-serah"
       ASV: "Timnath-serah"
       NET: "the city he requested"
       WEB: "Timnath Serah"
       Analysis: In Judges 2:9 it is called "Timnath-heres" (letters reversed). "Serah"
       may mean "portion remaining" or "abundance"; "Heres" means "sun." Some suggest the
       name was reversed to avoid association with sun worship. NET emphasizes the divine
       command ("As the Lord had instructed") while ASV uses "the commandment of Jehovah"
       and WEB "according to Yahweh's commandment" — the divine name distinction appears
       even in recording Joshua's personal inheritance.

v.51 — Hebrew "nachalot" — inheritances.
       KJV: "These are the inheritances, which Eleazar the priest, and Joshua the son of Nun...distributed for inheritance by lot"
       ESV: "These are the inheritances that Eleazar the priest and Joshua the son of Nun...allotted"
       ASV: "These are the inheritances, which Eleazar the priest, and Joshua the son of Nun...distributed for inheritance by lot"
       NET: "These are the land assignments that Eleazar the priest, Joshua son of Nun, and the Israelite tribal leaders made by drawing lots"
       WEB: "These are the inheritances which Eleazar the priest, Joshua the son of Nun...distributed for inheritance by lot"
       Analysis: NET's "land assignments" vs. KJV/ASV/WEB's "inheritances...distributed for
       inheritance" — the summation verse uses "nachalah" twice in Hebrew, emphasizing that
       all land is divine inheritance. NET loses this theological weight with secular
       administrative language.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Simeon         — Second son of Jacob and Leah. His tribe was absorbed
               into Judah, fulfilling Jacob's prophecy of scattering
               (Genesis 49:7). Simeon eventually disappears from the
               tribal lists and is not mentioned in Moses' blessing
               (Deuteronomy 33).

Dan            — Fifth son of Jacob (by Bilhah). His tribe was allotted
               coastal territory but could not hold it against Amorite/
               Philistine pressure. They migrated north and conquered
               Laish/Leshem (Judges 18). Dan later became a center of
               idolatry (Judges 18:30; 1 Kings 12:29).

Timnath-serah  — Joshua's personal inheritance in the hill country of
               Ephraim. He rebuilt the city and lived there until his
               death at 110 (Joshua 24:30). The name may mean "remaining
               portion" — fitting for the leader who took last.

Zebulun        — Tenth son of Jacob (sixth by Leah). His tribe received
               territory in lower Galilee. Jacob prophesied Zebulun
               would "dwell at the haven of the sea" (Genesis 49:13).
               Isaiah 9:1 associates Zebulun with the land that would
               see "a great light" — fulfilled in Christ's Galilean
               ministry (Matthew 4:13-16).

Naphtali       — Sixth son of Jacob (by Bilhah). His tribe received
               territory in upper Galilee, including the western shore
               of the Sea of Galilee. Like Zebulun, associated with
               Christ's ministry in Isaiah 9:1 and Matthew 4:13-16.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Joshua takes last (vv.49-50). The leader who could have claimed first
   choice waited until everyone else was served. This is Christ-like
   leadership: "The last shall be first" (Matthew 20:16). True authority
   serves rather than seizes.

2. Dan's failure to possess (v.47). Rather than fighting for their
   God-given territory, Dan abandoned it for an easier conquest. We face
   the same temptation: when God's assignment is hard, we look for
   shortcuts. But the easy path is not always God's path. Stay where God
   planted you.

3. Simeon within Judah (v.9). God's judgment on Simeon (Genesis 49:7)
   becomes God's provision — they receive cities within the strongest
   tribe. Even divine discipline is tempered with mercy. God scatters
   Simeon but places them under Judah's protection.

4. The distribution is complete (v.51). Every tribe has its place. God's
   promises are fulfilled — not partially but completely. What He
   promised to Abraham, He delivered to Joshua's generation. God finishes
   what He starts.

5. Rebuilding what is broken (v.50). Joshua's city needed rebuilding.
   He did not ask for a finished product but for a project. Sometimes
   God gives us broken things to rebuild — churches, relationships,
   communities. The inheritance may require work, but it is still a gift.
