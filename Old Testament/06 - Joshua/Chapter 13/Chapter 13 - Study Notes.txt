Joshua - Chapter 13
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God tells the aging Joshua that much land remains to be conquered and instructs him to divide it by faith. The inheritance of the Transjordan tribes and the exclusion of Levi from land allotment are reviewed.

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Unconquered land: Philistine territory (5 cities on the coastal plain), Sidon region (far north), and Gezer (central lowlands)
- Transjordan allotments: Reuben (south of Arnon to Heshbon), Gad (Gilead/central Transjordan), half-Manasseh (Bashan in the north)
- Joshua is now old; much territory remains to be divided even though the major campaigns are complete

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------
Enduring Word (David Guzik):
  https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/joshua-13/
  Guzik emphasizes that God told Joshua to divide land not yet conquered —
  an act of faith. He notes the tension: the conquest was real but
  incomplete. God's promise was certain, but Israel's obedience was
  partial. The Geshurites and Maacathites remaining "until this day"
  shows the cost of incomplete obedience.

Charles Spurgeon:
  "The tribe of Levi had God for their inheritance. What a portion! Let
  those who serve God full-time take comfort: if you have God, you have
  all things. The cattle on a thousand hills are His. He who possesses
  God possesses everything. Levi had no fields — but Levi had the LORD.
  Who would not make that exchange?"
REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. "There remaineth yet very much land to be possessed" (v.1). Even after
   great victories, there is more to take. The Christian life is never
   "arrived." There are always more promises to claim, more territory to
   possess, more growth to pursue. Complacency after past victories is
   a danger.

2. God commands distribution of unconquered land (v.6). Faith acts on
   God's promises before seeing full fulfillment. Joshua allocated land
   not yet taken — because God said it was theirs. We too must act on
   what God has promised, not merely on what we can see.

3. Partial obedience is disobedience (v.13). Israel did not drive out
   the Geshurites and Maacathites. These compromises seem small but
   compound over time. The enemies we tolerate today become the snares
   of tomorrow. What "Canaanites" are we allowing to remain?

4. "The LORD God of Israel was their inheritance" (v.33). Levi had no
   land — but they had God. This is the highest inheritance. Every
   believer is a "priest" (1 Peter 2:9) whose true portion is the Lord
   Himself. Do we value God's presence above earthly possessions?

5. Balaam's end (v.22). He who sought to profit from God's people died
   by their sword. Those who use spiritual gifts for personal gain will
   face judgment. The way of Balaam is a warning to every generation
   (2 Peter 2:15; Jude 11; Revelation 2:14).
