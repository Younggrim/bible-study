Joshua - Chapter 13
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God tells the aging Joshua that much land remains to be conquered and instructs him to divide it by faith. The inheritance of the Transjordan tribes and the exclusion of Levi from land allotment are reviewed.

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Unconquered land: Philistine territory (5 cities on the coastal plain), Sidon region (far north), and Gezer (central lowlands)
- Transjordan allotments: Reuben (south of Arnon to Heshbon), Gad (Gilead/central Transjordan), half-Manasseh (Bashan in the north)
- Joshua is now old; much territory remains to be divided even though the major campaigns are complete

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "Now Joshua was old and stricken in years; and the LORD said to
        him, You art old and stricken in years, and there remains yet
        very much land to be possessed." (ESV) — God acknowledges Joshua's age
        but does not release him from duty. There is still work to do.
        The remaining land must be allocated even if not yet conquered.

v.6  — "Only divide you it by lot to the Israelites for an
        inheritance, as I have commanded you." (ESV) — God commands Joshua to
        distribute land not yet taken. Faith allocates what God has
        promised before it is fully realized. The lot ensures God's
        sovereignty in the distribution (Proverbs 16:33).

v.13 — "Nevertheless the children of Israel expelled not the Geshurites,
        nor the Maachathites: but the Geshurites and the Maachathites
        dwell among the Israelites until this day." (ESV) — The first recorded
        failure to complete the conquest. Partial obedience is
        disobedience. These remaining peoples would become thorns
        (cf. Numbers 33:55).

v.14 — "Only to the tribe of Levi he gave none inheritance; the
        sacrifices of the LORD God of Israel made by fire are their
        inheritance, as he said to them." (ESV) — Levi's portion is God
        Himself and the offerings made to Him. They would receive cities
        to dwell in (chapter 21) but no territorial block.

v.22 — "Balaam also the son of Beor, the soothsayer, did the children of
        Israel slay with the sword among them that were slain by them." (ESV)
        — A parenthetical note: Balaam, who could not curse Israel but
        led them into sin at Baal-peor (Numbers 31:16), met his end by
        Israel's sword. Sin's wages are death.

v.33 — "But to the tribe of Levi Moses gave not any inheritance: the
        LORD God of Israel was their inheritance, as he said to them." (ESV)
        — The chapter's closing statement echoes v.14. The repetition
        emphasizes: God Himself is the greatest inheritance. Those who
        serve Him need no earthly portion — He is enough.

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------
Enduring Word (David Guzik):
  https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/joshua-13/
  Guzik emphasizes that God told Joshua to divide land not yet conquered —
  an act of faith. He notes the tension: the conquest was real but
  incomplete. God's promise was certain, but Israel's obedience was
  partial. The Geshurites and Maacathites remaining "until this day"
  shows the cost of incomplete obedience.

Charles Spurgeon:
  "The tribe of Levi had God for their inheritance. What a portion! Let
  those who serve God full-time take comfort: if you have God, you have
  all things. The cattle on a thousand hills are His. He who possesses
  God possesses everything. Levi had no fields — but Levi had the LORD.
  Who would not make that exchange?"
TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1  — Hebrew "zaqen ba bayamim" — lit. "old, come into the days."
       KJV: "old and stricken in years"
       ESV: "old and advanced in years"
       ASV: "Thou art old and well stricken in years"
       NET: "You are very old"
       WEB: "there remains yet very much land to be possessed"
       Analysis: KJV's "stricken" is archaic for "advanced." Both KJV/ESV
       convey extreme old age (Joshua was likely 90-100; he dies at 110 in
       24:29). NET simplifies, losing the Hebrew idiom. ASV preserves the
       traditional phrasing. NET also uses "conquered" where ASV/KJV use
       "possessed" (Hebrew "yarash") — "possessed" preserves the
       inheritance theology, as the land is to be owned as a divine
       inheritance, not merely militarily subdued. WEB closely follows the
       Hebrew "eretz harbeh m'od nish'arah l'rishtah," preserving both
       "very much" and "to possess."

v.2  — Hebrew "g'lilot" — regions, districts, circuits.
       KJV: "all the borders of the Philistines, and all Geshuri"
       ESV: "all the regions of the Philistines, and all those of the Geshurites"
       ASV: "all the regions of the Philistines, and all the Geshurites"
       NET: "all the territory of the Philistines and all the Geshurite area"
       WEB: "all the regions of the Philistines, and all the Geshurites"
       Analysis: ESV's "regions" is more precise than KJV's "borders."
       ASV and WEB follow ESV's approach. NET paraphrases with "territory"
       and "area," which is adequate but less distinctive.

v.3  — Hebrew "sarnei P'lishtim" — "Seren" (pl. "seranim"), a title
       unique to Philistine rulers.
       KJV: "five lords of the Philistines"
       ESV: "five rulers of the Philistines"
       ASV: "the five lords of the Philistines"
       NET: "the five Philistine rulers"
       WEB: "the five lords of the Philistines"
       Analysis: "Seren" is not the usual Hebrew "melek" (king) or "sar"
       (prince) — it may be related to the Greek "tyrannos." KJV's "lords"
       and ESV's "rulers" both approximate this distinctive title.
       ASV/WEB preserve KJV's "lords"; NET follows ESV's "rulers."

v.6  — Hebrew "nachalah" — inheritance, permanent divine allotment.
       KJV: "only divide thou it by lot unto the Israelites for an inheritance"
       ESV: "only allot it to Israel for an inheritance"
       ASV: "only allot thou it unto Israel for an inheritance"
       NET: "you be sure to parcel it out to Israel as I instructed you"
       WEB: "only divide it by lot to the Israelites for an inheritance"
       Analysis: NET's "parcel it out" is casual and loses the covenant
       concept of "inheritance" (nachalah) — a divinely granted,
       permanent, hereditary possession. KJV/ASV/WEB/ESV all preserve
       this theological weight.

v.12 — Hebrew "Repha'im" — an ancient race of large stature.
       KJV: "remnant of the giants"
       ESV: "remnant of the Rephaim"
       ASV: "the remnant of the Rephaim"
       NET: "the last of the Rephaites"
       WEB: "the remnant of the Rephaim"
       Analysis: KJV translates the term generically as "giants"; ESV/ASV/WEB
       transliterate the proper name. NET uses "Rephaites" (anglicized
       plural). The Rephaim were an ancient race of large stature
       (cf. Deuteronomy 2:11).

v.14 — Hebrew "ishei YHWH" — fire offerings of the LORD.
       KJV: "the sacrifices of the LORD God of Israel made by fire are their inheritance"
       ESV: "The offerings by fire to the LORD God of Israel are their inheritance"
       ASV: "the offerings of Jehovah...made by fire are his inheritance"
       NET: "their inheritance is the sacrificial offerings made to the Lord God of Israel"
       WEB: "the offerings of Yahweh, the God of Israel, made by fire are his inheritance"
       Analysis: "Isheh" = fire offering. Levi's sustenance came from the
       portions of sacrifices allotted to priests and Levites (Numbers
       18:8-20). NET reverses the word order, making the offerings primary.
       KJV/ASV/WEB preserve the Hebrew emphasis: Jehovah's fire-offerings
       are what constitute the Levites' inheritance. WEB/ASV foreground
       the divine name.

v.21 — Hebrew "n'sikhei Sichon" — "Nasikh" = one anointed, a prince.
       KJV: "dukes of Sihon"
       ESV: "princes of Sihon"
       ASV: "princes of Sihon"
       NET: "Sihon's princes"
       WEB: "princes of Sihon"
       Analysis: KJV's "dukes" is archaic; ESV/ASV/NET/WEB all use
       "princes," the more standard rendering. These were vassal rulers
       under Sihon's authority.

v.22 — Hebrew "ha-qosem" — diviner, one who practices divination.
       KJV: "the soothsayer"
       ESV: "the one who practiced divination"
       ASV: "Balaam also the son of Beor, the soothsayer"
       NET: "Balaam son of Beor, the omen reader"
       WEB: "Balaam also the son of Beor, the soothsayer"
       Analysis: "Qosem" = diviner. KJV/ASV/WEB use "soothsayer" — the
       traditional English term. NET uses "omen reader," which identifies
       Balaam's pagan practice more specifically. ESV expands descriptively.
       Note: Balaam is called a prophet elsewhere (2 Peter 2:16) but here
       is labeled by his forbidden practice (Deuteronomy 18:10).

v.27 — Hebrew "Kinnereth" — possibly from "kinnor" (harp).
       KJV: "the sea of Chinnereth"
       ESV: "the Sea of Chinnereth"
       ASV: "the sea of Chinnereth"
       NET: "the Sea of Kinnereth"
       WEB: "the sea of Chinnereth"
       Analysis: This is the Old Testament name for the Sea of Galilee
       (also called Gennesaret in the NT). "Kinnereth" may derive from
       "kinnor" (harp) — the lake's shape resembles a harp. NET uses a
       slightly different transliteration.

v.33 — Hebrew verbless clause (no explicit verb) — "YHWH Elohei Yisrael
       nachalatam."
       KJV: "the LORD God of Israel was their inheritance"
       ESV: "the LORD God of Israel is their inheritance"
       ASV: "Jehovah, the God of Israel, is their inheritance"
       NET: "their inheritance is the Lord God of Israel"
       WEB: "Yahweh, the God of Israel, is their inheritance"
       Analysis: KJV uses past tense "was"; ESV/ASV/WEB use present "is."
       The Hebrew has no explicit verb — both renderings are grammatically
       possible. ESV's present tense emphasizes the ongoing reality: God
       IS their inheritance. WEB/ASV foreground the divine name (Yahweh/
       Jehovah); NET buries it in the predicate. The personal God — YHWH —
       is what the Levites possess. The most significant theological
       statement of the chapter.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Stricken/Advanced in years — Hebrew "ba bayamim." An idiom for extreme
               old age. Joshua was likely 90-100 at this point.

Philistines    — A sea people who settled along the southern coastal plain
               of Canaan. Their five city-states (Gaza, Ashdod, Ashkelon,
               Gath, Ekron) remained unconquered and became Israel's
               persistent enemies through the period of the Judges and
               into the monarchy.

Seren          — The unique title for Philistine rulers (pl. "seranim").
               Not the standard Semitic term for king or prince. May
               reflect their Aegean/Indo-European origins.

Rephaim        — An ancient race of large stature inhabiting Canaan and
               Transjordan before Israel's arrival. Og king of Bashan
               was the last of them (Deuteronomy 3:11).

Transjordan    — The territory east of the Jordan River, allotted to
               Reuben, Gad, and half-Manasseh. Conquered under Moses
               from Sihon and Og.

Balaam         — Son of Beor, a diviner from Pethor in Mesopotamia. Hired
               by Balak king of Moab to curse Israel (Numbers 22-24).
               Unable to curse them, he later counseled Moab to seduce
               Israel into idolatry and immorality (Numbers 31:16).
               Killed by Israel's sword (v.22).

Inheritance (nachalah) — Hebrew term for a permanent, hereditary
               possession. In Israel, land was not merely property but
               a divine gift tied to covenant promises. It could not be
               permanently sold (Leviticus 25:23).


PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "There remaineth yet very much land to be possessed" (v.1). Even after
   great victories, there is more to take. The Christian life is never
   "arrived." There are always more promises to claim, more territory to
   possess, more growth to pursue. Complacency after past victories is
   a danger.

2. God commands distribution of unconquered land (v.6). Faith acts on
   God's promises before seeing full fulfillment. Joshua allocated land
   not yet taken — because God said it was theirs. We too must act on
   what God has promised, not merely on what we can see.

3. Partial obedience is disobedience (v.13). Israel did not drive out
   the Geshurites and Maacathites. These compromises seem small but
   compound over time. The enemies we tolerate today become the snares
   of tomorrow. What "Canaanites" are we allowing to remain?

4. "The LORD God of Israel was their inheritance" (v.33). Levi had no
   land — but they had God. This is the highest inheritance. Every
   believer is a "priest" (1 Peter 2:9) whose true portion is the Lord
   Himself. Do we value God's presence above earthly possessions?

5. Balaam's end (v.22). He who sought to profit from God's people died
   by their sword. Those who use spiritual gifts for personal gain will
   face judgment. The way of Balaam is a warning to every generation
   (2 Peter 2:15; Jude 11; Revelation 2:14).
