Joshua - Chapter 24
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Joshua gathers all Israel at Shechem, recounts God's faithfulness from Abraham to the conquest, and calls them to choose whom they will serve. The people covenant to serve the LORD, and Joshua dies at 110 years old.

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Shechem: Located between Mount Ebal and Mount Gerizim in the central highlands — Israel's covenant renewal site
- "Beyond the River" (v.2): Abraham's origin in Ur/Haran, beyond the Euphrates River in Mesopotamia
- Joshua buried at Timnath-serah in the hill country of Ephraim; Joseph's bones buried at Shechem

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------
Enduring Word (David Guzik):
  https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/joshua-24/
  Guzik emphasizes that Joshua's "choose you this day" is not an
  invitation to consider options but a demand for decisive commitment.
  The choice is not whether to serve — everyone serves something — but
  WHOM to serve. He also notes Joshua's surprising "Ye cannot serve the
  LORD" (v.19) as a test of sincerity: Joshua wants them to count the
  cost before pledging. Cheap commitment is worthless to a holy God.

Charles Spurgeon:
  "'Choose you this day whom ye will serve.' Not tomorrow — today. Not
  eventually — now. Joshua does not say, 'Think about it.' He says,
  'Choose.' And then he leads: 'As for me and my house, we will serve
  the LORD.' He does not wait for a committee vote. He does not take a
  poll. He declares his allegiance and invites others to follow. This is
  leadership. This is faith. This is the courage of conviction that
  changes nations."
REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. "Choose you this day whom ye will serve" (v.15). The most urgent
   question any person faces. There is no neutrality — everyone serves
   something. The only question is WHAT. Joshua presents the options
   clearly: dead gods of the past, local gods of the culture, or the
   living LORD. Choose. Today. Not tomorrow.

2. "As for me and my house, we will serve the LORD" (v.15). Joshua does
   not wait for consensus. He leads by declaring his own commitment
   first. Leaders go first. Fathers go first. The household follows the
   head. What have you declared for your house?

3. Grace precedes demand (vv.2-13). Before God asks for commitment, He
   recounts His gifts. "I took... I gave... I sent... I brought... I
   delivered." The call to serve is grounded in what God has already
   done. We do not serve to earn His favor — we serve because we have
   already received it.

4. "Ye cannot serve the LORD" (v.19). Joshua's shocking statement tests
   their sincerity. Serving God is not casual. He is holy, jealous, and
   will not tolerate rivals. Count the cost before you pledge (Luke
   14:28). Better to not vow than to vow and not pay (Ecclesiastes 5:5).

5. "Put away the strange gods which are among you" (v.23). Even after
   the conquest, some Israelites still had foreign gods! Commitment to
   the LORD requires active removal of rivals. What "gods" do we still
   harbor — comfort, approval, control, pleasure? Put them away. Today.

6. Joseph's bones buried at Shechem (v.32). A 400-year-old promise
   fulfilled. Joseph believed God would bring Israel to Canaan — and
   made them swear to bring his bones. Faith looks beyond death. Faith
   makes plans for a future it will not see. Joseph's bones testify:
   God keeps His word across centuries.

7. "Israel served the LORD all the days of Joshua" (v.31). One
   generation's faithfulness. But the next generation "knew not the LORD"
   (Judges 2:10). Faithfulness is not automatically inherited. Each
   generation must choose for itself. We cannot coast on our parents'
   faith.

8. Three burials close the book (vv.29-33). Joshua, Joseph, Eleazar —
   the leader, the patriarch, the priest. An era ends. The generation
   that conquered Canaan passes away. What remains? The land, the
   covenant, the choice — and the question: will the next generation
   serve the LORD?
