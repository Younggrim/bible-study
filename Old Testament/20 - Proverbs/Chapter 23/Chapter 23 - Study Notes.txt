Proverbs - Chapter 23
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The 'Sayings of the Wise' continued — warnings against gluttony, stinginess, removing ancient landmarks, and drunkenness; 'buy truth and sell it not'; and the famous extended warning against wine at the chapter's end.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Primarily Solomon (1:1, 10:1, 25:1), with sections by Agur
(ch.30) and King Lemuel (ch.31). Compiled over several centuries.
Proverbs is wisdom literature — practical instruction for godly
living. Hebrew title: 'Mishlei' — 'Proverbs/Comparisons.' Key
themes: the fear of the LORD as the beginning of wisdom (1:7, 9:10),
the two paths (wisdom vs. folly), the personification of Wisdom
(chs.1-9), and practical guidance for every area of life — speech,
money, work, relationships, leadership, and character.

Historical Context: Chapter 23 continues the "Words of the Wise"
section (22:17-24:34). The chapter addresses self-control in eating
(vv.1-3), the deceptive nature of riches (vv.4-5), the stingy host
(vv.6-8), parenting (vv.13-16, 22-25), envy of sinners (v.17), and
concludes with the most vivid and extended warning against
drunkenness in the Bible (vv.29-35). Verse 7 ("as he thinketh in
his heart, so is he") is often quoted about positive thinking, but
in context it describes the hypocrite who says "eat and drink" while
his heart begrudges every morsel.

Dominant Themes:
- Self-Control in Appetite (vv.1-3, 20-21)
- The Fleeting Nature of Wealth (vv.4-5)
- Parenting: Discipline and Joy (vv.13-16, 22-25)
- Don't Envy Sinners (v.17)
- The Devastating Effects of Drunkenness (vv.29-35)


KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.4-5 — "Do not toil to acquire wealth; be discerning enough to
         desist. When your eyes light on it, it is gone, for
         suddenly it sprouts wings, flying like an eagle toward
         heaven." (ESV) — Stop exhausting yourself chasing wealth.
         It is unstable — it grows wings and flies away. You are
         pursuing "that which is not" — an illusion of permanence
         that doesn't exist.

v.7  — "For he is like one who is inwardly calculating." (ESV) —
        In context: the stingy host calculates every bite you eat
        even while saying "help yourself." A person's true nature
        is their inner thoughts, not their outward words. Character
        is what you think when no one can hear.

v.17-18 — "Let not your heart envy sinners, but continue in the
           fear of the LORD all the day. Surely there is a future,
           and your hope will not be cut off." (ESV) — Don't envy
           sinners' apparent prosperity. There IS a future — and
           your hope will not be disappointed. Their end is
           judgment; yours is reward.

v.23 — "Buy truth, and do not sell it; buy wisdom, instruction,
        and understanding." (ESV) — Acquire truth at any cost.
        Never sell it at any price. Truth, wisdom, instruction,
        understanding — these are infinitely valuable. Whatever
        price they demand in effort, sacrifice, or discomfort —
        pay it. And never trade them away.

v.29-35 — The drunkenness passage. "Who has woe? Who has sorrow?
           ...Those who tarry long over wine... In the end it bites
           like a serpent." (ESV) — The most extended warning against
           drunkenness in Scripture. Seven effects described: woe,
           sorrow, strife, complaining, wounds, redness of eyes.
           Wine starts beautiful (red, sparkling) and ends deadly
           (serpent bite, adder sting). The final verse is haunting:
           "I must have another drink" — addiction's cycle.

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------
Enduring Word (David Guzik):
  https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/proverbs-23/
  Guzik notes that vv.29-35 is the most vivid warning against
  alcohol abuse in all of Scripture. The passage moves from question
  (who has woe?) to answer (the drunkard) to description (serpent
  bite) to psychology (addiction: "I will seek it yet again"). On
  v.5, he emphasizes that wealth is personified as having wings —
  it is inherently unstable and mobile. It flies away on its own;
  you cannot chain it down.

Charles Spurgeon:
  "Buy the truth, and sell it not. Buy it — whatever the cost.
  Truth may cost you friends, comfort, reputation, money. Buy it
  anyway. And sell it not — whatever the offered price. Men will
  offer you ease, popularity, advancement if you will compromise
  truth. Refuse. The man who owns truth is richer than the man who
  owns empires. And the man who sells truth for gain has made the
  worst trade in history."
TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.7  — KJV: "as he thinketh in his heart, so is he" / ESV: "he is
       like one who is inwardly calculating" — Hebrew "sha'ar
       benafsho" = one who calculates/reckons in his soul. KJV
       creates a general maxim; ESV keeps the specific context
       (the stingy host calculating costs). The Hebrew supports
       both readings.

v.31 — KJV: "when it moveth itself aright" / ESV: "when it
       sparkles in the cup" — Hebrew "yith'halekh bemesharim" =
       goes down smoothly/straightly. The wine's attractiveness:
       its color, its sparkle, its smooth taste. All deceptive —
       beneath the beauty is a serpent.

v.34 — KJV: "lieth upon the top of a mast" / ESV: "lies on the
       top of a mast" — The drunk person is so disoriented he
       doesn't realize the danger of his position — like sleeping
       at the top of a ship's mast in a storm.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Mixed Wine     — Hebrew "mimsak" = spiced, mixed, blended wine.
              Wine mixed with spices to increase potency and
              flavor. The "craft cocktails" of the ancient world —
              engineered for maximum intoxication.

Redeemer (Go'el) — Hebrew "go'el" = kinsman-redeemer, avenger of
              blood, one who recovers family property. In v.11, God
              Himself is the "go'el" of the fatherless — He will
              personally avenge those who exploit orphans.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Riches have wings (v.5). Stop treating money as permanent. It
   will fly away regardless of how tightly you grip it. Invest in
   what cannot fly: character, relationships, eternal rewards.

2. Buy truth at any price; sell it at no price (v.23). What is
   truth costing you right now? Whatever it costs — friends,
   comfort, advancement — pay it. And when pressure comes to
   compromise what you know is true — refuse at any price.

3. Don't envy sinners (v.17). Their prosperity is temporary; your
   hope is eternal. "Surely there is an end" — the future vindicates
   faithfulness. When the wicked seem to flourish, remember: the
   story isn't over.

4. Alcohol is a serpent in a cup (vv.31-32). It looks beautiful
   going down. It bites like a viper at the end. If you struggle
   with alcohol, hear this warning and take it seriously. The
   addiction cycle of v.35 ("I will seek it yet again") is real
   and deadly.

5. "Give me thine heart" (v.26). God's request — and every father's.
   Not mere outward compliance but the HEART. Are you giving God
   your heart, or just your behavior? He wants the inside, not
   merely the outside.
