Proverbs - Chapter 19
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Proverbs on poverty, discipline, and the fear of the LORD — better a poor man of integrity than a fool, the fear of the LORD leads to life, whoever is generous to the poor lends to the LORD, and discipline your son while there is hope.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Primarily Solomon (1:1, 10:1, 25:1), with sections by Agur
(ch.30) and King Lemuel (ch.31). Compiled over several centuries.
Proverbs is wisdom literature — practical instruction for godly
living. Hebrew title: 'Mishlei' — 'Proverbs/Comparisons.' Key
themes: the fear of the LORD as the beginning of wisdom (1:7, 9:10),
the two paths (wisdom vs. folly), the personification of Wisdom
(chs.1-9), and practical guidance for every area of life — speech,
money, work, relationships, leadership, and character.

Historical Context: Chapter 19 addresses poverty and wealth
(vv.1, 4, 6-7, 17, 22), God's sovereignty over human plans (v.21),
parenting and discipline (vv.13, 18, 26), and the fear of the LORD
as life (v.23). The chapter is notable for its compassion toward
the poor (v.17: lending to the poor = lending to God) and its
unflinching honesty about how the world treats the poor (vv.4, 7).
Verse 21 is one of the great sovereignty statements in Proverbs.

Dominant Themes:
- Poverty, Wealth, and Friendship (vv.1, 4, 6-7, 17, 22)
- God's Sovereignty (v.21)
- Discipline and Parenting (vv.13, 18)
- The Fear of the LORD (v.23)
- Patience and Passing Over Transgression (v.11)


KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.11 — "Good sense makes one slow to anger, and it is his glory
        to overlook an offense." (ESV) — A wise person delays
        anger deliberately. But even greater than slow anger is the
        ability to OVERLOOK an offense entirely. That is glory —
        resembling God Himself who passes over transgression.

v.17 — "Whoever is generous to the poor lends to the LORD, and he
        will repay him for his deed." (ESV) — Astonishing: giving
        to the poor is LENDING TO GOD. The LORD considers Himself
        in debt to the generous. He will repay. Your charity is not
        lost — it is invested with the highest-credit borrower in
        the universe.

v.21 — "Many are the plans in the mind of a man, but it is the
        purpose of the LORD that will stand." (ESV) — Man plans;
        God prevails. You may have a hundred schemes, but only
        God's purpose will ultimately stand. Align your plans with
        His counsel, or watch them dissolve.

v.23 — "The fear of the LORD leads to life, and whoever has it
        rests satisfied; he will not be visited by harm." (ESV) —
        The fear of the LORD leads to life, satisfaction, and
        protection from evil. Three comprehensive blessings from
        one disposition.

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------
Enduring Word (David Guzik):
  https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/proverbs-19/
  Guzik emphasizes v.17's remarkable claim: God considers Himself
  a debtor to those who show mercy to the poor. He notes this is
  extraordinary theology — the Creator owing the creature. On v.21,
  Guzik states: "Man proposes, God disposes. Plan freely but hold
  loosely, because only God's counsel stands."

Charles Spurgeon:
  "He that hath pity upon the poor lendeth unto the LORD. What
  security! What a debtor! You lend to the LORD of heaven and
  earth. Can He fail to repay? Can He become bankrupt? Impossible.
  Every penny given to the poor in Christ's name is entered into
  heaven's ledger, and the LORD Himself guarantees repayment with
  interest. Give freely — your investment is divinely secured."
TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.11 — KJV: "his glory to pass over a transgression" / ESV: "his
       glory to overlook an offense" — Hebrew "abar 'al pesha'" =
       to pass over/beyond a transgression. Same root as "Passover"
       (God passing over judgment). To overlook offense is godlike.

v.13 — KJV: "a continual dropping" / ESV: "a continual dripping" —
       Hebrew "deleph tored" = a constant drip. The image of water
       torture — relentless, maddening nagging. Not a downpour but
       an unending, small irritation.

v.17 — KJV: "lendeth unto the LORD" / ESV: "lending to the LORD" —
       Hebrew "malveh Yahweh" = one who lends to the LORD. The
       phrasing is deliberate: God positions Himself as BORROWER.
       Astonishing condescension — the Creator indebts Himself to
       the compassionate.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Pass Over      — To overlook, to choose not to address or avenge
              an offense. Not denial but deliberate grace. The
              ability to absorb wrong without demanding payment.
              This is described as "glory" — it reflects God's
              character of mercy.

Prudent Wife   — Hebrew "ishshah maskileth" = a woman of insight/
              understanding. Distinguished from beauty or wealth —
              she is valued for her wisdom and discernment. Such a
              wife is "from the LORD" — a divine gift beyond human
              arrangement.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Give to the poor as lending to God (v.17). Reframe every act of
   charity: you are not losing money — you are lending to the LORD.
   He guarantees repayment. Give with the confidence of a wise
   investor, not the reluctance of one throwing money away.

2. It is your GLORY to overlook offense (v.11). Not weakness —
   glory. The person who can absorb an insult without retaliation
   resembles God more than the person who demands justice for every
   slight. Choose glory. Let it go.

3. God's plan prevails over yours (v.21). You may have a hundred
   plans in your heart. Only God's counsel stands. Stop fighting
   providence. Align your plans with His revealed will, and hold
   the rest with open hands.

4. Discipline while there is hope (v.18). There is a window for
   correction — it closes. Delayed discipline becomes impossible
   discipline. Act now while character is still forming. Don't
   wait until patterns are cemented.

5. The fear of the LORD is life and satisfaction (v.23). Want life?
   Fear God. Want satisfaction? Fear God. Want protection? Fear God.
   Everything you're chasing through other means is available
   through one disposition: reverent submission to the LORD.
