Ezekiel - Chapter 4
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God commands Ezekiel to perform extreme sign-acts: draw Jerusalem on a brick and lay siege to it, lie on his left side for 390 days (for Israel's iniquity) and his right side for 40 days (for Judah's), then cook bread over dung as siege-food. The prophet's body becomes a living demonstration of Jerusalem's coming siege and exile.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Ezekiel son of Buzi (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Sign-Acts — The Siege Model
Key Themes: Embodied prophecy, the coming siege of Jerusalem
enacted in miniature, measured punishment (day = year), the
horror of siege conditions, God's concession to priestly
scruples (cow dung instead of human dung)

Historical Context: God commands Ezekiel to become a living
theater of Jerusalem's siege (which is still 5+ years away,
586 BC). The sign-acts are physical, prolonged, and public:
(1) A clay brick with Jerusalem drawn on it, surrounded by siege
equipment; (2) lying on his side for 390+40 days bound with
ropes; (3) eating rationed bread cooked over dung. Each element
communicates: siege is coming, it will be LONG, and the conditions
will be HORRIFIC. The 390 days = years of Israel's iniquity; 40
days = years of Judah's iniquity. Ezekiel's priestly objection to
cooking over HUMAN dung (v.14) — ceremonially defiling — is the
ONLY successful negotiation a prophet has with God in Ezekiel.
God concedes: cow dung instead.

Structure:
- The Siege Brick: Jerusalem Besieged (vv.1-3)
- Lie on Your Left Side: 390 Days for Israel (vv.4-5)
- Lie on Your Right Side: 40 Days for Judah (vv.6-8)
- Siege Bread: Rationed and Defiled (vv.9-17)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.3  — "This shall be a sign to the house of Israel" (KJV) —
        The enacted siege is a SIGN — not mere theater but
        prophetic ACTION that participates in what it represents.

v.5  — "I have laid upon thee the years of their iniquity,
        according to the number of the days, three hundred and
        ninety days: so shalt thou bear the iniquity of the house
        of Israel" (KJV) — Day = year. The prophet BEARS their
        iniquity physically. A foreshadowing of Christ bearing sin.

v.14 — "Then said I, Ah Lord GOD! behold, my soul hath not been
        polluted: for from my youth up even till now have I not
        eaten of that which dieth of itself" (KJV) — The priest
        objects on purity grounds: he has NEVER eaten unclean food.
        God concedes. Even in extreme sign-acts, God respects
        conscience.

v.16 — "Son of man, behold, I will break the staff of bread in
        Jerusalem: and they shall eat bread by weight, and with
        care; and they shall drink water by measure" (KJV) —
        Rationed food and water: the reality of siege. 20 shekels
        of bread per day (~8 ounces) and 1/6 hin of water (~1 quart).
        Slow starvation.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1 — "tile" (KJV) / "brick" (ESV) — Hebrew "levenah" = a clay
     brick (sun-dried or baked). The standard writing/drawing
     surface in Mesopotamia. Ezekiel draws a MAP of Jerusalem
     on it.

v.9 — "wheat, and barley, and beans, and lentiles, and millet,
     and fitches" (KJV/ESV) — Six different grains/legumes mixed
     together. This mixed-grain bread indicates SCARCITY: you
     combine whatever's available rather than using proper wheat.

v.12 — "dung that cometh out of man" (KJV) / "human dung" (ESV)
     — Hebrew "geleley ha-adam." Human excrement as fuel.
     Ceremonially defiling. God concedes to cow dung (v.15)
     after Ezekiel's objection.

v.16 — "staff of bread" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "matteh-lechem" = the
     staff/support of bread. Bread as the STAFF you lean on for
     survival. God BREAKS the staff — removes the support.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Sign-Acts     — Prophetic performances that EMBODY the message.
              The prophet's body becomes the sermon. Prolonged,
              public, physically costly. Common in Ezekiel.

Day-Year Principle — Each day of the sign-act represents one
              year of iniquity. 390 days = 390 years of northern
              Israel's sin; 40 days = 40 years of Judah's sin.

Siege Bread   — Mixed-grain, rationed, cooked over dung: the
              desperate food of a city under prolonged siege.
              Minimal calories, maximum humiliation.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Ezekiel's BODY became the message (vv.4-8). Sometimes God's
   word must be LIVED, not just spoken. Your life IS your sermon.
   What truth is God asking you to embody physically — not just
   believe mentally?

2. God conceded to the priest's conscience (vv.14-15). Even in
   extreme calling, God respects genuine conviction. If something
   violates your conscience before God, appeal — He may adjust.

3. 390 days on one side (v.5). THAT is commitment to a message.
   Over a year of discomfort to communicate ONE truth. Are you
   willing to endure prolonged difficulty for the sake of a
   message God has given you?

4. Mixed-grain siege bread (v.9). In desperate times, you mix
   whatever's available. The refined becomes crude, the pure
   becomes mixed. Siege produces survival-mode — but God
   preserves even through it.
