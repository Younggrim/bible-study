Ezekiel - Chapter 5
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God commands Ezekiel to shave his head and beard with a sword, then divide the hair into thirds: one third burned in the city-model, one third struck with the sword around the city, one third scattered to the wind. A tiny remnant is bound in the prophet's garment — but even some of THOSE are thrown into fire. Jerusalem will be made a horror: "I will do in thee that which I have not done."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Ezekiel son of Buzi (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Sign-Act + Judgment Oracle
Key Themes: The divided hair (thirds of Jerusalem's population),
famine/sword/scattering, a tiny remnant barely preserved,
Jerusalem worse than surrounding nations, unprecedented judgment,
"then shall they know that I am the LORD"

Historical Context: A SWORD used as a razor — shaving the prophet's
head and beard. In priestly law, shaving was associated with
mourning and shame; for a PRIEST (Ezekiel was one), it was
normally FORBIDDEN (Leviticus 21:5). God overrides priestly purity
FOR the message. The three portions represent Jerusalem's
population: 1/3 die of famine/plague (burned), 1/3 killed by
the sword (struck), 1/3 scattered among nations (wind). A few
hairs bound in the garment = tiny remnant. But even SOME of those
are cast into fire (the remnant is further refined). The oracle
that follows (vv.5-17) explains: Jerusalem is WORSE than pagan
nations — she had God's laws and REJECTED them. Nations without
law are more righteous than Israel WITH law.

Structure:
- Shave with a Sword, Divide the Hair (vv.1-4)
- Jerusalem's Sin: Worse Than the Nations (vv.5-8)
- Unprecedented Judgment (vv.9-10)
- Three Judgments: Plague, Sword, Scatter (vv.11-12)
- God's Fury Completed (vv.13-17)

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Greater privilege = greater accountability (vv.5-6). You have
   the FULL Bible, church history, Christian community — more
   spiritual privilege than ancient Israel had. Are you using it
   or ignoring it? The nations are more excusable than you.

2. WORSE than pagans (v.6). A professing Christian who lives
   worse than non-Christians is in a UNIQUELY dangerous position.
   Having light and choosing darkness is graver than never having
   light at all.

3. God's fury has an endpoint (v.13). Wrath is not purposeless
   rage — it reaches its goal and STOPS. If you're under
   discipline, it won't last forever. God's anger is measured
   and completed.

4. A few hairs bound in the garment (v.3). Even in the most
   severe judgment, God preserves a tiny remnant. YOU might be
   one of those tucked into God's garment — spared while
   everything around you burns.

5. They defiled God's sanctuary (v.11). What TRIGGERED this
   extreme judgment: bringing abominations into the holy place.
   Is there anything unholy that you've brought into your
   relationship with God — your worship, your prayer life, your
   inner sanctuary?
