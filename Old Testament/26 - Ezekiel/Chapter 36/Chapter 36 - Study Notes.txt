Ezekiel - Chapter 36
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God promises to restore Israel's LAND and PEOPLE — not for their sake but for His holy name's sake. "I will sprinkle clean water upon you... A new heart will I give you, and a new spirit will I put within you: and I will take away the stony heart... and I will put my spirit within you, and cause you to walk in my statutes." The most comprehensive new-covenant promise in Ezekiel.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Ezekiel son of Buzi (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Salvation Oracle — Land Restored + New Heart + Spirit Given
Key Themes: God's name profaned among nations, restoration for God's reputation (not Israel's merit), clean water sprinkled, new heart, new spirit, God's Spirit placed within, obedience enabled supernaturally, the land restored to Eden-like fertility

Historical Context: After the shepherd chapter (34) and Edom's judgment (35), God addresses the MOUNTAINS of Israel (contrasting ch.6 where they were cursed). The land itself will be restored: fertility, population, rebuilding. But the deeper restoration is INTERNAL (vv.25-27): water purification, heart transplant, Spirit implanted. This parallels Jeremiah 31:31-34 (new covenant) but adds the MECHANISM: God's Spirit CAUSES obedience. The chapter emphasizes GOD'S MOTIVE: "not for your sakes... but for mine holy name's sake" (v.22). Israel's restoration glorifies GOD, not Israel.

Structure:
- Prophecy to the Mountains: You Will Produce Again (vv.1-15)
- Israel Profaned God's Name Among Nations (vv.16-21)
- "Not for Your Sakes But for My Name's Sake" (v.22)
- Clean Water, New Heart, New Spirit (vv.25-27)
- Restoration: Land, Crops, Cities, Population (vv.28-38)

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Jerusalem (v.38): Capital of Judah; the holy city where the Temple stood.

Charles Spurgeon:
  "'I will put my spirit within you, and CAUSE you to walk in my statutes.' The new covenant doesn't just COMMAND obedience — it CAUSES it. God doesn't say 'try harder.' He says 'I'll put my Spirit in you and He'll produce what the law demanded.' THIS is grace."

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. "Not for your sakes" (v.22). Your salvation isn't because you're worth it — it's because GOD'S NAME is at stake. This eliminates boasting AND eliminates despair: you don't need to be "good enough" because the motive isn't about you.

2. The heart transplant (v.26). Stop trying to IMPROVE your stone heart. You need a REPLACEMENT. Ask God for the surgery — not behavior tips but nature change.

3. "Cause you to walk" (v.27). The Spirit doesn't just INVITE obedience — He CAUSES it. If you're fighting sin entirely in your own strength, you're operating under the old covenant. Invite the Spirit to produce what you can't generate.

4. God's reputation is tied to YOUR life (vv.20-23). When God's people live poorly, the WORLD blasphemes God's name. Your behavior affects God's reputation among the watching nations. Live accordingly.

5. Wasteland → Eden (v.35). God's restoration goes ALL THE WAY BACK to Genesis perfection. He doesn't settle for "slightly better than exile." He aims for PARADISE. His plans for you aren't modest.
