Ezekiel - Chapter 1
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The heavens open and Ezekiel receives the most elaborate theophany in Scripture. A storm from the north reveals four living creatures (each with four faces and four wings), whirling wheels within wheels covered with eyes, an expanse of crystal above them, and on the expanse a throne of sapphire with a figure like a man surrounded by fire and rainbow. "This was the appearance of the likeness of the glory of the LORD." Ezekiel falls on his face.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Ezekiel son of Buzi, a priest-turned-prophet among the
Babylonian exiles.

Date: Precisely dated — "the thirtieth year, the fourth month,
the fifth day" / "the fifth year of king Jehoiachin's captivity"
= July 593 BC. Ezekiel was approximately 30 years old (the age
for priestly service to begin, Numbers 4:3).

Classification: Prophetic Vision — The Throne-Chariot of God
(Merkabah)
Key Themes: God's glory appearing IN EXILE (not just in the
temple), the four living creatures, the wheels (omnidirectional
divine mobility), God's throne above all creation, the priest
becomes prophet

Historical Context: Ezekiel was among the 10,000 exiled with
Jehoiachin in 597 BC (2 Kings 24:14). He lived at Tel-Abib by
the Chebar canal (a Babylonian irrigation canal near Nippur).
At age 30 — when he SHOULD have begun priestly service in
Jerusalem's temple — God instead opens heaven and gives him a
VISION of the temple's God. The message: God's glory is NOT
confined to Jerusalem's temple. He appears on a MOBILE throne
in BABYLON. The glory that will LEAVE the temple (chs.8-11) was
never imprisoned there.

Structure:
- Date and Setting: By the Chebar Canal (vv.1-3)
- The Storm from the North (v.4)
- The Four Living Creatures (vv.5-14)
- The Wheels Within Wheels (vv.15-21)
- The Crystal Expanse Above (vv.22-25)
- The Throne: The Likeness of God's Glory (vv.26-28)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "the heavens were opened, and I saw visions of God" (KJV)
        — Heavens OPENED — in Babylon, not Jerusalem. God comes to
        where His people ARE, not where the temple WAS.

v.4  — "And I looked, and, behold, a whirlwind came out of the
        north, a great cloud, and a fire infolding itself" (KJV)
        — The theophany arrives as STORM: wind, cloud, fire.
        God's approach is overwhelming, not gentle.

v.16 — "their appearance and their work was as it were a wheel in
        the middle of a wheel" (KJV) — Wheels within wheels: the
        divine throne-chariot can move in ANY direction without
        turning. God is not limited to one direction or one
        destination. Omnidirectional sovereignty.

v.26 — "And above the firmament... the likeness of a throne, as
        the appearance of a sapphire stone: and upon the likeness
        of the throne was the likeness as the appearance of a man
        above upon it" (KJV) — At the HIGHEST point: a throne.
        On the throne: a HUMAN form (the glory of God in human-
        like appearance). Layered with "likeness" and "appearance"
        — language straining to describe the indescribable.

v.28 — "This was the appearance of the likeness of the glory of
        the LORD. And when I saw it, I fell upon my face" (KJV)
        — Not "the glory" directly but "the appearance of the
        likeness of the glory." Even this REDUCED representation
        causes Ezekiel to collapse. The only appropriate response
        to God's glory: facedown.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1  — "thirtieth year" (KJV/ESV) — Likely Ezekiel's AGE (30
      = priestly ordination age). Instead of beginning temple
      service, he begins prophetic ministry. The priesthood he
      could never exercise is replaced by prophetic vision.

v.4  — "amber" (KJV) / "gleaming metal" (ESV) — Hebrew
      "chashmal" = a glowing metal alloy (electrum? bronze-gold
      mixture?). Something bright, molten, shimmering. Modern
      Hebrew uses "chashmal" for electricity.

v.5  — "four living creatures" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "chayyot" =
      living beings. Later identified as CHERUBIM (10:15,20).
      Not angels (malakhim) but throne-bearers — creatures whose
      existence is defined by proximity to God.

v.10 — "face of a man... face of a lion... face of an ox... face
      of an eagle" (KJV/ESV) — Four faces representing: humanity
      (intelligence), lion (strength/royalty), ox (service/labor),
      eagle (swiftness/transcendence). Traditionally connected to
      the four Gospels.

v.16 — "wheel in the middle of a wheel" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew
      "ophan betokh ha-ophan." A gyroscopic arrangement allowing
      movement in any direction without turning. God's throne can
      go ANYWHERE instantly.

v.26 — "sapphire" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "sappir" = lapis lazuli or
      blue sapphire. The throne is BLUE — the color of heaven/
      sky. The throne IS heaven materialized.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Merkabah       — "Chariot" — the Jewish term for Ezekiel's throne-
              vision. The divine chariot-throne: mobile, living,
              multidirectional. God is not stationary.

Four Living Creatures — (Cherubim) Beings with four faces, four
              wings, and calf-like feet. Throne-bearers and glory-
              attendants. Their movement is the throne's movement.

Wheels Within Wheels — The throne's locomotion system:
              omnidirectional, spirit-driven, covered with EYES
              (divine omniscience). God's movement is never random.

Chebar Canal — An irrigation waterway near Nippur in Babylon
              (modern Iraq). The location of the Jewish exile
              community where Ezekiel lived. God appears at an
              irrigation ditch, not a holy site.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. God appeared in BABYLON (v.1). Not in Jerusalem's temple, not
   on holy ground — by a canal in exile. If you think God can't
   meet you where you are right now (in your "exile"), think
   again. His throne is MOBILE.

2. "I fell upon my face" (v.28). The only appropriate response to
   God's glory is facedown. Not theological analysis, not casual
   observation — PROSTRATION. When was the last time God's
   presence (in Scripture, worship, or life) put you on the floor?

3. Wheels within wheels = God moves in any direction instantly
   (v.16). God is not stuck in one pattern, one approach, one
   direction. He can pivot instantly. Stop assuming God must
   work the same way He did last time.

4. The appearance of the LIKENESS of the glory (v.28). Even this
   was overwhelming. Ezekiel didn't see God directly — he saw a
   representation of a representation of God's glory — and it
   was too much. How much MORE is the reality? Let that create
   appropriate awe.

5. A priest without a temple receives a vision of God (vv.1-3).
   When God takes away your expected ministry (Ezekiel couldn't
   serve as priest), He may give you something GREATER. The loss
   of one calling opened a bigger one.
