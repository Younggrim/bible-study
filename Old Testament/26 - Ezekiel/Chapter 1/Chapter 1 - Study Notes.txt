Ezekiel - Chapter 1
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The heavens open and Ezekiel receives the most elaborate theophany in Scripture. A storm from the north reveals four living creatures (each with four faces and four wings), whirling wheels within wheels covered with eyes, an expanse of crystal above them, and on the expanse a throne of sapphire with a figure like a man surrounded by fire and rainbow. "This was the appearance of the likeness of the glory of the LORD." Ezekiel falls on his face.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Ezekiel son of Buzi, a priest-turned-prophet among the
Babylonian exiles.

Date: Precisely dated — "the thirtieth year, the fourth month,
the fifth day" / "the fifth year of king Jehoiachin's captivity"
= July 593 BC. Ezekiel was approximately 30 years old (the age
for priestly service to begin, Numbers 4:3).

Classification: Prophetic Vision — The Throne-Chariot of God
(Merkabah)
Key Themes: God's glory appearing IN EXILE (not just in the
temple), the four living creatures, the wheels (omnidirectional
divine mobility), God's throne above all creation, the priest
becomes prophet

Historical Context: Ezekiel was among the 10,000 exiled with
Jehoiachin in 597 BC (2 Kings 24:14). He lived at Tel-Abib by
the Chebar canal (a Babylonian irrigation canal near Nippur).
At age 30 — when he SHOULD have begun priestly service in
Jerusalem's temple — God instead opens heaven and gives him a
VISION of the temple's God. The message: God's glory is NOT
confined to Jerusalem's temple. He appears on a MOBILE throne
in BABYLON. The glory that will LEAVE the temple (chs.8-11) was
never imprisoned there.

Structure:
- Date and Setting: By the Chebar Canal (vv.1-3)
- The Storm from the North (v.4)
- The Four Living Creatures (vv.5-14)
- The Wheels Within Wheels (vv.15-21)
- The Crystal Expanse Above (vv.22-25)
- The Throne: The Likeness of God's Glory (vv.26-28)

Charles Spurgeon:
  "Ezekiel saw God's throne IN BABYLON. Not in the temple, not
  in Jerusalem, not in the holy land — in EXILE. If God's glory
  can appear by a Babylonian canal, it can appear in your
  dungeon, your hospital room, your cubicle. The throne is MOBILE."

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. God appeared in BABYLON (v.1). Not in Jerusalem's temple, not
   on holy ground — by a canal in exile. If you think God can't
   meet you where you are right now (in your "exile"), think
   again. His throne is MOBILE.

2. "I fell upon my face" (v.28). The only appropriate response to
   God's glory is facedown. Not theological analysis, not casual
   observation — PROSTRATION. When was the last time God's
   presence (in Scripture, worship, or life) put you on the floor?

3. Wheels within wheels = God moves in any direction instantly
   (v.16). God is not stuck in one pattern, one approach, one
   direction. He can pivot instantly. Stop assuming God must
   work the same way He did last time.

4. The appearance of the LIKENESS of the glory (v.28). Even this
   was overwhelming. Ezekiel didn't see God directly — he saw a
   representation of a representation of God's glory — and it
   was too much. How much MORE is the reality? Let that create
   appropriate awe.

5. A priest without a temple receives a vision of God (vv.1-3).
   When God takes away your expected ministry (Ezekiel couldn't
   serve as priest), He may give you something GREATER. The loss
   of one calling opened a bigger one.
