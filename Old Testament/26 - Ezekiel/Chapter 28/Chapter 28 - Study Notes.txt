Ezekiel - Chapter 28
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The oracle against the king of Tyre — one of the most theologically significant passages in Ezekiel. The chapter begins by addressing the human "prince of Tyre" who says "I am a God" in his pride (vv.1-10), then shifts to a lament over the "king of Tyre" with language that transcends any human ruler: "Thou hast been in Eden the garden of God," "thou art the anointed cherub that covereth," "thou wast perfect in thy ways from the day that thou wast created, till iniquity was found in thee" (vv.11-19). Many theologians see here a description of Satan's original glory and fall. The chapter concludes with a brief oracle against Sidon and a promise of Israel's restoration.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Ezekiel son of Buzi (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Oracle / Lament / Satan Typology
Key Themes: The pride of the prince of Tyre, "I am a God,"
the king of Tyre in Eden, the anointed cherub, perfection
corrupted by pride, beauty becoming the cause of the fall,
cast from the mountain of God, Satan typology, judgment on
Sidon, Israel's restoration promise

Historical Context: The chapter addresses two figures: the
"prince" (nagid — v.2) and the "king" (melek — v.12) of Tyre.
The first is clearly the human ruler — likely Ethbaal III (also
called Ithobaal), who reigned during the Babylonian siege. He
is condemned for claiming deity: "I am a God, I sit in the seat
of God" (v.2). Ancient Near Eastern kings often claimed divine
status; Ethbaal took this to an extreme.

The second oracle (vv.12-19) shifts dramatically. The language
goes beyond any human king: "Thou hast been in Eden," "every
precious stone was thy covering," "thou art the anointed cherub
that covereth," "thou wast upon the holy mountain of God,"
"perfect... till iniquity was found in thee." Many interpreters
(church fathers, reformers, and modern scholars) see this as
using the king of Tyre as a TYPE of Satan — the original
guardian cherub whose beauty and perfection became the source
of his pride, leading to his expulsion from God's mountain.

Whether read as a literal description of Satan's fall or as
hyperbolic language about Tyre's king, the theological point is
identical: beauty and privilege, when they produce pride instead
of worship, lead to catastrophic fall.

Structure:
- The Prince of Tyre: "I Am a God" (vv.1-10)
- The King of Tyre: The Anointed Cherub Falls (vv.11-19)
- Oracle Against Sidon (vv.20-23)
- Promise of Israel's Restoration (vv.24-26)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.2  — "Son of man, say unto the prince of Tyrus, Thus saith the
        Lord GOD; Because thine heart is lifted up, and thou hast
        said, I am a God, I sit in the seat of God, in the midst
        of the seas; yet thou art a man, and not God, though thou
        set thine heart as the heart of God" (KJV) — The core
        sin: "I am a God." A man claiming divine status. God's
        response is almost sarcastic: "thou art a MAN." The gap
        between the claim and reality is infinite.

v.12 — "Thou sealest up the sum, full of wisdom, and perfect in
        beauty" (KJV) — "The sum" = the finished model, the
        pattern of perfection. This being was the HIGHEST
        expression of created beauty and wisdom. No creature
        surpassed him.

v.13 — "Thou hast been in Eden the garden of God; every precious
        stone was thy covering" (KJV) — Eden. Garden of God. No
        human king of Tyre was in Eden. The nine precious stones
        listed (sardius, topaz, diamond, beryl, onyx, jasper,
        sapphire, emerald, carbuncle) parallel the high priest's
        breastplate — suggesting a priestly/guardian role.

v.14 — "Thou art the anointed cherub that covereth; and I have
        set thee so: thou wast upon the holy mountain of God;
        thou hast walked up and down in the midst of the stones
        of fire" (KJV) — "Anointed cherub that covereth" — a
        guardian angel positioned at the very throne of God.
        "Stones of fire" = the fiery glory surrounding God's
        presence. This being had DIRECT ACCESS to God's throne.

v.15 — "Thou wast perfect in thy ways from the day that thou wast
        created, till iniquity was found in thee" (KJV) — Perfect
        from creation — UNTIL. The pivotal word. Created perfect
        but not eternally fixed in perfection. Iniquity was
        "found" — it originated from WITHIN, not from external
        temptation.

v.17 — "Thine heart was lifted up because of thy beauty, thou
        hast corrupted thy wisdom by reason of thy brightness"
        (KJV) — The mechanism of the fall: beauty became the
        CAUSE of pride. Wisdom was corrupted by its own
        brilliance. The gifts themselves became the trap. Self-
        admiration replaced God-worship.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.12 — "sealest up the sum" (KJV) — Hebrew "chotem toknit" =
      sealing the pattern/measurement. The idea: this being is
      the COMPLETE pattern of perfection — the sum total of what
      a creature can be. ESV: "You were the signet of perfection."
      Like a stamp that produces the perfect impression.

v.13 — "tabrets and... pipes" (KJV) — Hebrew "tuppekha
      un'qavekha" = your tambourines and your settings/sockets
      (for gemstones). Some interpret this as musical instruments
      built INTO this being's creation; others as the gold
      settings for the precious stones listed. Either way:
      craftsmanship embedded in creation.

v.14 — "anointed cherub that covereth" (KJV) — Hebrew "k'ruv
      mimshach hassokek" = the cherub of anointing/expansion who
      covers/guards. "Covering" suggests the protective posture
      of the cherubim over the ark (Exodus 25:20). This being's
      role was to GUARD God's holy presence.

v.16 — "cast thee as profane" (KJV) — Hebrew "va'achall'lekha"
      = I profaned you / I cast you out as common. From holy to
      profane — from sacred guardian to expelled rebel. The
      ultimate demotion.

v.12 — ASV: uses "Jehovah" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).
       NET: "says: “‘You were" for KJV's "GOD; Thou sealest up"
       WEB: uses "Yahweh" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).
v.13 — ASV: "wast" for KJV's "hast been"
       NET: "you were created they were prepared." for KJV's "that thou wast created."
       WEB: adds "emerald, chrysolite, onyx, jasper, sapphire, turquoise,"
v.14 — ASV: "thee, [so that]" for KJV's "thee so:"
       NET: "guardian cherub; you were on" for KJV's "cherub that covereth; and I have set thee so: thou wast upon"
       WEB: "who covers. Then" for KJV's "that covereth; and"
v.16 — ASV: "have destroyed" for KJV's "will destroy"
       NET: "God— the guardian cherub expelled you" for KJV's "God: and I will destroy thee, O covering cherub,"
       WEB: "your commerce, your insides were" for KJV's "thy merchandise they have"

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
The Anointed Cherub — The highest-ranking angelic being, created
              to guard/cover God's throne. "Anointed" implies
              consecration for sacred service. Traditionally
              identified as Satan before his fall.

Stones of Fire — The blazing glory surrounding God's throne on
              the holy mountain. The cherub "walked among" them —
              indicating intimate, direct access to God's
              radiant presence.

Satan Typology — The interpretive approach that reads vv.12-19
              as describing Satan's original state and fall,
              using the king of Tyre as a "type" (pattern/shadow).
              Supported by language that transcends any human king.

"Till Iniquity Was Found in Thee" — The pivotal moment when
              perfection gave way to pride. Iniquity originated
              INTERNALLY — not from external temptation but from
              self-generated pride in one's own beauty and wisdom.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "I am a God" (v.2). Every human being is tempted to self-
   deification. It doesn't require literal words — it's the
   attitude of self-sufficiency, self-worship, self-determination
   apart from God. You are a MAN (or woman). Not God. Live
   accordingly.

2. Beauty became the cause of the fall (v.17). Your greatest
   GIFTS are your greatest DANGER. Intelligence, talent, beauty,
   influence — all can become mirrors that replace the face of
   God. The more gifted you are, the more vigilant you must be
   against self-admiration.

3. "Perfect... TILL" (v.15). Created perfection does not
   guarantee permanent faithfulness. Angels fell. Adam fell. You
   can fall. Don't trust your track record — trust God's
   sustaining grace daily.

4. The highest can fall the furthest. The anointed cherub had the
   most intimate access to God — and fell the most catastrophically.
   Proximity to God without humility is the most dangerous
   position in the universe. Spiritual privilege requires
   corresponding vigilance.

5. God will restore His people (vv.25-26). After all the judgment
   oracles — against Tyre, against Sidon, against the world's
   pride — God promises to bring Israel back, plant them safely,
   and vindicate His holiness. Judgment is not God's last word.
   Restoration is.
