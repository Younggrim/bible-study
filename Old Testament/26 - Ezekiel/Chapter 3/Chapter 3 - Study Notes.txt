Ezekiel - Chapter 3
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Ezekiel eats the scroll — and it tastes like HONEY. He's sent to the exiles (not foreigners — they'd listen! Israel won't). God makes his forehead hard as diamond against their hard foreheads. He sits among the exiles for seven days, overwhelmed. Then God appoints him WATCHMAN: if you warn the wicked and they die, you're innocent. If you DON'T warn them, their blood is on YOUR hands.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Ezekiel son of Buzi (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Call Narrative — Eating the Scroll + Watchman Appointment
Key Themes: The scroll tastes like honey (God's word is sweet even when the message is heavy), the prophet's forehead hardened, the watchman's responsibility, blood-guilt for silence, seven days of stunned silence

Historical Context: Continuing the commissioning (chs.2-3), Ezekiel eats the scroll of lamentations — and it's SWEET (v.3). God's word is always sweet to consume, even when its CONTENT is judgment. Then God gives the stunning comparison: if sent to FOREIGNERS they'd listen (v.6) — but Israel won't. Why? "All the house of Israel are impudent and hardhearted" (v.7). God makes Ezekiel's face HARDER than theirs (diamond vs. flint). The watchman commission (vv.17-21) defines Ezekiel's responsibility: WARN. If he warns and they ignore — their blood, not his. If he fails to warn — their blood on HIS head. The chapter ends with another glory-vision and Ezekiel struck dumb until God opens his mouth.

Structure:
- Eat the Scroll: Sweet as Honey (vv.1-3)
- Sent to Israel (Who Won't Listen) (vv.4-11)
- The Spirit Lifts Him; the Glory Departs (vv.12-15)
- The Watchman Commission (vv.16-21)
- The Plain Vision: Struck Dumb (vv.22-27)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.3  — "Then did I eat it; and it was in my mouth as honey for
        sweetness" (KJV) — A scroll full of LAMENTATIONS tastes
        like HONEY. God's word is sweet IN ITSELF — regardless of
        its content. The act of receiving God's word is always
        nourishing.

v.7  — "But the house of Israel will not hearken unto thee; for
        they will not hearken unto me: for all the house of Israel
        are impudent and hardhearted" (KJV) — The logic: they
        reject Ezekiel because they reject GOD. The prophet isn't
        the problem. The people's hearts are.

v.8  — "Behold, I have made thy face strong against their faces,
        and thy forehead strong against their foreheads" (KJV) —
        God meets hardness with HARDER. If they're flint, you're
        DIAMOND. Don't back down.

v.17 — "Son of man, I have made thee a watchman unto the house of
        Israel: therefore hear the word at my mouth, and give them
        warning from me" (KJV) — The WATCHMAN commission: hear
        from God → warn the people. Not optional. Not "if you feel
        led." MANDATORY.

v.18 — "When I say unto the wicked, Thou shalt surely die; and
        thou givest him not warning... his blood will I require at
        thine hand" (KJV) — Blood-guilt for SILENCE. Failing to
        warn makes YOU accountable for their death. The most
        sobering responsibility in Scripture for any spiritual
        leader.

v.19 — "Yet if thou warn the wicked, and he turn not... he shall
        die in his iniquity; but thou hast delivered thy soul"
        (KJV) — If you WARN and they refuse: their blood, not
        yours. You're FREE. Your responsibility ends at delivery;
        their response is their own.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.3  — "honey for sweetness" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "ke-devash
      le-matoq" = like honey for sweetness. The sensory experience
      of RECEIVING God's word: sweet regardless of content.

v.9  — "adamant harder than flint" (KJV) / "like emery, harder
      than flint" (ESV) — Hebrew "shamir" = the hardest known
      stone (possibly corundum/diamond). God's prophet is made of
      harder material than his opponents.

v.12 — "the glory of the LORD" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "kevod YHWH."
      The same glory from chapter 1. It moves — "from his place"
      — confirming: God's glory is MOBILE, not temple-bound.

v.15 — "I sat where they sat" (KJV) / "I sat overwhelmed among
      them" (ESV) — Hebrew "va-eshev sham... mashemim." Seven
      days of stunned silence among the exiles. The vision was
      too overwhelming for immediate action.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Watchman      — (Hebrew: tsopheh) A sentinel posted on walls to
              observe approaching danger and SOUND THE ALARM.
              Ezekiel's role: hear from God, warn the people. The
              watchman's failure to warn = blood-guilt.

Blood-Guilt   — Accountability for another's death due to failure
              to warn. Not for their SIN (that's theirs) but for
              their IGNORANCE of danger (that's on the silent
              watchman).

Diamond Forehead — God hardening the prophet's resolve BEYOND the
              people's hardness. Whatever their resistance level,
              the prophet is made HARDER. You don't back down from
              hard people — God equips you to face them.

Honey-Sweet Word — The paradox: God's word of JUDGMENT tastes
              SWEET when consumed. The act of receiving truth from
              God is inherently nourishing — even when the truth
              is heavy.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. The scroll of lamentations tastes like honey (v.3). God's word
   is sweet in your mouth even when it's heavy in content. Don't
   avoid hard passages — CONSUMING them is still nourishing.

2. "His blood will I require at thine hand" (v.18). If you know
   someone is in spiritual danger and stay SILENT — you share
   responsibility. This isn't about being obnoxious; it's about
   being FAITHFUL. Speak the warning. Their response is theirs.

3. "Thou hast delivered thy soul" (v.19). Once you've warned
   faithfully, YOU'RE FREE. Their rejection doesn't make you
   guilty. Release the outcome. Your job: deliver. God's job:
   results.

4. God makes your forehead harder than theirs (v.8). Don't be
   intimidated by hardened people. God equips you with GREATER
   resolve than their resistance. Diamond beats flint.

5. Seven days of stunned silence (v.15). After the vision,
   Ezekiel sat OVERWHELMED for a week before acting. Some
   commissions are so heavy that you need time to absorb them
   before moving. Give yourself permission to be stunned.
