Ezekiel - Chapter 14
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Elders come to Ezekiel to inquire of God — but God reveals: "These men have set up idols in their hearts." He refuses to answer idol-worshippers. Even if Noah, Daniel, and Job were in the land, they'd only save THEMSELVES by their righteousness — not the nation. No intercession can save a doomed generation.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Ezekiel son of Buzi (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Oracle — Idols of the Heart + Limits of Intercession
Key Themes: INTERNAL idolatry (heart-idols vs. physical statues), God refusing to answer double-minded inquirers, even the most righteous cannot save others by their merit alone, four judgments (sword, famine, beasts, pestilence)

Historical Context: Exilic elders come to Ezekiel for a divine word — apparently seeking guidance. But God sees their HEARTS: they've set up idols INTERNALLY while outwardly approaching God's prophet. God won't answer people whose hearts are divided. The second section (vv.12-23) addresses a different question: "Can righteous people save a sinful nation by their presence?" Answer: NO. Even Noah (who saved his family from the flood), Daniel (contemporary to Ezekiel, serving in Babylon), and Job (the paradigm of righteous suffering) could only save THEMSELVES. No vicarious merit of saints can override national judgment.

Structure:
- Elders with Idols in Their Hearts (vv.1-5)
- Repent! Remove Your Idols (vv.6-8)
- The Deceived Prophet Punished (vv.9-11)
- Even Noah, Daniel, and Job Cannot Save Others (vv.12-20)
- Yet a Remnant Escapes (vv.21-23)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.3 — "Son of man, these men have set up their idols in their heart, and put the stumblingblock of their iniquity before their face: should I be enquired of at all by them?" (KJV) — HEART-IDOLS. Not physical statues but internal devotions. You can sit before a prophet while worshipping something else in your heart. God sees.

v.4 — "I the LORD will answer him that cometh according to the multitude of his idols" (KJV) — God will answer them — but according to their IDOLS, not according to His mercy. The answer will expose their real allegiance.

v.14 — "Though these three men, Noah, Daniel, and Job, were in it, they should deliver but their own souls by their righteousness" (KJV) — The three most righteous men in Scripture could only save THEMSELVES. No one else's righteousness covers you. You must stand before God on your own.

v.20 — "they shall deliver neither son nor daughter; they shall but deliver their own souls by their righteousness" (KJV) — Repeated for emphasis: not even your OWN CHILDREN are saved by your merit. Each person faces God individually.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.3 — "idols in their heart" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "gilluleihem el libbam" = their dung-idols (gillulim = a contemptuous term, from "galal" = dung-ball) raised up INTO their heart. Internal worship of worthless things.

v.4 — "according to the multitude of his idols" (KJV/ESV) — God answers PROPORTIONAL to their idolatry. The more idols, the more severe the answer. Their own multiplied idols determine the harshness of God's response.

v.9 — "the prophet is deceived" (KJV) / "if the prophet is deceived" (ESV) — Hebrew "puttah" = enticed/seduced. God Himself may entice a false prophet to give a false answer — then JUDGE both prophet and inquirer. Terrifying divine sovereignty over deception.

v.14 — "Daniel" — Some scholars note this may be "Dan'el" (a legendary righteous figure known from Ugaritic texts) rather than the biblical Daniel. Most evangelicals identify him as the biblical Daniel, Ezekiel's contemporary.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Idols of the Heart — Internal objects of worship that may have no physical form. Career, money, approval, comfort — anything that occupies the devotion that belongs to God alone. Invisible but real.

Inquiry Refused — God refuses to answer people whose hearts are divided. You cannot simultaneously worship idols and seek genuine guidance from God. Choose.

Noah, Daniel, Job — Three paradigms of exceptional righteousness: Noah (survived universal judgment), Daniel (faithful in pagan empire), Job (faithful in extreme suffering). Even THEIR merit can't save others.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Idols IN THEIR HEARTS" (v.3). You can sit in church, read your Bible, and consult God's prophet — while worshipping something else internally. God sees your HEART'S allegiance, not your body's location.

2. God won't answer the double-minded (v.4). If you're seeking God's guidance while clinging to competing loyalties, don't expect clarity. REMOVE the idols first — then He'll speak.

3. No one else's righteousness saves you (v.14). Your parents' faith, your pastor's holiness, your church's reputation — none of it covers YOUR personal standing before God. You face Him ALONE.

4. What are YOUR heart-idols? Not golden statues — but: approval? Financial security? A relationship? Control? Whatever you're internally worshipping that isn't God is an idol — and it's blocking divine communication.
