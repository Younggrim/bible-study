Ezekiel - Chapter 10
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The glory of the LORD DEPARTS from the temple. Ezekiel sees the throne-chariot vision AGAIN (the cherubim and wheels from ch.1) — now positioned at the temple. Coals of fire are scattered over Jerusalem. The glory rises from the cherubim, moves to the temple threshold, then to the east gate. God is LEAVING His house.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Ezekiel son of Buzi (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Vision — The Glory Departing (Part 2)
Key Themes: The glory of God leaving the temple, cherubim identified (= living creatures of ch.1), coals of fire scattered (judgment on Jerusalem), the mobile throne positioned to DEPART

Historical Context: This is one of the most theologically significant chapters in the OT. God's glory (Shekinah) — which filled the temple at Solomon's dedication (1 Kings 8:10-11) — NOW LEAVES. The departure is gradual and reluctant: from the cherubim (v.4) to the threshold (v.4), from the threshold to above the cherubim (v.18), from the temple to the east gate (v.19). God doesn't WANT to leave — He moves step by step, giving time for repentance. But the abominations of chapters 8-9 have made His presence impossible. The living creatures of chapter 1 are NOW identified as cherubim (v.15,20) — confirming the throne-chariot is the same one Ezekiel saw by the Chebar canal.

Structure:
- Coals of Fire Scattered Over Jerusalem (vv.1-8)
- The Wheels and Cherubim Described (vv.9-14)
- The Cherubim ARE the Living Creatures (vv.15-17)
- The Glory Moves to the East Gate (vv.18-22)

------------------------------------

Charles Spurgeon:
  "The glory left GRADUALLY — step by step, pause by pause. God doesn't abandon quickly. He lingers at the threshold, hesitates at the gate, pauses on the mountain. Every step is a chance for 'COME BACK!' But they never said it. And He left."

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. God leaves GRADUALLY (vv.4,18,19). He didn't abandon the temple instantly — He paused, lingered, gave time. If you sense God's presence withdrawing from any area of your life, it's not instant — it's a WARNING. He's at the threshold. Call Him back.

2. The same fire purifies OR destroys (v.2). God's holiness hasn't changed — YOUR condition determines the effect. Isaiah's coal cleansed him; Jerusalem's coals burned it. Is your heart prepared for God's fire?

3. God faces east when He leaves (v.19). The departure direction IS the return direction (43:1-4). Even in leaving, God signals His eventual return. His departures contain the promise of homecoming.

4. "Your house is left unto you DESOLATE" (Matthew 23:38 echoing this). A house without God's presence — no matter how ornate — is DESOLATE. Don't maintain a beautiful structure without the Presence. The building means nothing without the Glory.
