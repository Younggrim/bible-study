Ezekiel - Chapter 10
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The glory of the LORD DEPARTS from the temple. Ezekiel sees the throne-chariot vision AGAIN (the cherubim and wheels from ch.1) — now positioned at the temple. Coals of fire are scattered over Jerusalem. The glory rises from the cherubim, moves to the temple threshold, then to the east gate. God is LEAVING His house.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Ezekiel son of Buzi (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Vision — The Glory Departing (Part 2)
Key Themes: The glory of God leaving the temple, cherubim identified (= living creatures of ch.1), coals of fire scattered (judgment on Jerusalem), the mobile throne positioned to DEPART

Historical Context: This is one of the most theologically significant chapters in the OT. God's glory (Shekinah) — which filled the temple at Solomon's dedication (1 Kings 8:10-11) — NOW LEAVES. The departure is gradual and reluctant: from the cherubim (v.4) to the threshold (v.4), from the threshold to above the cherubim (v.18), from the temple to the east gate (v.19). God doesn't WANT to leave — He moves step by step, giving time for repentance. But the abominations of chapters 8-9 have made His presence impossible. The living creatures of chapter 1 are NOW identified as cherubim (v.15,20) — confirming the throne-chariot is the same one Ezekiel saw by the Chebar canal.

Structure:
- Coals of Fire Scattered Over Jerusalem (vv.1-8)
- The Wheels and Cherubim Described (vv.9-14)
- The Cherubim ARE the Living Creatures (vv.15-17)
- The Glory Moves to the East Gate (vv.18-22)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.4  — "Then the glory of the LORD went up from the cherub, and
        stood over the threshold of the house" (KJV) — The FIRST MOVE: glory rises from its place above the cherubim and pauses at the door. God is heading for the EXIT — but pauses.

v.18 — "Then the glory of the LORD departed from off the threshold
        of the house, and stood over the cherubims" (KJV) — The SECOND MOVE: from the threshold (door) to above the cherubim (the mobile throne). God has stepped OUT of the house.

v.19 — "And the cherubims lifted up their wings, and mounted up
        from the earth in my sight... and every one stood at the door of the east gate of the LORD's house; and the glory of the God of Israel was over them above" (KJV) — The THIRD MOVE: to the east gate. God faces EAST — toward the Mount of Olives, toward exile, AWAY from the temple.

v.2  — "Go in between the wheels... fill thine hand with coals of
        fire from between the cherubims, and scatter them over the city" (KJV) — The same coals that CLEANSED Isaiah (6:6-7) now JUDGE Jerusalem. Fire from God's presence can purify or destroy — depending on the recipient's state.

CROSS-REFERENCES
------------------------------------
v.2  — Isaiah 6:6-7 — Coals from the altar that cleansed Isaiah's lips. Same fire, different purpose.
v.4  — 1 Kings 8:10-11 — Glory FILLING the temple at dedication. Now it LEAVES.
v.4  — Ezekiel 9:3 — The glory's first movement (beginning of departure).
v.18 — Ezekiel 11:22-23 — Glory moves to the Mount of Olives (final departure).
v.19 — Ezekiel 43:1-4 — The glory RETURNS from the east (future restoration).
v.19 — Matthew 23:38 — "your house is left unto you desolate."
v.20 — Ezekiel 1:5-14 — The living creatures now identified as cherubim.

  "The glory left GRADUALLY — step by step, pause by pause. God doesn't abandon quickly. He lingers at the threshold, hesitates at the gate, pauses on the mountain. Every step is a chance for 'COME BACK!' But they never said it. And He left."

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.2 — "wheels" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "galgal" (different from "ophan" in ch.1 but referring to the same structures). The whirling/rotating quality emphasized.

v.4 — "threshold" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "miphtan" = the door-sill/threshold. The boundary between inside and outside. God's glory pauses AT THE DOOR — not yet outside but no longer inside.

v.14 — "the first face was the face of a cherub" (KJV/ESV) — In ch.1 it was "the face of an ox." Here "cherub" replaces "ox" — suggesting the OX face IS the cherub's distinctive face (or the perspective has shifted).

v.20 — "This is the living creature that I saw... by the river of Chebar" (KJV/ESV) — The identification: the beings in ch.1 (by the canal in Babylon) ARE the cherubim of the temple. Same God, same throne, both locations.

v.2 — ASV: "both thy hands" for KJV's "thine hand"
       NET: "cherubim. Fill your hands" for KJV's "cherub, and fill thine hand"
       WEB: "both your hands" for KJV's "thine hand"
v.4 — ASV: uses "Jehovah" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).
       NET: "temple. The temple" for KJV's "house; and the house"
       WEB: uses "Yahweh" for KJV's "LORD" (Hebrew YHWH).
v.14 — NET: "Each of the cherubim" for KJV's "And every one"
       WEB: adds "cherub. The"

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Shekinah Glory — The visible manifestation of God's presence (the cloud/fire that filled the tabernacle and temple). Its DEPARTURE from the temple means God has VACATED His house.

Gradual Departure — God's glory doesn't flash away instantly — it moves step by step (inner chamber → threshold → east gate → Mount of Olives). Each pause is an invitation to repent.

Coals of Fire — From between the cherubim: the same source that purifies (Isaiah 6) now destroys (Ezekiel 10). God's holiness is the same — human condition determines whether it heals or burns.

East Gate — The direction of departure AND return. The glory leaves EASTWARD; in 43:1-4 it returns FROM the east. The departure guarantees the return.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. God leaves GRADUALLY (vv.4,18,19). He didn't abandon the temple instantly — He paused, lingered, gave time. If you sense God's presence withdrawing from any area of your life, it's not instant — it's a WARNING. He's at the threshold. Call Him back.

2. The same fire purifies OR destroys (v.2). God's holiness hasn't changed — YOUR condition determines the effect. Isaiah's coal cleansed him; Jerusalem's coals burned it. Is your heart prepared for God's fire?

3. God faces east when He leaves (v.19). The departure direction IS the return direction (43:1-4). Even in leaving, God signals His eventual return. His departures contain the promise of homecoming.

4. "Your house is left unto you DESOLATE" (Matthew 23:38 echoing this). A house without God's presence — no matter how ornate — is DESOLATE. Don't maintain a beautiful structure without the Presence. The building means nothing without the Glory.
