Ezekiel - Chapter 6
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Oracle against the MOUNTAINS of Israel — the high places where idols are worshipped. God will scatter dead bodies around the altars, destroy the incense-stands, and spread slain Israelites among their idols. Yet a remnant will survive among the nations and REMEMBER God — broken-hearted over their own sin. "Then shall they know that I am the LORD."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Ezekiel son of Buzi (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Oracle — Against the Mountains/High Places
Key Themes: The high places of idolatry destroyed, corpses among
idols, scattered remnant, the remnant's broken-hearted memory,
"they shall know that I am the LORD" (Ezekiel's signature phrase)

Historical Context: The "mountains of Israel" are the LOCATIONS
of idol worship — the "high places" (bamot) where Canaanite
fertility worship flourished despite centuries of prophetic
condemnation. God doesn't just judge PEOPLE — He judges the
PLACES of sin. The imagery is deliberately gruesome: dead bodies
scattered AMONG the altars, bones AROUND the idols. The very
places designed for "worship" become mass graves. Yet vv.8-10
offer a surprising mercy: some survivors, scattered among nations,
will REMEMBER God with BROKEN HEARTS and loathe THEMSELVES for
their own evil. Self-loathing before God is the beginning of
restoration.

Structure:
- Set Your Face Against the Mountains (vv.1-2)
- God Destroys the High Places and Altars (vv.3-7)
- A Remnant Survives and Remembers (vv.8-10)
- Stamp Your Foot: Sword, Famine, Pestilence (vv.11-14)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.3  — "Behold, I, even I, will bring a sword upon you, and I
        will destroy your high places" (KJV) — God Himself brings
        the sword. Not just allowing judgment but PERSONALLY
        directing it against Israel's worship sites.

v.6  — "your altars may be laid waste and made desolate, and your
        idols may be broken and cease, and your images may be cut
        down" (KJV) — Every element of false worship systematically
        destroyed: altars, idols, incense stands, images. Complete
        demolition of the idolatrous infrastructure.

v.9  — "And they that escape of you shall remember me among the
        nations... and they shall lothe themselves for the evils
        which they have committed" (KJV) — The remnant's
        TRANSFORMATION: they REMEMBER God AND loathe their own sin.
        Genuine repentance: broken-hearted self-awareness.

v.10 — "And they shall know that I am the LORD, and that I have
        not said in vain that I would do this evil unto them" (KJV)
        — The PURPOSE of judgment: KNOWLEDGE OF GOD. "They shall
        know" = Ezekiel's signature (appears 70+ times). God's
        word is NOT empty threat.

v.11 — "Smite with thine hand, and stamp with thy foot, and say,
        Alas for all the evil abominations of the house of Israel!"
        (KJV) — Physical grief-actions (clapping/stamping) — not
        joy but HORROR at what sin has produced. The prophet
        mourns the sin-consequence connection.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.2  — "mountains of Israel" (KJV/ESV) — The hill country where
     the high places were built. Mountains = worship sites. The
     TERRAIN is addressed because the terrain hosted the sin.

v.4  — "images" (KJV) / "incense altars" (ESV) — Hebrew
     "chammanim" = sun-pillars or incense stands used in Baal
     worship. Small stone or clay structures for burning incense
     to false gods.

v.9  — "remember" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "zakhar" = to remember,
     call to mind. In exile, the survivors will RECALL God — not
     just intellectually but relationally. Memory as the beginning
     of return.

v.9  — "lothe themselves" (KJV) / "will be loathsome in their
     own sight" (ESV) — Hebrew "naqotu bifeneyhem" = they'll be
     disgusted with themselves. Self-directed revulsion at one's
     own sin. The deepest form of conviction.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
High Places   — (Hebrew: bamot) Hilltop worship sites for
              Canaanite religion adopted by Israel. The persistent
              sin that no king fully eradicated (even "good" kings
              often left them standing).

"They Shall Know That I Am the LORD" — Ezekiel's signature
              phrase (70+ times). The PURPOSE of both judgment and
              salvation: producing genuine KNOWLEDGE of God. Not
              information but recognition.

Self-Loathing Before God — The remnant's response: hating their
              own sin with visceral disgust. Not self-hatred
              (depressive) but sin-hatred (repentant). The
              beginning of genuine transformation.

Corpses Among Idols — The deliberate placement of dead bodies
              around idol-altars: the "worshippers" permanently
              joined to their "gods" in death. Gruesome irony.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. God judges the PLACES of sin (vv.3-6). Not just people but
   SYSTEMS, SITES, and STRUCTURES of idolatry. What "high places"
   exist in your life — patterns, locations, habits — that need
   to be demolished?

2. "They shall loathe themselves" (v.9). Genuine repentance isn't
   just fear of consequences — it's DISGUST with the sin itself.
   Have you reached the point where you HATE what you once loved?
   That's real transformation.

3. "They shall KNOW that I am the LORD" (v.10). Everything God
   does — judgment and mercy — serves one purpose: that people
   KNOW Him. All of history is a classroom. God is the lesson.

4. The remnant remembers (v.9). In exile, among the nations,
   SCATTERED — they remember. Sometimes you don't appreciate God
   until you're far from everything familiar. Distance can
   produce memory.

5. God's word is not "in vain" (v.10). Every threat God makes,
   He carries out. Every promise God makes, He fulfills. He has
   not spoken in vain. Take His word seriously — BOTH warnings
   and promises.
