Ezekiel - Chapter 47
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
One of the most stunning visions in all of Scripture: a river flows from beneath the threshold of the temple, running eastward, growing deeper with every step — ankle-deep, knee-deep, waist-deep, then a river too deep to cross. It flows to the Dead Sea and HEALS it, producing life where only death existed. Trees line its banks whose leaves never wither and whose fruit never fails. The chapter concludes with the boundaries of the promised land for the restored nation.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Ezekiel son of Buzi (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Vision — The Life-Giving River and
Land Boundaries
Key Themes: The river of God flowing from His presence, life
conquering death, healing of the Dead Sea, supernatural
fruitfulness, the boundaries of the restored land, inclusion of
sojourners in the inheritance

Historical Context: The temple vision now reaches its most
poetic and theologically rich moment. After the architectural
measurements (chs. 40-42), the glory's return (ch. 43), the
priestly regulations (ch. 44), the land allotment (ch. 45), and
the worship ordinances (ch. 46), Ezekiel sees the RESULT of
God's presence dwelling in the temple: LIFE FLOWS OUT.

The Dead Sea — the lowest point on earth, seven times saltier
than the ocean, utterly lifeless — becomes the ultimate symbol
of what happens when God's presence reaches a place. The
impossible is reversed. Death becomes life. Barrenness becomes
fruitfulness. What nothing else could heal, God's river heals.

This vision echoes Genesis 2 (the river from Eden watering the
garden), Joel 3:18 ("a fountain shall come forth of the house of
the LORD"), Zechariah 14:8 ("living waters shall go out from
Jerusalem"), and reaches its climactic fulfillment in Revelation
22:1-2 (the river of the water of life flowing from God's throne,
with trees whose leaves are "for the healing of the nations").
The Bible begins and ends with a garden, a river, and trees of
life. Ezekiel 47 is the prophetic bridge between the two.

Structure:
- The River Emerges from the Temple (vv.1-2)
- The River Deepens: Ankle, Knee, Waist, Swimming (vv.3-5)
- The River Brings Life Wherever It Flows (vv.6-10)
- The Marshes Remain Salt (v.11)
- The Trees of Healing Along the Banks (v.12)
- The Boundaries of the Promised Land (vv.13-20)
- Inheritance for Sojourners Among Israel (vv.21-23)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "Afterward he brought me again unto the door of the
        house; and, behold, waters issued out from under the
        threshold of the house eastward... and the waters came
        down from under from the right side of the house, at the
        south side of the altar" (KJV) — The water originates
        from UNDER THE THRESHOLD — from God's very dwelling place.
        It flows past the altar (where sacrifice was offered) and
        heads east (toward the Dead Sea). Life flows FROM God's
        presence, PAST the cross, and INTO the deadest places.

v.5  — "Afterward he measured a thousand; and it was a river
        that I could not pass over: for the waters were risen,
        waters to swim in, a river that could not be passed over"
        (KJV) — The river grows from a trickle to an ocean.
        Without any tributaries. Without rain or springs feeding
        it. It simply DEEPENS because it flows from an infinite
        source. God's life gets deeper the further it flows from
        His presence. What starts as ankle-deep becomes
        unfathomable.

v.8  — "Then said he unto me, These waters issue out toward the
        east country, and go down into the desert, and go into
        the sea: which being brought forth into the sea, the
        waters shall be healed" (KJV) — "THE WATERS SHALL BE
        HEALED." The Dead Sea — lifeless, toxic, irredeemable by
        any natural process — HEALED by God's river. There is
        nothing too dead for God's life to reach. No situation
        too toxic. No person too far gone.

v.9  — "And it shall come to pass, that every thing that liveth,
        which moveth, whithersoever the rivers shall come, shall
        live: and there shall be a very great multitude of fish"
        (KJV) — "EVERY THING... SHALL LIVE." Wherever this river
        touches, life explodes. Fish swarm. Death retreats. This
        is the character of God's presence — it does not merely
        stop death; it produces ABUNDANT life.

v.12 — "And by the river upon the bank thereof, on this side and
        on that side, shall grow all trees for meat, whose leaf
        shall not fade, neither shall the fruit thereof be
        consumed: it shall bring forth new fruit according to his
        months, because their waters they issued out of the
        sanctuary: and the fruit thereof shall be for meat, and
        the leaf thereof for medicine" (KJV) — Trees that never
        wither, fruit that never fails, leaves that HEAL. This
        is Revelation 22:2 — "the leaves of the tree were for
        the healing of the nations." Eden restored. Paradise
        regained. The curse reversed.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "threshold" — Hebrew "miphtān" = the door-sill, the
      bottom of the doorway. The water starts at the LOWEST point
      of the entrance — humble origins for a mighty river. God's
      greatest works often begin imperceptibly.

v.3  — "a thousand" — Hebrew "elep" = one thousand (cubits),
      approximately 1,750 feet with long cubits. Four
      measurements of 1,000 cubits each = approximately 1.3
      miles total. The river deepens every quarter-mile with no
      visible source of increase.

v.8  — "the sea" — Hebrew "hayyāmmāh" — referring to the
      Dead Sea (the Salt Sea / Sea of the Arabah). Located at
      the lowest point on earth (approximately 1,400 feet below
      sea level), with salinity of approximately 34% — no life
      can survive. The ultimate symbol of death in Israel's
      geography.

v.11 — "marshes" / "marishes" (KJV) — Hebrew "biṣṣā'ōṯ" =
      swamps, marshlands. These are NOT healed but remain salty —
      "given to salt." A practical provision (salt was necessary)
      and a theological reminder: even in restoration, some
      consequences remain as memorials.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Temple River  — The supernatural river flowing from beneath the
              temple threshold, growing deeper without tributaries,
              healing the Dead Sea, and producing trees of life.
              Symbol of God's life-giving presence flowing to the
              world's deadest places.

Dead Sea      — The Salt Sea (Hebrew: Yam ha-Melach), located at
              the lowest point on earth. Its extreme salinity
              makes it lifeless — the perfect symbol of what God's
              river reverses. Its healing is the ultimate miracle
              of the restored creation.

Trees of Life — Trees along the river's banks whose leaves never
              wither and whose fruit is perpetual. Their leaves
              serve as medicine/healing. Direct parallel to
              Revelation 22:2 and echo of Genesis 2:9 (the tree
              of life in Eden).

Living Water  — A Hebrew idiom for fresh, flowing water (as
              opposed to stagnant cistern water). Used by Jesus
              in John 4:10 and 7:38 as a metaphor for the Holy
              Spirit's life-giving power flowing from God through
              believers.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Life flows FROM God's presence (v.1). The river does not exist
   apart from the temple. Cut off from God's dwelling, there is
   no river, no healing, no life. Everything good in your life
   traces back to His presence. Stay near the threshold. Stay
   near the source.

2. The river deepens without tributaries (vv.3-5). No feeder
   streams. No rain. It simply grows because its SOURCE is
   infinite. Your spiritual life does not need external inputs to
   deepen — it needs only to keep flowing from God. The further
   you go with Him, the deeper it gets. Have you only waded in
   ankle-deep? There are swimming depths waiting.

3. God's river reaches the DEADEST place (v.8). The Dead Sea —
   the most lifeless body of water on earth. God does not send
   His river to places that are already thriving. He sends it to
   the DEAD places. Whatever feels most dead in your life — your
   marriage, your calling, your hope — that is exactly where
   God's river is headed.

4. "Everything shall live whithersoever the river cometh" (v.9).
   No exceptions. No cases too hard. No death too permanent.
   Where God's presence flows, LIFE follows. Period. Do you
   believe this for the deadest situations you're facing?

5. The trees produce fruit AND medicine (v.12). The fruit feeds;
   the leaves heal. God's river does not just sustain life — it
   restores what was broken. You are meant to be this kind of
   tree: someone who FEEDS others (fruit) and HEALS others
   (leaves). Both. Not just preaching truth but bringing
   restoration.
