Ezekiel - Chapter 43
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
THE GLORY RETURNS. In the climactic moment of Ezekiel's entire prophecy, the glory of the LORD — the same glory that departed from Jerusalem through the east gate in chapters 10-11 — returns from the east and fills the new temple. God declares "this is the place of my throne... where I will dwell in the midst of the children of Israel for ever." The chapter then provides the dimensions and consecration instructions for the great altar of burnt offering.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Ezekiel son of Buzi (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Vision — The Return of God's Glory
Key Themes: The glory of God returning, permanent divine
indwelling, God's throne established forever, the altar of burnt
offering dimensions and consecration, the call to repentance
through the vision of holiness

Historical Context: This is the moment everything in Ezekiel has
been building toward. In chapters 8-11, Ezekiel witnessed the
most devastating event in Israel's spiritual history: the glory
of the LORD departing from the temple — first from the cherubim
to the threshold (9:3), then to the east gate (10:19), then to
the mountain east of the city (11:23). God LEFT His own house
because of Israel's abominations.

Now, after 32 chapters of judgment, exile, restoration promises,
Gog's defeat, and the new temple's measurements — the glory
RETURNS. And it comes from THE EAST (v.2) — the same direction
it departed. God comes back the way He left. The exile of God's
presence is over.

The theological weight of this chapter cannot be overstated. For
Ezekiel — a priest who never served in a functioning temple, an
exile who watched God leave — this is EVERYTHING. God's presence
returns. God's throne is established. God says "FOR EVER." The
cycle of departure and return ends with permanent, irreversible
indwelling.

The chapter also provides the altar dimensions and a seven-day
consecration ceremony (vv.13-27), establishing that restored
relationship requires ongoing atonement. The glory returns, but
the altar ensures the relationship is maintained through
sacrifice.

Structure:
- The Glory Returns from the East (vv.1-5)
- God's Voice: "This Is My Throne — Forever" (vv.6-9)
- Command to Show Israel the Temple Plan (vv.10-12)
- The Altar of Burnt Offering: Dimensions (vv.13-17)
- The Altar Consecration: Seven Days (vv.18-27)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.2  — "And, behold, the glory of the God of Israel came from
        the way of the east: and his voice was like a noise of
        many waters: and the earth shined with his glory" (KJV)
        — FROM THE EAST. The same direction the glory departed
        (11:23). God returns the way He left. His voice like many
        waters (cf. Revelation 1:15). The earth itself SHINES.
        When God's glory moves, creation responds. This is the
        reversal of chapters 10-11 — the homecoming of God.

v.4  — "And the glory of the LORD came into the house by the way
        of the gate whose prospect is toward the east" (KJV) —
        The east gate — the same gate through which the glory
        departed. God does not sneak in through a back door. He
        returns triumphantly through the front gate. And this
        gate will be SHUT permanently afterward (44:2) — because
        the King has entered and will never leave again.

v.5  — "So the spirit took me up, and brought me into the inner
        court; and, behold, the glory of the LORD filled the
        house" (KJV) — "The glory of the LORD FILLED the house."
        Compare Exodus 40:34 (glory fills the tabernacle),
        1 Kings 8:10-11 (glory fills Solomon's temple). Three
        times in Scripture, God's glory fills a dwelling. Each
        time: overwhelming, all-consuming, total.

v.7  — "And he said unto me, Son of man, the place of my throne,
        and the place of the soles of my feet, where I will dwell
        in the midst of the children of Israel for ever, and my
        holy name, shall the house of Israel no more defile"
        (KJV) — "The place of my THRONE... where I will dwell...
        FOR EVER." Three staggering declarations: (1) This is
        God's THRONE — not just a visiting room. (2) He will
        DWELL — not visit. (3) FOR EVER — not temporarily. And
        Israel will NEVER AGAIN defile His name. The cycle ends.

v.9  — "Now let them put away their whoredom, and the carcases
        of their kings, far from me, and I will dwell in the
        midst of them for ever" (KJV) — God's condition: put away
        the idolatry ("whoredom") and the cult of dead kings
        ("carcases"). Then: permanent dwelling. Holiness and
        presence are inseparable.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.2  — "glory" — Hebrew "kāḇôḏ" = weight, heaviness, splendor.
      The visible manifestation of God's presence. Same word used
      throughout Exodus, 1 Kings, and Ezekiel 1-11. When kāḇôḏ
      shows up, God is THERE — tangibly, visibly, undeniably.

v.7  — "the place of my throne" — Hebrew "meqôm kis'î" = the
      location of my royal seat. God claims this temple not as a
      visiting dignitary but as a KING taking His throne. This is
      His seat of government, His ruling place.

v.7  — "the soles of my feet" — Hebrew "kap raglāy" = the flat
      of my feet (where I rest my feet). An expression for God's
      footstool (cf. Isaiah 66:1, "the earth is my footstool").
      God sits enthroned in heaven; His feet rest in the temple.
      The temple connects heaven and earth.

v.15 — "Ariel" — Hebrew "ari'ēl" = "lion of God" or "hearth of
      God." The name for the altar-hearth where sacrificial fire
      burns. The altar is the "lion" — powerful, consuming — or
      the "hearth" — warm, sustaining. Isaiah 29:1 also uses
      "Ariel" for Jerusalem's altar.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Glory (Kāḇôḏ) — The visible, weighty manifestation of God's
              presence. In Ezekiel, it appeared as fire, radiance,
              and cloud. Its departure (chs. 10-11) signaled
              judgment; its return (ch. 43) signals permanent
              restoration.

East Gate     — The gate facing sunrise, through which God's
              glory both departed and returned. After the glory
              re-enters in chapter 43, this gate is permanently
              shut (44:2) — the King has come home and will never
              leave.

Ariel         — "Lion of God" or "Hearth of God" — the name for
              the altar-hearth (v.15). The top of the great altar
              where fire consumed offerings. It is both fierce
              (lion) and warm (hearth) — judgment and fellowship
              meet at the altar.

Consecration  — The seven-day process of sanctifying the altar
              (vv.18-27) through sin offerings, blood application,
              and burnt offerings. Until the altar is consecrated,
              no offering can be accepted. Access to God requires
              proper preparation.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. THE GLORY RETURNS (vv.2-5). If you have experienced a season
   where God felt absent — where His presence seemed to have
   departed — this chapter is your promise. God COMES BACK. He
   returns the way He left. And when He returns, He comes to
   STAY. Whatever "east gate" He departed through, watch that
   same direction. He's coming home.

2. God returns as KING, not guest (v.7). "The place of my
   THRONE." God does not ask permission to re-enter. He does not
   negotiate terms. He claims His throne. In your life, is God a
   welcome guest or an enthroned King? There is a difference.
   Guests leave. Kings reign.

3. "For ever" — God's presence is permanent (v.7). He will not
   leave again. He will not be driven out by our failures. In
   Christ, believers have this promise: "I will never leave thee,
   nor forsake thee" (Hebrews 13:5). The Spirit's indwelling is
   God's glory filling YOUR temple (1 Corinthians 6:19). And He
   says: FOR EVER.

4. Holiness and presence are inseparable (v.9). "Put away their
   whoredom... and I will dwell." God does not compromise on
   holiness to maintain presence. Nor does He withdraw presence
   to punish. He offers both: "Be holy, and I will be THERE."
   The two go together. You cannot have one without the other.

5. The altar must be consecrated before service begins (vv.18-27).
   Seven days. Not rushed, not skipped. Before you serve God,
   let Him prepare you. Before you offer anything to God, let
   God sanctify the place of offering. Ministry without
   consecration is presumption.
