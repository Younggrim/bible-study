Ezekiel - Chapter 22
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Jerusalem is called "the bloody city" and her sins are catalogued in devastating detail: bloodshed, idolatry, sexual immorality, oppression of the vulnerable, Sabbath profanation, and corruption at every level of society. God declares that prophets, priests, princes, and people have ALL failed. The chapter climaxes with one of Scripture's most heartbreaking statements: "I sought for a man among them, that should make up the hedge... but I found none."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Ezekiel son of Buzi (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Indictment / Covenant Lawsuit
Key Themes: Jerusalem as "the bloody city," comprehensive sin
catalogue, corruption at every social level, the smelting
furnace of judgment, no intercessor found, leadership failure

Historical Context: This chapter functions as God's formal legal
indictment against Jerusalem — a covenant lawsuit listing every
charge before sentencing. The sins listed are not vague
generalities but specific violations of the Mosaic covenant:
idolatry (Exodus 20:3-5), bloodshed (Exodus 20:13), adultery
(Exodus 20:14), oppression of aliens and orphans (Exodus 22:21-
22), Sabbath violation (Exodus 20:8-11), and financial
exploitation (Exodus 22:25).

The chapter is structured in three oracles. The first (vv.1-16)
catalogues Jerusalem's sins. The second (vv.17-22) uses the
metaphor of a smelting furnace — Israel has become worthless
dross (impure metal) that must be melted down. The third
(vv.23-31) indicts every class of society: prophets who lie,
priests who profane the holy, princes who murder for profit,
and people who oppress the poor.

The devastating conclusion (v.30) reveals that God LOOKED for
someone to stand in the gap — an intercessor, a reformer, anyone
— and found NO ONE. This is not about God being unable; it's
about the total moral collapse of an entire society.

Structure:
- The Bloody City: Sins Catalogued (vv.1-16)
- The Smelting Furnace: Israel as Dross (vv.17-22)
- Every Class Indicted: No One Stands in the Gap (vv.23-31)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.2  — "Now, thou son of man, wilt thou judge, wilt thou judge
        the bloody city? yea, thou shalt shew her all her
        abominations" (KJV) — "The bloody city" — Jerusalem's
        new name. Repeated for emphasis. The holy city has become
        a slaughterhouse. God dares Ezekiel to judge her.

v.12 — "In thee have they taken gifts to shed blood; thou hast
        taken usury and increase, and thou hast greedily gained
        of thy neighbours by extortion, and hast forgotten me,
        saith the Lord GOD" (KJV) — The root cause beneath all
        the sins: "thou hast forgotten me." Bribery, usury,
        extortion — all symptoms of forgetting God.

v.26 — "Her priests have violated my law, and have profaned mine
        holy things: they have put no difference between the holy
        and profane, neither have they shewed difference between
        the unclean and the clean" (KJV) — The priests' specific
        failure: erasing the distinction between holy and common.
        When spiritual leaders stop distinguishing, the whole
        society collapses.

v.28 — "And her prophets have daubed them with untempered morter,
        seeing vanity, and divining lies unto them, saying, Thus
        saith the Lord GOD, when the LORD hath not spoken" (KJV)
        — False prophets whitewashing rot. They plaster over
        structural damage with "God told me" — but God never spoke.

v.30 — "And I sought for a man among them, that should make up
        the hedge, and stand in the gap before me for the land,
        that I should not destroy it: but I found none" (KJV) —
        The most devastating verse. God SEARCHED for one person
        to intercede. One. And found NOBODY. The absence of an
        intercessor seals the city's fate.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.2  — "bloody city" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "ir haddamim" = city of
      bloods (plural). Multiple bloodsheds, ongoing violence.
      The plural intensifies the horror — not one incident but
      a culture of bloodshed.

v.18 — "dross" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "sig" = the impurities that
      separate from metal during smelting. Tin, lead, iron, and
      copper residue — worthless byproduct. Israel was supposed
      to be silver; she has become slag.

v.28 — "untempered morter" (KJV) — Hebrew "taphel" = whitewash,
      plaster without straw/binding agent. Looks solid but has
      no structural integrity. ESV: "whitewash." The prophets
      cover cracks with cosmetics instead of addressing the
      foundation.

v.30 — "stand in the gap" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "omed bapperetz" =
      standing in the breach of a broken wall. Military imagery:
      when the wall is breached, a warrior stands in the opening
      to prevent the enemy from pouring through. No one would
      stand.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
The Bloody City — God's legal title for Jerusalem in this
              indictment. The holy city has earned the same name
              as Nineveh (Nahum 3:1) through systemic violence.

Dross — The worthless impurities separated from precious metal
              during smelting. Israel should be refined silver
              but has become only waste residue.

Stand in the Gap — To position oneself in a broken place (wall
              breach) to prevent destruction. Refers to
              intercession — one person who might have turned
              away God's wrath through prayer or righteous action.

Untempered Mortar — Whitewash without binding agent. Looks clean
              on the surface but has no structural strength.
              Represents false prophecy that covers over sin
              without addressing it.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Thou hast forgotten me" (v.12). Every sin listed in this
   chapter grows from one root: forgetting God. Bribery, lust,
   oppression, Sabbath-breaking — all fruit of the same tree.
   Guard your MEMORY of God above all else.

2. The gap needs someone to stand in it (v.30). God is STILL
   looking for intercessors. Will you be the one who prays when
   no one else will? The one who speaks truth when everyone else
   is silent? Don't let God search your generation and find the
   breach unmanned.

3. Leaders are held to a higher standard (vv.25-29). Prophets,
   priests, princes, people — God starts at the top. Leadership
   failure produces societal collapse. If you lead in any
   capacity, your failure doesn't stay private — it corrupts
   downward.

4. Whitewash doesn't fix foundations (v.28). Stop plastering over
   problems with spiritual-sounding language. "God told me
   everything is fine" is not a foundation repair — it's
   decoration on a condemned building. Face the cracks honestly.

5. God searches before He judges (v.30). Judgment is never God's
   first response. He LOOKS for an alternative. He SEEKS a
   reason to relent. When judgment finally falls, it's because
   every other option was exhausted.
