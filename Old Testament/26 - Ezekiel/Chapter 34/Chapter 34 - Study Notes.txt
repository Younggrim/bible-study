Ezekiel - Chapter 34
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
"Woe be to the shepherds of Israel that do feed themselves!" God denounces leaders who exploit rather than protect the flock. Because the shepherds failed, God Himself will become the Shepherd — searching, gathering, feeding, and healing His scattered sheep. He will set up ONE shepherd: "my servant David." He will make a covenant of peace, and showers of blessing will fall.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Ezekiel son of Buzi (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Classification: Prophetic Oracle — Against False Shepherds + The Divine Shepherd + Messianic Promise
Key Themes: Failed leadership indicted, God as personal Shepherd, gathering the scattered, the messianic "David," covenant of peace, blessings restored

Historical Context: After chapters of judgment (including oracles against nations, chs.25-32), chapter 34 begins the RESTORATION section of Ezekiel (chs.34-48). The "shepherds" (Israel's kings, priests, prophets, elders) FAILED: they fed themselves, didn't heal the sick, didn't bind the broken, didn't seek the lost (vv.2-4). The sheep were scattered among nations — the exile was partially the SHEPHERDS' fault. God's response: (1) I will judge the shepherds, (2) I MYSELF will shepherd My flock, (3) I will set up "my servant David" (the Messiah) as one shepherd. Jesus identifies Himself as this shepherd in John 10.

Structure:
- Woe to the Shepherds Who Feed Themselves (vv.1-6)
- God Against the Shepherds (vv.7-10)
- God Himself Will Shepherd (vv.11-16)
- God Judges Between Sheep (vv.17-22)
- "My Servant David" — One Shepherd (vv.23-24)
- The Covenant of Peace: Showers of Blessing (vv.25-31)

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.2  — "Woe be to the shepherds of Israel that do feed themselves!
        should not the shepherds feed the flocks?" (KJV) — The indictment: self-feeding leaders. They eat the fat, clothe themselves with wool, slaughter the fed — but DON'T FEED THE FLOCK.

v.4  — "The diseased have ye not strengthened, neither have ye
        healed that which was sick, neither have ye bound up that which was broken, neither have ye brought again that which was driven away, neither have ye sought that which was lost" (KJV) — FIVE failures: didn't strengthen, didn't heal, didn't bind, didn't bring back, didn't seek. The job description of a shepherd defined by NEGLECT.

v.11 — "Behold, I, even I, will both search my sheep, and seek
        them out" (KJV) — God takes over PERSONALLY. "I, even I" — emphasized divine initiative. The failures of human shepherds trigger DIVINE shepherding.

v.15 — "I will feed my flock, and I will cause them to lie down,
        saith the Lord GOD" (KJV) — God as Shepherd: feeding AND resting. Psalm 23:1-2 embodied: "He maketh me to lie down in green pastures."

v.23 — "And I will set up one shepherd over them, and he shall
        feed them, even my servant David; he shall feed them, and he shall be their shepherd" (KJV) — The messianic promise: ONE shepherd — "David" (= Christ, the ideal Davidic king). Not multiple competing leaders but ONE good shepherd.

v.26 — "I will make them and the places round about my hill a
        blessing; and I will cause the shower to come down in his season; there shall be showers of blessing" (KJV) — "Showers of blessing" — the phrase comes from HERE. Not scattered drops but SHOWERS. Abundant, seasonal, purposeful.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.2 — "shepherds" (KJV/ESV) — Hebrew "ro'im" = shepherds/pastors. Refers to ALL leaders: kings, priests, prophets, elders. Any who had responsibility for "feeding" God's people.

v.4 — "diseased... sick... broken... driven away... lost" (KJV/ESV) — Five categories of need: chronic illness, acute sickness, fractures/wounds, displacement, and loss. The complete spectrum of pastoral need — all unmet.

v.16 — "I will destroy the fat and the strong" (KJV) / "I will watch over the fat and the strong" (ESV) — Textual variant: Hebrew "ashmir" can mean "destroy" (KJV tradition) or "watch/guard" (ESV). If "destroy": God judges the POWERFUL sheep who bullied the weak. Context (vv.17-22) supports this.

v.23 — "my servant David" (KJV/ESV) — Not literal resurrected David but the ideal Davidic king — the MESSIAH. "David" functions as a title for the future king from David's line.

v.25 — "evil beasts" (KJV/ESV) — Predators removed. Under the covenant of peace, the THREATS that scattered the flock are eliminated. Safety restored.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Failed Shepherds — Leaders who exploited rather than served God's people. Their self-interest produced the scattering (exile). Leadership malpractice under divine judgment.

God as Shepherd — When human leaders fail, God takes over PERSONALLY. The divine Shepherd does what human shepherds refused: seeks, gathers, feeds, heals, binds, protects.

My Servant David — The messianic king: one shepherd, from David's line, ruling with God's authority. Fulfilled in Christ ("I am the good shepherd," John 10:11).

Covenant of Peace — The new relationship between God and His restored flock: predators removed, fruitfulness restored, showers of blessing, safety and abundance.

Showers of Blessing — (v.26) God's abundant, seasonal provision. Not scarce or unpredictable but SHOWERING — overwhelming in quantity, perfectly timed.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Are you a self-feeding shepherd? (v.2). If you're in ANY leadership role (parenting, managing, pastoring, teaching), ask: am I feeding MYSELF or feeding those in my care? Leadership is SERVICE, not extraction.

2. The five failures (v.4). Strengthen the weak. Heal the sick. Bind the broken. Bring back the strayed. Seek the lost. This IS the job. If you're not doing these, you're not shepherding — you're just occupying space.

3. "I, even I" (v.11). When human shepherds fail, God doesn't give up on the sheep — He takes over PERSONALLY. If every human leader in your life has disappointed you, God hasn't resigned. He's stepping in.

4. ONE shepherd (v.23). Not multiple competing voices but ONE. Christ. If you're confused by too many "shepherds" (teachers, systems, gurus), simplify: follow the ONE shepherd. His voice is enough.

5. "Showers of blessing" (v.26). God's intention: ABUNDANCE, not scarcity. "In his season" = at the right time. Are you positioned under the cloud? Are you in the place where blessings fall?
