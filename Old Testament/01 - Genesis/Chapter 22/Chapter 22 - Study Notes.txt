Genesis - Chapter 22
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God tests Abraham by commanding him to sacrifice Isaac; Abraham obeys, God provides a ram, and the covenant is reaffirmed.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 22 — the Binding of Isaac (Hebrew: the Akedah) —
is one of the most powerful and theologically rich chapters in the entire
Bible. God tests Abraham by commanding him to sacrifice Isaac — the son of
promise, the miracle child, the one through whom all nations would be blessed.
Abraham obeys, and at the last moment God provides a ram as substitute. This
chapter is the supreme OT picture of the gospel: a father offering his only
son, a substitute provided, and the place named "The LORD Will Provide." The
location — Mount Moriah — is where Solomon's temple would later stand and
near where Christ was crucified.

The Test (vv.1-2): "God did tempt [test] Abraham" (v.1). The command: "Take
now thy son, thine only son Isaac, whom thou lovest... and offer him there
for a burnt offering" (v.2). Four intensifiers: thy son, thine only son,
Isaac (by name), whom thou lovest. God asks for everything.

The Obedience (vv.3-10): Abraham rises early — no delay (v.3). Three days'
journey (v.4). "I and the lad will go yonder and worship, and come again to
you" (v.5) — faith that God would raise Isaac (Heb 11:19). Isaac carries the
wood; Abraham carries fire and knife (v.6). Isaac's question: "Where is the
lamb?" (v.7). Abraham's prophetic answer: "God will provide himself a lamb"
(v.8). Abraham binds Isaac, lays him on the altar, raises the knife (vv.9-10).

The Provision (vv.11-14): "Abraham, Abraham" — the angel stops him (v.11).
"Now I know that thou fearest God, seeing thou hast not withheld thy son"
(v.12). A ram caught in a thicket — offered "in the stead of his son" (v.13).
Abraham names the place "Jehovah-jireh" — "The LORD Will Provide" (v.14).

The Oath (vv.15-18): God swears by Himself (v.16). Because Abraham obeyed:
blessing, multiplication (stars/sand), victory over enemies, and "in thy seed
shall all the nations of the earth be blessed" (vv.17-18).

Epilogue (vv.19-24): Abraham returns to Beer-sheba (v.19). News of Nahor's
family — including Rebekah (v.23) — preparing for chapter 24.

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
• Mount Moriah (v.2) — identified with the Temple Mount in Jerusalem
  (2 Chronicles 3:1). The place where Abraham nearly sacrificed Isaac is
  where Solomon later built the Temple — and near where Christ was crucified.
• "In the mount of the LORD it shall be seen" (v.14) — naming the site
  prophetically. The three-day journey from Beer-sheba to Moriah is
  approximately 50 miles north.

Charles Spurgeon:
  Sermon: "God Will Provide" on v.8 (MTP Vol. 14, No. 837)
  "God will provide HIMSELF a lamb — the most prophetic sentence in Genesis.
  Abraham spoke better than he knew. God DID provide Himself a lamb — not
  just any lamb but THE Lamb: His own Son. On this very mountain (Moriah =
  the temple mount = Calvary's vicinity), God provided Himself as the
  sacrifice. The Father gave the Son. The question 'Where is the lamb?' is
  answered at the cross: 'Behold the Lamb of God.'"
  Sermon: "The Binding of Isaac" on vv.9-12 (MTP Vol. 22, No. 1318)
  "Abraham bound Isaac — and Isaac submitted. Isaac was likely a young man
  (he carried the wood); Abraham was over 100. Isaac could have resisted.
  But he submitted to his father's will — as Christ submitted to His Father's
  will at Calvary. 'Not my will, but thine, be done.' The son willingly laid
  on the altar. The father willingly raised the knife. And God provided a
  substitute."

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1  — KJV: "tempt" / ESV: "tested" — Hebrew "nissah" — to test, to prove.
       ESV correctly uses "tested" to avoid confusion with temptation to sin
       (James 1:13). Testing to reveal and strengthen faith.

v.2  — KJV: "thine only son" / ESV: "your only son" — Both equivalent.
       Hebrew "yechideka" — your only one, your unique one. Ishmael existed
       but Isaac was the unique son of promise. Same language used of Christ:
       "only begotten" (John 3:16).

v.2  — KJV: "Moriah" / ESV: "Moriah" — Both identical. Hebrew "Moriyyah" —
       possibly "the LORD sees/provides" or "the LORD appears." The same
       mountain where Solomon built the temple (2 Chr 3:1). Near Golgotha
       where Christ was crucified.

v.5  — KJV: "come again to you" / ESV: "come again to you" — Both identical.
       Abraham expected to return WITH Isaac. Hebrews 11:19 explains: he
       believed God could raise Isaac from the dead. Faith in resurrection.

v.8  — KJV: "God will provide himself a lamb" / ESV: "God will provide for
       himself the lamb" — Hebrew "Elohim yireh lo haseh" — God will see to
       it / provide for Himself the lamb. ESV adds "for" making the grammar
       clearer but slightly reducing the ambiguity: God will provide (a lamb)
       for Himself, OR God will provide Himself (as the lamb). Both are true
       — fulfilled in Christ.

v.12 — KJV: "now I know" / ESV: "now I know" — Both identical. Not that God
       learned something new but that Abraham's faith was now demonstrated,
       proven, visible.

v.13 — KJV: "in the stead of his son" / ESV: "instead of his son" — Both
       equivalent. Hebrew "tachat beno" — in place of, instead of, as a
       substitute for. The clearest OT picture of substitutionary atonement.

v.14 — KJV: "Jehovah-jireh" / ESV: "The LORD will provide" — Hebrew "Yahweh
       Yireh" — "The LORD Will Provide" or "The LORD Will See (to it)." KJV
       transliterates the Hebrew compound name; ESV translates its meaning.

v.16 — KJV: "By myself have I sworn" / ESV: "By myself I have sworn" — Both
       equivalent. God swears by Himself because there is nothing greater to
       swear by (Heb 6:13). The highest possible guarantee.

TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
- v.1 — NET: "God tested Abraham" — same as ESV; WEB: "God tested Abraham";
  ASV: "God did prove Abraham." ASV's archaic "prove" (test/demonstrate)
  shows the older English meaning of proving through trial.
- v.2 — NET: "Take your son—your only son, whom you love, Isaac" — uses
  em-dashes to emphasize the escalating identification (son → only → beloved
  → named), making the emotional weight of the command typographically visible.
- v.10 — NET: "prepared to slaughter his son" — uses "slaughter" rather than
  "slay" (KJV/ASV) or "kill" (general), employing sacrificial language that
  connects to the burnt offering context.
- v.14 — NET: "In the mountain of the Lord provision will be made" — renders
  "Yahweh-yireh" in passive voice, while WEB has "In Yahweh's mountain, it
  will be provided." ASV: "In the mount of Jehovah it shall be provided."
- v.16 — NET: "I solemnly swear by my own name, decrees the Lord" — adds
  "decrees" as a speech formula, making the oath format explicit.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
The Akedah     — "The Binding" — the Jewish name for this event. Abraham
               binding Isaac on the altar. The supreme test of faith.

Mount Moriah   — The location of the test. Later the temple mount in
               Jerusalem. Near Calvary. Where God provides.

"God will provide himself a lamb" — Abraham's prophetic answer. Fulfilled
               partially in the ram (v.13) and ultimately in Christ (John
               1:29).

In the stead of — Substitution. The ram died instead of Isaac. Christ died
               instead of us. The heart of the gospel.

Jehovah-jireh — "The LORD Will Provide." God's compound name from this
               event. He sees the need and provides the solution.

"Thine only son, whom thou lovest" — Language echoing John 3:16. The Father
               giving His only, beloved Son. Abraham's test foreshadows
               God's sacrifice.

By myself have I sworn — God's oath on His own character. The highest
               possible guarantee of His promises.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Take now thy son, thine only son Isaac, whom thou lovest" (v.2). God
   asked for Abraham's most precious possession. Is there anything you're
   holding back from God? What is your "Isaac" — the thing you love most
   that God might be asking you to surrender?

2. "Abraham rose up early in the morning" (v.3). No delay, no negotiation.
   Immediate obedience to the hardest command. When God asks something
   difficult, do you obey immediately or procrastinate?

3. "God will provide himself a lamb" (v.8). This is the answer to every
   need: God will provide. He provided a ram for Abraham; He provided THE
   Lamb (Christ) for us. Do you trust His provision?

4. "In the stead of his son" (v.13). Substitution — the heart of the gospel.
   Christ died in YOUR place. The knife that should have fallen on you fell
   on Him. Have you received this substitution?

5. "Jehovah-jireh" (v.14). The LORD provides — on the mountain of need, at
   the moment of crisis. Where do you need God's provision today? He sees
   your need and He will provide.

6. This chapter is the gospel in picture form: a father offers his only
   beloved son on a mountain, a substitute is provided, and through this
   sacrifice all nations are blessed. See Christ in every detail.
