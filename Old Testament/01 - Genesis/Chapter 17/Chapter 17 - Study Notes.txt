Genesis - Chapter 17
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God reaffirms His covenant, renames Abram to Abraham and Sarai to Sarah, institutes circumcision, and promises a son within a year.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 17 records the covenant of circumcision — 13 years
after Ishmael's birth (Abram is now 99). God appears with a new name for
Himself (El Shaddai — God Almighty), new names for Abram (Abraham) and Sarai
(Sarah), a covenant sign (circumcision), and the specific promise that SARAH
will bear the son of promise — Isaac. This chapter formalizes the Abrahamic
covenant with its sign and specifies the heir.

God Almighty Appears (vv.1-2): "I am the Almighty God; walk before me, and
be thou perfect" (v.1). El Shaddai — the God who is sufficient for every
impossibility. "Perfect" = blameless, wholehearted.

The Covenant Expanded (vv.3-8): Abraham falls on his face (v.3). God's
promises: father of many nations (v.4), new name (v.5), exceedingly fruitful
(v.6), kings from him (v.6), everlasting covenant (v.7), the land of Canaan
as everlasting possession (v.8).

Circumcision — The Covenant Sign (vv.9-14): Every male circumcised at eight
days old (vv.10-12). A sign "in your flesh" of the everlasting covenant (v.13).
The uncircumcised are "cut off" — covenant-breakers (v.14).

Sarah's Promise (vv.15-22): Sarai renamed Sarah ("princess") (v.15). She will
bear a son (v.16). Abraham laughs — "Shall a child be born unto him that is
an hundred years old?" (v.17). He asks for Ishmael (v.18). God insists: Sarah
will bear Isaac (v.19). Ishmael blessed but the covenant is with Isaac (vv.20-21).

Immediate Obedience (vv.23-27): Abraham circumcises every male "in the
selfsame day" (v.23). No delay. Complete obedience.

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
• Abraham is still at Mamre/Hebron during this chapter. The covenant renewal
  takes place in the same region where he has settled (cf. 13:18).
• Ishmael's prophesied territory: "He shall dwell in the presence of all his
  brethren" (cf. 16:12) — the Arabian desert regions east and south of Canaan.

------------------------------------

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1  — Hebrew "El Shaddai... hithhallek lephanay vehyeh tamim"
       KJV: "I am the Almighty God; walk before me, and be thou perfect"
       ESV: "I am God Almighty; walk before me, and be blameless"
       ASV: "I am God Almighty; walk before me, and be thou perfect"
       NET: "I am the Sovereign God. Walk before me and be blameless"
       WEB: "I am God Almighty. Walk before me, and be blameless"
       Analysis: NET renders "El Shaddai" as "Sovereign God" — emphasizing
       authority over power, unlike "Almighty" (KJV/ESV/ASV/WEB). "Shaddai"
       possibly from "shad" (breast = all-sufficient nourisher) or "shadad"
       (to overpower). KJV/ASV's "perfect" can mislead — Hebrew "tamim"
       means wholehearted/complete, not sinless perfection. ESV/NET/WEB's
       "blameless" is clearer.

v.5  — Hebrew "Avraham" — father of a multitude (name change from Abram).
       KJV: "Neither shall thy name any more be called Abram, but thy name shall be Abraham"
       ESV: "No longer shall your name be called Abram, but your name shall be Abraham"
       ASV: "Neither shall thy name any more be called Abram, but thy name shall be Abraham"
       NET: "No longer will your name be Abram. Instead, your name will be Abraham"
       WEB: "Your name will no longer be called Abram, but your name will be Abraham"
       Analysis: All essentially equivalent. "Avraham" = "father of a
       multitude." God inserts the "ah" sound — from His own name (Yahweh)
       — into Abraham's. Identity redefined by promise.

v.9  — Hebrew "eth-beriti tishmor" — my covenant you shall keep.
       KJV: "Thou shalt keep my covenant"
       ESV: "you shall keep my covenant"
       ASV: "thou shalt keep my covenant"
       NET: "you must keep the covenantal requirement I am imposing on you"
       WEB: "you shall keep my covenant"
       Analysis: NET renders in explicitly legal language — "covenantal
       requirement...imposing" — making the obligation aspect clear.
       KJV/ESV/ASV/WEB all use the simple "keep my covenant."

v.11 — Hebrew "oth berith" — sign of the covenant.
       KJV: "it shall be a token of the covenant"
       ESV: "it shall be a sign of the covenant"
       ASV: "it shall be a token of a covenant"
       NET: "This will be a reminder of the covenant"
       WEB: "It will be a token of a covenant"
       Analysis: NET shifts from visual symbol ("sign/token") to memorial
       function ("reminder"). KJV/ASV/WEB use "token" (slightly archaic);
       ESV uses "sign." Circumcision: a physical mark in the flesh on the
       eighth day (new beginnings).

v.17 — Hebrew "vayyitschaq" — and he laughed.
       KJV: "Abraham fell upon his face, and laughed"
       ESV: "Abraham fell on his face and laughed"
       ASV: "Abraham fell upon his face, and laughed"
       NET: "Abraham bowed down with his face to the ground and laughed"
       WEB: "Abraham fell on his face, and laughed"
       Analysis: All translations agree. Hebrew "yitschaq" = he laughed —
       the root of "Isaac" (Yitschaq = "he laughs"). NET expands "fell on
       his face" to "bowed down with his face to the ground" for modern
       clarity. Both Abraham (here) and Sarah (18:12) laugh — and God names
       the child "Laughter."

v.19 — Hebrew "Yitschaq" — he laughs (Isaac).
       KJV: "thou shalt call his name Isaac"
       ESV: "You shall call his name Isaac"
       ASV: "thou shalt call his name Isaac"
       NET: "you will name him Isaac"
       WEB: "you shall call his name Isaac"
       Analysis: All identical in substance. Named by God before birth. The
       laughter of disbelief becomes the laughter of joy.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
El Shaddai     — God Almighty. The all-sufficient God. Enough for every
               impossibility.

Abraham        — "Father of a multitude." New name given by God, replacing
               Abram ("exalted father"). Identity redefined by promise.

Sarah          — "Princess." New name replacing Sarai. Mother of nations
               and kings.

Circumcision   — The physical sign of the Abrahamic covenant. Cut in the
               flesh on the eighth day. Marks covenant membership.

Isaac          — "He laughs." The promised son, named by God before
               conception. Born of impossibility through God's power.

Everlasting covenant — God's permanent, unconditional commitment to Abraham
               and his seed. Never revoked, never expired.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "I am the Almighty God" (v.1). Whatever impossibility you face, God is
   El Shaddai — the All-Sufficient One. Is there anything too hard for Him?
   What impossible situation do you need to entrust to the Almighty?

2. "Walk before me, and be thou perfect" (v.1). God calls for wholehearted
   living in His presence. Not sinless perfection but undivided devotion. Is
   your heart fully His?

3. God changed Abraham's name — giving him a new identity based on promise,
   not performance. In Christ, you have a new identity too. Are you living
   from your old name or your new one?

4. Abraham obeyed "in the selfsame day" (v.23). No delay, no negotiation.
   When God speaks, do you obey immediately? Or do you procrastinate?

5. Abraham laughed at God's promise (v.17). God didn't rebuke him — He named
   the child "Laughter." God can handle your honest doubts. Bring them to
   Him rather than hiding them.
