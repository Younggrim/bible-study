Genesis - Chapter 23
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Sarah dies at age 127; Abraham purchases the cave of Machpelah from the Hittites as a burial place — his first land ownership in Canaan.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 23 records Sarah's death at age 127 and Abraham's
purchase of the cave of Machpelah as a burial site. This is the only land
Abraham ever owned in Canaan — a burial cave. He lived as a "stranger and
sojourner" (v.4) his entire life in the Promised Land, owning nothing except
a grave. Yet this purchase was an act of faith: buying land in the country God
promised, declaring that his family belonged there permanently.

Sarah's Death (vv.1-2): Sarah dies at 127 in Hebron (Kirjath-arba) (v.1).
Abraham mourns and weeps (v.2). Sarah is the only woman in Scripture whose
age at death is recorded — honoring her significance.

The Land Purchase (vv.3-18): Abraham negotiates with the Hittites (sons of
Heth) for a burial site (vv.3-4). They offer freely (v.6). Abraham insists on
paying full price for the cave of Machpelah from Ephron (vv.8-9). Ephron
offers it free, then names a price: 400 shekels of silver (vv.11-15). Abraham
pays in full, publicly (v.16). The transaction is legally witnessed (vv.17-18).

Sarah's Burial (vv.19-20): Abraham buries Sarah in the cave of Machpelah
near Mamre/Hebron (v.19). The field is legally secured as Abraham's possession
(v.20).

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
• Machpelah (v.9) — the cave and field near Hebron (also called Kirjath-arba,
  v.2) purchased as a burial site. Still identified today as the Cave of the
  Patriarchs in modern Hebron.
• This is the first piece of the Promised Land that Abraham actually owns —
  purchased from Ephron the Hittite for 400 shekels of silver (v.16).
• Hebron is approximately 19 miles south of Jerusalem in the Judean hill
  country.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.4  — "I am a sojourner and foreigner among you; give me property among you
        for a burying place." (ESV) — Abraham's pilgrim identity and faith
        claim.

v.16 — "Abraham weighed out for Ephron the silver... four hundred shekels."
        (ESV) — Full price paid; no obligation to anyone.

v.19 — "Abraham buried Sarah his wife in the cave of the field of Machpelah."
        (ESV) — The only land Abraham owned: a grave in the Promised Land.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.2  — KJV: "Kirjath-arba" / ESV: "Kiriath-arba" — Both equivalent
       (transliteration difference). "City of Four" (possibly four giants or
       four quarters). Later called Hebron. One of the oldest continuously
       inhabited cities in the world.

v.4  — KJV: "stranger and sojourner" / ESV: "sojourner and foreigner" —
       Hebrew "ger vetoshab" — a resident alien and temporary dweller. ESV
       reverses the order and uses "foreigner" for "sojourner." Abraham had
       no legal land rights.

v.6  — KJV: "a mighty prince" / ESV: "a prince of God" — Hebrew "nesi
       Elohim" — a prince of God. ESV is more literal; KJV interprets
       "Elohim" as an intensifier ("mighty"). The Hittites recognized
       Abraham's stature.

v.15 — KJV: "four hundred shekels" / ESV: "four hundred shekels" — Both
       identical. A high price. Jeremiah later bought a field for 17 shekels
       (Jer 32:9). Ephron's "generosity" was actually shrewd pricing.

v.17 — KJV: "were made sure" / ESV: "was made over" — Hebrew "qum" —
       established, confirmed. Both convey legal transfer. KJV emphasizes
       certainty; ESV emphasizes the transfer of ownership. A legal transfer
       with witnesses — binding and permanent.

TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
- v.4 — NET: "I am a foreign resident, a temporary settler, among you" —
  uses two descriptors to translate "ger vetoshab" (KJV: "stranger and
  sojourner"), emphasizing Abraham's legal status as a non-landowner.
- v.6 — NET: "you are a mighty prince among us" — renders Hebrew "nesi
  Elohim" (KJV: "a mighty prince" / ASV: "a prince of God") similarly.
  WEB: "a prince of God among us" — retains the literal "of God" connection.
- v.9 — NET: "Let him sell it to me publicly for the full price" — adds
  "publicly" to emphasize the legal/witness requirement of the transaction.
- v.16 — NET: "according to the standard measurement at the time" — adds
  an explanatory note about the weight standard, clarifying ancient commerce.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Machpelah      — "Double cave." The burial site Abraham purchased near
               Hebron. Became the family tomb for Abraham, Sarah, Isaac,
               Rebekah, Jacob, and Leah.

Stranger and sojourner — Abraham's self-description. A pilgrim with no
               permanent home in the land God promised. Faith without sight.

Made sure      — Legally confirmed. The purchase was public, witnessed, and
               permanent — Abraham's only land ownership in Canaan.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Abraham owned nothing in the Promised Land except a grave — yet he lived
   by faith that God's promise was certain. Can you live with unfulfilled
   promises, trusting God's timing?

2. Abraham insisted on paying full price (v.16). He wouldn't be indebted to
   anyone for what God promised. Where do you need to maintain integrity in
   how you receive God's provision?

3. Sarah's death reminds us that even the faithful die. But Abraham's
   purchase of a burial site was an act of faith: "We belong here. God's
   promise is certain." How do you express faith in the face of death?

4. Abraham was a "stranger and sojourner" (v.4). Are you living as a pilgrim
   — or have you settled too comfortably in this world?
