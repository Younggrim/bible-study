Genesis - Chapter 9
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God establishes a covenant with Noah, sets the rainbow as its sign, and gives new commands for post-flood humanity.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 9 records God's covenant with Noah — the first
explicit covenant in Scripture. God re-commissions humanity (echoing 1:28),
establishes new regulations for the post-flood world (permission to eat meat,
prohibition of blood, capital punishment for murder), and seals His promise
with the rainbow. The chapter also records Noah's failure (drunkenness) and
the resulting prophecy over his three sons — shaping the rest of biblical
history.

The Noahic Covenant (vv.1-17):
- Re-commission: "Be fruitful, and multiply, and replenish the earth" (v.1).
- New authority: animals now fear humans (v.2).
- New diet: permission to eat meat (v.3) — but not blood (v.4).
- Sanctity of life: murder requires capital punishment because man bears God's
  image (vv.5-6).
- The covenant promise: never again a universal flood (v.11).
- The sign: the rainbow (vv.12-17).

Noah's Failure (vv.20-23): Noah plants a vineyard, gets drunk, lies naked in
his tent (vv.20-21). Ham "saw the nakedness of his father" and told his
brothers (v.22) — likely involving more than just seeing (the Hebrew may imply
disrespect or exploitation). Shem and Japheth cover their father respectfully
(v.23).

The Prophecy (vv.24-27): Noah curses Canaan (Ham's son) — "a servant of
servants" (v.25). Blesses Shem — "Blessed be the LORD God of Shem" (v.26) —
the Messianic line. Blesses Japheth — "God shall enlarge Japheth, and he
shall dwell in the tents of Shem" (v.27) — Gentile inclusion.

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
• No specific geography — the focus is on God's covenant with Noah and the
  post-flood commands for all humanity.
• The prophecy over Shem, Ham, and Japheth (vv.25-27) sets up the Table of
  Nations (ch.10) and the geographical spread of peoples.

        re-commissioned after the flood.

        (ESV) — God's covenant promise.

        covenant between me and the earth." (ESV) — The rainbow as covenant
        sign.

        identified.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1  — KJV: "replenish the earth" / ESV: "fill the earth" — Hebrew "mil'u"
       — to fill. Same word and same issue as 1:28. ESV's "fill" is more
       accurate; KJV's "replenish" in 1611 meant "fill" but modern readers
       may misread it as "refill."

v.3  — KJV: "every moving thing that liveth shall be meat for you" / ESV:
       "Every moving thing that lives shall be food for you" — Hebrew
       "oklah" — food. KJV's "meat" meant "food" in 1611 (not specifically
       animal flesh). ESV's "food" avoids confusion. Before the flood,
       humanity was apparently vegetarian (1:29). Now meat is permitted —
       but not blood.

v.4  — KJV: "flesh with the life thereof, which is the blood thereof, shall
       ye not eat" / ESV: "But you shall not eat flesh with its life, that
       is, its blood" — Both equivalent. Blood represents life. Consuming
       blood is prohibited because life belongs to God. This prohibition
       predates and transcends the Mosaic Law.

v.5  — KJV: "at the hand of every man's brother will I require the life of
       man" / ESV: "From his fellow man I will require a reckoning for the
       life of man" — Hebrew "darash" — to seek, require, demand an
       accounting. ESV's "require a reckoning" makes the judicial sense
       clearer.

v.6  — KJV: "Whoso sheddeth man's blood, by man shall his blood be shed" /
       ESV: "Whoever sheds the blood of man, by man shall his blood be shed"
       — Both equivalent. The institution of capital punishment. The basis:
       the image of God. To murder a human is to assault God's image.

v.9  — KJV: "with your seed after you" / ESV: "your offspring after you" —
       Hebrew "zera" — seed, offspring. ESV's "offspring" is clearer for
       modern readers.

v.12 — KJV: "the token of the covenant" / ESV: "the sign of the covenant"
       — Hebrew "oth" — sign, token, mark. ESV's "sign" is more natural
       modern English; KJV's "token" is slightly archaic.

v.13 — KJV: "I do set my bow in the cloud" / ESV: "I have set my bow in
       the cloud" — Both equivalent. Hebrew "qesheth" — a war bow. God sets
       His weapon in the sky as a sign of peace. The bow points upward
       (toward God), not downward (toward earth).

v.20 — KJV: "Noah began to be an husbandman" / ESV: "Noah began to be a man
       of the soil" — Hebrew "ish ha'adamah" — a man of the ground/soil.
       ESV is more literal; KJV's "husbandman" (farmer) is archaic English.

v.22 — KJV: "saw the nakedness of his father" / ESV: "saw the nakedness of
       his father" — Both identical. Hebrew "ra'ah eth ervath" — the exact
       nature of Ham's sin is debated. At minimum: disrespect and
       exploitation of his father's vulnerability. He publicized what should
       have been covered.

v.25 — KJV: "Cursed be Canaan; a servant of servants" / ESV: "Cursed be
       Canaan; a servant of servants shall he be to his brothers" — Both
       equivalent. Noah curses Canaan (Ham's son), not Ham directly. This
       prophecy is fulfilled when Israel conquers Canaan. It is NOT a
       justification for racial slavery — it refers to specific nations, not
       races.

v.27 — KJV: "God shall enlarge Japheth, and he shall dwell in the tents of
       Shem" / ESV: "May God enlarge Japheth, and let him dwell in the tents
       of Shem" — Hebrew "yapht" — a wordplay on Japheth's name (enlarge/
       make spacious). ESV renders it as a wish/prayer ("May God..."); KJV
       as a declaration ("God shall..."). Both are valid readings of the
       Hebrew jussive form. Japheth's descendants will share in Shem's
       spiritual blessings — fulfilled as Gentiles are included in the
       Abrahamic covenant through Christ.

TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
- v.2 — NET: "are under your authority" — interprets "into your hand are they
  delivered" (ASV) as a statement of authority/dominion, making the governance
  concept explicit where KJV/ESV use physical "hand" imagery.
- v.12 — NET: "This is the guarantee of the covenant" — uniquely renders
  Hebrew "oth" (sign/token) as "guarantee," emphasizing the legal/binding
  nature of the covenant sign rather than merely visual.
- v.13 — WEB: "I set my rainbow in the cloud" — uses "rainbow" explicitly
  where KJV/ASV have simply "bow" and ESV "my bow." WEB makes the referent
  immediately clear for modern readers.
- v.26 — NET: "Worthy of praise is the Lord, the God of Shem!" — an
  interpretive expansion of "Blessed be the LORD God of Shem" (KJV/ESV),
  rendering the Hebrew blessing formula as a declaration of God's
  praiseworthiness rather than a wish for God to be blessed.
- v.27 — NET: "May God enlarge Japheth's territory and numbers!" — adds
  "territory and numbers" to make explicit what the enlargement entails,
  going beyond the Hebrew text which simply says "enlarge Japheth."

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Noahic Covenant — God's unconditional covenant with all creation: never
               again a universal flood. Signed with the rainbow.

Image of God as basis for sanctity of life — Murder is wrong because humans
               bear God's image. The foundation of human rights and justice.

The rainbow    — God's covenant sign. A war bow laid down — pointing toward
               heaven. A visible promise of mercy.

Blood prohibition — Blood represents life and belongs to God. Not to be
               consumed — a principle predating the Mosaic Law.

Capital punishment — Human government authorized to execute murderers. Based
               on the image of God, not mere social contract.

Shem, Ham, Japheth — Noah's three sons from whom all nations descend. Shem
               = Messianic line (Semites). Ham = Canaanites, Africans.
               Japheth = Europeans, Indo-Europeans.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "I do set my bow in the cloud" (v.13). Every rainbow is God preaching His
   faithfulness. Do you notice? Do you remember His promises when you see it?

2. "For in the image of God made he man" (v.6). Every human being bears God's
   image — regardless of race, status, or condition. How does this shape how
   you treat people?

3. Noah — the righteous man who walked with God — got drunk and was shamed
   (vv.20-21). Even the godly can fall. Are you guarding against complacency?
   Past faithfulness doesn't guarantee future obedience.

4. Shem and Japheth covered their father's shame (v.23). When someone you
   respect fails, do you expose them (like Ham) or cover them with grace
   (like Shem and Japheth)?

5. "God shall enlarge Japheth, and he shall dwell in the tents of Shem"
   (v.27). Gentiles sharing in Israel's spiritual blessings — fulfilled in
   Christ. If you're a Gentile believer, you are living in this prophecy.
