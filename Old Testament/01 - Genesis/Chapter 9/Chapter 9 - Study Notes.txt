Genesis - Chapter 9
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God establishes a covenant with Noah, sets the rainbow as its sign, and gives new commands for post-flood humanity.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 9 records God's covenant with Noah — the first
explicit covenant in Scripture. God re-commissions humanity (echoing 1:28),
establishes new regulations for the post-flood world (permission to eat meat,
prohibition of blood, capital punishment for murder), and seals His promise
with the rainbow. The chapter also records Noah's failure (drunkenness) and
the resulting prophecy over his three sons — shaping the rest of biblical
history.

The Noahic Covenant (vv.1-17):
- Re-commission: "Be fruitful, and multiply, and replenish the earth" (v.1).
- New authority: animals now fear humans (v.2).
- New diet: permission to eat meat (v.3) — but not blood (v.4).
- Sanctity of life: murder requires capital punishment because man bears God's
  image (vv.5-6).
- The covenant promise: never again a universal flood (v.11).
- The sign: the rainbow (vv.12-17).

Noah's Failure (vv.20-23): Noah plants a vineyard, gets drunk, lies naked in
his tent (vv.20-21). Ham "saw the nakedness of his father" and told his
brothers (v.22) — likely involving more than just seeing (the Hebrew may imply
disrespect or exploitation). Shem and Japheth cover their father respectfully
(v.23).

The Prophecy (vv.24-27): Noah curses Canaan (Ham's son) — "a servant of
servants" (v.25). Blesses Shem — "Blessed be the LORD God of Shem" (v.26) —
the Messianic line. Blesses Japheth — "God shall enlarge Japheth, and he
shall dwell in the tents of Shem" (v.27) — Gentile inclusion.

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
• No specific geography — the focus is on God's covenant with Noah and the
  post-flood commands for all humanity.
• The prophecy over Shem, Ham, and Japheth (vv.25-27) sets up the Table of
  Nations (ch.10) and the geographical spread of peoples.

        identified.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1  — Hebrew "mil'u eth-ha'arets" — fill the earth.
       KJV: "Be fruitful, and multiply, and replenish the earth"
       ESV: "Be fruitful and multiply and fill the earth"
       ASV: "Be fruitful, and multiply, and replenish the earth"
       NET: "Be fruitful and multiply and fill the earth"
       WEB: "Be fruitful, multiply, and fill the earth"
       Analysis: Same issue as 1:28. KJV/ASV's "replenish" in 1611 meant
       "fill" but modern readers may misread it as "refill." ESV/NET/WEB
       correctly use "fill" for the Hebrew "mil'u."

v.2  — Hebrew "beyedkhem nittanu" — into your hand they are delivered.
       KJV: "into your hand are they delivered"
       ESV: "into your hand they are delivered"
       ASV: "into your hand are they delivered"
       NET: "they are under your authority"
       WEB: "they are delivered into your hand"
       Analysis: NET interprets physical "hand" imagery as "authority/
       dominion" — making the governance concept explicit. All others retain
       the concrete Hebrew imagery.

v.6  — Hebrew "shophekh dam ha'adam ba'adam damo yishaphekh"
       KJV: "Whoso sheddeth man's blood, by man shall his blood be shed"
       ESV: "Whoever sheds the blood of man, by man shall his blood be shed"
       ASV: "Whoso sheddeth man's blood, by man shall his blood be shed"
       NET: "Whoever sheds human blood, by other humans will his blood be shed"
       WEB: "Whoever sheds man's blood, his blood will be shed by man"
       Analysis: All essentially identical — the institution of capital
       punishment. NET uses "human/humans" for the generic "adam." The basis:
       "for God made man in his own image" — to murder is to assault God's
       image. The poetic chiastic structure (A-B-B-A) is preserved in all.

v.12 — Hebrew "oth habberith" — the sign of the covenant.
       KJV: "the token of the covenant"
       ESV: "the sign of the covenant"
       ASV: "the token of the covenant"
       NET: "the guarantee of the covenant"
       WEB: "the sign of the covenant"
       Analysis: NET uniquely renders "oth" as "guarantee" — emphasizing the
       legal/binding nature of the covenant sign rather than merely visual.
       KJV/ASV's "token" is slightly archaic; ESV/WEB's "sign" is standard.

v.13 — Hebrew "qashti natatti be'anan" — my bow I have set in the cloud.
       KJV: "I do set my bow in the cloud"
       ESV: "I have set my bow in the cloud"
       ASV: "I do set my bow in the cloud"
       NET: "I will place my rainbow in the clouds"
       WEB: "I set my rainbow in the cloud"
       Analysis: WEB/NET use "rainbow" explicitly where KJV/ESV/ASV have
       simply "bow." Hebrew "qesheth" = a war bow. God sets His weapon in
       the sky as a sign of peace — the bow points upward (toward God), not
       downward (toward earth). WEB/NET make the referent immediately clear.

v.25 — Hebrew "arur Kena'an eved avadim yihyeh le'echav"
       KJV: "Cursed be Canaan; a servant of servants shall he be unto his brethren"
       ESV: "Cursed be Canaan; a servant of servants shall he be to his brothers"
       ASV: "Cursed be Canaan; A servant of servants shall he be unto his brethren"
       NET: "Cursed be Canaan! The lowest of slaves he will be to his brothers"
       WEB: "Canaan is cursed. He will be a servant of servants to his brothers"
       Analysis: NET renders "eved avadim" (servant of servants) as "lowest
       of slaves" — a superlative interpretation. WEB restructures as a
       statement rather than a curse formula. Noah curses Canaan (Ham's son),
       not Ham directly. This is NOT a justification for racial slavery — it
       refers to specific nations.

v.26 — Hebrew "barukh Yahweh Elohey Shem"
       KJV: "Blessed be the LORD God of Shem"
       ESV: "Blessed be the LORD, the God of Shem"
       ASV: "Blessed be Jehovah, the God of Shem"
       NET: "Worthy of praise is the Lord, the God of Shem!"
       WEB: "Blessed be Yahweh, the God of Shem"
       Analysis: NET's "Worthy of praise" is an interpretive expansion —
       rendering the blessing formula as a declaration of praiseworthiness.
       ASV uses "Jehovah"; WEB uses "Yahweh." God is identified specifically
       with Shem's line — the messianic line.

v.27 — Hebrew "yapht Elohim leYepheth" — God enlarge Japheth (wordplay on name).
       KJV: "God shall enlarge Japheth, and he shall dwell in the tents of Shem"
       ESV: "May God enlarge Japheth, and let him dwell in the tents of Shem"
       ASV: "God enlarge Japheth, And let him dwell in the tents of Shem"
       NET: "May God enlarge Japheth's territory and numbers! May he live in the tents of Shem"
       WEB: "May God enlarge Japheth. Let him dwell in the tents of Shem"
       Analysis: "Yapht" is a wordplay on Japheth's name (enlarge/make
       spacious). NET adds "territory and numbers" — going beyond the Hebrew.
       ESV renders as wish/prayer ("May..."); KJV as declaration ("shall").
       Japheth's descendants share in Shem's spiritual blessings — Gentiles
       included through Christ.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Noahic Covenant — God's unconditional covenant with all creation: never
               again a universal flood. Signed with the rainbow.

Image of God as basis for sanctity of life — Murder is wrong because humans
               bear God's image. The foundation of human rights and justice.

The rainbow    — God's covenant sign. A war bow laid down — pointing toward
               heaven. A visible promise of mercy.

Blood prohibition — Blood represents life and belongs to God. Not to be
               consumed — a principle predating the Mosaic Law.

Capital punishment — Human government authorized to execute murderers. Based
               on the image of God, not mere social contract.

Shem, Ham, Japheth — Noah's three sons from whom all nations descend. Shem
               = Messianic line (Semites). Ham = Canaanites, Africans.
               Japheth = Europeans, Indo-Europeans.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "I do set my bow in the cloud" (v.13). Every rainbow is God preaching His
   faithfulness. Do you notice? Do you remember His promises when you see it?

2. "For in the image of God made he man" (v.6). Every human being bears God's
   image — regardless of race, status, or condition. How does this shape how
   you treat people?

3. Noah — the righteous man who walked with God — got drunk and was shamed
   (vv.20-21). Even the godly can fall. Are you guarding against complacency?
   Past faithfulness doesn't guarantee future obedience.

4. Shem and Japheth covered their father's shame (v.23). When someone you
   respect fails, do you expose them (like Ham) or cover them with grace
   (like Shem and Japheth)?

5. "God shall enlarge Japheth, and he shall dwell in the tents of Shem"
   (v.27). Gentiles sharing in Israel's spiritual blessings — fulfilled in
   Christ. If you're a Gentile believer, you are living in this prophecy.
