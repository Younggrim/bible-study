Genesis - Chapter 43
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The brothers return to Egypt with Benjamin; Joseph hosts them at a feast but continues to conceal his identity.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 43 records the brothers' second trip to Egypt —
this time with Benjamin. The famine forces Jacob's hand: he must send Benjamin
or the family starves. Judah steps forward as surety — a dramatic change from
the man who sold Joseph (37:26-27). The chapter builds tension: Will Joseph
reveal himself? Will the brothers protect Benjamin? Joseph hosts them at a
feast, weeps privately at seeing Benjamin, and tests them further.

Judah's Surety (vv.1-14): The grain runs out (v.1). Jacob: "Go again, buy us
a little food" (v.2). Judah: we cannot go without Benjamin (vv.3-5). Jacob
blames them for mentioning Benjamin (v.6). Judah offers himself as surety:
"I will be surety for him; of my hand shalt thou require him" (v.9). Jacob
relents: "If I be bereaved of my children, I am bereaved" (v.14) — resignation
that is also faith.

The Journey (vv.15-25): They take double money, gifts, and Benjamin (v.15).
Joseph sees Benjamin and orders a feast (v.16). The brothers are afraid —
think it's about the money (vv.18). They explain to the steward about the
returned money (vv.20-22). The steward: "Your God, and the God of your father,
hath given you treasure" (v.23). Simeon released (v.23).

The Feast (vv.26-34): The brothers bow before Joseph — again (v.26). Joseph
asks about their father (v.27). He sees Benjamin: "God be gracious unto thee,
my son" (v.29). Joseph weeps privately — "his bowels did yearn upon his
brother" (v.30). They feast together. Joseph seats them in birth order — they
marvel (v.33). Benjamin receives five times more food (v.34). "They drank,
and were merry with him" (v.34).

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
• The same Canaan ↔ Egypt trade route — the brothers return to Egypt a second
  time, now with Benjamin. The journey takes several days each way.
• Joseph's house in Egypt — the brothers dine there. Joseph is now a high
  Egyptian official with a residence befitting his station.

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------
Enduring Word (David Guzik):
  https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/genesis-43/
  Guzik covers Judah's surety, Jacob's surrender, the feast, and Joseph's
  emotion at seeing Benjamin.

Charles Spurgeon:
  Sermon: "Judah's Surety" on v.9 (MTP Vol. 20, No. 1216)
  "I will be SURETY for him — Judah, who once sold his brother for profit,
  now offers himself as guarantee for his brother's safety. This is
  transformation. This is repentance made visible. The man who said 'What
  profit is it if we slay our brother?' (37:26) now says 'Let me bear the
  blame for ever.' From seller to surety — from exploiter to protector. This
  is what grace does. And Judah foreshadows Christ: the one from Judah's
  tribe who became surety for us all."

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "I will be surety for him" (v.9). Judah has changed — from seller to
   surety. Has YOUR character changed over the years? Can people see genuine
   transformation in you?

2. "If I be bereaved of my children, I am bereaved" (v.14). Jacob surrenders
   to God's will — whatever the cost. Can you say this? Can you release your
   grip on what you love most and trust God?

3. "Your God... hath given you treasure" (v.23). Even outsiders can see God's
   provision in your life. Is God's blessing visible to those around you?

4. Joseph wept at seeing Benjamin (v.30). Love is powerful and emotional.
   Are you moved by love for your family — even those who've hurt you?

5. Benjamin received five times more (v.34). Joseph tested whether the
   brothers would be jealous. They passed — "they drank, and were merry."
   How do you respond when others receive more than you? With jealousy or
   with joy?
