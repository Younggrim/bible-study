Genesis - Chapter 40
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Joseph interprets dreams for Pharaoh's cupbearer and baker in prison; the cupbearer is restored but forgets Joseph.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 40 records Joseph's time in prison — interpreting
dreams for Pharaoh's butler (cupbearer) and baker. This chapter is the bridge
between Joseph's suffering (chs.37-39) and his exaltation (ch.41). The dreams
demonstrate that God gives Joseph supernatural wisdom — preparing him for the
moment when Pharaoh will need a dream interpreter. But the chapter ends with
painful words: "yet did not the chief butler remember Joseph, but forgat him"
(v.23). Two more years of waiting (41:1).

The Butler and Baker Imprisoned (vv.1-4): Pharaoh's chief butler and chief
baker offend the king (v.1). Imprisoned in the same facility as Joseph (v.3).
Joseph is assigned to serve them (v.4).

The Dreams (vv.5-8): Both dream on the same night (v.5). Joseph notices their
sadness: "Wherefore look ye so sadly to day?" (v.7). They have no interpreter
(v.8). Joseph: "Do not interpretations belong to God? tell me them" (v.8) —
giving God the credit before he even hears the dreams.

The Butler's Dream Interpreted (vv.9-15): A vine with three branches bearing
grapes; the butler presses them into Pharaoh's cup (vv.9-11). Joseph: three
branches = three days; you'll be restored (vv.12-13). Joseph's request:
"Think on me... make mention of me unto Pharaoh" (v.14). "I was stolen away...
and here also have I done nothing" (v.15).

The Baker's Dream Interpreted (vv.16-19): Three baskets of bread on his head;
birds eat from the top basket (vv.16-17). Joseph: three baskets = three days;
you'll be hanged and birds will eat your flesh (vv.18-19). Honest even when
the news is bad.

Fulfillment and Forgetfulness (vv.20-23): On Pharaoh's birthday, both dreams
come true exactly (vv.20-22). "Yet did not the chief butler remember Joseph,
but forgat him" (v.23).

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
• Egypt — Joseph remains in the royal prison. The setting is entirely
  Egyptian, likely near the pharaoh's court (Nile Delta region).
• The chapter's geography is institutional rather than territorial — the
  prison is the world Joseph inhabits, yet God is with him even there.

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------
Enduring Word (David Guzik):
  https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/genesis-40/
  Guzik covers the prison dreams, Joseph's God-centered interpretation,
  the fulfillment, and the butler's forgetfulness.

Charles Spurgeon:
  Sermon: "Forgotten Joseph" on v.23 (MTP Vol. 14, No. 844)
  "The chief butler FORGAT him — after Joseph had served him, interpreted his
  dream, and asked only to be remembered. Forgotten. Two more years in prison
  because a man forgot. But God did not forget. Human memory fails; divine
  memory never does. The butler forgot — but God remembered. And when God's
  time came, the butler's memory was jogged (41:9). God uses even human
  forgetfulness to accomplish His timing."
  Sermon: "Interpretations Belong to God" on v.8 (MTP Vol. 22, No. 1330)
  "Do not interpretations belong to GOD? — Joseph gives credit before he
  performs. He doesn't say 'I can interpret' but 'God can.' This is the mark
  of a Spirit-filled man: pointing to God, not self. Every gift, every
  ability, every insight — belongs to God. Use it, but give Him the glory."

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. "Do not interpretations belong to God?" (v.8). When you have ability or
   insight, do you point to God or to yourself? Every gift belongs to Him.
   Give credit where it's due.

2. "Yet did not the chief butler remember Joseph, but forgat him" (v.23).
   Have you ever been forgotten by someone who promised to help? It hurts.
   But God doesn't forget. Human memory fails; divine memory never does.

3. Joseph served others even in prison (v.4) and noticed their sadness (v.7).
   Even in your own suffering, can you serve others and notice their pain?
   Ministry doesn't wait for ideal circumstances.

4. Joseph told the truth even when it was bad news (v.19). Are you honest
   even when the truth is uncomfortable? Integrity means speaking truth
   regardless of the response.

5. Two more years of waiting (41:1) because a man forgot. God's timing often
   includes delays we don't understand. Can you trust Him in the "forgotten"
   seasons? He hasn't forgotten you.
