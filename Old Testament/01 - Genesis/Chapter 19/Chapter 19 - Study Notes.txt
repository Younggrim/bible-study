Genesis - Chapter 19
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God destroys Sodom and Gomorrah with fire and brimstone; Lot escapes but his wife looks back and becomes a pillar of salt.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 19 records the destruction of Sodom and Gomorrah
— one of the most dramatic judgment narratives in Scripture. The two angels
from chapter 18 arrive in Sodom, experience the city's depravity firsthand,
rescue Lot and his daughters, and rain fire from heaven. The chapter shows the
end result of Lot's choices (13:12 — "pitched his tent toward Sodom"): he's
now IN Sodom, AT the gate (a position of civic leadership), and barely escapes
with his life. His wife looks back and becomes a pillar of salt. The chapter
ends with Lot's shameful cave episode — showing how Sodom corrupted even the
"righteous" man who lived there.

The Angels in Sodom (vv.1-11): Lot sits at Sodom's gate (v.1). He offers
hospitality (vv.2-3). The men of Sodom surround the house demanding the
visitors (vv.4-5). Lot offers his daughters instead (v.8 — showing how
corrupted his values have become). The angels rescue Lot and strike the mob
with blindness (vv.10-11).

The Rescue (vv.12-22): Angels warn Lot to gather his family (v.12). His
sons-in-law think he's joking (v.14). Lot lingers — the angels physically
drag him out (v.16). "The LORD being merciful unto him" (v.16). Told to flee
to the mountains; Lot negotiates for Zoar (vv.17-22).

The Destruction (vv.23-29): "The LORD rained upon Sodom and upon Gomorrah
brimstone and fire from the LORD out of heaven" (v.24). Total destruction
(v.25). Lot's wife looks back — becomes a pillar of salt (v.26). Abraham sees
the smoke from afar (vv.27-28). "God remembered Abraham, and sent Lot out"
(v.29) — Lot saved because of Abraham's intercession.

Lot's Disgrace (vv.30-38): Lot and daughters flee to a cave (v.30). Daughters
get Lot drunk and commit incest (vv.31-36). Result: Moab and Ammon — nations
that will trouble Israel for centuries (vv.37-38).

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
• Sodom and Gomorrah — located in the plain of the Jordan, likely near the
  southern end of the Dead Sea. Destroyed by "brimstone and fire" (v.24).
  The region remains desolate to this day.
• Zoar (v.22) — a small city at the edge of the plain where Lot initially
  flees. Its name means "small" (Lot pleads it is a "little one").
• The mountains east of the Dead Sea (v.30) — where Lot ultimately settles
  in a cave. His descendants (Moab, Ammon) inhabit modern Jordan.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.14 — "He seemed to his sons-in-law to be jesting." (ESV) — Lot had no
        credibility. His compromised life made his warning unbelievable.

v.16 — "But he lingered. So the men seized him and his wife and his two
        daughters by the hand, the LORD being merciful to him." (ESV) —
        Mercy dragging the reluctant to safety.

v.24 — "Then the LORD rained on Sodom and Gomorrah sulfur and fire from the
        LORD out of heaven." (ESV) — Divine judgment by fire.

v.26 — "But Lot's wife, behind him, looked back, and she became a pillar of
        salt." (ESV) — The cost of looking back.

v.29 — "God remembered Abraham and sent Lot out of the midst of the
        overthrow." (ESV) — Lot saved because of Abraham's intercession.

Charles Spurgeon:
  Sermon: "Remember Lot's Wife" on v.26 (MTP Vol. 7, No. 398)
  "She LOOKED BACK — just a look. Not a return, not a full rebellion — just
  a backward glance. But that glance revealed her heart: it was still in
  Sodom. Her body left but her heart stayed. Jesus said: 'Remember Lot's
  wife' (Luke 17:32). You can leave sin physically while your heart remains
  attached. A backward look can be fatal. Don't look back at what God has
  delivered you from."
  Sermon: "Lingering" on v.16 (MTP Vol. 11, No. 641)
  "While he LINGERED — judgment is coming, angels are urging, time is running
  out — and Lot lingers. He hesitates. He delays. He's reluctant to leave
  what he's invested in. How many sinners linger at the edge of salvation —
  knowing they should flee but reluctant to leave their Sodom? Don't linger.
  Judgment doesn't wait for the reluctant."

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1  — Hebrew "yoshev basha'ar" — sitting in the gate (civic authority).
       KJV: "Lot sat in the gate"
       ESV: "Lot was sitting in the gate of Sodom"
       ASV: "Lot sat in the gate of Sodom"
       NET: "Lot was sitting in the gateway of Sodom"
       WEB: "Lot sat in the gate of Sodom"
       Analysis: All translations are equivalent. The gate was the place of civic
       authority and business. Lot had risen to a position of influence in Sodom —
       fully integrated into the city he once only pitched his tent "toward."

v.5  — Hebrew "neda" — to know (used here in the sexual sense).
       KJV: "that we may know them"
       ESV: "that we may know them"
       ASV: "that we may know them"
       NET: "so we can take carnal knowledge of them"
       WEB: "that we may know them"
       Analysis: KJV, ESV, ASV, and WEB retain the Hebrew euphemism "know them,"
       preserving the biblical idiom. NET renders the Hebrew with its explicit sexual
       meaning ("carnal knowledge"), removing all ambiguity about the nature of
       Sodom's sin. The men demanded sexual access to the visitors.

v.9  — Hebrew "wayyiggash lishpot" — and he presses close to judge.
       KJV: "and he will needs be a judge"
       ESV: "This fellow... would keep acting as a judge"
       ASV: "and he will needs be a judge"
       NET: "This man came to live here as a foreigner, and now he dares to judge us!"
       WEB: "and he wants to be a judge"
       Analysis: NET adds "dares to" for emphasis, capturing the mob's outrage at
       Lot's moral authority as a newcomer. The mob resents a foreigner exercising
       moral judgment — showing Sodom's hostility to righteousness.

v.16 — Hebrew "yithmahemah" — hesitated, delayed, was reluctant.
       KJV: "while he lingered"
       ESV: "But he lingered"
       ASV: "But he lingered"
       NET: "When Lot hesitated"
       WEB: "But he lingered"
       Analysis: All translations capture the reluctance. Even with angels urging
       and judgment imminent, Lot was slow to leave. His heart was attached to
       Sodom. The mercy of God physically dragged him to safety (v.16b).

v.24 — Hebrew "gophrith wa'esh" — sulfur/brimstone and fire.
       KJV: "brimstone and fire from the LORD out of heaven"
       ESV: "sulfur and fire from the LORD out of heaven"
       ASV: "brimstone and fire from Jehovah out of heaven"
       NET: "the Lord rained down sulfur and fire...sent down from the sky by the Lord"
       WEB: "sulfur and fire from Yahweh out of the sky"
       Analysis: KJV/ASV use archaic "brimstone" (burning stone); ESV/NET/WEB use
       modern "sulfur." Same substance. Note "from the LORD" — divine origin, not
       natural disaster. NET and WEB use "sky" for "heaven" in this context.

v.26 — Hebrew "tabbeth" — looked intently, gazed back.
       KJV: "looked back"
       ESV: "looked back"
       ASV: "looked back from behind him"
       NET: "looked back longingly"
       WEB: "looked back from behind him"
       Analysis: KJV/ESV use the simple phrase. ASV/WEB add "from behind him,"
       specifying her position. NET uniquely adds "longingly" — an interpretive
       addition not in the Hebrew but capturing the emotional attachment to Sodom.
       More than a glance — a longing look. Her heart was still in Sodom. "Pillar
       of salt" — instantaneous judgment for disobedience.

v.29 — Hebrew "wayyizkor Elohim eth-Avraham" — God remembered Abraham.
       KJV: "God remembered Abraham"
       ESV: "God remembered Abraham"
       ASV: "God remembered Abraham"
       NET: "God remembered Abraham"
       WEB: "God remembered Abraham"
       Analysis: All five translations are identical. Lot was saved not because of
       his own righteousness but because of Abraham's intercession. "Remembered"
       carries the active-intervention sense — God acted on Abraham's behalf. The
       power of a righteous person's prayer for others.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Sodom's gate   — The place of civic authority. Lot had fully integrated into
               Sodom's society — from "toward" (13:12) to "in" (14:12) to
               "at the gate" (19:1).

Lingered       — Lot's reluctance to leave despite imminent judgment. His
               heart was attached to what he should have fled.

Brimstone and fire — God's judgment on Sodom. Sulfur and fire from heaven —
               total, irreversible destruction.

Looked back    — Lot's wife's fatal backward glance. Her body left Sodom but
               her heart remained. Disobedience with fatal consequences.

God remembered Abraham — Lot saved through Abraham's intercession. The power
               of a righteous person's prayer for others.

Pillar of salt — Lot's wife's fate. Instantaneous judgment for looking back.
               A permanent memorial to the danger of divided loyalty.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Lot's progression: toward Sodom (13:12) → in Sodom (14:12) → at Sodom's
   gate (19:1). Sin is progressive. Where are you in the progression? What
   started as "toward" may now be "in." Stop before you reach the gate.

2. "He seemed as one that mocked" (v.14). Lot's compromised life destroyed
   his credibility. When he finally spoke truth, no one believed him. Does
   your life give weight to your words? Or has compromise made your witness
   unbelievable?

3. "While he lingered" (v.16). Are you lingering when you should be fleeing?
   What are you reluctant to leave that God is calling you out of?

4. "Remember Lot's wife" (Luke 17:32). A backward look revealed a backward
   heart. When God delivers you from something, don't look back longingly.
   What are you tempted to look back at?

5. "God remembered Abraham, and sent Lot out" (v.29). Your prayers for others
   have power. Abraham's intercession saved Lot. Who are you interceding for?
   Don't stop praying.
