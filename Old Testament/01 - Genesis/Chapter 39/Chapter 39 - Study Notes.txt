Genesis - Chapter 39
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Joseph serves in Potiphar's house and prospers, but is falsely accused by Potiphar's wife and thrown into prison — yet God remains with him.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 39 resumes Joseph's story in Egypt. Sold as a
slave to Potiphar, Joseph rises to manage the entire household — because "the
LORD was with Joseph" (repeated four times: vv.2, 3, 21, 23). Then comes the
great test: Potiphar's wife's repeated sexual advances. Joseph refuses — not
because of consequences but because "how then can I do this great wickedness,
and sin against God?" (v.9). Falsely accused, he's imprisoned — but even
there, "the LORD was with Joseph" (v.21). The chapter demonstrates that
faithfulness doesn't prevent suffering but God's presence persists through it.

Joseph in Potiphar's House (vv.1-6): "The LORD was with Joseph" — he prospers
(v.2). Potiphar sees God's blessing and promotes Joseph over everything (vv.3-
4). "The LORD blessed the Egyptian's house for Joseph's sake" (v.5). Joseph
is given total authority (v.6). He is "goodly" and "well favoured" (v.6).

The Temptation (vv.7-12): Potiphar's wife: "Lie with me" (v.7). Joseph
refuses — three reasons: (1) his master trusts him (v.8), (2) she is another
man's wife (v.9), (3) it would be sin against GOD (v.9). She persists "day by
day" (v.10). He avoids her (v.10). One day she grabs his garment — he flees,
leaving it behind (vv.11-12).

The False Accusation (vv.13-20): She uses the garment as "evidence" (vv.13-
15). Tells the household servants (vv.14-15). Tells Potiphar (vv.17-18).
Potiphar's "wrath was kindled" — Joseph imprisoned (vv.19-20).

Joseph in Prison (vv.21-23): "The LORD was with Joseph, and shewed him mercy"
(v.21). He gains favor with the prison keeper (v.21). Put in charge of all
prisoners (v.22). "The LORD was with him, and that which he did, the LORD made
it to prosper" (v.23).

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
• Egypt — Joseph is now in the house of Potiphar, an officer of Pharaoh,
  likely in or near the capital city (possibly in the Nile Delta region,
  where later pharaohs had residences).
• The Egyptian prison — Joseph is placed in the royal prison where the king's
  prisoners are held, indicating proximity to the seat of government.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.2  — "The LORD was with Joseph, and he became a successful man." (ESV) —
        God's presence = prosperity (not always material, but always real).

v.9  — "How then can I do this great wickedness and sin against God?" (ESV)
        — Joseph's motivation for purity: God-consciousness.

v.12 — "He left his garment in her hand and fled and got out of the house."
        (ESV) — Fleeing temptation.

v.21 — "But the LORD was with Joseph and showed him steadfast love and gave
        him favor." (ESV) — God's presence in prison.

v.23 — "The LORD was with him. And whatever he did, the LORD made it
        succeed." (ESV) — Prosperity even in prison.

Charles Spurgeon:
  Sermon: "Joseph's Coat" on v.12 (MTP Vol. 14, No. 843)
  "He LEFT his garment — and FLED. This is the only way to handle sexual
  temptation: flee. Not negotiate, not reason, not linger — flee. Joseph
  didn't stay to argue. He didn't try to explain. He ran. And he lost his
  coat — again (cf. 37:23). Joseph keeps losing garments — stripped by
  brothers, stripped by Potiphar's wife. But God keeps clothing him with
  something better. Sometimes you must lose your coat to keep your character."
  Sermon: "Sin Against God" on v.9 (MTP Vol. 20, No. 1214)
  "How can I do this great wickedness, and sin against GOD? Not 'sin against
  my master' (though it was). Not 'sin against her husband' (though it was).
  Not 'sin against myself' (though it was). Sin against GOD. This is the
  ultimate deterrent: God-consciousness. When you see sin as primarily
  against God — not just against people or yourself — you have the strongest
  motivation for purity."

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.2  — KJV: "the LORD was with Joseph, and he was a prosperous man" / ESV:
       "The LORD was with Joseph, and he became a successful man" — KJV
       "prosperous" vs. ESV "successful." Hebrew "Yahweh eth Yoseph." The
       key phrase of the chapter — repeated four times (vv.2, 3, 21, 23).
       God's presence is the explanation for everything.

v.6  — KJV: "goodly person, and well favoured" / ESV: "handsome in form and
       appearance" — ESV modernizes the description. Hebrew "yepheh to'ar
       wipheh mar'eh" — beautiful in form and appearance. The same
       description used of Rachel (29:17). Joseph's attractiveness made him
       a target.

v.9  — KJV: "how then can I do this great wickedness, and sin against God?"
       / ESV: "How then can I do this great wickedness and sin against God?"
       — Both identical. Joseph sees all sin as ultimately against God. This
       God-ward perspective is the foundation of his integrity.

v.12 — KJV: "fled, and got him out" / ESV: "fled and got out of the house"
       — Both equivalent. Hebrew "yanas" — to flee, to escape. The same word
       used for fleeing from danger. Sexual temptation is treated as mortal
       danger — you run from it.

v.20 — KJV: "the prison" / ESV: "the prison" — Both identical. Hebrew "beth
       hassohar" — the house of the roundness (a round fortress). A royal
       prison for political prisoners — not a common jail.

v.21 — KJV: "shewed him mercy" / ESV: "showed him steadfast love" — ESV
       renders "chesed" as "steadfast love" (its standard translation for
       this word) rather than KJV's "mercy." Hebrew "chesed" — loyal love,
       covenant faithfulness. Even in prison, God's covenant love surrounds
       Joseph.

TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
- v.6 — WEB: "Now Joseph was well-built and handsome" — renders Hebrew
  "yepheh-to'ar vipheh mar'eh" directly. NET: "Now Joseph was well built and
  good-looking." KJV's "goodly person, and well favoured" is archaic.
- v.7 — NET: "Come to bed with me" — direct modern rendering repeated
  throughout (vv.7,12). KJV/ASV: "Lie with me." NET removes the euphemism.
- v.9 — NET: "how could I do such a great evil and sin against God?" — same
  across translations but NET emphasizes the question form. Joseph frames
  the refusal theologically, not merely socially.
- v.2 — NET: "The Lord was with Joseph. He was successful" — WEB: "Yahweh
  was with Joseph, and he was a prosperous man." The fourfold repetition
  of "the LORD/Yahweh was with Joseph" (vv.2,3,21,23) is emphasized across
  all translations as the chapter's theological key.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
"The LORD was with Joseph" — The chapter's theme (4x). God's presence as
               the explanation for prosperity, integrity, and perseverance.

"Sin against God" — Joseph's motivation for purity. All sin is ultimately
               against God — this perspective produces resistance.

Flee temptation — Joseph's strategy: run. Not negotiate, not linger — flee.
               The only effective response to sexual temptation.

False accusation — Suffering for doing right. Joseph is punished for his
               integrity. Faithfulness doesn't prevent injustice.

God's presence in prison — The LORD was with Joseph even in unjust
               imprisonment. God's presence is not limited by circumstances.

Prosperity in adversity — Joseph prospers in slavery and in prison. God's
               blessing operates regardless of external conditions.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "The LORD was with Joseph" (vv.2, 3, 21, 23). God's presence is the key
   to everything — prosperity, integrity, endurance. Is God's presence the
   defining reality of YOUR life?

2. "How then can I do this great wickedness, and sin against God?" (v.9).
   Joseph's motivation for purity was God-consciousness. When tempted, do you
   think about consequences (getting caught) or about God (sinning against
   Him)? Which is a stronger deterrent?

3. "He... fled" (v.12). Joseph ran from temptation. He didn't negotiate or
   linger. When sexual temptation comes, do you flee or flirt? Running is not
   weakness — it's wisdom.

4. Joseph did right and was punished for it (vv.19-20). Faithfulness doesn't
   guarantee fair treatment. Are you willing to suffer unjustly for doing
   right? God sees — and He's still with you.

5. "The LORD was with him" — in Potiphar's house AND in prison (vv.2, 21).
   God's presence doesn't depend on your location or circumstances. He is
   with you in the palace and in the pit. Where do you need to know His
   presence today?
