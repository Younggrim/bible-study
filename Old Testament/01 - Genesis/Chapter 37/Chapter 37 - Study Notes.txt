Genesis - Chapter 37
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Jacob favors Joseph with a special coat; Joseph's dreams of greatness provoke his brothers to sell him into slavery in Egypt.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 37 begins the Joseph narrative (chs.37-50) — the
longest continuous story in Genesis and one of the greatest narratives in all
of literature. Joseph is the most detailed type of Christ in the OT: beloved
son, sent by his father, rejected by his brothers, sold for silver, falsely
accused, exalted to save the world. This chapter introduces the conflict:
Jacob's favoritism, Joseph's dreams, and the brothers' hatred that leads to
Joseph being sold into slavery.

Joseph the Favorite (vv.1-4): Joseph is 17, tending flocks (v.2). He brings
a bad report about his brothers (v.2). "Israel loved Joseph more than all his
children" — gives him a "coat of many colours" (v.3). The brothers hate him
and "could not speak peaceably unto him" (v.4).

Joseph's Dreams (vv.5-11): Dream 1: sheaves bowing to Joseph's sheaf (vv.5-8).
The brothers: "Shalt thou indeed reign over us?" — they hate him more (v.8).
Dream 2: sun, moon, and eleven stars bowing to him (v.9). Even Jacob rebukes
him — but "observed the saying" (vv.10-11). The brothers envy him (v.11).

The Plot Against Joseph (vv.12-28): Jacob sends Joseph to check on his
brothers at Shechem (vv.13-14). He finds them at Dothan (v.17). They conspire
to kill him: "Behold, this dreamer cometh" (vv.18-20). Reuben intervenes:
"Let us not kill him" — plans to rescue him later (vv.21-22). They strip his
coat and throw him in a pit (vv.23-24). Judah suggests selling him to
Ishmaelites (vv.26-27). Sold for 20 pieces of silver (v.28).

The Deception (vv.29-36): Reuben returns — Joseph is gone (vv.29-30). The
brothers dip the coat in goat's blood (v.31). Jacob is deceived — "an evil
beast hath devoured him" (v.33). He mourns inconsolably (vv.34-35). Joseph
is sold to Potiphar in Egypt (v.36).

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
• Hebron (v.14) — Jacob's home, where Joseph is sent from. Located in the
  southern Judean hill country.
• Shechem (v.12) — approximately 50 miles north of Hebron, where the brothers
  are initially pasturing their flocks.
• Dothan (v.17) — about 15 miles north of Shechem, on a major trade route
  connecting Mesopotamia to Egypt. The Ishmaelite/Midianite caravan passes
  through here carrying spices to Egypt.
• Joseph's journey to Egypt follows the ancient Via Maris trade route — the
  main commercial highway of the ancient Near East.

        hatred.

        Hatred born of jealousy.

        of Joseph's God-given destiny.

v.20 — "Let us see what will become of his dreams." (ESV) — Human plans vs.
        God's purposes.

v.28 — "They sold Joseph to the Ishmaelites for twenty shekels of silver."
        (ESV) — The beloved son sold by his brothers.

v.36 — "The Midianites had sold him in Egypt to Potiphar." (ESV) — God's
        sovereignty: what brothers meant for evil, God meant for good.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.2  — KJV: "generations of Jacob" / ESV: "generations of Jacob" — Both
       identical. The tenth toledoth. Though titled "Jacob's generations,"
       the story is about Joseph. Jacob's legacy IS Joseph's story.

v.3  — KJV: "coat of many colours" / ESV: "robe of many colors" — Both
       equivalent (coat vs. robe). Hebrew "ketonet passim" — a long-sleeved,
       ornamental tunic. Possibly multi-colored or full-length (reaching
       palms and soles). A garment of distinction — marking Joseph as the
       favored heir.

v.11 — KJV: "observed the saying" / ESV: "kept the saying in mind" — ESV
       clarifies the meaning. Hebrew "shamar" — kept, guarded, pondered.
       Jacob recognized the dreams might be from God.

v.20 — KJV: "this dreamer" / ESV: "this dreamer" — Both identical. Hebrew
       "ba'al hachalomot" — literally "master of dreams" or "this lord of
       dreams." Spoken mockingly.

v.28 — KJV: "twenty pieces of silver" / ESV: "twenty shekels of silver" —
       ESV specifies the unit of currency (shekels). The price of a young
       male slave in the ancient Near East (cf. Leviticus 27:5). Jesus was
       sold for thirty (the price of an adult slave, Exodus 21:32).

v.31 — KJV: "killed a kid of the goats, and dipped the coat in the blood" /
       ESV: "slaughtered a goat and dipped the robe in the blood" — Both
       equivalent. Poetic justice: Jacob deceived Isaac with a goat (27:9);
       now his sons deceive him with a goat. You reap what you sow.

v.36 — KJV: "Potiphar, an officer of Pharaoh's" / ESV: "Potiphar, an
       officer of Pharaoh, the captain of the guard" — ESV adds the
       clarifying title. Egyptian name meaning "he whom Ra (the sun god)
       has given." Captain of Pharaoh's guard. Joseph enters the household
       of one of Egypt's most powerful men.

TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
- v.2 — NET: "Joseph brought back a bad report about them to their father" —
  all translations agree on "bad/evil report." NET's "bad report" is closest
  to the Hebrew "dibbatam ra'ah."
- v.3 — NET: "he made a special tunic for him" — renders Hebrew "ketonet
  passim" as "special tunic" without committing to "many colours" (KJV) or
  "of many colors" (ESV). WEB: "a coat of many colors." The Hebrew "passim"
  is uncertain: long-sleeved, richly ornamented, or multicolored.
- v.20 — NET: "we'll see how his dreams turn out!" — colloquial and ironic,
  where KJV has "we shall see what will become of his dreams."
- v.35 — NET: "So his father wept for him" — renders Jacob's grief. WEB uses
  the same basic rendering across translations. The irony: Jacob who deceived
  his own father is now deceived by his sons.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Coat of many colours — A distinctive garment marking Joseph as Jacob's
               favored heir. The visible symbol of favoritism that provoked
               hatred.

Joseph as type of Christ — The most detailed OT foreshadowing of Christ:
               beloved son, sent by father, rejected by brothers, sold for
               silver, falsely accused, exalted to save.

"This dreamer cometh" — The brothers' mockery of God-given vision. Human
               attempts to destroy divine purpose always fail.

Twenty pieces of silver — The price of a young slave. Joseph sold by his
               brothers as Christ was sold by Judas.

"We shall see what will become of his dreams" — Human defiance of God's
               revealed plan. The answer: the dreams come true — exactly.

Sold into Egypt — What brothers meant for destruction, God used for
               salvation. The beginning of God's plan to preserve Israel
               through famine.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Jacob's favoritism (v.3) produced hatred (v.4). If you're a parent, are
   you showing favoritism? It always damages. If you experienced favoritism
   (or its absence), how has it affected you?

2. "We shall see what will become of his dreams" (v.20). The brothers tried
   to destroy God's purpose — and failed. Has anyone tried to destroy what
   God has spoken over your life? God's purposes are indestructible.

3. Joseph was sold by his own brothers (v.28). Betrayal by family is the
   deepest wound. If you've experienced betrayal, know that God can use even
   this for good (50:20).

4. The brothers used a goat to deceive Jacob (v.31) — just as Jacob used a
   goat to deceive Isaac (27:9). You reap what you sow. What seeds are you
   planting that might come back to you?

5. Joseph's story begins with a pit (v.24) and ends with a palace (41:40).
   Your current "pit" is not your final destination. God is working — even
   when you can't see it.
