Genesis - Chapter 14
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Abram rescues Lot from a coalition of kings, then receives a blessing from the mysterious priest-king Melchizedek.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 14 is the first battle narrative in Scripture and
introduces one of the Bible's most mysterious figures: Melchizedek. Four
eastern kings raid the Jordan plain, capturing Lot (who now lives IN Sodom,
v.12 — he's moved from "toward" Sodom in 13:12). Abram rescues Lot with 318
trained servants, then encounters two kings: Melchizedek (who blesses him) and
the king of Sodom (whose offer he refuses). The chapter reveals Abram as
warrior, rescuer, worshipper, and man of integrity.

The War of the Kings (vv.1-12): Four eastern kings (led by Chedorlaomer of
Elam) defeat five kings of the Jordan plain (including Sodom and Gomorrah).
They plunder the cities and capture Lot (v.12).

Abram's Rescue (vv.13-16): Abram hears of Lot's capture (v.13). He arms 318
trained servants (v.14). Night attack — pursues the enemy to Dan and beyond
Damascus (vv.14-15). Recovers everything: goods, people, and Lot (v.16).

Melchizedek (vv.18-20): "King of Salem, priest of the most high God" (v.18).
Brings bread and wine (v.18). Blesses Abram (v.19). Abram gives him a tithe
of everything (v.20). A type of Christ: king-priest, no recorded genealogy,
blesses God's people (cf. Hebrews 7).

The King of Sodom's Offer (vv.21-24): "Give me the persons, and take the
goods" (v.21). Abram refuses everything — "lest thou shouldest say, I have
made Abram rich" (v.23). God alone will be his provider.

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
• The five cities of the plain: Sodom, Gomorrah, Admah, Zeboiim, and Zoar
  (v.2) — all near the southern Dead Sea region.
• The invading kings come from Shinar (Babylonia), Ellasar, Elam (modern
  Iran), and Goiim — a coalition from the east.
• Dan (v.14) — the northernmost extent of the land, showing Abram's military
  reach. Located at the foot of Mount Hermon.
• Salem (v.18) — identified with Jerusalem (Psalm 76:2). Melchizedek rules
  here. First biblical mention of this city.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.13 — Hebrew "Avram ha-Ivri" — Abram the Hebrew/one from beyond.
       KJV: "Abram the Hebrew"
       ESV: "Abram the Hebrew"
       ASV: "Abram the Hebrew"
       NET: "Abram the Hebrew"
       WEB: "Abram, the Hebrew"
       Analysis: All identical. "Ha-Ivri" = the one from beyond (the river).
       First use of "Hebrew" in Scripture. Identifies Abram as a foreigner —
       from beyond the Euphrates.

v.13 — Hebrew "ba'aley berith Avram" — lords/partners of Abram's covenant.
       KJV: "these were confederate with Abram"
       ESV: "these were allies of Abram"
       ASV: "these were confederate with Abram"
       NET: "these were allied by treaty with Abram"
       WEB: "These were allies of Abram"
       Analysis: NET specifies "by treaty" — making the formal political
       arrangement explicit. KJV/ASV's "confederate" is archaic. ESV/WEB's
       "allies" is clear and modern. Hebrew "ba'aley berith" = covenant
       partners.

v.14 — Hebrew "vayyareq eth-chanikav" — he armed/mobilized his trained men.
       KJV: "he armed his trained servants, born in his own house, three hundred and eighteen"
       ESV: "he led out his trained men, born in his house, 318 of them"
       ASV: "he led forth his trained men, born in his house, three hundred and eighteen"
       NET: "he mobilized his 318 trained men who had been born in his household"
       WEB: "he led out his trained men, born in his house, three hundred eighteen"
       Analysis: NET's "mobilized" captures military urgency better than
       "armed" (KJV) or "led out" (ESV/ASV/WEB). Hebrew "chanikav" =
       trained/dedicated ones — a private militia of considerable size.

v.18 — Hebrew "Malki-tsedeq melekh Shalem kohen le-El Elyon"
       KJV: "Melchizedek king of Salem... the priest of the most high God"
       ESV: "Melchizedek king of Salem... priest of God Most High"
       ASV: "Melchizedek king of Salem... priest of God Most High"
       NET: "Melchizedek king of Salem... priest of the Most High God"
       WEB: "Melchizedek king of Salem... priest of God Most High"
       Analysis: All essentially equivalent. "Malki-tsedeq" = "my king is
       righteousness." "Salem" (Shalem) = peace. Later = Jerusalem (Psalm
       76:2). "El Elyon" = God Most High. Melchizedek served the true God
       before Israel existed. A type of Christ: king-priest without recorded
       genealogy (Hebrews 7).

v.19 — Hebrew "qoneh shamayim va'arets" — possessor/creator of heaven and earth.
       KJV: "the most high God, the possessor of heaven and earth"
       ESV: "God Most High, Possessor of heaven and earth"
       ASV: "God Most High, possessor of heaven and earth"
       NET: "the Most High God, Creator of heaven and earth"
       WEB: "God Most High, possessor of heaven and earth"
       Analysis: NET boldly interprets "qoneh" as "Creator" where all others
       use "possessor/Possessor." The Hebrew can mean either — "to acquire,
       possess, create." NET's choice emphasizes God's creative sovereignty.
       God owns everything — the basis for Abram's refusal of Sodom's goods.

v.20 — Hebrew "ma'aser mikkol" — a tenth of everything.
       KJV: "he gave him tithes of all"
       ESV: "Abram gave him a tenth of everything"
       ASV: "he gave him a tenth of all"
       NET: "Abram gave Melchizedek a tenth of everything"
       WEB: "Abram gave him a tenth of all"
       Analysis: ESV/NET make explicit who gave and who received. KJV/ASV/WEB
       use pronoun "him." All convey the voluntary tithe — predating the
       Mosaic law by centuries. Abram acknowledges Melchizedek's superiority.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Melchizedek    — King of Salem, priest of God Most High. A type of Christ:
               king-priest without recorded genealogy. Blesses Abram and
               receives his tithe.

El Elyon       — "God Most High." The supreme God, possessor of heaven and
               earth. Above all other claimed deities.

Tithe          — A tenth given voluntarily to God's priest. Abram's tithe
               predates the Mosaic Law — it's a response of worship.

King of Salem  — King of peace. Salem = Jerusalem. Melchizedek ruled the
               city that would become Israel's capital and temple site.

Abram the Hebrew — "The one from beyond." Abram identified as a foreigner —
               from beyond the Euphrates. A stranger in Canaan.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Lot's choice (13:12) led to captivity (14:12). Bad decisions have
   consequences. Where might your "pitching toward Sodom" lead you?

2. Abram risked everything to rescue Lot — despite Lot's foolish choices.
   Are you willing to help those who've made bad decisions? Grace doesn't
   wait for people to deserve rescue.

3. Melchizedek appears with bread and wine — blessing and refreshment after
   battle. Christ meets you after your struggles with sustenance and blessing.
   Are you receiving from Him?

4. Abram refused Sodom's wealth (vv.22-23). He wouldn't let a pagan king
   take credit for God's provision. Where are you tempted to accept provision
   from sources that would compromise your testimony?

5. "I am thy shield, and thy exceeding great reward" (15:1 — immediately
   after this refusal). When you refuse worldly wealth for God's sake, God
   Himself becomes your reward. Is He enough?
