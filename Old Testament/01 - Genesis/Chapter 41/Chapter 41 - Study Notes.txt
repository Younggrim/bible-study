Genesis - Chapter 41
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Pharaoh dreams of seven fat and seven lean cows; Joseph interprets the dreams, is elevated to second-in-command, and prepares Egypt for famine.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 41 is Joseph's exaltation — from prison to palace
in a single day. After 13 years of suffering (sold at 17, exalted at 30),
God's timing arrives. Pharaoh dreams; no one can interpret. The butler finally
remembers Joseph. Joseph interprets the dreams (seven years of plenty followed
by seven years of famine), proposes a plan, and is immediately elevated to
second-in-command over all Egypt. The chapter demonstrates God's sovereign
timing: years of preparation for a moment of promotion.

Pharaoh's Dreams (vv.1-8): Two years after chapter 40 (v.1). Pharaoh dreams
of seven fat cows eaten by seven thin cows, and seven full ears consumed by
seven thin ears (vv.2-7). Egypt's magicians cannot interpret (v.8).

The Butler Remembers (vv.9-13): "I do remember my faults this day" (v.9). He
tells Pharaoh about Joseph (vv.10-13).

Joseph Before Pharaoh (vv.14-36): Joseph is rushed from prison — shaved,
changed (v.14). Pharaoh: "I have heard say of thee, that thou canst
understand a dream" (v.15). Joseph: "It is not in me: God shall give Pharaoh
an answer" (v.16). Joseph interprets: seven years plenty, seven years famine
(vv.25-31). "The dream was doubled... the thing is established by God" (v.32).
Joseph proposes a plan: appoint a wise man, store 20% during plenty (vv.33-36).

Joseph Exalted (vv.37-45): Pharaoh: "Can we find such a one as this is, a man
in whom the Spirit of God is?" (v.38). Joseph made ruler over all Egypt (v.41).
Given Pharaoh's ring, fine linen, gold chain, second chariot (vv.42-43). Named
Zaphnath-paaneah; given Asenath as wife (v.45). Joseph is 30 years old (v.46).

The Seven Years of Plenty (vv.46-52): Joseph gathers grain "as the sand of
the sea" (v.49). Two sons born: Manasseh ("God made me forget my toil") and
Ephraim ("God made me fruitful in affliction") (vv.51-52).

The Famine Begins (vv.53-57): Famine in all lands (v.54). "Go unto Joseph;
what he saith to you, do" (v.55). All countries come to Joseph (v.57).

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
• Egypt — Joseph rises from prison to Pharaoh's court. The entire chapter is
  set in Egypt, likely in the capital (possibly Memphis or a Delta city).
• The famine affects "all lands" (v.57) — nations come to Egypt to buy grain,
  including from Canaan. Egypt's Nile-fed agriculture made it the breadbasket
  of the ancient Near East during regional droughts.
• Joseph travels "over all the land of Egypt" (v.46) during the seven years
  of plenty, organizing storage in every city.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.16 — "It is not in me; God will give Pharaoh a favorable answer." (ESV) —
        Joseph deflects glory to God.

v.32 — "And the doubling of Pharaoh's dream means that the thing is fixed by
        God, and God will shortly bring it about." (ESV) — God's certainty.

v.38 — "Can we find a man like this, in whom is the Spirit of God?" (ESV) —
        Pharaoh recognizes God's Spirit in Joseph.

v.40 — "You shall be over my house, and all my people shall order themselves
        as you command." (ESV) — From prison to palace.

v.51-52 — "Manasseh... Ephraim" (ESV) — God made me forget my pain; God
        made me fruitful in affliction.

v.55 — "Go to Joseph. What he says to you, do." (ESV) — All must go through
        Joseph. A type of Christ.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1  — Hebrew "shenathayim yamim" — two full years of days.
       KJV: "two full years"
       ESV: "After two whole years"
       ASV: "at the end of two full years"
       NET: "After two full years"
       WEB: "At the end of two full years"
       Analysis: All equivalent. Two more years after the butler's release.
       Total time in prison: approximately 10-11 years. Total time from sale
       to exaltation: 13 years.

v.8  — Hebrew "chartummim" — magicians/diviner-priests.
       KJV: "magicians"
       ESV: "magicians"
       ASV: "magicians"
       NET: "all the diviner-priests of Egypt and all its wise men"
       WEB: "magicians"
       Analysis: NET renders "chartummim" as "diviner-priests" rather than
       "magicians," specifying their religious-divinatory function. All other
       translations use the traditional "magicians."

v.16 — Hebrew "bil'aday" — apart from me, not from me.
       KJV: "It is not in me: God shall give Pharaoh an answer of peace"
       ESV: "It is not in me; God will give Pharaoh a favorable answer"
       ASV: "It is not in me; God will give Pharaoh an answer of peace"
       NET: "It is not within my power...God will address Pharaoh's welfare"
       WEB: "It isn't in me. God will give Pharaoh an answer of peace"
       Analysis: KJV "answer of peace" vs. ESV "favorable answer." NET
       renders Joseph's deflection with "it is not within my power" more
       explicitly. Joseph consistently deflects credit to God (40:8, 41:16,
       41:25, 28).

v.38 — Hebrew "ruach Elohim" — the Spirit of God.
       KJV: "the Spirit of God"
       ESV: "the Spirit of God"
       ASV: "the Spirit of God"
       NET: "Can we find a man like this, one in whom the Spirit of God dwells?"
       WEB: "the Spirit of God"
       Analysis: All translations identical in substance. Even a pagan Pharaoh
       recognizes the divine Spirit in Joseph. NET adds "dwells" for emphasis.

v.43 — Hebrew "abrek" — possibly Egyptian for "kneel" or "attention."
       KJV: "Bow the knee"
       ESV: "Bow the knee!"
       ASV: "Bow the knee"
       NET: "Kneel down!"
       WEB: "Bow the knee!"
       Analysis: All equivalent. The Hebrew "abrek" may be Egyptian in origin.
       All Egypt bows to Joseph — a foreshadowing of Philippians 2:10.

v.45 — Hebrew/Egyptian "Zaphnath-paaneah" — meaning debated: possibly "God
       speaks and lives" or "sustainer of life."
       KJV: "Zaphnath-paaneah"
       ESV: "Zaphenath-paneah"
       ASV: "Zaphenath-paneah"
       NET: "Zaphenath-Paneah"
       WEB: "Zaphenath-Paneah"
       Analysis: Minor spelling variations in transliteration. NET adds
       explanatory notes about the probable Egyptian meaning ("the god speaks
       and he lives" or "sustainer of life").

v.46 — Hebrew "ben-sheloshim shanah" — son of thirty years.
       KJV: "Joseph was thirty years old"
       ESV: "Joseph was thirty years old"
       ASV: "Joseph was thirty years old"
       NET: "Joseph was thirty years old"
       WEB: "Joseph was thirty years old"
       Analysis: All identical. Sold at 17 (37:2). Thirteen years of
       suffering between the pit and the palace. Same age Jesus began His
       ministry (Luke 3:23).

v.51 — Hebrew "Menasheh" — causing to forget.
       KJV: "Manasseh"
       ESV: "Manasseh"
       ASV: "Manasseh"
       NET: "Manasseh"
       WEB: "Manasseh"
       Analysis: All identical. "Causing to forget." God healed Joseph's pain
       — He made him forget the toil and hardship of his father's house.

v.52 — Hebrew "Ephrayim" — double fruitfulness.
       KJV: "Ephraim"
       ESV: "Ephraim"
       ASV: "Ephraim"
       NET: "Ephraim"
       WEB: "Ephraim"
       Analysis: All identical. "Double fruitfulness." God made Joseph
       fruitful in the very land of his affliction.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
"It is not in me" — Joseph's consistent deflection of glory to God. All
               wisdom and ability come from God, not self.

From prison to palace — God's sovereign timing: years of hidden preparation
               followed by sudden, dramatic promotion.

Manasseh and Ephraim — Joseph's sons named for God's healing (forgetting
               pain) and God's blessing (fruitfulness in affliction).

"Go unto Joseph" — All must go through Joseph for bread. A type of Christ:
               all must go through Him for life.

Thirteen years — The gap between Joseph's dreams (age 17) and their
               fulfillment (age 30). God's preparation takes time.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "It is not in me: God shall give Pharaoh an answer" (v.16). When you
   succeed, do you deflect glory to God or absorb it yourself? Joseph
   consistently pointed to God.

2. Thirteen years from pit to palace. God's preparation often takes longer
   than we expect. Are you in a "preparation season"? Trust that God is
   training you for something.

3. "Manasseh... Ephraim" (vv.51-52). God can make you forget your pain AND
   make you fruitful in the place of your affliction. Have you experienced
   this? Can you trust Him for it?

4. "Go unto Joseph; what he saith to you, do" (v.55). All must go through
   Joseph for bread. All must go through Christ for life. Are you going to
   Him? Are you doing what He says?

5. Joseph was 30 when exalted — the same age Jesus began His ministry (Luke
   3:23). God has appointed times. Your moment is coming. Be faithful in the
   waiting.
