Genesis - Chapter 41
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Pharaoh dreams of seven fat and seven lean cows; Joseph interprets the dreams, is elevated to second-in-command, and prepares Egypt for famine.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 41 is Joseph's exaltation — from prison to palace
in a single day. After 13 years of suffering (sold at 17, exalted at 30),
God's timing arrives. Pharaoh dreams; no one can interpret. The butler finally
remembers Joseph. Joseph interprets the dreams (seven years of plenty followed
by seven years of famine), proposes a plan, and is immediately elevated to
second-in-command over all Egypt. The chapter demonstrates God's sovereign
timing: years of preparation for a moment of promotion.

Pharaoh's Dreams (vv.1-8): Two years after chapter 40 (v.1). Pharaoh dreams
of seven fat cows eaten by seven thin cows, and seven full ears consumed by
seven thin ears (vv.2-7). Egypt's magicians cannot interpret (v.8).

The Butler Remembers (vv.9-13): "I do remember my faults this day" (v.9). He
tells Pharaoh about Joseph (vv.10-13).

Joseph Before Pharaoh (vv.14-36): Joseph is rushed from prison — shaved,
changed (v.14). Pharaoh: "I have heard say of thee, that thou canst
understand a dream" (v.15). Joseph: "It is not in me: God shall give Pharaoh
an answer" (v.16). Joseph interprets: seven years plenty, seven years famine
(vv.25-31). "The dream was doubled... the thing is established by God" (v.32).
Joseph proposes a plan: appoint a wise man, store 20% during plenty (vv.33-36).

Joseph Exalted (vv.37-45): Pharaoh: "Can we find such a one as this is, a man
in whom the Spirit of God is?" (v.38). Joseph made ruler over all Egypt (v.41).
Given Pharaoh's ring, fine linen, gold chain, second chariot (vv.42-43). Named
Zaphnath-paaneah; given Asenath as wife (v.45). Joseph is 30 years old (v.46).

The Seven Years of Plenty (vv.46-52): Joseph gathers grain "as the sand of
the sea" (v.49). Two sons born: Manasseh ("God made me forget my toil") and
Ephraim ("God made me fruitful in affliction") (vv.51-52).

The Famine Begins (vv.53-57): Famine in all lands (v.54). "Go unto Joseph;
what he saith to you, do" (v.55). All countries come to Joseph (v.57).

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
• Egypt — Joseph rises from prison to Pharaoh's court. The entire chapter is
  set in Egypt, likely in the capital (possibly Memphis or a Delta city).
• The famine affects "all lands" (v.57) — nations come to Egypt to buy grain,
  including from Canaan. Egypt's Nile-fed agriculture made it the breadbasket
  of the ancient Near East during regional droughts.
• Joseph travels "over all the land of Egypt" (v.46) during the seven years
  of plenty, organizing storage in every city.

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------
Enduring Word (David Guzik):
  https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/genesis-41/
  Guzik covers the dreams, Joseph's interpretation, his exaltation, and the
  Christ-typology of Joseph as savior of the world.

Charles Spurgeon:
  Sermon: "From Prison to Palace" on vv.14, 40 (MTP Vol. 14, No. 845)
  "In one day — from dungeon to throne. Thirteen years of preparation for
  one moment of promotion. God's timing is not ours. He prepares in secret
  what He reveals in public. The prison was not wasted time — it was training
  time. Every injustice, every delay, every forgotten promise — all preparation
  for THIS moment. Trust God's timing. Your 'suddenly' is coming."

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. "It is not in me: God shall give Pharaoh an answer" (v.16). When you
   succeed, do you deflect glory to God or absorb it yourself? Joseph
   consistently pointed to God.

2. Thirteen years from pit to palace. God's preparation often takes longer
   than we expect. Are you in a "preparation season"? Trust that God is
   training you for something.

3. "Manasseh... Ephraim" (vv.51-52). God can make you forget your pain AND
   make you fruitful in the place of your affliction. Have you experienced
   this? Can you trust Him for it?

4. "Go unto Joseph; what he saith to you, do" (v.55). All must go through
   Joseph for bread. All must go through Christ for life. Are you going to
   Him? Are you doing what He says?

5. Joseph was 30 when exalted — the same age Jesus began His ministry (Luke
   3:23). God has appointed times. Your moment is coming. Be faithful in the
   waiting.
