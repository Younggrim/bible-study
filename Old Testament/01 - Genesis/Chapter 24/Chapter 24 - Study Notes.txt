Genesis - Chapter 24
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Abraham sends his servant to find a wife for Isaac; God providentially leads him to Rebekah, who willingly returns to marry Isaac.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 24 is the longest chapter in Genesis (67 verses)
and one of the most beautiful narratives in Scripture — the story of finding
a bride for Isaac. Abraham sends his servant (likely Eliezer, cf. 15:2) to
his homeland to find a wife from his own family. The servant prays, God
answers specifically, and Rebekah is brought to Isaac. The chapter is rich
with typology: Abraham (the Father) sends his servant (the Spirit) to find
a bride (the Church) for his son (Christ).

Abraham's Commission (vv.1-9): Abraham is old; God has blessed him (v.1). He
makes his servant swear: don't take a Canaanite wife for Isaac (v.3). Go to
my kindred (v.4). Don't take Isaac back there (v.6). God will send His angel
before you (v.7).

The Servant's Prayer and Answer (vv.10-27): At the well, the servant prays
for a specific sign: the woman who offers water to him AND his camels (vv.12-
14). "Before he had done speaking" — Rebekah appears (v.15). She does exactly
what he prayed (vv.18-20). The servant worships: "the LORD led me" (v.27).

The Negotiation (vv.28-60): The servant tells the whole story to Rebekah's
family (vv.34-48). Laban and Bethuel agree: "The thing proceedeth from the
LORD" (v.50). Rebekah is asked: "Wilt thou go?" She answers: "I will go"
(v.58).

The Meeting (vv.61-67): Isaac meditates in the field at evening (v.63).
Rebekah sees Isaac and dismounts (v.64). Isaac brings her into Sarah's tent;
she becomes his wife; he loves her; he is comforted (v.67).

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
• Mesopotamia / "the city of Nahor" (v.10) — Abraham's servant travels to
  northwest Mesopotamia (Haran region, modern southeastern Turkey/Syria
  border) to find a wife for Isaac.
• The journey back to Canaan covers approximately 500 miles along ancient
  caravan routes through the Fertile Crescent.
• Beer-lahai-roi (v.62) — where Isaac dwells in the Negev, the same well
  where God appeared to Hagar (16:14).

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------
Enduring Word (David Guzik):
  https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/genesis-24/
  Guzik covers the commission, the prayer, God's guidance, Rebekah's
  character, and the typological significance.

Charles Spurgeon:
  Sermon: "The LORD Led Me" on v.27 (MTP Vol. 22, No. 1320)
  "I being in the way, the LORD led me — the servant was IN THE WAY (obedient,
  moving, available) and the LORD LED (directed, guided, orchestrated). Both
  are necessary: you must be moving for God to steer. A parked car cannot be
  directed. Get in the way — obey what you know — and the LORD will lead you
  to the right place at the right time."

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "I being in the way, the LORD led me" (v.27). Are you "in the way" —
   actively obeying, moving forward in faith? God guides those who are
   moving, not those who are sitting still.

2. The servant prayed specifically and God answered specifically (vv.12-15).
   Do you pray with specificity? God is not bothered by detailed requests.

3. Rebekah's character was revealed by her actions: generosity, hard work,
   hospitality (vv.18-25). What do your actions reveal about your character?

4. "Wilt thou go with this man? I will go" (v.58). Rebekah left everything
   for an unseen bridegroom. Faith requires leaving the familiar for the
   promised. What is God asking you to leave?

5. "The thing proceedeth from the LORD" (v.50). When God orchestrates events,
   even unbelievers can recognize it. Is God's hand visible in your life?
