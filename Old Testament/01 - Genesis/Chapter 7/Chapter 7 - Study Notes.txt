Genesis - Chapter 7
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Noah, his family, and the animals enter the ark; God sends the flood and destroys all life on earth.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 7 records the flood itself — God's judgment on a
corrupt world and His preservation of Noah's family and the animals. The flood
is presented as a de-creation: the waters above and below (separated in 1:6-7)
come back together, returning the earth to its pre-creation watery state. It
is both judgment (destruction of the wicked) and salvation (preservation of
the righteous). The NT uses the flood as a type of both baptism (1 Pet 3:20-21)
and future judgment (2 Pet 3:5-7).

Entering the Ark (vv.1-10): God invites Noah: "Come thou and all thy house
into the ark" (v.1) — "come" not "go" (God is already there). Clean animals
by sevens; unclean by twos (v.2). Seven days' warning before the rain (v.4).
"Noah did according unto all that the LORD commanded him" (v.5). Noah was 600
years old when the flood came (v.6).

The Flood Begins (vv.11-16): Precise dating: "the six hundredth year of
Noah's life, in the second month, the seventeenth day" (v.11). Two sources:
"the fountains of the great deep broken up, and the windows of heaven were
opened" (v.11). Rain for forty days and nights (v.12). "The LORD shut him in"
(v.16) — God Himself closed the door.

The Flood's Devastation (vv.17-24): Waters increased, lifted the ark (v.17).
Waters prevailed "exceedingly" — covering all high hills and mountains (vv.19-
20). "All flesh died" — every land creature and every person outside the ark
(vv.21-23). "Noah only remained alive, and they that were with him in the ark"
(v.23). Waters prevailed 150 days (v.24).

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
• No specific geography — the flood covers the entire earth (vv.19-20).
• The "fountains of the great deep" and "windows of heaven" (v.11) describe
  waters from below and above engulfing all known geography.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "Then the LORD said to Noah, 'Go into the ark, you and all your
        household, for I have seen that you are righteous before me in this
        generation.'" (ESV) — God's invitation to safety.

v.5  — "And Noah did all that the LORD had commanded him." (ESV) — Repeated
        obedience (cf. 6:22).

v.11 — "All the fountains of the great deep burst forth, and the windows of
        the heavens were opened." (ESV) — Catastrophic, global judgment.

v.16 — "And the LORD shut him in." (ESV) — God seals Noah in safety.

v.23 — "Only Noah was left, and those who were with him in the ark." (ESV) —
        Salvation through the ark alone.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1  — KJV: "Come thou and all thy house into the ark" / ESV: "Go into the
       ark, you and all your household" — Hebrew "bo" — come/enter. This is
       a significant translation difference. KJV renders "bo" as "come"
       (implying God is already in the ark, inviting Noah toward Himself);
       ESV renders it as "go" (a directional command). The Hebrew "bo" most
       naturally means "come/enter" — KJV's rendering preserves the
       theological nuance that God invites Noah toward His presence.

v.2  — KJV: "by sevens, the male and his female" / ESV: "seven pairs...
       the male and his mate" — Hebrew "shib'ah shib'ah" — seven seven.
       Debated: seven total or seven pairs? ESV interprets as "seven pairs"
       (14 total); KJV's "by sevens" leaves it ambiguous. The distinction
       between clean and unclean animals predates the Mosaic Law.

v.3  — KJV: "to keep seed alive upon the face of all the earth" / ESV: "to
       keep their offspring alive on the face of all the earth" — Hebrew
       "zera" — seed, offspring. ESV's "offspring" is clearer for modern
       readers.

v.4  — KJV: "every living substance that I have made will I destroy" / ESV:
       "every living thing that I have made I will blot out" — Hebrew
       "emcheh" — to wipe away, blot out. ESV's "blot out" is more literal;
       KJV's "destroy" captures the result. "Forty days and forty nights" —
       both identical. Forty is a number of testing/judgment throughout
       Scripture.

v.11 — KJV: "all the fountains of the great deep broken up, and the windows
       of heaven were opened" / ESV: "all the fountains of the great deep
       burst forth, and the windows of the heavens were opened" — Hebrew
       "nib'qe'u" — burst open, split apart. ESV's "burst forth" is more
       vivid than KJV's "broken up." Both convey catastrophic eruption of
       subterranean waters.

v.16 — KJV: "the LORD shut him in" / ESV: "the LORD shut him in" — Both
       identical. Hebrew "Yahweh sagar ba'ado" — Yahweh closed [the door]
       behind him. God personally sealed the ark. Divine security.

v.19 — KJV: "all the high hills, that were under the whole heaven, were
       covered" / ESV: "all the high mountains under the whole heaven were
       covered" — Hebrew "harim" — hills or mountains. ESV's "mountains" is
       stronger; KJV's "hills" may understate the scope. The language
       indicates a universal, global flood — "under the whole heaven" =
       comprehensive.

v.23 — KJV: "Noah only remained alive, and they that were with him in the
       ark" / ESV: "Only Noah was left, and those who were with him in the
       ark" — Both equivalent. Salvation exclusively through the ark.

v.24 — KJV: "an hundred and fifty days" / ESV: "150 days" — Both identical
       in meaning. Five months of prevailing waters. The flood was not a
       brief rainstorm but a prolonged, catastrophic event.

TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
- v.1 — ASV/WEB both retain "Come" (Hebrew "bo"), preserving the theological
  nuance that God invites Noah toward Himself. NET also uses "Come into the
  ark." WEB renders "ark" as "ship" throughout the flood narrative.
- v.1 — NET: "I consider you godly" — interprets "righteous" (KJV/ESV) as
  "godly," shifting from legal/moral standing to character description.
- v.11 — NET: "the floodgates of the heavens were opened" — renders Hebrew
  "arubboth hashamayim" as "floodgates" (vs. "windows" in KJV/ESV/ASV/WEB),
  a more hydrological image that avoids the literal "windows" metaphor.
- v.19 — ASV: "all the high mountains that were under the whole heaven were
  covered" — uses "mountains" (like ESV) where KJV has weaker "hills,"
  strengthening the global scope of the flood.
- v.20 — NET converts "fifteen cubits" to "more than 20 feet," continuing
  its practice of modernizing measurements for accessibility.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
"Come into the ark" — God's invitation toward Himself. He is already in the
               place of safety and invites us to join Him.

Clean and unclean — Categories of animals predating the Mosaic Law. Clean
               animals (suitable for sacrifice) taken by sevens.

Forty days     — A period of testing/judgment. The duration of the rain
               (though the flood lasted much longer overall).

Fountains of the deep — Subterranean water sources that erupted during the
               flood. The earth's internal waters released.

The LORD shut him in — God personally sealing Noah in the ark. Divine
               security — God closes the door of salvation.

Noah only remained — Salvation exclusively through the ark. No other means
               of escape. One way of salvation.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Come thou... into the ark" (v.1). God says "come" — toward Himself. Are
   you responding to His invitation? He's not sending you away; He's inviting
   you in.

2. "The LORD shut him in" (v.16). God secures your salvation — not you. Your
   security depends on His hand, not yours. Does this give you confidence?

3. "Noah only remained alive, and they that were with him in the ark" (v.23).
   There was only one way of salvation — the ark. There is only one way now —
   Christ. Have you entered?

4. "As the days of Noe were, so shall also the coming of the Son of man be"
   (Matt 24:37). Jesus compared His return to Noah's flood — sudden judgment
   on an unprepared world. Are you prepared?

5. Noah obeyed completely (v.5) and was saved. Obedience is the response to
   grace. God provides the ark (grace); you must enter it (obedience). Have
   you done both — received grace AND responded with obedience?
