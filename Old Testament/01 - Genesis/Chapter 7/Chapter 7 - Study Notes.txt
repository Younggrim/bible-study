Genesis - Chapter 7
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Noah, his family, and the animals enter the ark; God sends the flood and destroys all life on earth.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Moses (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 7 records the flood itself — God's judgment on a
corrupt world and His preservation of Noah's family and the animals. The flood
is presented as a de-creation: the waters above and below (separated in 1:6-7)
come back together, returning the earth to its pre-creation watery state. It
is both judgment (destruction of the wicked) and salvation (preservation of
the righteous). The NT uses the flood as a type of both baptism (1 Pet 3:20-21)
and future judgment (2 Pet 3:5-7).

Entering the Ark (vv.1-10): God invites Noah: "Come thou and all thy house
into the ark" (v.1) — "come" not "go" (God is already there). Clean animals
by sevens; unclean by twos (v.2). Seven days' warning before the rain (v.4).
"Noah did according unto all that the LORD commanded him" (v.5). Noah was 600
years old when the flood came (v.6).

The Flood Begins (vv.11-16): Precise dating: "the six hundredth year of
Noah's life, in the second month, the seventeenth day" (v.11). Two sources:
"the fountains of the great deep broken up, and the windows of heaven were
opened" (v.11). Rain for forty days and nights (v.12). "The LORD shut him in"
(v.16) — God Himself closed the door.

The Flood's Devastation (vv.17-24): Waters increased, lifted the ark (v.17).
Waters prevailed "exceedingly" — covering all high hills and mountains (vv.19-
20). "All flesh died" — every land creature and every person outside the ark
(vv.21-23). "Noah only remained alive, and they that were with him in the ark"
(v.23). Waters prevailed 150 days (v.24).

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
• No specific geography — the flood covers the entire earth (vv.19-20).
• The "fountains of the great deep" and "windows of heaven" (v.11) describe
  waters from below and above engulfing all known geography.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "Then the LORD said to Noah, 'Go into the ark, you and all your
        household, for I have seen that you are righteous before me in this
        generation.'" (ESV) — God's invitation to safety.

v.5  — "And Noah did all that the LORD had commanded him." (ESV) — Repeated
        obedience (cf. 6:22).

v.11 — "All the fountains of the great deep burst forth, and the windows of
        the heavens were opened." (ESV) — Catastrophic, global judgment.

v.16 — "And the LORD shut him in." (ESV) — God seals Noah in safety.

v.23 — "Only Noah was left, and those who were with him in the ark." (ESV) —
        Salvation through the ark alone.

  Sermon: "The LORD Shut Him In" on v.16 (MTP Vol. 11, No. 634)
  "The LORD shut him in — not Noah. Noah didn't secure his own safety — God
  did. The same hand that shut Noah IN shut the world OUT. When God closes
  the door, no one can open it. When God secures you, no one can unsecure
  you. Your salvation depends not on your grip on the door handle but on
  God's hand that shut the door. He shut him in — safe, secure, sealed by
  God Himself."
  Sermon: "Come Into the Ark" on v.1 (MTP Vol. 20, No. 1201)
  "COME — not 'go.' God says 'come' because He is already in the ark. He
  doesn't send Noah away from Himself but invites him toward Himself. Christ
  says the same: 'Come unto me.' Not 'go somewhere' but 'come to where I am.'
  The ark is where God is — and He invites you in."

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1  — Hebrew "bo el-hattevah" — come/enter into the ark.
       KJV: "Come thou and all thy house into the ark"
       ESV: "Go into the ark, you and all your household"
       ASV: "Come thou and all thy house into the ark"
       NET: "Come into the ark, you and all your household"
       WEB: "Come with all of your household into the ship"
       Analysis: Significant theological difference. KJV/ASV/NET retain
       "Come" (Hebrew "bo") — implying God is already in the ark, inviting
       Noah toward Himself. ESV renders it as "Go" — a directional command
       only. WEB renders "tevah" (ark) as "ship" throughout the flood
       narrative. NET also uses "I consider you godly" where others say
       "righteous" — shifting from legal standing to character description.

v.2  — Hebrew "shib'ah shib'ah" — seven seven.
       KJV: "Of every clean beast thou shalt take to thee by sevens"
       ESV: "Take with you seven pairs of all clean animals"
       ASV: "Of every clean beast thou shalt take to thee seven and seven"
       NET: "of every clean animal, take seven"
       WEB: "You shall take seven pairs of every clean animal"
       Analysis: Debated — seven total or seven pairs? ESV/WEB interpret as
       "seven pairs" (14 total). KJV/ASV's "by sevens / seven and seven"
       leaves it ambiguous. NET says simply "take seven" without specifying
       pairs. The clean/unclean distinction predates the Mosaic Law.

v.11 — Hebrew "nib'qe'u kol-ma'yenoth tehom rabbah va'arubboth hashamayim niftachu"
       KJV: "all the fountains of the great deep broken up, and the windows of heaven were opened"
       ESV: "all the fountains of the great deep burst forth, and the windows of the heavens were opened"
       ASV: "all the fountains of the great deep were broken up, and the windows of heaven were opened"
       NET: "all the fountains of the great deep burst open and the floodgates of the heavens were opened"
       WEB: "all the fountains of the great deep burst open, and the sky's windows opened"
       Analysis: ESV/NET/WEB's "burst forth/open" is more vivid than KJV/
       ASV's "broken up." NET renders "arubboth" as "floodgates" — a more
       hydrological image that avoids the literal "windows" metaphor.
       Catastrophic eruption of subterranean waters combined with
       unprecedented precipitation.

v.16 — Hebrew "Yahweh sagar ba'ado" — the LORD closed [the door] behind him.
       KJV: "the LORD shut him in"
       ESV: "the LORD shut him in"
       ASV: "Jehovah shut him in"
       NET: "the LORD shut him in"
       WEB: "Yahweh shut him in"
       Analysis: All translations agree. God personally sealed the ark.
       Divine security — once God closes the door, no one can open it.
       ASV uses "Jehovah"; WEB uses "Yahweh."

v.19 — Hebrew "kol-heharim haggevohim asher-tachat kol-hashamayim"
       KJV: "all the high hills, that were under the whole heaven, were covered"
       ESV: "all the high mountains under the whole heaven were covered"
       ASV: "all the high mountains that were under the whole heaven were covered"
       NET: "all the high mountains under the entire sky were covered"
       WEB: "All the high mountains that were under the whole sky were covered"
       Analysis: KJV alone uses "hills" — Hebrew "harim" means mountains.
       All modern translations use "mountains," strengthening the global
       scope. NET uses "entire sky" instead of "whole heaven." The language
       indicates a universal flood — "under the whole heaven" = comprehensive.

v.20 — Hebrew "chamesh esreh ammah milma'lah" — fifteen cubits upward.
       KJV: "Fifteen cubits upward did the waters prevail"
       ESV: "the waters prevailed above the mountains, covering them fifteen cubits deep"
       ASV: "Fifteen cubits upward did the waters prevail"
       NET: "the waters rose more than twenty feet above the mountains"
       WEB: "The waters rose fifteen cubits higher"
       Analysis: NET converts cubits to modern measurements (20+ feet) for
       accessibility. ESV restructures for clarity, adding "covering them"
       and "deep." KJV/ASV retain the Hebrew word order.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
"Come into the ark" — God's invitation toward Himself. He is already in the
               place of safety and invites us to join Him.

Clean and unclean — Categories of animals predating the Mosaic Law. Clean
               animals (suitable for sacrifice) taken by sevens.

Forty days     — A period of testing/judgment. The duration of the rain
               (though the flood lasted much longer overall).

Fountains of the deep — Subterranean water sources that erupted during the
               flood. The earth's internal waters released.

The LORD shut him in — God personally sealing Noah in the ark. Divine
               security — God closes the door of salvation.

Noah only remained — Salvation exclusively through the ark. No other means
               of escape. One way of salvation.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Come thou... into the ark" (v.1). God says "come" — toward Himself. Are
   you responding to His invitation? He's not sending you away; He's inviting
   you in.

2. "The LORD shut him in" (v.16). God secures your salvation — not you. Your
   security depends on His hand, not yours. Does this give you confidence?

3. "Noah only remained alive, and they that were with him in the ark" (v.23).
   There was only one way of salvation — the ark. There is only one way now —
   Christ. Have you entered?

4. "As the days of Noe were, so shall also the coming of the Son of man be"
   (Matt 24:37). Jesus compared His return to Noah's flood — sudden judgment
   on an unprepared world. Are you prepared?

5. Noah obeyed completely (v.5) and was saved. Obedience is the response to
   grace. God provides the ark (grace); you must enter it (obedience). Have
   you done both — received grace AND responded with obedience?
