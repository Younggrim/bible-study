The Gospel of John

Chapter 1 English Standard Version

✦ Study Notes ✦

Chapter Summary

The eternal Word who was with God and was God becomes flesh — John the Baptist bears witness, and Jesus calls His first disciples, who begin to recognize Him as Messiah, Son of God, and King of Israel.

❧

Key Verses

v.1 — "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God." — Establishes the pre-existence and deity of Christ.
v.14 — "And the Word became flesh and dwelt among us, and we have seen his glory, glory as of the only Son from the Father, full of grace and truth." — The Incarnation.
v.12 — "But to all who did receive him, who believed in his name, he gave the right to become children of God." — Salvation offered to all who believe.
v.29 — "Behold, the Lamb of God, who takes away the sin of the world!" — John the Baptist's declaration of Jesus as the atoning sacrifice.
❧

Cross-References

  • v.1 → Genesis 1:1 — John intentionally echoes Genesis, showing Christ was present at creation.
  • v.1 → Colossians 1:16-17 — "All things were created by him and for him."
  • v.3 → Hebrews 1:2 — God made the universe through His Son.
  • v.14 → Philippians 2:6-8 — Christ emptying Himself, taking on human form.
  • v.23 → Isaiah 40:3 — The prophecy John the Baptist fulfills.
  • v.29 → Isaiah 53:7 — "He was led as a lamb to the slaughter."
  • v.51 → Genesis 28:12 — Jacob's ladder, fulfilled in Christ.

Translation Notes

  • v.1 — Greek "Logos" (Word) carries rich meaning — in Greek philosophy it meant reason/logic; in Jewish thought it connected to God's creative speech in Genesis 1.
  • v.1 — NET uniquely renders "the Word was fully God" — interpretive but theologically accurate emphasis on complete deity.
  • v.5 — Greek "katelaben" can mean both "to comprehend" (KJV) and "to overcome" (ESV). The darkness neither understood nor defeated the Light.
  • v.9 — ESV clarifies that it is the Light (Christ) who was coming into the world, not every man.
  • v.12 — Greek "exousian" — KJV uses "power" (authority); ESV uses "right." Both convey granted authority/privilege.
  • v.14 — NET renders "took up residence among us" — capturing the tabernacle/tent imagery of Greek "eskenosen." KJV/ESV/ASV/WEB all use "dwelt."
  • v.14 — KJV: "only begotten" reflects older tradition of eternal generation; ESV: "only Son" conveys uniqueness without implying a time of origin.
  • v.16 — Greek "charin anti charitos" — "grace for grace" (KJV) / "grace upon grace" (ESV). Grace replacing grace in continuous abundance.
  • v.18 — "monogenes theos" vs "monogenes huios" — significant textual variant. Some manuscripts read "Son," others "God." ESV follows the "God" reading based on older manuscripts.
  • v.34 — NET reads "the Chosen One" (Greek "eklektos") rather than "the Son of God" (Greek "huios"). A significant textual variant.
  • v.39 — NET converts "the tenth hour" to "about four o'clock in the afternoon" (Jewish time-reckoning from 6 AM).
  • v.42 — WEB reads "Jonah" where KJV/ESV/NET read "John" (textual variant reflecting WEB's Majority Text base).
  • v.51 — NET renders "Amen, amen" as "the solemn truth" rather than "truly, truly" (ESV) or "verily, verily" (KJV). WEB uses "Most certainly."

Reflection Questions

1. John 1:1 declares Jesus was "in the beginning" with God and was God. What does the eternal nature of Christ mean to you personally? How does it change how you pray or relate to Him?

2. Verse 12 says that to all who received Him, He gave the right to become children of God. Have you personally received Him? What does it mean in your daily life to live as a son of God?

3. John the Baptist consistently deflected attention away from himself (v.20). In what areas of your life do you struggle with wanting credit or recognition? How can John's example challenge you?

4. Jesus saw Nathanael under the fig tree before Philip called him (v.48). Jesus sees you fully — your private moments, your doubts, your struggles. How does being fully known by Christ bring you comfort or conviction?

5. "What seek ye?" (v.38) — The first words Jesus speaks in John's Gospel are a question directed at those following Him. What are you truly seeking right now?

Personal Application

  • The Word became flesh (v.14) — Jesus did not stay at a distance. He entered our world, our pain, our humanity. Where do you need to experience God not as a distant concept but as Emmanuel — God with us?
  • John the Baptist's entire purpose was to point others to Jesus (v.29). He had crowds, influence, and a platform — and he used it all to say "look at Him, not me." Where is your life pointing people?
  • "He came unto his own, and his own received him not" (v.11). Rejection is one of the deepest human pains. Jesus experienced it fully. How does knowing Christ understands rejection change how you handle it?
  • Andrew's first response after meeting Jesus was to find his brother Simon and bring him to Christ (vv.40-42). Who in your life — a brother, a friend, a coworker — needs you to say "We have found the Messiah"?
