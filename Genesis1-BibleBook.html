The Book of Genesis

Chapter 1 English Standard Version

✦ Study Notes ✦

Chapter Summary

God creates the universe in six days by His word, culminating in humanity made in His image. Days 1–3 give form to the formless (light, sky/waters, land/vegetation); Days 4–6 fill the void (sun/moon/stars, sea creatures/birds, animals/humanity). The repeated refrain: "And God said… and it was so… and God saw that it was good."

❧

Key Verses

v.1 — "In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth." — The foundational statement of all Scripture. God exists. God creates.
v.3 — "And God said, 'Let there be light,' and there was light." — Creation by divine speech. God's word has creative power.
v.26 — "Then God said, 'Let us make man in our image, after our likeness.'" — The plural "us" hints at the Trinity. Humanity as God's image-bearers.
v.27 — "So God created man in his own image, in the image of God he created him; male and female he created them." — The dignity of humanity.
v.31 — "And God saw everything that he had made, and behold, it was very good." — Creation's original goodness.

Translation Notes

  • v.1 — Hebrew "Elohim" — plural form with singular verb ("bara"). Plurality in unity — early hint of the Trinity.
  • v.1 — Hebrew "bara" — to create from nothing (ex nihilo). Used only of God in the OT.
  • v.2 — KJV: "moved upon" / ESV: "was hovering" — Hebrew "merachephet" (hovered, brooded like a bird). ESV captures the participle more precisely.
  • v.6 — KJV: "firmament" / ESV: "expanse" — Hebrew "raqia." ESV's "expanse" better conveys the sense of open, stretched-out space.
  • v.21 — KJV: "great whales" / ESV: "great sea creatures" — Hebrew "tanninim gedolim" refers to large marine animals generally.
  • v.26 — NET uniquely renders "Let us make humankind" — gender-inclusive, reflecting the collective meaning of Hebrew "adam."
  • v.28 — KJV: "replenish" / ESV: "fill" — Hebrew "mil'u" means "fill" (not "refill"). ESV is more accurate to modern English.

Reflection Questions

1. "In the beginning God" (v.1). Everything starts with God. Is He the starting point of your thinking, your decisions, your worldview? Or do you start with yourself and fit God in later?

2. "God said… and it was so." God's word has creative power. When He speaks, reality responds. Do you treat His word (Scripture) with that kind of authority in your life?

3. "Let us make man in our image" (v.26). You are made in God's image — designed to reflect Him. How does this shape your sense of identity, purpose, and worth? How does it shape how you treat others?

4. "Be fruitful, and multiply… and have dominion" (v.28). God gave humanity a mission: fill the earth and steward it well. How are you fulfilling this mandate — in your family, your work, your community?

5. "God saw every thing… and, behold, it was very good" (v.31). Creation was originally good — not evil, not meaningless. How does this shape your view of the physical world, your body, and material creation?

❧

Personal Application

  • The chapter shows God as powerful (He creates by speaking), wise (creation is ordered and purposeful), and good (everything is "good"). Which attribute of God do you most need to trust today?
  • "Let us make man in our image" — You bear God's stamp. However fallen, however marred — the image remains. Every human being carries divine dignity. How does this change how you see yourself and others?
  • God brought order from chaos (v.2 → v.31). He took what was formless and void and made it beautiful and purposeful. What area of your life feels chaotic right now? Trust that God can bring order there too.
  • God gave humanity dominion — not for exploitation but for responsible stewardship. How are you caring for what God has entrusted to you?
