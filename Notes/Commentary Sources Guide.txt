DAVID GUZIK — Enduring Word Commentary
========================================

Born: 1961 (Santa Cruz, California)
Active: 1990s–present
Affiliation: Calvary Chapel pastor and Bible teacher

Background:
David Guzik is a contemporary pastor-teacher who has produced one of the
most widely used free Bible commentaries on the internet. He pastored
Calvary Chapel Santa Barbara for many years and now serves at The Rock of
Roseville in California. His commentary covers every chapter of the Bible
and is available free at enduringword.com, with millions of monthly readers
worldwide.

Guzik's style is clear, accessible, and pastoral. He writes for the person
sitting in the pew on Sunday morning — not for the seminary professor. He
draws from a wide range of older commentators (often quoting Spurgeon,
Poole, Trapp, and Clarke) while presenting material in modern, readable
English. His work is not original scholarship in the academic sense — it is
a synthesis of the best commentary tradition made accessible to everyday
believers.

Why Selected:
- FREE and universally accessible online (enduringword.com)
- Covers every chapter of every book — no gaps
- Written in clear, modern English without jargon
- Provides a reliable evangelical interpretation as a starting point
- Includes links to deeper sources for those who want to dig further
- Updated and maintained — a living resource, not a static text
- Represents the contemporary evangelical mainstream

Best For: A first stop when studying any passage. Getting oriented to the
text before diving into older or more technical sources.


CHARLES HADDON SPURGEON (1834-1892)
========================================

Born: June 19, 1834 (Kelvedon, Essex, England)
Died: January 31, 1892 (Menton, France)
Affiliation: Reformed Baptist

Background:
Charles Spurgeon is widely regarded as the greatest English-speaking
preacher in history. Called "the Prince of Preachers," he pastored the
Metropolitan Tabernacle in London for 38 years (1854-1892), regularly
preaching to crowds of 5,000-6,000 people in an era before amplification.
His sermons were transcribed and published weekly, eventually filling 63
volumes (the Metropolitan Tabernacle Pulpit) — the largest set of books by
a single author in the English language.

Spurgeon was converted at age 15 and began preaching at 16. By 19 he was
the most popular preacher in London. He founded a pastors' college, an
orphanage, and numerous charitable works. He battled depression throughout
his life ("the minister's fainting fits") and suffered severe gout that
often confined him to bed. Despite this, his output was extraordinary.

His theology was robustly Calvinistic (Reformed Baptist), deeply devotional,
and intensely Christ-centered. He could move seamlessly from careful
exegesis to soaring rhetoric to practical application. His illustrations
drew from everyday life — farmers, merchants, children, weather — making
profound theology accessible to common people.

Spurgeon's works are entirely in the public domain, freely available online
through multiple archives. His Treasury of David (commentary on Psalms) and
Morning and Evening devotionals remain widely used today, over 130 years
after his death.

Why Selected:
- The greatest English-language preacher — unmatched rhetorical power
- Entirely public domain — no copyright restrictions
- Christ-centered — he found Jesus in every text, every passage
- Deeply pastoral — he preached to comfort the afflicted and afflict the
  comfortable
- Quotable — his phrases stick in the memory
- Reformed theology expressed with warmth, not coldness
- Covers vast portions of Scripture across 63 volumes of sermons
- His Treasury of David on Psalms is still the richest devotional commentary
  on the Psalter
- Bridges the gap between academic commentary and devotional reading

Best For: Seeing how a text PREACHES — how doctrine becomes doxology, how
theology becomes worship. When you want to be moved, not just informed.





========================================
HOW THESE TWO VOICES WORK TOGETHER
========================================

Each commentator brings a distinct gift to the study of Scripture:

  GUZIK (modern)     — "Here's what the text says and means in plain terms."
                       The starting point. Clear, accessible, current.

  SPURGEON (homiletical) — "Here's how this text PREACHES — how it moves the
                       heart toward Christ." The preacher's voice. Power,
                       illustration, application.

Together they provide:
- A modern evangelical interpretation (Guzik)
- A homiletical/preaching perspective (Spurgeon)

This combination covers the two primary ways Christians engage Scripture:
understanding (Guzik) and worship/application (Spurgeon).

A NOTE ON PUBLIC DOMAIN STATUS
========================================

Spurgeon (d. 1892) is fully in the public domain worldwide — his works can
be freely quoted, copied, and distributed without any copyright restriction.

David Guzik's Enduring Word Commentary is freely available online for
personal use and is widely quoted in Bible study materials. It is shared
under generous terms through enduringword.com.
