1 Corinthians - Chapter 16
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Paul gives instructions for the collection for Jerusalem's saints, outlines his travel plans, commends Timothy and Apollos, and closes with final exhortations — "Watch, stand firm in the faith, be courageous, be strong. Let all that you do be done in love."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Paul (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 16 is Paul's closing — practical instructions about
the collection for Jerusalem (vv.1-4), his travel plans (vv.5-9), commendations
of Timothy and Apollos (vv.10-12), final exhortations (vv.13-14), recognition
of faithful servants (vv.15-18), and greetings (vv.19-24).

The Collection (vv.1-4): Paul organized a financial gift from Gentile churches
for the poor Jewish believers in Jerusalem (see also Romans 15:25-27, 2
Corinthians 8-9). His instruction: systematic, proportional, weekly giving —
"as God hath prospered him" (v.2). Not impulsive but planned.

"Watch ye, stand fast in the faith, quit you like men, be strong" (v.13): Four
rapid-fire commands that summarize the Christian life — be alert, be grounded,
be courageous, be strong. "Let all your things be done with charity" (v.14) —
and do it all in love.

"Maranatha" (v.22): An Aramaic word meaning "Our Lord, come!" — one of the
earliest Christian prayers, preserved in its original language. It expresses
the early church's longing for Christ's return.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.2  — "On the first day of every week, each of you is to put something aside
        and store it up, as he may prosper." (ESV) — Systematic, proportional,
        weekly giving.

v.9  — "A wide door for effective work has opened to me, and there are many
        adversaries." (ESV) — Opportunity and opposition together.

v.13 — "Be watchful, stand firm in the faith, act like men, be strong." (ESV)
        — Four commands for Christian living.

v.14 — "Let all that you do be done in love." (ESV) — Love as the motivation
        for everything.

v.22 — "If anyone has no love for the Lord, let him be accursed. Our Lord,
        come!" (ESV) — The seriousness of not loving Christ; the longing for
        His return.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------

v.2  — KJV: "the first day of the week" / ESV: "On the first day of every week, each of you is" — Sunday. The early church gathered
       and gave on Sunday — the day of resurrection. This is one of the
       earliest references to Sunday as the Christian day of gathering.

v.9  — KJV: "a great door and effectual" / ESV: "for a wide door for effective work has opened" — Opportunity ("great door") and
       opposition ("many adversaries") coexist. Open doors don't mean easy
       paths.

v.13 — KJV: "quit you like men" / ESV: "in the faith, act like men, be" — Greek "andrizesthe" — act like men, be
       courageous, be manly. A call to courage and strength — not passivity.

v.22 — KJV: "Anathema Maranatha" / ESV: "be accursed. Our Lord, come!" — Two words: "Anathema" = accursed, devoted
       to destruction. "Maranatha" = Aramaic for "Our Lord, come!" The
       severity of not loving Christ + the hope of His return.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.2  — NET: "On the first day of the week, each of you should
       set aside some income and save it" — "first day of the week"
       — confirms Sunday as the gathering day. WEB: "On the first
       day of every week, let each one of you save." Systematic,
       proportional, regular giving.

v.9  — NET: "a door of great opportunity stands wide open for me,
       but there are many opponents" — opportunity and opposition
       together — a typical ministry pattern. WEB: "a great and
       effective door has opened to me, and there are many
       adversaries." Open doors don't mean easy roads.

v.13 — NET: "Stay alert, stand firm in the faith, show courage,
       be strong" — four military imperatives. WEB: "Watch! Stand
       firm in the faith! Be courageous! Be strong!" KJV: "Watch
       ye, stand fast in the faith, quit you like men, be strong."
       ASV: "Watch ye, stand fast in the faith, quit you like men,
       be strong." Vigilance, stability, courage, strength.

v.14 — NET: "Everything you do should be done in love" — the
       summary command after the imperatives. WEB: "Let all that
       you do be done in love." KJV: "Let all your things be done
       with charity." Strength without love is brutality; love
       without strength is sentimentality.

v.22 — NET: "If anyone does not love the Lord, let him be
       accursed. Maranatha!" — "Maranatha" (Aramaic: marana tha =
       "Our Lord, come!" or maran atha = "Our Lord has come").
       WEB: "If any man doesn't love the Lord Jesus Christ, let
       him be accursed. Come, Lord!" The oldest recorded prayer
       of the church in its original language.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Collection    — The financial gift Paul organized from Gentile churches for
               poor Jewish believers in Jerusalem — a symbol of unity.

First day of the week — Sunday — the day of Christ's resurrection and the
               early church's day of gathering and giving.

Quit you like men — Be courageous, act with manly strength and bravery.
               Not passivity but active, bold faithfulness.

Maranatha     — Aramaic: "Our Lord, come!" One of the earliest Christian
               prayers — expressing longing for Christ's return.

Anathema      — Accursed, devoted to destruction. The most severe warning:
               not loving Christ brings eternal consequences.

Great door    — An open opportunity for ministry. Paul saw opportunity and
               opposition simultaneously — both are normal.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "As God hath prospered him" (v.2). Giving should be proportional to what
   God has given you — systematic, planned, and generous. How intentional is
   your giving? Is it proportional to how God has prospered you?

2. "A great door and effectual is opened unto me, and there are many
   adversaries" (v.9). Opportunity and opposition come together. Don't be
   surprised when open doors come with resistance. Where is God opening a
   door for you that also has adversaries?

3. "Watch ye, stand fast in the faith, quit you like men, be strong" (v.13).
   Four commands: be alert, be grounded, be courageous, be strong. Which of
   these four do you most need to hear right now?

4. "Let all your things be done with charity" (v.14). After all the commands
   for strength and courage — love. Strength without love is brutality. Love
   without strength is sentimentality. You need both. How are you combining
   strength and love?

REFLECTION QUESTIONS
------------------------------------
1. Paul's giving instructions (vv.1-2) are practical: weekly, proportional,
   planned. How does your giving compare? Is it systematic or sporadic?
   Proportional or random?

2. Paul commends Stephanas' household for "addicting themselves to the ministry
   of the saints" (v.15). They were devoted — addicted — to serving. What are
   you addicted to? What if you were addicted to serving others?

3. "If any man love not the Lord Jesus Christ, let him be Anathema" (v.22).
   The most serious warning in the letter. Not loving Christ is not neutral —
   it is damnable. Do you love Christ? How do you know?

4. "Maranatha — Our Lord, come!" (v.22). Is this your prayer? Do you long for
   Christ's return? Or have you become so comfortable in this world that His
   return would feel like an interruption?

5. Paul ends with "My love be with you all in Christ Jesus" (v.24). After all
   the correction, all the rebuke, all the hard truth — love. How do you end
   difficult conversations? With love or with distance?
