1 Corinthians - Chapter 6
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Paul rebukes believers for suing each other in pagan courts, warns that the unrighteous will not inherit God's kingdom, and declares the body is a temple of the Holy Spirit — "you are not your own, for you were bought with a price."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Paul (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 6 addresses two problems: lawsuits between believers
(vv.1-11) and sexual immorality (vv.12-20). Both issues reflect the Corinthians'
failure to understand their new identity in Christ — they are washed, sanctified,
justified (v.11), and their bodies are temples of the Holy Spirit (v.19).

Lawsuits (vv.1-11): Believers were suing each other in pagan courts. Paul is
appalled — "Is it so, that there is not a wise man among you?" (v.5). His
argument: (1) saints will judge the world and angels — surely you can handle
small disputes (vv.2-3), (2) it's better to be wronged than to wrong a brother
(v.7), (3) the unrighteous will not inherit the kingdom (vv.9-10).

"Such Were Some of You" (v.11): One of the most powerful verses in the chapter.
After listing sins that exclude from the kingdom (vv.9-10), Paul says "and such
WERE some of you" — past tense. They had been fornicators, idolaters, adulterers,
thieves, drunkards — but they were "washed... sanctified... justified." The
Gospel transforms.

The Body as Temple (vv.12-20): The Corinthians used the slogan "All things are
lawful" to justify sexual sin. Paul corrects: your body is not yours — it is
"the temple of the Holy Ghost" (v.19), "bought with a price" (v.20). Sexual
sin is uniquely destructive because it sins against your own body — the Spirit's
dwelling place.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.7  — "Why not rather suffer wrong? Why not rather be defrauded?" (ESV) —
        Better to be wronged than to wrong the church's witness.

v.11 — "And such were some of you. But you were washed, you were sanctified,
        you were justified in the name of the Lord Jesus Christ and by the
        Spirit of our God." (ESV) — The transforming power of the Gospel.

v.12 — "All things are lawful for me, but not all things are helpful. All
        things are lawful for me, but I will not be dominated by anything."
        (ESV) — Freedom has limits: benefit and mastery.

v.18 — "Flee from sexual immorality." (ESV) — The command: don't fight it —
        flee it.

v.19-20 — "Your body is a temple of the Holy Spirit within you... You are not
        your own, for you were bought with a price. So glorify God in your
        body." (ESV) — The basis for sexual purity: your body belongs to God.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------

v.9  — KJV: "effeminate... abusers of themselves with mankind" — ESV: "men
       who practice homosexuality" — Two Greek words: "malakoi" (passive
       partner) and "arsenokoitai" (active partner). Paul addresses both
       roles in homosexual practice.

v.11 — KJV: "washed... sanctified... justified" / ESV: "you were sanctified, you were justified in the name" — Three aspects of salvation:
       cleansed (washed), set apart (sanctified), declared righteous
       (justified). All accomplished "in the name of the Lord Jesus, and by
       the Spirit of our God."

v.12 — KJV: "All things are lawful" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Likely a Corinthian slogan Paul quotes
       and then qualifies. Freedom is real — but it has two limits: (1) not
       everything is beneficial, (2) nothing should master you.

v.18 — KJV: "Flee fornication" / ESV: "Flee from sexual immorality. Every" — Greek "pheugete" — present imperative: keep
       fleeing, make it a habit. Don't negotiate with sexual temptation — run.

v.19 — KJV: "your body is the temple" / ESV: "know that your body is a temple" — "Your" is plural; "body" is singular.
       Each individual believer's body is a temple of the Spirit.

v.20 — KJV: "bought with a price" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "ēgorasthēte timēs" — purchased
       at a cost. The price: Christ's blood. You are not your own property.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.9  — NET: "Do you not know that the unrighteous will not
       inherit the kingdom of God?" — "unrighteous" (adikoi). WEB:
       "the unrighteous will not inherit God's Kingdom." The vice
       list follows (vv.9-10): fornicators, idolaters, adulterers,
       effeminate, homosexuals, thieves, covetous, drunkards,
       revilers, extortioners.

v.11 — NET: "Some of you once lived this way. But you were washed,
       you were sanctified, you were justified in the name of the
       Lord Jesus Christ and by the Spirit of our God" — three
       past-tense verbs: washed, sanctified, justified. WEB: "you
       were washed. You were sanctified. You were justified." Past
       identity transformed.

v.12 — NET: "'All things are lawful for me' — but not everything
       is beneficial" — Paul quotes the Corinthians' slogan back
       to them and qualifies it. WEB: "All things are lawful for
       me, but not all things are expedient." Liberty is not
       license.

v.19 — NET: "Do you not know that your body is the temple of the
       Holy Spirit who is in you?" — "your body" (to sOma hymOn)
       — HERE it is individual bodies (unlike 3:16 which is
       corporate). WEB: "your body is a temple of the Holy Spirit."
       KJV: "your body is the temple of the Holy Ghost." Individual
       sanctity.

v.20 — NET: "For you were bought at a price. Therefore glorify
       God with your body" — "bought at a price" (EgorasthEte
       timEs = purchased at cost). WEB: "you were bought with a
       price." KJV: "ye are bought with a price." Redemption
       language — slaves purchased and freed. The price: Christ's
       blood.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Lawsuits      — Legal disputes between believers taken to pagan courts. Paul
               says this shames the church and should be handled internally.

Such were some of you — The transforming power of the Gospel: past identity
               (sinners) replaced by new identity (washed, sanctified, justified).

All things lawful — A Corinthian slogan about Christian freedom. Paul qualifies:
               freedom has limits — benefit and mastery.

Flee fornication — The command to run from sexual temptation — not fight it,
               not negotiate with it — flee.

Temple of the Holy Ghost — Each believer's body is the Spirit's dwelling place.
               Sexual sin uniquely defiles this temple.

Bought with a price — Believers are purchased property — owned by God through
               Christ's blood. Your body is not yours to do with as you please.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Why do ye not rather take wrong?" (v.7). Paul says it's better to be
   cheated than to damage the church's witness through lawsuits. Where might
   you need to absorb a wrong rather than fight for your rights — for the
   sake of the Gospel?

2. "Such were some of you" (v.11). Whatever your past — whatever sins defined
   you — the Gospel says "WERE." Past tense. You are washed, sanctified,
   justified. Are you living in your new identity or still identifying with
   your old one?

3. "Flee fornication" (v.18). Not "resist" — FLEE. Joseph fled Potiphar's
   wife (Genesis 39). The strategy for sexual temptation is not willpower but
   distance. What do you need to flee from? What boundaries do you need?

4. "Your body is the temple of the Holy Ghost... ye are bought with a price"
   (vv.19-20). Your body belongs to God — purchased by Christ's blood. How
   does knowing your body is God's property change how you treat it — what
   you put in it, what you do with it, where you take it?

REFLECTION QUESTIONS
------------------------------------
1. Paul says believers will "judge the world" and "judge angels" (vv.2-3).
   If you're qualified for that, surely you can handle disputes among
   yourselves. How does your future role affect how you view present conflicts?

2. The vice list (vv.9-10) includes sins that are common and culturally
   accepted today. Paul says those who practice these things "shall not inherit
   the kingdom." How do you hold this truth with both conviction and compassion?

3. "All things are lawful... but I will not be brought under the power of any"
   (v.12). Freedom means nothing masters you. What has power over you — a
   substance, a habit, a screen, a relationship? If it masters you, you're
   not free.

4. Sexual sin is unique — "he that committeth fornication sinneth against his
   own body" (v.18). Why is sexual sin uniquely destructive? How does the
   "one flesh" principle (v.16) explain why sexual sin damages so deeply?

5. "Glorify God in your body" (v.20). Your body is for God's glory — not just
   your spirit, not just your mind — your BODY. What does it look like to
   glorify God with your physical body today?
