1 Corinthians - Chapter 14
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Paul prioritizes prophecy over tongues in corporate worship because prophecy edifies the whole church, establishes order for the exercise of gifts, and insists "let all things be done decently and in order."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Paul (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 14 applies the love principle (ch.13) to the use
of spiritual gifts in worship — specifically tongues and prophecy. The Corinthians
elevated tongues above all other gifts. Paul corrects: prophecy is superior to
tongues in corporate worship because it edifies the whole church, not just the
speaker. The governing principle: "Let all things be done unto edifying" (v.26).

Prophecy vs. Tongues (vv.1-25): Prophecy speaks to people — edification,
exhortation, comfort (v.3). Tongues speak to God (v.2) and edify only the
speaker (v.4). In corporate worship, what edifies the body takes priority.
Paul would rather speak "five words with my understanding" than "ten thousand
words in an unknown tongue" (v.19).

Order in Worship (vv.26-40): Paul gives practical guidelines: tongues limited
to 2-3 speakers with interpretation (v.27); prophets limited to 2-3 with
evaluation (v.29); everything done "decently and in order" (v.40). "God is not
the author of confusion, but of peace" (v.33).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "Pursue love, and earnestly desire the spiritual gifts, especially that
        you may prophesy." (ESV) — Pursue love first, then gifts — especially
        prophecy.

v.12 — "So with yourselves, since you are eager for manifestations of the
        Spirit, strive to excel in building up the church." (ESV) — The goal
        of all gifts: building up the body.

v.19 — "In church I would rather speak five words with my mind... than ten
        thousand words in a tongue." (ESV) — Intelligibility over
        impressiveness.

v.26 — "Let all things be done for building up." (ESV) — The governing
        principle for worship.

v.33 — "For God is not a God of confusion but of peace." (ESV) — Order
        reflects God's character.

v.40 — "But all things should be done decently and in order." (ESV) — The
        summary command for corporate worship.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------

v.2  — KJV: "unknown tongue" / ESV: "in a tongue speaks not" — "Unknown" is italicized in KJV (not in the
       Greek). It was added by translators for clarity. The gift may be known
       languages (as at Pentecost) or ecstatic speech — debated.

v.19 — KJV: "five words with my understanding" / ESV: "I would rather speak five words with my" — Paul values intelligible
       communication over impressive but incomprehensible speech. Teaching
       that people understand is more valuable than displays they don't.

v.33 — KJV: "God is not the author of confusion" / ESV: "For God is not a God of confusion but of peace. As" — Greek "akatastasia" —
       disorder, instability, chaos. God's character is orderly and peaceful;
       worship should reflect His nature.

v.34 — KJV: "Let your women keep silence" / ESV: "the women should keep silent" — One of the most debated verses.
       Interpretations: (1) absolute silence for women in all church settings,
       (2) silence regarding judging prophecy (v.29), (3) silence regarding
       disruptive questioning (v.35), (4) a Corinthian quote Paul corrects
       (v.36). Context and 11:5 (women praying/prophesying) suggest a limited
       application.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.2  — NET: "For the one who speaks in a tongue does not speak
       to people but to God" — "to God" (theO lalei) — tongues
       directed Godward, not humanward. WEB: "speaks not to men,
       but to God." KJV: "speaketh not unto men, but unto God."
       Private communication with God in prayer.

v.5  — NET: "I wish you all spoke in tongues, but even more I
       wish you would prophesy" — Paul doesn't forbid tongues
       (v.39: "do not forbid speaking in tongues") but values
       prophecy higher for corporate gatherings. WEB: "I desire
       that you all spoke with other languages, but rather that
       you would prophesy."

v.19 — NET: "in the church I want to speak five words with my
       mind to instruct others, rather than ten thousand words in a
       tongue" — "five words... ten thousand" — hyperbolic ratio.
       WEB: "I would rather speak five words with my understanding
       ...than ten thousand words in another language." Intelligibility
       over impressiveness.

v.33 — NET: "for God is not characterized by disorder but by
       peace" — "disorder" (akatastasias = instability/confusion).
       WEB: "God is not a God of confusion, but of peace." KJV:
       "God is not the author of confusion, but of peace." The
       character of God defines proper worship.

v.40 — NET: "But everything must be done in a proper and orderly
       manner" — "proper and orderly" (euschEmonOs kai kata taxin).
       WEB: "Let all things be done decently and in order." KJV:
       "Let all things be done decently and in order." The closing
       principle for all charismatic expression.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Prophecy      — Speaking God's message to people for edification, exhortation,
               and comfort (v.3). Intelligible, understandable, and building
               up the body.

Tongues       — Speaking in a language not known to the speaker. In corporate
               worship, requires interpretation to edify others.

Edification   — Building up — the primary purpose of all gifts in corporate
               worship. If it doesn't build up, it doesn't belong.

Interpretation — The gift of making tongues intelligible to the congregation.
               Without interpretation, tongues should not be used publicly.

Decently and in order — Worship that reflects God's character: orderly,
               peaceful, intelligible, and edifying.

Confusion     — Greek "akatastasia" — disorder, chaos. The opposite of God's
               nature. Chaotic worship does not honor God.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Let all things be done unto edifying" (v.26). Evaluate your church's
   worship by this standard: does it build people up? Is it intelligible?
   Does it produce growth? Or is it performance without edification?

2. Paul preferred "five words with my understanding" over "ten thousand words
   in an unknown tongue" (v.19). Clarity over impressiveness. In your
   communication of faith — teaching, sharing, praying — are you prioritizing
   being understood or being impressive?

3. "God is not the author of confusion, but of peace" (v.33). Worship should
   reflect God's character: orderly, peaceful, purposeful. Does your worship
   experience reflect God's nature?

4. "Let all things be done decently and in order" (v.40). Order is not the
   enemy of the Spirit — it is the Spirit's preference. How do you balance
   openness to the Spirit with orderly, edifying worship?
