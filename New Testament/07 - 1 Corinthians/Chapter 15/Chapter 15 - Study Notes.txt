1 Corinthians - Chapter 15
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The resurrection chapter — Paul presents the gospel (Christ died, was buried, rose again), proves the resurrection by witnesses, argues that without it faith is futile, and reveals the mystery: "we shall all be changed, in a moment, in the twinkling of an eye."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Paul (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 15 is the greatest chapter on the resurrection in
the Bible — 58 verses defending, explaining, and celebrating the bodily
resurrection of Christ and believers. Some in Corinth denied the resurrection
(v.12). Paul responds with the most comprehensive resurrection theology in
Scripture.

The Gospel Defined (vv.1-11): Paul states the Gospel in its simplest form:
"Christ died for our sins according to the scriptures; and that he was buried,
and that he rose again the third day according to the scriptures" (vv.3-4).
Three facts: death, burial, resurrection. Two qualifiers: for our sins,
according to the scriptures. This is the irreducible core of Christianity.

The Necessity of Resurrection (vv.12-19): If there is no resurrection, then
Christ is not risen. If Christ is not risen: preaching is vain (v.14), faith
is vain (v.14), apostles are liars (v.15), you're still in your sins (v.17),
the dead are perished (v.18), and Christians are "most miserable" (v.19).
Everything depends on the resurrection.

The Resurrection Body (vv.35-49): The resurrection body is real but transformed
— like a seed that dies and becomes a plant. Sown in corruption, raised in
incorruption. Sown in weakness, raised in power. Sown a natural body, raised
a spiritual body (vv.42-44).

The Victory (vv.50-58): "O death, where is thy sting? O grave, where is thy
victory?" (v.55). Death is defeated. The sting of death (sin) is removed. The
strength of sin (the law) is fulfilled. Victory is given "through our Lord
Jesus Christ" (v.57).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.3-4 — "Christ died for our sins in accordance with the Scriptures, that he
        was buried, that he was raised on the third day in accordance with the
        Scriptures." (ESV) — The Gospel in one sentence.

v.14 — "And if Christ has not been raised, then our preaching is in vain and
        your faith is in vain." (ESV) — Everything depends on the resurrection.

v.20 — "But in fact Christ has been raised from the dead, the firstfruits of
        those who have fallen asleep." (ESV) — The triumphant declaration:
        Christ IS risen.

v.22 — "For as in Adam all die, so also in Christ shall all be made alive."
        (ESV) — The two humanities: death in Adam, life in Christ.

v.55 — "O death, where is your victory? O death, where is your sting?" (ESV)
        — The taunt over defeated death.

v.57 — "But thanks be to God, who gives us the victory through our Lord Jesus
        Christ." (ESV) — Victory as a gift, not an achievement.

v.58 — "Therefore, my beloved brothers, be steadfast, immovable, always
        abounding in the work of the Lord, knowing that in the Lord your labor
        is not in vain." (ESV) — The practical application of resurrection
        hope.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------

v.3  — KJV: "first of all" / ESV: "to you as of first importance" — Greek "en prōtois" — of first importance. The
       resurrection is not one doctrine among many — it is THE doctrine of
       first importance.

v.10 — KJV: "by the grace of God I am what I am" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Paul's self-understanding:
       everything he is and does is grace. Not false humility — honest
       acknowledgment of God's work.

v.20 — KJV: "firstfruits" / ESV: Both equivalent. — The first portion of the harvest, guaranteeing
       the rest will follow. Christ's resurrection guarantees ours.

v.44 — KJV: "a spiritual body" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Not immaterial but Spirit-animated. The
       resurrection body is physical but transformed — powered by the Spirit
       rather than by natural life.

v.51 — KJV: "a mystery" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Something previously hidden, now revealed. Not all
       believers will die ("sleep") — some will be alive at Christ's return
       and will be instantly transformed.

v.52 — KJV: "in the twinkling of an eye" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Instantaneous transformation.
       The fastest measurable moment — the time it takes for light to reflect
       off the eye.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.3  — NET: "Christ died for our sins according to the
       scriptures" — "according to the scriptures" (kata tas
       graphas) — the death was scripturally predicted and
       scripturally interpreted. WEB: "Christ died for our sins
       according to the Scriptures." The gospel has four elements:
       died, buried, rose, appeared.

v.14 — NET: "and if Christ has not been raised, then our
       preaching is futile and your faith is empty" — "futile"
       (kenon = empty/vain). WEB: "our preaching is in vain, and
       your faith also is in vain." Without resurrection,
       Christianity collapses entirely — Paul stakes everything.

v.22 — NET: "For just as in Adam all die, so also in Christ all
       will be made alive" — "in Adam... in Christ" — the two
       representative heads of humanity. WEB: "as in Adam all die,
       so also in Christ all will be made alive." Universal death
       through Adam; life through Christ.

v.51 — NET: "Listen, I will tell you a mystery: We will not all
       sleep, but we will all be changed" — "mystery" (mystErion =
       previously hidden truth now revealed). WEB: "we will all be
       changed." KJV: "we shall all be changed." The rapture/
       transformation — mortality swallowed by life.

v.55 — NET: "Where, O death, is your victory? Where, O death, is
       your sting?" — quoting Hosea 13:14 and Isaiah 25:8. WEB:
       "Death, where is your sting? Hades, where is your victory?"
       KJV: "O death, where is thy sting? O grave, where is thy
       victory?" Death taunted by the resurrected — its power broken.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Gospel        — The good news defined: Christ died for our sins, was buried,
               and rose the third day — according to the Scriptures.

Firstfruits   — The first portion of the harvest — guaranteeing the full
               harvest will follow. Christ's resurrection guarantees ours.

Resurrection body — Physical but transformed — imperishable, glorious,
               powerful, spiritual (Spirit-animated). Not a ghost but a
               glorified physical body.

Last Adam     — Christ — the second representative head of humanity. The first
               Adam brought death; the last Adam brings life.

Sting of death — Sin — the thing that makes death terrifying. Remove sin and
               death loses its power to condemn.

Mystery       — A truth previously hidden, now revealed: not all will die —
               some will be transformed instantly at Christ's return.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "If Christ be not risen... your faith is also vain" (v.14). Everything
   depends on the resurrection. Do you believe it — not just intellectually
   but with your whole life? How does the resurrection change how you live
   TODAY?

2. "By the grace of God I am what I am" (v.10). Paul's identity was rooted
   in grace — not in his achievements or his past. What is your identity
   rooted in? Can you say "by grace I am what I am"?

3. "O death, where is thy sting?" (v.55). Death is defeated. For the believer,
   death has lost its power to condemn. How does this truth change how you
   face your own mortality? How does it change how you grieve?

4. "Be ye stedfast, unmovable, always abounding in the work of the Lord,
   forasmuch as ye know that your labour is not in vain" (v.58). Because of
   the resurrection, your work matters eternally. Nothing done for Christ is
   wasted. How does this motivate your daily faithfulness?

REFLECTION QUESTIONS
------------------------------------
1. Paul lists the resurrection appearances (vv.5-8) — eyewitness evidence.
   Over 500 people saw the risen Christ. How does the historical evidence for
   the resurrection strengthen your faith?

2. "As in Adam all die, even so in Christ shall all be made alive" (v.22).
   Two humanities — one in Adam (death), one in Christ (life). Which are you
   in? What difference does it make?

3. The resurrection body is "sown in corruption; raised in incorruption" (v.42).
   Your current body is temporary and decaying. Your future body will be
   permanent and glorious. How does this hope affect how you view aging,
   sickness, and physical limitation?

4. "The sting of death is sin; and the strength of sin is the law" (v.56).
   Christ removed the sting (forgave sin) and broke the strength (fulfilled
   the law). Death is now a doorway, not a dead end. How does this change
   your relationship with death?

5. "Your labour is not in vain in the Lord" (v.58). Because of the resurrection,
   nothing you do for Christ is wasted — even if you never see the results.
   What work are you doing that feels futile? This verse says it's not.
