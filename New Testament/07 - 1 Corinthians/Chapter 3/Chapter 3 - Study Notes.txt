1 Corinthians - Chapter 3
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Paul rebukes the Corinthians as spiritual infants, insists that ministers are merely servants — one plants, another waters, but God gives the growth — and warns that each person's work will be tested by fire on the day of judgment.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Paul (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 3 continues addressing the divisions in Corinth.
Paul diagnoses the problem: they are "carnal" — spiritual infants (vv.1-4).
He then uses two metaphors — a garden (vv.5-9) and a building (vv.10-17) — to
explain the proper view of Christian leaders and the seriousness of building
on the right foundation.

Spiritual Infancy (vv.1-4): The Corinthians were "babes in Christ" (v.1) —
still on milk, unable to handle solid food. The evidence of their immaturity:
"envying, and strife, and divisions" (v.3). Factionalism is a sign of spiritual
infancy, not sophistication.

The Garden (vv.5-9): Paul planted, Apollos watered — but "God gave the increase"
(v.6). Human leaders are merely servants; God is the one who produces growth.
"Neither is he that planteth any thing, neither he that watereth; but God that
giveth the increase" (v.7).

The Building (vv.10-17): Christ is the only foundation (v.11). What you build
on that foundation matters — gold, silver, precious stones (lasting work) vs.
wood, hay, stubble (worthless work). Fire will test every man's work (v.13).
If it survives, you receive a reward; if it burns, you suffer loss — "but he
himself shall be saved; yet so as by fire" (v.15).

"Ye are the temple of God" (v.16): The church — collectively — is God's temple.
The Spirit dwells in the community of believers. To destroy the church through
division is to defile God's temple — a serious offense (v.17).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.6  — "I planted, Apollos watered, but God gave the growth." (ESV) — Human
        effort is necessary but God produces the results.

v.7  — "So neither he who plants nor he who waters is anything, but only God
        who gives the growth." (ESV) — Leaders are nothing; God is everything.

v.11 — "No one can lay a foundation other than that which is laid, which is
        Jesus Christ." (ESV) — The only foundation: Christ.

v.13 — "Each one's work will become manifest, for the Day will disclose it,
        because it will be revealed by fire." (ESV) — The judgment of
        believers' works.

v.16 — "Do you not know that you are God's temple and that God's Spirit dwells
        in you?" (ESV) — The church as God's dwelling place.

v.21-23 — "All things are yours, whether Paul or Apollos or Cephas or the
        world or life or death... all are yours, and you are Christ's, and
        Christ is God's." (ESV) — Everything belongs to believers in Christ.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1  — KJV: "carnal" / ESV: "as people" — Greek "sarkinois" — fleshly, controlled by the flesh
       rather than the Spirit. Not unbelievers — but believers living like
       unbelievers.

v.6  — NET: "I planted, Apollos watered, but God caused it to
       grow" — "caused it to grow" (Euxanen = was causing growth,
       imperfect tense — continuous). WEB: "God gave the increase."
       KJV: "God gave the increase." Ministers are tools; God is
       the grower. Human effort necessary but not sufficient.

v.9  — KJV: "God's husbandry... God's building" / ESV: "building" — Two metaphors: you are
       God's field (He grows you) and God's building (He constructs you).
       Both emphasize God's ownership and activity.

v.11 — NET: "For no one can lay any foundation other than what is
       being laid, which is Jesus Christ" — "no one can" (ou
       dynatai) — impossibility, not just prohibition. WEB: "no
       one is able to lay another foundation." There is one
       foundation; everything else is superstructure.

v.13 — KJV: "revealed by fire" / ESV: Both equivalent. — The judgment of believers' works (not
       salvation). Fire tests quality — gold survives; hay burns. This is
       the "bēma" judgment (2 Corinthians 5:10), not the final judgment.
       NET: "each builder's work will be plainly seen, for the
       Day will make it clear, because it will be revealed by fire"
       — "the Day" (hE hEmera) = judgment day. WEB: "the Day will
       declare it." KJV: "the day shall declare it." Fire tests
       quality, not sincerity. Wood, hay, stubble burn; gold,
       silver, precious stones survive.

v.15 — KJV: "saved; yet so as by fire" / ESV: "be saved, but only as through fire" — Salvation is secure even if all
       works burn. The person is saved but suffers loss of reward — like
       escaping a burning house with nothing but your life.

v.16 — KJV: "ye are the temple" / ESV: "not know that you are God’s" — "Ye" is plural — the church collectively
       is God's temple. (1 Corinthians 6:19 applies it individually.) The
       Spirit dwells in the community of believers.
       NET: "Do you not know that you are God's temple and that
       God's Spirit lives in you?" — "you" is PLURAL (naos theou
       este) — the church collectively is the temple. WEB: "you
       are a temple of God." Not primarily individual bodies (that's
       6:19) but the corporate assembly.

v.21 — NET: "So then, no more boasting about mere mortals!" —
       "all things are yours" (v.21-22) — Paul, Apollos, Cephas,
       the world, life, death, present, future. WEB: "let no one
       boast in men." KJV: "let no man glory in men." All teachers
       belong to the church; the church doesn't belong to teachers.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Carnal        — Fleshly, worldly. Believers who live according to the flesh
               rather than the Spirit. Evidenced by envy, strife, and divisions.

Milk vs. Meat — Basic teaching vs. deeper truth. Spiritual infants need milk;
               mature believers can handle solid food.

God's husbandry — God's field/garden. Believers are the soil; leaders plant
               and water; God gives growth.

Foundation    — Jesus Christ — the only legitimate foundation for the church
               and for individual faith. Everything must be built on Him.

Gold/Silver/Precious stones — Work that survives God's testing fire — lasting,
               valuable, eternal ministry built on Christ.

Wood/Hay/Stubble — Work that burns — worthless, temporary, self-serving
               ministry that doesn't survive judgment.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Paul called the Corinthians "babes" because of their divisions (v.3).
   Factionalism is a sign of immaturity, not sophistication. How mature are
   you? Is there envy, strife, or division in your relationships with other
   believers?

2. "God gave the increase" (v.6). You can plant and water — but only God
   produces growth. Are you trying to force spiritual results through human
   effort? Can you trust God with the outcomes?

3. "Other foundation can no man lay than that is laid, which is Jesus Christ"
   (v.11). What is your life built on? If Christ is removed, does everything
   collapse? Or is He truly the foundation of everything?

4. "Every man's work shall be... revealed by fire" (v.13). Your work will be
   tested. Not your salvation — your work. What are you building — gold or
   hay? Will your life's work survive the fire?

REFLECTION QUESTIONS
------------------------------------
1. The Corinthians were "carnal" — believers living like unbelievers (v.3).
   Is there an area of your life where you are living more like the world
   than like Christ? What would spiritual maturity look like there?

2. "Neither is he that planteth any thing, neither he that watereth" (v.7).
   Leaders are nothing — God is everything. How does this truth prevent both
   hero-worship of leaders and discouragement in your own ministry?

3. The fire will test "of what sort it is" (v.13) — not how much but what
   kind. Quality matters more than quantity. Are you focused on doing much
   or doing well? What would "gold" quality work look like in your life?

4. "All things are yours" (v.21) — Paul, Apollos, Cephas, the world, life,
   death, present, future — ALL yours in Christ. You don't need to fight over
   leaders or resources. Everything belongs to you because you belong to Christ.
   How does this abundance mentality change how you relate to other believers?

5. "Ye are the temple of God" (v.16). The church — your community of believers
   — is God's dwelling place. How seriously do you take the health and unity
   of your church? Are you building it up or tearing it down?
