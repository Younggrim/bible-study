2 Timothy - Chapter 4
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Paul's final charge: "Preach the word; be instant in season, out of season" — for the time will come when people will not endure sound doctrine. Paul declares he has fought the good fight, finished the course, and a crown of righteousness awaits him.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Paul the Apostle (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 4 is Paul's final chapter — his last recorded
words before execution. It contains his solemn charge to Timothy to preach the
Word (vv.1-5), his personal testimony of a life well-lived (vv.6-8), and
practical requests and updates (vv.9-22). This is one of the most moving
passages in Scripture — a dying man's final words, full of confidence, peace,
and unshakeable faith. Paul faces death not with fear but with anticipation.

The Final Charge (vv.1-5): Paul charges Timothy "before God and the Lord Jesus
Christ" to preach the Word — in season and out (vv.1-2). People will reject
sound doctrine and prefer teachers who tell them what they want to hear (vv.
3-4). Timothy must stay sober, endure, evangelize, and fulfill his ministry
(v.5).

Paul's Farewell (vv.6-8): "I am now ready to be offered" — Paul sees his
death as a drink offering poured out (v.6). Three perfect-tense statements:
"I have fought a good fight, I have finished my course, I have kept the faith"
(v.7). A crown of righteousness awaits — for him and all who love Christ's
appearing (v.8).

Personal Matters (vv.9-22): Demas deserted (v.10). Only Luke remains (v.11).
Bring Mark (v.11). Bring my cloak and books (v.13). At his trial, everyone
abandoned him — "but the Lord stood with me" (vv.16-17). Final confidence:
"The Lord shall deliver me" (v.18).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.2 — "Preach the word; be ready in season and out of season; reprove,
 rebuke, and exhort, with complete patience and teaching." (ESV) —
 The preacher's mandate.

v.3-4 — "The time is coming when people will not endure sound teaching, but
 having itching ears they will accumulate for themselves teachers to
 suit their own passions, and will turn away from listening to the
 truth." (ESV) — The coming rejection of truth.

v.5 — "As for you, always be sober-minded, endure suffering, do the work of
 an evangelist, fulfill your ministry." (ESV) — Timothy's four-fold
 charge.

v.6 — "I am already being poured out as a drink offering, and the time of
 my departure has come." (ESV) — Paul faces death with readiness.

v.7 — "I have fought the good fight, I have finished the race, I have kept
 the faith." (ESV) — Paul's triumphant summary of his life.

v.8 — "Henceforth there is laid up for me the crown of righteousness." (ESV)
 — The reward awaiting the faithful.

v.17 — "But the Lord stood by me and strengthened me." (ESV) — When all
 others abandoned him, the Lord remained.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.2 — KJV: "be instant" / ESV: "be ready" — Greek "epistēthi" — be ready, stand by, be at
 hand. "In season, out of season" — Greek "eukairōs akairōs" — when
 convenient and when not. Preach whether the timing seems right or not.

v.3 — KJV: "itching ears" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "knēthomenoi tēn akoēn" — having their
 hearing tickled. They want teachers who scratch their itch — who tell
 them what they want to hear rather than what they need to hear.

v.6 — KJV: "ready to be offered" / ESV: "already" — Greek "spendomai" — poured out as a
 drink offering. Paul sees his death as a sacrifice poured out to God.
 "Departure" — Greek "analuseōs" — loosing, releasing. Like a ship
 loosing its moorings or a tent being struck. Death as departure.

v.7 — Three perfect tenses: "I have fought" (ēgōnismai), "I have finished"
 (teteleka), "I have kept" (tetērēka). Completed actions with ongoing
 results. Done. Finished. Accomplished.

v.8 — KJV: "crown of righteousness" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "stephanos tēs dikaiosunēs" —
 the victor's wreath consisting of righteousness. Not earned by works
 but given by the "righteous judge" to all who love His appearing.

v.10 — KJV: "Demas hath forsaken me, having loved this present world" / ESV: "Demas, in love with this present world" —
 Greek "agapēsas ton nun aiōna" — having loved the present age. Demas
 chose the world over Paul, comfort over suffering, the present over
 the future.

v.13 — KJV: "the books, but especially the parchments" / ESV: "books, and above all the parchments" — "Books" (biblia) =
 papyrus scrolls, possibly OT copies. "Parchments" (membranas) =
 animal-skin documents, possibly Paul's own writings or legal papers.
 Even facing death, Paul wanted to read and study.

v.16 — KJV: "no man stood with me" / ESV: "no one came to stand by me" — At his preliminary hearing (prima
 actio), everyone abandoned him. Like Jesus in Gethsemane. Paul's
 response mirrors Christ's: "I pray God that it may not be laid to
 their charge" (cf. Luke 23:34).

v.17 — KJV: "the mouth of the lion" / ESV: "lion’s mouth" — Possibly: (1) Nero himself, (2) Satan,
 (3) death, or (4) the arena (literal lions). Most likely figurative
 for extreme danger.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.2  — NET: "Preach the message, be ready whether it is
       convenient or not" — "be ready" (epistEthi = stand by/be at
       hand/be urgent). WEB: "be urgent in season and out of
       season." KJV: "be instant in season, out of season." Not
       just when it's comfortable or welcome.

v.3  — NET: "For there will be a time when people will not
       tolerate sound teaching" — "tolerate" (anechontai = put up
       with/endure). WEB: "they will not listen to the sound
       doctrine." KJV: "they will not endure sound doctrine."
       Doctrinal intolerance as a sign of the times.

v.6  — NET: "I am already being poured out as an offering, and
       the time for me to depart is at hand" — "poured out"
       (spendomai = drink offering being poured). WEB: "I am
       already being offered." KJV: "I am now ready to be offered."
       Paul's death as a sacrificial libation — voluntary, worship.

v.7  — NET: "I have competed well; I have finished the race; I
       have kept the faith!" — "competed" (agonismai = fought the
       contest). WEB: "I have fought the good fight." KJV: "I have
       fought a good fight." Three perfect tenses: the contest is
       over, the race is done, the faith is intact.

v.8  — NET: "a crown of righteousness awaits me, which the Lord,
       the righteous Judge, will award to me on that day" — "crown"
       (stephanos = victor's wreath, not diadEma/royal crown). WEB:
       "the crown of righteousness." Not earned by merit but
       awarded by the righteous Judge to all who love His appearing.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Preach the Word — The minister's primary task: proclaim Scripture faithfully,
 whether the audience is receptive or not.

In season, out of season — Whether convenient or inconvenient, whether
 welcomed or rejected. Always ready to proclaim truth.

Itching ears — The desire to hear only what is comfortable and affirming.
 Rejecting truth in favor of pleasant falsehood.

Ready to be offered — Paul's death as a drink offering poured out to God.
 Sacrifice willingly given.

Fought, finished, kept — Paul's three-fold summary: the battle fought, the
 race completed, the faith preserved. A life well-lived.

Crown of righteousness — The victor's reward given by Christ to all who
 faithfully serve and eagerly await His return.

The Lord stood with me — When all human support fails, Christ remains. The
 ultimate companionship in the darkest hour.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Preach the word; be instant in season, out of season" (v.2). Whether
 you're a pastor or not, are you sharing truth when it's convenient AND
 when it's not? Do you speak up even when the audience doesn't want to
 hear it?

2. "They will not endure sound doctrine" (v.3). Do you seek teachers who
 challenge you with truth, or teachers who tell you what you want to hear?
 Are your "ears itching" for comfortable messages?

3. "I have fought a good fight, I have finished my course, I have kept the
 faith" (v.7). At the end of your life, will you be able to say this? What
 needs to change now so you can say it then?

4. "Demas hath forsaken me, having loved this present world" (v.10). What
 "present world" temptations could pull you away from faithfulness? Where
 are you tempted to choose comfort over commitment?

5. "The Lord stood with me" (v.17). When have you experienced the Lord's
 presence in a time of abandonment or loneliness? He never leaves. Can you
 trust that even when everyone else does?

6. Paul wanted his books and parchments (v.13). Even facing death, he wanted
 to read and study. What does this say about lifelong learning? Are you
 still growing, still studying, still hungry for truth?
