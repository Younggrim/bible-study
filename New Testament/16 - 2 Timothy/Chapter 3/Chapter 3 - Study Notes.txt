2 Timothy - Chapter 3
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Paul warns of perilous times in the last days when people will be lovers of self rather than lovers of God, reminds Timothy of his own sufferings, and declares that all Scripture is God-breathed and profitable for teaching, reproof, correction, and training in righteousness.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Paul the Apostle (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 3 warns about the character of the "last days"
(vv.1-9), points to Paul's example of endurance (vv.10-13), and gives one of
the most important statements about Scripture in the Bible (vv.14-17). Paul
contrasts the moral decay of the last days with the sufficiency of Scripture
to equip the man of God for every good work.

Perilous Times (vv.1-9): The last days will be characterized by 18 vices
(vv.2-4) — people who are self-loving, money-loving, pleasure-loving rather
than God-loving. They have "a form of godliness" but deny its power (v.5).
They target vulnerable people (v.6), are "ever learning, and never able to
come to the knowledge of the truth" (v.7). Like Jannes and Jambres who
opposed Moses, they resist truth (v.8).

Paul's Example (vv.10-13): Timothy has observed Paul's doctrine, conduct,
purpose, faith, patience, love, endurance, and persecutions (vv.10-11). "All
that will live godly in Christ Jesus shall suffer persecution" (v.12). Evil
will worsen (v.13).

The Sufficiency of Scripture (vv.14-17): Continue in what you've learned
(v.14). Scripture makes wise unto salvation (v.15). "All scripture is given by
inspiration of God" (v.16) — profitable for doctrine, reproof, correction,
and instruction in righteousness. Result: the man of God is "perfect,
throughly furnished unto all good works" (v.17).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1 — "In the last days there will come times of difficulty." (ESV) —
 Dangerous times predicted.

v.5 — "Having the appearance of godliness, but denying its power." (ESV) —
 Religious appearance without spiritual reality.

v.12 — "All who desire to live a godly life in Christ Jesus will be
 persecuted." (ESV) — Godly living guarantees opposition.

v.14 — "Continue in what you have learned and have firmly believed." (ESV) —
 Persevere in truth despite cultural pressure.

v.16 — "All Scripture is breathed out by God and profitable for teaching, for
 reproof, for correction, and for training in righteousness." (ESV) —
 The inspiration and sufficiency of Scripture.

v.17 — "That the man of God may be complete, equipped for every good work."
 (ESV) — Scripture's purpose: complete equipping.

Charles Spurgeon:
 Sermon: "The Inspiration of Scripture" on v.16 (MTP Vol. 34, No. 2007)
 "ALL Scripture — not some. Given by inspiration of God — God-breathed.
 Not that God inspired the writers and they wrote as best they could — but

 God's words. And they are PROFITABLE — not merely interesting or historical
 but useful, practical, life-changing. For doctrine (what to believe),
 reproof (what's wrong), correction (how to fix it), instruction in

 world because it is a living rebuke to ungodliness."

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "perilous" / ESV: "times of difficulty" — Greek "chalepoi" — difficult, dangerous, hard to
 bear. Same word used of the Gadarene demoniacs (Matt 8:28) — "exceeding
 fierce." These will be fierce, savage times.

v.2-4 — 18 vices in Greek, many beginning with negative prefixes: self-lovers,
 money-lovers, boasters, proud, blasphemers, disobedient to parents,
 unthankful, unholy, without natural affection, truce-breakers, false
 accusers, without self-control, fierce, despisers of good, traitors,
 reckless, conceited, pleasure-lovers rather than God-lovers.

v.5 — KJV: "form of godliness" / ESV: "godliness" — Greek "morphōsin eusebeias" — the outward
 shape/appearance of religion. "Denying the power" — rejecting the
 transforming power of genuine faith. Religion without reality.

v.7 — KJV: "ever learning, and never able to come to the knowledge of the
 truth" / ESV: "always learning and never able to arrive at a knowledge of the truth" — Both equivalent. Intellectual curiosity without commitment. Accumulating
 information without transformation. Religious education without
 conversion.

v.8 — KJV: "Jannes and Jambres" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Traditional names (not in the OT) for
 Pharaoh's magicians who imitated Moses's miracles (Exodus 7:11).
 Jewish tradition preserved their names.

v.16 — KJV: "given by inspiration of God" / ESV: "breathed out by God" — Greek "theopneustos" — God-
 breathed. Not that God breathed INTO the text but that the text was
 breathed OUT by God. Scripture is the product of God's breath — His
 very words expressed through human authors.

v.16 — Four uses of Scripture: "doctrine" (didaskalia — teaching what is
 true), "reproof" (elegmon — exposing what is wrong), "correction"
 (epanorthōsin — restoring to right position), "instruction in
 righteousness" (paideian — training in right living).

v.17 — KJV: "perfect" / ESV: "complete" — Greek "artios" — complete, capable, proficient.
 KJV: "throughly furnished" / ESV: "equipped" — Greek "exērtismenos" — fully equipped. Scripture
 provides everything needed for every good work.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.1  — NET: "in the last days difficult times will come" —
       "difficult" (chalepoi = fierce/hard to bear/dangerous). WEB:
       "In the last days, grievous times will come." KJV: "perilous
       times shall come." The same word describes the Gadarene
       demoniacs (Matt 8:28) — savage, fierce times.

v.5  — NET: "They will maintain the outward appearance of
       religion but will have repudiated its power" — "outward
       appearance" (morphOsin = form/shape without substance). WEB:
       "holding a form of godliness, but having denied its power."
       KJV: "Having a form of godliness, but denying the power
       thereof." Religious exterior, empty interior.

v.12 — NET: "all who desire to live godly lives in Christ Jesus
       will be persecuted" — "all" (pantes) — no exceptions. WEB:
       "all who desire to live godly in Christ Jesus will suffer
       persecution." KJV: "all that will live godly in Christ Jesus
       shall suffer persecution." Guaranteed, not possible.

v.16 — NET: "Every scripture is inspired by God and useful for
       teaching, for reproof, for correction, and for training in
       righteousness" — "inspired" (theopneustos = God-breathed).
       WEB: "Every Scripture is God-breathed." KJV: "All scripture
       is given by inspiration of God." Not that God breathed INTO
       Scripture but that Scripture is breathed OUT by God.

v.17 — NET: "that the person dedicated to God may be capable and
       equipped for every good work" — "capable" (artios = complete/
       fit/ready). WEB: "complete, thoroughly equipped for every
       good work." KJV: "perfect, throughly furnished." Scripture's
       purpose: producing equipped people for God's work.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Perilous times — Dangerous, fierce, difficult seasons in the last days.
 Characterized by moral decay and religious hypocrisy.

Form of godliness — The outward appearance of religion without inner
 spiritual reality or transforming power.

Ever learning, never knowing — Intellectual curiosity without genuine
 conversion. Accumulating knowledge without transformation.

All Scripture — The entire body of sacred writings. Every part is God-
 breathed and profitable.

Inspiration/God-breathed — Greek "theopneustos" — breathed out by God.
 Scripture is God's own words expressed through human authors.

Profitable — Useful, beneficial, practical. Scripture is not merely
 informational but transformational.

Throughly furnished — Completely equipped. Scripture provides everything
 needed for the man of God to do every good work.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Review the vice list (vv.2-4). How many of these characterize our current
 culture? How many are creeping into the church? Into your own life? Be
 honest.

2. "Having a form of godliness, but denying the power thereof" (v.5). Is your
 faith merely formal — going through religious motions — or is there genuine
 transforming power? Where is the gap between your form and your reality?

3. "All that will live godly in Christ Jesus shall suffer persecution" (v.12).
 Are you experiencing any opposition for your faith? If not, why not? Is it
 because you're not living godly enough to provoke the world?

4. "All scripture is given by inspiration of God" (v.16). Do you treat
 Scripture as God's very words — authoritative, sufficient, profitable? Or
 as a human book with good advice? Your view of Scripture determines
 everything.

5. "Throughly furnished unto all good works" (v.17). Scripture is sufficient
 to equip you for EVERY good work. Are you using it for all four purposes:
 doctrine (what to believe), reproof (what's wrong), correction (how to fix
 it), instruction (how to live right)?
