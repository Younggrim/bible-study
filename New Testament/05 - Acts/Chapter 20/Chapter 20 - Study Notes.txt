Acts - Chapter 20
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Paul travels through Macedonia and Greece, raises Eutychus from death in Troas, and delivers his farewell address to the Ephesian elders at Miletus — warning of wolves and commending them to God's grace, declaring he is "innocent of the blood of all."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Luke (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details). The "we"
passages resume in v.5, indicating Luke rejoined Paul's team.

Historical Context: Chapter 20 records Paul's journey from Ephesus through
Macedonia and Greece toward Jerusalem, the raising of Eutychus at Troas
(vv.7-12), and Paul's farewell address to the Ephesian elders at Miletus
(vv.17-38) — one of the most emotional and theologically rich passages in Acts.

Paul's Farewell to the Ephesian Elders (vv.17-38): Paul knows he will never
see them again (v.25). He reviews his ministry among them (vv.18-21), declares
his readiness to suffer (vv.22-24), warns of coming dangers (vv.28-31), and
commends them to God (v.32). This is the only speech in Acts addressed to
Christian leaders — making it a model for pastoral ministry.

"I count not my life dear unto myself" (v.24): Paul's defining statement about
his priorities. His life was not his own — it belonged to Christ and to the
mission. He was willing to lose everything to "finish my course with joy."

"Feed the church of God, which he hath purchased with his own blood" (v.28):
One of the most theologically dense verses in Acts — affirming that the church
belongs to God, was purchased by blood, and must be fed (taught/shepherded) by
its leaders.

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Macedonia and Greece (v.2): Paul traveled through Macedonia (Philippi, Thessalonica, Berea) and spent three months in Greece (likely Corinth).
- Troas (v.6): Paul sailed from Philippi to Troas (on the Aegean coast of Asia Minor). The raising of Eutychus occurred here.
- Miletus (v.15): A coastal city about 30 miles south of Ephesus. Paul summoned the Ephesian elders here for his farewell address, unwilling to stop in Ephesus due to time constraints.

  flock. And this flock was purchased with God's own blood — treat it
  accordingly."

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. "I count not my life dear unto myself" (v.24). Paul's life was not his own
   — it belonged to Christ and the mission. How tightly are you holding onto
   your life — your comfort, your safety, your plans? What would it look like
   to hold your life loosely for Christ's sake?

2. "I have not shunned to declare unto you all the counsel of God" (v.27).
   Paul taught everything — not just the comfortable parts. Are you receiving
   the WHOLE counsel of God, or only the parts you like? Are you teaching
   others the full truth?

3. "Take heed unto yourselves" (v.28). The first responsibility of a leader
   is self-care — guarding your own soul. Before you can feed others, you must
   feed yourself. How is your own spiritual health? Are you taking heed to
   yourself?

4. "It is more blessed to give than to receive" (v.35). A saying of Jesus
   preserved only here. Is your life oriented toward giving or receiving?
   What would it look like to live this principle more fully this week?

1. Paul preached until midnight (v.7) — and Eutychus fell asleep and died.
   Paul raised him and kept preaching until dawn (v.11). His commitment to
   teaching was extraordinary. How committed are you to hearing and teaching
   God's Word?

2. Paul warned that "grievous wolves" would enter the church (v.29) and that
   false teachers would arise from within (v.30). How do you guard against
   false teaching in your own life and church? What are the warning signs?

3. Paul "commended them to God, and to the word of his grace" (v.32). When
   he left, he didn't leave them with a program — he left them with God and
   His Word. Are God and His Word sufficient for you, or do you need something
   more?

4. Paul worked with his own hands (v.34) and supported others. He modeled
   generosity and self-sufficiency. How does your work ethic reflect your
   faith? Are you a giver or a taker?

5. The elders "wept sore" at Paul's departure (v.37). Deep relationships
   marked the early church. How deep are your relationships with other
   believers? Do you have people who would weep at your departure?
