Acts - Chapter 6
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The church appoints seven deacons to serve tables so the apostles can devote themselves to prayer and the word, and Stephen — full of faith and power — performs wonders until false witnesses drag him before the council.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Luke (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 6 records the first internal conflict in the church
(the neglect of Greek-speaking widows), the appointment of the seven deacons,
and the introduction of Stephen — who will become the first Christian martyr
in chapter 7. The church is growing rapidly and facing growing pains.

The Conflict (vv.1-6): Greek-speaking Jewish believers (Hellenists) complained
that their widows were being overlooked in the daily food distribution, while
Hebrew-speaking widows were being served. This was a cultural/linguistic divide
within the church — not a doctrinal one. The apostles' solution was wise:
delegate the practical ministry to qualified men so the apostles could focus
on prayer and the Word.

The Seven (vv.5-6): Seven men were chosen — all with Greek names, suggesting
they were from the very community that felt neglected. The church didn't just
address the problem; they empowered the affected group to lead the solution.
Stephen and Philip are the most prominent of the seven.

Stephen (vv.8-15): Introduced as "full of faith and power" (v.8). He performed
miracles and debated with such wisdom that his opponents "were not able to
resist" (v.10). Unable to defeat him intellectually, they resorted to false
witnesses and brought him before the Sanhedrin. His face appeared "as the face
of an angel" (v.15).

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Jerusalem: The appointment of the seven deacons occurred in Jerusalem as the church grew. The Hellenistic (Greek-speaking) Jews and Hebraic (Aramaic-speaking) Jews were both part of the Jerusalem church.
- The Synagogue of the Freedmen (v.9): A synagogue in Jerusalem attended by Jews from Cyrene, Alexandria, Cilicia, and Asia — showing the diversity of Jerusalem's Jewish population.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.2  — "It is not right that we should give up preaching the word of God to
        serve tables." (ESV) — The apostles' priority: Word and prayer over
        administration.

v.3  — "Pick out from among you seven men of good repute, full of the Spirit
        and of wisdom." (ESV) — The qualifications for service: character,
        Spirit-filling, and wisdom.

v.4  — "But we will devote ourselves to prayer and to the ministry of the
        word." (ESV) — The apostles' two priorities: prayer and preaching.

v.7  — "And the word of God continued to increase, and the number of the
        disciples multiplied greatly in Jerusalem." (ESV) — The result of
        proper leadership structure: growth.

v.10 — "But they could not withstand the wisdom and the Spirit with which he
        was speaking." (ESV) — Stephen's Spirit-empowered wisdom was
        irresistible.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1  — KJV: "Grecians" / ESV: "Hellenists" — Greek-speaking Jewish believers,
       likely from the Diaspora. ESV uses the more precise term "Hellenists."
       "Hebrews" = Aramaic/Hebrew-speaking Jewish believers from Palestine.
       The divide was cultural and linguistic, not doctrinal.

v.2  — KJV: "leave the word of God, and serve tables" / ESV: "give up
       preaching the word of God to serve tables" — ESV makes explicit what
       KJV implies: "leave" means abandoning their preaching ministry. Not
       demeaning the work but distinguishing roles. Both roles require the
       Spirit.

v.3  — KJV: "of honest report, full of the Holy Ghost and wisdom" / ESV:
       "of good repute, full of the Spirit and of wisdom" — Both equivalent.
       The number seven may echo the Jewish practice of appointing seven
       leaders for community administration. These men are traditionally
       called "deacons" though the word isn't used here.

v.15 — KJV: "his face as it had been the face of an angel" / ESV: "his face
       was like the face of an angel" — Both identical in meaning. Stephen's
       face reflected divine glory — like Moses after meeting with God
       (Exodus 34:29). The Sanhedrin was looking at a man filled with God's
       presence.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.3  — NET: "select from among you seven men of good reputation,
       full of the Spirit and of wisdom" — three qualifications for
       deacons: reputation, Spirit-fullness, wisdom. WEB: "seven
       men of good report, full of the Holy Spirit and of wisdom."
       KJV: "seven men of honest report, full of the Holy Ghost
       and wisdom."

v.5  — NET: "They chose Stephen, a man full of faith and of the
       Holy Spirit" — Stephen stands out even among the Spirit-
       filled seven. WEB: "full of faith and of the Holy Spirit."
       He is both the first deacon highlighted and the first
       martyr.

v.7  — NET: "The word of God continued to spread, the number of
       disciples in Jerusalem increased greatly" — Luke's growth
       summaries mark transitions (also 9:31, 12:24, 16:5, 19:20).
       WEB: "The word of God increased." KJV: "the word of God
       increased." The word itself grows — active and alive.

v.10 — NET: "they were not able to resist the wisdom and the
       Spirit with which he spoke" — "resist" (antistEnai =
       withstand/stand against). WEB: "they weren't able to
       withstand the wisdom and the Spirit by which he spoke." When
       the Spirit speaks through a person, human arguments fail.

v.15 — NET: "everyone who was sitting in the council looked
       intently at him and saw his face was like the face of an
       angel" — "face of an angel" — radiant, like Moses after
       Sinai (Exodus 34:29-30). WEB: "his face was like the face
       of an angel." KJV: "as it had been the face of an angel."
       Glory shining through flesh.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Grecians/Hellenists — Greek-speaking Jewish believers, often from the Diaspora
               (Jews living outside Palestine). They spoke Greek as their
               primary language and followed Greek cultural customs.

Hebrews       — Aramaic/Hebrew-speaking Jewish believers from Palestine.
               They maintained traditional Jewish language and customs.

Daily ministration — The daily distribution of food to widows and the poor
               in the church community. An early form of organized charity.

Deacons       — From Greek "diakonos" (servant). Though the word isn't used
               in this passage, these seven men are traditionally considered
               the first deacons — servants appointed for practical ministry.

Libertines    — Freed slaves or descendants of freed slaves — Jews who had
               been enslaved by Rome and later released. They had their own
               synagogue in Jerusalem.

Face of an angel — A visible manifestation of God's glory on Stephen's face
               — supernatural radiance that the Sanhedrin could see.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. The apostles said "we will give ourselves continually to prayer, and to
   the ministry of the word" (v.4). These are the two non-negotiable priorities
   of spiritual leadership. How much of your time is devoted to prayer and
   Scripture? What would need to change to make these your top priorities?

2. The qualifications for serving tables were "full of the Holy Ghost and
   wisdom" (v.3). Even practical, "non-spiritual" tasks require Spirit-filling.
   Do you approach your daily work — your job, your chores, your responsibilities
   — as Spirit-filled service? Or do you reserve "spiritual" for church activities?

3. The church solved an internal conflict by empowering the affected group to
   lead the solution (v.5 — all seven have Greek names). When conflict arises,
   do you empower others or control the outcome? What would it look like to
   trust others with leadership?

4. "The word of God increased" (v.7) — the result of proper priorities and
   structure. When leaders focus on prayer and the Word, and practical needs
   are delegated to qualified servants, the church grows. What structural
   changes might be needed in your community for the Word to increase?

REFLECTION QUESTIONS
------------------------------------
1. The Hellenist widows were being "neglected" (v.1) — not intentionally but
   through oversight. Neglect is often unintentional but still harmful. Who
   in your community might be overlooked or neglected? What would it take to
   notice and address it?

2. The apostles didn't try to do everything themselves — they delegated (v.3).
   Many leaders burn out because they won't delegate. Where do you need to
   release control and trust others with responsibility?

3. Stephen was "full of faith and power" (v.8) — and his opponents "were not
   able to resist the wisdom and the spirit by which he spake" (v.10). When
   you can't be defeated by argument, the world resorts to false accusation
   (v.11). Have you ever been falsely accused because your faith was
   irrefutable? How did you respond?

4. Stephen's face shone "as the face of an angel" (v.15) — even before the
   Sanhedrin that would condemn him. Peace in the face of persecution comes
   from being full of the Spirit. How do you maintain peace and composure
   when facing hostility?

5. The church grew because the apostles focused on their calling and delegated
   the rest (v.7). What is YOUR specific calling? Are you focused on it, or
   are you distracted by things others should be doing?
