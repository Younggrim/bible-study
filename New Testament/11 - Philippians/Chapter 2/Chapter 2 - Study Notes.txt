Philippians - Chapter 2
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Paul calls for unity through humility, presenting Christ's self-emptying as the supreme example — He who was in the form of God took the form of a servant, humbled Himself to death on a cross, and was exalted with the name above every name.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Paul the Apostle (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 2 contains the "Christ Hymn" (vv.5-11) — one of
the most important Christological passages in Scripture. It describes Christ's
voluntary descent from divine glory to human servanthood to death on a cross,
followed by God's exaltation of Him to the highest place. Paul uses this as
the basis for his appeal to humility and unity among the Philippians.

The Appeal to Unity (vv.1-4): Paul urges the Philippians to be like-minded,
humble, and others-focused. "Let nothing be done through strife or vainglory"
(v.3). "Esteem other better than themselves" (v.3). "Look not every man on
his own things" (v.4).

The Christ Hymn (vv.5-11): The supreme example of humility. Christ — being
God — did not cling to His divine privileges but emptied Himself, took the
form of a servant, became human, and died on a cross (vv.6-8). Therefore God
exalted Him to the highest place and gave Him the name above every name (vv.
9-11). This is the pattern: humility precedes exaltation.

Work Out Your Salvation (vv.12-18): "Work out your own salvation with fear and
trembling" (v.12) — not work FOR salvation but work OUT what God has worked IN
(v.13). Live as lights in a dark world (v.15).

Timothy and Epaphroditus (vv.19-30): Paul commends two examples of Christ-like
service: Timothy, who genuinely cares for others (vv.19-24), and Epaphroditus,
who nearly died serving Paul (vv.25-30).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.3  — "Do nothing from selfish ambition or conceit, but in humility count
        others more significant than yourselves." (ESV) — The humility
        principle.

v.5  — "Have this mind among yourselves, which is yours in Christ Jesus."
        (ESV) — Think like Christ thinks.

v.6-7 — "Who, though he was in the form of God, did not count equality with
        God a thing to be grasped, but emptied himself, by taking the form
        of a servant." (ESV) — The Incarnation: God becoming servant.

v.8  — "He humbled himself by becoming obedient to the point of death, even
        death on a cross." (ESV) — The lowest point: crucifixion.

v.9-11 — "Therefore God has highly exalted him and bestowed on him the name
        that is above every name, so that at the name of Jesus every knee
        should bow... and every tongue confess that Jesus Christ is Lord."
        (ESV) — The highest exaltation.

v.12-13 — "Work out your own salvation with fear and trembling, for it is
        God who works in you, both to will and to work for his good
        pleasure." (ESV) — Human responsibility and divine sovereignty
        together.

v.15 — "You shine as lights in the world." (ESV) — Believers as stars in
        darkness.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------

v.6  — KJV: "form of God" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "morphē theou" — the essential nature,
       the very form of God. Not appearance but essence. Christ possessed the
       full divine nature.

v.6  — KJV: "thought it not robbery to be equal with God" — Greek
       "harpagmon" — something to be grasped, seized, clung to. Christ did
       not consider His equality with God something to exploit or hold onto
       selfishly. ESV: "did not count equality with God a thing to be
       grasped." NET: "did not regard equality with God as something to be
       exploited."

v.7  — KJV: "made himself of no reputation" / ESV: "but emptied himself, by taking the form" — Greek "heauton ekenōsen" —
       He emptied Himself. The "kenosis" — Christ's self-emptying. He did not
       empty Himself OF deity but emptied Himself BY taking on humanity. He
       added human nature; He did not subtract divine nature.

v.7  — KJV: "form of a servant" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Same word "morphē" as v.6. Just as He
       truly possessed the nature of God, He truly took on the nature of a
       servant. Full deity + full humanity.

v.8  — KJV: "death of the cross" / ESV: "even death on a cross" — The most shameful, painful, degrading
       form of execution in the Roman world. Reserved for slaves and the
       worst criminals. The lowest possible point of humiliation.

v.9  — KJV: "highly exalted" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "huperupsoō" — super-exalted, exalted
       to the highest possible degree. Used only here in the NT.

v.10 — KJV: "things in heaven, and things in earth, and things under the
       earth" — Three realms: angels, humans, and demons/the dead. Universal
       acknowledgment.

v.12 — KJV: "work out" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "katergazesthe" — to work to completion, to
       carry out to the finish. Not work FOR salvation but work OUT what God
       has already worked IN (v.13). Like working out a math problem — the
       answer is already there; you're bringing it to expression.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.5  — NET: "You should have the same attitude toward one
       another that Christ Jesus had" — "attitude" (phronein =
       mindset/way of thinking). WEB: "Have this in your mind,
       which was also in Christ Jesus." KJV: "Let this mind be in
       you, which was also in Christ Jesus." The Christ Hymn
       (vv.6-11) follows as the model.

v.6  — NET: "who though he existed in the form of God did not
       regard equality with God as something to be grasped" —
       "form" (morphE = essential nature/true form). "Grasped"
       (harpagmon = something to be clutched/exploited). WEB:
       "existing in the form of God, didn't consider equality with
       God a thing to be grasped." He didn't cling to His rights.

v.7  — NET: "but emptied himself by taking on the form of a
       slave" — "emptied" (ekenOsen = kenosis). WEB: "emptied
       himself, taking the form of a servant." KJV: "made himself
       of no reputation." What did He empty Himself OF? Not deity
       but the independent exercise of divine prerogatives.

v.9  — NET: "God exalted him and gave him the name that is above
       every name" — "the name" (to onoma = THE name). WEB: "God
       also highly exalted him." KJV: "God also hath highly exalted
       him." The name is "Lord" (kyrios, v.11) — the divine name
       (Yahweh in the LXX).

v.12 — NET: "work out your own salvation with fear and trembling"
       — "work out" (katergazesthe = bring to completion/carry to
       its goal). WEB: "work out your own salvation with fear and
       trembling." Not work FOR salvation but work OUT what God has
       worked IN (v.13: "God works in you").

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Kenosis        — Greek "kenōsis" — self-emptying. Christ's voluntary
               relinquishment of the independent exercise of divine
               attributes, not the loss of deity itself.

Form of God    — Greek "morphē theou" — the essential divine nature. Christ
               possessed full deity before, during, and after the Incarnation.

Form of a servant — The essential nature of a slave. Christ took on genuine
               human nature in its most humble expression.

Highly exalted — Super-exalted. God's response to Christ's humiliation:
               the highest possible honor and authority.

Name above every name — "Lord" (Kurios) — the divine name. The name that
               commands universal worship and submission.

Work out your salvation — Bring to full expression what God has already
               placed within you. Not earning salvation but living it out.

Fear and trembling — Not terror but reverent awe. Taking seriously the
               responsibility of living out God's work in you.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Esteem other better than themselves" (v.3). This is counter-cultural and
   counter-instinctual. Who do you need to esteem above yourself today? What
   would that look like practically?

2. "Let this mind be in you, which was also in Christ Jesus" (v.5). Christ's
   mind was one of voluntary descent — giving up privilege to serve. Where
   are you clinging to privilege, status, or rights that you could release
   for the sake of others?

3. "Made himself of no reputation" (v.7). In a world obsessed with personal
   brand and reputation, Christ emptied Himself. What would it look like for
   you to care less about your reputation and more about serving?

4. "Work out your own salvation with fear and trembling" (v.12). Are you
   actively working out what God has worked in? Where is your spiritual life
   stagnant? What area needs intentional effort?

5. "It is God which worketh in you" (v.13). The balance: you work, but God
   is the one working in you. How does this relieve pressure while
   maintaining responsibility?

6. "Ye shine as lights in the world" (v.15). In your workplace, neighborhood,
   and relationships — are you shining? Or blending in with the darkness?
