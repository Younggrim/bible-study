1 Timothy - Chapter 5
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Paul gives instructions on honoring widows, supporting elders who labor in the word, and maintaining impartiality — warning Timothy to "keep thyself pure" and to lay hands on no man hastily.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Paul the Apostle (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 5 gives practical instructions for pastoral care
of different groups in the church: older and younger members (vv.1-2), widows
(vv.3-16), and elders (vv.17-25). The extensive section on widows reflects
the first-century reality: without social safety nets, widows were extremely
vulnerable. The church had a formal "list" of widows it supported (v.9). Paul
establishes criteria for who qualifies and who doesn't.

Relating to Different Groups (vv.1-2): Treat older men as fathers, younger
men as brothers, older women as mothers, younger women as sisters — "with all
purity" (v.2).

Caring for Widows (vv.3-16): "Honour widows that are widows indeed" (v.3) —
those truly alone and dependent on God (v.5). Family should provide first
(vv.4, 8, 16). The "enrolled" widow must be 60+, faithful in marriage, known
for good works (vv.9-10). Younger widows should remarry (vv.11-14). The
famous v.8: "If any provide not for his own... he hath denied the faith."

Regarding Elders (vv.17-25): Elders who lead well deserve "double honour" —
especially those who preach and teach (v.17). Don't receive accusations
against elders without witnesses (v.19). Those who sin must be rebuked
publicly (v.20). Don't ordain hastily (v.22). Personal note about Timothy's
health (v.23).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.8 — "If anyone does not provide for his relatives, and especially for
 members of his household, he has denied the faith and is worse than
 an unbeliever." (ESV) — Family provision as a faith issue.

v.17 — "Let the elders who rule well be considered worthy of double honor,
 especially those who labor in preaching and teaching." (ESV) —
 Honoring faithful leaders.

v.19 — "Do not admit a charge against an elder except on the evidence of two
 or three witnesses." (ESV) — Protecting leaders from false
 accusations.

v.21 — "I charge you... to keep these rules without prejudging, doing nothing
 from partiality." (ESV) — Impartiality in church leadership.

v.22 — "Do not be hasty in the laying on of hands." (ESV) — Don't ordain
 hastily.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "rebuke not an elder" / ESV: "rebuke" — Greek "presbuterō" — could mean (1) an
 older man generally, or (2) a church elder specifically. Context
 suggests an older man (contrasted with "younger men"). "Intreat" —
 Greek "parakalei" — appeal to, encourage.

v.3 — KJV: "honour" / ESV: "honor" — Greek "tima" — honor, value, provide for. Includes
 financial support (cf. v.17 "double honour"). "Widows indeed" / ESV: "truly widows" — truly
 destitute widows with no family support.

v.8 — KJV: "provide" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "pronoei" — to think ahead, to plan for, to
 take care of. Provision requires forethought and planning. "Worse than
 an infidel" — even unbelievers care for family.

v.9 — KJV: "taken into the number" / ESV: "enrolled" — Greek "katalegesthō" — enrolled,
 registered on the official list. The church had a formal registry of
 supported widows with specific qualifications.

v.17 — KJV: "double honour" / ESV: "double" — Greek "diplēs timēs" — double honor/payment.
 Likely includes financial compensation (v.18 confirms this). Elders
 who teach well deserve generous support.

v.22 — KJV: "lay hands suddenly on no man" / ESV: "laying on of hands" — Ordination should not be hasty.
 Take time to evaluate character before placing someone in leadership.
 "Neither be partaker of other men's sins" — hasty ordination makes you
 responsible for their failures.

v.23 — KJV: "use a little wine" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Paul's practical health advice. Timothy
 apparently drank only water (perhaps for ascetic reasons). Paul
 recommends medicinal wine for his stomach problems.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.1  — NET: "Do not address an older man harshly but appeal to
       him as a father" — "appeal" (parakalei = encourage/exhort
       gently). WEB: "Don't rebuke an older man, but exhort him
       as a father." KJV: "Rebuke not an elder, but intreat him
       as a father." Age-appropriate pastoral care.

v.8  — NET: "But if someone does not provide for his own relatives
       and especially his immediate family, he has denied the faith
       and is worse than an unbeliever" — "worse than an unbeliever"
       (apistos cheirOn) — even pagans care for family. WEB: "worse
       than an unbeliever." Neglecting family is faith-denial.

v.17 — NET: "Elders who provide effective leadership must be
       counted worthy of double honor" — "double honor" (diplEs
       timEs = double compensation/respect). WEB: "counted worthy
       of double honor." KJV: "worthy of double honour." This
       includes financial support (v.18 — "the laborer is worthy
       of his wages").

v.22 — NET: "Do not lay hands on anyone hastily" — "hastily"
       (tacheOs = quickly/prematurely). WEB: "Lay hands hastily on
       no one." KJV: "Lay hands suddenly on no man." Ordination
       should not be rushed — premature endorsement shares in
       another's potential sins.

v.23 — NET: "use a little wine because of your stomach and your
       frequent illnesses" — a personal aside: Timothy had health
       issues. WEB: "use a little wine for your stomach's sake."
       Paul's pragmatism — neither total abstinence nor excess,
       but medicinal use.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Widows indeed — Truly destitute widows with no family support, devoted to
 God and prayer. Qualified for church financial support.

Provide for his own — Family financial responsibility as a basic expression
 of faith. Failure to provide = denial of the faith.

Double honour — Generous compensation and respect for elders who lead and
 teach well. Both financial and relational honor.

Lay hands suddenly — Hasty ordination without proper evaluation. A warning
 against placing untested people in leadership.

Impartiality — Treating all people equally in church discipline and care.
 No favoritism based on wealth, status, or relationship.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "If any provide not for his own" (v.8). Are you providing for your family
 — financially, emotionally, spiritually? This is your first ministry. How
 are you doing?

2. "Let the elders that rule well be counted worthy of double honour" (v.17).
 Do you honor your church leaders — with respect, encouragement, and
 financial generosity? How can you better support those who serve you
 spiritually?

3. "Doing nothing by partiality" (v.21). Are you impartial in how you treat
 people? Do you show favoritism to the wealthy, attractive, or powerful?
 How can you practice genuine impartiality?

4. "Lay hands suddenly on no man" (v.22). In your sphere of influence, are
 you careful about who you endorse, promote, or recommend? Hasty
 endorsement can lead to shared responsibility for failure.

5. How do you balance caring for the vulnerable (widows, the poor) with
 maintaining healthy boundaries and expectations (vv.3-16)?
