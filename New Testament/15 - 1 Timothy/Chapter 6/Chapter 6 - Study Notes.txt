1 Timothy - Chapter 6
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Paul addresses slaves and masters, warns that the love of money is the root of all evil, charges Timothy to "fight the good fight of faith," and commands the rich to be generous — guarding the deposit of truth entrusted to him.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Paul the Apostle (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 6 concludes 1 Timothy with instructions about
servants and masters (vv.1-2), a warning against false teachers who use
religion for profit (vv.3-5), the famous passage on contentment and the love
of money (vv.6-10), a personal charge to Timothy (vv.11-16), instructions for
the wealthy (vv.17-19), and a final appeal to guard the deposit (vv.20-21).

Servants and Masters (vv.1-2): Servants should honor their masters so God's
name isn't blasphemed (v.1). Those with believing masters should serve even
better — not less — because they're brothers (v.2).

False Teachers and Greed (vv.3-5): Those who teach differently are proud,
ignorant, and obsessed with controversies (vv.3-4). They suppose "gain is
godliness" — using religion for profit (v.5).

Contentment and Money (vv.6-10): "Godliness with contentment is great gain"
(v.6). We brought nothing in and take nothing out (v.7). Food and clothing
are enough (v.8). Those who desire riches fall into temptation (v.9). "The
love of money is the root of all evil" (v.10).

Charge to Timothy (vv.11-16): Flee greed; pursue righteousness, godliness,
faith, love, patience, meekness (v.11). "Fight the good fight of faith" (v.12).
Keep the commandment until Christ's appearing (v.14). Magnificent doxology to
God as King of kings (vv.15-16).

Instructions for the Rich (vv.17-19): Don't be arrogant or trust in wealth.
Trust in God. Be generous, ready to share (vv.17-18). Store up treasure for
the future (v.19).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.6 — "But godliness with contentment is great gain." (ESV) — The true
 wealth: godliness + contentment.

v.9 — "Those who desire to be rich fall into temptation, into a snare, into
 many senseless and harmful desires that plunge people into ruin." (ESV)
 — The danger of desiring wealth.

v.10 — "For the love of money is a root of all kinds of evils." (ESV) — Not
 money itself but the LOVE of money.

v.11 — "But as for you, O man of God, flee these things. Pursue
 righteousness, godliness, faith, love, steadfastness, gentleness."
 (ESV) — Flee and follow: the two movements of holiness.

v.12 — "Fight the good fight of the faith. Take hold of the eternal life."
 (ESV) — The Christian life as a fight worth fighting.

v.15-16 — "The blessed and only Sovereign, the King of kings and Lord of
 lords, who alone has immortality, who dwells in unapproachable
 light." (ESV) — God's transcendent majesty.

v.20 — "O Timothy, guard the deposit entrusted to you." (ESV) — Guard the
 deposit of truth.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.5 — KJV: "supposing that gain is godliness" / ESV: "godliness is a means of gain" — Greek "nomizontōn porismon
 einai tēn eusebeian" — thinking godliness is a means of profit. Using
 religion to get rich. The prosperity gospel in seed form.

v.6 — KJV: "contentment" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "autarkeia" — self-sufficiency,
 independence from external circumstances. Same word Paul uses in
 Philippians 4:11. Not self-sufficiency apart from God but sufficiency
 in God apart from circumstances.

v.9 — KJV: "will be rich" / ESV: "rich" — Greek "boulomenoi ploutein" — those who
 determine/resolve to be rich. Not those who happen to have wealth but
 those who set their hearts on acquiring it.

v.10 — KJV: "the root of all evil" / ESV: "root of all kinds of evils" — Greek "rhiza pantōn tōn kakōn" — a
 root of all kinds of evil. Some translations: "a root of all kinds of
 evils." The love of money is behind every category of sin.

v.12 — KJV: "fight" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "agōnizou" — agonize, contend, compete. Root
 of English "agonize." The Christian life requires intense effort.
 "Lay hold" — Greek "epilabou" — seize, grab firmly.

v.15 — KJV: "Potentate" / ESV: "Sovereign" — Greek "dunastēs" — sovereign ruler, one with
 absolute power. "King of kings" — supreme over all earthly rulers.

v.16 — KJV: "dwelling in the light which no man can approach unto" / ESV: "unapproachable light" — God's
 unapproachable holiness. His glory is so intense that no mortal can
 come near. "Whom no man hath seen, nor can see" — God in His essence
 is invisible to human eyes.

v.20 — KJV: "that which is committed to thy trust" / ESV: "entrusted" — Greek "tēn
 parathēkēn" — the deposit, the entrusted treasure. The body of
 apostolic truth entrusted to Timothy to guard and pass on.

v.20 — KJV: "science falsely so called" / ESV: "falsely called" — Greek "pseudōnumou gnōseōs" —
 falsely named knowledge. Early Gnosticism — claiming special
 "knowledge" (gnōsis) that contradicted the gospel.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.6  — NET: "godliness with contentment is great gain" —
       "contentment" (autarkeias = self-sufficiency/satisfaction
       with what one has). WEB: "godliness with contentment is
       great gain." KJV: "godliness with contentment is great
       gain." The combination — not godliness alone, not
       contentment alone, but both together.

v.10 — NET: "For the love of money is the root of all evils" —
       "root of ALL evils" (rhiza pantOn tOn kakOn). WEB: "the
       love of money is a root of all kinds of evil." KJV: "the
       love of money is the root of all evil." Note: love of money,
       not money itself. NET/KJV: "all evils"; WEB: "all kinds of
       evil" — the scope is debated.

v.12 — NET: "Compete well for the faith and be grasped by eternal
       life" — "compete" (agOnizou = agonize/strive). WEB: "Fight
       the good fight of faith." KJV: "Fight the good fight of
       faith." Athletic/military imagery — active exertion, not
       passive reception.

v.15 — NET: "the blessed and only Sovereign, the King of kings
       and Lord of lords" — "Sovereign" (dynastEs = Potentate/
       Ruler). WEB: "the blessed and only Ruler." KJV: "the
       blessed and only Potentate." A title later applied to
       Christ (Rev 19:16) here applied to God.

v.20 — NET: "guard what has been entrusted to you" — "what has
       been entrusted" (tEn parathEkEn = the deposit). WEB: "guard
       that which is committed to you." KJV: "keep that which is
       committed to thy trust." The gospel is a deposit to be
       guarded, not a product to be innovated upon.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Godliness with contentment — True spiritual wealth: knowing God and being
 satisfied with His provision. The greatest gain.

Love of money — Not money itself but the heart's attachment to it. The
 desire for wealth that becomes an idol and root of all evil.

Root of all evil — The love of money as the underlying cause behind every
 category of sin. Covetousness at the base of all wrongdoing.

Man of God — A title for Timothy (and all who serve God). One who belongs
 to God and represents Him.

Fight the good fight — The Christian life as noble combat. Intense effort
 in a worthy cause with an eternal prize.

King of kings — God's supreme sovereignty over all earthly rulers. No
 authority exists apart from His.

The deposit — The body of apostolic truth entrusted to faithful ministers
 to guard, teach, and pass on to the next generation.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Godliness with contentment is great gain" (v.6). Are you content? What
 would "enough" look like for you? Can you say that food and clothing are
 sufficient (v.8)?

2. "They that will be rich fall into temptation" (v.9). Examine your heart:
 is acquiring wealth a driving motivation? What "foolish and hurtful lusts"
 might the desire for money be producing in your life?

3. "The love of money is the root of all evil" (v.10). Where does the love
 of money show up in your decisions, priorities, and anxieties? How can you
 loosen money's grip on your heart?

4. "Flee these things; and follow after righteousness" (v.11). The Christian
 life has two movements: fleeing sin and pursuing virtue. What do you need
 to flee? What do you need to pursue?

5. "Fight the good fight of faith" (v.12). Are you fighting or coasting?
 What battles are you currently engaged in? Are they the RIGHT battles —
 good fights, not petty ones?

6. For those with financial resources (vv.17-19): Are you generous? Ready to
 share? Storing up treasure in heaven? Or trusting in "uncertain riches"?
