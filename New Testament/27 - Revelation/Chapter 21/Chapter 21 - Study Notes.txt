Revelation - Chapter 21
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
A new heaven and new earth appear, the holy city New Jerusalem descends as a bride adorned for her husband, God dwells with men wiping every tear — "Behold, I make all things new" — the city of pure gold with twelve gates and no need of sun, for the Lamb is its light.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: The Apostle John (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Historical Context: Chapter 21 is the Bible's grand finale — the new creation.
After judgment (ch.20), God makes "all things new" (v.5). The new heaven, new
earth, and New Jerusalem descend from God. This chapter answers every longing
of the human heart: God dwelling with His people, no more death or tears, all
things made new. It is the reversal of Genesis 3 — paradise restored and
surpassed. The curse is gone; God's presence is unmediated.

The New Creation (vv.1-8): New heaven and new earth — the first passed away
(v.1). No more sea (v.1). The New Jerusalem descends "as a bride adorned for
her husband" (v.2). God dwells with men — "the tabernacle of God is with men"
(v.3). No more tears, death, sorrow, crying, or pain (v.4). "Behold, I make
all things new" (v.5). "It is done" (v.6). Overcomers inherit all things (v.7).
The fearful, unbelieving, etc. — the lake of fire (v.8).

The New Jerusalem Described (vv.9-27): The city shown as "the bride, the
Lamb's wife" (v.9). Descending from heaven with God's glory (vv.10-11).
Twelve gates (tribes of Israel), twelve foundations (apostles) (vv.12-14).
Measured: a perfect cube — 12,000 furlongs (about 1,400 miles) in each
dimension (vv.15-17). Walls of jasper, city of pure gold like glass (v.18).
Foundations adorned with precious stones (vv.19-20). Gates of pearl, streets
of gold (v.21). No temple — God and the Lamb ARE the temple (v.22). No sun
or moon — God's glory is the light (v.23). Nations walk in its light (v.24).
Gates never shut (v.25). Nothing unclean enters (v.27).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1 — "Then I saw a new heaven and a new earth, for the first heaven and the
 first earth had passed away." (ESV) — New creation.

v.3 — "The dwelling place of God is with man. He will dwell with them, and
 they will be his people, and God himself will be with them as their
 God." (ESV) — God's presence unmediated.

v.4 — "He will wipe away every tear from their eyes, and death shall be no
 more, neither shall there be mourning, nor crying, nor pain anymore,
 for the former things have passed away." (ESV) — The end of all
 suffering.

v.5 — "Behold, I am making all things new." (ESV) — God's creative renewal.

v.22 — "I saw no temple in the city, for its temple is the Lord God the
 Almighty and the Lamb." (ESV) — No need for a temple — God IS the
 temple.

v.23 — "The city has no need of sun... for the glory of God gives it light,
 and its lamp is the Lamb." (ESV) — God's glory as light.

Charles Spurgeon:
 Sermon: "All Things New" on v.5 (MTP Vol. 11, No. 651)

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "I saw a new heaven and a new earth" / ESV: "Then I saw a new
 heaven and a new earth" — Both equivalent. Greek "kainon" — new in
 quality/character, not just in time (neos). A renewed, transformed
 creation — not a different one but a glorified one. KJV: "there was no
 more sea" / ESV: "the sea was no more" — Both equivalent. The sea in
 Revelation symbolizes chaos, evil, separation. No more chaos, no more
 separation.
       NET: "Then I saw a new heaven and a new earth, for the
       first heaven and earth had passed away" — "new" (kainon =
       new in quality/character, not neos/new in time). WEB: "a
       new heaven and a new earth." Not replacement but renewal —
       transformed creation, not annihilation.

v.2 — KJV: "prepared as a bride adorned for her husband" / ESV: "prepared
 as a bride adorned for her husband" — Both identical.
       NET: "the holy city — the new Jerusalem — descending out
       of heaven from God, made ready like a bride adorned for her
       husband" — "adorned" (kekosmEmenEn = beautifully arranged —
       English "cosmetics"). WEB: "prepared like a bride adorned
       for her husband." The city IS the bride — the church in
       its final, glorified form.

v.3 — KJV: "the tabernacle of God is with men, and he will dwell with them"
 / ESV: "the dwelling place of God is with man. He will dwell with them"
 — ESV uses "dwelling place" for Greek "skēnē" (tent/tabernacle). KJV's
 "tabernacle" preserves the OT connection. God "tents" among His people
 — the ultimate fulfillment of the Incarnation (John 1:14).
       NET: "the dwelling of God is with mankind! He will live
       among them" — "dwelling" (skEnE = tabernacle/tent). WEB:
       "God's dwelling is with people." KJV: "the tabernacle of God
       is with men." The tabernacle/temple trajectory reaches its
       goal — God dwelling permanently among His people with no
       barrier.

v.4 — KJV: "God shall wipe away all tears from their eyes; and there shall
 be no more death, neither sorrow, nor crying, neither shall there be any
 more pain" / ESV: "He will wipe away every tear from their eyes, and
 death shall be no more, neither shall there be mourning, nor crying, nor
 pain anymore" — ESV uses "mourning" (Greek "penthos") for KJV's
 "sorrow." Both equivalent in meaning.
       NET: "He will wipe away every tear from their eyes, and
       death will not exist any more" — "wipe away" (exaleipsei =
       completely remove). WEB: "He will wipe away every tear."
       KJV: "God shall wipe away all tears." The personal tenderness
       — God Himself touching faces, removing grief.

v.5 — KJV: "Behold, I make all things new" / ESV: "Behold, I am making all
 things new" — ESV's present tense "am making" (Greek "kaina poiō
 panta") emphasizes the ongoing nature of renewal. Both equivalent.
       NET: "Look! I am making all things new!" — "making new"
       (kaina poiO = creating anew). WEB: "Behold, I am making all
       things new." KJV: "Behold, I make all things new." Present
       tense — the renewal is not just future but already in
       process. Not "all new things" but "all things NEW.

v.6 — KJV: "It is done. I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the end. I
 will give unto him that is athirst of the fountain of the water of life
 freely" / ESV: "It is done! I am the Alpha and the Omega, the beginning
 and the end. To the thirsty I will give from the spring of the water of
 life without payment" — ESV's "without payment" (Greek "dōrean" —
 freely/as a gift) clarifies KJV's "freely."

v.7 — KJV: "He that overcometh shall inherit all things" / ESV: "The one who
 conquers will have this heritage" — ESV uses "conquers" (Greek "nikōn")
 for KJV's "overcometh" and "heritage" for "inherit all things."

v.8 — KJV: "the fearful, and unbelieving, and the abominable" / ESV: "the
 cowardly, the faithless, the detestable" — ESV's "cowardly" (Greek
 "deilois") is more precise than KJV's "fearful." ESV's "faithless"
 clarifies KJV's "unbelieving."

v.11 — KJV: "her light was like unto a stone most precious, even like a
 jasper stone, clear as crystal" / ESV: "its radiance like a most rare
 jewel, like a jasper, clear as crystal" — ESV uses "radiance" (Greek
 "phōstēr" — luminary/light-bearer) rather than "light."

v.16 — KJV: "twelve thousand furlongs" / ESV: "12,000 stadia" — Both
 equivalent (about 1,400 miles). A perfect cube — like the Holy of Holies
 (1 Kings 6:20). The entire city is the Most Holy Place — God's
 unmediated presence everywhere.

v.17 — KJV: "an hundred and forty and four cubits, according to the measure
 of a man, that is, of the angel" / ESV: "144 cubits by human
 measurement, which is also an angel's measurement" — Both equivalent.
 ESV clarifies that human and angelic measurements are the same.

v.22 — KJV: "I saw no temple therein: for the Lord God Almighty and the Lamb
 are the temple of it" / ESV: "I saw no temple in the city, for its
 temple is the Lord God the Almighty and the Lamb" — Both equivalent.
 Stunning: God Himself IS the temple — His presence fills everything. No
 building needed.

v.23 — KJV: "the glory of God did lighten it, and the Lamb is the light
 thereof" / ESV: "the glory of God gives it light, and its lamp is the
 Lamb" — ESV uses "lamp" (Greek "luchnos") for the Lamb's role — He is
 the vessel through which God's glory shines. Christ as the eternal
 source of illumination.

v.27 — KJV: "there shall in no wise enter into it any thing that defileth,
 neither whatsoever worketh abomination, or maketh a lie" / ESV: "nothing
 unclean will ever enter it, nor anyone who does what is detestable or
 false" — ESV is more concise. KJV: "the Lamb's book of life" / ESV:
 "the Lamb's book of life" — Both identical.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
New heaven and new earth — The renewed, glorified creation. Sin, death, and
 curse removed. Paradise restored and surpassed.

New Jerusalem — The holy city descending from God. The eternal dwelling of
 God's people. Both a city and a bride.

Tabernacle of God with men — God's unmediated, permanent presence with His
 people. The ultimate fulfillment of "Immanuel."

No more tears/death/pain — The complete removal of all consequences of sin.
 The former things passed away permanently.

All things new — God's creative renewal of everything. Not patching the old
 but making genuinely new.

No temple — God's presence so pervasive that no building is needed to
 contain it. He IS the temple.

Lamb is the light — Christ as the eternal source of illumination. No sun
 needed — God's glory lights everything.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "God shall wipe away all tears" (v.4). Every sorrow you've experienced
 has an expiration date. God Himself will personally remove every tear.
 Does this hope sustain you in current suffering?

2. "Behold, I make all things new" (v.5). God is not done creating. He will
 renew everything — including you. What in your life needs His renewing
 touch today?

3. "The tabernacle of God is with men" (v.3). The ultimate goal of
 redemption: God dwelling with His people. No barriers, no separation, no
 veil. Are you living now in the reality of His presence?

4. "No temple therein" (v.22). In eternity, you won't need a building to
 meet God — He will be everywhere. But even now, you ARE His temple (1 Cor
 3:16). Do you live like it?

5. This chapter is your destination. Everything you endure now is temporary;
 this is permanent. Does the certainty of the new creation change how you
 handle today's difficulties?
