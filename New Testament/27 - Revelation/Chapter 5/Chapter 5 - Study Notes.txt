Revelation - Chapter 5
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
A scroll sealed with seven seals and no one worthy to open it — until the Lion of Judah, the Lamb who was slain, takes the scroll, and all heaven erupts: "Worthy is the Lamb that was slain to receive power, and riches, and wisdom."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: The Apostle John (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Historical Context: Chapter 5 continues the throne room scene from chapter 4.
The focus shifts from God the Creator (ch.4) to Christ the Redeemer (ch.5).
A sealed scroll appears — representing God's plan for history/judgment — and
no one is worthy to open it. John weeps until the Lion of Judah (who is also
the slain Lamb) steps forward. This chapter contains some of the most
magnificent worship in all of Scripture.

The Sealed Scroll (vv.1-4): A scroll sealed with seven seals in God's right
hand (v.1). A strong angel asks: "Who is worthy to open the book?" (v.2). No
one in heaven, earth, or under the earth is found worthy (v.3). John weeps
greatly (v.4).

The Lion Who Is a Lamb (vv.5-7): An elder says: "The Lion of the tribe of
Juda, the Root of David, hath prevailed" (v.5). But when John looks, he sees
not a lion but "a Lamb as it had been slain" (v.6) — with seven horns
(complete power) and seven eyes (complete knowledge/Spirit). The Lamb takes
the scroll (v.7).

The New Song (vv.8-10): The creatures and elders fall before the Lamb (v.8).
Golden vials of incense = the prayers of saints (v.8). They sing: "Thou art
worthy... for thou wast slain, and hast redeemed us to God by thy blood out
of every kindred, and tongue, and people, and nation" (v.9).

Universal Worship (vv.11-14): Myriads of angels join: "Worthy is the Lamb
that was slain to receive power, and riches, and wisdom, and strength, and
honour, and glory, and blessing" (v.12). Every creature in all creation
worships (v.13). The elders fall down (v.14).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.5 — "The Lion of the tribe of Judah, the Root of David, has conquered, so
 that he can open the scroll." (ESV) — Christ as conquering Lion.

v.6 — "A Lamb standing, as though it had been slain." (ESV) — Christ as
 sacrificed Lamb. The Lion conquers BY being the slain Lamb.

v.9 — "Worthy are you to take the scroll and to open its seals, for you were
 slain, and by your blood you ransomed people for God from every tribe
 and language and people and nation." (ESV) — The new song of redemption.

v.12 — "Worthy is the Lamb who was slain, to receive power and wealth and
 wisdom and might and honor and glory and blessing!" (ESV) — Seven-
 fold praise to the Lamb.

v.13 — "To him who sits on the throne and to the Lamb be blessing and honor
 and glory and might forever and ever!" (ESV) — Universal worship of
 God and the Lamb.

Charles Spurgeon:
 Sermon: "Worthy Is the Lamb" on v.12 (MTP Vol. 10, No. 561)
 "Worthy is the Lamb THAT WAS SLAIN — not despite being slain but BECAUSE
 He was slain. His worthiness is in His sacrifice. He conquered by dying.
 He earned all authority by giving up all authority. Seven things He is
 worthy to receive: power, riches, wisdom, strength, honour, glory,
 blessing. All of them — because He was slain. The cross is not His shame
 — it is His crown."
 Sermon: "The Lion and the Lamb" on vv.5-6 (MTP Vol. 20, No. 1196)
 "John hears 'the Lion' but sees 'a Lamb.' This is the great paradox of
 Christ: He conquers as a Lion BY being a slain Lamb. His power is in His
 sacrifice. His victory is through His death. He doesn't overcome by force
 but by blood. The Lion's roar is the Lamb's bleat. The throne is won by
 the cross."

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "book" / ESV: "scroll" — Greek "biblion" — a scroll. ESV
 correctly renders this as "scroll" (the codex/book form didn't exist
 yet). Written on both sides (unusual — indicating fullness of content).
 Sealed with seven seals — completely sealed, requiring complete
 authority to open.

v.5 — KJV: "prevailed" / ESV: "conquered" — Greek "enikēsen" — conquered,
 overcame. ESV's "conquered" is more precise. The Lion conquered — but
 His method of conquest was the cross (v.6).

v.6 — KJV: "as it had been slain" / ESV: "as though it had been slain" —
 Greek "hōs esphagmenon" — as having been slaughtered. Both equivalent
 in meaning. The Lamb bears the marks of slaughter — even in glory, the
 wounds remain. "Seven horns" = complete power. "Seven eyes" = complete
 knowledge/the fullness of the Spirit.

v.8 — KJV: "odours, which are the prayers of saints" / ESV: "incense, which
 are the prayers of the saints" — Greek "thumiamata" — incense. ESV
 translates directly; KJV uses "odours" (fragrances). The prayers of
 God's people are precious — stored in golden bowls, presented before
 the throne.

v.9 — KJV: "a new song" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "ōdēn kainēn" — a song never sung before.
 New because redemption is new — unprecedented, unrepeatable.

v.9 — KJV: "every kindred, and tongue, and people, and nation" / ESV: "every
 tribe and language and people and nation" — KJV "kindred" = ESV
 "tribe" (Greek "phulēs"); KJV "tongue" = ESV "language" (Greek
 "glōssēs"). Both equivalent; ESV uses modern vocabulary. Four terms
 for universality. Redemption spans every ethnic, linguistic, cultural,
 and political boundary.

v.12 — KJV: "power, and riches, and wisdom, and strength, and honour, and
 glory, and blessing" / ESV: "power and wealth and wisdom and might and
 honor and glory and blessing" — KJV "riches" = ESV "wealth" (Greek
 "ploutos"); KJV "strength" = ESV "might" (Greek "ischus"). Seven
 attributes: power (dunamis), riches/wealth (ploutos), wisdom (sophia),
 strength/might (ischus), honour/honor (timē), glory (doxa), blessing
 (eulogia). Complete, perfect praise.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.5  — NET: "the Lion of the tribe of Judah, the root of
       David, has conquered" — "Lion" and "root" — messianic
       titles from Genesis 49:9 and Isaiah 11:1/10. WEB: "the
       Lion who is of the tribe of Judah." But when John LOOKS,
       he sees a LAMB (v.6) — the Lion conquers as a Lamb.

v.6  — NET: "a Lamb standing as though it had been killed" —
       "as though killed" (hOs esphagmenon = as having been
       slaughtered). WEB: "a Lamb standing, as though it had been
       slain." KJV: "a Lamb as it had been slain." The Lamb bears
       permanent marks of slaughter yet stands alive — resurrection
       body with crucifixion scars.

v.9  — NET: "You are worthy to take the scroll and to open its
       seals because you were killed, and at the cost of your own
       blood you have purchased for God persons from every tribe"
       — "purchased" (Egorasas = bought in the marketplace). WEB:
       "you bought us for God with your blood." Redemption as
       commercial transaction — the Lamb's blood as purchase price.

v.12 — NET: "Worthy is the Lamb who was killed to receive power
       and wealth and wisdom and might and honor and glory and
       praise!" — seven attributes (power, wealth, wisdom, might,
       honor, glory, praise). WEB: "Worthy is the Lamb who has
       been killed." Seven = completeness. The Lamb deserves ALL.

v.13 — NET: "To the one seated on the throne and to the Lamb be
       praise and honor and glory and ruling power forever" —
       "to the one on the throne AND to the Lamb" — equal worship
       to God and Christ. WEB: "To him who sits on the throne, and
       to the Lamb." Co-worship = co-deity.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Sealed scroll — God's plan for history and judgment. Completely sealed —
 requiring absolute authority and worthiness to open.

Lion of Judah — Christ as the conquering King from David's line. Powerful,
 royal, victorious.

Lamb as slain — Christ as the sacrificed Lamb. Still bearing the marks of
 slaughter even in glory. His sacrifice is eternal.

Seven horns — Complete, perfect power. Horns symbolize strength/authority
 in biblical imagery.

Seven eyes — Complete knowledge and the fullness of the Spirit. All-
 seeing, all-knowing.

New song — A song of redemption never sung before. Celebrating what
 Christ accomplished through His death.

Prayers of saints — The prayers of God's people, stored as incense in golden
 bowls. Precious, preserved, presented before God.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "No man was found worthy" (v.3). Apart from Christ, no one can accomplish
 God's purposes. Are you trying to be your own savior, your own solution?
 Only the Lamb is worthy.

2. "A Lamb as it had been slain" (v.6). Christ conquers through sacrifice,
 not force. How does this "upside-down" power change your understanding of
 strength and victory?

3. "Redeemed us to God by thy blood out of every kindred, and tongue" (v.9).
 Heaven is multi-ethnic, multi-cultural, multi-lingual. Does your life and
 your church reflect this diversity?

4. "Worthy is the Lamb" (v.12). Is Christ worthy of your complete devotion —
 all seven attributes: your power, riches, wisdom, strength, honor, glory,
 and blessing? Are you giving Him all of it?

5. "The prayers of saints" (v.8). Your prayers are stored in golden bowls
 before God's throne. They are precious to Him. Does this motivate you to
 pray more?
