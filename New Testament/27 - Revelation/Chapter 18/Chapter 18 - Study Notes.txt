Revelation - Chapter 18
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
"Babylon the great is fallen, is fallen" — heaven rejoices while kings and merchants weep over her destruction in one hour, and God's people are called to come out of her lest they share in her plagues.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: The Apostle John (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Historical Context: Chapter 18 describes the fall of Babylon in detail — the economic/commercial aspect of the world system. While chapter 17 focused on religious Babylon (the prostitute), chapter 18 focuses on commercial Babylon (the city). The chapter is structured as a funeral dirge: heaven rejoices while earth mourns. Kings, merchants, and sailors lament the loss of their wealth, while God's people are called to come out and rejoice.

Babylon's Fall Announced (vv.1-3): A powerful angel declares: "Babylon the great is fallen" (v.2). She has become a dwelling of demons (v.2). All nations participated in her corruption (v.3).

Call to Separate (vv.4-8): "Come out of her, my people" (v.4). Don't share her sins or plagues. Her sins have "reached unto heaven" (v.5). Her judgment comes "in one day" (v.8).

Three Laments (vv.9-19): Kings lament (vv.9-10): "Alas, alas, that great city!" Merchants lament (vv.11-17a): no one buys their goods anymore — including "slaves, and souls of men" (v.13). Sailors lament (vv.17b-19): "in one hour is she made desolate."

Heaven Rejoices (v.20): "Rejoice over her, thou heaven, and ye holy apostles and prophets; for God hath avenged you on her."

Final Destruction (vv.21-24): A millstone cast into the sea — "thus with violence shall that great city Babylon be thrown down" (v.21). No more music, craft, light, or marriage (vv.22-23). "In her was found the blood of prophets, and of saints" (v.24).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.2 — "Fallen, fallen is Babylon the great!" (ESV) — The world system's doom.

v.4 — "Come out of her, my people, lest you take part in her sins." (ESV) — The call to separation.

v.7 — "In her heart she says, 'I sit as a queen, I am no widow, and mourning I shall never see.'" (ESV) — Self-deception before destruction.

v.10 — "In a single hour your judgment has come." (ESV) — Sudden, total collapse.

v.20 — "Rejoice over her, O heaven, and you saints and apostles and prophets, for God has given judgment for you against her!" (ESV) — Heaven's joy at justice done.

v.23 — "All nations were deceived by your sorcery." (ESV) — Babylon's method: deception.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "the earth was lightened with his glory" / ESV: "the earth was
 made bright with his glory" — Both equivalent. The angel's glory
 illuminates the entire earth.

v.2 — KJV: "the habitation of devils, and the hold of every foul spirit,
 and a cage of every unclean and hateful bird" / ESV: "a dwelling place
 for demons, a haunt for every unclean spirit, a haunt for every unclean
 bird, a haunt for every unclean and detestable beast" — ESV uses
 "haunt" (Greek "phulakē" — prison/guard-post) for KJV's "hold" and
 "cage." ESV adds "detestable beast" following better manuscripts.

v.3 — KJV: "the wine of the wrath of her fornication" / ESV: "the wine of
 the passion of her sexual immorality" — ESV uses "passion" (Greek
 "thumos") rather than "wrath," and "sexual immorality" (Greek "porneia")
 rather than "fornication."

v.4 — KJV: "Come out of her, my people, that ye be not partakers of her
 sins" / ESV: "Come out of her, my people, lest you take part in her
 sins" — Both equivalent. Greek "exelthate" — aorist imperative: come
 out now, decisively. Echoes God's call to Lot (Gen 19), to Israel from
 Egypt, and to exiles from Babylon.

v.7 — KJV: "I sit a queen, and am no widow, and shall see no sorrow" / ESV:
 "I sit as a queen, I am no widow, and mourning I shall never see" —
 Both equivalent. ESV uses "mourning" for Greek "penthos" rather than
 KJV's "sorrow."

v.8 — KJV: "strong is the Lord God who judgeth her" / ESV: "mighty is the
 Lord God who has judged her" — ESV uses past tense "has judged" (Greek
 "krinas" — aorist participle), emphasizing the certainty of the
 completed judgment.

v.10 — KJV: "Alas, alas, that great city Babylon, that mighty city!" / ESV:
 "Alas! Alas! You great city, you mighty city, Babylon!" — Both
 equivalent. ESV's direct address ("You") is more dramatic.

v.13 — KJV: "slaves, and souls of men" / ESV: "slaves, that is, human
 souls" — Greek "sōmatōn, kai psuchas anthrōpōn" — bodies and souls of
 men. ESV's "that is" treats the second phrase as explanatory. The
 ultimate merchandise: human beings. Babylon trades in people.

v.17 — KJV: "in one hour so great riches is come to nought" / ESV: "in a
 single hour all this wealth has been laid waste" — Both equivalent.
 Repeated three times (vv.10, 17, 19). Babylon's collapse is sudden and
 total. What took centuries to build falls in an hour.

v.20 — KJV: "Rejoice over her, thou heaven, and ye holy apostles and
 prophets; for God hath avenged you on her" / ESV: "Rejoice over her, O
 heaven, and you saints and apostles and prophets, for God has given
 judgment for you against her!" — ESV adds "saints" and uses "given
 judgment for you against her" rather than "avenged you on her." ESV
 emphasizes judicial vindication rather than personal vengeance.

v.21 — KJV: "with violence shall that great city Babylon be thrown down" /
 ESV: "So will Babylon the great city be thrown down with violence" —
 Both equivalent. The millstone imagery: sudden, violent, irreversible.

v.23 — KJV: "by thy sorceries were all nations deceived" / ESV: "all
 nations were deceived by your sorcery" — Both equivalent. Greek
 "pharmakeia" — drugs/sorcery. Babylon deceives nations through
 intoxication — literal and spiritual.

v.24 — KJV: "in her was found the blood of prophets, and of saints, and of
 all that were slain upon the earth" / ESV: "in her was found the blood
 of prophets and of saints, and of all who have been slain on earth" —
 Both equivalent.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.2  — NET: "Fallen, fallen, is Babylon the great!" — doubled
       "fallen" (epesen epesen — echoing 14:8 and Isaiah 21:9).
       WEB: "Fallen, fallen is Babylon the great!" The announcement
       made earlier is now executed — the verdict becomes reality.

v.4  — NET: "Come out of her, my people, so you will not
       participate in her sins" — "come out" (exelthate = depart/
       leave). WEB: "Come out of her, my people." KJV: "Come out
       of her, my people." Echoing Jeremiah 51:45 and Isaiah 48:20
       — separation before judgment falls.

v.8  — NET: "For this reason, her plagues will come in a single
       day" — "single day" (en mia hEmera = in one day). WEB: "in
       one day her plagues will come." The swiftness of Babylon's
       fall — she thought she was invincible (v.7: "I sit as queen
       and will never see grief").

v.20 — NET: "Rejoice over her, O heaven, and you saints and
       apostles and prophets, for God has pronounced judgment
       against her on your behalf!" — "pronounced judgment on your
       behalf" (ekrinen ho theos to krima hymOn ex autEs). WEB:
       "God has judged your judgment on her." Heaven rejoices
       while earth mourns — perspectives depend on allegiance.

v.24 — NET: "The blood of saints and prophets was found in her,
       and of all those who had been killed on the earth" — "all
       those killed" (pantOn tOn esphagmenOn). WEB: "the blood of
       prophets and of saints, and of all who have been slain on
       the earth." Babylon is guilty of all martyrdom — the system
       behind every persecution.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Babylon fallen — The complete collapse of the world system — economic, political, religious. Sudden, total, irreversible.

Come out of her — God's call to His people to separate from the world system. Don't participate in her sins or share her judgment.

In one hour — The suddenness of Babylon's collapse. What seemed permanent and invincible falls instantly.

Slaves and souls of men — The world system's ultimate commodity: human beings. Trafficking in people — the worst of Babylon's sins.

Heaven rejoices — While earth mourns Babylon's fall, heaven celebrates. God's justice is cause for joy.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Come out of her, my people" (v.4). Where are you entangled with "Babylon" — the world's system of materialism, self-indulgence, and rebellion against God? What do you need to come out of?

2. "In one hour is thy judgment come" (v.10). Everything the world builds apart from God will collapse — suddenly. Are you investing in what lasts or what will fall?

3. "Slaves, and souls of men" (v.13). The world system commodifies people. Where do you see this today? How can you stand against it?

4. "Rejoice over her, thou heaven" (v.20). God's justice is cause for celebration. Can you rejoice when God judges evil — even when it disrupts the world's comfort?

5. "She saith in her heart, I sit a queen" (v.7). Self-deception precedes destruction. Where might you be deceived about your own security or self-sufficiency?
