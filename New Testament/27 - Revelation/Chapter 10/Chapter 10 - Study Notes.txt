Revelation - Chapter 10
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
A mighty angel with a little scroll announces that when the seventh trumpet sounds, the mystery of God will be finished — John eats the scroll which is sweet in his mouth but bitter in his stomach, and is told to prophesy again.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: The Apostle John (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Historical Context: Chapter 10 is an interlude between the sixth and seventh
trumpets (like chapter 7 was between the sixth and seventh seals). A mighty
angel descends with a little scroll, seven thunders speak (but are sealed),
and John is told to eat the scroll — sweet in the mouth but bitter in the
stomach. This chapter prepares for the final trumpet and the completion of
God's mystery.

The Mighty Angel (vv.1-4): A powerful angel descends — clothed with a cloud,
rainbow on his head, face like the sun, feet like fire (v.1). He straddles
sea and land (v.2) — claiming authority over all creation. He cries with a
lion's voice; seven thunders respond (v.3). John is told NOT to write what
the thunders said — some things remain sealed (v.4).

The Oath (vv.5-7): The angel swears by the eternal Creator: "there should be
time no longer" (v.6) — no more delay. When the seventh trumpet sounds, "the
mystery of God should be finished" (v.7).

The Little Scroll (vv.8-11): John is told to eat the scroll (v.8). It is
sweet as honey in his mouth but bitter in his stomach (vv.9-10). He must
"prophesy again" to many peoples and nations (v.11). God's word is sweet
(truth, promise) but bitter (judgment, suffering).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.6 — "There would be no more delay." (ESV) — No more delay — God's plan
 will be completed.

v.7 — "The mystery of God would be fulfilled, just as he announced to his
 servants the prophets." (ESV) — God's plan reaches completion.

v.9 — "It will make your stomach bitter, but in your mouth it will be sweet
 as honey." (ESV) — God's word: sweet truth, bitter reality.

v.11 — "You must again prophesy about many peoples and nations and languages
 and kings." (ESV) — John's continued prophetic mission.

 Sermon: "The Sweet and the Bitter" on vv.9-10 (MTP Vol. 25, No. 1502)
 "Sweet in the mouth — God's word is delightful to receive. Truth is sweet.
 Promise is sweet. The gospel is sweet. But bitter in the belly — when you

 truly digests God's word will know both sweetness and sorrow."

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "his feet as pillars of fire" / ESV: "his legs like pillars of
 fire" — Greek "podes" can mean feet or legs; ESV's "legs" better fits
 "pillars." The angel's description echoes Christ (1:15-16) but is
 likely a powerful angel, not Christ Himself (he swears by God in v.6).
       NET: "I saw another powerful angel descending from heaven,
       wrapped in a cloud, with a rainbow above his head" —
       "powerful angel" (angelon ischyron = a strong angel). WEB:
       "a mighty angel coming down out of the sky." Not Christ
       (angels are sent; Christ sends) but a glorious messenger.

v.4 — KJV: "seal up" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "sphragison" — seal, close up. Some
 revelation is withheld. Not everything is revealed — God has secrets.

v.6 — KJV: "that there should be time no longer" / ESV: "that there would be
 no more delay" — Greek "chronos ouketi estai" — ESV's "no more delay"
 is the better translation. Not that time ceases to exist but that
 God's delay in completing His plan is over. KJV's "time no longer" can
 be misread as the end of time itself.
       NET: "there will be no more delay!" — "no more delay"
       (chronos ouketi estai = time will be no more/no more time).
       WEB: "there will no longer be delay." KJV: "there should be
       time no longer." Debated: no more delay before judgment? or
       time itself ceasing? Context favors: no further postponement.

v.7 — KJV: "mystery of God" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "mustērion tou theou" — God's hidden
 plan now brought to completion. The full outworking of His redemptive
 purpose.
       NET: "the mystery of God is completed, just as he
       announced to his servants the prophets" — "mystery"
       (mystErion = hidden plan now revealed). WEB: "the mystery
       of God is finished." KJV: "the mystery of God should be
       finished." God's secret plan reaches its culmination.

v.9 — KJV: "eat it up" / ESV: "take and eat it" — Greek "kataphage" —
 devour it, consume it completely. Both equivalent in sense. Like
 Ezekiel (3:1-3), John must internalize God's message before
 proclaiming it.
       NET: "Take the scroll and eat it. It will be bitter in
       your stomach, but sweet as honey in your mouth" — "eat it"
       (kataphage = devour/consume completely). WEB: "Take it, and
       eat it up." Echoing Ezekiel 3:1-3 — internalizing God's
       word. Sweet truth contains bitter content (judgment).

v.11 — NET: "You must prophesy again about many peoples, nations,
       languages, and kings" — "must" (dei = divine necessity).
       WEB: "You must prophesy again." John's commission is not
       finished — more revelation follows. The universal scope
       continues.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Mighty angel — A powerful angelic being with authority over sea and land.
 Possibly Christ or a high-ranking angel.

Seven thunders — A sealed revelation. God spoke but John was forbidden to
 record it. Some things remain hidden.

No more delay — God's patience has a limit. His plan will reach completion
 at the seventh trumpet.

Mystery of God finished — God's hidden plan brought to full completion. What
 prophets foretold will be accomplished.

Sweet and bitter scroll — God's word: sweet to receive (truth, promise) but
 bitter to digest (judgment, suffering, responsibility).

Prophesy again — John's continued mission to proclaim God's word to all
 peoples. The prophet's calling continues.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "There should be time no longer" (v.6). God's patience has a limit. Are
 you living with urgency, knowing that delay will end and God's plan will
 be completed?

2. "Seal up those things" (v.4). God has secrets — things He chooses not to
 reveal. Can you accept that some questions won't be answered this side of
 eternity? Can you trust what He hasn't revealed?

3. "Sweet as honey... belly was bitter" (vv.9-10). God's word is both sweet
 and bitter. Do you experience both? The sweetness of His promises AND the
 bitterness of His judgments? A balanced diet of Scripture includes both.

4. "Thou must prophesy again" (v.11). After receiving God's word, you must
 share it. Are you keeping truth to yourself or proclaiming it to others?
