Revelation - Chapter 8
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The seventh seal brings silence in heaven for half an hour, then seven angels with trumpets begin to sound — hail and fire, a burning mountain, a falling star, and darkness striking a third of the earth, sea, rivers, and sky.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: The Apostle John (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Historical Context: Chapter 8 opens the seventh seal — which contains the
seven trumpet judgments (a pattern of judgments within judgments). The seventh
seal begins with dramatic silence in heaven (v.1), then the prayers of saints
are offered (vv.3-4), and the first four trumpets sound (vv.7-12). These
trumpet judgments intensify the seal judgments — affecting a third of the
earth rather than a quarter (cf. 6:8).

The Seventh Seal — Silence (v.1): Half an hour of silence in heaven. After
the ceaseless worship of chapters 4-5, silence is stunning. It represents
awe, anticipation, and the solemnity of what's coming.

Prayers and Fire (vv.2-6): Seven angels receive seven trumpets (v.2). Another
angel offers incense with the prayers of saints (vv.3-4). Then the censer is
filled with fire and cast to earth — voices, thunder, lightning, earthquake
(v.5). The prayers of saints trigger judgment.

The First Four Trumpets (vv.7-12):
- Trumpet 1 (v.7): Hail and fire mixed with blood. A third of trees and all
 green grass burned. (Echoes the Egyptian plague of hail, Exodus 9:23-25.)
- Trumpet 2 (vv.8-9): A great burning mountain cast into the sea. A third of
 the sea becomes blood, a third of sea creatures die, a third of ships
 destroyed.
- Trumpet 3 (vv.10-11): A star called Wormwood falls on rivers/springs. A
 third of waters become bitter; many die.
- Trumpet 4 (v.12): A third of sun, moon, and stars darkened. A third of
 daylight and nightlight removed.

Three Woes Announced (v.13): An angel (or eagle) flies through heaven
declaring three "woes" for the remaining three trumpets — the worst is yet
to come.

 Your prayers are not lost. They are not forgotten. They rise to God's

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. "Silence in heaven" (v.1). Even heaven pauses before God's judgment. Do
 you take God's judgment seriously? Or do you treat it casually?

2. "The prayers of the saints" (vv.3-4). Your prayers reach God's throne and
 trigger His action. Do you pray like your prayers matter? They do — they
 ascend before God Himself.

3. The trumpet judgments echo the Egyptian plagues (hail, blood, darkness).
 God judged Egypt to deliver Israel; He judges the world to deliver His
 people. How does this pattern encourage you?

4. "The rest of the men... repented not" (9:20-21, previewing what follows).
 Even severe judgment doesn't automatically produce repentance. What does
 this say about the hardness of the human heart?

5. These judgments are severe but partial (one-third). God leaves room for
 repentance. Are you responding to God's warnings, or ignoring them?
