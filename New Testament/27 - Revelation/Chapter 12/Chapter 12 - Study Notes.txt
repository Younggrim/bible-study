Revelation - Chapter 12
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
A woman clothed with the sun gives birth to a male child (Christ), a great red dragon (Satan) is cast out of heaven after war with Michael, and he pursues the woman and makes war with the rest of her offspring who keep God's commandments.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: The Apostle John (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Historical Context: Chapter 12 begins a new section of Revelation (chs.12-14)
that provides the spiritual backstory behind the earthly events. It reveals
the cosmic conflict between Satan and God's people throughout history. Using
vivid symbolic imagery — a woman, a dragon, and a male child — John shows
that the tribulation is the climax of an ancient war that began before human
history.

The Woman (vv.1-2): Clothed with the sun, moon under her feet, crown of
twelve stars (v.1). She is pregnant and in labor (v.2). Identity: Israel —
the nation from which the Messiah came. The imagery echoes Joseph's dream
(Genesis 37:9-10).

The Dragon (vv.3-4): A great red dragon with seven heads, ten horns, seven
crowns (v.3). His tail sweeps a third of heaven's stars (angels) to earth
(v.4). He waits to devour the child at birth (v.4). Identity: Satan (v.9).

The Male Child (v.5): Born to rule all nations with a rod of iron (v.5).
Caught up to God and His throne (v.5). Identity: Jesus Christ — born of
Israel, ascended to heaven.

The Woman's Flight (v.6): She flees to the wilderness — a place prepared by
God — for 1,260 days (v.6). Israel protected during the tribulation.

War in Heaven (vv.7-12): Michael and his angels fight the dragon (v.7). Satan
is cast out of heaven permanently (vv.8-9). He is identified: "that old
serpent, called the Devil, and Satan, which deceiveth the whole world" (v.9).
Victory declared: believers "overcame him by the blood of the Lamb, and by
the word of their testimony; and they loved not their lives unto the death"
(v.11). But woe to earth — Satan has great wrath because his time is short
(v.12).

The Dragon Pursues (vv.13-17): Cast to earth, Satan persecutes the woman
(Israel) (v.13). She is given eagle's wings to escape (v.14). Satan sends a
flood; the earth swallows it (vv.15-16). Enraged, he makes war with "the
remnant of her seed" — those who keep God's commandments and have Jesus's
testimony (v.17).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.5 — "She gave birth to a male child, one who is to rule all the nations
 with a rod of iron, but her child was caught up to God." (ESV) —
 Christ's birth and ascension.

v.9 — "The great dragon was thrown down, that ancient serpent, who is called
 the devil and Satan, the deceiver of the whole world." (ESV) — Satan's
 identity and defeat.

v.10 — "The accuser of our brothers has been thrown down, who accuses them
 before our God day and night." (ESV) — Satan as accuser — now defeated.

v.11 — "They have conquered him by the blood of the Lamb and by the word of
 their testimony, for they loved not their lives even unto death." (ESV)
 — The threefold means of victory over Satan.

v.12 — "The devil has come down to you in great wrath, because he knows that
 his time is short!" (ESV) — Satan's fury fueled by his limited time.

Charles Spurgeon:
 Sermon: "Overcoming by the Blood" on v.11 (MTP Vol. 11, No. 633)
 "They overcame him — three weapons: (1) The blood of the Lamb — the
 objective ground of victory. Satan's accusations are answered by Christ's
 blood. 'I am guilty — but the blood covers me.' (2) The word of their
 testimony — the subjective confession. They spoke what they believed. (3)
 They loved not their lives unto the death — total commitment. Satan cannot
 defeat someone who is not afraid to die. These three together are
 invincible against the accuser."

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "wonder" / ESV: "sign" — Greek "sēmeion" — sign. The ESV
 captures the Greek more precisely. Not just a wonder but a symbolic sign
 pointing to a greater reality.

v.2 — KJV: "travailing in birth, and pained to be delivered" / ESV: "crying
 out in birth pains and the agony of giving birth" — Both equivalent in
 meaning. ESV uses more contemporary language.

v.3 — KJV: "seven heads and ten horns, and seven crowns upon his heads" /
 ESV: "seven heads and ten horns, and on his heads seven diadems" — ESV
 uses "diadems" (Greek "diadēmata" — royal crowns) rather than generic
 "crowns." Seven heads = complete authority. Ten horns = complete power.
 Seven diadems = claimed sovereignty. Satan's counterfeit of divine
 authority.

v.4 — KJV: "the third part of the stars" / ESV: "a third of the stars" —
 Both equivalent. Angels who followed Satan in his rebellion. One-third
 of the angelic host became demons.

v.5 — KJV: "a man child, who was to rule all nations with a rod of iron" /
 ESV: "a male child, one who is to rule all the nations with a rod of
 iron" — Both equivalent. Greek "huion arsen" — a male son.

v.6 — KJV: "a thousand two hundred and threescore days" / ESV: "1,260 days"
 — Both identical in meaning. ESV uses numerals for clarity.

v.7 — KJV: "there was war in heaven" / ESV: "war arose in heaven" — Both
 equivalent. ESV's "arose" emphasizes the outbreak of conflict.

v.8 — KJV: "prevailed not; neither was their place found any more in heaven"
 / ESV: "he was defeated, and there was no longer any place for them in
 heaven" — ESV is more direct: "defeated" vs. KJV's "prevailed not."

v.9 — KJV: "that old serpent, called the Devil, and Satan, which deceiveth
 the whole world" / ESV: "that ancient serpent, who is called the devil
 and Satan, the deceiver of the whole world" — ESV uses "ancient" for
 Greek "archaios" (more precise than "old"). Four names: "the great
 dragon" (power/terror), "that ancient serpent" (Eden deceiver), "the
 Devil" (diabolos — slanderer), "Satan" (adversary). Each name reveals
 an aspect of his character.

v.10 — KJV: "the accuser of our brethren" / ESV: "the accuser of our
 brothers" — Both equivalent. Greek "katēgōr" — prosecutor, one who
 brings charges. Satan's primary activity: accusing believers before God.
 Now cast down — his access to accuse is removed.

v.11 — KJV: "they overcame him by the blood of the Lamb, and by the word of
 their testimony; and they loved not their lives unto the death" / ESV:
 "they have conquered him by the blood of the Lamb and by the word of
 their testimony, for they loved not their lives even unto death" — ESV
 uses "conquered" (Greek "nikaō") rather than "overcame." Three means of
 victory: (1) "blood of the Lamb" — Christ's atonement answers every
 accusation. (2) "word of their testimony" — bold confession of faith.
 (3) "loved not their lives unto death" — willingness to die rather than
 deny.

v.12 — KJV: "he knoweth that he hath but a short time" / ESV: "he knows
 that his time is short" — Both equivalent. ESV modernizes the phrasing.

v.14 — KJV: "time, and times, and half a time" / ESV: "a time, and times,
 and half a time" — Both equivalent. 1 + 2 + 0.5 = 3.5 years = 42
 months = 1,260 days. The second half of the tribulation.

v.17 — KJV: "the remnant of her seed, which keep the commandments of God,
 and have the testimony of Jesus Christ" / ESV: "the rest of her
 offspring, on those who keep the commandments of God and hold to the
 testimony of Jesus" — ESV uses "rest of her offspring" for Greek
 "loipōn tou spermatos" rather than KJV's "remnant of her seed." ESV's
 "hold to" emphasizes active adherence to testimony.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.1  — NET: "a woman clothed with the sun, and with the moon
       under her feet" — "clothed with the sun" (peribeblEmenE ton
       hElion). WEB: "a woman clothed with the sun." Israel/the
       church/Mary? — the imagery draws from Genesis 37:9 (Joseph's
       dream) suggesting Israel giving birth to the Messiah.

v.5  — NET: "She gave birth to a son, a male child, who is going
       to rule all the nations with an iron rod" — "iron rod"
       (rhabdO sidEra — quoting Psalm 2:9). WEB: "a son, a male
       child, who is to rule all the nations with a rod of iron."
       The child is Christ — born, ascended (caught up to God's
       throne).

v.7  — NET: "Then war broke out in heaven: Michael and his angels
       fought against the dragon" — "war" (polemos = battle/
       warfare). WEB: "There was war in heaven." KJV: "there was
       war in heaven." Michael leads heaven's armies; Satan and his
       angels are defeated and cast down permanently.

v.11 — NET: "they conquered him by the blood of the Lamb and by
       the word of their testimony, and they did not love their
       lives so much that they were afraid to die" — three weapons:
       blood (Christ's sacrifice), testimony (their witness), and
       willingness to die. WEB: "They overcame him." Martyrdom as
       victory, not defeat.

v.17 — NET: "the dragon became enraged at the woman and went away
       to make war on the rest of her children" — "the rest of her
       children" (tOn loipOn tou spermatos autEs = the remainder
       of her seed). WEB: "went away to make war with the rest of
       her offspring." Believers as targets — those who keep God's
       commandments and hold to Jesus' testimony.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
The woman — Israel, the nation from which Christ came. Protected by God
 during the tribulation.

The dragon — Satan. Identified by four names: dragon, serpent, Devil,
 Satan. The ancient enemy of God and His people.

The male child — Jesus Christ. Born of Israel, destined to rule all nations,
 ascended to God's throne.

War in heaven — The cosmic conflict between Michael's angels and Satan's
 forces. Satan permanently expelled from heaven.

Accuser cast down — Satan's role as prosecutor of believers is ended. His
 access to God's court is removed.

Blood of the Lamb — The primary weapon against Satan's accusations. Christ's
 sacrifice answers every charge.

Word of testimony — Bold confession of faith. Speaking what you believe
 despite opposition.

Loved not their lives — Willingness to die rather than deny Christ. The
 ultimate commitment that Satan cannot overcome.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "They overcame him by the blood of the Lamb" (v.11). When Satan accuses
 you — reminding you of your sin, your failure, your unworthiness — what
 is your answer? The blood. Christ's blood answers every accusation.

2. "The word of their testimony" (v.11). Are you speaking your faith boldly?
 Your testimony is a weapon against the enemy. Silence disarms you;
 confession empowers you.

3. "They loved not their lives unto the death" (v.11). Are you willing to
 lose everything for Christ? Satan cannot defeat someone who isn't afraid
 to die. What are you holding onto that makes you vulnerable?

4. "He knoweth that he hath but a short time" (v.12). Satan's fury is fueled
 by desperation — he knows his time is limited. His attacks are intense but
 temporary. Can you endure knowing his time is short?

5. "The remnant of her seed, which keep the commandments of God, and have the
 testimony of Jesus" (v.17). You are the target of Satan's war. Are you
 keeping God's commandments and holding Jesus's testimony? These identify
 you as the enemy's target — and God's protected.
