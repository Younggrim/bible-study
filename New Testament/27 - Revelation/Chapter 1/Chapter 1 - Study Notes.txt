Revelation - Chapter 1
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The revelation of Jesus Christ — John sees the glorified Son of Man among seven golden lampstands, hears His voice like many waters, and is commissioned to write what he sees to the seven churches of Asia.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: John (v.1, 4, 9) — the Apostle John, author of the Gospel of John
and 1-3 John. Written approximately AD 95-96 during the reign of Emperor
Domitian, who demanded worship as "Lord and God."

Recipient: "The seven churches which are in Asia" (v.4) — seven specific
churches in the Roman province of Asia (modern western Turkey): Ephesus,
Smyrna, Pergamos, Thyatira, Sardis, Philadelphia, and Laodicea.

Setting: John was exiled on the island of Patmos "for the word of God, and
for the testimony of Jesus Christ" (v.9). A small, rocky island in the
Aegean Sea used by Rome for political prisoners.

Purpose: "The Revelation of Jesus Christ" (v.1) — to reveal Christ in His
glory, to show "things which must shortly come to pass" (v.1), and to
encourage persecuted believers that Christ is sovereign and will triumph.

Structure of Revelation (v.19): "Write the things which thou hast seen"
(ch.1 — the vision of Christ), "the things which are" (chs.2-3 — the seven
churches), "the things which shall be hereafter" (chs.4-22 — future events).

Charles Spurgeon:
 Sermon: "Alpha and Omega" on v.8 (MTP Vol. 6, No. 345)
 "Alpha and Omega — the first and last letters of the Greek alphabet. Christ
 is the beginning of all things and the end of all things. He started
 creation and He will conclude history. He is the first word of the Bible
 and the last. He is the A and Z of salvation — He begins it and He finishes
 it. There is nothing before Him and nothing after Him. He encompasses all."
 Sermon: "Fear Not" on vv.17-18 (MTP Vol. 15, No. 876)
 "Fear not — the same words He spoke to fishermen on Galilee, He speaks to
 John on Patmos. I am the first and the last — eternal. I am he that liveth
 — alive. And was dead — He really died. And behold, I am alive for evermore
 — death could not hold Him. And have the keys of hell and of death — He
 controls both. If Christ holds the keys, death is not your master — He is."

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. "Blessed is he that readeth" (v.3). Revelation comes with a specific
 blessing for reading it. Are you engaging with this book or avoiding it?
 Don't let its difficulty keep you from its blessing.

2. "Unto him that loved us, and washed us from our sins" (v.5). Before any
 prophecy or judgment, John worships. Christ loved you and cleansed you.
 Start there.

3. "Every eye shall see him" (v.7). Christ's return will be visible and
 universal. Are you ready? Are you living in light of His coming?

4. "Fear not; I am the first and the last" (v.17). Whatever you fear, Christ
 is greater. He is eternal, alive, and holds the keys. What fear do you
 need to release to Him?

5. The glorified Christ (vv.13-16) is majestic, powerful, and awe-inspiring.
 Is your view of Jesus too small? Too familiar? Let this vision expand your
 understanding of who He really is.
