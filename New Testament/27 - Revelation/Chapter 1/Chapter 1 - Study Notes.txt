Revelation - Chapter 1
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The revelation of Jesus Christ — John sees the glorified Son of Man among seven golden lampstands, hears His voice like many waters, and is commissioned to write what he sees to the seven churches of Asia.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: John (v.1, 4, 9) — the Apostle John, author of the Gospel of John
and 1-3 John. Written approximately AD 95-96 during the reign of Emperor
Domitian, who demanded worship as "Lord and God."

Recipient: "The seven churches which are in Asia" (v.4) — seven specific
churches in the Roman province of Asia (modern western Turkey): Ephesus,
Smyrna, Pergamos, Thyatira, Sardis, Philadelphia, and Laodicea.

Setting: John was exiled on the island of Patmos "for the word of God, and
for the testimony of Jesus Christ" (v.9). A small, rocky island in the
Aegean Sea used by Rome for political prisoners.

Purpose: "The Revelation of Jesus Christ" (v.1) — to reveal Christ in His
glory, to show "things which must shortly come to pass" (v.1), and to
encourage persecuted believers that Christ is sovereign and will triumph.

Structure of Revelation (v.19): "Write the things which thou hast seen"
(ch.1 — the vision of Christ), "the things which are" (chs.2-3 — the seven
churches), "the things which shall be hereafter" (chs.4-22 — future events).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.3 — "Blessed is the one who reads aloud the words of this prophecy, and
 blessed are those who hear, and who keep what is written in it."
 (ESV) — The only book with a specific blessing for reading it.

v.5-6 — "To him who loves us and has freed us from our sins by his blood and
 made us a kingdom, priests to his God and Father." (ESV) — Doxology
 to Christ.

v.7 — "Behold, he is coming with the clouds, and every eye will see him."
 (ESV) — Christ's visible, universal return.

v.8 — "I am the Alpha and the Omega," says the Lord God, "who is and who
 was and who is to come, the Almighty." (ESV) — Christ's eternal
 deity.

v.17-18 — "Fear not, I am the first and the last, and the living one. I died,
 and behold I am alive forevermore, and I have the keys of Death and
 Hades." (ESV) — The risen Christ's authority.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "Revelation" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "Apokalupsis" — unveiling, uncovering,
 disclosure. Not concealment but revelation. The book reveals; it
 doesn't hide.

v.1 — KJV: "signified" / ESV: "made it known" — Greek "esēmanen" —
 communicated through signs/symbols. Much of Revelation is symbolic —
 "sign-ified." KJV preserves the "sign" connection; ESV clarifies the
 meaning but loses the etymological link.

v.4 — KJV: "him which is, and which was, and which is to come" / ESV: "him
 who is and who was and who is to come" — Both equivalent. A paraphrase
 of the divine name YHWH (I AM). Past, present, future — the eternal
 God.

v.5 — KJV: "faithful witness" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "martus pistos" — reliable testifier.
 "Martus" = root of English "martyr." Christ testified faithfully unto
 death.

v.8 — KJV: "Alpha and Omega" / ESV: "Alpha and the Omega" — First and last letters of the Greek alphabet.
 Christ encompasses everything from beginning to end.

v.10 — KJV: "in the Spirit" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "en pneumati" — in a state of spiritual
 ecstasy/vision. Not a normal state but a prophetic trance.

v.13 — KJV: "Son of man" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Daniel 7:13 imagery. Christ in His glorified,
 divine-human state. Seven features described (vv.13-16): garment,
 golden sash, white hair, fiery eyes, bronze feet, thunderous voice,
 sun-like face.

v.18 — KJV: "keys of hell and of death" / ESV: "keys of Death and Hades" —
 Greek "thanatos" (death) and "hadēs" (realm of the dead). Keys =
 authority, control. KJV uses "hell" for Hades; ESV transliterates the
 Greek. Christ has authority over death (physical) and Hades (the realm
 of the dead). He decides who enters and who exits.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.1  — NET: "The revelation of Jesus Christ, which God gave
       him to show his servants what must happen very soon" —
       "revelation" (apokalypsis = unveiling/disclosure). WEB:
       "The Revelation of Jesus Christ." KJV: "The Revelation of
       Jesus Christ." Not revelations (plural) but one singular
       unveiling of Christ in glory.

v.5  — NET: "To the one who loves us and has set us free from our
       sins at the cost of his own blood" — "set us free" (lysanti
       = loosed/released). Textual variant: "washed" (lousanti,
       KJV) vs. "freed" (lysanti, NET/ESV/WEB). WEB: "freed us
       from our sins." One letter difference in Greek — both
       theologically true.

v.7  — NET: "Look! He is returning with the clouds, and every eye
       will see him" — "returning with the clouds" (erchetai meta
       tOn nephelOn). WEB: "he is coming with the clouds." KJV:
       "he cometh with clouds." Echoing Daniel 7:13 — visible,
       public, undeniable return.

v.8  — NET: "I am the Alpha and the Omega... the one who is, and
       who was, and who is coming, the All-Powerful" — "All-Powerful"
       (pantokratOr = Almighty/ruler of all). WEB: "the Almighty."
       KJV: "the Almighty." The divine self-identification spans
       all time and holds all power.

v.17 — NET: "Do not be afraid! I am the first and the last" —
       "Do not be afraid" (mE phobou). WEB: "Don't be afraid."
       KJV: "Fear not." John falls as dead before the glorified
       Christ — the same Jesus who said "Fear not" during His
       earthly ministry says it again from heaven.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Revelation/Apocalypse — An unveiling, an uncovering. The book reveals
 Christ in His glory and God's plan for history.

Alpha and Omega — The first and last. Christ encompasses all of reality
 from beginning to end.

Seven churches — Seven literal churches in Asia Minor that also represent
 types of churches throughout history.

Seven candlesticks — The seven churches. Christ walks among them — present,
 watchful, involved.

Seven stars — The angels (messengers/pastors) of the seven churches. Held
 in Christ's right hand — under His authority and protection.

Keys of hell and death — Christ's authority over death and the afterlife.
 He controls the entrance and exit.

Son of man — Christ's self-designation from Daniel 7:13. The glorified,
 divine-human figure with all authority.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Blessed is he that readeth" (v.3). Revelation comes with a specific
 blessing for reading it. Are you engaging with this book or avoiding it?
 Don't let its difficulty keep you from its blessing.

2. "Unto him that loved us, and washed us from our sins" (v.5). Before any
 prophecy or judgment, John worships. Christ loved you and cleansed you.
 Start there.

3. "Every eye shall see him" (v.7). Christ's return will be visible and
 universal. Are you ready? Are you living in light of His coming?

4. "Fear not; I am the first and the last" (v.17). Whatever you fear, Christ
 is greater. He is eternal, alive, and holds the keys. What fear do you
 need to release to Him?

5. The glorified Christ (vv.13-16) is majestic, powerful, and awe-inspiring.
 Is your view of Jesus too small? Too familiar? Let this vision expand your
 understanding of who He really is.
