Revelation - Chapter 11
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Two witnesses prophesy for 1,260 days, are killed by the beast, lie dead in the street for three days, then are resurrected and ascend to heaven — the seventh trumpet sounds: "The kingdoms of this world are become the kingdoms of our Lord, and of his Christ."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: The Apostle John (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Historical Context: Chapter 11 continues the interlude with the measuring of
the temple (vv.1-2), the ministry of the two witnesses (vv.3-14), and then
the seventh trumpet sounds (vv.15-19). The two witnesses are among the most
debated figures in Revelation — powerful prophets who minister for 1,260 days,
are killed, resurrected, and taken to heaven. The seventh trumpet announces
Christ's kingdom.

The Temple Measured (vv.1-2): John measures the temple, altar, and worshippers
(v.1). The outer court is left unmeasured — given to Gentiles who trample
Jerusalem for 42 months (v.2). Measuring = claiming/protecting; not measuring
= giving over to judgment.

The Two Witnesses (vv.3-14): They prophesy 1,260 days in sackcloth (v.3).
Described as "two olive trees and two candlesticks" (v.4, cf. Zechariah 4).
They have power like Elijah (shutting rain) and Moses (turning water to blood)
(vv.5-6). After finishing their testimony, the beast kills them (v.7). Their
bodies lie in Jerusalem's streets for 3.5 days while the world celebrates
(vv.8-10). Then God resurrects them and calls them to heaven (vv.11-12). An
earthquake kills 7,000 (v.13).

The Seventh Trumpet (vv.15-19): "The kingdoms of this world are become the
kingdoms of our Lord, and of his Christ; and he shall reign for ever and ever"
(v.15). The elders worship (vv.16-17). God's wrath has come; time to judge
the dead and reward the faithful (v.18). The heavenly temple opens, revealing
the ark of the covenant (v.19).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.3 — "I will grant authority to my two witnesses, and they will prophesy
 for 1,260 days." (ESV) — God's witnesses empowered.

v.11 — "A breath of life from God entered them, and they stood up on their
 feet." (ESV) — Resurrection of the witnesses.

v.15 — "The kingdom of the world has become the kingdom of our Lord and of
 his Christ, and he shall reign forever and ever." (ESV) — The ultimate
 declaration of Christ's kingdom.

v.17 — "You have taken your great power and begun to reign." (ESV) — God
 assumes His rightful rule.

v.18 — "Your wrath came, and the time for the dead to be judged, and for
 rewarding your servants." (ESV) — Judgment and reward.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.2 — KJV: "forty and two months" / ESV: "forty-two months" — 3.5 years. The same period as 1,260
 days (v.3) and "time, times, and half a time" (12:14). The duration
 of the tribulation's second half.

v.3 — KJV: "two witnesses" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Identity debated: (1) Moses and Elijah
 (miracles match), (2) Enoch and Elijah (both taken without dying),
 (3) symbolic of the church's witness, (4) two literal end-times
 prophets. Their powers echo Moses (plagues) and Elijah (drought).

v.4 — KJV: "two olive trees, and the two candlesticks" / ESV: "two olive
 trees and the two lampstands" — KJV "candlesticks" = ESV "lampstands"
 (Greek "luchniai" — same word as in 1:20). From Zechariah 4. Olive
 trees supply oil (the Spirit); lampstands give light (witness).
 Spirit-empowered testimony.

v.8 — KJV: "which spiritually is called Sodom and Egypt" / ESV:
 "symbolically is called Sodom and Egypt" — Greek "pneumatikōs" —
 spiritually/symbolically. ESV's "symbolically" clarifies for modern
 readers. Jerusalem is compared to Sodom (immorality) and Egypt
 (oppression) — "where also our Lord was crucified" confirms the
 location.

v.7 — KJV: "the beast that ascendeth out of the bottomless pit" / ESV:
 "the beast that rises from the bottomless pit" — Both equivalent.
 First mention of "the beast" (to be developed in ch.13). He kills the
 witnesses only after they FINISH their testimony — God's timing.

v.15 — KJV: "The kingdoms of this world are become the kingdoms of our Lord"
 / ESV: "The kingdom of the world has become the kingdom of our Lord" —
 ESV follows manuscripts with singular "kingdom" (basileia); KJV uses
 plural "kingdoms." ESV's singular emphasizes the world as one unified
 system now transferred to Christ. Either way: all earthly authority
 transfers to Christ permanently.

v.19 — KJV: "the ark of his testament" / ESV: "the ark of his covenant" —
 Greek "kibōtos tēs diathēkēs" — KJV "testament" = ESV "covenant"
 (same Greek word "diathēkē"). ESV's "covenant" is more precise in
 modern English. The ark of the covenant seen in heaven. The earthly
 ark was a copy; the heavenly original is now revealed. God's
 faithfulness to His covenant.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.3  — NET: "I will grant my two witnesses authority to
       prophesy for 1,260 days" — "1,260 days" = 42 months = 3.5
       years (the symbolic half-week of Daniel 9:27). WEB: "my two
       witnesses will prophesy one thousand two hundred sixty days."
       Their identity is debated: Moses/Elijah? the church? two
       individuals?

v.7  — NET: "the beast that comes up from the abyss will make war
       on them and conquer them and kill them" — "the beast" (to
       thErion) — first appearance of this major figure. WEB: "the
       beast that comes up out of the abyss." The witnesses are
       invincible during their ministry (v.5) but killed at its end.

v.11 — NET: "after three and a half days a breath of life from
       God entered them and they stood on their feet" — "breath of
       life" (pneuma zOEs = spirit of life). WEB: "the breath of
       life from God entered into them." Echoing Ezekiel 37 (dry
       bones) — resurrection before their enemies' eyes.

v.15 — NET: "The kingdom of the world has become the kingdom of
       our Lord and of his Christ, and he will reign forever and
       ever" — "kingdom of the world" (hE basileia tou kosmou —
       singular: one kingdom transferred). WEB: "The kingdom of
       the world has become the Kingdom of our Lord." KJV: "The
       kingdoms of this world." The seventh trumpet's climactic
       announcement.

v.18 — NET: "the time has come to destroy those who destroy the
       earth" — "destroy those who destroy" (diaphtheirai tous
       diaphtheirontas). WEB: "to destroy those who destroy the
       earth." Judgment matches crime — those who corrupt creation
       are themselves destroyed.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Two witnesses — Powerful end-times prophets who testify for 1,260 days.
 Their powers echo Moses and Elijah.

42 months / 1,260 days — 3.5 years. The duration of Gentile trampling and
 the witnesses' ministry. Half of the tribulation period.

The beast — The Antichrist figure who ascends from the abyss. First
 mentioned here; developed in chapter 13.

Kingdoms become His — The transfer of all earthly authority to Christ. The
 ultimate goal of history.

Ark of the covenant — God's covenant faithfulness revealed in heaven. The
 original of which the earthly ark was a copy.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "The kingdoms of this world are become the kingdoms of our Lord" (v.15).
 This is history's destination. Every earthly power will bow to Christ. Does
 this certainty give you confidence when the world seems out of control?

2. The two witnesses are killed only AFTER finishing their testimony (v.7).
 God's servants are invincible until their work is done. Does this give you
 courage in your calling?

3. The world celebrates the witnesses' death (v.10) — but God has the last
 word (v.11). When the world celebrates evil, remember: God always has the
 final say. Resurrection follows death.

4. "Thou hast taken to thee thy great power, and hast reigned" (v.17). God
 will assert His power fully. Are you living in anticipation of His reign?
 Are you on the right side of history?
