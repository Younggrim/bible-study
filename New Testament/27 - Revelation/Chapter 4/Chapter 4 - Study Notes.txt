Revelation - Chapter 4
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
John is caught up to heaven's throne room — God sits on a throne surrounded by twenty-four elders, four living creatures, lightning and thunder, and ceaseless worship: "Holy, holy, holy, Lord God Almighty, which was, and is, and is to come."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: The Apostle John (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Historical Context: Chapter 4 begins the third section of Revelation — "the
things which shall be hereafter" (1:19). John is caught up to heaven through
an open door (v.1) and sees the throne room of God. This is the control center
of the universe — where all authority resides and from which all judgments
proceed. Chapters 4-5 form a unit: chapter 4 focuses on God the Creator on
His throne; chapter 5 focuses on Christ the Redeemer and the sealed scroll.

The Throne (vv.1-3): A door opened in heaven (v.1). A throne with One seated
on it — described in gemstone imagery: jasper (clear brilliance), sardine/
carnelian (fiery red), emerald rainbow (green — mercy) (vv.2-3).

The Court (vv.4-6a): Twenty-four elders on thrones, in white, with gold
crowns (v.4). Lightning, thunder, voices from the throne (v.5). Seven lamps
= the seven Spirits of God (v.5). A sea of glass like crystal (v.6a).

The Living Creatures (vv.6b-8): Four living creatures full of eyes — lion,
calf/ox, man, eagle (vv.6b-7). Six wings each (v.8). Ceaseless worship:
"Holy, holy, holy, Lord God Almighty, which was, and is, and is to come" (v.8).

Worship (vv.9-11): When the creatures worship, the elders fall down, cast
their crowns before the throne, and declare God worthy because He created all
things (vv.9-11).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1 — "Come up here, and I will show you what must take place after this."
 (ESV) — The invitation to see heaven's perspective.

v.2 — "Behold, a throne stood in heaven, with one seated on the throne."
 (ESV) — God enthroned — the central reality of the universe.

v.8 — "Holy, holy, holy, is the Lord God Almighty, who was and is and is to
 come!" (ESV) — The ceaseless worship of heaven.

v.11 — "Worthy are you, our Lord and God, to receive glory and honor and
 power, for you created all things, and by your will they existed and
 were created." (ESV) — God worthy as Creator.

 "Holy, holy, holy — three times, never two, never four — three. The
 triune God is thrice holy. And this is the ceaseless song of heaven — not
 'love, love, love' (though He is love) but 'holy, holy, holy.' Holiness
 is the attribute that governs all others. God's love is holy love. His
 justice is holy justice. His mercy is holy mercy. Heaven's first and
 constant declaration: He is holy."

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "after this" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "meta tauta" — after these things. Marks
 the transition from "things which are" (churches) to "things hereafter"
 (future events).
       NET: "After these things I looked, and there was a door
       standing open in heaven!" — "standing open" (aneOgmenE =
       opened and remaining open — perfect participle). WEB: "a
       door opened in heaven." The door opened for John gives
       readers access to heaven's perspective.

v.3  — NET: "the one seated on it was like jasper and carnelian
       in appearance" — "jasper" (iaspidi = likely diamond/clear
       crystal) and "carnelian" (sardiO = blood-red stone). WEB:
       "like a jasper stone and a sardius." God described in
       reflected light, not direct form — no anthropomorphism.

v.4 — KJV: "four and twenty elders" / ESV: "twenty-four elders" — Identity debated: (1) representatives
 of the redeemed (12 tribes + 12 apostles), (2) an angelic order, (3)
 OT and NT saints combined. Their white robes and crowns suggest
 redeemed humans.

v.6 — KJV: "beasts" / ESV: "living creatures" — Greek "zōa" — living
 creatures (not "thēria" = wild beasts). ESV correctly translates the
 Greek; KJV's "beasts" is misleading in modern English. These are
 exalted beings, not monsters. Similar to Ezekiel's cherubim and
 Isaiah's seraphim.
       NET: "In the middle of the throne and around the throne
       were four living creatures full of eyes in front and in
       back" — "living creatures" (zOa = living ones — not thEria/
       beasts). WEB: "four living creatures." KJV: "four beasts."
       NET/WEB correctly avoid "beasts" — these are exalted beings,
       not animals.

v.8 — KJV: "Holy, holy, holy" / ESV: Both equivalent. — The trisagion (thrice-holy). The only
 attribute of God repeated three times in Scripture. Emphasizes His
 absolute, infinite, transcendent holiness.
       NET: "Holy, Holy, Holy is the Lord God, the All-Powerful,
       Who was and who is, and who is still to come!" — the
       trisagion (thrice-holy). WEB: "Holy, holy, holy is the Lord
       God, the Almighty." KJV: "Holy, holy, holy, Lord God
       Almighty." Echoing Isaiah 6:3 — ceaseless worship (they
       never stop, day or night).

v.11 — KJV: "for thy pleasure they are and were created" / ESV: "by your
 will they existed and were created" — Greek "dia to thelēma sou" —
 because of Your will. KJV's "pleasure" emphasizes God's delight; ESV's
 "will" emphasizes God's sovereign choice. Both are valid renderings of
 "thelēma." All things exist because God willed them into existence.
 Creation is an act of divine will and pleasure.
       NET: "You are worthy, our Lord and God, to receive glory
       and honor and power, since you created all things" — "you
       created" (ektisas = you brought into being). WEB: "You
       created all things." Creation as the ground of worship —
       God is worshipped because He is Creator.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Throne — The central symbol of Revelation. God's sovereign authority
 over all creation. Everything proceeds from the throne.

Twenty-four elders — Representatives of the redeemed community. Crowned,
 robed in white, worshipping before God's throne.

Four living creatures — Exalted beings surrounding God's throne. Lion
 (majesty), ox (service), man (intelligence), eagle
 (sovereignty). Full of eyes = all-seeing awareness.

Holy, holy, holy — The trisagion. God's supreme attribute declared
 ceaselessly. Infinite, transcendent, absolute holiness.

Sea of glass — Calm, crystal-clear expanse before the throne. Suggests
 purity, separation, and the peace of God's presence.

Cast their crowns — The elders' response to God's worthiness: returning
 their honors to the One who gave them.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "A throne was set in heaven" (v.2). God is on His throne — sovereign,
 ruling, in control. Whatever chaos exists on earth, heaven's throne is
 stable. Does this give you peace?

2. "Holy, holy, holy" (v.8). Heaven's primary declaration is God's holiness.
 Do you take His holiness seriously? Does it produce reverence, awe, and
 worship in you?

3. The elders "cast their crowns before the throne" (v.10). Whatever honors
 or achievements you have — they belong to God. Are you willing to lay
 them at His feet? Or do you cling to your crowns?

4. "For thy pleasure they are and were created" (v.11). You exist because God
 willed it — for His pleasure. Does knowing you were created for God's
 pleasure change how you view your life's purpose?

5. This chapter is entirely about worship. How does your worship compare to
 heaven's? Is it ceaseless, passionate, God-centered? Or occasional,
 routine, self-centered?
