Revelation - Chapter 4
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
John is caught up to heaven's throne room — God sits on a throne surrounded by twenty-four elders, four living creatures, lightning and thunder, and ceaseless worship: "Holy, holy, holy, Lord God Almighty, which was, and is, and is to come."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: The Apostle John (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Historical Context: Chapter 4 begins the third section of Revelation — "the
things which shall be hereafter" (1:19). John is caught up to heaven through
an open door (v.1) and sees the throne room of God. This is the control center
of the universe — where all authority resides and from which all judgments
proceed. Chapters 4-5 form a unit: chapter 4 focuses on God the Creator on
His throne; chapter 5 focuses on Christ the Redeemer and the sealed scroll.

The Throne (vv.1-3): A door opened in heaven (v.1). A throne with One seated
on it — described in gemstone imagery: jasper (clear brilliance), sardine/
carnelian (fiery red), emerald rainbow (green — mercy) (vv.2-3).

The Court (vv.4-6a): Twenty-four elders on thrones, in white, with gold
crowns (v.4). Lightning, thunder, voices from the throne (v.5). Seven lamps
= the seven Spirits of God (v.5). A sea of glass like crystal (v.6a).

The Living Creatures (vv.6b-8): Four living creatures full of eyes — lion,
calf/ox, man, eagle (vv.6b-7). Six wings each (v.8). Ceaseless worship:
"Holy, holy, holy, Lord God Almighty, which was, and is, and is to come" (v.8).

Worship (vv.9-11): When the creatures worship, the elders fall down, cast
their crowns before the throne, and declare God worthy because He created all
things (vv.9-11).

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------
Enduring Word (David Guzik):
 https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/revelation-4/
 Guzik covers the heavenly door, the throne, the elders, the living
 creatures, and the worship of God as Creator.

Charles Spurgeon:
 Sermon: "Holy, Holy, Holy" on v.8 (MTP Vol. 8, No. 446)
 "Holy, holy, holy — three times, never two, never four — three. The
 triune God is thrice holy. And this is the ceaseless song of heaven — not
 'love, love, love' (though He is love) but 'holy, holy, holy.' Holiness
 is the attribute that governs all others. God's love is holy love. His
 justice is holy justice. His mercy is holy mercy. Heaven's first and
 constant declaration: He is holy."

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. "A throne was set in heaven" (v.2). God is on His throne — sovereign,
 ruling, in control. Whatever chaos exists on earth, heaven's throne is
 stable. Does this give you peace?

2. "Holy, holy, holy" (v.8). Heaven's primary declaration is God's holiness.
 Do you take His holiness seriously? Does it produce reverence, awe, and
 worship in you?

3. The elders "cast their crowns before the throne" (v.10). Whatever honors
 or achievements you have — they belong to God. Are you willing to lay
 them at His feet? Or do you cling to your crowns?

4. "For thy pleasure they are and were created" (v.11). You exist because God
 willed it — for His pleasure. Does knowing you were created for God's
 pleasure change how you view your life's purpose?

5. This chapter is entirely about worship. How does your worship compare to
 heaven's? Is it ceaseless, passionate, God-centered? Or occasional,
 routine, self-centered?
