Revelation - Chapter 17
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The great prostitute Babylon rides a scarlet beast, drunk with the blood of the saints — she represents the world system of false religion and political power opposed to God, and the ten horns will ultimately turn against her and burn her with fire.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: The Apostle John (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Historical Context: Chapters 17-18 expand on Babylon's judgment (introduced in 14:8 and 16:19). Chapter 17 reveals "Mystery Babylon" — the great prostitute who represents the world's false religious/political system. She rides the beast (the Antichrist) but is ultimately destroyed by him. The chapter uses symbolic imagery to depict the relationship between false religion, political power, and their mutual destruction.

The Great Prostitute (vv.1-6): She sits on "many waters" (peoples/nations, v.15). Kings committed fornication with her (v.2). She rides a scarlet beast with seven heads and ten horns (v.3). Dressed in purple/scarlet, adorned with gold/jewels, holding a golden cup full of abominations (v.4). Her name: "MYSTERY, BABYLON THE GREAT, THE MOTHER OF HARLOTS" (v.5). Drunk with the blood of saints (v.6).

The Mystery Explained (vv.7-18): The beast "was, and is not, and shall ascend" (v.8). Seven heads = seven mountains/kings (vv.9-10). The beast is an eighth king (v.11). Ten horns = ten kings who give power to the beast (vv.12-13). They war against the Lamb — and the Lamb overcomes (v.14). The ten kings turn on the prostitute and destroy her (vv.16-17). The woman = "that great city, which reigneth over the kings of the earth" (v.18).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.5 — "Babylon the great, mother of prostitutes and of earth's abominations." (ESV) — The world system's identity.

v.8 — "The beast that you saw was, and is not, and is about to rise from the bottomless pit and go to destruction." (ESV) — The beast's counterfeit resurrection.

v.14 — "They will make war on the Lamb, and the Lamb will conquer them, for he is Lord of lords and King of kings." (ESV) — The Lamb's certain victory.

v.17 — "God has put it into their hearts to carry out his purpose." (ESV) — Even evil serves God's purposes.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "the great whore that sitteth upon many waters" / ESV: "the
 great prostitute who is seated on many waters" — Greek "pornēs tēs
 megalēs" — the great prostitute. ESV uses "prostitute" (more precise)
 rather than KJV's "whore." Spiritual unfaithfulness depicted as sexual
 immorality.
       NET: "the judgment of the great prostitute who is
       seated on many waters" — "great prostitute" (tEs pornEs tEs
       megalEs). WEB: "the great prostitute." KJV: "the great
       whore." False religion/political power seducing the nations
       — "many waters" = peoples and nations (v.15).

v.2 — KJV: "committed fornication" / ESV: "committed sexual immorality" —
 ESV uses the broader "sexual immorality" for Greek "porneuō." Both
 convey spiritual/political unfaithfulness to God.

v.3 — KJV: "a woman sit upon a scarlet coloured beast, full of names of
 blasphemy" / ESV: "a woman sitting on a scarlet beast that was full of
 blasphemous names" — Both equivalent. ESV modernizes the phrasing.

v.4 — KJV: "decked with gold and precious stones and pearls" / ESV:
 "adorned with gold and jewels and pearls" — Both equivalent. The
 prostitute's outward splendor conceals inner corruption.

v.5 — KJV: "MYSTERY, BABYLON THE GREAT, THE MOTHER OF HARLOTS AND
 ABOMINATIONS OF THE EARTH" / ESV: "Babylon the great, mother of
 prostitutes and of earth's abominations" — ESV treats "mystery" as a
 descriptor ("a name of mystery") rather than part of the title itself.
 ESV uses "prostitutes" for KJV's "harlots." She is a "mystery" — a
 hidden reality now revealed.
       NET: "Babylon the Great, the Mother of prostitutes and of
       the detestable things of the earth" — "Mother of prostitutes"
       (mEtEr tOn pornOn). WEB: "BABYLON THE GREAT, THE MOTHER OF
       THE PROSTITUTES." The source/originator of all false
       religion and corruption.

v.6 — KJV: "drunken with the blood of the saints, and with the blood of the
 martyrs of Jesus" / ESV: "drunk with the blood of the saints, the blood
 of the martyrs of Jesus" — Both equivalent. KJV's "drunken" vs. ESV's
 "drunk" is merely archaic vs. modern English.

v.8 — KJV: "shall ascend out of the bottomless pit, and go into perdition" /
 ESV: "is about to rise from the bottomless pit and go to destruction" —
 ESV uses "destruction" for Greek "apōleian" rather than KJV's
 "perdition." Both mean utter ruin/loss.
       NET: "The beast that you saw was, and is not, but is about
       to come up from the abyss" — "was, and is not, and is about
       to come" — a satanic parody of God's title ("who is, was,
       and is to come," 1:8). WEB: "The beast that you saw was,
       and is not; and is about to come up." A counterfeit
       resurrection.

v.9 — KJV: "seven mountains, on which the woman sitteth" / ESV: "seven
 mountains on which the woman is seated" — Both equivalent. Often
 identified with Rome (the city on seven hills). But could also be
 symbolic of complete political power.

v.14 — KJV: "the Lamb shall overcome them: for he is Lord of lords, and King
 of kings: and they that are with him are called, and chosen, and
 faithful" / ESV: "the Lamb will conquer them, for he is Lord of lords
 and King of kings, and those with him are called and chosen and
 faithful" — ESV uses "conquer" (Greek "nikēsei") rather than
 "overcome." Three descriptions of those with the Lamb: called
 (invited), chosen (selected), faithful (persevering).
       NET: "They will make war with the Lamb, but the Lamb will
       conquer them, because he is Lord of lords and King of kings"
       — "Lord of lords" (kyrios kyriOn). WEB: "the Lamb will
       overcome them, for he is Lord of lords, and King of kings."
       The outcome is certain before the battle begins.

v.16 — KJV: "shall make her desolate and naked, and shall eat her flesh, and
 burn her with fire" / ESV: "will make her desolate and naked, and devour
 her flesh and burn her up with fire" — Both equivalent. ESV's "devour"
 is slightly more vivid than KJV's "eat."

v.17 — KJV: "God hath put in their hearts to fulfil his will" / ESV: "God
 has put it into their hearts to carry out his purpose" — Both
 equivalent. ESV's "carry out his purpose" clarifies KJV's "fulfil his
 will."
       NET: "God has put into their minds to carry out his
       purpose" — "put into their minds" (edOken... poiEsai tEn
       gnOmEn autou = gave to do His purpose). WEB: "God has put
       in their hearts to do what he has in mind." Even the beast's
       allies serve God's sovereign plan unknowingly.

v.18 — KJV: "that great city, which reigneth over the kings of the earth" /
 ESV: "the great city that has dominion over the kings of the earth" —
 Both equivalent. ESV uses "dominion" for Greek "basileian" rather than
 "reigneth."

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Mystery Babylon — The world's false religious/political system. Spiritual prostitution — unfaithfulness to God through alliance with evil.

The great prostitute — False religion that seduces nations and persecutes saints. Rides the beast but is ultimately destroyed by it.

Lord of lords, King of kings — Christ's supreme title. Above every authority, every ruler, every power.

God put in their hearts — Even evil actors fulfill God's sovereign plan. He uses their rebellion to accomplish His purposes.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "The Lamb shall overcome them" (v.14). No matter how powerful evil appears, the Lamb wins. Does this give you confidence when the world seems to be winning?

2. "God hath put in their hearts to fulfil his will" (v.17). Even evil serves God's purposes. Can you trust His sovereignty when you can't see His plan?

3. "Come out of her, my people" (18:4, previewing the next chapter). Are you entangled with "Babylon" — the world system? Where do you need to separate?

4. Those with the Lamb are "called, and chosen, and faithful" (v.14). Are you faithful? Calling and choosing are God's work; faithfulness is your response.
