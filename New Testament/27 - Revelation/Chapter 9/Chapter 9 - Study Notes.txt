Revelation - Chapter 9
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The fifth trumpet opens the bottomless pit releasing tormenting locusts, and the sixth trumpet unleashes four angels who kill a third of mankind — yet those who survive still refuse to repent of their idolatry and murders.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: The Apostle John (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Historical Context: Chapter 9 contains the fifth and sixth trumpet judgments
— the first two "woes" announced in 8:13. These are the most terrifying
judgments yet: demonic locusts from the abyss (5th trumpet) and a demonic
army of 200 million that kills a third of humanity (6th trumpet). Despite
this unprecedented devastation, the chapter ends with humanity's stubborn
refusal to repent (vv.20-21).

Fifth Trumpet / First Woe (vv.1-12): A star (fallen angel) opens the
bottomless pit (v.1). Smoke darkens the sky (v.2). Demonic locusts emerge —
not to kill but to torment for five months (vv.3-5). Men seek death but
cannot find it (v.6). The locusts are described in terrifying detail: horse-
like, crowned, human-faced, lion-toothed, armored, with scorpion tails
(vv.7-10). Their king: Abaddon/Apollyon — "Destroyer" (v.11).

Sixth Trumpet / Second Woe (vv.13-21): A voice from the golden altar commands
the release of four angels bound at the Euphrates (vv.13-14). They were
prepared for this exact moment — to kill a third of mankind (v.15). An army
of 200 million with lion-headed horses breathing fire, smoke, and brimstone
(vv.16-19). A third of humanity killed (v.18).

Humanity's Response (vv.20-21): Despite all this, "the rest of the men...
repented not" (v.20). They continued worshipping demons and idols, and
refused to repent of murders, sorceries, fornication, and thefts (vv.20-21).

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------
Enduring Word (David Guzik):
 https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/revelation-9/
 Guzik covers the abyss, the demonic locusts, Abaddon/Apollyon, the
 200-million army, and humanity's refusal to repent.

Charles Spurgeon:
 Sermon: "Impenitence" on vv.20-21 (MTP Vol. 20, No. 1192)
 "They repented NOT. After all this — after a third of humanity killed,
 after demonic torment, after cosmic devastation — they still refused to
 repent. This is the hardness of the human heart. Judgment alone does not
 produce repentance. Only grace produces repentance. You can terrify a man
 into compliance but not into love. Only the goodness of God leads to
 repentance (Romans 2:4)."

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. "Men seek death, and shall not find it" (v.6). There are fates worse than
 death. This should produce urgency about the gospel — people need to be
 rescued before judgment falls.

2. "Repented not" (v.20). Judgment alone doesn't produce repentance — only
 grace does. Are you relying on God's grace for transformation, or trying
 to scare yourself into obedience?

3. God's timing is precise (v.15) — "an hour, and a day, and a month, and a
 year." Nothing in God's plan is random or accidental. Can you trust His
 timing in your life?

4. The sealed (7:3) are protected from the locusts (v.4). God distinguishes
 between His people and the world. Are you sealed — marked as His?

5. "Neither repented they of their murders, nor of their sorceries, nor of
 their fornication, nor of their thefts" (v.21). Four categories of sin
 that persist despite judgment. Which ones characterize our culture today?
 Are you standing against them?
