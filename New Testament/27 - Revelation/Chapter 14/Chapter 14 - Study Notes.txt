Revelation - Chapter 14
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The Lamb stands on Mount Zion with the 144,000, three angels announce eternal gospel and Babylon's fall, and the harvest of the earth begins — "Blessed are the dead which die in the Lord from henceforth."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: The Apostle John (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Historical Context: Chapter 14 provides a preview of coming events — a series
of visions showing the ultimate outcome before the bowl judgments begin. It
contrasts the 144,000 with the Lamb (vv.1-5) against those who take the
beast's mark (vv.9-11). Three angels announce messages (vv.6-13), and two
harvests depict final judgment (vv.14-20). This chapter assures believers:
despite chapter 13's terror, God wins.

The 144,000 with the Lamb (vv.1-5): The Lamb stands on Mount Zion with
144,000 who have the Father's name on their foreheads (v.1). They sing a new
song only they can learn (vv.2-3). They are pure, follow the Lamb, are
redeemed as firstfruits, and are without guile or fault (vv.4-5).

Three Angelic Announcements (vv.6-13):
- Angel 1 (vv.6-7): The "everlasting gospel" proclaimed to all nations:
 "Fear God, and give glory to him; for the hour of his judgment is come."
- Angel 2 (v.8): "Babylon is fallen, is fallen" — the world system's doom
 announced.
- Angel 3 (vv.9-11): Warning: those who worship the beast and take his mark
 will drink God's wrath "without mixture" — eternal torment.

"Blessed are the dead which die in the Lord" (v.13) — they rest from labor
and their works follow them.

Two Harvests (vv.14-20): The grain harvest — the Son of man reaps the earth
(vv.14-16). The grape harvest — grapes gathered and thrown into "the great
winepress of the wrath of God" (vv.17-20). Blood flows for 1,600 furlongs
(about 200 miles) — the length of Israel.

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------
Enduring Word (David Guzik):
 https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/revelation-14/
 Guzik covers the 144,000, the three angels, the harvest imagery, and the
 winepress of God's wrath.

Charles Spurgeon:
 Sermon: "Blessed Are the Dead" on v.13 (MTP Vol. 11, No. 632)
 "Blessed are the dead which die IN THE LORD. Not all dead are blessed —
 only those who die in the Lord. And their blessedness: they REST from their
 labours — no more toil, no more struggle, no more weariness. And their
 works DO FOLLOW them — not precede them (works don't earn heaven) but
 follow them (works are rewarded in heaven). Die in the Lord and you rest;
 your works are not lost but rewarded."

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. "Having his Father's name written in their foreheads" (v.1). Whose name
 do you bear — God's or the world's? Your identity is marked by your
 allegiance.

2. "Fear God, and give glory to him" (v.7). The eternal gospel is simple:
 fear God, glorify Him, worship the Creator. Is this the center of your
 life?

3. "Here is the patience of the saints" (v.12). In the face of the beast's
 power and God's coming wrath, saints are called to patient endurance. Are
 you enduring faithfully?

4. "Blessed are the dead which die in the Lord" (v.13). Death in Christ is
 not tragedy but blessing — rest and reward. Does this change how you view
 death — yours or others'?

5. The two harvests (vv.14-20) show that judgment is coming — both for
 salvation (grain) and for wrath (grapes). Which harvest will you be part
 of?
