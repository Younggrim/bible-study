Revelation - Chapter 14
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The Lamb stands on Mount Zion with the 144,000, three angels announce eternal gospel and Babylon's fall, and the harvest of the earth begins — "Blessed are the dead which die in the Lord from henceforth."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: The Apostle John (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Historical Context: Chapter 14 provides a preview of coming events — a series
of visions showing the ultimate outcome before the bowl judgments begin. It
contrasts the 144,000 with the Lamb (vv.1-5) against those who take the
beast's mark (vv.9-11). Three angels announce messages (vv.6-13), and two
harvests depict final judgment (vv.14-20). This chapter assures believers:
despite chapter 13's terror, God wins.

The 144,000 with the Lamb (vv.1-5): The Lamb stands on Mount Zion with
144,000 who have the Father's name on their foreheads (v.1). They sing a new
song only they can learn (vv.2-3). They are pure, follow the Lamb, are
redeemed as firstfruits, and are without guile or fault (vv.4-5).

Three Angelic Announcements (vv.6-13):
- Angel 1 (vv.6-7): The "everlasting gospel" proclaimed to all nations:
 "Fear God, and give glory to him; for the hour of his judgment is come."
- Angel 2 (v.8): "Babylon is fallen, is fallen" — the world system's doom
 announced.
- Angel 3 (vv.9-11): Warning: those who worship the beast and take his mark
 will drink God's wrath "without mixture" — eternal torment.

"Blessed are the dead which die in the Lord" (v.13) — they rest from labor
and their works follow them.

Two Harvests (vv.14-20): The grain harvest — the Son of man reaps the earth
(vv.14-16). The grape harvest — grapes gathered and thrown into "the great
winepress of the wrath of God" (vv.17-20). Blood flows for 1,600 furlongs
(about 200 miles) — the length of Israel.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1 — "The Lamb standing on Mount Zion, and with him 144,000 who had his name
 and his Father's name written on their foreheads." (ESV) — God's seal
 vs. the beast's mark.

v.7 — "Fear God and give him glory, for the hour of his judgment has come."
 (ESV) — The eternal gospel's message.

v.10 — "He also will drink the wine of God's wrath, poured full strength
 into the cup of his anger." (ESV) — Undiluted wrath for
 beast-worshippers.

v.12 — "Here is a call for the endurance of the saints, those who keep the
 commandments of God and their faith in Jesus." (ESV) — Endurance
 defined.

v.13 — "Blessed are the dead who die in the Lord from now on... that they
 may rest from their labors, for their deeds follow them!" (ESV) —
 Blessedness of dying in Christ.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "having his Father's name written in their foreheads" / ESV: "who
 had his name and his Father's name written on their foreheads" — ESV
 includes both the Lamb's name AND the Father's name (following better
 manuscript evidence). Contrast with 13:16-17 (the beast's mark). God's
 people bear God's name; the world bears the beast's mark.

v.2 — KJV: "the voice of many waters... the voice of a great thunder... the
 voice of harpers harping with their harps" / ESV: "the roar of many
 waters... the sound of loud thunder... the sound of harpists playing on
 their harps" — Both equivalent. ESV uses "roar" for greater intensity.

v.3 — KJV: "no man could learn that song but the hundred and forty and four
 thousand, which were redeemed from the earth" / ESV: "No one could learn
 that song except the 144,000 who had been redeemed from the earth" —
 Both equivalent. ESV modernizes the phrasing.

v.4 — KJV: "These are they which were not defiled with women; for they are
 virgins" / ESV: "It is these who have not defiled themselves with women,
 for they are virgins" — Both equivalent. Greek "parthenoi" — can be
 literal (celibate) or figurative (spiritually pure, not defiled by
 idolatry/the beast system). "Firstfruits" — the first and best of the
 harvest, dedicated to God.

v.5 — KJV: "in their mouth was found no guile: for they are without fault
 before the throne of God" / ESV: "in their mouth no lie was found, for
 they are blameless" — ESV uses "lie" for Greek "pseudos" (more precise
 than KJV's "guile"). ESV omits "before the throne of God" (absent in
 best manuscripts). ESV's "blameless" = Greek "amōmoi" (without blemish).

v.6 — KJV: "the everlasting gospel" / ESV: "an eternal gospel" — KJV uses
 definite article "the"; ESV uses indefinite "an." Greek has no article
 here. Both translate "euangelion aiōnion."

v.8 — KJV: "Babylon is fallen, is fallen, that great city, because she made
 all nations drink of the wine of the wrath of her fornication" / ESV:
 "Fallen, fallen is Babylon the great, she who made all nations drink the
 wine of the passion of her sexual immorality" — ESV uses "passion"
 (Greek "thumos" — fury/passion) and "sexual immorality" (Greek
 "porneia") rather than KJV's "wrath" and "fornication." "Babylon"
 represents the world system opposed to God.

v.10 — KJV: "poured out without mixture into the cup of his indignation" /
 ESV: "poured full strength into the cup of his anger" — Greek "akratou"
 — unmixed, undiluted. ESV's "full strength" captures the meaning well.
 In the ancient world, wine was usually diluted with water. God's wrath
 will be full strength — no mercy mixed in.

v.11 — KJV: "the smoke of their torment ascendeth up for ever and ever" /
 ESV: "the smoke of their torment goes up forever and ever" — Both
 equivalent.

v.12 — KJV: "Here is the patience of the saints: here are they that keep the
 commandments of God, and the faith of Jesus" / ESV: "Here is a call for
 the endurance of the saints, those who keep the commandments of God and
 their faith in Jesus" — ESV's "endurance" (Greek "hypomonē") is more
 precise than KJV's "patience." ESV's "faith in Jesus" interprets the
 genitive differently than KJV's "faith of Jesus."

v.13 — KJV: "their works do follow them" / ESV: "their deeds follow them" —
 ESV uses "deeds" (Greek "erga") rather than "works." Both equivalent.
 Works don't precede (earning entrance) but follow (receiving reward).
 Salvation is by grace; reward is by works.

v.14 — KJV: "one sat like unto the Son of man" / ESV: "seated on the cloud
 one like a son of man" — Both equivalent. ESV's lowercase "son of man"
 reflects the Greek (no definite article here), though the reference to
 Christ is clear from Daniel 7:13.

v.20 — KJV: "a thousand and six hundred furlongs" / ESV: "1,600 stadia" —
 Both equivalent. Greek "stadiōn" — about 200 miles. The approximate
 length of Israel. Blood reaching horse bridles = total, devastating
 judgment.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.1  — NET: "the Lamb was standing on Mount Zion, and with him
       were one hundred and forty-four thousand" — "Mount Zion"
       (to oros SiOn) — heavenly Zion, in contrast to the beast's
       earthly domain. WEB: "the Lamb standing on Mount Zion." The
       sealed ones of chapter 7 appear here victorious.

v.6  — NET: "an eternal gospel to proclaim to those who live on
       the earth — to every nation, tribe, language, and people" —
       "eternal gospel" (euangelion aiOnion). WEB: "an eternal Good
       News." Even at the end, the gospel is proclaimed — God's
       mercy persists until the last possible moment.

v.8  — NET: "Fallen, fallen is Babylon the great" — "fallen,
       fallen" (epesen epesen — doubled for emphasis/certainty).
       WEB: "Fallen, fallen is Babylon the great." Echoing Isaiah
       21:9 — the announcement precedes the detailed destruction
       (chapters 17-18).

v.13 — NET: "Blessed are the dead who die in the Lord from this
       point on" — "from this point on" (ap' arti = from now on).
       WEB: "Blessed are the dead who die in the Lord from now on."
       KJV: "Blessed are the dead which die in the Lord from
       henceforth." The second of seven beatitudes in Revelation.

v.20 — NET: "the winepress was stomped outside the city, and
       blood poured out of the winepress up to the horses' bridles
       for a distance of almost two hundred miles" — "two hundred
       miles" (stadiOn chiliOn hexakosiOn = 1,600 stadia). WEB:
       "one thousand six hundred stadia." Hyperbolic imagery of
       devastating judgment — rivers of blood.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
144,000 with the Lamb — God's sealed servants standing victorious with
 Christ. Bearing God's name, not the beast's mark.

Everlasting gospel — The eternal message: fear God, give Him glory, worship
 the Creator. Proclaimed to all nations even during judgment.

Babylon is fallen — The announcement of the world system's doom. Political,
 economic, and religious rebellion against God — destroyed.

Without mixture — God's wrath undiluted. Full strength, no mercy mixed in.
 The final judgment on those who chose the beast.

Blessed are the dead in the Lord — Those who die in Christ rest from labor
 and their works are rewarded. Death in Christ is gain.

Winepress of wrath — Final judgment depicted as grapes crushed in a press.
 The blood of judgment flows abundantly.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Having his Father's name written in their foreheads" (v.1). Whose name
 do you bear — God's or the world's? Your identity is marked by your
 allegiance.

2. "Fear God, and give glory to him" (v.7). The eternal gospel is simple:
 fear God, glorify Him, worship the Creator. Is this the center of your
 life?

3. "Here is the patience of the saints" (v.12). In the face of the beast's
 power and God's coming wrath, saints are called to patient endurance. Are
 you enduring faithfully?

4. "Blessed are the dead which die in the Lord" (v.13). Death in Christ is
 not tragedy but blessing — rest and reward. Does this change how you view
 death — yours or others'?

5. The two harvests (vv.14-20) show that judgment is coming — both for
 salvation (grain) and for wrath (grapes). Which harvest will you be part
 of?
