Revelation - Chapter 13
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Two beasts arise: one from the sea with blasphemous authority over all nations (the Antichrist), and one from the earth who performs signs and enforces the mark of the beast — "his number is Six hundred threescore and six."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: The Apostle John (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Historical Context: Chapter 13 introduces Satan's two primary agents during
the tribulation: the beast from the sea (the Antichrist, vv.1-10) and the
beast from the earth (the False Prophet, vv.11-18). Together with the dragon
(Satan, ch.12), they form an unholy trinity — a counterfeit of Father, Son,
and Spirit. This chapter describes the Antichrist's political/military power
and the False Prophet's religious deception, culminating in the infamous
"mark of the beast" (666).

The Beast from the Sea — The Antichrist (vv.1-10): Rises from the sea (the
nations) with seven heads, ten horns, ten crowns (v.1). Combines features of
Daniel's four beasts: leopard, bear, lion (v.2). The dragon gives him power
(v.2). A fatal wound healed — the world marvels (v.3). He blasphemes God for
42 months (vv.5-6). He makes war with the saints and overcomes them (v.7).
All whose names are not in the Lamb's book worship him (v.8).

The Beast from the Earth — The False Prophet (vv.11-18): Rises from the earth
with lamb-like horns but dragon speech (v.11). He promotes worship of the
first beast (v.12). He performs signs — even fire from heaven (v.13). He
deceives through miracles (v.14). He gives life to the beast's image (v.15).
He enforces the mark — without it, no one can buy or sell (vv.16-17). The
number: 666 (v.18).

Charles Spurgeon:
 Sermon: "The Patience of the Saints" on v.10 (MTP Vol. 14, No. 830)
 "Here is the patience and the faith of the saints. In the face of
 overwhelming evil — a beast with global power, forced worship, economic
 control — what is required? Not military victory but patience and faith.
 Endurance, not escape. Trust, not triumph (yet). The saints overcome not
 by fighting the beast but by remaining faithful to the Lamb."

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. "The dragon gave him his power" (v.2). Behind every human evil is a
 spiritual reality. Do you recognize the spiritual forces behind worldly
 systems that oppose God?

2. "All that dwell upon the earth shall worship him, whose names are not
 written in the book of life" (v.8). Only those in the Lamb's book resist.
 Is your name written there? What gives you confidence?

3. "Here is the patience and the faith of the saints" (v.10). In the face of
 overwhelming evil, God calls for patience and faith — not panic. How do
 you respond when evil seems to be winning?

4. The False Prophet looks like a lamb but speaks like a dragon (v.11). Not
 everything that looks spiritual IS spiritual. How do you discern between
 genuine and counterfeit spirituality?

5. The mark represents total allegiance to a system opposed to God. What
 "marks" of worldly allegiance are you tempted to accept — compromises that
 give you access to the world's system at the cost of faithfulness?
