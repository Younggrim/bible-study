Revelation - Chapter 13
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Two beasts arise: one from the sea with blasphemous authority over all nations (the Antichrist), and one from the earth who performs signs and enforces the mark of the beast — "his number is Six hundred threescore and six."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: The Apostle John (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Historical Context: Chapter 13 introduces Satan's two primary agents during
the tribulation: the beast from the sea (the Antichrist, vv.1-10) and the
beast from the earth (the False Prophet, vv.11-18). Together with the dragon
(Satan, ch.12), they form an unholy trinity — a counterfeit of Father, Son,
and Spirit. This chapter describes the Antichrist's political/military power
and the False Prophet's religious deception, culminating in the infamous
"mark of the beast" (666).

The Beast from the Sea — The Antichrist (vv.1-10): Rises from the sea (the
nations) with seven heads, ten horns, ten crowns (v.1). Combines features of
Daniel's four beasts: leopard, bear, lion (v.2). The dragon gives him power
(v.2). A fatal wound healed — the world marvels (v.3). He blasphemes God for
42 months (vv.5-6). He makes war with the saints and overcomes them (v.7).
All whose names are not in the Lamb's book worship him (v.8).

The Beast from the Earth — The False Prophet (vv.11-18): Rises from the earth
with lamb-like horns but dragon speech (v.11). He promotes worship of the
first beast (v.12). He performs signs — even fire from heaven (v.13). He
deceives through miracles (v.14). He gives life to the beast's image (v.15).
He enforces the mark — without it, no one can buy or sell (vv.16-17). The
number: 666 (v.18).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.2 — "The dragon gave him his power and his throne and great authority."
 (ESV) — Satan empowers the Antichrist.

v.7 — "It was allowed to make war on the saints and to conquer them. And
 authority was given it over every tribe and people and language and
 nation." (ESV) — Global authority over all peoples.

v.8 — "All who dwell on earth will worship it, everyone whose name has not
 been written in the book of life." (ESV) — Universal worship except
 the elect.

v.10 — "Here is a call for the endurance and faith of the saints." (ESV) —
 Endurance required.

v.16-17 — "It causes all... to be marked... so that no one can buy or sell
 unless he has the mark." (ESV) — Economic control through the mark.

v.18 — "Its number is 666." (ESV) — 666: the number of the beast.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "a beast rise up out of the sea, having seven heads and ten
 horns, and upon his horns ten crowns" / ESV: "a beast rising out of the
 sea, with ten horns and seven heads, with ten diadems on its horns" —
 Greek "thērion" — a wild beast (different from "zōon" — living creature
 in ch.4). ESV uses "diadems" (Greek "diadēmata") for royal crowns. Note
 the order difference: KJV lists heads first, ESV lists horns first.
       NET: "I saw a beast coming up out of the sea. It had
       ten horns and seven heads" — "out of the sea" (ek tEs
       thalassEs). WEB: "a beast coming up out of the sea." The
       sea represents chaos/nations (17:15). Daniel 7's four beasts
       combined into one — a composite of all human opposition to
       God.

v.2 — KJV: "like unto a leopard, and his feet were as the feet of a bear,
 and his mouth as the mouth of a lion" / ESV: "like a leopard; its feet
 were like a bear's, and its mouth was like a lion's mouth" — Both
 equivalent. Combines Daniel's four beasts (Dan 7): leopard (Greece —
 speed), bear (Persia — strength), lion (Babylon — majesty). The final
 beast incorporates all previous empires' characteristics.

v.3 — KJV: "one of his heads as it were wounded to death; and his deadly
 wound was healed" / ESV: "One of its heads seemed to have a mortal
 wound, but its mortal wound was healed" — ESV's "mortal wound" is more
 precise than KJV's "wounded to death." A counterfeit resurrection. The
 Antichrist mimics Christ's death and resurrection to deceive.

v.4 — KJV: "Who is like unto the beast? who is able to make war with him?"
 / ESV: "Who is like the beast, and who can fight against it?" — Both
 equivalent. A blasphemous parody of "Who is like unto thee, O LORD?"
 (Exodus 15:11).
       NET: "they worshiped the dragon because he gave ruling
       authority to the beast" — "worshiped the dragon" (prosekynEsan
       tO drakonti). WEB: "they worshiped the dragon." KJV: "they
       worshipped the dragon." Satan's ultimate goal achieved —
       worship directed to himself through a human proxy.

v.5 — KJV: "a mouth speaking great things and blasphemies" / ESV: "a mouth
 uttering haughty and blasphemous words" — ESV adds "haughty" (Greek
 "megala" — great/boastful things). KJV's "great things" is more literal.

v.7 — KJV: "to make war with the saints, and to overcome them" / ESV: "to
 make war on the saints and to conquer them" — ESV uses "conquer" (Greek
 "nikaō") rather than "overcome."

v.8 — KJV: "the Lamb slain from the foundation of the world" / ESV: "the
 book of life of the Lamb who was slain" — Significant structural
 difference. KJV connects "from the foundation of the world" with the
 Lamb's slaying (planned before creation). ESV connects it with names
 written in the book (elected before creation). Greek syntax allows both
 readings.
       NET: "all those who live on the earth will worship the
       beast, everyone whose name has not been written since the
       foundation of the world in the book of life" — "since the
       foundation" (apo katabolEs kosmou). WEB: "from the
       foundation of the world." Names written BEFORE creation —
       divine foreknowledge and election.

v.10 — KJV: "He that leadeth into captivity shall go into captivity: he that
 killeth with the sword must be killed with the sword" / ESV: "If anyone
 is to be taken captive, to captivity he goes; if anyone is to be slain
 with the sword, with the sword must he be slain" — ESV follows a
 different textual variant, reading it as a call to accept God's
 sovereign will rather than a promise of retribution.
       NET: "This requires steadfast endurance and faith from the
       saints" — "steadfast endurance" (hypomonE = patient
       perseverance under pressure). WEB: "Here is the perseverance
       and the faith of the saints." In the face of the beast's
       power, endurance IS victory.

v.11 — KJV: "two horns like a lamb, and he spake as a dragon" / ESV: "two
 horns like a lamb and it spoke like a dragon" — Both equivalent.
 Religious appearance (lamb-like) but demonic speech (dragon). The False
 Prophet looks spiritual but speaks Satan's words.

v.15 — KJV: "he had power to give life unto the image of the beast" / ESV:
 "it was allowed to give breath to the image of the beast" — ESV uses
 "breath" (Greek "pneuma") rather than "life." ESV's "allowed" emphasizes
 God's permissive sovereignty over the beast's actions.

v.16 — KJV: "to receive a mark in their right hand, or in their foreheads"
 / ESV: "to be marked on the right hand or the forehead" — Greek
 "charagma" — a stamp, brand, engraving. Like a slave's brand or a
 coin's imprint. KJV says "in" (suggesting embedded); ESV says "on"
 (suggesting external). Greek "epi" can mean either.

v.18 — KJV: "Six hundred threescore and six" / ESV: "666" — Both identical
 in value. ESV uses numerals. Interpretations: (1) Nero Caesar in Hebrew
 gematria, (2) symbolic: 6 = man's number (one short of 7/perfection),
 tripled for emphasis — the ultimate human pretension to deity, (3) a
 future identification system.
       NET: "let the one who has insight calculate the beast's
       number, for it is man's number, and his number is 666" —
       "man's number" (arithmos anthrOpou). WEB: "his number is
       six hundred sixty-six." Some manuscripts read 616. Gematria
       (number = letters) most commonly yields "Nero Caesar" in
       Hebrew. Ultimate meaning debated across millennia.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Beast from the sea — The Antichrist. A political/military leader empowered
 by Satan with global authority.

Beast from the earth — The False Prophet. A religious leader who promotes
 worship of the Antichrist through signs and deception.

Unholy trinity — Satan (counterfeit Father), Antichrist (counterfeit Son),
 False Prophet (counterfeit Spirit). A demonic imitation.

Mark of the beast — A mark of allegiance to the Antichrist. Required for
 economic participation. Represents total submission to evil.

666 — The number of the beast. Man's number (6) tripled — the
 ultimate human attempt to be God, always falling short.

Book of life — God's register of the redeemed. Those written in it will
 not worship the beast.

Patience and faith — What saints need during the beast's reign. Not military
 victory but faithful endurance.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "The dragon gave him his power" (v.2). Behind every human evil is a
 spiritual reality. Do you recognize the spiritual forces behind worldly
 systems that oppose God?

2. "All that dwell upon the earth shall worship him, whose names are not
 written in the book of life" (v.8). Only those in the Lamb's book resist.
 Is your name written there? What gives you confidence?

3. "Here is the patience and the faith of the saints" (v.10). In the face of
 overwhelming evil, God calls for patience and faith — not panic. How do
 you respond when evil seems to be winning?

4. The False Prophet looks like a lamb but speaks like a dragon (v.11). Not
 everything that looks spiritual IS spiritual. How do you discern between
 genuine and counterfeit spirituality?

5. The mark represents total allegiance to a system opposed to God. What
 "marks" of worldly allegiance are you tempted to accept — compromises that
 give you access to the world's system at the cost of faithfulness?
