Revelation - Chapter 7
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
An interlude: 144,000 from Israel are sealed for protection, and a great multitude from every nation stands before the throne in white robes — "these are they which came out of great tribulation, and have washed their robes in the blood of the Lamb."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: The Apostle John (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Historical Context: Chapter 7 is an interlude between the sixth and seventh
seals — answering the question of 6:17: "Who shall be able to stand?" Answer:
those sealed by God. Two groups are presented: 144,000 sealed from Israel
(vv.1-8) and a great innumerable multitude from all nations (vv.9-17). This
chapter provides assurance: even during judgment, God protects His own.

The 144,000 Sealed (vv.1-8): Four angels hold back destructive winds (v.1).
Another angel with God's seal commands: don't harm the earth until God's
servants are sealed (vv.2-3). 144,000 sealed — 12,000 from each of 12 tribes
of Israel (vv.4-8). Interpretations: (1) literal Jewish believers preserved
during the tribulation, (2) symbolic of the complete church (spiritual Israel),
(3) Jewish evangelists during the tribulation.

The Great Multitude (vv.9-17): An innumerable multitude from every nation,
in white robes with palm branches (v.9). They cry: "Salvation to our God...
and unto the Lamb" (v.10). Angels, elders, and creatures worship (vv.11-12).
Identity: "These are they which came out of great tribulation, and have washed
their robes... in the blood of the Lamb" (v.14). Their reward: God's presence,
no more hunger/thirst/heat, the Lamb as shepherd, tears wiped away (vv.15-17).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.3 — "Do not harm the earth or the sea or the trees, until we have sealed
 the servants of our God on their foreheads." (ESV) — God protects His
 own before judgment falls.

v.9 — "A great multitude that no one could number, from every nation, from
 all tribes and peoples and languages." (ESV) — The scope of redemption.

v.14 — "These are the ones coming out of the great tribulation. They have
 washed their robes and made them white in the blood of the Lamb."
 (ESV) — Tribulation saints cleansed by Christ's blood.

v.16 — "They shall hunger no more, neither thirst anymore." (ESV) — The end
 of all suffering.

v.17 — "For the Lamb in the midst of the throne will be their shepherd, and
 he will guide them to springs of living water, and God will wipe away
 every tear from their eyes." (ESV) — The Lamb as eternal shepherd.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.3 — KJV: "sealed" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "sphragisōmen" — to stamp with a seal. Marks
 ownership and protection. God's seal = divine protection during
 judgment.
       NET: "Do not damage the earth or the sea or the trees
       until we have put a seal on the foreheads of the servants
       of our God" — "seal" (sphragisOmen = stamp with a mark of
       ownership/protection). WEB: "until we have sealed the
       bondservants of our God." Divine marking before judgment
       falls — protection for God's own.

v.4 — KJV: "an hundred and forty and four thousand" / ESV: "144,000" — Both
 equivalent; ESV uses numerals, KJV spells out. 12 x 12 x 1,000.
 Whether literal or symbolic, it represents completeness — not one of
 God's chosen is missing.

v.9 — KJV: "a great multitude, which no man could number" / ESV: "great multitude that no one could number" — In contrast to
 the specific 144,000, this group is innumerable. The full harvest of
 redemption from all peoples.
       NET: "a large crowd that no one could count, from every
       nation, tribe, people, and language" — "no one could count"
       (arithmEsai auton oudeis edynato). WEB: "a great multitude,
       which no man could count." The redeemed are innumerable —
       God's saving purpose succeeds on a massive scale.

v.14 — KJV: "great tribulation" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "tēs thlipseōs tēs megalēs" — THE
 tribulation, THE great one. The specific end-times period of
 unprecedented suffering (cf. Matt 24:21, Dan 12:1).
       NET: "These are the ones who have come out of the great
       tribulation. They have washed their robes and made them
       white in the blood of the Lamb" — "washed... in blood" —
       paradox: blood that whitens. WEB: "washed their robes, and
       made them white in the Lamb's blood." Purity through
       sacrifice, not self-effort.

v.14 — KJV: "washed their robes... in the blood of the Lamb" / ESV: "washed their robes and made them white in the blood of the Lamb" — A paradox:
 blood that whitens. Christ's blood cleanses rather than stains.
       NET: "These are the ones who have come out of the great
       tribulation. They have washed their robes and made them
       white in the blood of the Lamb" — "washed... in blood" —
       paradox: blood that whitens. WEB: "washed their robes, and
       made them white in the Lamb's blood." Purity through
       sacrifice, not self-effort.

v.15 — KJV: "shall dwell among them" / ESV: "will shelter them with his
 presence" — Greek "skēnosei ep' autous" — will spread His tabernacle
 over them. ESV's "shelter with his presence" captures the protective
 tent/tabernacle imagery better than KJV's "dwell among."

v.16 — NET: "They will never go hungry or be thirsty again, and
       the sun will not beat down on them" — echoing Isaiah 49:10.
       WEB: "They will never be hungry or thirsty any more." The
       end of all deprivation — reversing the sufferings of
       earthly tribulation.

v.17 — KJV: "living fountains of waters" / ESV: "springs of living water" —
 Greek "zōsas pēgas hudatōn" — springs of living water. KJV
 "fountains" vs. ESV "springs" — same Greek word "pēgas." ESV's word
 order follows the Greek more naturally. Eternal refreshment, never-
 ending satisfaction (cf. John 4:14, 7:38).
       NET: "the Lamb in the middle of the throne will shepherd
       them and lead them to springs of living water" — "shepherd"
       (poimanei = tend/feed/guide). WEB: "the Lamb who is in the
       middle of the throne will be their shepherd." The slain Lamb
       becomes the Shepherd — imagery from Psalm 23 and John 10
       consummated in eternity.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
144,000 — God's sealed servants from Israel's tribes. Whether literal
 Jewish believers or symbolic of the complete church is debated.

Sealed in foreheads — Marked by God for protection. Divine ownership and
 preservation during judgment.

Great multitude — The innumerable company of the redeemed from all nations.
 The full harvest of Christ's redemption.

Great tribulation — The unprecedented period of suffering before Christ's
 return. These saints came through it by faith.

Washed in the blood — Cleansed by Christ's sacrifice. Made pure through His
 death, not their own merit.

God shall wipe away tears — The ultimate comfort: God personally removing
 every trace of sorrow from His people forever.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Sealed the servants of our God" (v.3). God protects His own even in
 judgment. Are you sealed — marked as His? Do you live with the confidence
 of divine protection?

2. "A great multitude, which no man could number" (v.9). Heaven will be
 vastly diverse — every nation, language, people. Does your life and
 witness contribute to this multitude?

3. "Came out of great tribulation" (v.14). These saints endured the worst
 and came through. Whatever you're facing, you can come through it too —
 washed in the Lamb's blood.

4. "God shall wipe away all tears" (v.17). Every sorrow has an expiration
 date. God Himself will personally remove every tear. Does this hope
 sustain you in current grief?

5. "The Lamb... shall feed them, and shall lead them" (v.17). The Lamb is
 your shepherd — feeding, leading, satisfying. Are you following Him to
 living waters?
