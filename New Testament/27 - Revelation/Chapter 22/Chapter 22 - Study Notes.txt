Revelation - Chapter 22
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The river of life flows from God's throne, the tree of life bears twelve fruits, His servants see His face, and Jesus declares "Behold, I come quickly" — the Spirit and the Bride say "Come," and Scripture's final prayer: "Even so, come, Lord Jesus."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: The Apostle John (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Historical Context: Chapter 22 is the final chapter of the Bible. It
completes the vision of the New Jerusalem (vv.1-5), then provides the
epilogue to the entire book of Revelation (vv.6-21). The chapter brings the
Bible full circle: Genesis opened with a garden, a tree of life, a river, and
God's presence — all lost through sin. Revelation closes with a city-garden,
the tree of life restored, a river of life, and God's presence regained
forever. The curse is reversed. Paradise is restored.

The River and Tree of Life (vv.1-5): A pure river of water of life flows from
God's throne (v.1). The tree of life on both sides of the river — bearing
twelve fruits monthly, leaves for healing nations (v.2). No more curse (v.3).
God's servants serve Him, see His face, bear His name (vv.3-4). No night —
God is their light — they reign forever (v.5).

The Epilogue (vv.6-21): "These sayings are faithful and true" (v.6). "Behold,
I come quickly" — three times (vv.7, 12, 20). Blessed are those who keep this
prophecy (v.7). John warned not to worship the angel (vv.8-9). "Seal not the
sayings" — the time is near (v.10). Christ comes with reward (v.12). "I am
Alpha and Omega" (v.13). The final invitation: "The Spirit and the bride say,
Come... whosoever will, let him take the water of life freely" (v.17). Warning
against adding to or taking from this book (vv.18-19). "Surely I come quickly.
Amen. Even so, come, Lord Jesus" (v.20). Grace (v.21).

v.21 — "The grace of the Lord Jesus be with all. Amen." (ESV) — The Bible's

COMMENTARY REFERENCES
------------------------------------
Enduring Word (David Guzik):
 https://enduringword.com/bible-commentary/revelation-22/
 Guzik covers the river and tree of life, the no-more-curse promise, the
 final invitation, and the Bible's closing words.

Charles Spurgeon:
 Sermon: "Come, Lord Jesus" on v.20 (MTP Vol. 22, No. 1326)
 "Even so, come, Lord Jesus — the last prayer of the Bible. The church's
 deepest longing expressed in four words. Come — we are ready. Come — we are
 waiting. Come — we are longing. Come, Lord Jesus. Not 'come eventually' but
 'come quickly.' This should be the prayer of every believer: not fear of
 His coming but desire for it. Even so — yes, truly, absolutely — come."
 Sermon: "Whosoever Will" on v.17 (MTP Vol. 30, No. 1808)
 "Whosoever WILL — the widest possible invitation. No one excluded by God
 who is not excluded by their own unwillingness. The Spirit says come. The
 bride says come. The hearer says come. The thirsty are invited. Whosoever
 wills may take. Freely — without cost, without merit, without condition
 except willingness. The Bible ends not with a threat but with an invitation.
 Come."

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "a pure river of water of life, clear as crystal, proceeding out
 of the throne of God and of the Lamb" / ESV: "the river of the water of
 life, bright as crystal, flowing from the throne of God and of the Lamb"
 — Both equivalent. ESV uses "bright" for Greek "lampron" (shining/
 radiant) rather than KJV's "clear." Greek "hudatos zōēs" — living
 water. The river flows from "the throne of God AND of the Lamb" —
 Father and Son together as the source of life.

v.2 — KJV: "the tree of life, which bare twelve manner of fruits, and
 yielded her fruit every month: and the leaves of the tree were for the
 healing of the nations" / ESV: "the tree of life with its twelve kinds
 of fruit, yielding its fruit each month. The leaves of the tree were for
 the healing of the nations" — Both equivalent. Greek "xulon zōēs" — the
 tree of life from Genesis 2:9, now restored. "Healing" — Greek
 "therapeian" — health/wholeness, not curing sickness but providing
 ongoing vitality.

v.3 — KJV: "there shall be no more curse" / ESV: "No longer will there be
 anything accursed" — ESV's "anything accursed" (Greek "katathema") is
 more expansive than KJV's "no more curse." The curse of Genesis 3:17 is
 permanently reversed. Everything that sin corrupted is restored.

v.4 — KJV: "they shall see his face; and his name shall be in their
 foreheads" / ESV: "They will see his face, and his name will be on
 their foreheads" — Both equivalent. The beatific vision. In the OT, no
 one could see God's face and live (Exodus 33:20). In eternity, believers
 see Him face to face — the ultimate intimacy.

v.5 — KJV: "there shall be no night there; and they need no candle, neither
 light of the sun; for the Lord God giveth them light: and they shall
 reign for ever and ever" / ESV: "And night will be no more. They will
 need no light of lamp or sun, for the Lord God will be their light, and
 they will reign forever and ever" — ESV uses "lamp" for KJV's "candle"
 (Greek "luchnos"). Both equivalent in meaning.

v.7 — KJV: "Behold, I come quickly: blessed is he that keepeth the sayings
 of the prophecy of this book" / ESV: "And behold, I am coming soon.
 Blessed is the one who keeps the words of the prophecy of this book" —
 ESV uses "soon" for Greek "tachu" rather than KJV's "quickly." The word
 can mean either "soon" or "suddenly."

v.11 — KJV: "He that is unjust, let him be unjust still: and he which is
 filthy, let him be filthy still: and he that is righteous, let him be
 righteous still: and he that is holy, let him be holy still" / ESV: "Let
 the evildoer still do evil, and the filthy still be filthy, and the
 righteous still do right, and the holy still be holy" — ESV uses
 "evildoer" for KJV's "unjust." Both convey the finality of character at
 judgment — no more opportunity to change.

v.12 — KJV: "my reward is with me, to give every man according as his work
 shall be" / ESV: "bringing my recompense with me, to repay each one for
 what he has done" — ESV uses "recompense" (Greek "misthos" — wages/
 reward) and "repay" rather than KJV's "reward" and "give."

v.13 — KJV: "I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the end, the first and
 the last" / ESV: "I am the Alpha and the Omega, the first and the last,
 the beginning and the end" — Note the different order: KJV puts
 "beginning and end" second; ESV puts "first and last" second. Three
 pairs expressing the same truth: Christ encompasses all of reality.

v.14 — KJV: "Blessed are they that do his commandments, that they may have
 right to the tree of life" / ESV: "Blessed are those who wash their
 robes, so that they may have the right to the tree of life" —
 Significant textual difference. KJV follows the Textus Receptus ("do his
 commandments" — Greek "poiountes tas entolas autou"). ESV follows
 earlier manuscripts ("wash their robes" — Greek "plunontes tas stolas
 autōn"). The difference is one letter in Greek.

v.16 — KJV: "I am the root and the offspring of David, and the bright and
 morning star" / ESV: "I am the root and the descendant of David, the
 bright morning star" — ESV uses "descendant" for Greek "genos"
 (offspring/descendant). Christ is both David's ancestor (root — He
 created David) and David's descendant (offspring — born from David's
 line). "The bright morning star" — the star that announces the dawn.

v.17 — KJV: "the Spirit and the bride say, Come... And whosoever will, let
 him take the water of life freely" / ESV: "The Spirit and the Bride say,
 'Come.'... let the one who desires take the water of life without price"
 — ESV uses "without price" (Greek "dōrean" — as a gift, without
 payment) for KJV's "freely." Greek "ho thelōn" — the one who wills/
 desires. The final invitation is to anyone who wants to come.

v.19 — KJV: "God shall take away his part out of the book of life" / ESV:
 "God will take away his share in the tree of life" — Significant textual
 difference. KJV reads "book of life" (Textus Receptus); ESV reads "tree
 of life" (Greek "xulou" — tree, following earlier manuscripts).

v.20 — KJV: "Surely I come quickly. Amen. Even so, come, Lord Jesus" / ESV:
 "Surely I am coming soon." Amen. Come, Lord Jesus!" — ESV uses "soon"
 for "quickly." Greek "Nai, erchou, Kurie Iēsou" — Yes, come, Lord
 Jesus. The church's final prayer. Echoes the Aramaic "Maranatha" (1 Cor
 16:22).

v.21 — KJV: "The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all. Amen." /
 ESV: "The grace of the Lord Jesus be with all. Amen." — ESV omits
 "Christ" and "you" (following shorter manuscript readings). The Bible's
 final word is GRACE. From Genesis to Revelation, grace has the last
 word.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.1  — NET: "Then the angel showed me the river of the water
       of life — Loss clear as crystal — flowing from the throne
       of God and of the Lamb" — "water of life" (hydatos zOEs).
       WEB: "a river of water of life, clear as crystal." Echoing
       Ezekiel 47 and Genesis 2 — paradise restored and surpassed.

v.2  — NET: "On each side of the river is the tree of life
       producing twelve kinds of fruit, yielding its fruit every
       month" — "tree of life" (xylon zOEs = wood/tree of life).
       WEB: "the tree of life." Genesis 3 barred access; Revelation
       22 restores it — no more cherubim guarding the way.

v.4  — NET: "They will see his face, and his name will be on
       their foreheads" — "see his face" (opsontai to prosOpon
       autou). WEB: "They will see his face." The beatific vision
       — what Moses was denied (Exodus 33:20) believers receive
       eternally. Face-to-face with God.

v.17 — NET: "And the Spirit and the bride say, 'Come!' And let
       the one who hears say, 'Come!' And let the one who is
       thirsty come; let the one who wants it take the water of
       life free of charge" — "free of charge" (dOrean = as a
       gift). WEB: "let him who desires take the water of life
       freely." The final gospel invitation — free, universal,
       Spirit-empowered.

v.20 — NET: "The one who testifies to these things says, 'Yes, I
       am coming soon!' Amen! Come, Lord Jesus!" — "Come, Lord
       Jesus" (erchou kyrie IEsou — Maranatha in Aramaic). WEB:
       "Amen! Yes, come, Lord Jesus." KJV: "Even so, come, Lord
       Jesus." The last prayer of the Bible — the church's longing
       across two millennia, still unanswered, still urgent.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
River of water of life — Eternal life flowing from God's throne. Unending
 refreshment and vitality in God's presence.

Tree of life — Restored from Eden. Bearing fruit perpetually, providing
 healing/wholeness. Access regained through Christ.

No more curse — The complete reversal of Genesis 3. Sin's consequences
 permanently removed. Paradise restored.

See His face — The beatific vision. Face-to-face intimacy with God —
 impossible under the curse, eternal in the new creation.

Alpha and Omega — Christ as the beginning and end of all things. He
 encompasses all reality.

Bright and morning star — Christ as the herald of eternal day. The star
 that announces dawn — the end of night forever.

Whosoever will — The Bible's final invitation. Open to anyone who desires.
 No one excluded except by their own refusal.

Come, Lord Jesus — The church's final prayer. Longing for Christ's return.
 The appropriate response to all of Revelation.

Grace — The Bible's last word. From creation to consummation, grace
 defines God's relationship with humanity.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "There shall be no more curse" (v.3). Everything sin broke will be fixed.
 Every consequence of the fall — reversed. What aspect of the curse do you
 most long to see removed?

2. "They shall see his face" (v.4). Face-to-face with God — the ultimate
 destiny. Does this hope draw you? Is seeing God's face your deepest
 desire?

3. "Behold, I come quickly" (v.12). Christ's return is imminent. Are you
 ready? Are you living in anticipation? Can you say with John, "Even so,
 come, Lord Jesus"?

4. "Whosoever will, let him take the water of life freely" (v.17). The
 Bible's final invitation is open to anyone who will come. Have you come?
 Are you inviting others to come?

5. "The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all" (v.21). The Bible
 ends with grace. Whatever you face, grace is the final word. God's grace
 is with you — today, tomorrow, and forever.

6. The Bible begins with paradise lost (Genesis 1-3) and ends with paradise
 restored (Revelation 21-22). The whole story is about God bringing His
 people home. You are part of that story. How does knowing the ending
 change how you live the middle?
