Revelation - Chapter 16
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The seven bowls of wrath are poured out: sores, sea turned to blood, rivers to blood, scorching heat, darkness on the beast's kingdom, the Euphrates dried up for eastern kings, and the final earthquake destroying cities — "It is done."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: The Apostle John (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Historical Context: Chapter 16 contains the seven bowl (vial) judgments — the
final, most severe outpouring of God's wrath. Unlike the seals (one-quarter)
and trumpets (one-third), the bowls are total and complete. They echo the
Egyptian plagues but on a global scale. Despite unprecedented suffering,
humanity blasphemes God and refuses to repent (vv.9, 11, 21). The chapter
culminates in Armageddon (v.16) and the declaration "It is done" (v.17).

The Seven Bowls:
- Bowl 1 (v.2): Painful sores on those with the beast's mark.
- Bowl 2 (v.3): The entire sea becomes blood; all sea life dies.
- Bowl 3 (vv.4-7): Rivers/springs become blood. Heaven declares God's justice.
- Bowl 4 (vv.8-9): The sun scorches men with fire. They blaspheme, don't repent.
- Bowl 5 (vv.10-11): Darkness on the beast's kingdom. Pain. Blasphemy. No repentance.
- Bowl 6 (vv.12-16): Euphrates dried up. Demonic spirits gather kings to Armageddon.
- Bowl 7 (vv.17-21): "It is done." Greatest earthquake ever. Babylon judged. 100-pound hailstones. Blasphemy.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.7 — "Yes, Lord God the Almighty, true and just are your judgments!" (ESV) —
 Heaven affirms God's justice.

v.9 — "They did not repent and give him glory." (ESV) — Stubborn refusal
 despite judgment.

v.15 — "Behold, I am coming like a thief! Blessed is the one who stays
 awake." (ESV) — Christ's warning inserted amid judgment.

v.16 — "They assembled them at the place... called Armageddon." (ESV) — The
 final battle's location.

v.17 — "It is done!" (ESV) — God's wrath completed.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "pour out the vials of the wrath of God upon the earth" / ESV:
 "pour out on the earth the seven bowls of the wrath of God" — ESV uses
 "bowls" (Greek "phialas" — broad, shallow bowls poured out quickly).
 KJV's "vials" suggests narrow containers, which is less accurate.

v.2 — KJV: "a noisome and grievous sore" / ESV: "harmful and painful sores"
 — Both equivalent. KJV's "noisome" (archaic for noxious/harmful) is
 clarified by ESV's "harmful."

v.3 — KJV: "it became as the blood of a dead man" / ESV: "it became like
 the blood of a corpse" — Both equivalent. ESV's "corpse" is more direct
 than KJV's "dead man." The sea becomes thick, coagulated, lifeless.

v.5 — KJV: "Thou art righteous, O Lord, which art, and wast, and shalt be"
 / ESV: "Just are you, O Holy One, who is and who was" — Significant
 difference: KJV includes "shalt be" (future); ESV omits it (following
 better manuscripts). The omission may suggest God's future coming is now
 present — He has arrived in judgment. ESV uses "Holy One" where KJV has
 "Lord."

v.6 — KJV: "for they are worthy" / ESV: "It is what they deserve!" — ESV
 makes the retributive justice more explicit. Greek "axioi" — worthy,
 deserving. They shed blood; they receive blood to drink.

v.7 — KJV: "Even so, Lord God Almighty, true and righteous are thy
 judgments" / ESV: "Yes, Lord God the Almighty, true and just are your
 judgments!" — Both equivalent. ESV uses "just" for Greek "dikaiai"
 rather than KJV's "righteous."

v.9 — KJV: "repented not to give him glory" / ESV: "They did not repent and
 give him glory" — Both equivalent. Three times in this chapter (vv.9,
 11, 21) humanity refuses to repent. Judgment alone cannot produce
 repentance.

v.13 — KJV: "three unclean spirits like frogs" / ESV: "three unclean
 spirits like frogs" — Both identical. Frogs = unclean animals (Lev
 11:10), associated with the Egyptian plague (Exodus 8).

v.14 — KJV: "the spirits of devils, working miracles" / ESV: "demonic
 spirits, performing signs" — ESV's "demonic spirits" is more direct
 than KJV's "spirits of devils." ESV uses "signs" (Greek "sēmeia")
 rather than "miracles."

v.15 — KJV: "Blessed is he that watcheth, and keepeth his garments" / ESV:
 "Blessed is the one who stays awake, keeping his garments on" — Both
 equivalent. ESV modernizes the language.

v.16 — KJV: "Armageddon" / ESV: "Armageddon" — Both identical. Hebrew
 "Har-Megiddo" — the mountain/hill of Megiddo. A plain in northern
 Israel where many battles were fought. The gathering place for the final
 battle against Christ.

v.17 — KJV: "It is done" / ESV: "It is done!" — Both identical. Greek
 "gegonen" — it has happened, it is accomplished. Perfect tense:
 completed with permanent results.

v.19 — KJV: "the cup of the wine of the fierceness of his wrath" / ESV:
 "the cup of the wine of the fury of his wrath" — ESV uses "fury" for
 Greek "thumos" (burning anger) vs. KJV's "fierceness." Both convey
 intense, passionate wrath.

v.21 — KJV: "every stone about the weight of a talent" / ESV: "about one
 hundred pounds each" — ESV converts the measurement. A talent = about
 75-100 pounds. Hailstones of unprecedented size. KJV: "blasphemed God
 because of the plague of the hail" / ESV: "they cursed God for the
 plague of the hail" — ESV uses "cursed" for Greek "eblasphēmēsan"
 rather than "blasphemed."

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.1  — NET: "Go and pour out on the earth the seven bowls of
       the wrath of God" — "pour out" (ekcheate = empty/dump out).
       WEB: "Go and pour out the seven bowls of the wrath of God."
       No restraint, no mixture — pure, undiluted divine wrath
       released.

v.5  — NET: "You are just — the one who is and who was, the Holy
       One — because you have passed these judgments" — "you are
       just" (dikaios ei). WEB: "You are righteous." Even the
       angel of the waters affirms God's justice in judgment —
       these plagues are DESERVED.

v.9  — NET: "people were scorched by the terrible heat, yet they
       blasphemed the name of God... and refused to repent" —
       "refused to repent" (ou metenoEsan). WEB: "they didn't
       repent." Repeated refrain (vv.9, 11, 21) — judgment hardens
       rather than softens those who refuse God.

v.15 — NET: "Look! I will come like a thief! Blessed is the one
       who stays alert" — "stays alert" (grEgorOn = watching).
       WEB: "Blessed is he who watches." KJV: "Blessed is he that
       watcheth." Christ's voice interrupts the bowl judgments —
       the third beatitude of Revelation.

v.16 — NET: "they assembled them at the place that in Hebrew is
       called Armageddon" — "Armageddon" (HarmagedOn = Mountain of
       Megiddo). WEB: "Har-Magedon." KJV: "Armageddon." The great
       battlefield — whether literal or symbolic of the final
       conflict between God and evil.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Bowl judgments — The seven final, complete outpourings of God's wrath. Total, not partial. The climax of divine judgment.

Armageddon — The gathering place for the final battle. Har-Megiddo in northern Israel. Where the world's armies assemble against Christ.

It is done — The completion of God's wrath. Judgment finished, plan accomplished.

Repented not — Humanity's persistent refusal to turn to God despite devastating judgment. The ultimate hardness of heart.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "True and righteous are thy judgments" (v.7). Even in the most severe judgment, God is just. Can you trust His justice even when it seems harsh?

2. "They repented not" (vv.9, 11, 21). Three times — stubborn refusal. What does it take for YOU to repent? Don't wait for judgment to drive you to God — come now through grace.

3. "Behold, I come as a thief" (v.15). Even amid final judgments, Christ warns: be ready. Are you watching? Are you keeping your garments?

4. "It is done" (v.17). God's plan will be completed. Nothing can stop it. Are you on the right side of "done"?
