2 John - Chapter 1
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
John writes to the "elect lady" urging her to walk in truth and love, warns against deceivers who deny Christ's coming in the flesh, and instructs believers not to receive false teachers into their homes lest they share in their wicked works.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: "The elder" (v.1) — the Apostle John. Written approximately AD 85-95.
The shortest book in the NT by word count (in Greek).

Recipient: "The elect lady and her children" (v.1) — either a specific woman
and her family, or (more likely) a local church personified as a lady with
her members as children. "Her elect sister" (v.13) would be another church.

Purpose: To encourage walking in truth and love (vv.4-6) while warning against
receiving false teachers who deny Christ's incarnation (vv.7-11). A brief,
urgent letter balancing truth and love — you need both.

Theme: Truth and love together. Walk in truth (v.4), walk in love (v.6), but
don't extend hospitality to those who deny Christ (vv.10-11).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.4 — "I rejoiced greatly to find some of your children walking in the
 truth." (ESV) — Joy in others' faithfulness.

v.6 — "This is love, that we walk according to his commandments." (ESV) —
 Love defined as obedience.

v.7 — "Many deceivers have gone out into the world, those who do not confess
 the coming of Jesus Christ in the flesh. Such a one is the deceiver
 and the antichrist." (ESV) — The incarnation test.

v.8 — "Watch yourselves, so that you may not lose what we have worked for,
 but may win a full reward." (ESV) — Guard your progress.

v.9 — "Everyone who goes on ahead and does not abide in the teaching of
 Christ, does not have God." (ESV) — Staying in Christ's teaching =
 having God.

v.10 — "If anyone comes to you and does not bring this teaching, do not
 receive him into your house." (ESV) — Don't support false teachers.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "the elder" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "ho presbuteros" —
 the old man, the elder. John's self-designation in his later years.

v.1 — KJV: "elect lady" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "eklektē kuria" —
 chosen lady. Either a proper name (Lady Electa or Kyria) or a
 personification of a church.

v.6 — KJV: "walk after his commandments" / ESV: "walk according to his
 commandments" — Both equivalent. Love defined as obedience to God's
 commands.

v.7 — KJV: "confess not that Jesus Christ is come in the flesh" / ESV:
 "do not confess the coming of Jesus Christ in the flesh" — Greek
 "erchomenon en sarki" — present participle: coming in flesh. The
 Gnostic heresy denied Christ's true, ongoing humanity.

v.8 — KJV: "look to yourselves, that we lose not those things which we have
 wrought" / ESV: "watch yourselves, so that you may not lose what we
 have worked for" — Greek "misthon plērē" — complete reward. Don't
 lose what you've built through association with false teaching.

v.9 — KJV: "transgresseth" / ESV: "goes on ahead" — Greek "proagōn" — goes
 ahead, goes beyond. Those who "progress" beyond Christ's teaching
 actually regress away from God.

v.10 — KJV: "receive him not into your house" / ESV: Both equivalent. — In
 the first century, traveling teachers depended on hospitality. Hosting
 a false teacher = supporting his ministry. Don't provide a platform
 for error.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.1  — NET: "To the elect lady and her children, whom I love
       in truth" — "elect lady" (eklektE kyria) — debated: a
       specific woman or a church personified? WEB: "the chosen
       lady." KJV: "the elect lady." If a church, "her children"
       are its members; "her sister" (v.13) is another church.

v.7  — NET: "many deceivers have gone out into the world, people
       who do not confess Jesus as Christ coming in the flesh" —
       "coming in the flesh" (erchomenon en sarki = present
       participle). WEB: "who don't confess that Jesus Christ came
       in the flesh." The incarnation as the test of orthodoxy —
       deny it and you have "the deceiver and the antichrist."

v.8  — NET: "Watch out, so that you do not lose the things we
       have worked for" — "worked for" (eirgasametha). WEB: "Watch
       yourselves, that we don't lose the things which we have
       accomplished." KJV: "that we lose not those things which we
       have wrought." Progress can be lost — vigilance required.

v.9  — NET: "Everyone who goes on ahead and does not remain in
       the teaching of Christ does not have God" — "goes on ahead"
       (proagOn = progresses beyond). WEB: "Whoever transgresses
       and doesn't remain in the teaching of Christ." "Progress"
       beyond Christ's teaching is actually regress — departure
       from God.

v.10 — NET: "do not receive him into your house and do not give
       him any greeting" — "greeting" (chairein = rejoice/welcome).
       WEB: "don't receive him into your house, and don't welcome
       him." Hospitality to false teachers = partnership in their
       deception (v.11). Not rude but protective.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Walking in truth — Living consistently with God's revealed truth. Truth as
 lifestyle, not just belief.

Love = obedience — John's definition: love is walking in God's commands.
 Not sentiment but submission.

Deceiver and antichrist — One who denies Christ's incarnation. Both a
 theological error and a spiritual identity.

Receive him not — Don't provide hospitality, support, or platform to false
 teachers. Hospitality = endorsement.

Full reward — The complete reward for faithful service. Can be diminished
 by association with false teaching.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Walking in truth" (v.4). Is your life consistent with what you believe?
 Are you walking in truth or just talking about it?

2. "This is love, that we walk after his commandments" (v.6). Do you define
 love by feelings or by obedience? Biblical love is expressed through
 keeping God's commands.

3. "Receive him not into your house" (v.10). Are you discerning about who you
 support, platform, and endorse? Supporting false teachers makes you a
 partner in their work.

4. How do you balance truth and love? Some emphasize truth without love
 (harsh). Others emphasize love without truth (permissive). John holds both
 together. Where do you lean?
