2 John - Chapter 1
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
John writes to the "elect lady" urging her to walk in truth and love, warns against deceivers who deny Christ's coming in the flesh, and instructs believers not to receive false teachers into their homes lest they share in their wicked works.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: "The elder" (v.1) — the Apostle John. Written approximately AD 85-95.
The shortest book in the NT by word count (in Greek).

Recipient: "The elect lady and her children" (v.1) — either a specific woman
and her family, or (more likely) a local church personified as a lady with
her members as children. "Her elect sister" (v.13) would be another church.

Purpose: To encourage walking in truth and love (vv.4-6) while warning against
receiving false teachers who deny Christ's incarnation (vv.7-11). A brief,
urgent letter balancing truth and love — you need both.

Theme: Truth and love together. Walk in truth (v.4), walk in love (v.6), but
don't extend hospitality to those who deny Christ (vv.10-11).

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Walking in truth" (v.4). Is your life consistent with what you believe?
 Are you walking in truth or just talking about it?

2. "This is love, that we walk after his commandments" (v.6). Do you define
 love by feelings or by obedience? Biblical love is expressed through
 keeping God's commands.

3. "Receive him not into your house" (v.10). Are you discerning about who you
 support, platform, and endorse? Supporting false teachers makes you a
 partner in their work.

4. How do you balance truth and love? Some emphasize truth without love
 (harsh). Others emphasize love without truth (permissive). John holds both
 together. Where do you lean?
