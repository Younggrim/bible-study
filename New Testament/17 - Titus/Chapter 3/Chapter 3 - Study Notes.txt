Titus - Chapter 3
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Paul reminds believers to be subject to authorities, gentle toward all, and to remember their own former foolishness — saved "not by works of righteousness which we have done, but according to his mercy" through the washing of regeneration and renewal of the Holy Spirit.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Paul the Apostle (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 3 concludes Titus with instructions about civic
responsibility (vv.1-2), a magnificent summary of salvation (vv.3-7), the
importance of good works (v.8), warnings about divisive people (vv.9-11), and
personal instructions (vv.12-15). Verses 3-7 are considered one of the
clearest and most beautiful summaries of the gospel in all of Paul's writings.

Civic Responsibility (vv.1-2): Be subject to authorities, obey, be ready for
good works, speak evil of no one, be gentle and meek toward all.

The Gospel Summary (vv.3-7): "We ourselves also were" — foolish, disobedient,
deceived, enslaved (v.3). "But" — God's kindness and love appeared (v.4).
"Not by works" — but by mercy, through regeneration and the Spirit's renewal
(v.5). The Spirit poured out abundantly through Christ (v.6). Justified by
grace, made heirs of eternal life (v.7).

Good Works and Divisive People (vv.8-11): Believers should "maintain good
works" (v.8). Avoid foolish controversies (v.9). Warn a divisive person twice;
then reject him (vv.10-11).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.3 — "For we ourselves were once foolish, disobedient, led astray, slaves
 to various passions and pleasures." (ESV) — What we were before grace.

v.4-5 — "But when the goodness and loving kindness of God our Savior
 appeared, he saved us, not because of works done by us in
 righteousness, but according to his own mercy, by the washing of
 regeneration and renewal of the Holy Spirit." (ESV) — Salvation by
 mercy, not works.

v.5 — "The washing of regeneration and renewal of the Holy Spirit." (ESV) —
 The new birth and Spirit's renewal.

v.7 — "So that being justified by his grace we might become heirs according
 to the hope of eternal life." (ESV) — Justified by grace, heirs of
 life.

v.8 — "So that those who have believed in God may be careful to devote
 themselves to good works." (ESV) — Faith produces intentional good
 works.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "principalities and powers" / ESV: "rulers and authorities" — Here referring to civil
 authorities (not spiritual beings as in Eph 6:12). KJV: "obey magistrates"
 / ESV: "to be obedient" — Greek "peitharchein" — to obey those in authority.

v.4 — KJV: "kindness and love" / ESV: "kindness" — Greek "chrēstotēs kai philanthrōpia" —
 kindness and love-for-humanity. "Philanthropy" comes from this word.
 God's love is specifically directed toward humanity.

v.5 — KJV: "washing of regeneration" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "loutrou palingenesias" — the
 bath/washing of new birth. "Regeneration" (palingenesia) = being born
 again. Used only here and Matt 19:28 in the NT. The "washing" likely
 refers to spiritual cleansing (not baptism as the means of salvation,
 given the context of "not by works").

v.5 — KJV: "renewing of the Holy Ghost" / ESV: "renewal of the Holy Spirit" — Greek "anakainōseōs pneumatos
 hagiou" — the Spirit's work of making new. Regeneration (new birth)
 and renewal (ongoing transformation) — both the Spirit's work.

v.8 — KJV: "faithful saying" / ESV: "saying" — The fifth and final "faithful saying" in the
 Pastoral Epistles. "Maintain good works" — Greek "proistasthai kalōn
 ergōn" — to be devoted to, to engage in, to give attention to good
 works.

v.10 — KJV: "heretick" / ESV: "a person who stirs up division" — Greek "hairetikon" — a factious person, one who
 causes division. Not primarily about wrong doctrine but about divisive
 behavior. "After the first and second admonition reject" — two
 warnings, then separation.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.3  — NET: "For we too were once foolish, disobedient,
       misled, enslaved to various passions and desires" — "we too"
       (Emen... kai hEmeis = we ourselves also). WEB: "For we were
       also once foolish." The "we" prevents self-righteousness —
       Paul includes himself in the pre-conversion description.

v.4  — NET: "But when the kindness of God our Savior and his love
       for mankind appeared" — "love for mankind" (philanthrOpia =
       philanthropy). WEB: "the kindness of God our Savior and his
       love toward mankind appeared." KJV: "the kindness and love
       of God our Saviour toward man appeared." God is a
       philanthropist — He loves humanity.

v.5  — NET: "he saved us not by works of righteousness that we
       have done but on the basis of his mercy, through the washing
       of the new birth and the renewing of the Holy Spirit" —
       "washing of the new birth" (loutrou palingenesias = bath of
       regeneration). WEB: "the washing of regeneration." KJV:
       "the washing of regeneration." Baptismal language connected
       to spiritual rebirth — the relationship is debated.

v.8  — NET: "This saying is trustworthy" — "trustworthy" (pistos
       ho logos = faithful is the word — a Pastoral Epistles
       formula). WEB: "This saying is faithful." KJV: "This is a
       faithful saying." Paul uses this formula five times in the
       Pastorals to mark key doctrinal statements.

v.10 — NET: "Reject a divisive person after one or two warnings"
       — "divisive person" (hairetikon anthrOpon = factious/
       heretical person). WEB: "Avoid a man who is causing
       divisions." KJV: "A man that is an heretick after the first
       and second admonition reject." Two chances, then separation.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Washing of regeneration — The spiritual cleansing that accompanies new birth.
 God's act of making a person spiritually alive and clean.

Renewing of the Holy Ghost — The Spirit's ongoing work of transformation.
 Making the believer new from the inside out.

Justified by grace — Declared righteous before God as a free gift. Not
 earned but given through Christ's work.

Heirs of eternal life — Full inheritance rights to eternal life. Believers
 are not just saved but made heirs of everything God promises.

Maintain good works — Intentional, devoted pursuit of doing good. Not
 accidental but deliberate engagement in righteous action.

Heretic/Divisive person — One who causes factions and divisions in the
 church. Warned twice, then rejected.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "We ourselves also were sometimes foolish, disobedient, deceived" (v.3).
 Remember what you were before grace. Does remembering your past keep you
 humble and compassionate toward others who are still there?

2. "Not by works of righteousness which we have done, but according to his
 mercy" (v.5). Are you resting in God's mercy or still trying to earn His
 favor? Salvation is His work, not yours.

3. "Careful to maintain good works" (v.8). Saved by grace, not works — but
 saved FOR works. Are you intentionally pursuing good works? Not to earn
 salvation but because you have it?

4. "Avoid foolish questions and genealogies and contentions" (v.9). What
 controversies and arguments consume your time and energy without producing
 anything profitable? What would happen if you simply avoided them?

5. How do you handle divisive people (vv.10-11)? Do you warn them? Do you
 eventually separate if they refuse to change? Or do you let them continue
 causing damage?
