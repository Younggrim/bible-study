Hebrews - Chapter 10
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Animal sacrifices could never take away sins, but Christ offered one sacrifice for sins forever — therefore we have confidence to enter the holiest by His blood, and the author warns severely against willful sin after receiving knowledge of truth.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Unknown (see Chapter 1 notes for full discussion).

Historical Context: Chapter 10 concludes the doctrinal argument of Hebrews
(the superiority of Christ's sacrifice) and transitions to practical
application. The first half (vv.1-18) demonstrates the finality of Christ's
one sacrifice vs. the repetition of OT sacrifices. The second half (vv.19-39)
draws out three practical responses: draw near (v.22), hold fast (v.23),
consider one another (v.24). It also contains the fourth warning passage
(vv.26-31) and encouragement to persevere (vv.32-39).

The Finality of Christ's Sacrifice (vv.1-18): The Law's sacrifices, repeated
endlessly, could never perfect worshippers (vv.1-4). Christ came to do God's
will — offering His body once for all (vv.5-10). OT priests stood daily
offering ineffective sacrifices; Christ sat down after one effective sacrifice
(vv.11-12). By one offering He perfected forever those being sanctified (v.14).
The new covenant promise: sins remembered no more (vv.16-17). Where sins are
forgiven, no more offering is needed (v.18).

Three Exhortations (vv.19-25): (1) Draw near to God with confidence (v.22).
(2) Hold fast your confession without wavering (v.23). (3) Consider how to
stir one another to love and good works — don't forsake assembling together
(vv.24-25).

Warning #4 (vv.26-31): Willful sin after receiving truth leaves no sacrifice
for sins — only fearful judgment (vv.26-27). Treading the Son of God underfoot,
counting His blood unholy, and insulting the Spirit of grace deserve severe
punishment (v.29). "It is a fearful thing to fall into the hands of the living
God" (v.31).

Encouragement to Persevere (vv.32-39): Remember your former endurance (vv.32-
34). Don't throw away your confidence (v.35). You need patience (v.36). "The
just shall live by faith" (v.38). "We are not of them who draw back" (v.39).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.10 — "We have been sanctified through the offering of the body of Jesus
 Christ once for all." (ESV) — One offering, permanent sanctification.

v.12 — "But when Christ had offered for all time a single sacrifice for
 sins, he sat down at the right hand of God." (ESV) — Sat down = work
 finished.

v.14 — "For by a single offering he has perfected for all time those who are
 being sanctified." (ESV) — One offering, forever perfected.

v.19-22 — "Therefore, brothers, since we have confidence to enter the holy
 places by the blood of Jesus, by the new and living way... let us
 draw near." (ESV) — Bold access through Christ's blood.

v.23 — "Let us hold fast the confession of our hope without wavering." (ESV)
 — Unwavering confession.

v.24-25 — "And let us consider how to stir up one another to love and good
 works, not neglecting to meet together." (ESV) — Mutual encouragement
 and faithful gathering.

v.31 — "It is a fearful thing to fall into the hands of the living God."
 (ESV) — The severity of rejecting Christ.

v.35 — "Therefore do not throw away your confidence, which has a great
 reward." (ESV) — Don't abandon your confidence.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "shadow" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "skian" — a shadow, not the reality. The Law
 was a shadow cast by the approaching reality (Christ).

v.10 — KJV: "once for all" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "ephapax" — one time with permanent,
 unrepeatable results. The defining word of Christ's sacrifice.

v.19 — KJV: "boldness" / ESV: "confidence" — Greek "parrēsian" — confidence, freedom of speech,
 fearless access. We enter God's presence without fear.

v.20 — KJV: "new and living way" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "prosphaton kai zōsan" — freshly
 slain and living. A paradox: the way is through death (Christ's) but
 it is alive. "Through the veil, that is to say, his flesh" — Christ's
 body is the veil that was torn to give us access.

v.22 — KJV: "full assurance of faith" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "plērophoria pisteōs" —
 complete confidence, unwavering certainty.

v.25 — KJV: "assembling of ourselves together" / ESV: "neglecting to meet together" — Greek "episunagōgēn" —
 gathering together. The importance of corporate worship and mutual
 encouragement.

v.26 — KJV: "sin wilfully" / ESV: "sinning" — Greek "hekousiōs hamartanontōn" — sinning
 deliberately, voluntarily. Not sins of weakness but deliberate,
 sustained rejection of known truth.

v.29 — KJV: "trodden under foot" / ESV: "underfoot" — Greek "katapatēsas" — trampled, treated
 with contempt. Three offenses: trampling the Son, profaning His blood,
 insulting the Spirit.

v.38 — KJV: "the just shall live by faith" / ESV: "live by faith" — Quoted from Habakkuk 2:4. Also
 in Romans 1:17 and Galatians 3:11. The motto of the Reformation.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.1  — NET: "the law is only a shadow of the good things to
       come and not the reality itself" — "shadow" (skian) vs.
       "reality" (eikona = image/true form). WEB: "a shadow of the
       good things to come, not the very image." The law was a
       silhouette — Christ is the substance casting the shadow.

v.10 — NET: "we have been made holy through the offering of the
       body of Jesus Christ once for all" — "made holy"
       (hEgiasmenoi esmen = we have been sanctified — perfect
       tense). WEB: "we have been sanctified." KJV: "we are
       sanctified." Perfect tense: completed act with ongoing
       result. Positional holiness is settled.

v.14 — NET: "by one offering he has perfected for all time those
       who are made holy" — "perfected for all time" (teteleiOken
       eis to diEnekes). WEB: "he has perfected forever those who
       are being sanctified." "Perfected" (past/complete) + "being
       sanctified" (present/ongoing) — positional and progressive.

v.19 — NET: "we have confidence to enter the most holy place by
       the blood of Jesus" — "confidence" (parrEsian = boldness/
       freedom of speech/access). WEB: "boldness to enter into the
       holy place." KJV: "boldness to enter into the holiest." Not
       timid approach but confident access — the veil is torn.

v.31 — NET: "It is a terrifying thing to fall into the hands of
       the living God" — "terrifying" (phoberon = fearful/dreadful).
       WEB: "It is a fearful thing to fall into the hands of the
       living God." KJV: "It is a fearful thing." After the warning
       of vv.26-30, this conclusion — God as judge is terrifying.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Once for all — Christ's single, unrepeatable sacrifice with permanent
 results. Never needs repetition.

Sat down — Christ's posture of completion. OT priests stood (work
 never done); Christ sat (work finished).

Perfected forever — Believers made permanently complete before God through
 Christ's one offering.

New and living way — Access to God through Christ's torn body. New (freshly
 opened), living (through a living person), through the veil.

Provoke unto love — Stirring one another toward love and good works. The
 purpose of Christian community.

Sin wilfully — Deliberate, sustained rejection of known truth. Not sins
 of weakness but apostasy.

The just shall live by faith — The righteous person's life principle: faith,
 not sight; trust, not works.

Cast not away your confidence — Don't abandon the boldness that comes from
 knowing Christ. Persevere.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "By one offering he hath perfected for ever" (v.14). You are PERFECTED
 FOREVER by Christ's one sacrifice. Do you live in that reality, or do you
 keep trying to add to what Christ has done?

2. "Let us draw near" (v.22). Are you drawing near to God with confidence?
 Or keeping your distance out of guilt, fear, or unworthiness? The way is
 open — come boldly.

3. "Not forsaking the assembling of ourselves together" (v.25). Are you
 faithful in gathering with other believers? Do you see church attendance
 as optional or essential? You need others and they need you.

4. "Cast not away therefore your confidence" (v.35). What threatens your
 spiritual confidence? Suffering? Doubt? Weariness? Don't throw it away —
 it has "great recompence of reward."

5. "The just shall live by faith" (v.38). Are you living by faith or by
 sight? By trust or by control? Faith is the operating principle of the
 righteous life.
