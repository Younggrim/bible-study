Hebrews - Chapter 9
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
The old tabernacle's rituals were shadows pointing to Christ, who entered the heavenly Holy of Holies once for all with His own blood — not the blood of goats and calves — obtaining eternal redemption and cleansing the conscience from dead works.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Unknown (see Chapter 1 notes for full discussion).

Historical Context: Chapter 9 compares the old covenant's worship system with
Christ's superior sacrifice. The author describes the earthly tabernacle and
its limitations (vv.1-10), then shows how Christ entered the heavenly
sanctuary with His own blood, obtaining eternal redemption (vv.11-28). This
chapter contains some of the most important atonement theology in the NT.

The Earthly Tabernacle (vv.1-10): Description of the Holy Place and Most Holy
Place with their furnishings (vv.1-5). The priests served daily in the first
room; the high priest entered the second room only once a year with blood
(vv.6-7). The Holy Spirit was signifying that access to God was "not yet made
manifest" under the old system (v.8). The old sacrifices could not perfect the
conscience (v.9) — they were temporary, "imposed until the time of
reformation" (v.10).

Christ's Superior Sacrifice (vv.11-22): Christ entered the greater, heavenly
tabernacle (v.11). Not with animal blood but His own blood (v.12). He obtained
"eternal redemption" — once for all (v.12). If animal blood purified the flesh,
"how much more shall the blood of Christ... purge your conscience from dead
works" (vv.13-14). He is mediator of the new testament/covenant (v.15). A
testament requires the death of the testator (vv.16-17). "Without shedding of
blood is no remission" (v.22).

Christ's Once-for-All Appearance (vv.23-28): Christ entered heaven itself —
not a man-made copy (v.24). He doesn't offer Himself repeatedly (v.25). He
appeared "once in the end of the world... to put away sin by the sacrifice of
himself" (v.26). As men die once then face judgment, Christ was offered once
to bear sins — and will appear a second time for salvation (vv.27-28).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.12 — "He entered once for all into the holy places, not by means of the
 blood of goats and calves but by means of his own blood, thus
 securing an eternal redemption." (ESV) — Christ's blood, eternal
 redemption, once.

v.14 — "How much more will the blood of Christ, who through the eternal
 Spirit offered himself without blemish to God, purify our conscience
 from dead works to serve the living God." (ESV) — Conscience cleansed
 for service.

v.22 — "Without the shedding of blood there is no forgiveness of sins."
 (ESV) — The necessity of blood for forgiveness.

v.24 — "For Christ has entered... into heaven itself, now to appear in the
 presence of God on our behalf." (ESV) — Christ in heaven representing
 us.

v.26 — "But as it is, he has appeared once for all at the end of the ages to
 put away sin by the sacrifice of himself." (ESV) — One appearance,
 sin put away.

v.27-28 — "And just as it is appointed for man to die once, and after that
 comes judgment, so Christ, having been offered once to bear the sins
 of many, will appear a second time, not to deal with sin but to save
 those who are eagerly waiting for him." (ESV) — Death, judgment, and
 Christ's return.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.8 — KJV: "the way into the holiest of all was not yet made manifest" / ESV: "way into the holy places is not yet" —
 Under the old system, access to God's presence was restricted. The
 veil said "keep out." Christ's death tore the veil and opened access.

v.10 — KJV: "time of reformation" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "kairou diorthōseōs" — the time
 of setting things right, the time of the new order. Christ's coming
 was the "reformation" — the setting right of the worship system.

v.12 — KJV: "eternal redemption" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "aiōnian lutrōsin" — redemption
 that lasts forever. Not temporary (like animal sacrifices that needed
 repeating) but permanent, once-for-all.

v.14 — KJV: "through the eternal Spirit" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Christ offered Himself through
 the Holy Spirit. The Trinity involved in atonement: the Son offers
 Himself through the Spirit to the Father.

v.14 — KJV: "dead works" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Works done apart from faith, works that produce
 spiritual death, works done to earn salvation. The conscience is
 cleansed FROM these to serve the living God.

v.15 — KJV: "testament" / ESV: "covenant" — Greek "diathēkē" — can mean both "covenant" and
 "testament/will." Paul plays on both meanings: it is a covenant that
 requires the death of the one who made it (like a will that takes
 effect at death).

v.22 — KJV: "remission" / ESV: "forgiveness" — Greek "aphesis" — release, forgiveness, sending
 away. Sin is "sent away" through blood sacrifice.

v.26 — KJV: "put away sin" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "athetēsin tēs hamartias" — the
 annulment, cancellation, setting aside of sin. Christ didn't just
 cover sin — He put it away, cancelled it, annulled it.

v.28 — KJV: "without sin" / ESV: "sins" — Greek "chōris hamartias" — apart from sin. His
 second coming is not to deal with sin (that's done) but to bring
 salvation to completion.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.5  — NET: "the cherubim of glory overshadowing the mercy
       seat" — "mercy seat" (hilastErion = place of propitiation/
       atonement cover). WEB: "the cherubim of glory overshadowing
       the mercy seat." KJV: "the mercyseat." The lid of the Ark
       where blood was sprinkled — the meeting point of God's
       holiness and mercy.

v.12 — NET: "he entered once for all into the most holy place not
       by the blood of goats and calves but by his own blood" —
       "once for all" (ephapax). WEB: "once for all." KJV: "once."
       The decisive, unrepeatable entry — no annual repetition
       like the earthly high priest.

v.14 — NET: "how much more will the blood of Christ, who through
       the eternal Spirit offered himself without blemish to God,
       purify our consciences" — "eternal Spirit" (pneumatos
       aiOniou). WEB: "through the eternal Spirit." Christ's
       sacrifice offered through the Spirit — Trinitarian atonement.

v.22 — NET: "without the shedding of blood there is no
       forgiveness" — "shedding of blood" (haimatekchysias =
       blood-pouring). WEB: "apart from shedding of blood there is
       no remission." KJV: "without shedding of blood is no
       remission." A foundational OT principle fulfilled in Christ.

v.27 — NET: "people are appointed to die once, and then to face
       judgment" — "appointed" (apokeitai = laid up/reserved/stored
       away). WEB: "it is appointed for men to die once." KJV: "it
       is appointed unto men once to die." The universality and
       finality of death — then judgment. No second chances.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Eternal redemption — Release from sin's bondage that lasts forever. Purchased
 once by Christ's blood, never needing repetition.

Purge the conscience — Cleanse the inner sense of guilt. Not just external
 ritual purity but internal freedom from condemnation.

Dead works — Works done apart from faith, or works done to earn
 salvation. The conscience must be cleansed from reliance on
 these.

Without shedding of blood — God's principle: forgiveness requires death.
 Either the sinner or a substitute must die.

Mediator of the new testament — Christ as the go-between who establishes
 the new covenant through His death.

Once offered — Christ's single, unrepeatable sacrifice. One death, one
 offering, permanent results.

Second appearing — Christ's return — not to deal with sin (finished) but to
 bring final salvation to those who wait for Him.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Purge your conscience from dead works to serve the living God" (v.14).
 Is your conscience clean? Are you free from guilt — both for past sins
 AND for trying to earn God's favor through "dead works"? A clean
 conscience enables joyful service.

2. "Without shedding of blood is no remission" (v.22). The cross was
 necessary — there was no other way. Do you appreciate the cost of your
 forgiveness? Does the blood of Christ move you to gratitude?

3. "He appeared to put away sin" (v.26). Christ didn't just cover your sin —
 He put it away, cancelled it, annulled it. Do you live like your sin is
 gone, or do you keep retrieving what God has put away?

4. "Appointed unto men once to die, but after this the judgment" (v.27). Life
 is short and judgment is certain. Are you ready? Is there anything you
 need to settle before that appointment?

5. "Unto them that look for him shall he appear the second time" (v.28). Are
 you looking for Christ's return? Living in anticipation? His second coming
 is "without sin" — not to judge you but to save you completely.
