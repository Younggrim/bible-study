Hebrews - Chapter 1
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
God, who spoke through prophets in many ways, has now spoken finally through His Son — who is the radiance of God's glory, the exact imprint of His nature, superior to all angels, and the heir of all things.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Unknown. Hebrews is the only NT book without a named author. Suggested
authors include Paul, Barnabas, Apollos, Luke, Priscilla, and others. Origen
(3rd century) famously said, "Who wrote Hebrews, God alone knows." The style
is different from Paul's letters, but the theology is consistent with Pauline
thought. Written before AD 70 (the temple is still standing, cf. 8:4, 10:11).

Recipient: Jewish Christians (hence "Hebrews") who were tempted to return to
Judaism under persecution. They were considering abandoning Christ for the
familiar comfort of the old covenant system — temple, priesthood, sacrifices.

Purpose: To demonstrate that Christ is BETTER than everything in the old
covenant — better than angels (chs.1-2), Moses (ch.3), Joshua (ch.4), Aaron
(chs.5-7), the old covenant (chs.8-10). Therefore: don't go back. Hold fast.

Theme: The supremacy and sufficiency of Christ. "Better" appears 13 times.
Christ is the final, complete, superior revelation of God.

Chapter 1: Christ is superior to angels — proven by seven OT quotations.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1-2 — "Long ago, at many times and in many ways, God spoke to our fathers
 by the prophets, but in these last days he has spoken to us by his
 Son." (ESV) — Progressive revelation culminating in Christ.

v.3 — "He is the radiance of the glory of God and the exact imprint of his
 nature, and he upholds the universe by the word of his power. After
 making purification for sins, he sat down at the right hand of the
 Majesty on high." (ESV) — Seven descriptions of Christ in one verse.

v.8 — "But of the Son he says, 'Your throne, O God, is forever and ever.'"
 (ESV) — The Father calls the Son "God."

v.10-12 — "You, Lord, laid the foundation of the earth in the beginning...
 they will perish, but you remain." (ESV) — Christ as eternal
 Creator.

v.14 — "Are they not all ministering spirits sent out to serve for the sake
 of those who are to inherit salvation?" (ESV) — Angels serve believers.

Charles Spurgeon:
 Sermon: "The Express Image" on v.3 (MTP Vol. 34, No. 2044)

 imprint. As a seal leaves its exact impression in wax, so Christ is the

 His love IS God's love. He is not a copy — He is the original made visible."
 Sermon: "The Sat-Down Saviour" on v.3 (MTP Vol. 17, No. 1023)

 is accomplished. He sat down — not from exhaustion but from completion."

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "at sundry times and in divers manners" / ESV: "long ago, at many times and in many ways" — Greek "polumerōs kai
 polutropōs" — in many portions and in many ways. God's OT revelation
 was fragmentary and varied. Christ is the complete, final word.

v.2 — KJV: "by his Son" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "en huiō" — in Son (no article). Not "a
 son" or "the Son" but the quality of Sonship. God spoke in the
 character of Son — His final, definitive communication.

v.3 — Seven descriptions of Christ: (1) brightness of His glory (radiance),
 (2) express image of His person (exact representation), (3) upholding
 all things (sustaining the universe), (4) by the word of His power
 (by His powerful word), (5) purged our sins (accomplished atonement),
 (6) sat down (work completed), (7) at the right hand (position of
 honor and authority).

v.3 — KJV: "brightness" / ESV: "radiance" — Greek "apaugasma" — radiance, effulgence. Light
 streaming from a source. Christ is the radiance of God's glory.
 KJV: "express image" / ESV: "exact imprint" — Greek "charaktēr" — exact imprint, precise
 representation. Root of English "character."

v.4 — KJV: "better than the angels" / ESV: "superior to angels" — Greek "kreittōn" — superior, more
 excellent. The key word of Hebrews (13 times). Christ is better.

v.8 — KJV: "Thy throne, O God" / ESV: "Your throne, O God" — The Father addresses the Son as "God"
 (theos). One of the clearest affirmations of Christ's deity in
 Scripture.

v.13 — KJV: "Sit on my right hand" / ESV: "Sit at my right hand" — Psalm 110:1, the most quoted OT verse
 in the NT. The right hand = the position of supreme honor and
 authority. No angel was ever invited to sit there.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.1  — NET: "God spoke to our ancestors through the prophets
       at many times and in various ways" — "at many times and in
       various ways" (polymerOs kai polytropOs). WEB: "at many
       times and in many ways." KJV: "at sundry times and in divers
       manners." The revelation was fragmentary; now in the Son it
       is complete and final.

v.2  — NET: "whom he appointed heir of all things, and through
       whom he created the world" — "the world" (tous aiOnas = the
       ages/eons — time and space). WEB: "through whom also he made
       the worlds." KJV: "by whom also he made the worlds." Christ
       made not just matter but time itself.

v.3  — NET: "He is the radiance of his glory and the
       representation of his essence" — "radiance" (apaugasma =
       effulgence/shining forth). "Representation" (charaktEr =
       exact imprint/stamp). WEB: "the very image of his substance."
       KJV: "the express image of his person." Two metaphors:
       light radiating and a die striking a coin.

v.3  — NET: "sustaining all things by his powerful word" —
       "sustaining" (pherOn = carrying/bearing/maintaining). WEB:
       "upholding all things by the word of his power." KJV:
       "upholding all things." Not static support but active,
       purposeful carrying toward a destination.

v.14 — NET: "Are they not all ministering spirits, sent to serve
       those who will inherit salvation?" — "ministering spirits"
       (leitourgika pneumata = liturgical/servant spirits). WEB:
       "serving spirits." Angels exist to serve believers — not
       the other way around.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Sundry times and divers manners — God's varied, fragmentary OT revelation
 through different prophets, methods, and occasions.

Brightness of His glory — Christ as the radiance streaming from God's glory.
 Not reflected light but emanating light.

Express image — The exact representation of God's being. Christ is the
 precise imprint of God's nature.

Upholding all things — Christ sustaining the entire universe by His powerful
 word. Continuous, active maintenance of all reality.

Sat down — Christ's completed work. Unlike OT priests who never sat
 (work never done), Christ sat because atonement is finished.

Better than angels — Christ's superiority over all angelic beings. He is
 worshipped by them, not one of them.

Ministering spirits — Angels as servants sent to serve believers. They serve
 us; we worship Christ.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Hath in these last days spoken unto us by his Son" (v.2). God's final
 word is Jesus. Are you listening to Him above all other voices? Do you
 treat Christ as God's definitive revelation?

2. Seven descriptions of Christ (v.3). Which one strikes you most today?
 The radiance of glory? The sustainer of all things? The one who purged
 your sins? The one who sat down in victory?

3. "Sat down on the right hand" (v.3). Christ's work is DONE. Are you
 resting in His finished work, or still trying to add to it? The sacrifice
 is complete.

4. "Thy throne, O God, is for ever and ever" (v.8). The Father calls the Son
 "God." How does Christ's full deity affect your worship, your trust, your
 obedience?

5. Angels are "ministering spirits" sent to serve you (v.14). You are served
 by angels — not because of your worth but because of your inheritance.
 How does this encourage you?
