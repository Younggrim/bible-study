Hebrews - Chapter 5
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Christ is appointed as high priest in the order of Melchizedek (not Aaron), learned obedience through suffering, and became the source of eternal salvation — but the readers are rebuked as still needing milk when they should be teachers.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Unknown (see Chapter 1 notes for full discussion).

Historical Context: Chapter 5 begins the extended discussion of Christ's
priesthood that runs through chapter 10. The author first describes the
qualifications of any high priest (vv.1-4), then shows how Christ meets and
exceeds them (vv.5-10). The chapter ends with a rebuke: the readers should be
mature enough for this teaching but have become spiritually dull (vv.11-14).
The Melchizedek priesthood is introduced here but developed fully in chapter 7.

Qualifications of a High Priest (vv.1-4): Taken from among men (v.1).
Appointed to represent men before God (v.1). Able to sympathize with the
weak because he shares their weakness (v.2). Must offer sacrifice for his own
sins as well as the people's (v.3). Called by God, not self-appointed (v.4).

Christ's Priesthood (vv.5-10): Christ didn't appoint Himself — God declared
Him Son (v.5) and priest "after the order of Melchisedec" (v.6). In His
earthly life, He prayed with "strong crying and tears" (v.7 — Gethsemane).
Though a Son, He "learned obedience by the things which he suffered" (v.8).
Made perfect, He became "the author of eternal salvation" (v.9).

Spiritual Immaturity Rebuked (vv.11-14): The readers are "dull of hearing"
(v.11). They should be teachers by now but need to relearn basics (v.12).
They need milk, not solid food (v.12). Mature believers have "senses exercised
to discern both good and evil" (v.14).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.7 — "In the days of his flesh, Jesus offered up prayers and supplications,
 with loud cries and tears, to him who was able to save him from death,
 and he was heard because of his reverence." (ESV) — Christ's
 agonizing prayer in Gethsemane.

v.8 — "Although he was a son, he learned obedience through what he
 suffered." (ESV) — Obedience learned through suffering.

v.9 — "And being made perfect, he became the source of eternal salvation to
 all who obey him." (ESV) — Christ as the source of eternal salvation.

v.12 — "For though by this time you ought to be teachers, you need someone
 to teach you again... the basic principles." (ESV) — Spiritual
 regression.

v.14 — "But solid food is for the mature, for those who have their powers of
 discernment trained by constant practice to distinguish good from
 evil." (ESV) — Maturity through practice.

Charles Spurgeon:
 Sermon: "Learned Obedience" on v.8 (MTP Vol. 15, No. 897)
 "Though He were a Son — not despite being a Son but as a Son. Sonship does
 not exempt from suffering; it gives suffering its meaning. He LEARNED

 costs. He experienced obedience in its most difficult form: obedience unto
 death. And by that learning, He became the author of salvation for all who

 Sermon: "Spiritual Meat" on vv.12-14 (MTP Vol. 22, No. 1288)

 instead of forward. Time has passed but growth has not occurred. The cure?
 Exercise. 'By reason of USE have their senses exercised.' Maturity comes
 through practice — using what you know, applying what you learn, exercising
 your spiritual faculties daily."

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.2 — KJV: "have compassion" / ESV: "deal gently" — Greek "metriopathein" — to deal gently, to
 moderate one's feelings. A human priest can sympathize because he
 shares human weakness. Christ sympathizes perfectly (4:15) without
 sharing sinful weakness.

v.6 — KJV: "order of Melchisedec" / ESV: "order of Melchizedek" — Greek "taxin Melchisedek" — the order,
 rank, or type of Melchizedek. Not the Aaronic/Levitical order but a
 different, superior priestly order. Developed in chapter 7.

v.7 — KJV: "strong crying and tears" / ESV: "loud cries and tears" — Greek "krauge ischura kai dakrua" —
 loud cries and tears. Refers to Gethsemane (Matt 26:36-44) and
 possibly the cross. "Was heard in that he feared" — Greek "eisakoustheis
 apo tēs eulabeias" — heard because of His reverent submission. God
 didn't save Him FROM death but THROUGH death (resurrection).
       NET: "During his earthly life Christ offered both
       requests and supplications, with loud cries and tears" —
       "loud cries and tears" (meta kraugEs ischyras kai dakryOn).
       WEB: "with strong crying and tears." KJV: "with strong
       crying and tears." Gethsemane — Jesus' prayers were not
       calm and composed but agonized.

v.8 — KJV: "learned obedience" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "emathen... tēn hupakoēn" — He
 learned obedience experientially. Not that He was disobedient and
 learned to obey, but that He experienced what obedience costs in a
 fallen world — through suffering.
       NET: "Although he was a son, he learned obedience through
       the things he suffered" — "learned" (emathen = learned by
       experience). WEB: "though he was a Son, yet learned
       obedience by the things which he suffered." Even the Son
       needed experiential learning — obedience was not automatic
       for His human nature.

v.9 — KJV: "made perfect" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "teleiōtheis" — completed, brought to
 the goal. Not moral perfection (He was always sinless) but vocational
 completion — fully qualified as Savior through the experience of
 suffering and death.
       NET: "he became the source of eternal salvation to all who
       obey him" — "source" (aitios = cause/author). WEB: "author
       of eternal salvation." KJV: "the author of eternal
       salvation." Christ didn't just procure salvation — He IS
       its origin and cause.

v.11 — KJV: "dull of hearing" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "nōthroi tais akoais" — sluggish in
 hearing, slow to understand. Not inability but unwillingness — they've
 become lazy listeners.

v.12 — NET: "you have come to need milk, not solid food" — "milk"
       (galaktos) vs. "solid food" (stereas trophEs). WEB: "you
       have become such as have need of milk." KJV: "ye have need
       of milk." A rebuke: by now they should be teachers, but
       they've regressed to infancy.

v.14 — KJV: "senses exercised" / ESV: "powers of discernment trained by constant practice" — Greek "aisthētēria gegumnasmena" — faculties
 trained through practice. "Gymnasium-trained" perception. Discernment
 comes through repeated use, not just knowledge.
       NET: "solid food is for the mature, whose perceptions are
       trained by practice to discern both good and evil" — "trained
       by practice" (gegymnasmenOn = exercised/gymnasiumed). WEB:
       "those who by reason of use have their senses exercised."
       Spiritual maturity comes through repeated exercise of
       discernment — not theory but practice.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
High priest — One who represents people before God, offering sacrifices
 for sins. Christ is the ultimate, final High Priest.

Order of Melchizedek — A priestly order different from and superior to
 Aaron's. Eternal, without genealogical succession.

Strong crying and tears — Christ's agonizing prayer in Gethsemane. Full
 humanity experiencing the weight of the cross.

Learned obedience — Experiential knowledge of what obedience costs. Not
 learning TO obey but learning what obedience MEANS through
 suffering.

Author of eternal salvation — Christ as the source, cause, and originator
 of salvation that lasts forever.

Milk vs. meat — Basic doctrine vs. advanced teaching. Spiritual infants
 need basics; mature believers need depth.

Senses exercised — Spiritual discernment developed through practice. Like
 physical training — repeated use builds capacity.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Learned obedience by the things which he suffered" (v.8). What is
 suffering teaching you about obedience? Can you see God using difficulty
 to deepen your submission to Him?

2. "Ye ought to be teachers" (v.12). Given how long you've been a believer,
 where should you be spiritually? Are you growing or regressing? Are you
 teaching others or still needing to be taught basics?

3. "Have need of milk, and not of strong meat" (v.12). Are you still on
 spiritual milk — basic truths — or are you ready for solid food? What
 would it take to move from milk to meat?

4. "By reason of use have their senses exercised" (v.14). Maturity comes
 through PRACTICE — using what you know. Are you applying Scripture daily?
 Are you exercising discernment? Knowledge without application keeps you
 immature.

5. "Strong crying and tears" (v.7). Jesus prayed with intense emotion and
 desperation. How does your prayer life compare? Do you bring your deepest
 struggles to God with that kind of honesty and urgency?
