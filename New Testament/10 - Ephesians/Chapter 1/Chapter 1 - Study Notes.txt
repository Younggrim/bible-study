Ephesians - Chapter 1
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Paul praises God for every spiritual blessing in Christ — election, predestination, redemption, and the sealing of the Holy Spirit as a guarantee of our inheritance — then prays that believers would know the surpassing greatness of God's power toward them.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Paul the Apostle (v.1). Written from prison in Rome (cf. 3:1, 4:1,
6:20), approximately AD 60-62. Ephesians is one of Paul's four "Prison
Epistles" (along with Philippians, Colossians, and Philemon).

Recipient: The church at Ephesus — a major city in Asia Minor (modern Turkey),
home to the temple of Artemis (one of the Seven Wonders). Paul spent over two
years there (Acts 19:10), and the church became a center for the gospel in
Asia. Some early manuscripts omit "at Ephesus" (v.1), suggesting this may
have been a circular letter to multiple churches.

Purpose: Unlike most of Paul's letters, Ephesians does not address a specific
problem. It is a theological masterpiece — a sweeping vision of God's eternal
purpose in Christ and the church. Chapters 1-3 are doctrinal (what God has
done); chapters 4-6 are practical (how we should live).

Chapter 1 Overview: One of the richest chapters in Scripture. Verses 3-14 are
a single sentence in Greek — a breathless doxology covering the work of the
Father (vv.3-6), the Son (vv.7-12), and the Spirit (vv.13-14). Verses 15-23
are Paul's prayer for the Ephesians to understand what they have in Christ.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.3  — "Blessed be the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, who has
        blessed us in Christ with every spiritual blessing in the heavenly
        places." (ESV) — ALL spiritual blessings — already given, already ours.

v.4  — "He chose us in him before the foundation of the world, that we should
        be holy and blameless before him." (ESV) — Election: chosen before
        creation.

v.6  — "To the praise of his glorious grace, with which he has blessed us in
        the Beloved." (ESV) — Accepted in Christ — not by performance.

v.7  — "In him we have redemption through his blood, the forgiveness of our
        trespasses, according to the riches of his grace." (ESV) — Redemption
        and forgiveness through Christ's blood.

v.13 — "You were sealed with the promised Holy Spirit." (ESV) — The Spirit as
        God's seal of ownership on believers.

v.14 — "Who is the guarantee of our inheritance." (ESV) — The Spirit as down
        payment guaranteeing our full inheritance.

v.17 — "The Spirit of wisdom and of revelation in the knowledge of him." (ESV)
        — Paul's prayer: that they would truly KNOW God.

v.19-20 — "The immeasurable greatness of his power toward us who believe,
        according to the working of his great might that he worked in Christ
        when he raised him from the dead." (ESV) — Resurrection power
        available to believers.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.3  — KJV: "heavenly places" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "epouraniois" — "the heavenlies."
       Used 5 times in Ephesians (1:3, 1:20, 2:6, 3:10, 6:12). A uniquely
       Ephesian concept — the spiritual realm where believers are already
       seated with Christ.
       NET: "Blessed is the God and Father of our Lord Jesus
       Christ, who has blessed us with every spiritual blessing in
       the heavenly realms in Christ" — "heavenly realms" (tois
       epouraniois = the heavenlies). WEB: "in the heavenly places."
       KJV: "in heavenly places." A key Ephesians phrase (1:3, 20;
       2:6; 3:10; 6:12) — the spiritual dimension where believers
       are already seated.

v.4  — KJV: "chosen us in him before the foundation of the world" / ESV: "chose us in him before the foundation of the world" — Greek
       "exelexato" (aorist middle) — God chose for Himself. The timing:
       before creation. The sphere: "in Christ." The purpose: holiness.
       NET: "he chose us in Christ before the foundation of the
       world" — "before the foundation" (pro katabolEs kosmou).
       WEB: "before the foundation of the world." Election is
       pre-temporal — decided before creation, not in response to
       foreseen faith or merit.

v.6  — KJV: "accepted in the beloved" — Greek "echaritōsen" — graced,
       favored, made objects of grace. Same root as "grace" (charis). ESV:
       "blessed us in the Beloved." NET: "freely bestowed on us in the One
       he loves."

v.7  — NET: "In him we have redemption through his blood, the
       forgiveness of our trespasses" — "redemption" (apolytrOsin
       = ransom/release by payment). WEB: "redemption through his
       blood." KJV: "redemption through his blood." Slave-market
       language — purchased out of bondage at the cost of blood.

v.10 — KJV: "dispensation of the fulness of times" / ESV: "as a plan for the fullness of time" — Greek "oikonomia" —
       administration, stewardship, plan. God's plan for the culmination of
       history: unite all things in Christ.

v.13 — KJV: "sealed" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "esphragisthēte" — stamped with a seal of
       ownership. In the ancient world, a seal indicated: (1) ownership,
       (2) authenticity, (3) security. The Spirit is God's seal on us.
       NET: "you were sealed with the promised Holy Spirit" —
       "sealed" (esphragisthEte = stamped with a seal of ownership).
       WEB: "you were sealed with the Holy Spirit of promise." KJV:
       "ye were sealed with that holy Spirit of promise." A seal
       marks ownership, authenticity, and security.

v.14 — KJV: "earnest" / ESV: "is the guarantee" — Greek "arrabōn" — down payment, deposit, guarantee.
       A commercial term: the first installment that guarantees the full
       payment. The Spirit now is the guarantee of glory later.

v.19 — KJV uses four Greek words for power: "dunamis" (inherent power),
       "energeia" (working, active energy), "kratos" (manifested strength),
       "ischus" (might, force). Paul piles up power words to convey the
       immensity of God's power available to believers.
       NET: "what is the incomparably great power for us who
       believe" — "incomparably great" (hyperballon megethos =
       surpassing greatness). WEB: "the exceeding greatness of his
       power toward us who believe." The same power that raised
       Christ from the dead (v.20) works in believers.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Heavenly places — The spiritual realm where believers are already positioned
               in Christ. Not a future location but a present spiritual
               reality.

Election/Chosen — God's sovereign choice of believers before creation. A
               doctrine of comfort: your salvation originates in God's
               eternal purpose, not your fragile decision.

Predestination — God's predetermined plan for those He chose — specifically,
               adoption as sons (v.5) and conformity to Christ (Rom 8:29).

Redemption     — Greek "apolutrōsis" — release by payment of a ransom. The
               price: Christ's blood. The result: forgiveness of sins.

Sealed         — Marked with God's seal of ownership through the Holy Spirit.
               Indicates ownership, authenticity, and security.

Earnest/Deposit — The Holy Spirit as God's down payment guaranteeing our full
               inheritance. What we have now is a foretaste of what's coming.

Mystery        — Greek "mustērion" — not something mysterious but something
               previously hidden now revealed. God's plan to unite all things
               in Christ.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "All spiritual blessings" (v.3). You already have EVERY spiritual blessing
   in Christ. You're not working toward God's favor — you already have it.
   How does this change the way you approach today?

2. "Chosen before the foundation of the world" (v.4). God chose you before
   you existed. Your salvation doesn't depend on your performance — it
   originates in God's eternal purpose. Does this give you security or raise
   questions? Sit with both.

3. "Accepted in the beloved" (v.6). You are accepted — not because of what
   you've done but because of who you're in. Where do you still feel
   unaccepted? Can you receive this truth?

4. "Sealed with the Holy Spirit" (v.13). God has put His seal of ownership on
   you. You belong to Him. You are secure. How does this affect your sense of
   identity and security?

5. Paul prays for "the spirit of wisdom and revelation" (v.17). What would
   change if you truly knew the hope of God's calling, the riches of His
   inheritance, and the greatness of His power? Pray this prayer for yourself.
