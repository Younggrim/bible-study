Ephesians - Chapter 4
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Paul urges believers to walk worthy of their calling in unity, explains that Christ gave gifted leaders to equip the saints for ministry until all reach maturity, and calls them to put off the old self and put on the new self created in righteousness.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Paul the Apostle (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 4 marks the transition from doctrine (chs.1-3) to
practice (chs.4-6). Paul's "therefore" (v.1) connects everything that follows
to everything that preceded. Because of who you are in Christ (chs.1-3), here
is how you should live (chs.4-6). The chapter covers three themes: unity in
the body (vv.1-16), putting off the old self (vv.17-24), and practical
holiness (vv.25-32).

Walk Worthy — Unity (vv.1-16): Paul urges believers to "walk worthy" of their
calling with humility, gentleness, patience, and love (vv.1-3). He lists seven
"ones" that form the basis of Christian unity (vv.4-6). Christ gave gifted
leaders — apostles, prophets, evangelists, pastors, teachers — to equip the
saints for ministry and build up the body (vv.11-12). The goal: maturity,
measured by "the stature of the fulness of Christ" (v.13).

Put Off the Old, Put On the New (vv.17-24): Gentile believers must no longer
live like pagans — darkened in understanding, alienated from God (vv.17-19).
They must "put off" the old man and "put on" the new man, created in
righteousness and holiness (vv.22-24). Like changing clothes.

Practical Holiness (vv.25-32): Specific commands: speak truth, handle anger
properly, don't steal but work and give, speak words that build up, don't
grieve the Spirit, put away bitterness, be kind and forgiving (vv.25-32).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "I therefore, a prisoner for the Lord, urge you to walk in a manner
        worthy of the calling to which you have been called." (ESV) — The
        great "therefore" — live up to who you are in Christ.

v.3  — "Eager to maintain the unity of the Spirit in the bond of peace." (ESV)
        — Unity is given by the Spirit; we must maintain it.

v.4-6 — "There is one body and one Spirit... one Lord, one faith, one baptism,
        one God and Father of all." (ESV) — Seven "ones" of Christian unity.

v.11-12 — "He gave the apostles, the prophets, the evangelists, the shepherds
        and teachers, to equip the saints for the work of ministry." (ESV) —
        Gifted leaders equip saints for ministry.

v.15 — "Rather, speaking the truth in love, we are to grow up in every way
        into him." (ESV) — Truth and love together produce growth.

v.22-24 — "Put off your old self... and put on the new self, created after the
        likeness of God in true righteousness and holiness." (ESV) — The
        clothing metaphor of sanctification.

v.26 — "Be angry and do not sin; do not let the sun go down on your anger."
        (ESV) — Anger is permitted; sinful anger is not.

v.29 — "Let no corrupting talk come out of your mouths, but only such as is
        good for building up." (ESV) — Words that build, not destroy.

v.32 — "Be kind to one another, tenderhearted, forgiving one another, as God
        in Christ forgave you." (ESV) — Forgive as forgiven.

  Sermon: "Grieving the Holy Spirit" on v.30 (MTP Vol. 4, No. 182)
  "Grieve not the Holy Spirit. Not 'anger' — grieve. The word implies
  tenderness, love, sensitivity. A master is angered by a servant's
  disobedience. A father is grieved by a child's rebellion. The Spirit
  dwells in you as a loving person — and your sin causes Him sorrow. What
  grieves Him? Bitterness, wrath, anger, evil speaking — the sins of
  relationship. He is the Spirit of unity, and disunity grieves Him."

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1  — KJV: "vocation" — Greek "klēsis" — calling. Not occupation but divine
       calling. ESV: "calling to which you have been called."

v.3  — NET: "making every effort to keep the unity of the
       Spirit in the bond of peace" — "making every effort"
       (spoudazontes = being diligent/eager). WEB: "being eager to
       keep the unity of the Spirit." KJV: "Endeavouring to keep
       the unity." Unity exists; our job is to MAINTAIN it, not
       create it.

v.8  — KJV: "led captivity captive" / ESV: "he led a host of captives, and" — Quotes Psalm 68:18 but changes "received
       gifts" to "gave gifts." Paul interprets Christ's ascension: He
       conquered His enemies (led captivity captive) and distributed the
       spoils (gave gifts to men).

v.9  — KJV: "lower parts of the earth" / ESV: "into the lower regions, the earth?" — Debated. Options: (1) Christ's
       descent into Hades between death and resurrection, (2) the Incarnation
       (descending from heaven to earth), (3) Christ's burial. Most likely
       refers to the Incarnation or burial.

v.11 — KJV: "pastors and teachers" / ESV: "the shepherds and teachers" — Greek grammar links these with one
       article ("tous de poimenas kai didaskalous"), suggesting one office:
       pastor-teachers. Those who shepherd by teaching.
       NET: "It was he who gave some as apostles, some as
       prophets, some as evangelists, and some as pastors and
       teachers" — "gave" (edOken = gifted). WEB: "He gave some
       to be apostles." The leaders ARE the gifts to the church —
       not just their abilities but their persons.

v.12 — KJV punctuation is crucial. Older versions: "For the perfecting of the
       saints, for the work of the ministry, for the edifying of the body."
       Better: "For the perfecting of the saints FOR the work of the ministry
       FOR the edifying of the body." Leaders equip saints; saints do ministry;
       the body is built up.

v.13 — NET: "until we all attain to the unity of the faith and of
       the knowledge of the Son of God — to a mature person" —
       "mature person" (andra teleion = complete/full-grown man).
       WEB: "to a full grown man." KJV: "unto a perfect man." Not
       individual perfection but corporate maturity — the whole
       body growing up together.

v.15 — NET: "speaking the truth in love, we will in all things
       grow up into him who is the head, that is, Christ" —
       "speaking the truth in love" (alEtheuontes en agapE =
       truthing in love — a rare verb). WEB: "speaking truth in
       love." Truth without love wounds; love without truth deceives.
       Both together produce growth.

v.22 — KJV: "conversation" — Greek "anastrophē" — manner of life, conduct
       (not speech). ESV: "former manner of life."

v.26 — KJV: "Be ye angry, and sin not" / ESV: "Be angry and do not sin" — Quotes Psalm 4:4. Anger itself is
       not sin — it's a God-given emotion. But it must be handled quickly
       ("let not the sun go down") and not given to the devil (v.27).
       NET: "Be angry and do not sin; do not let the sun go down
       on the cause of your anger" — quoting Psalm 4:4. "Be angry"
       (orgizesthe) — anger itself is not sin; nursing it overnight
       is. WEB: "Be angry, and don't sin." KJV: "Be ye angry, and
       sin not." A time limit on anger — sunset is the deadline.

v.30 — KJV: "grieve" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "lupeō" — to cause sorrow, to sadden. The
       Spirit is a person who can be grieved — not an impersonal force.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Walk worthy    — Living in a manner consistent with one's calling in Christ.
               Conduct that matches identity.

Unity of the Spirit — The oneness already created by the Spirit among
               believers. Not something to create but to maintain.

Apostles, prophets, evangelists, pastors, teachers — The five gifted
               leadership roles Christ gave to equip the church for ministry.

Perfecting/Equipping — Greek "katartismos" — to mend, complete, prepare,
               train. Leaders prepare saints for their ministry work.

Old man/New man — The former self (corrupted by sin) and the new self
               (created by God in righteousness). Put off/put on like clothes.

Grieve the Spirit — To cause sorrow to the Holy Spirit through sin —
               especially relational sins like bitterness, anger, and
               unforgiveness.

Corrupt communication — Greek "sapros logos" — rotten words. Speech that
               tears down rather than builds up.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Walk worthy" (v.1). Is your daily conduct consistent with your calling in
   Christ? Where is the gap between who you are positionally and how you live
   practically?

2. "Endeavouring to keep the unity" (v.3). Unity requires effort — it doesn't
   happen automatically. Where are you actively working to maintain unity in
   your relationships, your church, your community?

3. "For the perfecting of the saints, for the work of the ministry" (v.12).
   Every believer is called to ministry — not just pastors. What is YOUR
   ministry? How are you being equipped for it?

4. "Put off the old man... put on the new man" (vv.22-24). What specific
   "old man" habits do you need to take off? What "new man" qualities do you
   need to put on? Be specific.

5. "Be ye angry, and sin not" (v.26). How do you handle anger? Do you stuff
   it (letting the sun go down on it) or explode (sinning in it)? What does
   righteous anger look like?

6. "Be ye kind one to another, tenderhearted, forgiving one another" (v.32).
   Is there someone you need to forgive? The standard: "as God for Christ's
   sake hath forgiven you." How much has God forgiven you?
