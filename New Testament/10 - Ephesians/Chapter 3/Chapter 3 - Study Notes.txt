Ephesians - Chapter 3
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Paul reveals the mystery hidden for ages — that Gentiles are fellow heirs with Israel in Christ — and prays that believers would be strengthened by the Spirit to comprehend the love of Christ which surpasses knowledge, being filled with all the fullness of God.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Paul the Apostle (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 3 reveals the "mystery" that was hidden for ages
but now revealed: Gentiles are fellow heirs with Jews in Christ (v.6). Paul
sees himself as the specific steward of this mystery to the Gentiles (v.8).
The chapter concludes with one of the most magnificent prayers in Scripture
(vv.14-21) and a doxology that soars beyond human comprehension (v.20).

Paul's Digression (vv.1-13): Paul begins "For this cause I Paul..." (v.1)
but doesn't complete the thought until v.14. In between, he explains his
unique role as apostle to the Gentiles and the mystery revealed to him. He
is "the prisoner of Jesus Christ for you Gentiles" (v.1) — his imprisonment
is FOR them, not despite them.

The Mystery Revealed (vv.2-6): The mystery hidden for ages: "That the
Gentiles should be fellowheirs, and of the same body, and partakers of his
promise in Christ by the gospel" (v.6). Three "fellow" words: fellow-heirs,
fellow-members, fellow-partakers. Complete equality.

Paul's Ministry (vv.7-13): Paul — "less than the least of all saints" (v.8)
— was given grace to preach "the unsearchable riches of Christ" to Gentiles.
The church now displays God's "manifold wisdom" to spiritual powers (v.10).

Paul's Prayer (vv.14-19): Paul prays for: (1) inner strengthening by the
Spirit (v.16), (2) Christ dwelling in their hearts (v.17), (3) comprehension
of Christ's love in all its dimensions (v.18), (4) being filled with all the
fullness of God (v.19).

The Doxology (vv.20-21): "Exceeding abundantly above all that we ask or
think" — God's ability surpasses our imagination.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.6  — "This mystery is that the Gentiles are fellow heirs, members of the
        same body, and partakers of the promise in Christ Jesus through the
        gospel." (ESV) — The mystery: complete Gentile inclusion.

v.8  — "To me, though I am the very least of all the saints, this grace was
        given, to preach to the Gentiles the unsearchable riches of Christ."
        (ESV) — Paul's humility and calling.

v.10 — "So that through the church the manifold wisdom of God might now be
        made known to the rulers and authorities in the heavenly places." (ESV)
        — The church displays God's wisdom to spiritual beings.

v.16 — "That according to the riches of his glory he may grant you to be
        strengthened with power through his Spirit in your inner being." (ESV)
        — Inner strength from the Spirit.

v.17 — "So that Christ may dwell in your hearts through faith." (ESV) —
        Christ at home in the believer's heart.

v.18-19 — "May have strength to comprehend... what is the breadth and length
        and height and depth, and to know the love of Christ that surpasses
        knowledge." (ESV) — Love beyond knowledge.

v.20 — "Now to him who is able to do far more abundantly than all that we ask
        or think, according to the power at work within us." (ESV) — God's
        ability exceeds our imagination.

Charles Spurgeon:
  Sermon: "Exceeding Abundantly" on v.20 (MTP Vol. 25, No. 1503)
  "Above all we ask — that would be enough. Above all we think — that goes
  further still. Abundantly above — more still. Exceeding abundantly above —
  Paul runs out of words. He piles up language because God's ability exceeds
  all human expression. Whatever you can imagine God doing for you — He can
  do infinitely more. Your imagination is the limit of your asking, but it
  is not the limit of His giving."
  Sermon: "The Love of Christ Which Passeth Knowledge" on v.19 (MTP Vol. 18)
  "To know the love which passeth knowledge — a paradox. You can know it
  experientially though you can never exhaust it intellectually. It is like
  standing at the ocean's edge — you can wade in, swim, even dive deep, but
  you will never reach the bottom or the far shore. His love has breadth
  enough for all, length enough for eternity, depth enough for the worst
  sinner, height enough to reach the throne."

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------

v.1  — KJV: "the prisoner of Jesus Christ" / ESV: "Paul, a prisoner of Christ Jesus on behalf" — Not Rome's prisoner but
       Christ's. Paul reframes his imprisonment: he is in chains because of
       his ministry to Gentiles, and he belongs to Christ, not Caesar.

v.6  — Three "syn-" (together) compounds in Greek: "synklēronoma" (fellow-
       heirs), "syssōma" (fellow-body), "symmetocha" (fellow-partakers).
       Paul coins "syssōma" — it appears nowhere else in Greek literature.
       Complete, equal participation.

v.8  — KJV: "less than the least" / ESV: "though I am the very least of all" — Greek "elachistoterō" — a comparative
       form of a superlative. Paul invents a word: "leaster than the least."
       Grammatically impossible but theologically profound — his humility
       grows as his understanding of grace deepens.

v.10 — KJV: "manifold wisdom" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "polupoikilos" — many-colored,
       multi-faceted, infinitely varied. Like a diamond with countless
       facets. The church displays God's wisdom in all its variety to
       spiritual powers.

v.16 — KJV: "inner man" / ESV: "in your inner being" — Greek "esō anthrōpon" — the inner person, the
       spiritual self as opposed to the outer, physical self. The real you
       that needs strengthening.

v.18 — KJV: "breadth, and length, and depth, and height" / ESV: "what is the breadth and length and height and depth" — Four dimensions
       of Christ's love. Some see: breadth = extends to all people; length =
       lasts forever; depth = reaches the lowest sinner; height = lifts to
       the highest glory.

v.19 — KJV: "filled with all the fulness of God" / ESV: "may be filled with all the fullness of God" — Greek "plērōma" — the
       same word used of Christ in Col 2:9. Believers are to be filled with
       the very fullness of God Himself — an inexhaustible goal.

v.20 — KJV: "exceeding abundantly above" / ESV: "to do far more abundantly than all" — Greek "huper ek perissou" —
       super-abundantly beyond. Three intensifiers stacked: above + out of +
       abundance. Paul strains language to express God's limitless ability.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.5  — NET: "that the Gentiles are fellow heirs, fellow
       members of the body, and fellow partakers of the promise
       in Christ Jesus" — three "fellow" (syn-) compounds:
       synklEronoma, syssOma, symmetocha. WEB: "fellow heirs and
       fellow members of the body, and fellow partakers of his
       promise." The triple syn- emphasizes full equality.

v.8  — NET: "To me — Loss less than the least of all the saints —
       this grace was given" — "less than the least" (tO
       elachistoterO = a comparative of a superlative — grammatically
       impossible, emotionally powerful). WEB: "to me, the very
       least of all saints." Paul invents a word to express his
       unworthiness.

v.16 — NET: "that he may grant you to be strengthened with power
       through his Spirit in the inner person" — "inner person"
       (eis ton esO anthrOpon). WEB: "strengthened with power
       through his Spirit in the inner person." KJV: "strengthened
       with might by his Spirit in the inner man." The Spirit's
       primary work is internal.

v.18 — NET: "may be able to comprehend with all the saints what
       is the breadth and length and height and depth" — four
       dimensions of Christ's love. WEB: "the breadth and length
       and height and depth." No object specified — the love is so
       vast it requires spatial metaphor yet exceeds all dimensions.

v.20 — NET: "Now to him who by the power that is working within
       us is able to do far beyond all that we ask or think" —
       "far beyond" (hyper ek perissou = super-abundantly above).
       WEB: "exceedingly abundantly above all that we ask or
       think." KJV: "exceeding abundantly above all that we ask or
       think." Triple intensification — God's ability exceeds
       imagination.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Mystery        — Something previously hidden in God's plan, now revealed
               through the apostles. Specifically: Gentile equality in Christ.

Dispensation   — Greek "oikonomia" — stewardship, administration. Paul was
               given the stewardship of revealing God's grace to Gentiles.

Unsearchable riches — Greek "anexichniaston" — untraceable, unfathomable.
               Christ's riches cannot be fully explored or exhausted.

Manifold wisdom — Greek "polupoikilos" — many-colored, multi-faceted. God's
               wisdom displayed in infinite variety through the church.

Inner man      — The spiritual self, the true person within. The seat of
               spiritual life that needs strengthening by the Spirit.

Rooted and grounded — Two metaphors: a tree with deep roots (stability) and
               a building with a solid foundation (security). Both in love.

Fullness of God — The complete character and presence of God filling the
               believer. An inexhaustible, ever-expanding reality.

Doxology       — A formal expression of praise to God. Ephesians 3:20-21 is
               one of the greatest doxologies in Scripture.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Less than the least of all saints" (v.8). Paul's humility grew as his
   understanding of grace deepened. Is that true for you? Does knowing more
   of God's grace make you more humble or more proud?

2. "The unsearchable riches of Christ" (v.8). Christ's riches are
   inexhaustible — you will never reach the bottom. What "riches of Christ"
   have you discovered recently? What remains unexplored?

3. "The manifold wisdom of God" (v.10). The church — with all its messiness —
   displays God's wisdom to spiritual powers. How does this change your view
   of the church? Of your role in it?

4. "Strengthened with might by his Spirit in the inner man" (v.16). Where do
   you need inner strengthening right now? Are you cultivating your inner life
   or neglecting it for external activity?

5. "To know the love of Christ, which passeth knowledge" (v.19). Have you
   experienced Christ's love in a way that goes beyond intellectual
   understanding? What would it look like to be "filled with all the fulness
   of God"?

6. "Exceeding abundantly above all that we ask or think" (v.20). What are you
   asking God for? Whatever it is — He can do infinitely more. Are your
   prayers too small?
