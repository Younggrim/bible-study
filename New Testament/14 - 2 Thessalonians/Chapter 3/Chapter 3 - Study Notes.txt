2 Thessalonians - Chapter 3
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Paul requests prayer for the gospel's advance, commands the idle to work ("if anyone is not willing to work, let him not eat"), and instructs the church to admonish the disorderly while not treating them as enemies.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Paul the Apostle (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 3 addresses a practical problem in the
Thessalonian church: some members had stopped working, apparently because they
believed Christ's return was imminent (why work if the world is ending?). They
became idle busybodies, mooching off others. Paul's response is direct: "If
any would not work, neither should he eat" (v.10). He models the solution with
his own example of self-supporting labor (vv.7-9) and prescribes church
discipline for the persistently idle (vv.6, 14-15).

Prayer Request (vv.1-2): Paul asks for prayer that the gospel would spread
freely and that he would be delivered from "unreasonable and wicked men" (v.2).

God's Faithfulness (vv.3-5): "The Lord is faithful" (v.3) — He will establish
and protect them. Paul has confidence they will obey (v.4).

The Idle Must Work (vv.6-15): Paul commands withdrawal from the disorderly
(v.6). He points to his own example: he worked night and day to avoid being a
burden (vv.7-9). The rule: no work, no food (v.10). The idle should "with
quietness work and eat their own bread" (v.12). Those who refuse should be
noted and avoided — but treated as brothers, not enemies (vv.14-15).

Benediction (vv.16-18): "The Lord of peace himself give you peace always by
all means" (v.16). Paul's authenticating signature (v.17).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1 — "Pray for us, that the word of the Lord may speed ahead and be
 honored." (ESV) — Prayer for the gospel's unhindered advance.

v.3 — "The Lord is faithful. He will establish you and guard you against the
 evil one." (ESV) — God's faithfulness as the believer's security.

v.5 — "May the Lord direct your hearts to the love of God and to the
 steadfastness of Christ." (ESV) — Hearts directed toward love and
 hope.

v.6 — "Keep away from any brother who is walking in idleness." (ESV) —
 Church discipline for persistent disobedience.

v.10 — "If anyone is not willing to work, let him not eat." (ESV) — The work
 ethic principle: no work, no food.

v.13 — "Do not grow weary in doing good." (ESV) — Perseverance in doing good
 despite others' laziness.

v.15 — "Do not regard him as an enemy, but warn him as a brother." (ESV) —
 Discipline with love, not hostility.

Charles Spurgeon:
 Sermon: "The Lord Is Faithful" on v.3 (MTP Vol. 30, No. 1797)
 "The Lord IS faithful. Not 'was' — is. Not 'will be' — is. Present tense,
 continuous reality. Right now, in your current trial, the Lord is faithful.
 He shall establish you — make you firm. And keep you from evil — guard you
 from the evil one. Your security rests not on your faithfulness to Him but
 on His faithfulness to you. And He has never failed anyone who trusted Him."
 Sermon: "If Any Would Not Work" on v.10 (MTP Vol. 22, No. 1317)
 "If any would not work — not 'cannot' but 'would not.' Paul is not
 speaking of the disabled, the sick, or those who cannot find work. He
 speaks of those who WILL not work — the voluntarily idle. Christianity does
 not encourage laziness. Grace does not excuse irresponsibility. The same
 God who provides also commands: work."

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "have free course" / ESV: "speed ahead" — Greek "trechē" — to run. Paul pictures the
 gospel as a runner in a race — running freely, unhindered, toward the
 finish line. "Be glorified" / ESV: "be honored" — honored, received with respect.

v.2 — KJV: "unreasonable" / ESV: "wicked and evil" — Greek "atopōn" — out of place, absurd, wicked.
 People who are irrational in their opposition to the gospel.

v.3 — KJV: "stablish" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "stērixei" — to make firm, strengthen, set
 solidly. "Keep you from evil" — Greek "phulaxei apo tou ponērou" —
 guard from the evil one (Satan) or from evil generally.

v.6 — KJV: "disorderly" / ESV: "walking in idleness" — Greek "ataktōs" — out of order, out of rank. A
 military term for soldiers who break formation. Here: those who refuse
 to work and live off others.

v.10 — KJV: "would not work" / ESV: "would" — Greek "ou thelei ergazesthai" — does not
 WILL to work, refuses to work. Voluntary idleness, not inability.
 "Neither should he eat" — not cruelty but consequence. Hunger
 motivates work.

v.11 — KJV: "working not at all, but are busybodies" / ESV: "busybodies" — A Greek wordplay:
 "mēden ergazomenous alla periergazomenous" — not working but
 overworking (at meddling). They're busy — just not at anything useful.

v.14 — KJV: "note that man" / ESV: "note" — Greek "sēmeiousthe" — mark, take special note
 of. Identify the person publicly. "Have no company" — Greek "mē
 sunanamignusthai" — don't mix with, don't associate closely.

v.15 — KJV: "count him not as an enemy, but admonish him as a brother" / ESV: "enemy, but warn him as a brother" —
 The goal of discipline is restoration, not punishment. He's still
 family — just family that needs correction.

v.17 — KJV: "the token in every epistle: so I write" / ESV: "every letter of mine; it is the way I write" — Paul's handwritten
 signature as authentication. Necessary because forged letters were
 circulating (2:2). His distinctive handwriting proved genuineness.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.3  — NET: "But the Lord is faithful, and he will strengthen
       you and guard you from the evil one" — "guard" (phylaxei =
       protect/keep). WEB: "the Lord is faithful, who will
       establish you and guard you from the evil one." God's
       faithfulness, not ours, is the ground of security.

v.6  — NET: "keep away from every brother who lives an undisciplined
       life" — "undisciplined" (ataktOs = out of order/disorderly —
       a military term for breaking ranks). WEB: "every brother who
       walks in rebellion." KJV: "every brother that walketh
       disorderly." Not moral scandal but refusal to work.

v.10 — NET: "if someone does not want to work, he should not eat"
       — "does not want" (ou thelei = is unwilling — not unable).
       WEB: "if anyone will not work, don't let him eat." KJV: "if
       any would not work, neither should he eat." The command
       targets willful idleness, not the disabled or unemployed.

v.13 — NET: "do not grow weary in doing what is right" — "grow
       weary" (enkakEsEte = lose heart/become discouraged). WEB:
       "don't be weary in doing well." KJV: "be not weary in well
       doing." Persistence in good despite the disobedience of
       others — don't let their failure stop your faithfulness.

v.15 — NET: "Do not regard him as an enemy but admonish him as a
       brother" — "admonish" (noutheteite = put sense into/correct
       by instruction). WEB: "admonish him as a brother." KJV:
       "admonish him as a brother." Discipline without dehumanization
       — the goal is restoration, not rejection.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Free course — The gospel running unhindered like a racer. Prayer for the
 removal of obstacles to the Word's spread.

The Lord is faithful — God's unchanging reliability. The foundation of
 believer's security — not their own faithfulness but His.

Disorderly — Out of rank, undisciplined. Specifically: refusing to work
 and living off others' generosity.

Would not work — Voluntary idleness. Not inability but unwillingness.
 Distinguished from those who cannot work due to circumstances.

Busybodies — Those who are busy meddling in others' affairs rather than
 doing their own work. Active in everything except productivity.

Church discipline — The process of confronting, warning, and if necessary
 withdrawing fellowship from persistently disobedient members.
 Goal: restoration, not punishment.

Admonish as a brother — Correction given in love, recognizing the person's
 identity as family. Firm but not hostile.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "The word of the Lord may have free course" (v.1). Are you praying for
 the gospel's advance? What obstacles to the gospel can you pray against?
 How can you help the Word "run freely"?

2. "The Lord is faithful" (v.3). Where do you need to trust God's
 faithfulness right now? He will establish you and keep you. Can you rest
 in that?

3. "If any would not work, neither should he eat" (v.10). What's your work
 ethic? Are you contributing or consuming? Are you productive or idle?
 How does your work honor God?

4. "Working not at all, but are busybodies" (v.11). Are you busy with the
 right things? Or are you active in meddling, gossip, and others' business
 while neglecting your own responsibilities?

5. "Be not weary in well doing" (v.13). Others' laziness can discourage those
 who work hard. Are you tempted to give up doing good because others aren't
 pulling their weight? Keep going.

6. "Count him not as an enemy, but admonish him as a brother" (v.15). When
 you need to confront someone, do you do it with love (as a brother) or
 with hostility (as an enemy)? The goal is always restoration.
