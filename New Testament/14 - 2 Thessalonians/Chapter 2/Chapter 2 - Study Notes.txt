2 Thessalonians - Chapter 2
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Paul corrects the false teaching that the Day of the Lord has already come — it will not arrive until the man of lawlessness is revealed, the one whom the Lord Jesus will destroy with the breath of His mouth at His coming.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Paul the Apostle (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 2 addresses the primary reason Paul wrote this
letter: someone had told the Thessalonians (falsely claiming Paul's authority)
that "the day of Christ is at hand" — that it had already come (v.2). This
caused panic and confusion. Paul corrects this by explaining that two things
must happen first: (1) the great apostasy/falling away, and (2) the revelation
of the "man of sin" (Antichrist). This is one of the most important and
debated eschatological passages in the NT.

Don't Be Shaken (vv.1-2): Paul urges them not to be disturbed by false claims
— whether by "spirit" (prophecy), "word" (teaching), or "letter" (forged
correspondence) — that the Day of the Lord has already arrived.

The Man of Sin (vv.3-12): Before that Day comes, there must be: (1) "a falling
away" (apostasia — the great apostasy, v.3), and (2) the revelation of "the
man of sin, the son of perdition" (v.3) — who exalts himself above God and
sits in God's temple claiming to be God (v.4). Something/someone currently
"restrains" him (vv.6-7). When the restrainer is removed, the lawless one will
be revealed — and Christ will destroy him at His coming (v.8).

God's Chosen (vv.13-17): In contrast to those who perish, the Thessalonians
are "chosen to salvation" (v.13). Paul urges them to "stand fast" and hold to
the traditions they were taught (v.15).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.3 — "That day will not come, unless the rebellion comes first, and the man
 of lawlessness is revealed, the son of destruction." (ESV) — Two
 prerequisites before the Day of the Lord.

v.4 — "Who opposes and exalts himself against every so-called god or object
 of worship, so that he takes his seat in the temple of God,
 proclaiming himself to be God." (ESV) — The Antichrist's supreme
 blasphemy.

v.7 — "For the mystery of lawlessness is already at work. Only he who now
 restrains it will do so until he is out of the way." (ESV) — Evil
 restrained until the appointed time.

v.8 — "And then the lawless one will be revealed, whom the Lord Jesus will
 kill with the breath of his mouth and bring to nothing by the
 appearance of his coming." (ESV) — Christ's effortless destruction of
 the Antichrist.

v.10 — "They refused to love the truth and so be saved." (ESV) — The root
 cause of deception: rejecting truth.

v.13 — "God chose you as the firstfruits to be saved, through sanctification
 by the Spirit and belief in the truth." (ESV) — Election,
 sanctification, and faith.

v.15 — "Stand firm and hold to the traditions that you were taught." (ESV) —
 Hold firm to apostolic teaching.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.2 — KJV: "the day of Christ is at hand" / ESV: "the day of the Lord has come" — Greek "enestēken" — has come,
 is present, has already arrived. Not "is near" but "is here." Someone
 told them the Day had already come — causing panic.

v.3 — KJV: "a falling away" / ESV: "the rebellion" — Greek "hē apostasia" — THE apostasy, the
 great rebellion, the definitive departure from the faith. A specific
 end-times event, not general backsliding.

v.3 — KJV: "man of sin... son of perdition" / ESV: "man of lawlessness is revealed, the son" — Titles for the Antichrist.
 "Man of sin" = sin personified. "Son of perdition" = destined for
 destruction (same title used of Judas, John 17:12).

v.6 — KJV: "what withholdeth" / ESV: "what" — Greek "to katechon" (neuter) — "that which
 restrains." v.7: "he who now letteth" — Greek "ho katechōn" (masculine)
 — "he who restrains." The restrainer is both a thing and a person.
 Interpretations: (1) the Holy Spirit, (2) the Roman Empire/government,
 (3) the church, (4) Michael the archangel. Most evangelicals identify
 the restrainer as the Holy Spirit working through the church.

v.7 — KJV: "letteth" / ESV: "restrains" — Old English for "restrains" (not "allows"). "The
 mystery of iniquity doth already work" — lawlessness is already
 operating in hidden form; it will be fully revealed when restraint is
 removed.

v.8 — KJV: "the spirit of his mouth" / ESV: "mouth" — Christ destroys the Antichrist with
 a word — effortlessly. "The brightness of his coming" — Greek
 "epiphaneia tēs parousias" — the appearing of His presence. Christ's
 mere appearance is enough.

v.11 — KJV: "strong delusion" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "energeian planēs" — an active working
 of deception. God sends it as judgment on those who rejected truth.
 Judicial hardening: those who refuse truth are given over to lies.

v.15 — KJV: "traditions" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "paradoseis" — things handed down, passed
 on. Not human traditions but apostolic teaching — whether oral ("by
 word") or written ("our epistle").

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.3  — NET: "that day will not come unless the rebellion comes
       first and the man of lawlessness is revealed" — "rebellion"
       (apostasia = apostasy/falling away). WEB: "unless the
       departure comes first." KJV: "except there come a falling
       away first." Whether "departure" means doctrinal apostasy
       or physical departure (rapture) is debated.

v.4  — NET: "He opposes and exalts himself above every so-called
       god or object of worship, and as a result he takes his seat
       in God's temple" — "takes his seat" (kathisai = sits down).
       WEB: "he sits as God in the temple of God." The man of
       lawlessness claims divine status — ultimate blasphemy.

v.7  — NET: "For the hidden power of lawlessness is already at
       work" — "hidden power" (mystErion tEs anomias = mystery of
       lawlessness). WEB: "the mystery of lawlessness already
       works." KJV: "the mystery of iniquity doth already work."
       Evil is active NOW but restrained — waiting for the right
       moment.

v.8  — NET: "the Lord will destroy him with the breath of his
       mouth" — "breath of his mouth" (tO pneumati tou stomatos
       autou). WEB: "whom the Lord will kill with the breath of his
       mouth." KJV: "whom the Lord shall consume with the spirit
       of his mouth." Christ's word alone destroys the antichrist —
       no battle required.

v.13 — NET: "God chose you from the beginning for salvation
       through sanctification by the Spirit and faith in the truth"
       — "from the beginning" (ap' archEs). Some manuscripts:
       "firstfruits" (aparchEn). WEB: "God chose you from the
       beginning." KJV: "God hath from the beginning chosen you."
       Election, sanctification, and faith together.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Day of the Lord/Day of Christ — The future time of Christ's return, judgment,
 and the establishment of His kingdom.

Falling away/Apostasy — The great end-times rebellion against God and His
 truth. A definitive, large-scale departure from the faith.

Man of sin/Son of perdition — The Antichrist. The ultimate human embodiment
 of rebellion against God. Destined for destruction.

The restrainer — The person/force currently holding back the full revelation
 of the Antichrist. Most commonly identified as the Holy Spirit
 working through the church.

Mystery of iniquity — Lawlessness already at work in hidden form. The spirit
 of antichrist operating before the Antichrist himself appears.

Strong delusion — God's judicial act of giving those who reject truth over
 to believe lies. A consequence of refusing to love the truth.

Traditions — Apostolic teaching passed down — both oral and written. The
 authoritative instruction of the apostles.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Be not soon shaken in mind, or be troubled" (v.2). False teaching about
 end times causes fear and confusion. How do you respond to sensational
 claims about prophecy? Do you test them against Scripture or get swept up?

2. "The mystery of iniquity doth already work" (v.7). Evil is already at work
 in the world — restrained but active. Do you see evidence of this? How
 does knowing there's a restrainer give you confidence?

3. "They received not the love of the truth" (v.10). Do you LOVE truth — not
 just know it? Is truth something you embrace affectionately or merely
 acknowledge intellectually? Loving truth is the safeguard against
 deception.

4. "Stand fast, and hold the traditions which ye have been taught" (v.15). In
 a world of shifting opinions and novel teachings, are you anchored in
 apostolic truth (Scripture)? What are you holding fast to?

5. "God hath from the beginning chosen you to salvation" (v.13). In the midst
 of a passage about deception and judgment, Paul reminds them of their
 election. How does God's sovereign choice comfort you when the world seems
 chaotic?
