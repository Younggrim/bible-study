2 Corinthians - Chapter 6
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Paul pleads with the Corinthians not to receive God's grace in vain, catalogs his sufferings as credentials of authentic ministry, and commands: "Do not be unequally yoked with unbelievers" — for what fellowship has light with darkness?

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Paul (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 6 contains Paul's appeal not to receive God's grace
"in vain" (v.1), his catalog of sufferings that validate his ministry (vv.3-10),
his emotional appeal for the Corinthians to open their hearts (vv.11-13), and
the command to separate from unbelievers in binding partnerships (vv.14-18).

The Marks of Authentic Ministry (vv.3-10): One of the most powerful passages
on ministry credentials in the NT. Paul lists what validates his ministry — not
success or comfort but: patience, afflictions, stripes, imprisonments, labors,
purity, knowledge, kindness, the Holy Spirit, genuine love, truth, God's power,
and a series of paradoxes (vv.8-10).

The Paradoxes (vv.8-10): "As deceivers, and yet true; as unknown, and yet well
known; as dying, and behold, we live; as chastened, and not killed; as sorrowful,
yet alway rejoicing; as poor, yet making many rich; as having nothing, and yet
possessing all things." The Christian life is a series of paradoxes — the world
sees one thing; reality is another.

Unequal Yoking (vv.14-18): "Be ye not unequally yoked together with unbelievers"
(v.14). Paul uses five rhetorical questions to show the incompatibility of
believers and unbelievers in binding partnerships. The principle: light and
darkness cannot coexist in the same yoke.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.2  — "Behold, now is the favorable time; behold, now is the day of
        salvation." (ESV) — The urgency of responding to God today.

v.4  — "As servants of God we commend ourselves in every way: by great
        endurance." (ESV) — Patience as the first mark of authentic ministry.

v.10 — "As sorrowful, yet always rejoicing; as poor, yet making many rich; as
        having nothing, yet possessing everything." (ESV) — The paradoxes of
        Christian experience.

v.14 — "Do not be unequally yoked with unbelievers." (ESV) — The command to
        avoid binding partnerships with unbelievers.

v.17 — "Therefore go out from their midst, and be separate from them, says the
        Lord." (ESV) — The call to holy separation.

v.18 — "And I will be a father to you, and you shall be sons and daughters to
        me, says the Lord Almighty." (ESV) — The promise of intimate
        relationship with God.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------

v.2  — KJV: "now is the accepted time" / ESV: "Behold, now is the favorable time" — Greek "kairos" — the opportune
       moment, the right time. Not chronological time (chronos) but decisive
       time — the moment of opportunity that must be seized.

v.10 — KJV: "having nothing, and yet possessing all things" / ESV: "rich; as having nothing, yet possessing everything" — The ultimate
       paradox: materially poor but spiritually owning everything. "All things
       are yours" (1 Corinthians 3:21-23).

v.14 — KJV: "unequally yoked" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "heterozugountes" — yoked with a
       different kind. From Deuteronomy 22:10 — don't yoke an ox and donkey
       together (different natures, different strengths, different directions).

v.15 — KJV: "Belial" / ESV: Both equivalent. — A Hebrew term for worthlessness/wickedness, used as
       a name for Satan. Christ and Belial have nothing in common.

v.17 — KJV: "come out from among them" / ESV: "Therefore go out from their midst, and be" — Quoting Isaiah 52:11. Not physical
       isolation from the world but refusal to be bound in partnerships that
       compromise holiness.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.2  — NET: "now is the acceptable time; now is the day of
       salvation!" — "now" (nyn) repeated for emphasis — quoting
       Isaiah 49:8. WEB: "now is the acceptable time. Behold, now
       is the day of salvation." Urgency of present opportunity.

v.4  — NET: "as servants of God we commend ourselves in every
       way: with great endurance, in afflictions, in hardships" —
       Paul's credential list: 28 items of suffering and virtue.
       WEB: "in great endurance, in afflictions, in hardships."
       Authentic ministry authenticated by suffering, not success.

v.10 — NET: "as sorrowful, but always rejoicing; as poor, but
       making many rich; as having nothing, and yet possessing
       everything" — paradox on paradox — the apostolic life defies
       human categories. WEB: "as sorrowful, yet always rejoicing."
       The Christian life is simultaneously suffering and joy.

v.14 — NET: "Do not become partners with those who do not believe.
       For what partnership is there between righteousness and
       lawlessness?" — "partners" (heterozygountes = unequally
       yoked). WEB: "Don't be unequally yoked with unbelievers."
       KJV: "Be ye not unequally yoked together with unbelievers."
       The metaphor is agricultural — two different animals in one yoke.

v.18 — NET: "and I will be a father to you, and you will be my
       sons and daughters" — "sons and daughters" — quoting/adapting
       2 Samuel 7:14 with the addition of "daughters" (not in the
       OT source). WEB: "you will be my sons and daughters." Paul
       expands the promise to include women explicitly.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Accepted time — The present moment of God's favor — the opportunity to respond
               that must not be delayed.

Marks of ministry — The credentials that validate genuine ministry: suffering,
               patience, purity, love, truth, and power — not success or comfort.

Paradoxes     — The contradictions of Christian experience: sorrowful yet
               rejoicing, poor yet rich, having nothing yet possessing all.

Unequally yoked — Bound in a partnership with someone of fundamentally
               different nature and direction. Applied to marriage, business,
               and binding alliances.

Belial        — A name for Satan meaning "worthlessness" or "wickedness."
               Christ and Belial are completely incompatible.

Separation    — Not isolation from the world but refusal to be bound in
               compromising partnerships. "In the world but not of it."

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Now is the accepted time; behold, now is the day of salvation" (v.2). Is
   there something God is calling you to respond to TODAY? Don't delay.
   Tomorrow is not guaranteed. What decision are you postponing?

2. The paradoxes of vv.8-10 describe the Christian life: sorrowful yet
   rejoicing, poor yet making others rich, having nothing yet possessing all.
   Which paradox most describes your current experience? How do you hold both
   realities together?

3. "Be ye not unequally yoked together with unbelievers" (v.14). This applies
   to any binding partnership — marriage, business, deep alliances. Are you
   in any relationship that is pulling you away from Christ? What needs to
   change?

4. "I will be a Father unto you, and ye shall be my sons and daughters" (v.18).
   The reward of separation: intimate relationship with God as Father. Is the
   promise of deeper intimacy with God worth whatever you need to separate from?

REFLECTION QUESTIONS
------------------------------------
1. Paul's ministry credentials (vv.4-10) include suffering, not success. How
   does this challenge the modern tendency to validate ministry by numbers,
   buildings, and budgets?

2. "As having nothing, and yet possessing all things" (v.10). Paul was
   materially poor but spiritually wealthy. How do you measure wealth — by
   what you have or by what you are in Christ?

3. The unequal yoke (v.14) is often applied to marriage — but it extends to
   any binding partnership. What partnerships in your life (business, close
   friendships, alliances) might be pulling you in a direction incompatible
   with your faith?

4. "Come out from among them, and be ye separate" (v.17). Separation is not
   isolation — Jesus ate with sinners. It is refusing to be BOUND to what
   opposes God. How do you maintain relationships with unbelievers without
   being yoked to their values?

5. God promises "I will receive you... I will be a Father unto you" (vv.17-18).
   The reward of holy separation is deeper intimacy with God. Is there
   something you need to "come out from" to experience more of God's
   fatherly presence?
