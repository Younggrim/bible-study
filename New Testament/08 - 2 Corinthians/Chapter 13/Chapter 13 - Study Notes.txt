2 Corinthians - Chapter 13
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Paul warns he will not spare the disobedient on his third visit, urges self-examination ("Examine yourselves, whether ye be in the faith"), and closes with the Trinitarian benediction: "The grace of the Lord Jesus Christ, and the love of God, and the communion of the Holy Ghost, be with you all."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Paul (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 13 is Paul's closing — a final warning (vv.1-4),
a call to self-examination (vv.5-6), a prayer for their perfection (vv.7-10),
and a benediction (vv.11-14). The letter ends with one of the most beautiful
Trinitarian blessings in Scripture (v.14).

The Call to Self-Examination (v.5): "Examine yourselves, whether ye be in the
faith; prove your own selves." Instead of examining Paul (demanding proof of
his apostleship), they should examine themselves. The question is not "Is Paul
legitimate?" but "Am I genuinely in the faith?"

The Trinitarian Benediction (v.14): "The grace of the Lord Jesus Christ, and
the love of God, and the communion of the Holy Ghost, be with you all." The
most complete Trinitarian blessing in the NT — grace from Christ, love from
the Father, fellowship from the Spirit. Three persons, one God, three distinct
gifts.

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. "Examine yourselves, whether ye be in the faith" (v.5). When did you last
   seriously examine your faith? Not assume it — examine it. Is Christ in you?
   Is there evidence of genuine spiritual life? Don't coast on past decisions
   — examine present reality.

2. "We can do nothing against the truth, but for the truth" (v.8). Truth is
   unstoppable. You can fight it, deny it, suppress it — but you cannot defeat
   it. Are you on the side of truth or fighting against it?

3. "Be perfect, be of good comfort, be of one mind, live in peace" (v.11).
   Five final commands: be complete, be encouraged, be unified, live peacefully.
   Which of these does your community most need right now?

4. "The grace of the Lord Jesus Christ, and the love of God, and the communion
   of the Holy Ghost" (v.14). Three gifts from three persons: grace, love,
   fellowship. Which do you most need to experience today?

1. Paul says "examine yourselves" (v.5) — not "examine your pastor" or
   "examine your church." Self-examination is the priority. What evidence of
   genuine faith do you see in your life? What concerns you?

2. "Know ye not your own selves, how that Jesus Christ is in you?" (v.5). The
   normal Christian experience: Christ IN you. Do you have assurance of
   salvation? What gives you confidence that Christ is in you?

3. Paul's final commands (v.11) include "be of one mind" and "live in peace."
   Unity and peace are not optional extras — they are commands. Where is there
   disunity or conflict in your relationships that needs to be addressed?

4. The Trinitarian benediction (v.14) is the most common blessing used in
   churches today. Do you receive it as a genuine prayer or as a ritual
   closing? What would it mean to truly experience grace, love, and fellowship
   from the Trinity?

5. 2 Corinthians ends where it began — with grace (1:2, 13:14). The letter
   is bookended by grace. How has grace been the theme of your life this year?
   Where do you need more grace?
