2 Corinthians - Chapter 10
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Paul defends his apostolic authority against challengers, declaring that the weapons of his warfare are not fleshly but divinely powerful — "casting down imaginations, and every high thing that exalteth itself against the knowledge of God."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Paul (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapters 10-13 form the most intense section of 2 Corinthians
— Paul's defense of his apostleship against false teachers ("super-apostles")
who had infiltrated Corinth. These chapters are passionate, personal, and
sometimes sarcastic. Paul defends not for his own sake but for the Gospel's sake.

Spiritual Warfare (vv.3-5): One of the most important passages on spiritual
warfare in the NT. "The weapons of our warfare are not carnal, but mighty
through God" (v.4). The battle is for the mind — "casting down imaginations,
and every high thing that exalteth itself against the knowledge of God, and
bringing into captivity every thought to the obedience of Christ" (v.5).

The Accusation (v.10): Paul's critics said "his letters are weighty and powerful;
but his bodily presence is weak, and his speech contemptible." They judged by
appearance — Paul was unimpressive physically and rhetorically. Paul's response:
what I am in letters, I will be in person (v.11).

"He that glorieth, let him glory in the Lord" (v.17): The chapter's conclusion
— echoing 1 Corinthians 1:31. The only legitimate boast is the Lord. "Not he
that commendeth himself is approved, but whom the Lord commendeth" (v.18).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.4  — "For the weapons of our warfare are not of the flesh but have divine
        power to destroy strongholds." (ESV) — Spiritual weapons for spiritual
        battles.

v.5  — "We destroy arguments and every lofty opinion raised against the
        knowledge of God, and take every thought captive to obey Christ." (ESV)
        — The battle for the mind.

v.12 — "When they measure themselves by one another and compare themselves
        with one another, they are without understanding." (ESV) — The folly
        of self-comparison.

v.17 — "Let the one who boasts, boast in the Lord." (ESV) — The only
        legitimate boast.

v.18 — "For it is not the one who commends himself who is approved, but the
        one whom the Lord commends." (ESV) — God's approval is what matters.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.3  — NET: "the weapons of our warfare are not human weapons,
       but are made powerful by God for tearing down strongholds"
       — "strongholds" (ochyrOmatOn = fortifications). WEB: "the
       weapons of our warfare are not of the flesh, but mighty
       before God to the throwing down of strongholds." Spiritual
       warfare requires spiritual weapons.

v.4  — KJV: "mighty through God" / ESV: "of the flesh but have divine" — Greek "dunata tō theō" — powerful in God,
       divinely powerful. Not human strength but God's power working through
       spiritual weapons.
       NET: "We tear down arguments" — "arguments" (logismous =
       reasonings/speculations). WEB: "throwing down imaginations."
       KJV: "Casting down imaginations." The battlefield is the
       mind — false ideologies and human reasoning opposed to God.

v.5  — KJV: "imaginations" / ESV: "We destroy arguments and" — Greek "logismous" — reasonings, arguments,
       thought patterns. The battle is intellectual/spiritual — wrong thinking
       must be demolished and replaced with Christ's truth.
       NET: "and every arrogant obstacle that is raised up against
       the knowledge of God, and we take every thought captive to
       make it obey Christ" — "every thought captive" (pan noEma
       aichmalOtizontes). WEB: "bringing every thought into
       captivity to the obedience of Christ." Comprehensive mental
       discipline — no thought exempt from Christ's lordship.

v.5  — KJV: "bringing into captivity every thought" / ESV: "of God, and take every thought captive" — Military language:
       taking prisoners of war. Every thought that rebels against Christ must
       be captured and made obedient.
       NET: "and every arrogant obstacle that is raised up against
       the knowledge of God, and we take every thought captive to
       make it obey Christ" — "every thought captive" (pan noEma
       aichmalOtizontes). WEB: "bringing every thought into
       captivity to the obedience of Christ." Comprehensive mental
       discipline — no thought exempt from Christ's lordship.

v.10 — NET: "His letters are weighty and powerful, but his
       physical presence is weak and his speech is of no account"
       — the opponents' critique of Paul. WEB: "his bodily presence
       is weak, and his speech is despised." Paul may have had
       physical ailments and lacked rhetorical polish.

v.12 — KJV: "are not wise" / ESV: "without understanding" — Understatement. Comparing yourself to others
       (rather than to God's standard) is foolish — it always produces either
       pride or despair.
       NET: "we do not dare to classify or compare ourselves with
       some of those who recommend themselves" — "compare ourselves"
       (synkrinai heautous) — measuring ministry by comparison is
       foolish. WEB: "comparing themselves with themselves." Self-
       referential metrics produce self-deception.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Spiritual warfare — The battle against spiritual forces using spiritual weapons
               — prayer, Scripture, truth, faith. Not physical but real.

Strongholds   — Fortified positions of wrong thinking — deeply entrenched
               lies, arguments, and thought patterns that oppose God's truth.

Imaginations  — Greek "logismoi" — reasonings, arguments, philosophies that
               exalt themselves against the knowledge of God.

Captive thoughts — Every thought brought under Christ's authority — not
               allowed to roam free but submitted to His lordship.

Carnal weapons — Human methods — eloquence, manipulation, political power,
               social pressure. Ineffective against spiritual strongholds.

Self-commendation — Praising yourself. Paul says it's worthless — only God's
               commendation matters.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "The weapons of our warfare are not carnal" (v.4). You cannot fight
   spiritual battles with human weapons. Prayer, Scripture, truth, and faith
   are your weapons. Are you using spiritual weapons or relying on human
   strategies?

2. "Bringing into captivity every thought to the obedience of Christ" (v.5).
   The battle is in your mind — every thought must be captured and submitted
   to Christ. What thoughts are running free in your mind that need to be
   captured? Anxiety? Lust? Bitterness? Lies?

3. "They measuring themselves by themselves... are not wise" (v.12). Comparing
   yourself to others always distorts reality. Are you comparing yourself to
   other men — producing either pride or discouragement? The only standard
   is Christ.

4. "Not he that commendeth himself is approved, but whom the Lord commendeth"
   (v.18). Whose approval are you seeking — your own, others', or God's?
   Only God's commendation matters eternally.
