John - Chapter 19
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Pilate scourges Jesus and presents Him ("Behold the man!"), delivers Him to crucifixion under the inscription "King of the Jews," Jesus commits His mother to John, declares "It is finished," and is buried in Joseph's garden tomb.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: The Apostle John — an eyewitness to the crucifixion (v.26,35).

Historical Context: Chapter 19 records the crucifixion and burial of Jesus.
John's account is the most detailed of the four Gospels, written by someone
who stood at the foot of the cross. He records seven statements Jesus made
from the cross (the "Seven Last Words"), though John records only three of them.

Roman Crucifixion: One of the most brutal forms of execution ever devised.
The condemned was first scourged — beaten with a flagellum (leather whip
embedded with bone and metal) that tore flesh to the bone. Then forced to
carry the crossbeam to the execution site. Nailed through the wrists and feet,
the victim died of asphyxiation as the body's weight made breathing impossible.
Death could take hours or days.

"It Is Finished" (v.30): Greek "tetelestai" — a single word meaning "paid in
full," "accomplished," "completed." The same word was stamped on paid bills in
the ancient world. Jesus' final word from the cross was not a cry of defeat
but a declaration of victory — the work of redemption was complete.

Fulfilled Prophecy: John notes multiple Old Testament prophecies fulfilled at
the crucifixion: the soldiers casting lots for His garment (Psalm 22:18), no
bones broken (Exodus 12:46, Numbers 9:12, Psalm 34:20), and the piercing
(Zechariah 12:10).

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- The Praetorium (v.9): Pilate's judgment hall in Jerusalem where Jesus was scourged and sentenced.
- Gabbatha / the Pavement (v.13): The stone pavement where Pilate rendered his verdict. A raised platform for public judgment.
- Golgotha (v.17): "The place of a skull" — outside Jerusalem's walls. John preserves the Hebrew/Aramaic name.
- A garden near Golgotha (v.41): Joseph of Arimathea's tomb was in a garden near the crucifixion site — a new tomb where Jesus was buried.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.5  — "Behold the man!" (ESV) (Ecce Homo) — Pilate's presentation of the
        beaten Jesus — intended to evoke pity, but the crowd demanded
        crucifixion.

v.11 — "You would have no authority over me at all unless it had been given
        you from above." (ESV) — All human authority is derived from God. Even
        Pilate's power over Jesus was given by the Father.

v.26-27 — Jesus caring for His mother from the cross — even in agony, He
        fulfilled His responsibility as a son.

v.30 — "It is finished." (ESV) — Tetelestai — paid in full. The greatest three
        words in human history.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1  — KJV: "Pilate therefore took Jesus, and scourged him" / ESV: "Then Pilate took Jesus and flogged him" — Greek "emastigōsen" — KJV uses "scourged"; ESV uses "flogged." Both refer to the brutal Roman flagellum — a whip with multiple leather thongs embedded with bone and metal.

v.5  — KJV: "Behold the man!" / ESV: "Behold the man!" — Latin "Ecce Homo" — Both identical. Pilate's words have become one of the most famous phrases in history.
  [ASV/NET/WEB]: NET: "Behold, the man!" — "the man" (ho anthrōpos).
       WEB: "Behold, the man!" KJV: "Behold the man!" Latin: Ecce
       homo. Pilate presents Jesus beaten, crowned with thorns —
       perhaps hoping for pity. Unwittingly, he declares a
       theological truth: behold what a man truly is.

v.11 — KJV: "Thou couldest have no power at all against me, except it were given thee from above" / ESV: "You would have no authority over me at all unless it had been given you from above" — Greek "exousian" / "anōthen" — KJV uses "power"; ESV uses "authority." "From above" (anōthen) — the same word used in John 3:3 for being "born from above." All authority is derived from God.

v.14 — KJV: "about the sixth hour" / ESV: "about the sixth hour" — Both identical. John's timing differs from Mark 15:25 ("the third hour"). This is one of the most discussed chronological questions in the Gospels. Possible explanations: different time-reckoning systems (Roman vs. Jewish), or John rounding differently.

v.17 — NET: "carrying his own cross he went out to the place
       called 'The Place of the Skull'" — "carrying his own cross"
       (bastazōn heautō ton stauron). WEB: "carrying his cross."
       John doesn't mention Simon of Cyrene — he emphasizes Jesus
       bearing His own burden. Theologicaly: no one else carries
       His cross for Him.

v.26 — NET: "Woman, look, here is your son! ... Look, here is
       your mother!" — Jesus provides for His mother from the cross.
       WEB: "Woman, behold, your son! ... Behold, your mother!"
       KJV: "Woman, behold thy son!" Even in agony, He fulfills
       the command to honor His mother.

v.30 — KJV: "It is finished: and he bowed his head, and gave up the ghost" / ESV: "It is finished, and he bowed his head and gave up his spirit" — Greek "tetelestai" / "paredōken to pneuma" — KJV uses "gave up the ghost" (archaic); ESV uses "gave up his spirit." He handed over His spirit — an act of will, not passive death.
  [ASV/NET/WEB]: NET: "It is completed!" — "completed" (tetelestai = paid
       in full, finished, accomplished). KJV: "It is finished."
       WEB: "It is finished!" A single Greek word — perfect tense.
       The work of redemption is not in progress but DONE. An
       accounting term: the debt is paid.

v.34 — KJV: "one of the soldiers with a spear pierced his side, and forthwith came there out blood and water" / ESV: "one of the soldiers pierced his side with a spear, and at once there came out blood and water" — Both equivalent. Medically significant — the blood and water indicate pericardial effusion, confirming death. Theologically: blood of atonement and water of cleansing.
  [ASV/NET/WEB]: NET: "one of the soldiers pierced his side with a spear,
       and blood and water flowed out immediately" — "blood and
       water" — medically consistent with pericardial effusion
       (confirming death). WEB: "blood and water came out." John
       emphasizes his eyewitness testimony (v.35). Luke the
       physician would appreciate this detail from the non-
       physician John.

v.39 — KJV: "about an hundred pound weight" / ESV: "about seventy-five pounds in weight" — Greek "litras hekaton" — KJV uses Roman pounds (100 litrai ≈ 75 modern pounds). ESV converts to modern measurement. An extravagant amount — fit for a king's burial.


GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Scourge       — A Roman flagellum — a whip with multiple leather thongs
               embedded with bone, metal, or glass. Designed to tear flesh.
               Many died from scourging alone.

Golgotha      — Aramaic for "place of a skull." Latin: Calvary. The site of
               the crucifixion, just outside Jerusalem's walls.

Tetelestai    — Greek: "It is finished" / "paid in full." A commercial term
               stamped on receipts when a debt was fully paid. Jesus' final
               declaration from the cross.

Hyssop        — A plant used in Jewish purification rituals (Exodus 12:22,
               Psalm 51:7). Used to lift the vinegar to Jesus' lips — a
               detail connecting His death to the Passover.

Gabbatha      — Aramaic for "elevated place" or "pavement." The stone
               pavement where Pilate's judgment seat was located.

Sepulchre     — A tomb, burial place. Joseph of Arimathea's new tomb, cut
               from rock, was used for Jesus' burial.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "It is finished" (v.30). The work of your salvation is complete — not
   in progress, not dependent on your performance. Jesus paid the full debt.
   Are you living in the freedom of a finished salvation, or are you still
   trying to add to what Christ has already completed?

2. Even while dying on the cross, Jesus made sure His mother was cared for
   (vv.26-27). In the middle of His greatest suffering, He was thinking of
   others. As a man, how do you care for the people who depend on you even
   when you are under pressure or in pain?

3. Joseph of Arimathea was a secret disciple "for fear of the Jews" (v.38).
   But at the moment of greatest risk — after the crucifixion, when following
   Jesus seemed most dangerous — he came forward. What is it that finally
   moves a man from secret faith to public faith? What is holding you back
   from being more open about your faith?

4. Nicodemus, who came to Jesus by night in chapter 3, now brings 100 pounds
   of spices to honor Jesus' body. His faith grew quietly but surely. How is
   your faith growing? Are you moving toward Christ or staying in the same
   place you were a year ago?

REFLECTION QUESTIONS
------------------------------------
1. Pilate declared Jesus innocent three times (18:38, 19:4, 19:6) and still
   condemned Him. He knew the truth and chose political safety. Where in your
   life are you choosing safety or comfort over what you know is right?

2. The soldiers cast lots for Jesus' garment while He hung dying above them
   (v.23-24). They were indifferent to the greatest event in human history.
   Is it possible to be physically present at religious events while being
   spiritually indifferent? How do you guard against that in your own life?

3. "Tetelestai — It is finished." What does it mean to you personally that
   your salvation is complete? How does a finished salvation change how you
   relate to God — in prayer, in guilt, in confidence?

4. Jesus was crucified between two criminals (v.18). He died in the place of
   sinners, surrounded by sinners, identified with sinners. What does it mean
   to you that the Son of God chose to be numbered with transgressors?

5. John stood at the cross (v.26) while most of the disciples had fled. What
   does it cost to stand with Christ when standing with Him is dangerous or
   unpopular? When have you stood, and when have you fled?
