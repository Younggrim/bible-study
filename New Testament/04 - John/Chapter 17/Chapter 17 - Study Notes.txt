John - Chapter 17
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Jesus prays His High Priestly Prayer — for His own glorification, for His disciples' sanctification and protection, and for all future believers' unity — "that they may be one, even as we are one."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: The Apostle John (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 17 is Jesus' High Priestly Prayer — the longest
recorded prayer of Jesus in Scripture, prayed on the night of His arrest. It
is sometimes called the "Lord's Prayer" in the truest sense — the prayer Jesus
Himself prayed, as opposed to the model prayer He gave His disciples (Matthew 6).

Structure: The prayer has three movements:
  (1) Jesus prays for Himself — for glorification (vv.1-5)
  (2) Jesus prays for His disciples — for protection, joy, and sanctification (vv.6-19)
  (3) Jesus prays for all future believers — for unity (vv.20-26)

This means Jesus prayed for you personally in this prayer (v.20). He prayed
for your unity with other believers, your protection from evil, and that you
would be with Him in glory.

Theological Significance: This chapter reveals the inner life of the Trinity —
the love between Father and Son before creation (v.24), the shared glory (v.5),
and the mission of the Son to make the Father known (v.26).

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- En route to Gethsemane: Jesus prayed this High Priestly Prayer on the night of His arrest — possibly while walking toward the Mount of Olives or already in the garden.
- "The world" and "out of the world" (vv.11,15): Jesus speaks of the world as the environment of the disciples' mission — they are sent INTO it but kept FROM its evil.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.3  — "And this is eternal life, that they know you, the only true God, and
        Jesus Christ whom you have sent." (ESV) — Eternal life defined not as
        a destination but as a relationship — knowing God.

v.15 — "I do not ask that you take them out of the world, but that you keep
        them from the evil one." (ESV) — In the world but not of it.

v.17 — "Sanctify them in the truth; your word is truth." (ESV) — The instrument
        of sanctification — God's Word.

v.20-21 — "I do not ask for these only, but also for those who will believe in
        me through their word, that they may all be one." (ESV) — Jesus
        prayed for you specifically.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.2  — KJV: "thou hast given him power over all flesh" / ESV: "you have given him authority over all flesh" — Greek "exousian" — KJV uses "power"; ESV uses "authority." "Authority" is more precise — it refers to delegated right to act, not merely raw power.

v.3  — KJV: "that they might know thee the only true God" / ESV: "that they know you, the only true God" — Greek "ginōskō" — Both equivalent. Experiential, relational knowledge, not merely intellectual knowledge. The same word used for intimate knowledge between husband and wife. Eternal life is knowing God personally, not knowing about Him.
  [ASV/NET/WEB]: NET: "Now this is eternal life — that they know you, the
       only true God, and Jesus Christ, whom you sent" — eternal
       life DEFINED as knowing God — relational, not merely
       durational. WEB: "This is eternal life, that they should
       know you." KJV: "this is life eternal, that they might
       know thee." Knowledge here is intimate, covenantal knowing.

v.5  — NET: "glorify me at your side with the glory I had with
       you before the world was created" — "before the world was
       created" — pre-existence claim. WEB: "with the glory which
       I had with you before the world existed." KJV: "before the
       world was." Jesus prays to resume eternal glory temporarily
       veiled in incarnation.

v.11 — KJV: "keep through thine own name those whom thou hast given me" / ESV: "keep them in your name, which you have given me" — Both equivalent. The Father's name is the sphere of protection. To be kept "in His name" means to be kept within the reality of who God is.
  [ASV/NET/WEB]: NET: "Holy Father, keep them safe in your name" — "keep
       them safe" (tērēson autous) — preservation/guarding. WEB:
       "keep them in your name." KJV: "keep through thine own name
       those whom thou hast given me." Protection through the
       power of God's name/character.

v.12 — KJV: "the son of perdition" / ESV: "the son of destruction" — Greek "apōleias" — KJV uses "perdition" (archaic); ESV uses "destruction." Both refer to Judas Iscariot — the only one of the twelve who was lost.

v.15 — KJV: "keep them from the evil" / ESV: "keep them from the evil one" — Greek "tou ponērou" — Can be translated "evil" (abstract) or "the evil one" (Satan). ESV specifies Satan; KJV leaves it ambiguous. Both are theologically valid.

v.17 — KJV: "Sanctify them through thy truth: thy word is truth" / ESV: "Sanctify them in the truth; your word is truth" — Greek "en tē alētheia" — KJV uses "through"; ESV uses "in." Both convey that the Word of God is the instrument of sanctification.
  [ASV/NET/WEB]: NET: "Set them apart in the truth; your word is truth" —
       "set apart" (hagiason = sanctify/consecrate). WEB: "Sanctify
       them in your truth." KJV: "Sanctify them through thy truth."
       Truth is the instrument of sanctification — God's word
       separates and consecrates.

v.21 — KJV: "that they all may be one; as thou, Father, art in me, and I in thee" / ESV: "that they may all be one, just as you, Father, are in me, and I in you" — Both equivalent. The unity Jesus prays for is modeled on the unity of the Trinity — not organizational uniformity but deep relational oneness rooted in shared life and love.
  [ASV/NET/WEB]: NET: "that they will all be one, just as you, Father, are
       in me and I am in you" — the unity Jesus prays for is
       modeled on Trinitarian unity. WEB: "that they may all be
       one." KJV: "That they all may be one." Not organizational
       uniformity but organic, relational oneness.

v.24 — KJV: "that they may behold my glory" / ESV: "to see my glory that you have given me" — Greek "theōrōsin" — Both equivalent. Jesus' ultimate desire for His people is that they see Him in His full glory — the glory He shared with the Father before creation.


GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Sanctify      — To set apart, make holy, consecrate. In v.17, it refers to
               the ongoing process of being made more like Christ through
               the truth of God's Word.

Glorify       — To display the full weight and worth of someone. Jesus asks
               the Father to glorify Him so that He can glorify the Father —
               the cross and resurrection as the ultimate display of God's glory.

Eternal life  — Defined in v.3 not as endless existence but as knowing God —
               a present, ongoing, relational experience that begins now and
               continues forever.

Son of perdition — Judas Iscariot. "Perdition" means destruction or ruin.
               The only one of the twelve who was lost — fulfilling Scripture
               (Psalm 41:9).

Manifest      — To make known, reveal, display. Jesus manifested the Father's
               name — made God's character and nature visible through His life.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "This is life eternal, that they might know thee" (v.3). Eternal life is
   not primarily about going to heaven — it is about knowing God. How would
   you describe your knowledge of God right now? Is it growing? Is it personal
   and relational, or mostly intellectual?

2. Jesus prayed for you in this prayer (v.20). Before you were born, He
   interceded for you. He is still interceding for you now (Hebrews 7:25).
   How does knowing that Jesus is actively praying for you right now change
   how you face your current struggles?

3. "I pray not that thou shouldest take them out of the world, but that thou
   shouldest keep them from the evil" (v.15). God's plan is not to remove
   you from difficulty but to protect you in it. Where are you asking God
   to remove you from a situation when He may be calling you to be kept
   in it for His purposes?

4. "Sanctify them through thy truth: thy word is truth" (v.17). The primary
   instrument of your growth in holiness is the Word of God. How much time
   are you spending in Scripture? Is it changing you — your thinking, your
   desires, your behavior?

REFLECTION QUESTIONS
------------------------------------
1. Jesus said "I have finished the work which thou gavest me to do" (v.4) —
   before the cross. He lived with such clarity of purpose that He could say
   His work was done. What is the work God has given you to do? Are you doing
   it, or are you distracted by things that aren't your assignment?

2. Jesus prayed that His joy would be fulfilled in His disciples (v.13). His
   joy — not a manufactured happiness but the deep joy of living in the Father's
   will. Is that the kind of joy you're experiencing? What is the difference
   between Christ's joy and the happiness the world offers?

3. Jesus prayed for the unity of all believers (vv.20-23) — and said that
   unity is the evidence to the world that God sent Him. How does division
   among Christians damage the witness of the Gospel? What is your role in
   pursuing unity with other believers, even those you disagree with?

4. "The love wherewith thou hast loved me may be in them" (v.26). The Father's
   love for the Son — the eternal, infinite love within the Trinity — is the
   same love He has for you. Does that feel real to you? What would it look
   like to live from that love rather than constantly seeking love and approval
   from other sources?

5. Jesus prayed "that they may behold my glory" (v.24) — His ultimate desire
   for His people is that they see Him in His full glory. What does it mean
   to you that the goal of your entire life is to see and know Jesus more
   fully — and that this will be completed in eternity?
