Matthew - Chapter 28
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Christ is risen — the angel announces the resurrection to the women at the empty tomb, Jesus appears to His disciples in Galilee, and commissions them to make disciples of all nations, promising "I am with you always, even unto the end of the world."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Matthew (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 28 records the resurrection of Jesus and the Great
Commission — the climax and conclusion of Matthew's Gospel. It is the shortest
chapter in Matthew but contains the most important event in human history and
the most important command ever given.

The Resurrection: Early Sunday morning (the first day of the week), the women
came to the tomb and found it empty. An angel announced "He is not here: for
he is risen, as he said" (v.6). The resurrection is the vindication of
everything Jesus claimed — His deity, His authority, His power over death, and
the sufficiency of His sacrifice.

The Guard's Report (vv.11-15): The religious leaders bribed the soldiers to say
the disciples stole the body. This is actually evidence FOR the resurrection —
even the enemies of Jesus admitted the tomb was empty. They couldn't deny it;
they could only try to explain it away.

The Great Commission (vv.18-20): Jesus' final words in Matthew — the marching
orders for the church until He returns. It contains: (1) the authority — "All
power is given unto me," (2) the command — "Go... teach all nations," (3) the
method — "baptizing... teaching," and (4) the promise — "I am with you alway."

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- The tomb (vv.1-6): The rock-hewn tomb near Golgotha in Jerusalem. The women came at dawn and found it empty.
- Galilee (v.16): Jesus instructed the disciples to meet Him in Galilee after the resurrection. The Great Commission was given on a mountain in Galilee.
- "All nations" (v.19): The geographic scope of the commission — from Jerusalem to the ends of the earth. The Gospel that began in a small corner of the Roman Empire is to reach every nation.

  unstoppable."

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "He is not here: for he is risen" (v.6). The resurrection is not a nice
   idea — it is a historical fact that changes everything. If Jesus is risen,
   then death is defeated, sin is paid for, and every promise He made is
   guaranteed. Do you live like the resurrection is true?

2. "All power is given unto me" (v.18). The One who sends you has all
   authority in heaven and on earth. You are not going in your own strength
   or authority. How does knowing that Jesus has ALL authority change how you
   approach the mission He's given you?

3. "Go ye therefore, and teach all nations" (v.19). This is not optional —
   it is a command. Every believer is commissioned. What does "going" look
   like in your specific context — your workplace, your neighborhood, your
   relationships?

4. "I am with you alway, even unto the end of the world" (v.20). The last
   words of Matthew's Gospel — a promise of permanent presence. You are never
   alone. In your hardest moments, your loneliest seasons, your most
   challenging assignments — He is with you. Do you believe that?

REFLECTION QUESTIONS
------------------------------------
1. The women came to the tomb expecting a dead body (v.1). They found an
   empty tomb and a risen Lord. Where in your life are you expecting death
   when God may be preparing resurrection? What situation have you given up
   on that God might still be working in?

2. The guards were bribed to lie (vv.12-15). The enemies of Jesus couldn't
   deny the empty tomb — they could only try to explain it away. What does
   the fact that even His enemies admitted the tomb was empty tell you about
   the strength of the resurrection evidence?

3. "Some doubted" (v.17) — even in the presence of the risen Christ. Jesus
   didn't wait for everyone to have perfect faith before giving the commission.
   He works through imperfect, doubting people. How does that encourage you
   in your own moments of uncertainty?

4. The Great Commission has four elements: authority (v.18), command (v.19a),
   method (v.19b-20a), and promise (v.20b). Which of these do you most need
   to hear right now? Which one are you neglecting?

5. Matthew's Gospel begins with "Emmanuel — God with us" (1:23) and ends with
   "I am with you always" (28:20). The entire story is about God's desire to
   be present with His people. How does the reality of God's constant presence
   shape your daily life — your decisions, your courage, your peace?
