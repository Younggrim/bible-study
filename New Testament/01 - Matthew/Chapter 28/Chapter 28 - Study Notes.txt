Matthew - Chapter 28
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Christ is risen — the angel announces the resurrection to the women at the empty tomb, Jesus appears to His disciples in Galilee, and commissions them to make disciples of all nations, promising "I am with you always, even unto the end of the world."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Matthew (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 28 records the resurrection of Jesus and the Great
Commission — the climax and conclusion of Matthew's Gospel. It is the shortest
chapter in Matthew but contains the most important event in human history and
the most important command ever given.

The Resurrection: Early Sunday morning (the first day of the week), the women
came to the tomb and found it empty. An angel announced "He is not here: for
he is risen, as he said" (v.6). The resurrection is the vindication of
everything Jesus claimed — His deity, His authority, His power over death, and
the sufficiency of His sacrifice.

The Guard's Report (vv.11-15): The religious leaders bribed the soldiers to say
the disciples stole the body. This is actually evidence FOR the resurrection —
even the enemies of Jesus admitted the tomb was empty. They couldn't deny it;
they could only try to explain it away.

The Great Commission (vv.18-20): Jesus' final words in Matthew — the marching
orders for the church until He returns. It contains: (1) the authority — "All
power is given unto me," (2) the command — "Go... teach all nations," (3) the
method — "baptizing... teaching," and (4) the promise — "I am with you alway."

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- The tomb (vv.1-6): The rock-hewn tomb near Golgotha in Jerusalem. The women came at dawn and found it empty.
- Galilee (v.16): Jesus instructed the disciples to meet Him in Galilee after the resurrection. The Great Commission was given on a mountain in Galilee.
- "All nations" (v.19): The geographic scope of the commission — from Jerusalem to the ends of the earth. The Gospel that began in a small corner of the Roman Empire is to reach every nation.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.6  — "He is not here, for he has risen, as he said." (ESV) — The
        announcement that changed everything. The tomb is empty. Death is
        defeated.

v.18 — "All authority in heaven and on earth has been given to me." (ESV) —
        The authority behind the commission — Jesus has all authority,
        everywhere.

v.19 — "Go therefore and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the
        name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit." (ESV) —
        The Great Commission — the church's mission until Christ returns.

v.20 — "And behold, I am with you always, to the end of the age." (ESV) —
        The promise that sustains the mission — Christ's permanent presence.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1  — KJV: "In the end of the sabbath, as it began to dawn toward the first
       day of the week" — ESV: "After the Sabbath, toward the dawn of the
       first day of the week" — The Greek is slightly ambiguous but all
       translations agree: early Sunday morning.

v.17 — KJV: "but some doubted" — ESV: "but some doubted" — Greek "edistasan"
       — to hesitate, to be uncertain. Even in the presence of the risen
       Christ, some hesitated. Jesus gave the commission anyway — to doubters
       and worshippers alike.

v.19 — KJV: "teach all nations" — ESV: "make disciples of all nations" —
       Greek "mathēteusate" — to make disciples, not merely to teach
       information. The command is to produce followers of Christ, not just
       informed people.

v.19 — KJV: "in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy
       Ghost" / ESV: "in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the
       Holy Spirit" — "Name" is singular (not "names") — one God in three
       persons. The Trinitarian formula in baptism reflects the Trinitarian
       nature of God.

v.20 — KJV: "the end of the world" — ESV: "the end of the age" — Greek
       "sunteleia tou aiōnos" — the consummation of the age. Christ's
       presence with His church is guaranteed until He returns.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.2  — NET: "there was a violent earthquake, for an angel of
       the Lord descending from heaven came and rolled away the
       stone" — "violent earthquake" (seismos megas). WEB: "a
       great earthquake." The same creation that groaned at His
       death shakes at His rising.

v.6  — NET: "He is not here, for he has been raised, just as he
       said" — "has been raised" (EgerthE — passive: God raised
       Him). WEB: "He is not here, for he has risen." KJV: "He is
       not here: for he is risen." The angel's announcement —
       the tomb is evidence, not residence.

v.18 — NET: "All authority in heaven and on earth has been given
       to me" — "all authority" (pasa exousia = every kind of
       power, in every realm). WEB: "All authority has been given
       to me." KJV: "All power is given unto me." The universal
       scope grounds the universal commission.

v.19 — NET: "Therefore go and make disciples of all nations,
       baptizing them in the name of the Father and the Son and
       the Holy Spirit" — "make disciples" (mathEteusate = the
       main imperative — going, baptizing, teaching are participles
       supporting it). WEB: "Go and make disciples of all nations."
       The single command is discipling; the method is going,
       baptizing, teaching.

v.20 — NET: "I am with you always, to the end of the age" —
       "always" (pasas tas hEmeras = all the days). WEB: "I am
       with you always, even to the end of the age." KJV: "I am
       with you alway, even unto the end of the world." Emmanuel
       ("God with us," 1:23) bookends the Gospel — He who was
       WITH us at birth is WITH us until the end.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Resurrection  — The bodily rising of Jesus from the dead on the third day.
               Not a spiritual metaphor but a physical, historical event —
               the same body that was crucified was raised, transformed and
               glorified.

Great Commission — Jesus' final command to His disciples (vv.18-20) — the
               church's mission to make disciples of all nations through
               going, baptizing, and teaching.

Baptism       — Immersion in water in the name of the Father, Son, and Holy
               Spirit — the public declaration of faith and identification
               with Christ's death, burial, and resurrection.

Disciples     — Greek "mathētai" — learners, followers, apprentices. The
               Great Commission is to make disciples — not just converts
               but lifelong followers who obey everything Jesus commanded.

All nations   — Greek "panta ta ethnē" — all the ethnic groups/peoples of
               the world. The Gospel is for every tribe, tongue, and nation
               — not just Israel.

Emmanuel      — "God with us" (Matthew 1:23). The Gospel begins with the
               promise of God's presence (1:23) and ends with it (28:20) —
               forming an inclusio that frames the entire book.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "He is not here: for he is risen" (v.6). The resurrection is not a nice
   idea — it is a historical fact that changes everything. If Jesus is risen,
   then death is defeated, sin is paid for, and every promise He made is
   guaranteed. Do you live like the resurrection is true?

2. "All power is given unto me" (v.18). The One who sends you has all
   authority in heaven and on earth. You are not going in your own strength
   or authority. How does knowing that Jesus has ALL authority change how you
   approach the mission He's given you?

3. "Go ye therefore, and teach all nations" (v.19). This is not optional —
   it is a command. Every believer is commissioned. What does "going" look
   like in your specific context — your workplace, your neighborhood, your
   relationships?

4. "I am with you alway, even unto the end of the world" (v.20). The last
   words of Matthew's Gospel — a promise of permanent presence. You are never
   alone. In your hardest moments, your loneliest seasons, your most
   challenging assignments — He is with you. Do you believe that?

REFLECTION QUESTIONS
------------------------------------
1. The women came to the tomb expecting a dead body (v.1). They found an
   empty tomb and a risen Lord. Where in your life are you expecting death
   when God may be preparing resurrection? What situation have you given up
   on that God might still be working in?

2. The guards were bribed to lie (vv.12-15). The enemies of Jesus couldn't
   deny the empty tomb — they could only try to explain it away. What does
   the fact that even His enemies admitted the tomb was empty tell you about
   the strength of the resurrection evidence?

3. "Some doubted" (v.17) — even in the presence of the risen Christ. Jesus
   didn't wait for everyone to have perfect faith before giving the commission.
   He works through imperfect, doubting people. How does that encourage you
   in your own moments of uncertainty?

4. The Great Commission has four elements: authority (v.18), command (v.19a),
   method (v.19b-20a), and promise (v.20b). Which of these do you most need
   to hear right now? Which one are you neglecting?

5. Matthew's Gospel begins with "Emmanuel — God with us" (1:23) and ends with
   "I am with you always" (28:20). The entire story is about God's desire to
   be present with His people. How does the reality of God's constant presence
   shape your daily life — your decisions, your courage, your peace?
