Matthew - Chapter 4
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Jesus defeats Satan's three temptations in the wilderness with Scripture, begins His public ministry in Galilee proclaiming "Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is at hand," and calls His first disciples.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Matthew (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 4 records the temptation of Jesus in the wilderness,
the beginning of His Galilean ministry, and the calling of the first four disciples.
The temptation follows immediately after the baptism — the Spirit leads Jesus
into the wilderness specifically to be tested (v.1). This is not an accident
but a divine appointment.

The Temptation: Jesus fasted 40 days — echoing Israel's 40 years in the
wilderness. Where Israel failed every test, Jesus passed every one. He is the
true Israel, the obedient Son who does what Israel could not. Every temptation
was answered with Scripture from Deuteronomy — the book Moses wrote to prepare
Israel for the Promised Land.

The Three Temptations follow a pattern identified in 1 John 2:16:
  (1) Stones to bread — the lust of the flesh (physical appetite)
  (2) Jump from the Temple — the pride of life (testing God, seeking spectacle)
  (3) Kingdoms of the world — the lust of the eyes (power and possession)

Galilee of the Gentiles (v.15): Jesus based His ministry in Capernaum, in the
region of Galilee — a mixed Jewish-Gentile area looked down upon by Jerusalem
elites. This fulfilled Isaiah 9:1-2 and signaled the Gospel's reach to all people.

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- Judean Wilderness: The barren, desolate region where Jesus was tempted for 40 days. Traditionally located west of Jericho.
- Pinnacle of the Temple: The southeastern corner of the Temple complex in Jerusalem, overlooking the Kidron Valley — a dramatic drop.
- Capernaum: A fishing town on the northwest shore of the Sea of Galilee. Jesus made this His ministry headquarters ("His own city," Matt. 9:1).
- Galilee, Decapolis, Jerusalem, Judea, beyond the Jordan (v.25): The regions from which crowds gathered, showing the breadth of Jesus' early fame.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.4  — "It is written, 'Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word
        that comes from the mouth of God.'" (ESV) — Jesus' weapon against
        temptation: Scripture. The same weapon available to every believer.

v.10 — "Be gone, Satan! For it is written, 'You shall worship the Lord your
        God and him only shall you serve.'" (ESV) — Worship is the ultimate
        battleground. Satan wants what belongs to God alone.

v.17 — "Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is at hand." (ESV) — Jesus begins
        His ministry with the same message as John — repentance.

v.19 — "Follow me, and I will make you fishers of men." (ESV) — The call of
        Christ — simple, direct, transforming.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1  — KJV: "led up of the Spirit into the wilderness to be tempted" — ESV:
       "led up by the Spirit into the wilderness to be tempted" — The Spirit
       led Jesus into temptation — not to cause Him to sin but to test and
       prove His obedience. God tests; Satan tempts. Both are present here.
       NET: "Then Jesus was led by the Spirit into the
       wilderness to be tempted by the devil" — "led by the Spirit"
       (anEchthE hypo tou pneumatos) — the Spirit deliberately
       takes Jesus into testing. WEB: "led up by the Spirit."
       God initiates the confrontation with evil.

v.3  — KJV: "If thou be the Son of God" — ESV: "If you are the Son of God"
       — The Greek "ei" with indicative mood means "since you are" — Satan
       is not questioning Jesus' identity but using it as leverage. "Since
       you're the Son of God, use your power for yourself."

v.4  — NET: "It is written: 'Man does not live by bread alone,
       but by every word that comes from the mouth of God'" —
       quoting Deuteronomy 8:3. WEB: "Man shall not live by bread
       alone." Jesus defeats each temptation with "It is written"
       — Scripture as the weapon against Satan.

v.10 — NET: "Go away, Satan! For it is written: 'You are to
       worship the Lord your God and serve only him'" — "Go away"
       (hypage = be gone/get behind). WEB: "Get behind me, Satan!"
       KJV: "Get thee hence, Satan." A direct command to the devil
       — Jesus exercises authority over the tempter.

v.11 — KJV: "angels came and ministered unto him" / ESV: "angels came and
       were ministering to him" — ESV uses the progressive "were ministering"
       (ongoing care). After the battle, God provided. The wilderness was
       followed by angelic care. Obedience through testing is always followed
       by God's provision.

v.17 — NET: "From that time Jesus began to preach this message:
       'Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is near'" — the same
       message as John the Baptist (3:2). WEB: "Repent! For the
       Kingdom of Heaven is at hand." Continuity between the
       forerunner and the King — identical call.

v.19 — KJV: "fishers of men" / ESV: "fishers of men" — Both identical. Greek
       "halieis anthrōpōn." A play on their occupation. Jesus meets people
       where they are and calls them to use their existing skills for His
       kingdom purposes.
       NET: "Follow me, and I will turn you into fishers of
       people" — "fishers of people" (halieis anthrOpOn). WEB:
       "fishers for men." KJV: "fishers of men." NET uses inclusive
       "people"; the vocation changes from catching fish (for death)
       to catching humans (for life).

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Tempted       — Greek "peirazō" — to test, try, prove. Used both for God
               testing (proving) and Satan tempting (trying to cause failure).
               The same word, different purposes.

Pinnacle      — The highest point of the Temple — likely the southeast corner
               overlooking the Kidron Valley, a drop of hundreds of feet.

Wilderness    — The Judean wilderness — a barren, rocky desert east of
               Jerusalem toward the Dead Sea. Jesus spent 40 days here alone.

Capernaum     — A fishing village on the northwest shore of the Sea of Galilee.
               Jesus made it His base of operations for His Galilean ministry.

Decapolis     — "Ten cities" — a league of ten Greco-Roman cities east of the
               Jordan River. Largely Gentile population. Jesus' fame spread
               even there (v.25).

Fishers of men — Jesus' metaphor for evangelism — drawing people into the
               kingdom as fishermen draw fish into nets.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. Jesus answered every temptation with "It is written" — Scripture. Not
   feelings, not logic, not willpower — the Word of God. How well do you
   know Scripture well enough to use it when temptation comes? What specific
   verses are you memorizing to fight specific temptations in your life?

2. Satan offered Jesus "all the kingdoms of the world" (v.8-9) — a shortcut
   to what was already His by right, without the cross. The enemy often offers
   us shortcuts to legitimate things through illegitimate means. Where in your
   life are you being offered a shortcut that bypasses God's path?

3. Jesus began His ministry in Galilee — the despised region, the margins.
   He didn't start in Jerusalem with the religious establishment. Where is
   God calling you to minister that feels beneath you or outside your comfort
   zone?

4. Peter, Andrew, James, and John left their nets and their father immediately
   (vv.20,22). No negotiation, no delay. What would you have to leave to
   follow Jesus more fully? What nets are you still holding onto?

REFLECTION QUESTIONS
------------------------------------
1. The Spirit led Jesus into the wilderness to be tempted (v.1). God sometimes
   leads us into difficult seasons deliberately — not to harm us but to prove
   and strengthen our faith. Can you identify a wilderness season in your life
   that God used to deepen your character? What did you learn?

2. Satan used Scripture to tempt Jesus (v.6, quoting Psalm 91). The Bible can
   be misused to justify almost anything. How do you guard against using
   Scripture to rationalize what you want to do rather than to discover what
   God wants?

3. Jesus refused to turn stones to bread even though He was genuinely hungry
   and had the power to do it. He chose to live within human limitations by
   faith. Where in your life are you tempted to use your abilities, position,
   or resources to bypass trust in God?

4. "Follow me, and I will make you fishers of men" (v.19). The disciples left
   everything immediately. What does radical, immediate obedience to Christ's
   call look like in your life right now? Is there a call you've been delaying?

5. Jesus' fame spread throughout Syria and beyond (v.24) — and He hadn't even
   preached the Sermon on the Mount yet. It was His healing ministry that drew
   the crowds. How does meeting people's practical needs open doors for the
   Gospel in your own context?
