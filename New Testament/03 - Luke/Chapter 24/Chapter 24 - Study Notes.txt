Luke - Chapter 24
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Women find the empty tomb, Jesus appears to two disciples on the Emmaus road opening the Scriptures, reveals Himself to the eleven, commissions them as witnesses, and ascends into heaven — "beginning from Jerusalem."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Luke (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 24 is the final chapter of Luke's Gospel — recording
the resurrection, the Emmaus road encounter, Jesus' appearance to the disciples,
and the ascension. Luke's resurrection account emphasizes the physical reality
of the risen Christ (He eats fish, shows His wounds) and the opening of the
disciples' understanding of Scripture.

The Emmaus Road (vv.13-35): Unique to Luke and one of the most beautiful
narratives in the Gospels. Two disciples walking away from Jerusalem in despair
are joined by the risen Jesus — whom they don't recognize. He walks with them,
opens the Scriptures to them, and is finally recognized "in the breaking of
bread" (v.35). Then He vanishes. Their response: "Did not our heart burn within
us, while he talked with us by the way?"

The Physical Resurrection (vv.36-43): Jesus appears to the gathered disciples,
shows them His hands and feet, and eats broiled fish. This is not a ghost or
a vision — it is a physical, bodily resurrection. Luke emphasizes this against
any spiritualizing of the resurrection.

The Great Commission in Luke (vv.46-49): "Repentance and remission of sins
should be preached in his name among all nations, beginning at Jerusalem. And
ye are witnesses of these things." The mission: preach repentance and forgiveness
to all nations. The power: "endued with power from on high" (the Holy Spirit,
fulfilled at Pentecost).

MAP & GEOGRAPHY NOTES
------------------------------------
- The tomb (vv.1-12): The rock-hewn tomb near Golgotha in Jerusalem where the women found the stone rolled away.
- The road to Emmaus (v.13): A village about 7 miles from Jerusalem. Two disciples walked this road on resurrection afternoon when Jesus appeared to them.
- Jerusalem (v.33): The disciples returned from Emmaus to Jerusalem where Jesus appeared to the gathered eleven.
- Bethany (v.50): Jesus led them out to Bethany on the Mount of Olives, where He ascended into heaven.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.5-6 — "Why do you seek the living among the dead? He is not here, but has
        risen." (ESV) — The resurrection announcement.

v.27 — "And beginning with Moses and all the Prophets, he interpreted to them
        in all the Scriptures the things concerning himself." (ESV) — All
        Scripture points to Christ.

v.32 — "Did not our hearts burn within us while he talked to us on the road,
        while he opened to us the Scriptures?" (ESV) — The effect of Christ
        opening Scripture: burning hearts.

v.45 — "Then he opened their minds to understand the Scriptures." (ESV) —
        Understanding Scripture requires divine illumination.

v.46-47 — "Thus it is written, that the Christ should suffer and on the third
        day rise from the dead, and that repentance for the forgiveness of
        sins should be proclaimed in his name to all nations." (ESV) — The
        Gospel summarized: Christ's death, resurrection, and the call to
        repentance for all nations.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.5  — KJV: "Why seek ye the living among the dead?" / ESV: "Why do you seek
       the living among the dead?" — Both equivalent. One of the most
       memorable questions in Scripture. The angels redirect the women's
       search — Jesus is not among the dead; He is alive.

v.12 — KJV includes this verse / ESV includes it with a footnote noting it
       is absent from some manuscripts. Most scholars include it — it is
       consistent with John 20:3-8 and fits Luke's narrative.

v.27 — KJV: "beginning at Moses and all the prophets" / ESV: "beginning
       with Moses and all the Prophets" — Both equivalent. Jesus gave the
       first complete Christological interpretation of the Old Testament.
       Every section of Scripture — Law, Prophets, Psalms (v.44) — points
       to Him.

v.32 — KJV: "Did not our heart burn within us" / ESV: "Did not our hearts
       burn within us" — Both equivalent. The "burning heart" is the
       experience of the Spirit illuminating Scripture. It is not mere
       intellectual understanding but a deep, warming, convicting encounter
       with truth.

v.51 — KJV: "carried up into heaven" / ESV: "was carried up into heaven" —
       Both equivalent. Some manuscripts omit this phrase (ESV footnote).
       Acts 1:9-11 provides the fuller ascension account. Luke's Gospel ends
       with worship and joy — the proper response to the risen, ascended
       Christ.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.5  — NET: "Why do you look for the living among the dead?" —
       a gentle rebuke unique to Luke. WEB: "Why do you seek the
       living among the dead?" KJV: "Why seek ye the living among
       the dead?" The question reframes everything — He was never
       going to stay there.

v.16 — NET: "their eyes were kept from recognizing him" — divine
       passive: God prevented their recognition. WEB: "their eyes
       were kept from recognizing him." KJV: "their eyes were
       holden that they should not know him." The concealment is
       purposeful — so the Scriptures can do the revealing.

v.27 — NET: "beginning from Moses and all the prophets, he
       interpreted for them the things written about himself in all
       the scriptures" — "interpreted" (diermēneusen = explained
       thoroughly). WEB: "beginning from Moses and from all the
       prophets, he explained to them." KJV: "he expounded."
       Christ is the subject of all Scripture.

v.32 — NET: "Were not our hearts burning within us while he was
       speaking with us on the road, while he was explaining the
       scriptures to us?" — "burning" (kaiomenē). WEB: "our heart
       burning within us." KJV: "Did not our heart burn within us?"
       Scripture ignites the heart when Christ opens it.

v.45 — NET: "Then he opened their minds so they could understand
       the scriptures" — "opened their minds" (diēnoixen ton noun)
       — only God can do this. WEB: "Then he opened their minds,
       that they might understand the Scriptures." Understanding
       Scripture requires divine illumination, not just human
       intelligence.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Emmaus        — A village about 7 miles (60 furlongs) from Jerusalem. Its
               exact location is debated. The site of the risen Jesus'
               appearance to two disciples.

Breaking of bread — The act of sharing a meal — specifically the blessing
               and breaking of bread. The disciples recognized Jesus in this
               familiar act (v.35). Also connected to the Lord's Supper.

Burning heart — The inner experience of the Spirit illuminating Scripture —
               a deep warming, conviction, and joy that accompanies genuine
               encounter with Christ through His Word.

Opened their understanding — Divine illumination — God enabling the mind to
               comprehend what was previously hidden. Understanding Scripture
               requires the Spirit's work (v.45).

Power from on high — The Holy Spirit — promised by Jesus and fulfilled at
               Pentecost (Acts 2). The disciples were to wait for this
               empowerment before beginning their mission.

Ascension     — Jesus' bodily departure from earth to heaven — His return to
               the Father's right hand. The conclusion of His earthly ministry
               and the beginning of His heavenly intercession.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Why seek ye the living among the dead?" (v.5). Are you looking for Jesus
   in dead places — dead religion, dead habits, dead traditions? He is alive
   and present. Where do you need to redirect your search?

2. The Emmaus disciples were walking away from Jerusalem in despair — and Jesus
   joined them on the road (v.15). He meets you in your disappointment, your
   confusion, your walking away. He doesn't wait for you to come back; He
   comes to you. Where is He walking with you right now that you haven't
   recognized?

3. "Did not our heart burn within us, while he opened to us the scriptures?"
   (v.32). When did your heart last burn while reading the Bible? If it's been
   a while, ask Christ to open the Scriptures to you again. Ask for burning.

4. Jesus "opened their understanding, that they might understand the scriptures"
   (v.45). You cannot understand the Bible fully without the Spirit's help.
   Do you pray before you read? Do you ask God to open your understanding?

REFLECTION QUESTIONS
------------------------------------
1. The women came to the tomb expecting a dead body (v.1). They found an empty
   tomb and a living Lord. What expectations about God do you need to let go
   of? Where is He doing something you didn't expect?

2. Jesus walked with the Emmaus disciples for hours before they recognized Him
   (v.16). Sometimes Christ is present in our lives and we don't see Him. What
   might be preventing you from recognizing His presence right now?

3. "Beginning at Moses and all the prophets, he expounded unto them in all the
   scriptures the things concerning himself" (v.27). The entire Old Testament
   points to Jesus. How does reading the OT with Christ as the center change
   how you understand it?

4. Jesus ate fish to prove He was physically risen (vv.42-43). The resurrection
   is not a metaphor — it is a physical, historical event. How does the bodily
   resurrection of Christ affect your hope for your own future resurrection?

5. "Ye are witnesses of these things" (v.48). You are a witness — not just a
   believer but a testifier. What have you witnessed of Christ's work in your
   life? Who needs to hear your testimony?
