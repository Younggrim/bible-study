James - Chapter 2
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
James condemns showing favoritism to the rich over the poor, declares that faith without works is dead — "as the body without the spirit is dead, so faith without works is dead also" — using Abraham and Rahab as examples of active faith.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: James, the brother of Jesus (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Historical Context: Chapter 2 addresses two related issues: favoritism in the
church (vv.1-13) and the relationship between faith and works (vv.14-26). The
early church included both rich and poor, and the temptation to favor the
wealthy was real. James condemns this as incompatible with faith in Christ.
He then tackles the most debated question in James: Can faith without works
save? His answer: No. Genuine faith always produces works. Faith and works are
not opposed — they are inseparable.

Favoritism Condemned (vv.1-13): Don't show partiality based on wealth (vv.1-4).
God chose the poor to be rich in faith (v.5). The rich often oppress believers
(vv.6-7). The "royal law" — love your neighbor — condemns favoritism (v.8).
Partiality is sin (v.9). Breaking one point of the Law makes you a lawbreaker
(vv.10-11). Live as those judged by "the law of liberty" (v.12). "Mercy
rejoiceth against judgment" (v.13).

Faith and Works (vv.14-26): Can faith without works save? (v.14). Faith that
doesn't help the needy is useless (vv.15-16). "Faith, if it hath not works,
is dead" (v.17). Show me your faith BY your works (v.18). Even demons believe
— and tremble (v.19). Abraham was "justified by works" when he offered Isaac
(vv.21-23). Rahab was justified by works (v.25). "Faith without works is dead"
(v.26).

Note: James does not contradict Paul. Paul speaks of justification BEFORE GOD
(declared righteous by faith alone, Rom 3:28). James speaks of justification
BEFORE MEN (demonstrated righteous by works, v.18). Paul addresses how we are
saved; James addresses how we know we are saved.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1 — "My brothers, show no partiality as you hold the faith in our Lord
 Jesus Christ, the Lord of glory." (ESV) — Faith and favoritism are
 incompatible.

v.8 — "You shall love your neighbor as yourself." (ESV) — The royal law.

v.13 — "Mercy triumphs over judgment." (ESV) — Mercy triumphs over judgment.

v.17 — "So also faith by itself, if it does not have works, is dead." (ESV)
 — Dead faith: faith without evidence.

v.18 — "Show me your faith apart from your works, and I will show you my
 faith by my works." (ESV) — Works as faith's evidence.

v.19 — "Even the demons believe—and shudder!" (ESV) — Intellectual belief
 without transformation is demonic, not saving.

v.22 — "Faith was active along with his works, and faith was completed by
 his works." (ESV) — Faith and works cooperating.

v.26 — "For as the body apart from the spirit is dead, so also faith apart
 from works is dead." (ESV) — The definitive statement.

Charles Spurgeon:
 Sermon: "Faith and Works" on vv.17-18 (MTP Vol. 18, No. 1078)
 "Faith without works is dead — not weak, not sick, not sleeping — DEAD.
 A dead faith is no faith at all. It is a corpse of faith — it has the form
 but not the life. How do you know faith is alive? By its works. As a tree
 is known by its fruit, faith is known by its works. James does not say
 works save you — he says works PROVE you are saved. Show me your faith BY
 your works. Faith that doesn't work doesn't exist."
 Sermon: "Mercy Rejoiceth Against Judgment" on v.13 (MTP Vol. 11, No. 624)

 that mercy is greater. The merciful man will receive mercy. The unmerciful
 man will receive strict judgment. Show mercy — and mercy will triumph over
 the judgment you deserve."

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "respect of persons" — Greek "prosōpolēmpsia" — face-receiving,
 judging by external appearance. Favoritism based on outward status.
       NET: "do not show prejudice" — "prejudice"
       (prosOpolEmpsiais = face-receiving/favoritism). WEB: "don't
       show partiality." KJV: "have not the faith... with respect
       of persons." Faith in Jesus and favoritism are incompatible
       — you cannot hold both simultaneously.

v.2 — KJV: "assembly" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "sunagōgēn" — synagogue. James uses the
 Jewish term for their gathering place, reflecting the early Jewish-
 Christian context.

v.8 — KJV: "royal law" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "nomon basilikon" — the king's law, the
 supreme law. Love of neighbor is the law that rules over all other
 laws.

v.14 — KJV: "can faith save him?" / ESV: "faith save" — Greek "mē dunatai hē pistis sōsai
 auton?" — The Greek expects a negative answer: "That kind of faith
 can't save him, can it?" The article "hē" (THE faith) points to the
 specific faith just described — faith without works.
       NET: "What good is it, my brothers and sisters, if someone
       claims to have faith but does not have works?" — "claims"
       (legE = says). WEB: "if a man says he has faith." KJV: "if
       a man say he hath faith." The emphasis: SAYS vs. SHOWS.
       Verbal faith without visible works is suspect.

v.19 — KJV: "the devils also believe" — Demons have correct theology — they
 believe God is one. But their belief produces trembling, not
 obedience. Intellectual assent without life change is not saving faith.
       NET: "You believe that God is one; well and good. Even the
       demons believe that — and tremble with fear" — "tremble"
       (phrissousin = shudder/have hair stand on end). WEB: "the
       demons also believe, and shudder." Even demons have correct
       theology — belief alone is not saving faith.

v.21 — KJV: "justified by works" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "edikaiōthē ex ergōn" — declared
 righteous from/by works. James uses "justified" in the sense of
 "demonstrated to be righteous" (before men), not "declared righteous"
 (before God, as Paul uses it).

v.22 — KJV: "faith wrought with his works" / ESV: "works, and faith" — Greek "sunērgei" — worked
 together with, cooperated. Faith and works are partners, not enemies.
 "Made perfect" — Greek "eteleiōthē" — brought to completion. Works
 complete/perfect faith by expressing it.

v.23 — NET: "Abraham believed God and it was credited to him as
       righteousness" — quoting Genesis 15:6 (same text Paul uses
       in Romans 4). WEB: "it was accounted to him as righteousness."
       James and Paul are not contradicting but complementing — Paul
       addresses the ground of justification; James addresses its
       evidence.

v.26 — KJV: "the body without the spirit is dead" / ESV: "body apart from the spirit is dead" — The analogy: as a body
 without breath/spirit is a corpse, faith without works is a corpse.
 Works are to faith what breath is to the body — the evidence of life.
       NET: "as the body without the spirit is dead, so also
       faith without works is dead" — "without the spirit" (chOris
       pneumatos = apart from breath/spirit). WEB: "faith apart
       from works is dead." An analogy: works are to faith what
       breath is to the body — the evidence of life, not its cause.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Respect of persons — Favoritism, partiality. Judging people by external
 appearance (wealth, status) rather than character.

Royal law — "Love your neighbor as yourself." The supreme law that
 governs all other commands.

Mercy rejoiceth against judgment — Mercy triumphs over strict justice. The
 merciful receive mercy; the unmerciful face judgment.

Faith without works is dead — Faith that produces no visible change is not
 genuine faith. It is a corpse — the form without the life.

Justified by works — Demonstrated to be righteous through visible actions.
 Not how we are saved but how we prove we are saved.

Devils believe — Intellectual assent to truth without life transformation.
 Correct theology without obedience is demonic, not saving.

Faith wrought with works — Faith and works cooperating as partners. Works
 complete faith by expressing it visibly.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Have not the faith... with respect of persons" (v.1). Do you treat people
 differently based on their wealth, appearance, or status? How would Jesus
 treat the poorly dressed person who walks into your church?

2. "Mercy rejoiceth against judgment" (v.13). Are you merciful toward others?
 Or do you demand strict justice for everyone except yourself? The measure
 of mercy you give is the measure you'll receive.

3. "Faith, if it hath not works, is dead" (v.17). Examine your faith: what
 visible evidence does it produce? If someone looked at your life, would
 they see living faith or dead religion?

4. "The devils also believe, and tremble" (v.19). Is your faith merely
 intellectual agreement with facts about God? Or does it produce obedience,
 love, and transformation? What's the difference between your faith and a
 demon's?

5. "Shew me thy faith by thy works" (v.18). If someone asked you to prove
 your faith, what would you point to? What works demonstrate that your
 faith is alive?
