James - Chapter 4
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
James exposes the source of quarrels (unfulfilled desires), calls worldliness spiritual adultery, commands "submit yourselves to God, resist the devil and he will flee from you," and warns against presumptuous planning that ignores God's sovereignty.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: James, the brother of Jesus (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Historical Context: Chapter 4 diagnoses the root cause of conflict (internal
desires, vv.1-3), warns against worldliness (vv.4-6), calls for repentance
and humility (vv.7-10), condemns judgmental speech (vv.11-12), and rebukes
presumptuous planning (vv.13-17). The chapter is blunt and confrontational —
James pulls no punches about the spiritual condition of his readers.

The Source of Conflict (vv.1-3): Wars and fights come from internal lusts
(v.1). You want but don't have; you don't have because you don't ask; you ask
but don't receive because you ask selfishly (vv.2-3).

Worldliness Condemned (vv.4-6): Friendship with the world = enmity with God
(v.4). You cannot be both God's friend and the world's friend. But God gives
"more grace" (v.6) — He resists the proud but gives grace to the humble.

The Call to Repentance (vv.7-10): Ten imperatives in rapid succession: submit
to God, resist the devil, draw near to God, cleanse your hands, purify your
hearts, be afflicted, mourn, weep, turn laughter to mourning, humble
yourselves (vv.7-10). Result: "He shall lift you up" (v.10).

Against Judging (vv.11-12): Don't speak evil of brothers — you're not the
judge; God is (vv.11-12).

Against Presumption (vv.13-17): Don't boast about tomorrow — life is a vapor
(v.14). Say "If the Lord will" (v.15). Knowing good and not doing it is sin
(v.17).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.4 — "Friendship with the world is enmity with God." (ESV) — You must
 choose: God or the world.

v.6 — "God opposes the proud but gives grace to the humble." (ESV) — Pride
 repels God; humility attracts grace.

v.7 — "Submit yourselves therefore to God. Resist the devil, and he will
 flee from you." (ESV) — Submit + resist = victory.

v.8 — "Draw near to God, and he will draw near to you." (ESV) — God
 responds to those who approach Him.

v.10 — "Humble yourselves before the Lord, and he will exalt you." (ESV) —
 Humility precedes exaltation.

v.14 — "What is your life? For you are a mist that appears for a little time
 and then vanishes." (ESV) — Life's brevity.

v.17 — "So whoever knows the right thing to do and fails to do it, for him
 it is sin." (ESV) — Sins of omission.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "lusts that war in your members" / ESV: "your passions are at
 war within you" — Greek "hēdonōn" — pleasures, desires for pleasure.
 Root of English "hedonism." Internal desires wage war and produce
 external conflicts.

v.4 — KJV: "adulterers and adulteresses" / ESV: "adulterous people" —
 Spiritual adultery — unfaithfulness to God by loving the world. OT
 imagery of Israel as God's unfaithful wife (Hosea, Ezekiel 16). ESV
 uses gender-neutral "people" for the collective address.

v.5 — KJV: "the spirit that dwelleth in us lusteth to envy" / ESV: "He
 yearns jealously over the spirit that he has made to dwell in us" —
 Difficult verse with varied translations. Likely: God's Spirit yearns
 jealously for our undivided devotion.

v.6 — KJV: "more grace" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "meizona charin" —
 greater grace. However strong our sin, God's grace is stronger. But
 it flows to the humble, not the proud.

v.7 — KJV: "resist the devil, and he will flee" / ESV: Both equivalent. —
 The promise: active resistance (in God's strength, after submission to
 God) causes Satan to flee. He cannot stand against a submitted,
 resisting believer.

v.8 — KJV: "double minded" / ESV: "double-minded" — Greek "dipsuchoi" —
 two-souled (same word as 1:8). Divided between God and the world. The
 cure: purify your hearts.

v.9 — KJV: "Be afflicted, and mourn, and weep" / ESV: "Be wretched and
 mourn and weep" — Greek "talaipōrēsate" — be miserable, feel the
 weight of your sin. A call to genuine repentance, not superficial
 sorrow.

v.14 — KJV: "vapour" / ESV: "mist" — Greek "atmis" — steam, mist, vapor.
 Visible briefly, then gone. The most vivid biblical image of life's
 brevity.

v.17 — KJV: "knoweth to do good, and doeth it not" / ESV: "whoever knows
 the right thing to do and fails to do it" — Both equivalent in
 meaning. Sins of omission. Not just doing wrong but failing to do
 right. Knowledge creates responsibility.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.2  — NET: "You desire and do not have; you murder and covet
       and are not able to obtain" — "murder" (phoneuete) — some
       manuscripts read "envy" (phthoneite). WEB: "You kill, and
       covet." KJV: "Ye kill, and desire to have." Whether literal
       murder or metaphorical is debated — James uses extreme
       language.

v.4  — NET: "Adulterers, do you not know that friendship with the
       world is hostility toward God?" — "Adulterers" (moichalides
       = adulteresses, feminine). WEB: "You adulteresses!" KJV:
       "Ye adulterers and adulteresses." The OT image of spiritual
       infidelity — Israel as God's unfaithful wife.

v.6  — NET: "God opposes the proud, but he gives grace to the
       humble" — quoting Proverbs 3:34 (LXX). "Opposes"
       (antitassetai = sets himself in battle array against). WEB:
       "God resists the proud." God actively resists the proud —
       military language.

v.7  — NET: "resist the devil and he will flee from you" —
       "resist" (antistEte = stand against/oppose). WEB: "Resist
       the devil, and he will flee from you." KJV: "Resist the
       devil, and he will flee from you." A promise: resistance
       produces retreat. The devil cannot endure determined opposition.

v.14 — NET: "you do not know about tomorrow. What is your life
       like?" — "What is your life?" (poia gar hE zOE hymOn).
       "You are a puff of smoke" (atmis = vapor/mist). WEB: "you
       are a vapor." KJV: "It is even a vapour." Life's brevity
       as morning mist — visible briefly, then gone.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Friendship with the world — Adopting the world's values, priorities, and
 pleasures. Spiritual adultery against God.

God resisteth the proud — God actively opposes the arrogant. Pride repels
 divine grace.

Submit to God — Voluntary surrender to God's authority. The prerequisite
 for resisting the devil.

Resist the devil — Active opposition to Satan's temptations and schemes.
 Done from a position of submission to God.

Draw nigh to God — Intentional approach to God through prayer, worship, and
 obedience. God responds to every genuine approach.

Life is a vapour — The brevity and uncertainty of human life. Here briefly,
 then gone. A call to urgency and humility.

Sins of omission — Knowing the right thing to do and failing to do it.
 Inaction when action is required.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "From whence come wars and fightings among you?" (v.1). What conflicts
 are you currently in? Can you trace them to internal desires — wanting
 something you don't have? What's the real root?

2. "The friendship of the world is enmity with God" (v.4). Where are you
 trying to be friends with both God and the world? You can't have both.
 What worldly values have you adopted that conflict with God's?

3. "Submit yourselves therefore to God. Resist the devil" (v.7). Are you
 submitted to God? That's the prerequisite for effective resistance. Where
 do you need to submit before you can resist?

4. "Draw nigh to God, and he will draw nigh to you" (v.8). Are you drawing
 near or drifting away? What practical step can you take today to draw
 closer to God?

5. "What is your life? It is even a vapour" (v.14). Life is short and
 uncertain. Are you living with urgency? Are you presuming on tomorrow?
 What would you do differently if you truly believed life is a vapor?

6. "To him that knoweth to do good, and doeth it not, to him it is sin"
 (v.17). What good do you know you should be doing but aren't? Inaction
 is sin when you know the right thing.
