Jude - Chapter 1
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Jude urges believers to contend earnestly for the faith against ungodly men who have crept in unnoticed, cites examples of divine judgment (fallen angels, Sodom, Korah), and closes with a magnificent doxology — "unto him that is able to keep you from falling."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Jude (v.1) — "the servant of Jesus Christ, and brother of James."
This identifies him as Jude (Judas), the half-brother of Jesus (Matt 13:55,
Mark 6:3) and brother of James (the author of James and leader of the
Jerusalem church). Like James, Jude humbly identifies himself as Christ's
servant rather than His brother.

Written approximately AD 65-80. One of the shortest books in the NT (25
verses).

Recipient: "Them that are sanctified by God the Father, and preserved in
Jesus Christ, and called" (v.1) — believers generally.

Purpose: Jude intended to write about "the common salvation" but was compelled
to write urgently about false teachers who had infiltrated the church (v.3).
He exhorts believers to "earnestly contend for the faith" (v.3). The letter
closely parallels 2 Peter 2.

Theme: Contending for the faith against false teachers. Warning, examples of
judgment, and encouragement to persevere.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.3 — "Contend for the faith that was once for all delivered to the saints."
 (ESV) — Fight for the faith.

v.4 — "Certain people have crept in unnoticed... perverting the grace of our
 God into sensuality and denying our only Master and Lord." (ESV) —
 False teachers: grace twisted into license.

v.11 — "Woe to them! For they walked in the way of Cain and abandoned
 themselves for the sake of gain to Balaam's error and perished in
 Korah's rebellion." (ESV) — Three OT examples of rebellion.

v.20-21 — "But you, beloved, building yourselves up in your most holy faith
 and praying in the Holy Spirit, keep yourselves in the love of God,
 waiting for the mercy of our Lord Jesus Christ that leads to eternal
 life." (ESV) — Four-fold exhortation.

v.24-25 — "Now to him who is able to keep you from stumbling and to present
 you blameless before the presence of his glory with great joy, to the
 only God, our Savior, through Jesus Christ our Lord, be glory,
 majesty, dominion, and authority, before all time and now and
 forever." (ESV) — One of Scripture's greatest doxologies.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "preserved in Jesus Christ" / ESV: "kept for Jesus Christ" —
 Greek "tētērēmenois" — kept, guarded, watched over. Believers are
 kept safe for Christ. ESV "kept for" emphasizes the purpose; KJV
 "preserved in" emphasizes the location.

v.3 — KJV: "earnestly contend" / ESV: "contend" — Greek "epagōnizesthai" —
 to fight upon, to contend intensely. An athletic/military term for
 intense struggle. KJV: "once delivered" / ESV: "once for all
 delivered" — Greek "hapax paradotheisē" — once for all handed over.
 The faith is complete — no new revelations needed.
       NET: "I now feel compelled instead to write to encourage
       you to contend earnestly for the faith" — "contend earnestly"
       (epagOnizesthai = fight strenuously/agonize for). WEB:
       "to contend earnestly for the faith." KJV: "earnestly
       contend for the faith." Athletic/military intensity —
       THE faith (body of doctrine) needs active defense.

v.4 — KJV: "crept in unawares" / ESV: "crept in unnoticed" — Greek
 "pareiseduesan" — slipped in secretly, smuggled in alongside. False
 teachers enter subtly. KJV: "lasciviousness" / ESV: "sensuality" —
 Greek "aselgeian" — sensuality, license, lack of moral restraint.
       NET: "certain men have secretly slipped in among you" —
       "secretly slipped in" (pareisedysan = crept in sideways/
       infiltrated). WEB: "there are certain men who crept in
       secretly." KJV: "certain men crept in unawares." They
       didn't announce themselves — stealth entry into the church.

v.9 — KJV: "Michael the archangel... durst not bring against him a railing
 accusation" / ESV: "did not presume to pronounce a blasphemous
 judgment" — Even he didn't presume to rebuke Satan directly but said
 "The Lord rebuke thee." False teachers are bolder than angels.
       NET: "Michael the archangel, when he argued with the devil
       and disputed about the body of Moses, did not dare to bring
       a slandering judgment" — "did not dare" (ouk etolmEsen =
       did not presume). WEB: "dared not bring against him a
       railing judgment." Even an archangel defers to God in
       dealing with Satan — unlike the false teachers (v.10).

v.11 — KJV: "the way of Cain... the error of Balaam... the gainsaying of
 Core" / ESV: "the way of Cain... Balaam's error... Korah's rebellion"
 — Three examples: "way of Cain" (religious hypocrisy/murder), "error
 of Balaam" (using ministry for profit), "gainsaying/rebellion of
 Korah" (rebellion against God-appointed authority).

v.12 — KJV: "spots in your feasts of charity" / ESV: "blemishes on your
 love feasts" — Greek "spilades" — hidden rocks (dangerous to ships) or
 blemishes. They feast with believers while being spiritually dangerous.

v.14 — NET: "Enoch, the seventh from Adam, prophesied about them"
       — Jude quotes 1 Enoch (an apocryphal book). WEB: "Enoch,
       the seventh from Adam, also prophesied." Quoting a non-
       canonical source does not canonize it — Paul quoted pagan
       poets (Acts 17:28; Titus 1:12) the same way.

v.20 — KJV: "building up yourselves on your most holy faith" / ESV:
 "building yourselves up in your most holy faith" — Both equivalent.
 Greek "epoikodomountes" — building upon the foundation. Active,
 intentional spiritual construction.

v.24 — KJV: "keep you from falling" / ESV: "keep you from stumbling" —
 Greek "phulaxai humas aptaistous" — to guard you without stumbling.
 God's preserving power. KJV: "faultless" / ESV: "blameless" — Greek
 "amōmous" — without blemish, without defect. The same word used of
 sacrificial animals — spotless.
       NET: "Now to the one who is able to keep you from falling,
       and to cause you to stand, without blemish, in joyful
       exultation, before his glorious presence" — "keep from
       falling" (phylaxai aptaistous = guard without stumbling).
       WEB: "able to keep you from stumbling." KJV: "able to keep
       you from falling." God's preserving power — the final
       doxology and the book's crowning promise.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Contend for the faith — Intensely fight to preserve and defend the body of
 apostolic truth. Not optional but urgent.

Once delivered — The faith given once for all — complete, final, not to be
 altered. The apostolic deposit of truth.

Crept in unawares — False teachers entering the church secretly, subtly,
 disguised as genuine believers.

Grace into lasciviousness — Twisting God's grace into a license for sin.
 "God forgives, so sin doesn't matter."

Way of Cain, error of Balaam, gainsaying of Korah — Three patterns of
 rebellion: religious hypocrisy, ministry for profit, defiance
 of authority.

Keep you from falling — God's power to preserve believers from stumbling.
 Divine protection, not human effort.

Present you faultless — God's ultimate goal: presenting believers spotless
 before His glory with exceeding joy.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Earnestly contend for the faith" (v.3). Are you actively defending the
 faith — or passively watching it be eroded? What does "contending" look
 like in your context?

2. "Turning the grace of our God into lasciviousness" (v.4). Have you ever
 used grace as an excuse for sin? "God will forgive" is not a license to
 sin — it's a motivation for holiness.

3. "Building up yourselves on your most holy faith, praying in the Holy
 Ghost, keep yourselves in the love of God" (vv.20-21). Four actions:
 build (study), pray (in the Spirit), keep (stay in God's love), look
 (anticipate Christ's return). Which needs more attention?

4. "Him that is able to keep you from falling" (v.24). Your perseverance
 depends on God's keeping power, not your grip. Does this give you
 confidence? He is ABLE to keep you.

5. "Present you faultless before the presence of his glory with exceeding
 joy" (v.24). This is your destiny: faultless, joyful, in God's presence.
 Not because of your perfection but because of His keeping power. Let this
 hope sustain you.
