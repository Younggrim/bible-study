Galatians - Chapter 1
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Paul defends his apostleship and gospel as received directly from Christ (not from man), pronounces a double curse on anyone who preaches a different gospel, and recounts his conversion and early years — emphasizing his independence from the Jerusalem apostles.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: The Apostle Paul, writing approximately AD 48-49 (possibly the earliest
of Paul's letters) or AD 55 (if written to churches in northern Galatia).

Audience: The churches of Galatia — likely the churches Paul founded on his
first missionary journey (Pisidian Antioch, Iconium, Lystra, Derbe — Acts 13-14).

Purpose: To defend the Gospel of grace against "Judaizers" — false teachers who
insisted that Gentile believers must be circumcised and follow the Mosaic Law
to be saved. Galatians is Paul's most passionate, urgent letter — written to
protect the Gospel from corruption.

The Crisis: After Paul left, false teachers arrived and told the Galatians they
needed to add Law-keeping to faith in Christ. Paul is astonished: "I marvel
that ye are so soon removed" (v.6). He pronounces a double curse on anyone who
preaches a different gospel (vv.8-9) — the strongest language in any of his letters.

Chapter 1 Content: No thanksgiving (unusual for Paul — he's too upset). Instead:
the Gospel defined (vv.1-5), the crisis stated (vv.6-10), and Paul's apostolic
authority defended (vv.11-24).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.6  — "I am astonished that you are so quickly deserting him who called you
        in the grace of Christ and are turning to a different gospel." (ESV)
        — Paul's astonishment at their defection.

v.8  — "But even if we or an angel from heaven should preach to you a gospel
        contrary to the one we preached to you, let him be accursed." (ESV) —
        The strongest possible condemnation of false gospels.

v.10 — "If I were still trying to please man, I would not be a servant of
        Christ." (ESV) — You cannot serve Christ and seek human approval
        simultaneously.

v.15-16 — "But when he who had set me apart before I was born, and who called
        me by his grace, was pleased to reveal his Son to me." (ESV) — Paul's
        calling: sovereign, gracious, revelatory.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.4  — NET: "who gave himself for our sins to rescue us from
       this present evil age" — "rescue" (exelEtai = deliver/
       extract). WEB: "who gave himself for our sins, that he
       might deliver us out of this present evil age." KJV: "that
       he might deliver us from this present evil world." The cross
       as rescue operation from an evil system.

v.6  — KJV: "another gospel" / ESV: "to a different gospel" — Greek "heteron euangelion" — a different kind
       of gospel. Then Paul corrects: "which is not another" (v.7) — Greek
       "allo" — there is no other gospel of the same kind. There is only ONE
       Gospel.
       NET: "I am astonished that you are so quickly deserting
       the one who called you by the grace of Christ" — "astonished"
       (thaumazO = amazed/marvel). WEB: "I marvel that you are so
       quickly deserting." No thanksgiving section — Paul is too
       upset. This is the angriest letter in the NT.

v.8  — KJV: "accursed" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "anathema" — devoted to destruction, under
       God's curse. The strongest condemnation Paul ever pronounces.
       NET: "But even if we or an angel from heaven should preach
       a gospel contrary to the one we preached to you, let him be
       condemned to hell!" — "condemned to hell" (anathema estO).
       WEB: "let him be cursed." KJV: "let him be accursed." NET
       intensifies the translation; the Greek is a solemn curse.

v.10 — KJV: "do I seek to please men?" / ESV: "or of God? Or am I trying to please man?" — Paul's critics accused him of
       people-pleasing (softening the Gospel for Gentiles). His response:
       if I pleased men, I wouldn't be Christ's servant.

v.12 — KJV: "by the revelation of Jesus Christ" / ESV: "received it through a revelation of Jesus Christ" — Paul's Gospel came directly
       from Christ — not from human teachers, not from the other apostles.
       This establishes its authority independent of human mediation.

v.15 — KJV: "separated me from my mother's womb" / ESV: "But when he who had set me apart before I was born" — Echoing Jeremiah 1:5 and
       Isaiah 49:1. Paul's calling was predetermined before birth — sovereign
       election.
       NET: "set me apart from birth and called me by his grace"
       — "set apart from birth" (aphorisas me ek koilias mEtros
       mou = separated me from my mother's womb). WEB: "separated
       me from my mother's womb." Echoes Jeremiah 1:5 — prophetic
       calling before birth.

v.16 — NET: "to reveal his Son in me so that I could preach him
       among the Gentiles" — "in me" (en emoi) — not just "to me"
       but "in me." WEB: "to reveal his Son in me." The revelation
       was internal transformation, not just information transfer.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Judaizers     — False teachers who insisted Gentile believers must be
               circumcised and follow the Mosaic Law to be saved. They added
               works to grace.

Another gospel — A perverted message that adds human works to faith in Christ.
               Not truly "another" gospel — there is only one.

Anathema      — Greek for "accursed" — devoted to destruction under God's
               curse. The strongest possible condemnation.

Revelation    — Direct disclosure from God — not learned from humans but
               received from Christ Himself.

Grace of Christ — The unmerited favor of God in Christ — the heart of the
               Gospel that the Judaizers were corrupting by adding works.

Separated from the womb — God's sovereign choice of Paul before birth —
               predestination for a specific calling.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "I marvel that ye are so soon removed" (v.6). The Galatians quickly
   abandoned grace for legalism. How easily do you drift from grace back to
   performance-based religion? What pulls you toward earning God's favor
   rather than receiving it?

2. "Though we, or an angel from heaven, preach any other gospel... let him be
   accursed" (v.8). The Gospel is non-negotiable. No one — no matter how
   impressive — has the right to change it. How do you evaluate teaching?
   Do you test everything against the Gospel of grace?

3. "If I yet pleased men, I should not be the servant of Christ" (v.10). You
   cannot serve Christ and seek human approval simultaneously. Where is
   people-pleasing compromising your faithfulness to Christ?

4. "When it pleased God... to reveal his Son in me" (vv.15-16). Your calling
   is God's initiative — sovereign, gracious, and purposeful. How does knowing
   that God chose you before birth affect your sense of identity and purpose?

REFLECTION QUESTIONS
------------------------------------
1. Paul skips his usual thanksgiving — he's too upset. The Gospel was at stake.
   What would make you this urgent? Is there anything you care about enough
   to fight for with this intensity?

2. The Judaizers added circumcision to faith. What do people add to the Gospel
   today? Church membership? Baptism? Moral performance? Political alignment?
   How do you keep the Gospel pure — grace alone, faith alone, Christ alone?

3. Paul received his Gospel "by the revelation of Jesus Christ" (v.12) — not
   from human teachers. How does this establish the Gospel's authority? Why
   does it matter that Paul's message came directly from Christ?

4. Paul's past (persecutor) became his testimony (vv.13,23). "He which
   persecuted us in times past now preacheth the faith." How has God
   transformed your past into a testimony?

5. "They glorified God in me" (v.24). Paul's transformation caused others to
   praise God. Does your life cause people to glorify God? What would need
   to change for that to be more true?
