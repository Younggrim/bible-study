Galatians - Chapter 4
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Believers are no longer slaves but adopted sons who cry "Abba, Father," Paul warns the Galatians against returning to bondage, and uses the allegory of Hagar and Sarah to contrast the covenant of law-slavery with the covenant of promise-freedom.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Paul (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 4 continues Paul's argument for freedom from the
Law using two illustrations: the heir who is no different from a slave until
maturity (vv.1-7), and the allegory of Hagar and Sarah (vv.21-31). Between
these, Paul makes a personal appeal to the Galatians (vv.8-20).

Sons, Not Slaves (vv.1-7): Before Christ, believers were like underage heirs —
legally owners but practically no different from slaves, "under tutors and
governors" (v.2). But "when the fulness of the time was come, God sent forth
his Son" (v.4) to redeem those under the Law and give them "the adoption of
sons" (v.5). The Spirit confirms this: "Abba, Father" (v.6).

"The Fulness of the Time" (v.4): God sent Christ at exactly the right moment
in history — when Roman roads connected the world, Greek was the universal
language, and Jewish synagogues existed in every city. God's timing is perfect.

The Hagar/Sarah Allegory (vv.21-31): Two women = two covenants. Hagar (slave)
= the old covenant of Law from Sinai = bondage. Sarah (free) = the new covenant
of promise = freedom. Believers are "children of promise" like Isaac — born of
the free woman, not the slave.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.4-5 — "But when the fullness of time had come, God sent forth his Son, born
        of woman, born under the law, to redeem those who were under the law,
        so that we might receive adoption as sons." (ESV) — The Incarnation's
        purpose: redemption and adoption.

v.6  — "And because you are sons, God has sent the Spirit of his Son into our
        hearts, crying, 'Abba! Father!'" (ESV) — The Spirit's witness to our
        sonship.

v.7  — "So you are no longer a slave, but a son, and if a son, then an heir
        through God." (ESV) — From slave to son to heir.

v.9  — "How can you turn back again to the weak and worthless elementary
        principles of the world?" (ESV) — The absurdity of returning to
        bondage after experiencing freedom.

v.16 — "Have I then become your enemy by telling you the truth?" (ESV) —
        Truth-telling can cost friendships.

v.19 — "My little children, for whom I am again in the anguish of childbirth
        until Christ is formed in you!" (ESV) — Paul's pastoral anguish.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------

v.3  — KJV: "elements of the world" / ESV: "elementary principles of the world" — Greek "stoicheia tou kosmou" — basic
       principles, elementary teachings. Could refer to: (1) the basic
       principles of religion (Law), (2) elemental spirits, or (3) the ABCs
       of the world's system. Paul says they are "weak and beggarly" (v.9).

v.4  — KJV: "made of a woman" / ESV: "his Son, born of woman, born" — Affirms Christ's genuine humanity — born of
       a woman, fully human. "Made under the law" — born as a Jew, subject
       to the Law He came to fulfill.

v.5  — KJV: "adoption of sons" / ESV: "receive adoption as sons" — Greek "huiothesian" — the legal act of
       adopting someone as a full heir. Believers are not natural-born children
       of God — they are adopted with full inheritance rights.

v.6  — KJV: "Abba, Father" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Aramaic + Greek. The intimate family word
       combined with the formal title. Full access with full reverence.

v.24 — KJV: "which things are an allegory" / ESV: "Now this may be interpreted allegorically: these" — Paul uses the OT narrative
       typologically — Hagar and Sarah represent two covenants. Not denying
       the historical reality but drawing spiritual meaning from it.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.4  — NET: "But when the appropriate time had come, God sent
       out his Son, born of a woman, born under the law" — "the
       appropriate time" (to plErOma tou chronou = the fullness of
       time). WEB: "when the fullness of the time came." KJV: "when
       the fulness of the time was come." History has a divine
       timetable — Christ arrived at the precise moment.

v.6  — NET: "And because you are sons, God sent the Spirit of his
       Son into our hearts, who calls 'Abba! Father!'" — "Abba"
       (Aramaic intimate address). WEB: "crying, 'Abba, Father!'"
       KJV: "crying, Abba, Father." The Spirit enables the
       intimate cry that proves adoption — slaves don't call the
       master "Daddy."

v.9  — NET: "how can you turn back again to the weak and
       worthless basic forces? Do you want to be enslaved to them
       all over again?" — "basic forces" (stoicheia = elemental
       principles/rudiments). WEB: "the weak and miserable elemental
       principles." KJV: "weak and beggarly elements." Returning to
       law-keeping is regression, not progress.

v.19 — NET: "My children — Loss Loss I am again undergoing birth
       pains until Christ is formed in you!" — "formed" (morphOthE
       = takes shape). WEB: "until Christ is formed in you." KJV:
       "until Christ be formed in you." Paul as a mother in labor
       — the goal is Christ's character reproduced in them.

v.26 — NET: "But the Jerusalem above is free, and she is our
       mother" — "Jerusalem above" — the heavenly city, the true
       homeland of faith. WEB: "the Jerusalem that is above is
       free, which is the mother of us all." Two Jerusalems: earthly
       (bondage) and heavenly (freedom).

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Fullness of time — The perfect moment in history when God sent Christ —
               prepared by Roman infrastructure, Greek language, and Jewish
               expectation.

Adoption of sons — The legal act of receiving full inheritance rights as a
               child of God — not by birth but by grace through faith.

Abba          — Aramaic for "Father" — intimate, familial, personal. The
               Spirit's cry in the believer's heart confirming sonship.

Elements of the world — Basic, elementary principles — whether religious rules
               or worldly systems. "Weak and beggarly" compared to Christ.

Hagar/Sarah allegory — Two women representing two covenants: Hagar = Law/
               bondage/Sinai; Sarah = promise/freedom/heavenly Jerusalem.

Children of promise — Believers as spiritual descendants of Isaac — born not
               of human effort (flesh) but of God's promise (Spirit).

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Thou art no more a servant, but a son" (v.7). You are not God's slave —
   you are His child. Do you relate to God as a slave (fear, duty, performance)
   or as a son (love, intimacy, confidence)?

2. "How turn ye again to the weak and beggarly elements?" (v.9). After
   experiencing freedom in Christ, why would you go back to religious bondage?
   What "weak and beggarly elements" are you tempted to return to — rules,
   rituals, performance-based religion?

3. "Am I therefore become your enemy, because I tell you the truth?" (v.16).
   Truth-telling can cost relationships. Are you willing to tell people the
   truth even when it makes you unpopular? How do you handle it when someone
   tells YOU uncomfortable truth?

4. "Until Christ be formed in you" (v.19). Paul's goal for the Galatians:
   Christ formed in them — not just believed in but formed, shaped, visible.
   Is Christ being formed in you? What does that look like practically?
