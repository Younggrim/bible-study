Galatians - Chapter 5
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
"Stand fast in the liberty wherewith Christ hath made us free" — Paul warns that circumcision obligates one to keep the whole Law, lists the works of the flesh against the fruit of the Spirit (love, joy, peace...), and declares those who belong to Christ have crucified the flesh.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Paul (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 5 is the practical turning point of Galatians.
Having established the theological argument for justification by faith (chs.
1-4), Paul now addresses the practical question: If we are free from the Law,
how should we live? His answer: Walk in the Spirit. This chapter contains two
of the most famous passages in all of Paul's letters — the works of the flesh
(vv.19-21) and the fruit of the Spirit (vv.22-23).

Stand Fast in Liberty (vv.1-12): Paul opens with a ringing declaration of
freedom: "Stand fast therefore in the liberty wherewith Christ hath made us
free" (v.1). He warns that accepting circumcision as necessary for salvation
means accepting the entire Law — and falling from grace (v.4). The only thing
that counts is "faith which worketh by love" (v.6).

Liberty, Not License (vv.13-15): Freedom is not an excuse for sin. True
liberty expresses itself in love: "By love serve one another" (v.13). The
entire Law is fulfilled in one command: "Thou shalt love thy neighbour as
thyself" (v.14).

Walk in the Spirit (vv.16-26): The flesh and Spirit are at war (v.17). Paul
lists the works of the flesh — 15 sins covering sexual immorality, idolatry,
relational conflict, and excess (vv.19-21). Then the fruit of the Spirit —
9 qualities that describe the character of Christ (vv.22-23). Those who belong
to Christ "have crucified the flesh with the affections and lusts" (v.24).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "For freedom Christ has set us free; stand firm therefore, and do not
        submit again to a yoke of slavery." (ESV) — The great declaration of
        Christian freedom.

v.4  — "You are severed from Christ, you who would be justified by the law;
        you have fallen away from grace." (ESV) — Seeking justification by
        Law means abandoning grace.

v.6  — "For in Christ Jesus neither circumcision nor uncircumcision counts for
        anything, but only faith working through love." (ESV) — The only thing
        that matters: faith expressing itself through love.

v.13 — "For you were called to freedom, brothers. Only do not use your freedom
        as an opportunity for the flesh, but through love serve one another."
        (ESV) — Freedom's purpose: loving service.

v.16 — "But I say, walk by the Spirit, and you will not gratify the desires of
        the flesh." (ESV) — The key to victory over sin: Spirit-led living.

v.17 — "For the desires of the flesh are against the Spirit, and the desires
        of the Spirit are against the flesh, for these are opposed to each
        other." (ESV) — The ongoing internal war.

v.22-23 — "But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, patience,
        kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, self-control; against
        such things there is no law." (ESV) — The nine-fold fruit of the
        Spirit.

v.24 — "And those who belong to Christ Jesus have crucified the flesh with
        its passions and desires." (ESV) — Past tense — a decisive act.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------

v.1  — KJV: "Stand fast therefore in the liberty" — ESV: "For freedom Christ
       has set us free; stand firm therefore." The ESV makes explicit what KJV
       implies: Christ freed us FOR freedom — freedom is both the means and
       the goal.

v.4  — KJV: "ye are fallen from grace" / ESV: "you have fallen away from grace" — Does not mean loss of salvation.
       Greek "ekpiptō" means "to fall away from, to lose one's grasp on."
       Those seeking justification by Law have fallen away from the principle
       of grace — they've abandoned the grace method for the works method.

v.6  — KJV: "faith which worketh by love" / ESV: "but only faith working through love" — Greek "pistis di' agapēs
       energoumenē" — faith energized by love, faith that expresses itself
       through love. Not dead faith but active, loving faith.

v.12 — KJV: "I would they were even cut off which trouble you" / ESV: "I wish those who unsettle you would emasculate themselves!" — Paul's
       strong language. "Cut off" (Greek "apokoptō") — some scholars see a
       wordplay: those who insist on circumcision should go further and
       mutilate themselves entirely. NET: "I wish those agitators would go
       so far as to castrate themselves!"

v.17 — KJV: "so that ye cannot do the things that ye would" / ESV: "each other, to keep you from doing the things you want to do" — The flesh
       prevents you from doing what the Spirit desires, and the Spirit
       prevents you from doing what the flesh desires. Constant tension.

v.19-21 — Works of the flesh: 15 sins in four categories:
       Sexual sins: adultery, fornication, uncleanness, lasciviousness
       Religious sins: idolatry, witchcraft (Greek "pharmakeia" — sorcery,
       drug use in pagan rituals)
       Relational sins: hatred, variance, emulations, wrath, strife,
       seditions, heresies, envyings
       Excess: murders, drunkenness, revellings

v.22-23 — Fruit of the Spirit: 9 qualities in three triads:
       Godward: love, joy, peace
       Manward: longsuffering, gentleness, goodness
       Selfward: faith (faithfulness), meekness, temperance (self-control)

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.1  — NET: "For freedom Christ has set us free. Stand firm,
       then, and do not be subject again to the yoke of slavery" —
       "for freedom" (tE eleutheria = for the purpose of freedom).
       WEB: "Stand firm therefore in the liberty by which Christ
       has made us free." Freedom is both the means and the goal
       of Christ's work.

v.4  — NET: "You who are trying to be declared righteous by the
       law have been alienated from Christ; you have fallen away
       from grace!" — "fallen away from grace" (tEs charitos
       exepesate). WEB: "you are fallen from grace." Not loss of
       salvation but departure from the grace-principle back to
       law-principle.

v.6  — NET: "For in Christ Jesus neither circumcision nor
       uncircumcision carries any weight — the only thing that
       matters is faith working through love" — "faith working
       through love" (pistis di' agapEs energoumenE). WEB: "faith
       working through love." KJV: "faith which worketh by love."
       Not bare faith or bare love — faith energized by love.

v.17 — NET: "For the flesh has desires that are opposed to the
       Spirit, and the Spirit has desires that are opposed to the
       flesh" — "opposed" (antikeitai = set against/in conflict).
       WEB: "the flesh lusts against the Spirit, and the Spirit
       against the flesh." Ongoing internal war — not passive
       coexistence.

v.22 — NET: "But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace,
       patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, and
       self-control" — "fruit" (karpos) is SINGULAR — one fruit
       with nine characteristics. WEB: "the fruit of the Spirit is
       love, joy, peace..." Not fruits (plural) but one integrated
       character produced by the Spirit.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Stand fast    — Greek "stēkō" — to stand firm, hold your ground, refuse to
               be moved. A military term for holding position.

Fallen from grace — Not loss of salvation but departure from the grace
               principle. Choosing Law over grace as the basis of one's
               relationship with God.

Faith working by love — Living faith that expresses itself through love.
               The only thing that counts in Christ.

Leaven        — Yeast — a small amount that permeates the whole batch. False
               teaching spreads like leaven through a community.

Works of the flesh — The natural output of the sinful nature when it is not
               restrained by the Spirit. 15 sins listed in four categories.

Fruit of the Spirit — The natural output of the Holy Spirit's work in a
               believer's life. Nine qualities that describe Christ's
               character reproduced in us.

Pharmakeia    — Greek word translated "witchcraft" (v.20) — literally
               "drug use" — refers to sorcery involving potions and drugs
               in pagan worship. Root of English "pharmacy."

Crucified the flesh — A decisive past act (aorist tense) — believers have
               already put the flesh to death positionally. Now they must
               live out that reality daily.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Stand fast in the liberty" (v.1). What threatens your freedom in Christ?
   Legalism (adding rules to the gospel)? License (using freedom as an excuse
   for sin)? How do you "stand fast" against both?

2. "Faith which worketh by love" (v.6). Is your faith active and loving, or
   passive and self-focused? How does your faith express itself in love toward
   others this week?

3. "By love serve one another" (v.13). Freedom's purpose is not self-
   indulgence but loving service. Who can you serve this week? How does
   serving others actually increase your sense of freedom?

4. "Walk in the Spirit" (v.16). This is the key to the Christian life. What
   does "walking in the Spirit" look like practically for you? How do you
   cultivate sensitivity to the Spirit's leading?

5. Review the works of the flesh (vv.19-21) honestly. Which ones are you most
   susceptible to? Not just the obvious ones — variance (quarreling), wrath,
   strife, envyings are on the same list as adultery and idolatry.

6. Review the fruit of the Spirit (vv.22-23). Which ones are most evident in
   your life? Which ones are least developed? Remember — you don't manufacture
   fruit. You abide in the vine (John 15) and fruit grows naturally.
