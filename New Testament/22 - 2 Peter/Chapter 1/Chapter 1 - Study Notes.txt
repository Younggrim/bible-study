2 Peter - Chapter 1
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Peter reminds believers of their precious promises and calls them to supplement faith with virtue, knowledge, self-control, and love — confirming their calling and election, and affirming that Scripture came not by human will but as men spoke from God, carried by the Holy Spirit.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Simon Peter (v.1). Written approximately AD 65-68, shortly before
Peter's martyrdom (v.14; cf. John 21:18-19). This is Peter's final letter —
his "last will and testament" to the church.

Recipient: The same audience as 1 Peter (3:1) — believers scattered throughout
Asia Minor.

Purpose: To warn against false teachers (ch.2) and scoffers who deny Christ's
return (ch.3), while encouraging believers to grow in knowledge and godliness
(ch.1). Peter knows his death is near and wants to leave a lasting reminder.

Theme: True knowledge of God vs. false teaching. "Knowledge" (gnōsis/
epignōsis) appears 16 times in 3 chapters. True knowledge produces godliness;
false knowledge produces immorality.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.3 — "His divine power has granted to us all things that pertain to life
 and godliness, through the knowledge of him." (ESV) — Everything
 needed is already given.

v.4 — "He has granted to us his precious and very great promises, so that
 through them you may become partakers of the divine nature." (ESV) —
 Sharing in God's nature through His promises.

v.5-7 — "make every effort to supplement your faith with virtue, and virtue
 with knowledge, and knowledge with self-control, and self-control
 with steadfastness, and steadfastness with godliness, and godliness
 with brotherly affection, and brotherly affection with love." (ESV)
 — The ladder of spiritual growth.

v.10 — "be all the more diligent to confirm your calling and election."
 (ESV) — Confirm your calling through growth.

v.20-21 — "no prophecy of Scripture comes from someone's own interpretation.
 For no prophecy was ever produced by the will of man, but men spoke
 from God as they were carried along by the Holy Spirit." (ESV) —
 Scripture's divine origin.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "Simon Peter" / ESV: "Simeon Peter" — ESV uses the more Hebraic
 "Simeon" (Greek "Sumeōn"). KJV: "like precious faith" / ESV: "a
 faith of equal standing" — Greek "isotimos" — equally honored,
 equally valuable faith.

v.3 — KJV: "all things that pertain unto life and godliness" / ESV: "all
 things that pertain to life and godliness" — Both equivalent.
 Everything needed for both physical life and spiritual godliness.
 Already given (perfect tense) — not still coming.

v.4 — KJV: "partakers of the divine nature" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek
 "theias koinōnoi phuseōs" — sharers in God's nature. Not becoming God
 but sharing in His moral character — holiness, love, righteousness.

v.5 — KJV: "giving all diligence, add to your faith virtue" / ESV: "make
 every effort to supplement your faith with virtue" — Greek
 "epichorēgēsate" — supply generously, furnish abundantly. Not minimal
 effort but lavish investment in growth.

v.10 — KJV: "make your calling and election sure" / ESV: "confirm your
 calling and election" — Not that election is uncertain but that you
 confirm it through growth. Fruit proves the root. Growth confirms
 genuineness.

v.16 — KJV: "cunningly devised fables" / ESV: "cleverly devised myths" —
 Greek "sesophismenois muthois" — cleverly invented myths. Peter
 contrasts: we didn't follow myths — we were eyewitnesses.

v.19 — KJV: "more sure word of prophecy" / ESV: "the prophetic word more
 fully confirmed" — Scripture is even more reliable than eyewitness
 experience. Peter saw the Transfiguration — but Scripture is "more
 sure."

v.21 — KJV: "moved by the Holy Ghost" / ESV: "carried along by the Holy
 Spirit" — Greek "pheromenoi hupo pneumatos hagiou" — carried along by
 the Holy Spirit. Like wind carrying a sailing ship. Human authors,
 divine direction.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.3  — NET: "His divine power has bestowed on us everything
       necessary for life and godliness" — "everything necessary"
       (panta... ta pros zOEn). WEB: "seeing that his divine power
       has granted to us all things." KJV: "all things that pertain
       unto life and godliness." Complete provision — nothing
       lacking for spiritual life.

v.4  — NET: "precious and most magnificent promises, so that
       through them you may become partakers of the divine nature"
       — "partakers of divine nature" (theias koinOnoi physeOs).
       WEB: "partakers of the divine nature." KJV: "partakers of
       the divine nature." Not becoming God but sharing in His
       moral character — the Eastern church's concept of theosis.

v.5  — NET: "make every effort to supplement your faith with
       excellence" — "supplement" (epichorEgEsate = supply/furnish
       generously). WEB: "adding on your part all diligence." A
       chain of virtues: faith, virtue, knowledge, self-control,
       perseverance, godliness, brotherly love, love.

v.19 — NET: "we possess the prophetic word as an altogether
       reliable thing" — "altogether reliable" (bebaioteron =
       more sure/more confirmed). WEB: "the more sure word of
       prophecy." KJV: "a more sure word of prophecy." Scripture
       is more reliable than even eyewitness experiences (v.18 —
       the Transfiguration).

v.21 — NET: "men spoke from God as they were carried along by the
       Holy Spirit" — "carried along" (pheromenoi = borne/moved —
       same word for a ship driven by wind). WEB: "moved by the
       Holy Spirit." KJV: "moved by the Holy Ghost." Human authors
       carried by divine wind — not dictation but organic
       inspiration.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
All things given — Complete divine provision for life and godliness. Already
 supplied through knowledge of Christ.

Partakers of divine nature — Sharing in God's moral character. Not
 deification but transformation into His likeness.

The growth ladder — Seven qualities to add progressively: virtue, knowledge,
 self-control, patience, godliness, brotherly kindness, love.

Make calling sure — Confirming genuine election through visible spiritual
 growth. Fruit proves the root.

Eyewitnesses — Peter's firsthand experience of Christ's glory at the
 Transfiguration. Not myths but reality.

Moved by the Holy Ghost — The Spirit carrying human authors along so they
 wrote God's words. Divine inspiration through human
 instruments.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "His divine power hath given unto us ALL things" (v.3). You already have
 everything you need for life and godliness. Are you accessing what's
 already given, or looking for something more?

2. Review the growth ladder (vv.5-7). Where are you on it? Which quality
 needs the most development? Are you actively "adding" or passively
 stagnating?

3. "Give diligence to make your calling and election sure" (v.10). Is your
 life producing evidence of genuine faith? Can you see growth? If not, what
 needs to change?

4. "Holy men of God spake as they were moved by the Holy Ghost" (v.21). Do
 you treat Scripture as God's word — carried along by His Spirit? Or as
 merely human opinion? Your view of Scripture determines everything.

5. Peter knew death was near (v.14) and focused on leaving truth behind
 (v.15). What legacy are you building? What truth are you passing on?
