2 Peter - Chapter 3
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Scoffers will deny Christ's return, but the Lord is not slow — "one day is with the Lord as a thousand years" — He is patient, wanting none to perish, and the day of the Lord will come like a thief, dissolving the heavens and earth by fire as we await new heavens and a new earth.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Simon Peter (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Historical Context: Chapter 3 addresses scoffers who mock Christ's return
(vv.1-7), explains God's patience (vv.8-9), describes the Day of the Lord
(vv.10-13), and gives final exhortations (vv.14-18). Some in the early church
were losing hope because Christ hadn't returned yet. Scoffers used the delay
to argue He would never come. Peter responds: God's timing is not human
timing, His patience is for salvation, and the Day WILL come.

Scoffers and the Promise (vv.1-7): In the last days, scoffers will mock:
"Where is the promise of his coming?" (vv.3-4). They willfully ignore that
God destroyed the world by water before (vv.5-6). The present heavens and
earth are "reserved unto fire" for judgment day (v.7).

God's Patience (vv.8-9): One day with the Lord is as a thousand years (v.8).
God is not slow — He is patient, "not willing that any should perish, but
that all should come to repentance" (v.9).

The Day of the Lord (vv.10-13): It will come "as a thief" — suddenly,
unexpectedly (v.10). The heavens will pass away, elements melt, earth burned
up (v.10). Since everything will be dissolved, "what manner of persons ought
ye to be?" (v.11). We look for "new heavens and a new earth, wherein dwelleth
righteousness" (v.13).

Final Exhortations (vv.14-18): Be diligent to be found in peace, spotless,
blameless (v.14). God's patience = salvation (v.15). Paul wrote the same
things (vv.15-16). Beware of being led astray (v.17). "Grow in grace, and in
the knowledge of our Lord" (v.18).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.8 — "But do not overlook this one fact, beloved, that with the Lord one
 day is as a thousand years, and a thousand years as one day." (ESV) —
 God's perspective on time.

v.9 — "The Lord is not slow to fulfill his promise as some count slowness,
 but is patient toward you, not wishing that any should perish, but
 that all should reach repentance." (ESV) — God's patience explained.

v.10 — "But the day of the Lord will come like a thief, and then the heavens
 will pass away with a roar." (ESV) — The Day's sudden, catastrophic
 arrival.

v.11 — "what sort of people ought you to be in lives of holiness and
 godliness." (ESV) — Eschatology producing holiness.

v.13 — "But according to his promise we are waiting for new heavens and a
 new earth in which righteousness dwells." (ESV) — The ultimate hope.

v.18 — "But grow in the grace and knowledge of our Lord and Savior Jesus
 Christ." (ESV) — Peter's final command: keep growing.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.3 — KJV: "scoffers, walking after their own lusts" / ESV: "scoffers will
 come in the last days with scoffing, following their own sinful
 desires" — Greek "empaiktai" — mockers, those who ridicule. Their
 mockery is morally motivated. They deny Christ's return because they
 don't want to be accountable.

v.8 — KJV: "one day is with the Lord as a thousand years" / ESV: "with the
 Lord one day is as a thousand years" — Both equivalent. Not a
 mathematical formula but a statement about God's relationship to time.
 He is not bound by it.

v.9 — KJV: "slack" / ESV: "slow" — Greek "bradunei" — to be slow, to
 delay. God is not slow — He is patient. KJV: "longsuffering" / ESV:
 "patient" — Greek "makrothumei" — patient, long-tempered. His delay is
 mercy, not inability. KJV: "not willing that any should perish" / ESV:
 "not wishing that any should perish" — Both equivalent in meaning.

v.10 — KJV: "elements shall melt with fervent heat" / ESV: "the heavenly
 bodies will be burned up and dissolved" — Greek "stoicheia
 luthēsetai" — the basic components will be dissolved/released. KJV:
 "fervent heat" / ESV: "burned up" — Greek "kausoumena" — burning,
 being consumed by fire.

v.11 — KJV: "what manner of persons ought ye to be in all holy conversation
 and godliness" / ESV: "what sort of people ought you to be in lives of
 holiness and godliness" — KJV "conversation" = manner of life/conduct
 (not speech).

v.13 — KJV: "new heavens and a new earth" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek
 "kainous" — new in quality, not just in time. A renewed, transformed
 creation — not annihilation but re-creation.

v.15 — KJV: "our beloved brother Paul" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Peter
 acknowledges Paul's letters as authoritative Scripture (v.16: "the
 other scriptures"). Remarkable given their earlier conflict (Gal 2:11).

v.16 — KJV: "some things hard to be understood" / ESV: "some things in them
 that are hard to understand" — Both equivalent. Peter admits Paul is
 sometimes difficult! KJV: "unlearned and unstable wrest" / ESV:
 "ignorant and unstable twist" — twist, distort. Mishandling Scripture
 leads to destruction.

v.18 — KJV: "grow in grace" / ESV: "grow in the grace" — Both equivalent.
 Peter's final word: keep growing. Not "arrive" but "grow." The
 Christian life is perpetual growth in grace and knowledge.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.8  — NET: "with the Lord one day is like a thousand years
       and a thousand years are like one day" — quoting Psalm 90:4.
       WEB: "one day is with the Lord as a thousand years, and a
       thousand years as one day." God operates outside human time
       — what seems like delay is divine patience.

v.9  — NET: "The Lord is not slow concerning his promise, as some
       regard slowness, but is being patient toward you because he
       does not wish for any to perish" — "does not wish" (mE
       boulomenos = not wanting/not purposing). WEB: "not wishing
       that anyone should perish." KJV: "not willing that any
       should perish." God's patience has a redemptive purpose.

v.10 — NET: "the day of the Lord will come like a thief" —
       "like a thief" (hOs kleptEs) — unexpected timing. WEB:
       "the day of the Lord will come as a thief in the night."
       "The heavens will disappear with a rushing sound" (rhoizEdon
       = with a roar/whizzing). KJV: "with a great noise." Cosmic
       dissolution — elements melting.

v.13 — NET: "we are waiting for new heavens and a new earth, in
       which righteousness truly resides" — "truly resides"
       (dikaiosynE katoikei = righteousness dwells/is at home).
       WEB: "new heavens and a new earth, in which righteousness
       dwells." Righteousness will be the atmosphere of the new
       creation — evil permanently excluded.

v.18 — NET: "grow in the grace and knowledge of our Lord and
       Savior Jesus Christ" — "grow" (auxanete = increase). WEB:
       "grow in the grace and knowledge of our Lord and Savior
       Jesus Christ." The letter's final command: keep growing.
       Stagnation is dangerous (ch.2); growth is the alternative.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Scoffers — Those who mock Christ's return. Morally motivated: they deny
 accountability because they want to live without restraint.

God is not slack — The delay of Christ's return is not inability or
 forgetfulness but patience — giving time for repentance.

Not willing that any should perish — God's desire for universal salvation.
 His patience extends the opportunity for repentance.

Day of the Lord — The future day of judgment and cosmic transformation.
 Sudden, catastrophic, and final.

New heavens and new earth — The renewed creation where righteousness dwells
 permanently. The ultimate hope of believers.

Grow in grace — Peter's final command: continuous spiritual growth in God's
 grace and knowledge of Christ. Never stop growing.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Not willing that any should perish" (v.9). God's patience with the world
 is for salvation. How does this change your view of the "delay" of
 Christ's return? Are you using the time to share the gospel?

2. "What manner of persons ought ye to be?" (v.11). If everything will be
 dissolved, what matters? How should the certainty of the end shape your
 priorities, values, and daily choices?

3. "New heavens and a new earth, wherein dwelleth righteousness" (v.13). This
 is your ultimate destination — a world where righteousness is at home.
 Does this hope sustain you in a world where unrighteousness seems to win?

4. "Grow in grace, and in the knowledge of our Lord" (v.18). Peter's last
 word: keep growing. Are you growing? In grace? In knowledge of Christ?
 What's your growth plan?

5. "Beware lest ye also, being led away with the error of the wicked, fall
 from your own stedfastness" (v.17). What errors threaten your stability?
 How do you guard against being led astray?
