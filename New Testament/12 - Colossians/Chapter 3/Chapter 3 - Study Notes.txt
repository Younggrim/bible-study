Colossians - Chapter 3
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Since believers are raised with Christ, they must put to death earthly sins, put on compassion and love, let the word of Christ dwell richly, and do everything in the name of the Lord Jesus — with instructions for household relationships.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Paul the Apostle (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 3 is the practical application of Christ's
supremacy and sufficiency (chs.1-2). Since you are risen with Christ (v.1)
and complete in Him (2:10), here is how to live. The structure parallels
Ephesians 4-6: put off the old (vv.5-11), put on the new (vv.12-17), and
live it out in relationships (vv.18-25). The key principle: "Christ is all,
and in all" (v.11).

Seek Things Above (vv.1-4): Since you died and rose with Christ, set your
mind on heavenly things. Your life is "hid with Christ in God" (v.3) — secure,
hidden, waiting to be revealed in glory (v.4).

Put Off the Old (vv.5-11): Two lists of sins to "mortify" and "put off":
(1) Sexual/selfish sins: fornication, uncleanness, inordinate affection, evil
desire, covetousness (v.5). (2) Relational sins: anger, wrath, malice,
blasphemy, filthy communication, lying (vv.8-9). The old man is gone; the new
man is being renewed (vv.9-10).

Put On the New (vv.12-17): As God's chosen people, put on: mercy, kindness,
humility, meekness, patience, forbearance, forgiveness (vv.12-13). Above all:
love (v.14). Let Christ's peace rule, His word dwell richly, and do
everything in His name (vv.15-17).

Household Code (vv.18-25): Wives/husbands, children/fathers, servants/masters
— all relationships transformed by "as unto the Lord" (v.23).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1-2 — "If then you have been raised with Christ, seek the things that are
        above... Set your minds on things that are above, not on things that
        are on earth." (ESV) — Heavenly mindset based on resurrection
        identity.

v.3  — "For you have died, and your life is hidden with Christ in God."
        (ESV) — Your true life is hidden, secure, in Christ.

v.11 — "Christ is all, and in all." — The summary of Colossians: Christ is
        everything, in everyone who believes.

v.12-13 — "Put on then, as God's chosen ones, holy and beloved, compassionate
        hearts, kindness, humility, meekness, and patience, bearing with one
        another and forgiving each other." (ESV) — The new wardrobe.

v.14 — "And above all these put on love, which binds everything together in
        perfect harmony." (ESV) — Love as the binding garment over all
        virtues.

v.16 — "Let the word of Christ dwell in you richly." (ESV) — Scripture
        saturating the believer's life.

v.17 — "And whatever you do, in word or deed, do everything in the name of
        the Lord Jesus." (ESV) — Everything under Christ's authority and for
        His glory.

v.23 — "Whatever you do, work heartily, as for the Lord and not for men."
        (ESV) — Work as worship.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------

v.1  — KJV: "If ye then be risen" — Greek "ei oun synēgerthēte" — "Since
       you were raised" (first class condition = assumed true). Not "if maybe"
       but "since indeed." ESV: "If then you have been raised with Christ."

v.2  — KJV: "Set your affection" / ESV: "Set your minds on things that are" — Greek "phroneite" — think, set your mind,
       focus your attention. A deliberate, ongoing choice of mental focus.

v.3  — KJV: "hid" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "kekruptai" — hidden, concealed, stored safely.
       Perfect tense = permanently hidden. Root of English "crypt."

v.5  — KJV: "mortify" / ESV: "Put to death therefore" — Greek "nekrōsate" — put to death, make dead. Aorist
       imperative = do it decisively, once for all. Kill these sins — don't
       negotiate with them.

v.9  — KJV: "put off the old man" / ESV: "you have put off the old self with its" — Greek "apekdusamenoi" — stripped off
       like dirty clothes. Aorist participle = already done. You HAVE stripped
       off the old self — now live accordingly.

v.11 — KJV: "Barbarian, Scythian" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Barbarians were non-Greek speakers;
       Scythians were considered the most savage of barbarians. Even the
       deepest cultural divisions are erased in Christ.

v.14 — KJV: "charity" / ESV: "which binds" — Greek "agapē" — love. "Bond of perfectness" —
       the garment that ties all other virtues together into a complete whole.

v.16 — KJV: "dwell in you richly" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "enoikeitō... plousiōs" — make
       its home in you abundantly. Not a visitor but a permanent, wealthy
       resident. The Word should saturate every area of life.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.1  — NET: "if then you have been raised with Christ, keep
       seeking the things above" — "keep seeking" (zEteite = present
       imperative — continue seeking). WEB: "seek the things that
       are above." KJV: "seek those things which are above." The
       indicative (raised) grounds the imperative (seek) — what you
       ARE determines what you DO.

v.3  — NET: "your life is hidden with Christ in God" — "hidden"
       (kekryptai = concealed/stored securely). WEB: "your life is
       hidden with Christ in God." KJV: "your life is hid with
       Christ in God." Triple security: your life, in Christ, in
       God. Nothing can reach it.

v.11 — NET: "there is neither Greek nor Jew, circumcised or
       uncircumcised, barbarian, Scythian, slave or free, but
       Christ is all and in all" — "Christ is all and in all"
       (panta kai en pasin Christos). WEB: "Christ is all, and in
       all." The categories collapse — only Christ matters.

v.16 — NET: "Let the word of Christ dwell in you richly" —
       "richly" (plousiOs = abundantly/lavishly). WEB: "Let the
       word of Christ dwell in you richly." KJV: "Let the word of
       Christ dwell in you richly." Not a little Scripture but
       saturating, overflowing presence of Christ's word.

v.17 — NET: "whatever you do in word or deed, do it all in the
       name of the Lord Jesus" — "whatever" (pan ho ti) — no
       exception. WEB: "whatever you do, in word or in deed, do
       all in the name of the Lord Jesus." The universal standard —
       every action under His authority and for His glory.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Risen with Christ — The believer's spiritual resurrection — already
               accomplished positionally, to be lived out practically.

Hid with Christ in God — The believer's life is secure, concealed in the
               safest possible location: in Christ, who is in God.

Mortify        — Put to death. A decisive, violent act against sin. Not
               manage, moderate, or negotiate — kill.

Old man/New man — The former self (Adam's nature) stripped off; the new self
               (Christ's nature) put on. Like changing clothes.

Christ is all, and in all — Christ is everything that matters and is present
               in every believer regardless of background.

Bond of perfectness — Love as the garment that holds all other virtues
               together in complete unity.

Word of Christ dwelling richly — Scripture making its permanent, abundant
               home in the believer's life — saturating thought, speech,
               and action.

As to the Lord — The transforming principle: every task, relationship, and
               duty performed as service to Christ, not merely to people.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Set your affection on things above" (v.2). What occupies your mind most?
   Earthly concerns or heavenly realities? What practical steps can you take
   to redirect your mental focus upward?

2. "Mortify therefore your members" (v.5). Which sins on Paul's list do you
   need to "put to death" — not manage or moderate but kill? Are you being
   decisive or negotiating with sin?

3. "Put on... bowels of mercies, kindness, humbleness" (v.12). Which of
   these virtues is hardest for you to "put on"? Remember — you put them on
   as "the elect of God, holy and beloved." Your identity enables your
   behavior.

4. "Forgiving one another... even as Christ forgave you" (v.13). Is there
   someone you need to forgive? The standard is how Christ forgave you —
   freely, fully, at great cost to Himself.

5. "Let the word of Christ dwell in you richly" (v.16). Is Scripture a
   visitor in your life or a permanent resident? How can you let it "dwell
   richly" — not just read occasionally but saturate your thinking?

6. "Whatsoever ye do, do it heartily, as to the Lord" (v.23). How would your
   work change if you truly saw Jesus as your employer? Your attitude? Your
   effort? Your integrity when no one is watching?
