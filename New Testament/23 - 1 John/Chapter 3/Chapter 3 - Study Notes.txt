1 John - Chapter 3
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
"Behold, what manner of love the Father hath bestowed upon us, that we should be called the sons of God" — when He appears we shall be like Him, whoever is born of God does not practice sin, and we know we have passed from death to life because we love the brethren.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: The Apostle John (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Historical Context: Chapter 3 explores what it means to be God's children
(vv.1-3), the incompatibility of sin with new birth (vv.4-10), the command
to love in action (vv.11-18), and assurance before God (vv.19-24). John draws
sharp contrasts: children of God vs. children of the devil, righteousness vs.
sin, love vs. hatred, life vs. death.

Children of God (vv.1-3): "Behold, what manner of love" — we are called
God's children (v.1). The world doesn't recognize us because it didn't
recognize Him (v.1). We don't yet know what we'll be — but when He appears,
"we shall be like him" (v.2). This hope purifies (v.3).

Sin and New Birth (vv.4-10): Sin is lawlessness (v.4). Christ came to take
away sins (v.5). Those who abide in Him don't practice sin (v.6). Those born
of God don't make sin their habit — God's seed remains in them (v.9). This
distinguishes God's children from the devil's (v.10).

Love in Action (vv.11-18): Love one another — not like Cain (vv.11-12). The
world hates us (v.13). Love proves we've passed from death to life (v.14).
Hatred = murder (v.15). Christ's love defined: He laid down His life (v.16).
We should do the same (v.16). If you have resources and see a brother in need
but don't help — how does God's love dwell in you? (v.17). "Let us not love
in word... but in deed and in truth" (v.18).

Assurance (vv.19-24): By loving in deed, we know we're of the truth (v.19).
If our heart condemns us, God is greater than our heart (v.20). If our heart
doesn't condemn us, we have confidence before God (v.21). His commandment:
believe in Christ and love one another (v.23).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1 — "See what kind of love the Father has given to us, that we should be
 called children of God." (ESV) — The wonder of adoption.

v.2 — "We shall be like him, because we shall see him as he is." (ESV) —
 Future transformation at Christ's appearing.

v.9 — "No one born of God makes a practice of sinning, for God's seed abides
 in him." (ESV) — New birth produces new behavior.

v.16 — "By this we know love, that he laid down his life for us, and we
 ought to lay down our lives for the brothers." (ESV) — Love defined
 by sacrifice.

v.18 — "Let us not love in word or talk but in deed and in truth." (ESV) —
 Love in action, not just words.

v.20 — "If our heart condemns us, God is greater than our heart." (ESV) —
 God's grace greater than our guilt.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "what manner of love" / ESV: "what kind of love" — Greek
 "potapēn agapēn" — what kind of, what foreign/exotic love. A love so
 unusual it seems from another world. KJV: "sons of God" / ESV:
 "children of God" — Greek "tekna theou" — children (born of God).

v.2 — KJV: "we shall be like him" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "homoioi
 autō esometha" — we will be similar to Him. Not identical (we don't
 become God) but transformed into His likeness.

v.6 — KJV: "whosoever abideth in him sinneth not" / ESV: "no one who abides
 in him keeps on sinning" — Greek "ouch hamartanei" — does not keep on
 sinning (present tense = habitual action). Not sinless perfection but
 sin is no longer the pattern/habit of life. ESV makes the continuous
 aspect clearer.

v.9 — KJV: "doth not commit sin" / ESV: "does not make a practice of
 sinning" — Greek "hamartian ou poiei" — does not practice sin (present
 tense). "His seed" — God's nature/life implanted in the believer.
 "Cannot sin" — cannot make sin a lifestyle because God's nature within
 resists it.

v.16 — KJV: "hereby perceive we the love of God" / ESV: "by this we know
 love" — Greek "egnōkamen tēn agapēn" — we have come to know love.
 Love is defined by Christ's sacrifice — not by feelings but by self-
 giving action.

v.17 — KJV: "shutteth up his bowels of compassion" / ESV: "closes his heart
 against him" — Greek "kleisē ta splagchna" — closes his inner
 feelings/compassion. "Bowels" in KJV = the seat of emotions in ancient
 thought.

v.20 — KJV: "God is greater than our heart" / ESV: Both equivalent. — When
 your conscience condemns you, God's grace is bigger than your guilt. He
 knows everything (more than your heart knows) and still loves you.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.1  — NET: "See what sort of love the Father has given to us:
       that we should be called God's children" — "what sort"
       (potapEn = what country/what kind — amazement at its foreign
       quality). WEB: "See how great a love the Father has given to
       us." KJV: "Behold, what manner of love." This love is
       otherworldly — it doesn't originate on earth.

v.2  — NET: "when he is revealed we will be like him, because we
       will see him just as he is" — "like him" (homoioi autO).
       WEB: "we will be like him; for we will see him just as he
       is." Seeing produces becoming — the beatific vision
       transforms the viewer.

v.9  — NET: "Everyone who has been fathered by God does not
       practice sin" — "practice sin" (hamartian ou poiei = does
       not keep on sinning). WEB: "Whoever is born of God doesn't
       commit sin." KJV: "doth not commit sin." The present tense
       indicates habitual practice — not sinless perfection but a
       changed pattern of life.

v.14 — NET: "We know that we have crossed over from death to life
       because we love our fellow Christians" — "crossed over"
       (metabebEkamen = have transferred/migrated). WEB: "We know
       that we have passed out of death into life." KJV: "We know
       that we have passed from death unto life." Perfect tense —
       a completed transfer with present results.

v.16 — NET: "We have come to know love by this: that Jesus laid
       down his life for us" — "laid down" (ethEken = placed/set
       down deliberately). WEB: "he laid down his life for us."
       Love defined by action, not emotion — self-sacrificial
       giving is the measure.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Sons of God — Believers' identity as God's adopted children. Not earned
 but bestowed by love.

We shall be like Him — Future transformation into Christ's likeness at His
 appearing. The ultimate destiny of believers.

Doth not commit sin — The born-again person does not make sin a habitual
 lifestyle. Sin is no longer the pattern.

God's seed — The divine nature/life implanted in believers at new birth.
 It resists sin and produces righteousness.

Love in deed and truth — Practical, active love — not just words but
 sacrificial action meeting real needs.

God is greater than our heart — God's grace exceeds our guilt. His knowledge
 is complete and His love is unconditional.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "What manner of love... that we should be called the sons of God" (v.1).
 Do you marvel at your adoption? Or has it become routine? Stop and behold
 the love that made you God's child.

2. "We shall be like him" (v.2). Your destiny is Christlikeness. Does this
 hope purify you now (v.3)? Does knowing where you're headed change how
 you live today?

3. "Whosoever is born of God doth not commit sin" (v.9). Is sin still your
 habit and pattern? Or has new birth produced a new direction? The test is
 not perfection but trajectory.

4. "Let us not love in word... but in deed and in truth" (v.18). Is your
 love practical or just verbal? Who needs your active, sacrificial love
 this week? What deed can you do?

5. "If our heart condemn us, God is greater than our heart" (v.20). When
 guilt overwhelms you, remember: God is bigger than your feelings. His
 grace is greater than your condemnation.
