1 John - Chapter 1
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
John declares the Word of life which was from the beginning — seen, heard, and touched — so that readers may have fellowship with the Father and Son, walking in the light where Christ's blood cleanses from all sin, and confessing sin to find faithful forgiveness.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: The Apostle John (unnamed but identified by style, theology, and
early church tradition). The same author as the Gospel of John and Revelation.
Written approximately AD 85-95 from Ephesus, where John spent his later years.

Recipient: Believers in Asia Minor (likely churches around Ephesus). John
writes as an elderly pastor ("my little children") to a community threatened
by false teachers who denied Christ's true humanity (early Gnosticism).

Purpose: To give assurance of salvation (5:13), to combat false teaching about
Christ's nature, and to call believers to walk in the light, love one another,
and keep God's commandments. John provides "tests of life" — how to know you
truly know God.

Theme: Fellowship with God and assurance of salvation. Three tests repeated:
the doctrinal test (believing rightly about Christ), the moral test (obeying
God's commands), and the social test (loving one another).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1 — "That which was from the beginning, which we have heard, which we have
 seen with our eyes, which we have looked upon, and have touched with
 our hands, concerning the word of life." (ESV) — Eyewitness testimony
 of Christ's reality.

v.3 — "So that you too may have fellowship with us; and indeed our fellowship
 is with the Father and with his Son Jesus Christ." (ESV) — Fellowship
 as the goal.

v.5 — "God is light, and in him is no darkness at all." (ESV) — God's nature:
 pure light.

v.7 — "If we walk in the light... the blood of Jesus his Son cleanses us
 from all sin." (ESV) — Walking in light + Christ's blood = ongoing
 cleansing.

v.9 — "If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to forgive us our
 sins and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness." (ESV) — The promise
 of confession.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "handled" / ESV: "touched" — Four sensory verbs: heard (akoō),
 seen (horaō), looked upon (theaomai — gazed at intently), handled/
 touched (psēlaphaō — physically felt). John emphasizes: Christ was
 REAL, physical, tangible. Against Gnostics who denied His true
 humanity.

v.5 — KJV: "God is light" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Not "God has light" or
 "God gives light" but God IS light. His essential nature is light —
 truth, purity, holiness. "No darkness at all" — Greek "ouk estin...
 oudemia" — absolutely none.

v.7 — KJV: "cleanseth" / ESV: "cleanses" — Greek "katharizei" — present
 tense: keeps on cleansing. Ongoing, continuous purification as we walk
 in the light.

v.9 — KJV: "faithful and just" / ESV: Both equivalent. — God's forgiveness
 is not arbitrary mercy but faithful promise-keeping and just response
 to Christ's atonement. Because Christ paid, God is JUST to forgive.

v.10 — KJV: "we make him a liar" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Denying our sin
 contradicts God's word that all have sinned. It calls God a liar.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.1  — NET: "what existed from the beginning, what we have
       heard, what we have seen with our eyes" — four verbs of
       firsthand experience: heard, seen, observed, touched. WEB:
       "that which was from the beginning, that which we have
       heard." KJV: "That which was from the beginning." John
       stresses physical, sensory witness against Gnostic denial.

v.3  — NET: "so that you may have fellowship with us (and indeed
       our fellowship is with the Father and with his Son)" —
       "fellowship" (koinOnia = shared participation/partnership).
       WEB: "our fellowship is with the Father, and with his Son."
       Horizontal fellowship is grounded in vertical fellowship.

v.5  — NET: "God is light, and in him there is no darkness at
       all" — "no darkness at all" (skotia en autO ouk estin
       oudemia = double negative for emphasis). WEB: "in him is no
       darkness at all." Absolute moral purity — zero shadow.

v.7  — NET: "if we walk in the light as he himself is in the
       light, we have fellowship with one another" — "walk in the
       light" (en tO phOti peripatOmen). WEB: "walk in the light."
       Walking = ongoing lifestyle. Light = transparency/holiness.
       Result = genuine community AND cleansing (v.7b).

v.9  — NET: "he is faithful and righteous, forgiving us our sins
       and cleansing us from all unrighteousness" — "faithful and
       righteous" (pistos... dikaios) — forgiveness is not mercy
       contradicting justice but justice fulfilled through Christ.
       WEB: "faithful and righteous to forgive." God is JUST in
       forgiving because Christ paid.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Word of life — Jesus Christ as the eternal Word who gives life. Both the
 message and the person.

Fellowship — Greek "koinōnia" — sharing, partnership, communion. Deep
 relational connection with God and other believers.

God is light — God's essential nature: truth, purity, holiness, goodness.
 No mixture of darkness whatsoever.

Walk in the light — Living in openness, honesty, and obedience. Not sinless
 perfection but transparent living before God.

Confess — Greek "homologeō" — to say the same thing, to agree with.
 Agreeing with God about your sin — calling it what He calls it.

Faithful and just — God's character guaranteeing forgiveness. Faithful to
 His promise; just because Christ's payment satisfies justice.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "God is light, and in him is no darkness at all" (v.5). Is there darkness
 in your life that you're hiding from God? He is pure light — nothing is
 hidden from Him. Are you walking in the light or hiding in shadows?

2. "If we walk in the light... the blood of Jesus Christ his Son cleanseth
 us from all sin" (v.7). Walking in the light doesn't mean sinless
 perfection — it means living openly before God. When you do, Christ's
 blood keeps cleansing you. Are you walking openly?

3. "If we confess our sins" (v.9). Is there unconfessed sin in your life?
 Confession is agreeing with God — calling sin what He calls it. He
 promises to forgive AND cleanse. What do you need to confess today?

4. "If we say that we have no sin, we deceive ourselves" (v.8). Self-
 deception about sin is dangerous. Are you honest about your sinfulness?
 Or do you minimize, excuse, or deny?

5. "That your joy may be full" (v.4). John writes so that joy would be
 complete. Is your joy full? Fellowship with God and honest living produce
 full joy. What's blocking yours?
