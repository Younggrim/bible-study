1 John - Chapter 4
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
John commands believers to test the spirits (every spirit confessing Jesus Christ came in the flesh is from God), declares "God is love" and that His love was demonstrated in sending His Son as propitiation — "perfect love casteth out fear."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: The Apostle John (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Historical Context: Chapter 4 is the heart of 1 John — containing the
greatest statements about God's love in all of Scripture. It addresses testing
spirits/false prophets (vv.1-6) and then unfolds the nature of God's love and
its implications (vv.7-21). "God is love" (vv.8, 16) is the defining
declaration. The false teachers (Gnostics) claimed special spiritual knowledge
but lacked love; John shows that love is the true test of knowing God.

Testing the Spirits (vv.1-6): Don't believe every spirit — test them (v.1).
The test: does it confess Jesus Christ came in the flesh? (v.2). Denying
Christ's incarnation = the spirit of antichrist (v.3). "Greater is he that is
in you, than he that is in the world" (v.4). False teachers speak the world's
language and the world listens (v.5).

God Is Love (vv.7-21): Love one another — love is from God (v.7). "He that
loveth not knoweth not God; for God is love" (v.8). God's love demonstrated:
He sent His Son so we might live (v.9). Love defined: not that we loved God
but that He loved us and sent His Son as propitiation (v.10). If God so loved
us, we ought to love one another (v.11). God's love is perfected in us when
we love (v.12). "God is love; and he that dwelleth in love dwelleth in God"
(v.16). Perfect love casts out fear (v.18). "We love him, because he first
loved us" (v.19). You can't love God and hate your brother (v.20).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1 — "Do not believe every spirit, but test the spirits to see whether they
 are from God." (ESV) — Test everything.

v.4 — "He who is in you is greater than he who is in the world." (ESV) — The
 Spirit in you is greater than Satan in the world.

v.8 — "Anyone who does not love does not know God, because God is love."
 (ESV) — God's essential nature IS love.

v.10 — "In this is love, not that we have loved God but that he loved us and
 sent his Son to be the propitiation for our sins." (ESV) — Love
 defined by God's initiative.

v.18 — "There is no fear in love, but perfect love casts out fear." (ESV) —
 Love and fear cannot coexist.

v.19 — "We love because he first loved us." (ESV) — God's love precedes and
 produces ours.

Charles Spurgeon:
 Sermon: "God Is Love" on v.8 (MTP Vol. 7, No. 392)
 "God IS love — not God has love, not God shows love — God IS love. Love is
 not one of His attributes among many — it is His very essence. Everything
 He does flows from love. His justice is loving justice. His holiness is
 loving holiness. Even His wrath is the wrath of love against what destroys
 the beloved. And if God is love, then to know God is to know love, and to
 not love is to not know God."
 Sermon: "Perfect Love Casteth Out Fear" on v.18 (MTP Vol. 23, No. 1391)
 "Perfect love casteth OUT fear — not suppresses it, not manages it — casts
 it out. Evicts it. Fear and love cannot occupy the same heart. If you fear
 God's judgment, you haven't yet understood His love. If you fear His
 rejection, you haven't yet grasped His acceptance. Let His love fill you
 and fear will have no room to stay."

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "try the spirits" / ESV: "test the spirits" — Greek "dokimazete
 ta pneumata" — test, examine, prove. The same word used for testing
 metals. Not gullibility but discernment.

v.2 — KJV: "Jesus Christ is come in the flesh" / ESV: "Jesus Christ has
 come in the flesh" — Both equivalent. The incarnation test. The
 Gnostics denied Christ's true humanity. Confessing He came "in the
 flesh" = affirming genuine incarnation.

v.8 — KJV: "God is love" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "ho theos agapē
 estin." Not "God is loving" (an attribute) but "God IS love" (His
 essence). Love defines who God is at His core.

v.10 — KJV: "propitiation" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "hilasmon" —
 satisfaction, appeasement. God's love is not sentimental — it dealt
 with sin through sacrifice.

v.18 — KJV: "perfect love casteth out fear" / ESV: "perfect love casts out
 fear" — Both equivalent. Greek "teleia agapē exō ballei ton phobon" —
 mature/complete love throws fear outside. KJV: "fear hath torment" /
 ESV: "fear has to do with punishment" — Greek "kolasin" — punishment.
 Fear anticipates punishment; love assures acceptance.

v.19 — KJV: "we love him, because he first loved us" / ESV: "we love
 because he first loved us" — Greek "autos prōtos ēgapēsen hēmas" — He
 loved us first. God's love is the cause; ours is the response. He
 initiated; we respond. ESV omits "him" (some manuscripts lack it).

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.1  — NET: "do not believe every spirit, but test the spirits
       to determine if they are from God" — "test" (dokimazete =
       examine/prove/assay). WEB: "test the spirits." KJV: "try
       the spirits." Discernment is commanded, not optional —
       gullibility is not a fruit of the Spirit.

v.8  — NET: "The one who does not love does not know God, because
       God is love" — "God is love" (ho theos agapE estin). WEB:
       "God is love." KJV: "God is love." Not "love is God" (making
       love ultimate) but "God is love" (love defined by God's
       character). God defines love, not the reverse.

v.10 — NET: "In this is love: not that we have loved God, but
       that he loved us and sent his Son to be the atoning
       sacrifice for our sins" — love's definition: God's initiative,
       not our response. WEB: "sent his Son to be the atoning
       sacrifice." Love originates with God, not with us.

v.18 — NET: "There is no fear in love, but perfect love drives
       out fear" — "drives out" (ballei = throws out/casts out —
       violent expulsion). WEB: "perfect love casts out fear." KJV:
       "perfect love casteth out fear." Love and fear cannot coexist
       — God's love evicts our terror of judgment.

v.19 — NET: "We love because he first loved us" — "he first
       loved" (autos prOtos EgapEsen). WEB: "We love him, because
       he first loved us." Some manuscripts include "him" (KJV/WEB);
       others have just "we love" (NET/ESV). Either way: God's
       love precedes and enables ours.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Try/Test the spirits — Evaluate spiritual claims against the standard of
 Christ's incarnation. Discernment, not gullibility.

God is love — God's essential nature. Not merely an attribute but His
 very being. Everything He does flows from love.

He first loved us — God's love as the initiator. Our love is always a
 response to His prior love.

Perfect love casteth out fear — Mature understanding of God's love eliminates
 fear of judgment. Love and fear cannot coexist.

Propitiation — God's love expressed through satisfying His own justice.
 Love that deals with sin, not ignores it.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Try the spirits" (v.1). Do you test what you hear against Scripture? Not
 every spiritual-sounding message is from God. How do you discern truth
 from error?

2. "Greater is he that is in you, than he that is in the world" (v.4). The
 Spirit in you is greater than any force against you. Do you live in this
 confidence or in fear of the enemy?

3. "God is love" (v.8). Not just loving — love itself. How does this shape
 your understanding of everything God does — including discipline, silence,
 and allowing suffering?

4. "Perfect love casteth out fear" (v.18). Where does fear still grip you —
 fear of judgment, rejection, failure? Let God's love cast it out. You are
 loved; you are accepted; you are secure.

5. "We love him, because he first loved us" (v.19). Your love for God is a
 response to His love for you. Are you responding? Is His love producing
 love in you — for Him and for others?
