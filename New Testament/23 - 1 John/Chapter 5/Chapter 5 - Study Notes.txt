1 John - Chapter 5
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Whoever believes Jesus is the Christ is born of God, faith is the victory that overcomes the world, the Spirit and water and blood bear witness, and John writes "that ye may know that ye have eternal life" — closing with a warning against idols.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: The Apostle John (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Historical Context: Chapter 5 concludes 1 John with the themes of faith that
overcomes (vv.1-5), the witness to Christ (vv.6-12), assurance of eternal
life (v.13), confidence in prayer (vv.14-17), and three certainties (vv.18-
21). Verse 13 states the letter's purpose: "that ye may KNOW that ye have
eternal life." John wants believers to have assurance, not doubt.

Faith Overcomes the World (vv.1-5): Believing Jesus is the Christ = born of
God (v.1). Loving God + keeping commandments = loving God's children (v.2).
His commandments are not burdensome (v.3). "Whatsoever is born of God
overcometh the world" — the victory is our faith (v.4).

The Witness to Christ (vv.6-12): Christ came by water (baptism) and blood
(cross) — not water only (v.6). Three witnesses: Spirit, water, blood (v.8).
God's testimony about His Son is greater than human testimony (v.9). "He that
hath the Son hath life" (v.12).

Assurance and Prayer (vv.13-17): "That ye may KNOW that ye have eternal life"
(v.13). Confidence in prayer: ask according to His will and He hears (vv.14-
15). Praying for a brother who sins (vv.16-17).

Three Certainties (vv.18-21): (1) The born-again don't practice sin; the evil
one doesn't touch them (v.18). (2) We are of God; the world lies in the evil
one (v.19). (3) The Son of God has come and given us understanding (v.20).
Final word: "Keep yourselves from idols" (v.21).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.4 — "Everyone who has been born of God overcomes the world. And this is
 the victory that has overcome the world—our faith." (ESV) — Faith as
 the world-overcoming victory.

v.11-12 — "God gave us eternal life, and this life is in his Son. Whoever
 has the Son has life; whoever does not have the Son of God does not
 have life." (ESV) — Life is in the Son — have Him, have life.

v.13 — "I write these things to you who believe in the name of the Son of
 God, that you may know that you have eternal life." (ESV) — The
 purpose of 1 John: assurance.

v.14-15 — "If we ask anything according to his will he hears us. And if we
 know that he hears us... we know that we have the requests." (ESV) —
 Confidence in prayer aligned with God's will.

v.20 — "We know that the Son of God has come and has given us understanding,
 so that we may know him who is true." (ESV) — Knowing the true God
 through Christ.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.3 — KJV: "his commandments are not grievous" / ESV: "his commandments are
 not burdensome" — Greek "bareiai ouk eisin" — not heavy, not
 burdensome. God's commands are not oppressive — they are the path of
 freedom, not bondage.

v.4 — KJV: "overcometh the world" / ESV: "overcomes the world" — Greek
 "nikōsa" — conquers, is victorious over. Present tense: keeps on
 overcoming. "Our faith" — Greek "hē pistis hēmōn" — THE faith, our
 faith. Faith is the instrument of victory.

v.6 — KJV: "came by water and blood" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Christ's
 baptism (beginning of ministry) and cross (end of ministry). Against
 Gnostics who said the divine Christ left Jesus before the cross. No —
 He came by BOTH water AND blood.

v.7-8 — KJV includes the "Johannine Comma" (v.7b-8a): "the Father, the
 Word, and the Holy Ghost: and these three are one. And there are three
 that bear witness in earth" — ESV omits this, following most early
 Greek manuscripts. Most scholars consider it a later addition. The
 doctrine of the Trinity is established elsewhere regardless.

v.13 — KJV: "that ye may KNOW that ye have eternal life" / ESV: "that you
 may know that you have eternal life" — Both equivalent. Greek "eidēte"
 — that you may know with certainty. John's stated purpose: assurance
 of salvation.

v.16 — KJV: "sin unto death" / ESV: "sin that leads to death" — Debated.
 Options: (1) physical death as discipline (cf. Acts 5, 1 Cor 11:30),
 (2) apostasy/blasphemy against the Spirit, (3) any sin that results in
 premature physical death.

v.19 — KJV: "the whole world lieth in wickedness" / ESV: "the whole world
 lies in the power of the evil one" — Greek "en tō ponērō keitai" —
 lies in the evil one. ESV makes the personal reference to Satan
 clearer.

v.21 — KJV: "keep yourselves from idols" / ESV: Both equivalent. — An
 abrupt ending. Anything that takes God's place is an idol. After a
 letter about knowing the true God, the final word: don't substitute
 anything for Him.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.3  — NET: "For this is the love of God: that we keep his
       commandments. And his commandments do not weigh us down" —
       "do not weigh us down" (bareiai ouk eisin = are not heavy/
       burdensome). WEB: "his commandments are not grievous." KJV:
       "his commandments are not grievous." Obedience is not
       oppressive for the born-again — love makes duty light.

v.4  — NET: "this is the conquering power that has conquered the
       world: our faith" — "conquering power" (nikE = victory).
       WEB: "this is the victory that has overcome the world: our
       faith." KJV: "this is the victory that overcometh the world,
       even our faith." Faith IS the victory — not produces it
       but IS it.

v.7  — NET: "For there are three that testify" — the longer
       reading (Comma Johanneum: "in heaven, the Father, the Word,
       and the Holy Ghost") is found in KJV but NOT in NET/ESV/WEB/
       ASV. It is absent from all Greek manuscripts before the 16th
       century. NET omits it following all early manuscript evidence.

v.13 — NET: "I have written these things to you who believe in
       the name of the Son of God so that you may know that you
       have eternal life" — "that you may KNOW" (hina eidEte =
       that you may have settled knowledge). WEB: "that you may
       know that you have eternal life." Assurance is possible and
       intended — John writes for certainty, not doubt.

v.21 — NET: "Little children, guard yourselves from idols" —
       "guard yourselves" (phylaxate heauta = keep/protect
       yourselves). WEB: "Little children, keep yourselves from
       idols." KJV: "Little children, keep yourselves from idols."
       The abrupt ending — the opposite of loving God is idolatry.
       A final warning.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Overcometh the world — Faith's victory over the world's temptations, values,
 and opposition. Ongoing conquest through trust in Christ.

He that hath the Son hath life — Eternal life is inseparable from Christ.
 Possessing Christ = possessing life.

That ye may know — John's purpose: certainty of salvation. Not arrogant
 presumption but confident assurance based on God's word.

According to His will — The condition for confident prayer. Aligning our
 requests with God's purposes guarantees His hearing.

Sin unto death — A sin that results in physical death as divine discipline.
 Distinguished from sins that don't carry this consequence.

Keep from idols — Anything that replaces God in your affection, trust, or
 devotion. The final warning: don't substitute.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "This is the victory that overcometh the world, even our faith" (v.4).
 Is your faith overcoming the world — its temptations, its values, its
 pressure? Or is the world overcoming you?

2. "He that hath the Son hath life" (v.12). Do you have the Son? Then you
 have life — right now, present tense. Are you living in that assurance?

3. "That ye may KNOW that ye have eternal life" (v.13). Do you know? Not
 hope, not wish — know. John wrote so you could have certainty. What gives
 you assurance?

4. "If we ask any thing according to his will, he heareth us" (v.14). Are
 your prayers aligned with God's will? How do you discern His will so you
 can pray with confidence?

5. "Keep yourselves from idols" (v.21). What idols compete for God's place
 in your life — money, comfort, approval, control, entertainment? The
 final word of 1 John: guard against substitutes.
