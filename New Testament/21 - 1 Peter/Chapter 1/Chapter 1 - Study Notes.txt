1 Peter - Chapter 1
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Peter praises God for a living hope through Christ's resurrection, encourages believers whose faith is refined by trials like gold by fire, and calls them to holiness — "ye were not redeemed with corruptible things, as silver and gold... but with the precious blood of Christ."

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Peter the Apostle (v.1), with Silvanus (Silas) as secretary (5:12).
Written from "Babylon" (5:13) — likely a code name for Rome. Approximately
AD 62-64, during or just before Nero's persecution.

Recipient: "Strangers scattered" throughout five Roman provinces in Asia Minor
(modern Turkey): Pontus, Galatia, Cappadocia, Asia, and Bithynia (v.1). A
mix of Jewish and Gentile Christians facing increasing persecution.

Purpose: To encourage believers suffering for their faith. Peter's message:
suffering is temporary, glory is coming, and your identity in Christ gives you
hope that transcends circumstances. The letter is saturated with hope — the
word appears repeatedly.

Theme: Hope and holiness in the midst of suffering. Living as "strangers and
pilgrims" (2:11) in a hostile world while awaiting an eternal inheritance.

 (ESV) — The call to holiness.

v.18-19 — "you were ransomed...not with perishable things such as silver or
 gold, but with the precious blood of Christ, like that of a lamb
 without blemish or spot." (ESV) — Redemption by Christ's blood.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "strangers scattered" / ESV: "elect exiles of the Dispersion" —
 Greek "parepidēmois diasporas" — sojourners of the dispersion. Aliens,
 temporary residents. Their true home is heaven; earth is foreign
 territory.

v.2 — KJV: "elect according to the foreknowledge of God" / ESV: "according
 to the foreknowledge of God" — Both equivalent. Election rooted in
 God's foreknowledge. Trinitarian: Father's foreknowledge, Spirit's
 sanctification, Son's blood.

v.3 — KJV: "lively hope" / ESV: "living hope" — Greek "elpida zōsan" — a
 living hope. Not dead or dying but alive — because it's based on a
 living Christ (risen from the dead).

v.4 — KJV: "incorruptible, and undefiled, and that fadeth not away" / ESV:
 "imperishable, undefiled, and unfading" — Four negatives describing
 the inheritance: incorruptible (cannot decay), undefiled (cannot be
 stained), fadeth not away (cannot wilt), reserved in heaven (cannot
 be lost).

v.5 — KJV: "kept" / ESV: "being guarded" — Greek "phrouroumenous" —
 guarded, garrisoned. A military term: God's power stands guard over
 believers like soldiers protecting a city.

v.7 — KJV: "trial of your faith" / ESV: "tested genuineness of your faith"
 — Gold is purified by fire — impurities burn away, pure gold remains.
 Faith is purified by trials — what's genuine remains and shines
 brighter.

v.13 — KJV: "gird up the loins of your mind" / ESV: "preparing your minds
 for action" — Greek "anazōsamenoi tas osphuas tēs dianoias" — tucking
 up the robes of your mind. A metaphor for mental readiness and
 disciplined thinking.

v.19 — KJV: "precious blood" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "timiō
 haimati" — costly, valuable, honored blood. The most expensive
 substance in the universe — the blood of God's Son.

v.23 — KJV: "born again" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek
 "anagegennēmenoi" — having been born again. Perfect tense = a
 completed action with ongoing results. KJV: "incorruptible seed" /
 ESV: "imperishable seed" — the Word of God as the imperishable seed
 of new birth.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.1  — NET: "those temporarily residing abroad... who are
       chosen" — "temporarily residing abroad" (parepidEmois
       diasopras = sojourners of the dispersion). WEB: "foreigners
       of the Dispersion." KJV: "strangers scattered." Believers
       as aliens — this world is not home.

v.7  — NET: "so that your faith, which is more precious than gold
       — gold that is tested by fire — may be found to result in
       praise" — "tested by fire" (dia pyros de dokimazomenou).
       WEB: "more precious than gold which perishes, even though
       it is tested by fire." Gold is refined by heat; faith by
       trials. Faith is MORE precious than refined gold.

v.12 — NET: "things angels long to catch a glimpse of" — "long
       to catch a glimpse" (epithymousin parakupsai = desire to
       stoop down and peer into). WEB: "which things angels desire
       to look into." KJV: "which things the angels desire to look
       into." Angels crane forward to see — the gospel amazes even
       heavenly beings.

v.18 — NET: "you were redeemed... not by perishable things like
       silver or gold" — "redeemed" (elytrOthEte = ransomed/freed
       by payment). WEB: "knowing that you were redeemed." KJV:
       "ye were not redeemed with corruptible things." The cost of
       freedom: not money but blood (v.19).

v.23 — NET: "you have been born anew, not from perishable but
       from imperishable seed, through the living and enduring word
       of God" — "imperishable seed" (sporas aphthartou). WEB:
       "having been born again... of incorruptible seed." KJV:
       "being born again... by the word of God." The seed that
       causes new birth cannot decay.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Strangers/Sojourners — Believers as temporary residents on earth. Their true
 citizenship is in heaven.

Living hope — Hope that is alive because it's based on the risen Christ.
 Not wishful thinking but confident expectation.

Inheritance incorruptible — The believer's eternal reward: cannot decay,
 cannot be stained, cannot fade, cannot be stolen.

Kept by the power of God — Divine preservation. God's power guards believers
 through faith unto final salvation.

Trial of faith — Testing that purifies faith like fire purifies gold.
 Removes impurities and reveals genuineness.

Precious blood — The costly, valuable blood of Christ. The price of
 redemption — infinitely more valuable than silver or gold.

Born again of incorruptible seed — New birth through God's imperishable
 Word. A permanent, irreversible spiritual birth.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "A lively hope by the resurrection" (v.3). Is your hope alive? Is it
 based on the risen Christ? Or has it become dead, routine, merely
 intellectual?

2. "An inheritance... reserved in heaven for you" (v.4). You have an
 inheritance that cannot be corrupted, stained, or taken away. Does this
 eternal perspective change how you handle temporary losses?

3. "The trial of your faith, being much more precious than of gold" (v.7).
 Your current trial is refining your faith — making it purer and more
 precious. Can you see the refining purpose in your suffering?

4. "Whom having not seen, ye love" (v.8). You love someone you've never seen.
 Is that love growing or cooling? How do you cultivate love for the unseen
 Christ?

5. "Be ye holy; for I am holy" (vv.15-16). Holiness is not optional — it's
 the family resemblance. God is holy; His children must be holy. Where does
 your life need to more closely reflect His character?
