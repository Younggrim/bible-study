1 Peter - Chapter 5
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Peter exhorts elders to shepherd God's flock willingly (not for gain), calls younger believers to humble themselves, warns "your adversary the devil walketh about as a roaring lion, seeking whom he may devour," and promises God will restore and establish them after suffering.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Peter the Apostle (see Chapter 1 notes for full details).

Historical Context: Chapter 5 concludes 1 Peter with instructions for elders
(vv.1-4), instructions for the younger/all (vv.5-7), a warning about the
devil (vv.8-9), a promise of restoration (v.10), and final greetings (vv.11-
14). Peter writes as a "fellow elder" and "witness of the sufferings of
Christ" (v.1) — the fisherman who denied Jesus is now the shepherd who
strengthens others.

Instructions for Elders (vv.1-4): Feed/shepherd the flock (v.2). Serve
willingly, not under compulsion (v.2). Not for money but eagerly (v.2). Not
domineering but as examples (v.3). When the Chief Shepherd appears, you'll
receive an unfading crown of glory (v.4).

Instructions for All (vv.5-7): Younger: submit to elders (v.5). All: be
clothed with humility — God resists the proud, gives grace to the humble
(v.5). Humble yourselves under God's mighty hand — He will exalt you in due
time (v.6). Cast all your anxiety on Him — He cares for you (v.7).

The Devil and Resistance (vv.8-9): Be sober and vigilant (v.8). The devil
prowls like a roaring lion seeking prey (v.8). Resist him, firm in faith,
knowing your brothers worldwide suffer the same (v.9).

Promise of Restoration (v.10): "The God of all grace... after that ye have
suffered a while, make you perfect, stablish, strengthen, settle you."

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.2 — "shepherd the flock of God that is among you, exercising oversight,
 not under compulsion, but willingly." (ESV) — Shepherd willingly.

v.5 — "God opposes the proud but gives grace to the humble." (ESV) —
 Humility attracts grace; pride repels it.

v.6 — "Humble yourselves, therefore, under the mighty hand of God so that
 at the proper time he may exalt you." (ESV) — Humble now, exalted
 later.

v.7 — "casting all your anxieties on him, because he cares for you." (ESV)
 — Give God your anxiety — He cares.

v.8 — "Be sober-minded; be watchful. Your adversary the devil prowls around
 like a roaring lion, seeking someone to devour." (ESV) — The devil's
 strategy: prowl and devour.

v.10 — "And after you have suffered a little while, the God of all grace...
 will himself restore, confirm, strengthen, and establish you." (ESV)
 — Suffering is temporary; God's restoration is certain.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.2 — KJV: "feed the flock" / ESV: "shepherd the flock" — Greek
 "poimanate" — shepherd (not just feed). The full work of a shepherd:
 feeding, leading, protecting, caring. KJV: "taking the oversight" /
 ESV: "exercising oversight" — Greek "episkopountes" — overseeing,
 watching over.

v.3 — KJV: "lords over God's heritage" / ESV: "domineering over those in
 your charge" — Greek "katakurieuontes" — domineering, lording it over.
 KJV: "heritage" / ESV: "those in your charge" — Greek "klērōn" — the
 allotted portions, the flock assigned to each elder.

v.4 — KJV: "chief Shepherd" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek
 "archipoimenos" — the supreme shepherd. Christ is the Chief Shepherd;
 elders are under-shepherds. KJV: "a crown of glory that fadeth not
 away" / ESV: "the unfading crown of glory" — Greek "amarantinon" —
 unfading, imperishable.

v.5 — KJV: "be clothed with humility" / ESV: "clothe yourselves... with
 humility" — Greek "egkombōsasthe" — to tie on like a servant's apron.
 The word may echo John 13 where Jesus tied on a towel to wash feet.

v.7 — KJV: "casting all your care upon him; for he careth for you" / ESV:
 "casting all your anxieties on him, because he cares for you" — Greek
 "epirripsantes" — to throw upon, to hurl onto. Aorist participle: do
 it decisively, once for all. KJV: "care" / ESV: "anxieties" — Greek
 "merimnan" — anxiety, worry. "Careth" / "cares" — Greek "melei" — it
 matters to Him, He is concerned.

v.8 — KJV: "adversary" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "antidikos" —
 opponent in a lawsuit, legal adversary. "Roaring lion" — lions roar to
 paralyze prey with fear. Satan uses fear as a weapon.

v.10 — KJV: "make you perfect, stablish, strengthen, settle you" / ESV:
 "restore, confirm, strengthen, and establish you" — Four verbs of
 restoration: "perfect/restore" (katartisei — mend), "stablish/confirm"
 (stērixei — make firm), "strengthen" (sthenōsei — make strong),
 "settle/establish" (themeliōsei — establish on a foundation). God's
 complete restoration after suffering.

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.2  — NET: "shepherd the flock of God among you, exercising
       oversight not by compulsion but willingly" — "shepherd"
       (poimanate = tend/pastor/feed). WEB: "Shepherd the flock of
       God which is among you." KJV: "Feed the flock of God." Three
       contrasts: not compulsion but willing, not greed but eager,
       not domineering but exemplary.

v.5  — NET: "clothe yourselves with humility toward one another"
       — "clothe yourselves" (enkombOsasthe = tie on like an apron/
       slave's garment). WEB: "be clothed with humility." KJV:
       "be clothed with humility." The rare word may echo Jesus
       tying on a towel to wash feet (John 13).

v.7  — NET: "by casting all your cares on him because he cares
       for you" — "casting" (epirripsantes = throwing upon — a
       decisive action). WEB: "casting all your worries on him."
       KJV: "Casting all your care upon him." An active throwing —
       not gently placing but hurling your anxieties onto God.

v.8  — NET: "your adversary the devil prowls around like a
       roaring lion, looking for someone to devour" — "prowls"
       (peripatei = walks about). "Devour" (katapiein = swallow
       whole). WEB: "the devil, walks around like a roaring lion,
       seeking whom he may devour." A predator seeking isolated,
       weakened prey.

v.10 — NET: "after you have suffered for a little while, the God
       of all grace... will himself restore, confirm, strengthen,
       and establish you" — four verbs of divine action: restore
       (katartisei), confirm (stErixei), strengthen (sthenOsei),
       establish (themeliOsei). WEB: "restore, confirm, strengthen,
       and establish you." God Himself does the rebuilding.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Feed/Shepherd the flock — The elder's primary responsibility: comprehensive
 care of God's people — feeding, leading, protecting.

Chief Shepherd — Christ as the supreme shepherd over all under-shepherds
 (elders). He will reward faithful service.

Clothed with humility — Humility worn like a servant's apron. Visible,
 intentional, active humility.

Casting all your care — Throwing your anxieties onto God decisively. Not
 carrying them yourself but transferring them to Him.

Roaring lion — Satan's strategy: prowling, seeking, using fear to
 paralyze. Active, purposeful, dangerous.

Resist stedfast — Stand firm against the devil in faith. Not passive but
 active resistance from a position of faith.

After ye have suffered a while — Suffering is temporary ("a while") and
 purposeful. God's restoration follows.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Casting all your care upon him; for he careth for you" (v.7). What
 anxieties are you carrying that you need to cast on God? He cares — your
 concerns matter to Him. Will you throw them onto Him today?

2. "Be sober, be vigilant" (v.8). Are you spiritually alert? Or drowsy and
 careless? The devil is active — are you watchful? Where are you most
 vulnerable to his attacks?

3. "Humble yourselves under the mighty hand of God" (v.6). Are you fighting
 God's hand or submitting to it? Humility now leads to exaltation later.
 Where do you need to humble yourself?

4. "After that ye have suffered a while" (v.10). Your suffering has an
 expiration date — "a while." And after it: God will perfect, establish,
 strengthen, and settle you. Can you endure knowing restoration is coming?

5. For leaders: Are you shepherding willingly, eagerly, and by example
 (vv.2-3)? Or under compulsion, for money, and by domineering? What kind
 of leader are you?
