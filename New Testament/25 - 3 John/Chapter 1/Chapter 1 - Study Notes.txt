3 John - Chapter 1
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
John commends Gaius for his faithful hospitality toward traveling missionaries, condemns Diotrephes who loves preeminence and rejects apostolic authority, and commends Demetrius whose good testimony is confirmed by all.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: "The elder" (v.1) — the Apostle John. Written approximately AD 85-95.
The shortest book in the NT by verse count.

Recipient: Gaius — a beloved believer commended for his hospitality to
traveling missionaries (vv.5-8). A common name; this Gaius cannot be certainly
identified with others of the same name in the NT.

Purpose: To commend Gaius for his hospitality (vv.5-8), to condemn Diotrephes
for his arrogance and refusal of John's authority (vv.9-10), and to recommend
Demetrius as trustworthy (v.12). A personal letter about church leadership —
contrasting good and bad examples.

Theme: Hospitality, truth, and church leadership. Three men contrasted: Gaius
(faithful hospitality), Diotrephes (selfish ambition), Demetrius (good
reputation).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.2 — "I pray that all may go well with you and that you may be in good
 health, as it goes well with your soul." (ESV) — Wholistic well-being
 rooted in spiritual health.

v.4 — "I have no greater joy than to hear that my children are walking in
 the truth." (ESV) — A pastor's greatest joy.

v.8 — "We ought to support people like these, that we may be fellow workers
 for the truth." (ESV) — Supporting missionaries = partnering in truth.

v.9 — "Diotrephes, who likes to put himself first, does not acknowledge our
 authority." (ESV) — The danger of self-promoting leadership.

v.11 — "Whoever does good is from God; whoever does evil has not seen God."
 (ESV) — Actions reveal spiritual reality.

TRANSLATION NOTES
------------------------------------
v.2 — KJV: "prosper and be in health" / ESV: "that all may go well with you
 and that you may be in good health" — Greek "euodousthai kai
 hugiainein" — to go well and to be healthy. "Even as thy soul
 prospereth" — Gaius's spiritual health was the standard. John wishes
 his physical life would match his spiritual vitality.

v.4 — KJV: "my children walk in truth" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "en
 alētheia peripatountas" — walking in truth. A pastor's greatest joy.

v.7 — KJV: "taking nothing of the Gentiles" / ESV: "accepting nothing from
 the Gentiles" — These missionaries refused support from unbelievers —
 depending entirely on Christian hospitality. This made believers'
 support essential.

v.8 — KJV: "fellowhelpers to the truth" / ESV: "fellow workers for the
 truth" — Greek "sunergoi tē alētheia" — co-workers with the truth.
 Supporting missionaries = partnering in their work.

v.9 — KJV: "loveth to have the preeminence" / ESV: "likes to put himself
 first" — Greek "philoprōteuōn" — loving to be first. A word used only
 here in the NT. Diotrephes's sin: self-promotion, desire for control.

v.10 — KJV: "prating against us with malicious words" / ESV: "talking wicked
 nonsense against us" — Greek "phluarōn" — talking nonsense, making
 false charges. Diotrephes slandered John and excommunicated those who
 disagreed with him.

v.11 — KJV: "follow not that which is evil, but that which is good" / ESV:
 "do not imitate evil but imitate good" — Greek "mimou" — imitate.
 Don't imitate Diotrephes (evil); imitate Demetrius (good).

ADDITIONAL TRANSLATION NOTES (ASV, NET, WEB)
------------------------------------
v.2  — NET: "I pray that all may go well with you and that you
       may be in good health, just as it is well with your soul" —
       "go well" (euodousthai = prosper/succeed). WEB: "I pray that
       you may prosper in all things and be healthy." KJV: "I wish
       above all things that thou mayest prosper and be in health."
       A standard greeting, not a health-wealth promise.

v.4  — NET: "I have no greater joy than this: to hear that my
       children are living according to the truth" — "living
       according to the truth" (en alEtheia peripatounta = walking
       in truth). WEB: "to hear of my children walking in truth."
       The aged apostle's greatest joy — spiritual faithfulness.

v.7  — NET: "For they went out for the sake of the Name,
       accepting nothing from the Gentiles" — "the Name" (tou
       onomatos = THE name — Jesus). WEB: "for the sake of the
       Name." KJV: "for his name's sake." Missionaries who refuse
       support from unbelievers to maintain integrity.

v.9  — NET: "Diotrephes, who loves to be first among them, does
       not acknowledge us" — "loves to be first" (ho philoprOteuOn
       = the one loving preeminence). WEB: "Diotrephes, who loves
       to be first among them." A one-word character diagnosis:
       lover of first place. Authority addiction.

v.11 — NET: "Dear friend, do not imitate what is bad but what is
       good. The one who does good is of God; the one who does
       what is bad has not seen God" — "imitate" (mimou = mimic/
       copy). WEB: "Don't imitate that which is evil, but that
       which is good." Two models: Diotrephes (bad) and Demetrius
       (good). Choose your example wisely.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Gaius — A faithful believer commended for hospitality to traveling
 missionaries. The positive example.

Diotrephes — A self-promoting church leader who rejected John's authority
 and excommunicated dissenters. The negative example.

Demetrius — A man with a universally good reputation. The recommended
 example.

Fellowhelpers to the truth — Those who support gospel workers become partners
 in their ministry. Hospitality as mission partnership.

Loveth the preeminence — The desire to be first, to control, to dominate.
 The opposite of servant leadership.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Even as thy soul prospereth" (v.2). Is your soul prospering? Is your
 spiritual health the strongest part of your life? Or is it lagging behind
 your physical/professional life?

2. "I have no greater joy than to hear that my children walk in truth" (v.4).
 Who are your spiritual children — people you've influenced? Are they
 walking in truth? What joy does their faithfulness bring you?

3. "We ought to receive such, that we might be fellowhelpers to the truth"
 (v.8). How do you support gospel workers — missionaries, pastors, teachers?
 Supporting them makes you a partner in their work.

4. "Diotrephes, who loveth to have the preeminence" (v.9). Is there any
 Diotrephes in you — desire for control, love of being first, refusal to
 submit to authority? Self-promotion destroys community.

5. Which of the three men do you most resemble: Gaius (generous, hospitable,
 faithful), Diotrephes (self-promoting, controlling, divisive), or
 Demetrius (well-regarded, consistent, trustworthy)?
