Romans - Chapter 13
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Christians are to submit to governing authorities as God's servants, fulfill the Law through love of neighbor, and live urgently — "knowing the time, that now it is high time to awake out of sleep" — putting on the armor of light.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Paul (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 13 addresses the Christian's relationship to
government (vv.1-7), the debt of love (vv.8-10), and the urgency of the times
(vv.11-14). Paul writes to Christians living in Rome — the capital of the empire
— where the relationship between faith and political power was a daily reality.

Submission to Government (vv.1-7): Paul teaches that governing authorities are
"ordained of God" (v.1) and serve as His "ministers" (v.4) to restrain evil and
promote good. Christians should submit, pay taxes, and show respect. This is
the general principle — not an absolute that overrides obedience to God (Acts
5:29) when government commands what God forbids.

The Debt of Love (vv.8-10): The only debt Christians should carry is the
ongoing debt to love one another. Love fulfills the entire Law — every
commandment is summarized in "love thy neighbour as thyself" (v.9).

"Put on the Lord Jesus Christ" (v.14): The verse that converted Augustine. He
heard a child's voice saying "Take up and read" — opened to this passage and
was transformed. "Put on Christ" — clothe yourself in His character, His
priorities, His way of living.

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.1  — "Let every person be subject to the governing authorities. For there
        is no authority except from God, and those that exist have been
        instituted by God." (ESV) — Government as God's institution.

v.4  — "He is God's servant for your good... an avenger who carries out God's
        wrath on the wrongdoer." (ESV) — Government's God-given role: reward
        good, punish evil.

v.8  — "Owe no one anything, except to love each other, for the one who loves
        another has fulfilled the law." (ESV) — Love as the fulfillment of
        all law.

v.10 — "Love does no wrong to a neighbor; therefore love is the fulfilling of
        the law." (ESV) — Love defined negatively: it does no harm.

v.14 — "Put on the Lord Jesus Christ, and make no provision for the flesh, to
        gratify its desires." (ESV) — The positive and negative commands: put
        on Christ, don't feed the flesh.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1  — KJV: "higher powers" — ESV: "governing authorities" — Greek "exousiais
       huperechousais" — authorities that are over you. This includes all
       levels of government — local, national, and imperial.
       NET: "Let every person be subject to the governing
       authorities" — "be subject" (hypotassesthO = place yourself
       under in an orderly way). WEB: "Let every soul be in
       subjection to the higher authorities." KJV: "Let every soul
       be subject unto the higher powers." Not blind obedience but
       ordered respect for God-established authority.

v.1  — KJV: "ordained of God" / ESV: "have been instituted by God" — Greek "tetagmenai" — arranged, appointed,
       established by God. Government is God's institution — even imperfect
       government serves His purposes of restraining evil.
       NET: "Let every person be subject to the governing
       authorities" — "be subject" (hypotassesthO = place yourself
       under in an orderly way). WEB: "Let every soul be in
       subjection to the higher authorities." KJV: "Let every soul
       be subject unto the higher powers." Not blind obedience but
       ordered respect for God-established authority.

v.4  — KJV: "beareth not the sword in vain" / ESV: "not bear the sword in vain. For he is" — The "sword" represents the
       government's God-given authority to use force — including capital
       punishment — to restrain evil and protect the innocent.
       NET: "for it is God's servant for your good" — the state
       as "God's servant" (theou diakonos). WEB: "for he is a
       servant of God to you for good." KJV: "the minister of God
       to thee for good." Government exists for public good under
       God's sovereignty.

v.8  — NET: "Owe no one anything, except to love one another" —
       "owe" (opheilete = be indebted). WEB: "Owe no one anything,
       except to love one another." KJV: "Owe no man any thing, but
       to love one another." Love is the one debt never fully paid.

v.11 — KJV: "our salvation nearer than when we believed" / ESV: "is nearer to us now than when we first believed" — "Salvation" here
       refers to final salvation — glorification, the return of Christ. Every
       day brings it closer.
       NET: "it is already the hour for you to awake from sleep"
       — "awake" (egerthEnai = rise/be raised — resurrection
       language). WEB: "knowing the time, that it is already time
       for you to awaken out of sleep." KJV: "now it is high time
       to awake out of sleep." Spiritual drowsiness is dangerous.

v.14 — KJV: "make not provision for the flesh" / ESV: "and make no provision for the flesh" — Greek "pronoian mē poieisthe"
       — don't plan ahead for the flesh's desires. Don't set up situations
       where temptation is easy. Cut off the supply lines of sin.
       NET: "Instead, put on the Lord Jesus Christ, and make no
       provision for the flesh to arouse its desires" — "put on"
       (endysasthe = clothe yourself with). WEB: "put on the Lord
       Jesus Christ." KJV: "put ye on the Lord Jesus Christ."
       Augustine's conversion verse — he read this and was changed.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Higher powers — Governing authorities — human government at all levels.
               Established by God to restrain evil and promote order.

Minister of God — Government officials as God's servants — whether they know
               it or not. They serve God's purpose of maintaining order.

Sword         — The symbol of government's authority to use force — including
               the death penalty — to punish evil and protect the innocent.

Debt of love  — The only ongoing obligation Christians should carry: to love
               one another continually. A debt that is never fully paid.

Armor of light — The spiritual equipment for living in the "daytime" of
               Christ's coming kingdom — righteousness, holiness, and love.

Put on Christ — To clothe yourself in Christ's character — making His
               qualities visible in your daily life.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "Let every soul be subject unto the higher powers" (v.1). How do you relate
   to government — with respect or with contempt? Even when you disagree with
   leaders, do you honor the institution God established?

2. "Owe no man any thing, but to love one another" (v.8). Love is a debt you
   can never fully pay — it's always owed. Who do you owe love to today that
   you haven't paid? What would it look like to pay that debt?

3. "It is high time to awake out of sleep" (v.11). Spiritual drowsiness is
   dangerous — especially as Christ's return draws nearer. Are you spiritually
   awake and alert, or have you drifted into complacency?

4. "Put ye on the Lord Jesus Christ, and make not provision for the flesh"
   (v.14). Two commands: put ON Christ (positive) and don't feed the flesh
   (negative). What are you putting on? What are you feeding? Are you making
   it easy for sin by leaving room for it?

REFLECTION QUESTIONS
------------------------------------
1. Paul says government is "ordained of God" (v.1) — even the Roman Empire
   that would eventually execute him. How do you submit to government you
   disagree with while maintaining obedience to God? Where is the line?

2. "Love worketh no ill to his neighbour" (v.10). Love is defined by what it
   does NOT do — it does no harm. Evaluate your relationships: are you causing
   harm to anyone — through words, actions, neglect, or indifference?

3. "The night is far spent, the day is at hand" (v.12). We live between
   Christ's first and second coming — the night is ending, the day is
   approaching. How does living in this "already/not yet" tension affect your
   daily priorities?

4. "Make not provision for the flesh" (v.14). Don't plan for sin. Don't put
   yourself in situations where temptation is easy. What "provisions" for the
   flesh do you need to eliminate — access, habits, environments, relationships?

5. Augustine was converted by reading v.14. One verse changed his life. Is
   there a verse God is using to speak to you right now? What would it look
   like to obey it immediately and completely?
