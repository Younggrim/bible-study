1 Thessalonians - Chapter 3
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Paul sent Timothy to strengthen the Thessalonians during persecution, rejoices at Timothy's good report of their faith, and prays that the Lord would cause them to increase and abound in love for one another and for all.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Paul the Apostle (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 3 reveals Paul's deep pastoral concern for the
young Thessalonian church. Unable to return himself (2:18), Paul sent Timothy
from Athens to check on them (v.2). Timothy returned with good news: their
faith and love were strong (v.6). Paul's relief is palpable — "now we live,
if ye stand fast" (v.8). The chapter ends with a prayer for their growth in
love and holiness before Christ's coming (vv.11-13).

Timothy's Mission (vv.1-5): Paul couldn't bear the uncertainty any longer
(v.1). He sent Timothy to "establish" and "comfort" them (v.2), fearing the
"tempter" might have undermined their faith through persecution (v.5). Paul
had warned them suffering was coming (v.4) — and it did.

Timothy's Report (vv.6-10): Good news: faith and love intact, fond memories
of Paul, mutual desire to reunite (v.6). Paul was "comforted" by their faith
even in his own affliction (v.7). Their steadfastness gave him life (v.8).

Paul's Prayer (vv.11-13): Three requests: (1) that God would direct his path
back to them (v.11), (2) that they would increase in love (v.12), (3) that
their hearts would be established in holiness at Christ's coming (v.13).

KEY VERSES
------------------------------------
v.3 — "That no one be moved by these afflictions. For you yourselves know
 that we are destined for this." (ESV) — Suffering is appointed, not
 accidental.

v.8 — "For now we live, if you are standing fast in the Lord." (ESV) — A
 pastor's life is bound to his people's faithfulness.

v.12 — "May the Lord make you increase and abound in love for one another
 and for all." (ESV) — Love that grows and overflows.

v.13 — "So that he may establish your hearts blameless in holiness before
 our God and Father, at the coming of our Lord Jesus with all his
 saints." (ESV) — Holiness in view of Christ's return.

TRANSLATION COMPARISON
------------------------------------
v.1 — KJV: "could no longer forbear" / ESV: "could bear it no longer" — Greek "mēketi stegontes" — no
 longer able to contain ourselves, unable to bear it any longer. The
 word "stegō" means to cover, contain, endure. Paul's concern was
 bursting out.

v.2 — KJV: "establish" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "stērixai" — to strengthen, make firm, set
 solidly. "Comfort" — Greek "parakalesai" — to encourage, come
 alongside. Timothy's dual mission: strengthen and encourage.
       NET: "we sent Timothy... to strengthen and encourage
       you about your faith" — "strengthen" (stErixai = establish/
       make firm). WEB: "to establish you and to comfort you." KJV:
       "to establish you, and to comfort you." Two pastoral verbs:
       stabilize and encourage.

v.3 — KJV: "moved" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "sainesthai" — to be shaken, disturbed, swayed.
 Originally meant "to wag the tail" (of a dog being flattered). Don't
 be swayed by persecution.

v.5 — KJV: "the tempter" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "ho peirazōn" — the one who tests/tempts.
 Satan's specific role: using suffering to tempt believers to abandon
 faith.
       NET: "I sent to find out about your faith, for fear that
       the tempter somehow tempted you" — "the tempter" (ho
       peirazOn = the one who tests/tempts). WEB: "the tempter had
       tempted you." KJV: "the tempter have tempted you." Satan
       has a specific role: testing faith through suffering.

v.8  — NET: "For now we are alive again, if you stand firm in the
       Lord" — "alive again" (zOmen = we live). WEB: "we live, if
       you stand firm in the Lord." Their faithfulness gives Paul
       life — pastoral joy is tied to converts' perseverance.

v.10 — KJV: "perfect that which is lacking" / ESV: "supply what is lacking" — Greek "katartisai ta
 husterēmata" — to mend, complete, supply what is deficient. Their
 faith was genuine but incomplete — they needed further instruction.
       NET: "as we pray earnestly night and day to see you in
       person and to supply what is lacking in your faith" —
       "supply what is lacking" (katartisai ta hysterEmata =
       complete the deficiencies/mend the gaps). WEB: "perfect
       that which is lacking." Faith has gaps that need filling
       through ongoing teaching.

v.12 — NET: "may the Lord cause you to increase and overflow with
       love for one another" — "overflow" (perisseuai = abound/
       exceed). WEB: "abound in love toward one another." KJV:
       "abound in love one toward another." Love should exceed
       normal bounds — not just adequate but overflowing.

v.13 — KJV: "with all his saints" / ESV: Both equivalent. — Greek "hagiōn" — holy ones. Could refer
 to: (1) believers who have died, (2) angels, or (3) both. Christ
 returns accompanied by His holy ones.

GLOSSARY
------------------------------------
Appointed to affliction — Suffering as part of God's plan for believers.
 Not random but purposeful, not punishment but training.

The tempter — Satan in his role of using trials to tempt believers away
 from faith. He exploits suffering to create doubt.

Establish — To strengthen, make firm, set on solid ground. What young
 believers need in the face of persecution.

Stand fast — To remain firm, hold position, not be moved. The evidence
 of genuine faith under pressure.

Unblameable in holiness — The goal of Christian growth: blameless character
 at Christ's return. Not sinless perfection but consistent
 holiness.

PERSONAL REFLECTION & APPLICATION
------------------------------------
1. "We are appointed thereunto" (v.3). Suffering is not accidental — it's
 appointed. How does knowing this change your response to trials? Does it
 bring comfort or raise questions?

2. "Now we live, if ye stand fast" (v.8). Paul's joy was tied to their
 faithfulness. Whose spiritual growth brings you joy? Whose faithfulness
 gives you life? Are you investing in others at that level?

3. "Increase and abound in love" (v.12). Love should be growing — not static.
 Is your love increasing? Toward fellow believers? Toward "all men" —
 including difficult people and outsiders?

4. "Unblameable in holiness... at the coming of our Lord" (v.13). If Christ
 returned today, would your heart be found "unblameable in holiness"? What
 needs to change to be ready?
