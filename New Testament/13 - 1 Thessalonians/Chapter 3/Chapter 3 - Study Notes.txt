1 Thessalonians - Chapter 3
Bible Study Notes
========================================

CHAPTER SUMMARY
------------------------------------
Paul sent Timothy to strengthen the Thessalonians during persecution, rejoices at Timothy's good report of their faith, and prays that the Lord would cause them to increase and abound in love for one another and for all.

AUTHORSHIP & HISTORICAL BACKGROUND
------------------------------------
Author: Paul the Apostle (see Chapter 1 notes for full authorship details).

Historical Context: Chapter 3 reveals Paul's deep pastoral concern for the
young Thessalonian church. Unable to return himself (2:18), Paul sent Timothy
from Athens to check on them (v.2). Timothy returned with good news: their
faith and love were strong (v.6). Paul's relief is palpable — "now we live,
if ye stand fast" (v.8). The chapter ends with a prayer for their growth in
love and holiness before Christ's coming (vv.11-13).

Timothy's Mission (vv.1-5): Paul couldn't bear the uncertainty any longer
(v.1). He sent Timothy to "establish" and "comfort" them (v.2), fearing the
"tempter" might have undermined their faith through persecution (v.5). Paul
had warned them suffering was coming (v.4) — and it did.

Timothy's Report (vv.6-10): Good news: faith and love intact, fond memories
of Paul, mutual desire to reunite (v.6). Paul was "comforted" by their faith
even in his own affliction (v.7). Their steadfastness gave him life (v.8).

Paul's Prayer (vv.11-13): Three requests: (1) that God would direct his path
back to them (v.11), (2) that they would increase in love (v.12), (3) that
their hearts would be established in holiness at Christ's coming (v.13).

Charles Spurgeon:
 Sermon: "Appointed to Affliction" on v.3 (MTP Vol. 20, No. 1185)
 "We are appointed thereunto. Affliction is not an accident — it is an
 appointment. God has scheduled your trials as surely as He has scheduled
 your blessings. The same hand that writes your joys writes your sorrows.
 Knowing this changes everything: you are not a victim of random misfortune
 but a child under a Father's wise discipline."

REFLECTION
------------------------------------
1. "We are appointed thereunto" (v.3). Suffering is not accidental — it's
 appointed. How does knowing this change your response to trials? Does it
 bring comfort or raise questions?

2. "Now we live, if ye stand fast" (v.8). Paul's joy was tied to their
 faithfulness. Whose spiritual growth brings you joy? Whose faithfulness
 gives you life? Are you investing in others at that level?

3. "Increase and abound in love" (v.12). Love should be growing — not static.
 Is your love increasing? Toward fellow believers? Toward "all men" —
 including difficult people and outsiders?

4. "Unblameable in holiness... at the coming of our Lord" (v.13). If Christ
 returned today, would your heart be found "unblameable in holiness"? What
 needs to change to be ready?
